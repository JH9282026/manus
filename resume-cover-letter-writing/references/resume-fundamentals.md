## Resume Fundamentals



### What Is a Resume?

A resume is a concise marketing document that summarizes your professional qualifications, experience, and accomplishments. Its primary purpose is to generate interest and secure interviews—not to tell your complete life story or serve as a comprehensive work history.

**Key characteristics of effective resumes:**

- **Targeted:** Tailored to specific positions and employers
- **Concise:** Typically 1-2 pages, focused on relevant information
- **Achievement-focused:** Emphasizes results and impact, not just duties
- **Scannable:** Easy to read quickly with clear visual hierarchy
- **Keyword-optimized:** Contains terms that match job requirements
- **Error-free:** Professional in every detail



### Resume vs. CV

Understanding the distinction between resumes and curriculum vitae (CVs) is important:

**Resume:**
- 1-2 pages (rarely longer except for executives)
- Focused on relevant experience for target position
- Common in business, industry, and most professional fields
- Standard in the United States for most positions

**Curriculum Vitae (CV):**
- No length limit—as long as needed
- Comprehensive record of academic and professional history
- Includes publications, presentations, grants, teaching experience
- Standard for academic, research, and medical positions
- Common in Europe and international applications

This guide focuses primarily on resume writing, though many principles apply to CVs as well.
