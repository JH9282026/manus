## Addressing Employment Gaps


### In the Resume

If gaps are short or explainable:
- Include years only (not months) to minimize visibility
- List relevant activities during gap (consulting, education, volunteering)
- Be prepared to explain in interviews

If gaps are significant:
- Consider functional resume format
- Include a "Career Break" entry if appropriate
- Focus heavily on skills and achievements

**Example gap handling:**

```
CAREER BREAK – 2020-2021
Full-time caregiver for family member
• Completed Google Data Analytics Certificate
• Provided consulting support for two early-stage startups
• Maintained industry knowledge through professional development
```


### In the Cover Letter

Cover letters can proactively address gaps:

"After taking time to care for a family member in 2021, I've spent the past year consulting for startups and completing advanced certifications. I'm now eager to return to a full-time leadership role where I can apply both my previous experience and the fresh perspectives I've gained."
