# Competitive Analysis & Consumer Research

## Competitive Landscape Mapping

### Competitor Identification
1. **Direct competitors** — Same product, same market
2. **Indirect competitors** — Different product, same need
3. **Potential competitors** — Adjacent markets, future threat
4. **Substitute products** — Alternative solutions to same problem

### Competitive Intelligence Gathering
| Source | Data Available |
|--------|--------------|
| Company websites | Products, pricing, positioning |
| Annual reports/10-K | Revenue, strategy, risk factors |
| Job postings | Expansion plans, technology stack |
| Patent filings | Innovation direction |
| Social media | Brand voice, customer sentiment |
| Review sites | Customer satisfaction, pain points |
| Trade shows | Product roadmap, partnerships |

### Competitor Profiling Template
For each major competitor, document:
1. **Overview** — Size, market share, positioning
2. **Products/Services** — Offerings, pricing, features
3. **Strengths** — Competitive advantages
4. **Weaknesses** — Known limitations, vulnerabilities
5. **Strategy** — Growth direction, recent moves
6. **Customer base** — Target segments, key accounts
7. **Technology** — Platform, capabilities, innovation

## Porter's Five Forces Analysis

| Force | Key Questions | Impact Rating |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| Rivalry | Number of competitors, industry growth, differentiation | High/Medium/Low |
| Buyer power | Concentration, switching costs, price sensitivity | High/Medium/Low |
| Supplier power | Concentration, uniqueness, switching costs | High/Medium/Low |
| New entrants | Barriers, capital needs, brand loyalty | High/Medium/Low |
| Substitutes | Availability, price-performance, switching ease | High/Medium/Low |

## Consumer Behavior Research

### Research Methods
| Method | Sample Size | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| In-depth interviews | 10-30 | Deep insights, motivations |
| Focus groups | 6-10 per group | Group dynamics, concept testing |
| Surveys | 200-1,000+ | Quantitative data, trends |
| Ethnographic observation | 10-20 | Natural behavior patterns |
| A/B testing | 1,000+ | Behavioral preferences |

### Consumer Segmentation Approaches
| Type | Variables | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Demographic | Age, income, education | Millennials, $75K+ income |
| Geographic | Region, urban/rural, climate | Northeast urban |
| Psychographic | Values, lifestyle, personality | Health-conscious, adventurous |
| Behavioral | Usage rate, loyalty, benefits sought | Heavy users, brand switchers |
| Needs-based | Problems to solve, jobs to be done | Time-saving, cost-reducing |

### Customer Journey Mapping
1. **Awareness** — How do they discover solutions?
2. **Consideration** — What criteria matter most?
3. **Decision** — What triggers purchase?
4. **Purchase** — What's the buying experience?
5. **Post-purchase** — Satisfaction, loyalty, advocacy?
