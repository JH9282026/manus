# Storytelling Through Editing

Building narrative and emotion through edit choices.

---

## The Kuleshov Effect

### Principle

Viewers derive meaning from shot juxtaposition, not individual shots:
- Same shot takes different meanings based on adjacent shots
- Face + soup = hunger
- Face + coffin = grief
- Face + child = love

### Applications

- Create character emotions through context
- Generate meaning not present in individual shots
- Manipulate audience interpretation
- Build complex emotional narratives

---

## Building Emotional Arcs

### Sequence Structure

**Setup (Exposition)**
- Establish location, characters, mood
- Longer shots, deliberate pacing
- Build context

**Development (Rising Action)**
- Introduce conflict or tension
- Accelerate pacing gradually
- Increase cutting frequency

**Climax**
- Maximum intensity
- Fastest cutting or most dramatic long take
- Peak emotional engagement

**Resolution**
- Decelerate pacing
- Return to contemplative rhythm
- Allow emotional processing

---

## Parallel Editing

### Cross-Cutting Between Storylines

Intercut two or more simultaneous storylines:
- Build suspense through comparison
- Create dramatic irony
- Compress time across locations
- Build toward convergence

### Implementation

**Establishing Separation**
- Clearly differentiate locations initially
- Use distinct visual looks for each thread
- Establish time relationship

**Building Tension**
- Increase cutting frequency as tension builds
- Cut at moments of uncertainty
- Leave storylines at critical points

**Convergence**
- Build toward storylines meeting
- Accelerate cutting as convergence approaches
- Time for maximum impact

## Montage Types

### Metric Montage

- Cuts based on time regardless of content
- Creates mechanical, rhythmic effect
- Often synced to music beat

### Rhythmic Montage

- Cuts based on movement within shots
- Creates flowing, dynamic sequences
- Movement leads to next shot

### Tonal Montage

- Cuts based on emotional quality
- Similar moods connect shots
- Creates atmospheric sequences

### Intellectual Montage

- Juxtaposition creates ideas
- Abstract concepts through concrete images
- Challenges viewer to synthesize meaning

---

## Montage Construction

### Planning

1. Define purpose (show time passage, build emotion, convey info)
2. Select musical accompaniment
3. Gather footage supporting theme
4. Identify key moments

### Assembly

1. Rough cut to music
2. Identify strongest moments
3. Create logical or emotional progression
4. Vary shot scales and compositions

### Best Practices

- Limit duration (30 seconds to 3 minutes)
- Vary shot types (wide, medium, close)
- Include movement and action
- Connect to narrative before and after

## Creating Subtext

### Juxtaposition Techniques

- **Contrast**: Cut between opposing elements
- **Parallel**: Similar actions in different contexts
- **Symbolic**: Connect concrete with abstract
- **Ironic**: Undercut surface meaning

### Example

Wealthy dinner party cuts to homeless person:
- Creates social commentary
- Meaning emerges from juxtaposition
- Neither shot alone carries message

---

## Genre-Specific Approaches

### Action

- Fast cutting (1-3 seconds)
- Cut on action for continuity
- Speed ramping for impact
- Heavy sound design

### Drama

- Longer takes
- Focus on performance
- Match editing to character state
- Subtle transitions

### Comedy

- Timing is everything
- Hold for reactions
- Strategic pauses
- Quick transitions for energy

### Documentary

- Interview-driven structure
- B-roll coverage
- Create narrative from reality
- Maintain authenticity
