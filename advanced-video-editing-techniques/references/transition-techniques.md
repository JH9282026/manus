# Advanced Transition Techniques

Beyond cuts, dissolves, and fades.

---

## Whip Pans / Swish Pans

### Technique
1. End outgoing shot with rapid pan
2. Begin incoming shot mid-pan
3. Match blur direction and intensity
4. Cut within the motion blur

### Applications
- Music videos
- Action sequences
- Travel montages
- Comedy timing

---

## Invisible Cuts

### Techniques
- Cut during rapid camera movement
- Cut when subject blocks frame
- Cut during flashes or bright lights
- Cut on motivated movements
- Digital masking to blend

### Applications
- Create illusion of long takes
- Stitch multiple locations
- Hide performance differences
- Build "one-shot" sequences

---

## Match Movement Transitions

### Principle
Same camera movement across cut creates continuous feel.

### Requirements
- Match speed and direction
- Similar lighting conditions
- Complementary compositions

---

## Masking Transitions

### Technique
Use moving objects to hide cut point:
- Subject passes camera
- Door opening/closing
- Object wipe across frame

### Planning
Requires careful choreography or creative discovery.

---

## Morphing Transitions

### Process
1. Select similar compositions
2. Apply morph effect
3. Adjust keyframes
4. Refine timing

### Applications
- Age progression
- Before/after
- Surreal sequences

---

## Transition Selection Guide

| Need | Best Choice |
|------|------------|
| Invisible edit | Cut |
| Time passage | Dissolve |
| Scene end | Fade |
| Energy burst | Whip pan |
| Magic trick | Invisible cut |
| Surreal | Morph |

### Rules
- Cuts are most versatile
- Stylized transitions need consistency
- Less is usually more
- Match tone of content
