# Customer Success

Design and execute proactive customer success programs that drive retention, expansion, and advocacy through health scoring, playbook automation, and strategic engagement.

## Overview

Customer success in 2026 is a revenue-driving function, not just support. It combines predictive health scoring, automated playbooks, and strategic relationship management to ensure customers achieve their desired outcomes — and expand their investment. This skill provides frameworks for every stage of the customer lifecycle, from onboarding through renewal and expansion.

## Customer Health Scoring Framework

| Health Signal | Weight | Data Source | Green | Yellow | Red |
|--------------|--------|-------------|-------|--------|-----|
| Product usage (DAU/MAU) | 25% | Product analytics | >60% | 30–60% | <30% |
| Feature adoption depth | 20% | Feature tracking | >5 core features | 3–5 features | <3 features |
| Support ticket sentiment | 15% | NLP on tickets | Positive/neutral | Mixed | Negative trend |
| NPS/CSAT score | 15% | Survey platform | >8 | 6–8 | <6 |
| Executive engagement | 10% | CRM activity | Monthly contact | Quarterly | >90 days silent |
| Contract utilization | 10% | Billing system | >80% of licenses | 50–80% | <50% |
| Payment behavior | 5% | Finance system | On time | Occasionally late | Consistently late |

### Composite Score Calculation

- **Score 80–100 (Healthy):** Focus on expansion and advocacy. Trigger upsell playbook.
- **Score 60–79 (Monitor):** Schedule proactive check-in. Review adoption gaps.
- **Score 40–59 (At Risk):** Escalate to CS manager. Trigger intervention playbook within 48 hours.
- **Score 0–39 (Critical):** Executive sponsor engagement. Daily monitoring. Dedicated rescue plan.

## Customer Lifecycle Playbooks

| Lifecycle Stage | Playbook | Trigger | Goal | Key Actions |
|----------------|----------|---------|------|-------------|
| Onboarding (0–90 days) | Quick Start | Contract signed | Time-to-first-value <14 days | Kickoff call, config, training, milestone check |
| Adoption (90–180 days) | Deep Adoption | Onboarding complete | >3 features adopted | Feature workshops, use-case mapping, champion training |
| Growth (180–365 days) | Expansion | Health score >80 | Upsell/cross-sell | Business review, ROI analysis, expansion proposal |
| Renewal (60 days pre) | Renewal | 60 days before renewal | On-time renewal >95% | Value summary, pricing review, renewal negotiation |
| At-Risk (any time) | Intervention | Health score <60 | Prevent churn | Root cause analysis, executive call, remediation plan |
| Advocacy (ongoing) | Champion | NPS >9, 12+ months | References & referrals | Case study, speaking opportunity, referral program |

## QBR (Quarterly Business Review) Framework

### Preparation Checklist
1. Pull usage analytics for the quarter (adoption, active users, feature utilization)
2. Calculate ROI metrics tied to customer's stated business goals
3. Document support interactions and resolution trends
4. Prepare expansion opportunities mapped to customer roadmap
5. Draft strategic recommendations (3–5 specific actions)

### QBR Agenda Structure (60 minutes)

| Block | Duration | Content | Owner |
|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| Business outcomes review | 15 min | KPIs achieved vs. goals, ROI summary | CS Manager |
| Usage & adoption insights | 10 min | Feature adoption, user engagement trends | CS Manager |
| Support & satisfaction | 5 min | Ticket trends, CSAT, resolved issues | Support Lead |
| Product roadmap preview | 10 min | Upcoming features relevant to customer | Product |
| Strategic recommendations | 10 min | 3–5 specific actions to increase value | CS Manager |
| Expansion discussion | 5 min | New use cases, additional seats/features | CS + Sales |
| Action items & next steps | 5 min | Document owners and deadlines | All |

### QBR Best Practices
- Send the deck 48 hours in advance so executives come prepared
- Lead with business outcomes, not product features
- Include a "value delivered" dollar amount tied to their stated goals
- Never surprise the customer — flag issues before the QBR
- End with a mutual success plan for the next quarter

## Key Metrics & Benchmarks

| Metric | Formula | Good | Great | Elite |
|--------|---------|------|-------|-------|
| Gross Revenue Retention (GRR) | (Start ARR − Churn − Contraction) / Start ARR | >85% | >90% | >95% |
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | (Start ARR + Expansion − Churn − Contraction) / Start ARR | >100% | >110% | >130% |
| Time to First Value | Days from contract to first "aha" moment | <30 days | <14 days | <7 days |
| Onboarding completion rate | % completing all onboarding milestones | >70% | >85% | >95% |
| QBR attendance rate | % of customers attending scheduled QBRs | >60% | >75% | >90% |
| NPS | Net Promoter Score | >30 | >50 | >70 |
| Logo churn rate | Customers lost / Total customers | <10% | <5% | <2% |

## Best Practices

1. **Segment your book of business** — Apply high-touch (1:few), mid-touch (1:many), and tech-touch (1:all) models based on ARR and strategic value
2. **Define and track time-to-value** — The single most predictive metric for long-term retention
3. **Build internal champions** — Multi-thread relationships; never depend on a single contact
4. **Automate the repeatable** — Use playbooks for standard interventions so CSMs focus on strategic work
5. **Tie everything to business outcomes** — Customers don't care about features; they care about results
6. **Proactive > reactive** — A CS team that only responds to problems is a support team with a different title
