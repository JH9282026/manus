# Case Study Copywriting

Transform customer success stories into compelling marketing assets that demonstrate product value through real-world results.

## Overview

Case study copywriting combines storytelling techniques with data-driven evidence to create persuasive narratives that resonate with potential customers. Unlike other marketing content, case studies provide concrete evidence of capabilities through actual outcomes achieved by real customers.

## Case Study Structure

### Standard Format

| Section | Purpose | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Executive Summary | Quick overview for time-pressed readers | 100-200 words |
| Challenge | Establish stakes and customer situation | 200-300 words |
| Solution | How your product addressed the challenge | 200-300 words |
| Implementation | Deployment and adoption process | 150-200 words |
| Results | Concrete, measurable outcomes | 300-400 words |
| Testimonial | Customer endorsement quote | 50-100 words |
| Call-to-Action | Next step for readers | 25-50 words |

### Executive Summary Template

"[Company Name], a [brief description], faced [primary challenge]. After implementing [your solution], they achieved [key result 1], [key result 2], and [key result 3] within [timeframe]."

## Case Study Types

| Type | Focus | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Customer Success Story | Overall positive experience | Brand building |
| Problem-Solution | Specific challenge resolution | Addressing objections |
| Before-After | Dramatic transformation | Visual impact |
| ROI-Focused | Financial impact and returns | CFO/finance audiences |
| Industry-Specific | Vertical-tailored challenges | Niche marketing |
| Product/Feature | Specific capabilities | Product marketing |

## Writing the Challenge Section

### Key Questions to Address

- What was broken or inefficient?
- What were the business consequences?
- Why was solving this problem urgent?
- What had they tried that didn't work?

### Challenge Writing Tips

- Paint a vivid picture of pain points
- Quantify the problem when possible
- Explain previous failed attempts
- Describe business impact
- Create emotional resonance with similar prospects

## Writing the Results Section

### Types of Metrics

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Revenue | Revenue increase, new customers, deal size growth |
| Efficiency | Time savings, productivity gains, capacity increases |
| Cost | Direct reduction, labor savings, waste elimination |
| Quality | Error reduction, satisfaction scores, compliance |

### Presenting Data Effectively

| Format | Example | Impact |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Percentage | "340% increase in conversions" | High impact |
| Absolute with context | "Saved 2,500 hours annually—equivalent to 1.2 FTEs" | Tangible |
| Before-After | "Reduced processing from 45 to 3 minutes" | Clear comparison |
| ROI | "$2.4M revenue against $50K investment—48x ROI" | Business case |
| Time-to-Value | "Positive ROI within 90 days" | Speed emphasis |

## Interview Process

### Selecting Candidates

| Criterion | Why It Matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| Measurable results | Provides concrete evidence |
| Brand recognition | Adds credibility |
| Industry relevance | Enables targeting |
| Willingness to participate | Ensures cooperation |
| Story uniqueness | Differentiates content |

### Interview Questions

**Challenge Questions:**
- What problems were you trying to solve?
- How was this impacting your business?
- What had you tried that didn't work?

**Solution Questions:**
- Why did you choose [our solution]?
- What made us stand out from alternatives?

**Results Questions:**
- What results have you seen?
- Can you quantify the improvement?
- How quickly did you see results?

**Testimonial Questions:**
- How would you describe [solution] to a colleague?
- What would you tell someone considering [solution]?

### Getting Quantifiable Results

**Prompts for extracting metrics:**
- "If you had to put a number on it, what would you estimate?"
- "How does that compare to before?"
- "What percentage improvement have you seen?"
- "Can you walk me through the calculation?"

## Using Quotes Effectively

### Strong Quote Characteristics

- Specific, not generic
- Emotionally resonant
- Include concrete details
- Sound natural and authentic
- Support key claims

### Quote Placement

| Position | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Opening hook | Draw readers in |
| After challenge | Validate the problem |
| Following statistics | Add human context |
| During solution | Explain decision |
| Closing | Endorsement and CTA |

### Quote Examples

**Weak**: "The product is great and we're very happy with it."

**Strong**: "Before implementing [Solution], I dreaded Monday mornings because I'd spend half my day on manual reporting. Now I actually look forward to seeing our real-time dashboard—it's completely changed how I work."

## Case Study Lengths

| Format | Length | Best For |
|--------|--------|----------|
| One-Page | 300-500 words | Sales leave-behinds, quick overviews |
| Standard | 800-1,500 words | Website, sales enablement |
| In-Depth | 2,000-4,000 words | Enterprise sales, complex solutions |
| Video | 2-5 minutes | Social media, website hero content |
| Micro (Social) | 50-200 words | LinkedIn, Twitter, quick consumption |

## B2B vs B2C Considerations

| Element | B2B Approach | B2C Approach |
|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Length | 2-6 pages | 1-2 pages |
| Tone | Professional, data-driven | Personal, emotional |
| Metrics | ROI, efficiency, cost savings | Satisfaction, lifestyle |
| Visuals | Charts, graphs, logos | Lifestyle photos, before/after |
| CTA | Contact sales, request demo | Buy now, start trial |
