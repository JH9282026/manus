# Writing Techniques

Crafting compelling case study narratives.

---

## Storytelling Structure

### Hero's Journey for Case Studies

| Stage | Case Study Application |
|-------|------------------------|
| Ordinary World | Customer before the challenge |
| Call to Adventure | Problem emerges |
| Refusal/Hesitation | Previous failed attempts |
| Meeting the Mentor | Discovering your solution |
| Crossing Threshold | Decision to implement |
| Tests and Allies | Implementation challenges |
| Ordeal | Critical turning point |
| Reward | Initial results |
| Return with Elixir | Full transformation, advocacy |

**Key Insight**: The customer is the hero; your brand is the mentor.

### Three-Act Structure

| Act | Content | Percentage |
|-----|---------|------------|
| Setup (Challenge) | Customer situation, pain points | 25% |
| Confrontation (Solution) | Selection, implementation | 35% |
| Resolution (Results) | Outcomes, transformation | 40% |

## Opening Techniques

### The Dramatic Result Lead

> "In just six months, TechCorp increased their conversion rate by 340%—a transformation that seemed impossible when they first approached us."

### The Relatable Problem Lead

> "Like many growing SaaS companies, DataFlow was drowning in spreadsheets. Their sales team spent more time on manual data entry than actually selling."

### The Quote Lead

> "'We knew we had to change something, or we wouldn't survive the year,' recalls Jennifer Martinez, VP of Operations at RetailMax."

### The Before-After Contrast

> "Two years ago, processing a single customer order took FinanceFirst 45 minutes. Today, it takes 3 minutes."

## Writing the Challenge Section

### Structure

1. Set the scene (company context)
2. Introduce the problem (what was broken)
3. Explain the stakes (business impact)
4. Show previous attempts (what didn't work)
5. Create urgency (why change was needed now)

### Techniques

| Technique | Example |
|-----------|--------|
| Quantify the pain | "Losing $50K monthly to inefficient processes" |
| Use vivid language | "Drowning in spreadsheets" |
| Show human impact | "Team members worked weekends just to keep up" |
| Create relatability | "Like many growing companies..." |

---

## Writing the Solution Section

### Elements to Include

- Why they chose your solution
- Key features/capabilities used
- Decision-making process
- What differentiated you from alternatives

### Avoiding Sales-Speak

| Too Promotional | Better |
|-----------------|--------|
| "Our revolutionary platform" | "The platform's drag-and-drop interface" |
| "Industry-leading solution" | "The integration with their existing CRM" |
| "Powerful features" | "The automated reporting saved 10 hours weekly" |

## Writing the Results Section

### Data Presentation

**Multiple Formats for Impact**:

1. **Statistics**: "340% increase in conversions"
2. **Dollar figures**: "$2.4M in additional revenue"
3. **Time metrics**: "Reduced from 45 minutes to 3 minutes"
4. **Visual callouts**: Large, bold statistics
5. **Quote validation**: Customer confirming the numbers

### Results Writing Template

```
The impact was immediate and measurable:

• **[Metric 1]**: [Specific result with context]
• **[Metric 2]**: [Specific result with context]
• **[Metric 3]**: [Specific result with context]

"[Quote validating results]" says [Name, Title].

Beyond the numbers, [unexpected benefit or qualitative improvement].
```

## Using Quotes

### Quote Integration Techniques

**Standalone**:
> "This changed everything for our team."
> — Sarah Chen, Marketing Director

**Embedded**:
The results, Chen says, were "beyond anything we expected."

**Section Opener**:
> "We knew we had to change something."

Jennifer Martinez, VP of Operations, had been struggling with...

### Editing Quotes

**Acceptable**:
- Minor grammar corrections
- Removing filler words (um, you know)
- Shortening for clarity
- Adding context in brackets [our team]

**Not Acceptable**:
- Changing meaning
- Adding words they didn't say
- Combining separate statements without indication

## Formatting for Readability

### Scannable Elements

- **Bold** key statistics and outcomes
- Use bullet points for lists
- Include pull quotes in callout boxes
- Add subheadings every 200-300 words
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)

### Visual Hierarchy

1. Title with key result
2. Executive summary box
3. Challenge section with problem callout
4. Solution section
5. Results with statistics callouts
6. Quote box
7. CTA section

### Callout Box Template

```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│        340%               │
│  Increase in Conversions  │
└─────────────────────────────┘
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Too promotional | Reads like sales copy | Focus on customer's story |
| Vague results | "Significant improvement" | Always quantify |
| Missing customer voice | No quotes | Include 3-5 quotes minimum |
| Burying the lead | Results at the end only | Lead with key result |
| Too long | Loses reader attention | Trim to essential elements |
| No CTA | Reader doesn't know next step | Clear call to action |
