# Interview Guide

Comprehensive guidance for conducting case study interviews.

---

## Pre-Interview Preparation

### Research Checklist

- [ ] Review account history and notes
- [ ] Analyze usage data and metrics
- [ ] Study customer's industry and competitors
- [ ] Review their website and recent news
- [ ] Identify potential story angles
- [ ] Prepare role-specific questions

### Interview Logistics

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Duration | 30-60 minutes |
| Format | Video call (for rapport and recording) |
| Attendees | Champion + decision-maker if possible |
| Recording | Always request permission |
| Timing | Schedule during less busy periods |
| Advance Prep | Send questions in advance |

## Question Bank

### Opening Questions (Build Rapport)

- Tell me about your role and responsibilities
- How did you first hear about [our solution]?
- What was your initial impression?
- Walk me through your typical day before and after [solution]

### Challenge Questions (Establish Stakes)

- What problems were you trying to solve?
- How was this challenge impacting your business?
- What would happen if you didn't solve this problem?
- What had you tried before that didn't work?
- How long had you been dealing with this issue?
- Who else was affected by this problem?

### Decision Questions (Show Selection)

- What other solutions did you consider?
- Why did you choose [our solution] over alternatives?
- What specific features or capabilities were most important?
- Who was involved in the decision?
- What concerns did you have before purchasing?
- How did you justify the investment internally?

### Implementation Questions (Tell the Journey)

- Walk me through the implementation process
- How long did it take to get up and running?
- Were there any challenges during implementation?
- How was the support during onboarding?
- What surprised you during the process?
- How did your team adapt to the new solution?

### Results Questions (Get the Proof)

- What results have you seen since implementation?
- Can you quantify the improvement in [specific metric]?
- How quickly did you see results?
- What unexpected benefits have you experienced?
- How has this impacted other areas of your business?
- What would have happened without this solution?

### Testimonial Questions (Get Quotable Content)

- How would you describe [our solution] to a colleague?
- What would you say to someone considering [our solution]?
- What's the one thing that stands out most about working with us?
- Would you recommend us? What would you tell them?
- If you could only say one thing about your experience, what would it be?

## Interview Techniques

### Building Rapport

| Technique | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Start easy | Begin with conversational questions |
| Show interest | Reference their specific achievements |
| Active listening | Use verbal acknowledgments |
| Follow emotions | Dig into strong reactions |

### Extracting Stories

| Prompt | Purpose |
|--------|--------|
| "Tell me about a time when..." | Get specific examples |
| "Walk me through..." | Get step-by-step detail |
| "What happened next?" | Continue the narrative |
| "How did that make you feel?" | Get emotional content |

### Getting Specifics

| Technique | Example |
|-----------|--------|
| Ask for numbers | "Can you put a percentage on that?" |
| Request timeframes | "How long did that take?" |
| Seek comparisons | "How does that compare to before?" |
| Follow vague answers | "Can you be more specific?" |

### Getting Metrics

**When they say vague things like "significant improvement":**

- "If you had to estimate, what percentage would you say?"
- "Roughly how many hours per week does that save?"
- "What was it before versus now?"
- "How would you measure that improvement?"
- "What number would your boss want to hear?"

## Recording and Documentation

### Recording Best Practices

1. **Always request permission** before recording
2. Use reliable software (Zoom, Otter.ai)
3. Take written notes as backup
4. Test recording before starting
5. Save recording immediately after

### Note-Taking During Interview

- Mark timestamps for key moments
- Highlight potential quotes immediately
- Note emotional reactions
- Flag statistics and metrics
- Capture exact wording when possible

### Post-Interview Processing

1. Transcribe within 24-48 hours
2. Highlight best quotes
3. Extract all metrics and statistics
4. Identify story arc
5. Note any follow-up questions needed
6. Draft outline while memory is fresh

## Handling Common Situations

### Reluctant Interviewee

**Signs**: Short answers, hesitation, minimal detail

**Solutions**:
- Ask more open-ended questions
- Share examples from other case studies
- Emphasize their expertise
- Make questions more specific

### Vague Answers

**Signs**: "It was good," "Much better," "Significant improvement"

**Solutions**:
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "If you had to put a number on it..."
- "Walk me through what that looks like in practice"

### Off-Topic Tangents

**Solutions**:
- "That's interesting—let me note that. Coming back to..."
- "I want to make sure we cover [topic]—can you tell me about..."
- Allow brief tangents (may reveal valuable insights)

### Multiple Interviewees

**Approach**:
- Direct questions to specific people
- Ensure everyone contributes
- Note who said what for attribution
- Ask for different perspectives
