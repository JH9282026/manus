# Interview Process

Guide to conducting customer interviews for case studies.

---

## Interview Preparation

### Pre-Interview Research
- Review customer relationship history
- Understand product usage and features
- Gather available metrics and data
- Know the customer's industry context
- Identify key stakeholders

### Scheduling
- 45-60 minutes ideal
- Video call for rapport (if possible)
- Include decision-maker and user
- Send topics in advance

### Materials to Prepare
- Interview guide with questions
- Recording consent form
- Background information sheet
- Note-taking template

---

## Interview Questions

### Background Questions
- "Tell me about your role and [Company]."
- "What was happening in the business when you started looking for a solution?"
- "Who else was involved in the decision?"

### Challenge Questions
- "What was the main problem you were trying to solve?"
- "How was this impacting your day-to-day work?"
- "What was the cost of not solving this problem?"
- "What had you tried before?"

### Solution Questions
- "What made you choose [Product]?"
- "Walk me through the implementation process."
- "Which features do you use most? Why?"
- "How did the team adapt to the new system?"

### Results Questions
- "What changed after implementing [Product]?"
- "Can you share any specific metrics or numbers?"
- "What surprised you about the results?"
- "How has this affected other areas of the business?"

### Quote-Worthy Questions
- "What would you say to someone considering [Product]?"
- "How would you describe the impact in one sentence?"
- "What's the biggest difference between before and after?"

---

## Interview Techniques

### Building Rapport
- Start with easy, open questions
- Show genuine interest
- Use active listening
- Acknowledge their expertise

### Probing for Details
- "Tell me more about that."
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "What do you mean by [term]?"
- "How did that make you feel?"

### Getting Numbers
- "Do you have any metrics on that?"
- "Roughly, what percentage improvement?"
- "How much time does that save per week?"
- "What was it before vs. after?"

### Capturing Quotes
- Note powerful phrases verbatim
- Ask permission to quote
- Read back for accuracy
- Clarify attribution

---

## Post-Interview Process

### Transcription
- Transcribe recording within 24 hours
- Highlight key quotes
- Note timestamp for important moments
- Flag follow-up questions

### Follow-Up
- Thank you email same day
- Send draft for review
- Clarify any questions
- Confirm quote approval

### Data Gathering
- Request specific metrics
- Ask for supporting documentation
- Get logo and photo permissions
- Confirm approval process

---

## Approval Process

### Review Stages
1. Internal draft review
2. Customer contact review
3. Customer legal/PR review
4. Final approval and sign-off

### Common Concerns
| Concern | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Competitive sensitivity | Remove specific numbers, use percentages |
| Quote attribution | Offer anonymity options |
| Technical accuracy | Fact-check with customer |
| Legal compliance | Clear with customer's legal team |

### Approval Timeline
- Allow 2-3 weeks for full approval
- Build into project timeline
- Have backup cases in pipeline
