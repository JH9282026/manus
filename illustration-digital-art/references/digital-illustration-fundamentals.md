# Digital Illustration Fundamentals

## Overview

Digital illustration combines traditional art principles with digital tools. Understanding fundamentals—composition, color theory, lighting, and perspective—is essential regardless of style or medium.

---

## Composition Principles

**Rule of Thirds**: Divide canvas into 3×3 grid. Place focal points at intersections for dynamic composition.

**Golden Ratio**: Use 1:1.618 ratio for naturally pleasing proportions. Found throughout nature and classical art.

**Leading Lines**: Use lines to guide viewer's eye toward focal point. Roads, rivers, arms, gaze direction.

**Framing**: Use elements to frame subject. Windows, doorways, trees create natural frames.

**Balance**: Symmetrical balance feels formal and stable. Asymmetrical balance feels dynamic and interesting.

**Negative Space**: Empty space is as important as filled space. Gives composition room to breathe.

---

## Color Theory for Illustration

**Color Wheel Relationships**:
- Complementary: Opposite colors (red/green, blue/orange). High contrast, vibrant.
- Analogous: Adjacent colors (blue, blue-green, green). Harmonious, cohesive.
- Triadic: Three evenly spaced colors. Balanced, vibrant.
- Split-Complementary: Base color plus two adjacent to complement. Softer than complementary.

**Color Temperature**: Warm colors (red, orange, yellow) advance and energize. Cool colors (blue, green, purple) recede and calm.

**Value**: Lightness/darkness of color. More important than hue for readability and depth.

**Saturation**: Intensity of color. High saturation is vibrant, low saturation is muted. Use variety for interest.

## Lighting and Shading

**Light Sources**: Establish clear light direction. Consistent lighting creates believable scenes.

**Core Shadow**: Darkest part of shadow on object itself. Opposite the light source.

**Cast Shadow**: Shadow object casts on other surfaces. Shape reveals object form and light direction.

**Reflected Light**: Light bouncing from nearby surfaces into shadows. Prevents shadows from going pure black.

**Highlights**: Brightest points where light hits directly. Usually small and intense.

**Ambient Occlusion**: Subtle darkening where surfaces meet. Adds depth and grounding.

**Rim Lighting**: Light from behind creates bright edge. Separates subject from background.

---

## Perspective Basics

**One-Point Perspective**: Single vanishing point. Used for frontal views, roads, hallways.

**Two-Point Perspective**: Two vanishing points. Used for corner views of buildings, rooms.

**Three-Point Perspective**: Three vanishing points. Used for extreme angles, looking up or down.

**Atmospheric Perspective**: Distant objects are lighter, less detailed, cooler in color. Creates depth.

**Foreshortening**: Objects appear compressed when viewed at angle. Arms reaching toward viewer, etc.

**Horizon Line**: Eye level of viewer. Objects above horizon show bottom, objects below show top.

---

## Digital Tools and Techniques

**Layers**: Separate elements on different layers. Enables non-destructive editing and easy adjustments.

**Blending Modes**: Multiply for shadows, Screen for highlights, Overlay for texture. Experiment with modes.

**Clipping Masks**: Constrain painting to layer below. Essential for shading without going outside lines.

**Adjustment Layers**: Non-destructive color and tone adjustments. Hue/Saturation, Curves, Color Balance.

**Brushes**: Custom brushes for texture and efficiency. Build library of go-to brushes.

**Shortcuts**: Learn keyboard shortcuts for common tools. Dramatically speeds up workflow.

---

## Workflow Best Practices

**Start with Thumbnails**: Quick small sketches to explore composition. Cheap to iterate.

**Rough Sketch**: Establish proportions and placement. Don't worry about details yet.

**Clean Line Art**: Refine sketch into clean lines. Use separate layer from sketch.

**Block in Colors**: Fill large areas with flat color. Establish color palette.

**Add Shading**: Build up shadows and highlights. Work from dark to light.

**Details and Polish**: Add final details, textures, and refinements. Don't start here.

**Save Versions**: Save major milestones. Enables backtracking if you go wrong direction.
