# Porter's Five Forces - Automotive Industry

Comprehensive industry structure analysis.

---

## Competitive Rivalry (Very High)

### Factors

- Multiple global competitors
- High fixed costs drive volume pressure
- EV transition creating disruption
- Overcapacity in mature markets
- Brand differentiation eroding

### Implications

- Price pressure on commoditized segments
- Innovation race to differentiate
- Consolidation opportunities
- Partnership necessity for EV/autonomy

---

## Threat of New Entrants (Moderate-High)

### New Entrant Types

**EV Startups**:
- Tesla (established)
- Rivian, Lucid (scaling)
- Chinese EV brands

**Tech Companies**:
- Apple (potential)
- Google/Waymo (autonomy)
- Sony

**Chinese OEMs**:
- BYD
- NIO
- XPeng

### Barriers to Entry

| Barrier | Level | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Capital requirements | High | But EV lowers manufacturing complexity |
| Brand establishment | High | Customer trust takes decades |
| Distribution network | High | Dealer networks costly |
| Technology | Medium | EV reduces engine complexity |
| Regulatory compliance | High | Safety, emissions standards |

---

## Buyer Power (Moderate-High)

### Factors Increasing Power

- Information availability (online research)
- Price transparency
- Declining brand loyalty
- Alternative mobility options
- Subscription/lease flexibility

### Factors Limiting Power

- Emotional purchase decisions
- Brand prestige (luxury segment)
- High switching costs (if financed)
- Limited local dealer options

---

## Supplier Power (High)

### Critical Supply Dependencies

**Battery Supply Chain**:
- Limited lithium, cobalt supply
- Few major cell manufacturers
- Vertical integration attempts

**Semiconductor Shortage**:
- Supply chain disruption impact
- Single-source dependencies
- Chip complexity increasing

**Key Suppliers**:
- Tier 1 suppliers consolidated
- Technology suppliers (screens, sensors)
- Raw material concentration

---

## Threat of Substitutes (Growing)

### Substitute Categories

**Mobility Services**:
- Ride-hailing (Uber, Lyft)
- Car-sharing services
- Subscription services

**Alternative Transport**:
- Public transit (urban)
- E-bikes, scooters
- Remote work reducing commutes

**Future Threats**:
- Autonomous ride services
- Mobility-as-a-Service platforms

### Market Impact

- Urban markets most affected
- Young consumers delaying ownership
- Total miles driven still growing
- Suburban/rural less affected
