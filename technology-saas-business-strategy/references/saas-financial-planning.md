# SaaS Financial Planning and Forecasting

## Revenue Forecasting

### Bottom-Up Approach
1. Start with current MRR/ARR
2. Add new customer acquisition projections
3. Add expansion revenue estimates
4. Subtract churn and contraction
5. Calculate net new MRR/ARR

### Top-Down Approach
1. Estimate total addressable market (TAM)
2. Project market share
3. Calculate expected revenue
4. Validate against capacity and resources

## Cash Flow Management

### The Cash Flow Trough
- **Challenge**: Upfront CAC, revenue recovered over time
- **Impact**: Negative cash flow during growth
- **Mitigation**: 
  - Annual contracts with upfront payment
  - Efficient CAC payback (<12 months)
  - Adequate funding runway

### Cash Flow Projections
- Monthly cash flow forecasts
- Scenario planning (best/base/worst case)
- Runway calculation
- Burn rate monitoring

## Unit Economics

### Key Calculations
- **LTV**: Customer lifetime value
- **CAC**: Customer acquisition cost
- **LTV:CAC Ratio**: Target 3:1 or higher
- **CAC Payback**: Target <12 months
- **Gross Margin**: Target 70%+

### Break-Even Analysis
- Fixed costs + Variable costs
- Revenue required to break even
- Timeline to profitability

## Fundraising and Valuation

### Valuation Multiples
- **ARR Multiple**: Typical 5-15x ARR for growth SaaS
- **Factors**: Growth rate, NRR, margins, market size

### Fundraising Stages
- **Seed**: $500K-2M, product development
- **Series A**: $2-15M, product-market fit and scaling
- **Series B+**: $15M+, growth and expansion

### Metrics Investors Care About
- ARR and growth rate
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- LTV:CAC ratio
- Rule of 40
- Gross margin
- Burn rate and runway

## Financial Planning Best Practices

1. **Conservative Assumptions**: Better to exceed than miss
2. **Scenario Planning**: Model multiple scenarios
3. **Regular Updates**: Update forecasts monthly/quarterly
4. **Metric Tracking**: Monitor key metrics continuously
5. **Alignment**: Ensure finance, sales, and product aligned
