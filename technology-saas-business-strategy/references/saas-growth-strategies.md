# SaaS Growth Strategies

## Product-Led Growth (PLG)

### Definition
Product itself drives acquisition, conversion, and expansion.

### Key Elements
- **Free Trial or Freemium**: Low-friction entry
- **Self-Service Onboarding**: Users can start without sales
- **Time-to-Value**: Quick wins and "aha moments"
- **Viral Loops**: Built-in sharing and collaboration
- **In-Product Upsells**: Upgrade prompts within product

### Examples
- Slack, Dropbox, Zoom, Notion, Figma

## Sales-Led Growth

### Characteristics
- Outbound sales and marketing
- High-touch sales process
- Enterprise focus
- Longer sales cycles

### When to Use
- Complex products requiring explanation
- High ACV (>$10K)
- Enterprise customers
- Customization needed

## Customer Success and Expansion

### Strategies
- **Onboarding Excellence**: Ensure customers achieve value quickly
- **Proactive Support**: Anticipate and address issues
- **Upselling**: Move customers to higher tiers
- **Cross-Selling**: Sell additional products
- **Usage-Based Expansion**: Revenue grows with usage

### Metrics
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- Expansion MRR
- Customer Health Scores

## Channel Strategies

### Direct Sales
- Inside sales teams
- Field sales for enterprise
- Account-based marketing

### Partner Channels
- Resellers and distributors
- System integrators
- Technology partners
- Affiliate programs

### Digital Marketing
- Content marketing and SEO
- Paid advertising (Google, LinkedIn, Facebook)
- Email marketing
- Social media

## International Expansion

### Considerations
- Localization (language, currency, compliance)
- Local payment methods
- Regional data centers
- Local partnerships
- Cultural adaptation

## Vertical SaaS Strategy

### Benefits
- Higher ARPU
- Lower churn
- Embedded workflows
- Regulatory compliance built-in

### Examples
- Veeva (pharma), Toast (restaurants), Procore (construction)
