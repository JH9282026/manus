# SaaS Pricing Models and Strategies

## Core Pricing Models

### 1. Subscription Pricing
- **Flat-Rate**: Single price for all features
- **Tiered**: Multiple plans with increasing features/limits
- **Per-User**: Price scales with number of users
- **Per-Feature**: Pay for specific features needed

### 2. Usage-Based Pricing
- Charge based on consumption (API calls, storage, transactions)
- Aligns cost with value delivered
- Examples: AWS, Twilio, Stripe

### 3. Freemium
- Free tier with limited features
- Paid tiers for advanced features
- Conversion rate typically 2-5%

### 4. Hybrid Models
- Combination of subscription + usage
- Base fee + overage charges
- Examples: Slack, Snowflake

## Pricing Strategy Best Practices

1. **Value-Based Pricing**: Price based on value delivered, not cost
2. **Simple and Transparent**: Easy to understand and predict
3. **Room to Grow**: Allow customers to expand within pricing model
4. **Competitive Positioning**: Align with market expectations
5. **Test and Iterate**: Continuously optimize pricing

## Key Considerations

- **Customer Segmentation**: Different pricing for SMB, mid-market, enterprise
- **Packaging**: Bundle features strategically
- **Discounting**: Annual discounts to improve cash flow
- **Expansion Revenue**: Enable upsells and cross-sells
