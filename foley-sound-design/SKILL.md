# Foley Sound Design

Professional foley sound design techniques.

## Overview

Foley sound design involves the reproduction of everyday sound effects in post-production to enhance audio quality of films, videos, and other media. Named after sound effects artist Jack Foley, this technique uses live performance with props and specialized studios to create realistic and immersive sounds that complement or replace on-set recordings.

This skill covers the three main categories of foley (feet, moves, specifics), creative prop techniques, recording methods, and integration with broader sound design workflows for film, television, games, and animation.

## Core Concepts

### Foley Categories

**Feet (Footsteps)** — Creating footstep sounds using various shoes and specialized surfaces (Foley Pits) like marble, gravel, wood to match character movement and environment

**Moves (Clothing/Body)** — Subtle sounds of fabric rustling and body movement; achieved by rubbing similar materials near microphone

**Specifics (Object Interactions)** — All other sounds: doors, glass breaking, doorbells, prop handling, exaggerated sounds for animation

### Creative Foley Techniques

**Snow Crunching** — Corn starch in leather pouch

**Bird Wings** — Gloves flapping

**Breaking Bones** — Celery, frozen romaine lettuce

**Horse Hooves** — Coconut shells

**Squishing Noises** — Gelatin and hand soap

**Head Injuries** — Shattering chicken carcasses with baseball bats

**Blood Dripping** — Soaking washcloth, striking individual fingers on it

### Foley Stage Equipment

**Props Collection** — Diverse objects and materials for sound creation

**Foley Pits** — Various surfaces (marble, gravel, wood, sand) for footsteps

**Microphones** — Close miking for detail, mid/stereo for air and perspective

**Recording Equipment** — High-quality recorders, preamps, monitoring

## Professional Workflows

### Foley Process

1. **Spotting and Notating** — Watch film, identify every sound needed, note timing
2. **Gathering Props** — Collect objects and materials for desired sounds
3. **Performance and Recording** — Perform sounds live in sync with on-screen action
4. **Creative Problem-Solving** — Develop ingenious methods for recreating sounds
5. **Multiple Takes** — Record several versions for best performance
6. **Post-Recording Integration** — Edit, name, conform tracks; balance with dialogue and music

### Microphone Techniques

**Close Miking** — Captures detail and nuance

**Mid/Stereo Options** — Provides air and perspective

**Moving Microphones** — Follow action to match perspective shifts

### Collaboration with Sound Design

Foley artists work closely with sound designers and re-recording mixers to create cohesive audio landscape, with sound designers leading overall sonic identity and integrating foley into final mix.
