
### Universal Design for Video

Universal design aims to create content usable by all people without need for adaptation. For video, this means building accessibility into the production process rather than adding it afterwards.

### Inclusive Production Planning

**Pre-Production Considerations**:
- Plan for captions from the start
- Script with audio descriptions in mind
- Consider visual accessibility in design
- Plan for multiple language versions
- Budget for accessibility

**Production Techniques**:
- Clear, slow speech patterns
- Adequate pauses for descriptions
- On-screen text read aloud
- Accessible graphics and animations
- Good lighting and contrast

**Post-Production**:
- Professional captioning
- Audio description production
- Accessible player selection
- Quality assurance testing
- Multiple format delivery

### Designing for Diverse Audiences

**Visual Design Accessibility**:
- High contrast text and graphics
- Legible font choices and sizes
- Color-blind safe palettes
- No reliance on color alone for information
- Clear visual hierarchy

**Audio Accessibility**:
- Clear dialogue recording
- Balanced audio mix
- Described visual information
- Accessible music and sound effects
- Volume consistency

**Cognitive Accessibility**:
- Clear, simple language
- Logical content structure
- Consistent navigation
- Adequate processing time
- Supportive cues and context
