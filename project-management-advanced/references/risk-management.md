# Risk Management - Comprehensive Framework

Advanced techniques for identifying, assessing, and managing project risks.

## Risk Identification

### Risk Categories

**Technical Risks:**
- Technology complexity or immaturity
- Integration challenges
- Performance or scalability issues
- Technical skill gaps

**Schedule Risks:**
- Unrealistic estimates
- Dependencies on other projects
- Resource availability
- Scope creep

**Budget Risks:**
- Cost overruns
- Scope changes
- Economic factors
- Vendor pricing changes

**Resource Risks:**
- Key person dependencies
- Skill gaps
- Turnover
- Competing priorities

**Stakeholder Risks:**
- Resistance to change
- Changing requirements
- Lack of engagement
- Political issues

**External Risks:**
- Regulatory changes
- Market shifts
- Vendor issues
- Natural disasters

### Identification Techniques

**Brainstorming:**
- Facilitated session with team and stakeholders
- Generate comprehensive list without judgment
- Categorize and consolidate

**Expert Interviews:**
- One-on-one with subject matter experts
- Leverage experience from similar projects
- Identify risks specific to domain

**SWOT Analysis:**
- Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
- Internal and external perspective
- Strategic risk identification

**Assumption Analysis:**
- Review all project assumptions
- Assess validity and impact if wrong
- Identify risks from invalid assumptions

**Checklist:**
- Use standard risk checklist
- Customize for project type and industry
- Ensure comprehensive coverage

## Risk Assessment

### Qualitative Assessment

**Probability Scale:**
- Very Low (< 10%)
- Low (10-30%)
- Medium (30-50%)
- High (50-70%)
- Very High (> 70%)

**Impact Scale:**
- Very Low: Minimal impact
- Low: Minor impact, easily absorbed
- Medium: Moderate impact, requires management
- High: Significant impact, major objectives at risk
- Very High: Catastrophic, project failure possible

**Risk Score:**
Risk Score = Probability × Impact

**Risk Matrix:**

| Probability/Impact | Very Low | Low | Medium | High | Very High |
|-------------------|----------|-----|--------|------|-----------|
| **Very High** | Medium | High | High | Critical | Critical |
| **High** | Low | Medium | High | High | Critical |
| **Medium** | Low | Low | Medium | High | High |
| **Low** | Very Low | Low | Low | Medium | High |
| **Very Low** | Very Low | Very Low | Low | Low | Medium |

### Quantitative Assessment

**Expected Monetary Value (EMV):**
EMV = Probability × Impact (in dollars)

**Monte Carlo Simulation:**
- Model range of possible outcomes
- Run thousands of simulations
- Determine probability distribution
- Identify confidence levels for schedule and budget

**Decision Tree Analysis:**
- Map decision points and outcomes
- Calculate expected value of each path
- Optimize decision-making

## Risk Response Strategies

### For Threats (Negative Risks)

**Avoid:**
- Eliminate risk by changing approach
- Example: Use proven technology instead of cutting-edge

**Mitigate:**
- Reduce probability or impact
- Example: Add testing to reduce defect risk

**Transfer:**
- Shift risk to third party
- Example: Insurance, fixed-price contract

**Accept:**
- Acknowledge and prepare contingency
- Active: Develop contingency plan
- Passive: Deal with if it occurs

### For Opportunities (Positive Risks)

**Exploit:**
- Ensure opportunity is realized
- Example: Assign best resources

**Enhance:**
- Increase probability or impact
- Example: Add resources to accelerate

**Share:**
- Partner to capture opportunity
- Example: Joint venture

**Accept:**
- Take advantage if it occurs

## Risk Monitoring and Control

### Risk Register

**Components:**
- Risk ID and description
- Category
- Probability and impact assessment
- Risk score and priority
- Response strategy
- Action plan and owner
- Status and updates
- Residual risk after response

### Monitoring Activities

**Regular Risk Reviews:**
- Weekly for high-priority risks
- Monthly for all risks
- Ad-hoc when triggers occur

**Risk Triggers:**
- Early warning signs that risk is materializing
- Monitor triggers proactively
- Execute response when triggered

**Risk Audits:**
- Periodic review of risk management effectiveness
- Assess quality of risk identification and response
- Identify improvements to process

### Risk Reporting

**Status Reports:**
- Top 5-10 risks
- Changes in risk assessment
- Risks requiring escalation
- Effectiveness of responses

**Risk Dashboard:**
- Risk distribution by category
- Risk trends over time
- Open vs. closed risks
- Risk exposure (sum of EMV)

## Advanced Risk Techniques

### Risk Breakdown Structure (RBS)

Hierarchical decomposition of risks by category:
- Level 1: Major categories (Technical, Schedule, Budget, etc.)
- Level 2: Subcategories
- Level 3: Specific risks

### Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)

**Process:**
1. Identify potential failure modes
2. Assess severity, occurrence, detection
3. Calculate Risk Priority Number (RPN) = Severity × Occurrence × Detection
4. Prioritize high RPN items
5. Develop mitigation actions

### Sensitivity Analysis

- Identify which risks have greatest impact on objectives
- Focus mitigation efforts on high-sensitivity risks
- Understand interdependencies

### Reserve Analysis

**Contingency Reserve:**
- For known risks (identified in risk register)
- Calculated based on risk assessment
- Controlled by project manager

**Management Reserve:**
- For unknown risks (unknown unknowns)
- Typically 5-10% of budget
- Controlled by senior management

## Risk Management Best Practices

**Do's:**
- Identify risks early and continuously
- Involve team and stakeholders in identification
- Assess risks objectively with data
- Develop specific, actionable response plans
- Assign clear ownership for each risk
- Monitor risks regularly and proactively
- Update risk assessments as project progresses
- Communicate risks transparently

**Don'ts:**
- Don't ignore low-probability, high-impact risks
- Don't assume risks will go away on their own
- Don't wait for risks to become issues
- Don't hide risks from stakeholders
- Don't over-focus on threats while ignoring opportunities
- Don't treat risk management as one-time activity
