# Industry-Specific Regulations

## Introduction

While many regulatory principles apply across industries, each sector faces unique regulatory frameworks tailored to its specific risks, market dynamics, and public policy objectives. Understanding industry-specific regulations is essential for effective compliance, strategic planning, and competitive positioning.

## Financial Services

### Banking and Depository Institutions

**Prudential Regulation**

*Capital Requirements*
- **Basel III Framework**: International standards for bank capital adequacy
  - Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) minimum: 4.5% of risk-weighted assets
  - Total capital minimum: 8% of risk-weighted assets
  - Capital conservation buffer: 2.5% additional requirement
  - Countercyclical buffer: 0-2.5% based on credit conditions
- **Stress Testing**: Annual Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review (CCAR)
- **Leverage Ratio**: Supplementary leverage ratio of 3% for large banks

*Liquidity Requirements*
- **Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)**: High-quality liquid assets to cover 30-day stress
- **Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)**: Stable funding for assets and activities
- **Resolution Planning**: "Living wills" for orderly resolution in failure

**Consumer Protection**

*Truth in Lending Act (TILA)*
- Disclosure of credit terms and costs (APR, fees, payment schedule)
- Right of rescission for certain transactions
- Ability-to-repay requirements for mortgages
- Restrictions on high-cost mortgages

*Fair Lending Laws*
- **Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)**: Prohibits discrimination in credit
- **Fair Housing Act**: Prohibits discrimination in housing-related credit
- **Community Reinvestment Act (CRA)**: Encourages lending in underserved areas
- **Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA)**: Requires reporting of lending data

*Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) Rules*
- Mortgage servicing standards
- Prepaid card disclosures
- Payday lending restrictions
- Debt collection practices

**Anti-Money Laundering (AML)**

*Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) Requirements*
- Customer Identification Program (CIP)
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
- Suspicious Activity Reporting (SARs)
- Currency Transaction Reporting (CTRs)
- AML program requirements (policies, officer, training, audit)

*OFAC Sanctions Compliance*
- Screening against Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list
- Blocking and rejecting prohibited transactions
- Reporting to Office of Foreign Assets Control
- Country and sectoral sanctions programs

### Securities and Investment Management

**Securities Regulation**

*Securities Act of 1933*
- Registration of securities offerings
- Prospectus disclosure requirements
- Exemptions for private placements (Regulation D)
- Liability for misstatements and omissions

*Securities Exchange Act of 1934*
- Registration of securities exchanges and broker-dealers
- Periodic reporting (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K)
- Proxy solicitation rules
- Insider trading prohibitions
- Market manipulation prohibitions

*Investment Company Act of 1940*
- Registration and regulation of mutual funds
- Governance and board requirements
- Restrictions on transactions with affiliates
- Custody and valuation requirements

*Investment Advisers Act of 1940*
- Registration of investment advisers
- Fiduciary duty to clients
- Disclosure of conflicts of interest
- Custody and compliance program requirements

**Market Regulation**

*Market Structure Rules*
- Regulation National Market System (Reg NMS)
- Order protection and best execution
- Market access and risk controls (Reg SCI)
- Alternative trading systems (Reg ATS)

*Trading Practices*
- Short sale restrictions (Reg SHO)
- Margin requirements (Reg T)
- Pattern day trader rules
- High-frequency trading oversight

### Insurance

**Solvency Regulation**

*Risk-Based Capital (RBC)*
- Minimum capital requirements based on risk profile
- Asset risk, insurance risk, interest rate risk, business risk
- Regulatory action levels for undercapitalized insurers
- Own Risk and Solvency Assessment (ORSA)

*Reserves and Liabilities*
- Statutory accounting principles (SAP)
- Reserve adequacy requirements
- Actuarial certification and opinion
- Reinsurance credit and collateral requirements

**Product Regulation**

*Rate and Form Filing*
- Prior approval, file-and-use, or use-and-file systems
- Actuarial justification of rates
- Policy form approval and standardization
- Restrictions on unfair discrimination

*Consumer Protection*
- Unfair claims settlement practices acts
- Timely payment of claims
- Disclosure of policy terms and exclusions
- Replacement and suitability requirements

**Market Conduct**

*Producer Licensing*
- Agent and broker licensing requirements
- Continuing education requirements
- Appointment and termination reporting
- Background checks and fitness standards

*Sales Practices*
- Suitability and best interest standards
- Disclosure of commissions and conflicts
- Prohibition of rebating and inducements
- Advertising and marketing standards

## Healthcare and Life Sciences

### Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices

**Pre-Market Approval**

*Drug Approval Process (FDA)*
- **Investigational New Drug (IND)**: Application to begin clinical trials
- **Phase I-III Clinical Trials**: Safety and efficacy testing in humans
- **New Drug Application (NDA)**: Comprehensive data submission for approval
- **Biologics License Application (BLA)**: For biological products
- **Accelerated Approval**: For serious conditions with unmet needs

*Device Approval Process*
- **510(k) Premarket Notification**: Substantial equivalence to predicate device
- **Premarket Approval (PMA)**: For high-risk devices (Class III)
- **De Novo Classification**: For novel low-to-moderate risk devices
- **Breakthrough Device Designation**: Expedited review for innovative devices

**Manufacturing and Quality**

*Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)*
- Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) for drugs
- Quality Management System (QMS) for devices
- Facility registration and inspection
- Process validation and control
- Batch release and stability testing

*Supply Chain Integrity*
- Drug Supply Chain Security Act (DSCSA)
- Serialization and track-and-trace requirements
- Verification of product authenticity
- Quarantine and investigation of suspect products

**Post-Market Surveillance**

*Adverse Event Reporting*
- Mandatory reporting of serious adverse events
- Periodic safety update reports (PSURs)
- Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies (REMS)
- Post-market surveillance studies

*Product Recalls*
- Voluntary and mandatory recall authority
- Recall classification (Class I, II, III)
- Public notification requirements
- Corrective and preventive actions (CAPA)

### Healthcare Delivery

**Provider Regulation**

*Licensing and Credentialing*
- State medical board licensure
- Hospital credentialing and privileging
- Specialty board certification
- Continuing medical education (CME)

*Quality and Safety Standards*
- Joint Commission accreditation
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Conditions of Participation
- National Patient Safety Goals
- Quality reporting and value-based payment programs

**Patient Privacy and Security**

*Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)*
- **Privacy Rule**: Protects individually identifiable health information
- **Security Rule**: Safeguards for electronic protected health information (ePHI)
- **Breach Notification Rule**: Notification of breaches affecting 500+ individuals
- **Enforcement Rule**: Penalties and enforcement procedures

*HIPAA Requirements*
- Notice of privacy practices
- Patient rights (access, amendment, accounting of disclosures)
- Business associate agreements
- Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
- Risk analysis and management

**Fraud and Abuse**

*Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS)*
- Prohibits remuneration for referrals of federal healthcare program business
- Criminal penalties and civil monetary penalties
- Safe harbors for certain arrangements
- Disclosure and transparency requirements

*Stark Law (Physician Self-Referral)*
- Prohibits physician referrals for designated health services (DHS) to entities with which they have financial relationships
- Exceptions for certain arrangements
- Strict liability (no intent required)
- Refund and reporting obligations

*False Claims Act (FCA)*
- Prohibits submission of false claims for payment
- Qui tam provisions allowing whistleblower lawsuits
- Treble damages and penalties
- Corporate Integrity Agreements (CIAs)

## Technology and Telecommunications

### Data Protection and Privacy

**General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**

*Scope and Applicability*
- Applies to processing of personal data of EU residents
- Extraterritorial application to non-EU organizations
- Broad definition of personal data and processing
- Special categories of sensitive data

*Key Requirements*
- **Lawful Basis**: Consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests
- **Data Subject Rights**: Access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection
- **Privacy by Design and Default**: Embed privacy in systems and processes
- **Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)**: For high-risk processing
- **Data Breach Notification**: Within 72 hours to supervisory authority
- **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**: Required for certain organizations

*Accountability and Governance*
- Records of processing activities
- Data processing agreements with processors
- Codes of conduct and certification mechanisms
- Cooperation with supervisory authorities
- Penalties up to €20 million or 4% of global revenue

**California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA)**

*Consumer Rights*
- Right to know what personal information is collected
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt-out of sale of personal information
- Right to non-discrimination for exercising rights
- Right to correct inaccurate information (CPRA)
- Right to limit use of sensitive personal information (CPRA)

*Business Obligations*
- Privacy policy disclosure requirements
- Mechanisms for submitting requests and opt-outs
- Verification of consumer requests
- Service provider and contractor agreements
- Data minimization and purpose limitation (CPRA)
- Risk assessments for high-risk processing (CPRA)

### Cybersecurity

**Sector-Specific Cybersecurity Regulations**

*Financial Services*
- **GLBA Safeguards Rule**: Information security program requirements
- **FFIEC Cybersecurity Assessment Tool**: Risk assessment framework
- **NYDFS Cybersecurity Regulation**: Comprehensive cybersecurity requirements for NY financial institutions
- **SEC Cybersecurity Rules**: Disclosure and governance requirements for public companies

*Critical Infrastructure*
- **NERC CIP Standards**: Cybersecurity for electric grid
- **TSA Security Directives**: Cybersecurity for pipelines and transportation
- **CISA Cyber Incident Reporting**: Reporting of significant cyber incidents

*Healthcare*
- **HIPAA Security Rule**: Safeguards for ePHI
- **FDA Cybersecurity Guidance**: Medical device cybersecurity
- **HHS 405(d) Program**: Cybersecurity practices for health industry

### Telecommunications

**Network Regulation**

*Common Carrier Obligations*
- Non-discrimination and reasonable rates
- Interconnection and unbundling requirements
- Universal service obligations and funding
- Emergency services (911/E911)

*Spectrum Management*
- Spectrum licensing and auctions
- Technical standards and interference protection
- Spectrum sharing and coordination
- International coordination (ITU)

**Content and Platform Regulation**

*Section 230 (Communications Decency Act)*
- Immunity for platforms for third-party content
- Good Samaritan provisions for content moderation
- Exceptions for federal criminal law and intellectual property
- Ongoing debates about reform and scope

*Net Neutrality*
- Prohibition of blocking, throttling, and paid prioritization (when in effect)
- Transparency requirements for network management
- Regulatory classification of broadband (Title I vs. Title II)
- State-level net neutrality laws

## Energy and Environment

### Environmental Protection

**Air Quality**

*Clean Air Act (CAA)*
- National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS)
- State Implementation Plans (SIPs)
- New Source Performance Standards (NSPS)
- Prevention of Significant Deterioration (PSD)
- Title V operating permits
- Hazardous Air Pollutants (HAPs) and Maximum Achievable Control Technology (MACT)

*Greenhouse Gas Regulation*
- EPA greenhouse gas reporting
- Greenhouse gas permitting for major sources
- Clean Power Plan and subsequent rules
- State and regional cap-and-trade programs (RGGI, California)

**Water Quality**

*Clean Water Act (CWA)*
- National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) permits
- Water quality standards and Total Maximum Daily Loads (TMDLs)
- Wetlands protection (Section 404 permits)
- Oil spill prevention and response
- Stormwater management

*Safe Drinking Water Act (SDWA)*
- Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs)
- Public water system monitoring and reporting
- Underground injection control (UIC)
- Source water protection

**Waste Management**

*Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA)*
- Hazardous waste identification and listing
- Cradle-to-grave tracking and manifesting
- Treatment, storage, and disposal facility (TSDF) permits
- Land disposal restrictions
- Underground storage tank (UST) regulation

*Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act (CERCLA/Superfund)*
- Liability for hazardous substance releases
- National Priorities List (NPL) of contaminated sites
- Remedial investigation and feasibility study (RI/FS)
- Potentially responsible party (PRP) liability
- Natural resource damage assessment

### Energy Regulation

**Electric Power**

*Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)*
- Wholesale electricity rates and transmission
- Electric reliability standards (delegated to NERC)
- Hydroelectric licensing
- Market oversight and manipulation prevention

*State Public Utility Commissions*
- Retail electricity rates and service quality
- Integrated resource planning
- Renewable portfolio standards (RPS)
- Distributed generation and net metering

**Oil and Gas**

*Upstream Regulation*
- Leasing of federal lands and offshore areas
- Drilling permits and environmental review
- Well construction and integrity standards
- Hydraulic fracturing disclosure (state level)
- Methane emissions reduction

*Midstream and Downstream*
- Pipeline safety (PHMSA regulations)
- Pipeline siting and eminent domain
- Refinery air quality and safety
- Fuel quality standards (RFS, CAFE)

## Transportation

### Aviation

**Safety Regulation (FAA)**

*Aircraft Certification*
- Type certification for aircraft design
- Production certification for manufacturing
- Airworthiness certification for individual aircraft
- Supplemental type certificates (STCs) for modifications

*Operational Regulation*
- Pilot certification and medical requirements
- Air carrier operating certificates (Part 121, 135)
- Maintenance and inspection requirements
- Flight operations and crew duty time limits

**Economic Regulation**

*Department of Transportation (DOT)*
- Consumer protection rules (tarmac delays, refunds, baggage)
- Airline competition and antitrust oversight
- International route authorities and bilateral agreements
- Accessibility requirements (Air Carrier Access Act)

### Automotive

**Vehicle Safety (NHTSA)**

*Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS)*
- Crashworthiness and occupant protection
- Lighting, braking, and steering systems
- Tires and wheels
- Defect reporting and recalls

*Autonomous Vehicles*
- Voluntary safety self-assessment
- State-level testing and deployment regulations
- Liability and insurance frameworks
- Cybersecurity and data privacy

**Emissions and Fuel Economy (EPA/NHTSA)**

*Clean Air Act Vehicle Standards*
- Tailpipe emission standards (Tier 3)
- Greenhouse gas emission standards
- Zero-emission vehicle (ZEV) mandates (state level)
- Onboard diagnostics (OBD) requirements

*Corporate Average Fuel Economy (CAFE)*
- Fleet-average fuel economy standards
- Credit trading and banking
- Alternative fuel vehicle credits
- Penalties for non-compliance

## Conclusion

Industry-specific regulations reflect the unique characteristics, risks, and public policy objectives of each sector. Effective regulatory research and analysis requires deep understanding of these specialized frameworks, their evolution, and their practical application. Organizations must invest in industry expertise, maintain robust compliance programs, and engage proactively with regulators and industry stakeholders to navigate these complex regulatory landscapes successfully.
