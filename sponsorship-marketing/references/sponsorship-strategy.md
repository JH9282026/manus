## Strategic Planning Framework

### Sponsorship Objectives Hierarchy

**Tier 1: Business Objectives**
- Revenue growth targets
- Market share expansion
- Customer acquisition goals
- Brand equity building
- Competitive positioning

**Tier 2: Marketing Objectives**
- Brand awareness increase
- Consideration and preference
- Customer engagement
- Lead generation
- Content creation

**Tier 3: Sponsorship-Specific Objectives**
- Audience reach targets
- Engagement metrics
- Activation participation
- Media value generation
- Partnership relationship building

### SMART Goal Setting for Sponsorships

**Specific**: "Increase brand awareness among 18-34 year old males in key markets"
- Not: "Improve brand awareness"

**Measurable**: "Generate 500 million impressions and 15% aided brand recall lift"
- Not: "Get lots of exposure"

**Achievable**: Based on property reach and historical benchmarks
- Not: Unrealistic expectations disconnected from property capabilities

**Relevant**: Aligned with target audience and business priorities
- Not: Sponsoring properties that don't reach target customers

**Time-Bound**: "Within 12-month sponsorship period"
- Not: Open-ended without clear timeline

## Sponsorship Portfolio Strategy

### Portfolio Architecture

**Flagship Sponsorships (1-3 properties)**
- **Investment**: 40-50% of total budget
- **Duration**: 3-5+ year partnerships
- **Scope**: National or international reach
- **Purpose**: Broad awareness, brand building, market leadership
- **Examples**: Major sports leagues, premier festivals, broadcast properties

**Strategic Sponsorships (3-6 properties)**
- **Investment**: 30-40% of total budget
- **Duration**: 2-3 year partnerships
- **Scope**: Regional or category-specific
- **Purpose**: Targeted engagement, market penetration, competitive positioning
- **Examples**: Regional events, category-relevant properties, emerging platforms

**Tactical Sponsorships (5-10 properties)**
- **Investment**: 15-20% of total budget
- **Duration**: 1-2 year partnerships
- **Scope**: Local or niche audiences
- **Purpose**: Community connection, testing, flexibility
- **Examples**: Local events, grassroots initiatives, cause partnerships

**Innovation Fund (Reserve)**
- **Investment**: 5-10% of total budget
- **Duration**: Pilot programs, short-term tests
- **Scope**: Experimental and emerging
- **Purpose**: Learning, innovation, future opportunity identification
- **Examples**: New sports, digital platforms, unconventional partnerships

### Portfolio Balance Considerations

**Geographic Balance**
- National vs. regional vs. local coverage
- Key market prioritization
- Market development needs
- Distribution and sales alignment

**Seasonal Balance**
- Year-round presence vs. seasonal concentration
- Peak selling season alignment
- Competitive activity timing
- Budget and resource capacity

**Category Diversity**
- Sports vs. entertainment vs. culture vs. cause
- Risk mitigation through diversification
- Audience reach across interests
- Brand positioning breadth

**Audience Lifecycle**
- Awareness-building properties
- Consideration-driving properties
- Conversion-focused properties
- Loyalty and retention properties

## Property Selection and Evaluation

### Comprehensive Evaluation Scorecard

**Audience Fit (30 points)**
- Target demographic match (10 points)
- Psychographic alignment (8 points)
- Audience size and quality (7 points)
- Geographic relevance (5 points)

**Brand Alignment (25 points)**
- Values and positioning fit (10 points)
- Category relevance (8 points)
- Brand safety and reputation (7 points)

**Activation Potential (25 points)**
- Access and integration opportunities (10 points)
- Content creation potential (8 points)
- Exclusivity and protection (7 points)

**Commercial Viability (20 points)**
- Cost efficiency and ROI potential (10 points)
- Measurability and tracking (5 points)
- Growth trajectory (5 points)

**Total Score**: 100 points
- **90-100**: Exceptional fit, priority pursuit
- **75-89**: Strong fit, serious consideration
- **60-74**: Moderate fit, conditional interest
- **Below 60**: Poor fit, pass

### Due Diligence Checklist

**Property Performance**
- Historical attendance/viewership trends
- Media coverage and reach
- Social media following and engagement
- Financial stability and management
- Growth plans and investment

**Sponsorship Program**
- Current sponsor roster and satisfaction
- Available inventory and exclusivity
- Activation support and resources
- Measurement and reporting capabilities
- Pricing and market comparables

**Risk Assessment**
- Reputational risks and controversies
- Financial viability and continuity
- Competitive landscape and clutter
- Regulatory and legal considerations
- Force majeure and contingency plans

## Category-Specific Strategies

### Sports Sponsorship Strategy

**League vs. Team vs. Athlete**
- **League**: Broad reach, national scale, higher cost, less flexibility
- **Team**: Regional focus, passionate fanbase, moderate cost, local relevance
- **Athlete**: Personal connection, social reach, lower cost, higher risk

**Sport Selection Criteria**
- Audience demographics and size
- Growth trajectory and momentum
- Media coverage and visibility
- Activation opportunities
- Competitive landscape

**Emerging Opportunities**
- Women's sports (WNBA, NWSL, women's soccer)
- Esports and gaming
- Action and adventure sports
- Pickleball and emerging sports
- College athletics (NIL era)

**Music Partnerships**
- Festival sponsorships for mass reach
- Tour sponsorships for sustained engagement
- Venue naming rights for long-term presence
- Artist partnerships for authentic connection

**Arts and Culture**

- Theater and performing arts
- Film festivals and premieres

**Lifestyle and Experiential**

- Fashion and design events
- Travel and adventure experiences

### Cause and Community Sponsorship

**Community Sponsorship**

## Owned IP and Platform Development

### Creating Proprietary Properties

**Benefits of Owned IP**
- Complete control over brand integration
- Ownership of content and data
- Scalability and evolution flexibility
- Long-term asset value creation
- No competitor presence

**Owned Property Models**
- Branded events and experiences
- Content series and platforms
- Awards and recognition programs
- Competitions and challenges
- Community platforms and networks

**Hybrid Sponsorship + Owned IP**
- Sponsor existing property for reach
- Create owned sub-property or activation
- Leverage sponsorship audience for owned IP
- Build long-term asset while benefiting from partnership

### Case Study: Red Bull's Owned IP Strategy

**Approach**
- Created proprietary events (Red Bull Air Race, Crashed Ice, Rampage)
- Owns content production and distribution
- Sponsors athletes and teams as brand ambassadors
- Built media house (Red Bull Media House)
- Combines owned IP with strategic sponsorships

**Results**
- Complete brand control and integration
- Massive content library and distribution
- Global reach without reliance on third parties
- Premium brand positioning
- Diversified revenue streams

## Budget Planning and Allocation

### Total Sponsorship Investment Formula

**Rights Fees**: Base cost for sponsorship assets
- Negotiated with property
- Typically 25-40% of total sponsorship investment

**Activation Budget**: Marketing and experiential execution
- Rule of thumb: 1.5-3x rights fees
- Minimum 1x for basic activation
- 2-3x for competitive, impactful activation

**Measurement and Analytics**: Tracking and reporting
- 5-10% of total investment
- Technology, research, and analysis

**Contingency**: Buffer for opportunities and issues
- 10% of total budget
- Flexibility for optimization and unexpected needs

**Example Budget Breakdown**
- Rights Fees: $1,000,000 (30%)
- Activation: $2,000,000 (60%)
- Measurement: $200,000 (6%)
- Contingency: $133,000 (4%)
- **Total**: $3,333,000

### Activation Budget Allocation

**Experiential (40%)**
- On-site activations and installations
- Hospitality and VIP experiences
- Product sampling and demonstrations
- Staffing and logistics

**Digital and Social (30%)**
- Content creation and production
- Social media advertising and promotion
- Influencer partnerships
- Digital platform integration

**Traditional Marketing (20%)**
- TV and broadcast integration
- Print and outdoor advertising
- PR and media relations
- Retail and point-of-sale

**Internal Engagement (10%)**
- Employee experiences and education
- Sales enablement and tools
- Internal communications
- Training and development

## Competitive Strategy

### Competitive Sponsorship Analysis

**Competitor Sponsorship Audit**
- Identify all competitor sponsorships
- Assess investment levels and commitment
- Evaluate activation quality and innovation
- Measure share of voice and impact
- Identify gaps and opportunities

**Competitive Response Strategies**

**Ambush Prevention**
- Secure category exclusivity
- Activate aggressively to own mindshare
- Monitor competitor activity closely
- Enforce exclusivity provisions
- Build strong property relationships

**Strategic Differentiation**
- Sponsor different but complementary properties
- Innovate in activation to stand out
- Focus on underserved audience segments
- Create owned IP to control narrative
- Build long-term partnerships vs. one-offs

**Offensive Tactics**
- Pursue competitor's expiring sponsorships
- Outbid on high-value opportunities
- Activate more effectively than competitors
- Create superior fan experiences
- Leverage data and insights for advantage

### Share of Voice Optimization

**Measuring Share of Voice**
- Sponsorship mentions vs. competitors
- Media coverage volume and quality
- Social media presence and engagement
- On-site visibility and prominence
- Fan awareness and recall

**Increasing Share of Voice**
- Invest in activation, not just rights
- Create newsworthy moments and content
- Leverage talent and influencers
- Maximize owned and earned media
- Integrate across all marketing channels

## Global vs. Local Sponsorship Strategy

### Global Sponsorship Approach

**Benefits**
- Consistent brand positioning worldwide
- Economies of scale in activation
- Simplified management and coordination
- Global properties (Olympics, World Cup, F1)

**Challenges**
- Less local relevance and customization
- Higher investment requirements
- Cultural differences and sensitivities
- Coordination complexity

**Best Practices**
- Global framework with local flexibility
- Centralized strategy, decentralized execution
- Cultural adaptation of activations
- Local market input and collaboration

### Local Sponsorship Approach

**Benefits**
- High local relevance and connection
- Community relationships and goodwill
- Flexibility and agility
- Lower investment thresholds

**Challenges**
- Fragmented brand presence
- Inefficiencies and duplication
- Inconsistent brand positioning
- Management complexity at scale

**Best Practices**
- Clear brand guidelines and guardrails
- Centralized approval for major deals
- Shared learnings and best practices
- Portfolio view across all markets

### Glocal Strategy (Hybrid)

**Approach**
- Global flagship sponsorships for brand building
- Local sponsorships for market relevance
- Consistent brand positioning with local activation
- Centralized strategy with local execution

**Example: Coca-Cola**
- Global: FIFA World Cup, Olympics
- Regional: Local football leagues, festivals
- Consistent brand message with local cultural adaptation
- Centralized measurement with local flexibility
