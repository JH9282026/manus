# Props and Materials Reference

This comprehensive reference catalogs Foley props, creative substitutions, and material selection strategies for creating convincing sound effects.

## Prop Selection Philosophy

### Sound Over Appearance

**Core Principle**: The prop doesn't need to look like the on-screen object; it needs to sound like it.

**Why Substitutes Work Better**:
- Real objects often don't sound "right" on camera
- Microphones hear differently than human ears
- Exaggeration enhances believability
- Practical considerations (safety, availability)

**Examples**:
- Coconut shells sound more like horse hooves than real hooves
- Corn starch sounds more like snow than real snow
- Leather gloves sound better for punches than actual fists

**Finding the Right Sound**:
- Experiment with multiple options
- Trust your ears, not your eyes
- Test with picture
- Get feedback from team

### Snow and Ice

**Snow Sounds**:

**Corn Starch**:
- In leather pouch: Squeeze for crunch
- In box: Press with thumbs for footsteps
- Most common snow substitute
- Adjustable consistency

**Potato Starch**:
- Alternative to corn starch
- Slightly different sound
- Experiment with both

**Salt**:
- Coarse salt in bag
- Crunchy texture
- Mix with corn starch

**Technique**:
- Vary pressure for different snow types
- Fresh snow: Light, crisp
- Packed snow: Denser, crunchier
- Wet snow: Heavier, wetter sound

**Ice Sounds**:
- Cellophane crinkled
- Plastic wrap
- Thin plastic sheets
- Cracking: Break thin plastic or ice cube trays

## Impacts and Violence

### Punches and Hits

**Body Punches**:

**Leather Gloves**:
- Slap together for punch sound
- Vary force for different impacts
- Most common method
- Clean, controllable

**Cabbage/Lettuce**:
- Hit or squeeze for body impact
- Wet, organic sound
- Messy but effective
- Frozen lettuce for bone breaks

**Chicken Carcass**:
- Hit with bat or fist
- Very realistic
- Extremely messy
- Use with caution

**Phone Books**:
- Slap together
- Hit with hand
- Dull thud
- Body blows

**Steak or Roast**:
- Slap or punch
- Wet impact
- Realistic
- Messy

**Wet Chamois/Towel**:
- Slap or hit
- Wet impact sound
- Less messy than meat
- Reusable

**Technique**:
- Vary force for different punch types
- Jab: Quick, light
- Hook: Heavier, more impact
- Uppercut: Upward motion sound
- Layer multiple elements

### Bone Breaks and Cracks

**Celery**:
- Snap for bone break
- Clean, crisp sound
- Easy to control
- Most common method

**Carrots**:
- Crunch or snap
- Harder than celery
- Different tonal quality

**Frozen Lettuce**:
- Twist and snap
- Wet, crunchy sound
- Realistic
- Used in major films

**Walnuts**:
- Crack on hard surface
- Sharp crack
- Small bones

**Acorns**:
- Similar to walnuts
- Smaller sound
- Fingers, toes

**Apples**:
- Twist and break
- Wet crunch
- Larger bones

**Technique**:
- Match size of vegetable to bone
- Celery: Arms, legs
- Carrots: Smaller bones
- Walnuts: Fingers, toes
- Layer for complex breaks

### Falls and Impacts

**Body Falls**:

**Sandbags**:
- Drop for body fall
- Various sizes for different body types
- Adjustable weight

**Duffel Bag**:
- Filled with clothes or soft items
- Drop or throw
- Softer impact

**Pillows/Cushions**:
- Soft landing
- Throw or drop
- Layer with other sounds

**Technique**:
- Match weight to character
- Height of drop affects sound
- Surface matters (wood, concrete, etc.)
- Layer with clothing rustle, breath

**Object Impacts**:
- Match material (wood, metal, plastic)
- Use actual objects when possible
- Vary force
- Consider surface

## Creative Substitutions

### Famous Foley Tricks

**Horse Hooves**:
- **Coconut shells**: Cut in half, stuffed with padding
- Most iconic Foley trick
- More convincing than real hooves
- Vary rhythm and surface

**Fire**:
- **Cellophane**: Crinkle for crackling
- **Plastic bags**: Crumple for flames
- **Audio tape**: Balled up, crinkled
- Never use real fire in studio

**Thunder**:
- **Large thin metal sheet**: Shake for rumble
- "Thunder sheet"
- Vary intensity

**Rain**:
- **Spray bottle**: Light rain
- **Shower**: Heavier rain
- **Rice on surface**: Patter
- **Frying bacon**: Sizzle (creative)

**Wind**:
- **Blow across mic**: Carefully
- **Fabric waving**: Whoosh
- **Thin material**: Flutter

**Creaks**:
- **Old chair**: Sit and shift weight
- **Rusty hinge**: Open and close
- **Leather**: Twist and bend
- **Water-soaked wood**: Stress

**Whoosh**:
- **Thin stick**: Swing through air
- **Arrow (no tip)**: Fast movement
- **Fishing rod**: Flexible whoosh
- **Dowel**: Various sizes

**Squishing/Guts**:
- **Gelatin**: Squeeze and squish
- **Hand soap**: Wet, squishy
- **Wet sponge**: Squeeze
- **Overripe fruit**: Squash

**Ice Cubes**:
- **Walnuts in glass**: Don't melt
- **Dice**: Rattle
- **Small stones**: Clink

**Grass/Brush**:
- **Audio tape**: Balled up, crinkled
- **Straw**: Rustle
- **Dried leaves**: Crunch
- **Artificial plants**: Consistent
