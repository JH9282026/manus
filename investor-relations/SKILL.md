# Investor Relations

Manage strategic communications between a public company and its investment community to build trust, ensure transparency, and support fair valuation.

## Overview

Investor Relations (IR) bridges corporate strategy and capital markets. Effective IR programs ensure timely, accurate, and compliant financial communication to analysts, institutional investors, retail shareholders, and regulators. This skill covers the full IR cycle: earnings communications, investor targeting, analyst relations, shareholder activism defense, proxy season, and IR performance metrics — all within Regulation FD and SEC filing requirements.

## Earnings Communication Framework

### Quarterly Earnings Timeline

| Weeks Before | Activity | Owner |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| T-6 | Begin drafting press release, align with finance on estimates | IR + Finance |
| T-4 | Draft earnings script and slide deck | IR + CEO/CFO |
| T-3 | Internal review — legal, audit committee | Legal + Board |
| T-2 | Prepare Q&A document (50–80 anticipated questions) | IR |
| T-1 | Rehearsal call with CEO and CFO | IR + Executives |
| T-0 (after market close) | File 8-K, issue press release, host earnings call | IR |
| T+1 | Post transcript and replay, update IR website | IR |
| T+2 to T+5 | Post-earnings investor meetings and conferences | IR + Executives |

### Press Release Structure
1. **Headline:** Quarter, revenue, EPS vs. guidance
2. **CEO quote:** Strategic narrative (2–3 sentences)
3. **Financial highlights table:** Revenue, operating income, EPS, cash flow
4. **Segment results:** Revenue and margin by business unit
5. **Guidance:** Next quarter and/or full-year outlook
6. **CFO quote:** Financial strategy and capital allocation
7. **Financial statements:** Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow
8. **Non-GAAP reconciliations:** Adjusted EBITDA, free cash flow, adjusted EPS

### Earnings Call Best Practices
- Keep prepared remarks to 20–25 minutes; maximize Q&A time
- CEO covers strategy and operational highlights; CFO covers financials
- Never speculate — stick to facts and approved guidance ranges
- Have a "parking lot" for questions to address in follow-up
- Record and transcribe every call for Reg FD compliance

## Regulatory Compliance Quick Reference

| Regulation | Requirement | Penalty for Non-Compliance | Key Action |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------------|------------|
| Reg FD | No selective material disclosure | SEC enforcement, fines | Disclose to all simultaneously |
| 10-K (Annual) | File within 60 days of fiscal year-end | Delinquent filer, potential delisting | Coordinate with external auditors |
| 10-Q (Quarterly) | File within 40 days of quarter-end | Same as above | Align with finance calendar |
| 8-K (Current) | File within 4 business days of material event | SEC action | Monitor for triggering events |
| Insider trading (Rule 10b-5) | No trading on material non-public info (MNPI) | Criminal prosecution, disgorgement | Enforce trading windows and pre-clearance |
| Proxy (14A) | Distribute proxy 40+ days before annual meeting | Delay annual meeting | Start preparation 4 months ahead |
| Reg G | Reconcile any non-GAAP metrics to GAAP | SEC comment letter | Always include reconciliation tables |

## Shareholder Analysis Framework

| Investor Type | Typical Holding Period | Engagement Strategy | Frequency |
|--------------|----------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Long-only fundamental | 2–5 years | Deep relationship, CEO/CFO access | Quarterly meetings |
| Growth equity | 1–3 years | Growth metrics focus, product roadmap | Quarterly updates |
| Index/passive | Indefinite (tracks index) | Proxy voting, governance focus | Annual governance meeting |
| Hedge fund (long) | 6–18 months | Catalyst-driven, financial detail | As requested |
| Activist | Variable | Board-level engagement, defense prep | Immediate upon approach |
| Retail | Variable | IR website, social media, annual meeting | Ongoing digital |

### Shareholder Base Health Metrics
- **Institutional ownership %:** Target 60–85% for mid/large cap
- **Top 10 holders concentration:** Monitor for >40% (risk)
- **Turnover rate:** <50% annually indicates stable base
- **Analyst coverage:** 5–15 analysts for mid-cap, 15–30 for large-cap
- **Geographic diversification:** Track domestic vs. international holders

## Activist Investor Response Framework

| Stage | Action | Timeline |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Early detection | Monitor 13-D/G filings, unusual volume, short interest | Ongoing |
| Initial approach | Assess demands, engage advisors (legal, banking, proxy) | Within 48 hours |
| Board briefing | Present activist profile, demands, and response options | Within 1 week |
| Private engagement | CEO/Board meeting with activist, seek resolution | 2–4 weeks |
| Public response (if needed) | Press release, investor letter, white paper | If private fails |
| Proxy fight (worst case) | Proxy solicitation, shareholder outreach, ISS/Glass Lewis engagement | 3–6 months |

## Best Practices

- **Tell a consistent story** — earnings narrative should connect quarter to quarter with a clear strategic arc
- **Prepare for worst-case questions** — if you wouldn't want to be asked it, prepare an answer for it
- **Maintain a quiet period** — typically 2–4 weeks before earnings; no investor meetings or guidance updates
- **Track consensus closely** — if the Street is modeling too high/low, consider pre-announcing
- **Engage proxy advisors early** — ISS and Glass Lewis influence 20–30% of institutional votes
- **Update the IR website regularly** — stale websites signal poor governance to institutional investors
