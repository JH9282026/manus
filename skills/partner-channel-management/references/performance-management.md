# Partner Performance Management

Frameworks for establishing performance metrics, conducting business reviews, and optimizing partner performance through data-driven insights and coaching.

---

## Partner Performance Metrics

### Pipeline Metrics

**Active Pipeline Value**
- **Definition**: Total value of open opportunities registered by partner
- **Formula**: Sum of all open opportunity amounts
- **Target**: 3-5x quarterly quota (varies by sales cycle and win rate)
- **Frequency**: Weekly tracking, monthly review
- **Use**: Assess pipeline health and forecast accuracy

**Pipeline Coverage Ratio**
- **Definition**: Pipeline value divided by quota for period
- **Formula**: Total Pipeline / Quota
- **Target**: 3-5x for healthy coverage
- **Frequency**: Weekly
- **Use**: Early warning indicator for quota attainment risk

**Deal Registration Rate**
- **Definition**: Number of deals registered per month
- **Formula**: Count of new deal registrations in period
- **Target**: Varies by partner tier and market (e.g., 5-10/month for active partner)
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Use**: Measure partner activity and engagement

**Pipeline Velocity**
- **Definition**: Speed at which deals move through pipeline
- **Formula**: (# Opportunities × Average Deal Size × Win Rate) / Average Sales Cycle Days
- **Target**: Benchmark against top performers
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Use**: Identify bottlenecks and coaching opportunities

**Conversion Rate (Registration to Close)**
- **Definition**: Percentage of registered deals that close as won
- **Formula**: (Closed-Won Deals / Total Registered Deals) × 100%
- **Target**: 20-40% (varies by sales model)
- **Frequency**: Quarterly
- **Use**: Quality signal for deal qualification and partner effectiveness

**Average Deal Size**
- **Definition**: Mean value of closed-won deals
- **Formula**: Total Revenue / Number of Closed-Won Deals
- **Target**: Benchmark against company average and partner tier
- **Frequency**: Quarterly
- **Use**: Assess partner's ability to sell value and upsell

### Enablement Metrics

**Portal Engagement**
- **Definition**: Partner portal login frequency and activity
- **Metrics**: Logins per month, pages viewed, time on site
- **Target**: 10+ logins/month for active partners
- **Use**: Measure partner engagement and resource utilization

**Training Completion Rate**
- **Definition**: Percentage of assigned training completed
- **Formula**: (Completed Modules / Assigned Modules) × 100%
- **Target**: 100% for required training, 80%+ for recommended
- **Use**: Ensure partner readiness and knowledge

**Certification Attainment**
- **Definition**: Number and level of certifications achieved
- **Metrics**: # certified individuals, certification levels, currency
- **Target**: Varies by partner tier (e.g., Gold = 5+ certified)
- **Use**: Qualify partners for tiers and complex deals

**Marketing Asset Utilization**
- **Definition**: Downloads and usage of marketing materials
- **Metrics**: Downloads per month, asset types, campaigns launched
- **Target**: 5+ asset downloads/month for active partners
- **Use**: Assess marketing engagement and demand generation activity

**MDF Utilization Rate**
- **Definition**: Percentage of allocated MDF actually used
- **Formula**: (MDF Spent / MDF Allocated) × 100%
- **Target**: 80-100%
- **Use**: Measure partner marketing investment and engagement

**Time-to-First-Deal**
- **Definition**: Days from partner onboarding to first closed deal
- **Formula**: First Deal Close Date - Onboarding Start Date
- **Target**: <90 days
- **Use**: Assess onboarding effectiveness and partner ramp

### Sales Performance Metrics

**Partner-Sourced Revenue**
- **Definition**: Revenue from deals originated by partner
- **Formula**: Sum of closed-won deal values where partner is source
- **Target**: Varies by partner tier and quota
- **Frequency**: Monthly, quarterly, annually
- **Use**: Primary revenue attribution and quota attainment

**Partner-Influenced Revenue**
- **Definition**: Revenue from deals where partner assisted but didn't originate
- **Formula**: Sum of closed-won deal values where partner influenced
- **Target**: Typically 2-3x sourced revenue
- **Use**: Measure partner's broader impact on revenue

**Quota Attainment**
- **Definition**: Actual revenue as percentage of assigned quota
- **Formula**: (Actual Revenue / Quota) × 100%
- **Target**: 100%+ (aim for 60-80% of partners achieving quota)
- **Frequency**: Quarterly, annually
- **Use**: Performance evaluation and tier qualification

**Win Rate**
- **Definition**: Percentage of opportunities that close as won
- **Formula**: (Closed-Won Deals / Total Closed Deals) × 100%
- **Target**: 25-40% (varies by sales model)
- **Frequency**: Quarterly
- **Use**: Assess partner sales effectiveness and qualification discipline

**Sales Cycle Length**
- **Definition**: Average days from opportunity creation to close
- **Formula**: Average (Close Date - Create Date) for closed-won deals
- **Target**: Benchmark against company average
- **Frequency**: Quarterly
- **Use**: Identify efficiency opportunities and coaching needs

**Revenue per Partner**
- **Definition**: Average revenue generated per partner
- **Formula**: Total Partner Revenue / Number of Active Partners
- **Target**: Varies by business model and partner tier
- **Frequency**: Quarterly, annually
- **Use**: Assess partner productivity and program efficiency

**Partner Program ROI**
- **Definition**: Return on investment for partner program
- **Formula**: (Partner Revenue - Partner Costs) / Partner Costs
- **Partner Costs**: Commissions + program expenses + headcount
- **Target**: 3-5x ROI
- **Frequency**: Annually
- **Use**: Justify program investment and expansion

### Customer Success Metrics

**Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)**
- **Definition**: Customer satisfaction score for partner-sold customers
- **Formula**: (Satisfied Customers / Total Surveyed) × 100%
- **Target**: 85%+ satisfaction
- **Frequency**: Quarterly surveys
- **Use**: Assess partner delivery quality and customer experience

**Net Promoter Score (NPS)**
- **Definition**: Customer likelihood to recommend (partner-sold customers)
- **Formula**: % Promoters - % Detractors
- **Target**: 50+ (varies by industry)
- **Frequency**: Quarterly or bi-annually
- **Use**: Measure customer loyalty and partner relationship quality

**Customer Churn Rate**
- **Definition**: Percentage of partner-sold customers who churn
- **Formula**: (Churned Customers / Total Customers) × 100%
- **Target**: <10% annually (varies by business model)
- **Frequency**: Monthly, quarterly
- **Use**: Assess partner's customer success and support quality

**Implementation Satisfaction**
- **Definition**: Customer satisfaction with implementation process
- **Formula**: Average rating on implementation survey (1-5 scale)
- **Target**: 4.0+ average
- **Frequency**: Post-implementation survey
- **Use**: Evaluate partner technical delivery and project management

**Upsell/Cross-Sell Success**
- **Definition**: Revenue from expansions in partner-sold accounts
- **Metrics**: Expansion revenue, % of customers expanded, average expansion size
- **Target**: 20-30% of customers expand annually
- **Frequency**: Quarterly
- **Use**: Measure partner's ability to grow accounts and drive adoption

### Partner Health Metrics

**Partner Satisfaction Score**
- **Definition**: Partner's satisfaction with vendor relationship and program
- **Formula**: Survey score (1-5 scale) across multiple dimensions
- **Dimensions**: Support quality, enablement, economics, communication, tools
- **Target**: 4.0+ average
- **Frequency**: Bi-annually
- **Use**: Identify at-risk partners and improvement opportunities

**Partner Retention Rate**
- **Definition**: Percentage of partners who remain active year-over-year
- **Formula**: (Partners Retained / Total Partners at Start) × 100%
- **Target**: 80-90%
- **Frequency**: Annually
- **Use**: Measure program health and partner loyalty

**Partner Lifetime Value (PLV)**
- **Definition**: Total value a partner brings over relationship lifetime
- **Formula**: (Average Annual Revenue per Partner × Average Partner Tenure) - Partner Costs
- **Target**: Varies by business model
- **Frequency**: Annually
- **Use**: Assess long-term partner value and investment decisions

**Engagement Score**
- **Definition**: Composite score of partner activity and engagement
- **Components**: Portal logins, training completion, deal registrations, communication responsiveness
- **Formula**: Weighted average of component scores (0-100)
- **Target**: 70+ for active partners
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Use**: Early warning indicator for partner disengagement

## Partner Business Reviews

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Structure

**Preparation (1-2 weeks before)**

**Partner Manager Preparation**:
- Gather performance data and metrics
- Analyze trends and identify insights
- Prepare presentation deck
- Identify discussion topics and agenda items
- Schedule meeting and send calendar invite

**Partner Preparation**:
- Review performance dashboard
- Prepare pipeline and forecast update
- Identify challenges and support needs
- Gather feedback and questions

**QBR Agenda (90 minutes)**

**1. Welcome and Relationship Check-In** (10 min)
- Introductions (if new attendees)
- Relationship health and satisfaction
- Wins and celebrations since last QBR
- Agenda review and objectives

**2. Performance Review** (30 min)
- Revenue performance vs. quota
- Pipeline health and coverage
- Win rate and deal velocity trends
- Key wins and losses analysis
- Enablement and engagement metrics
- Customer success and satisfaction

**3. Pipeline and Forecast Review** (20 min)
- Current quarter forecast and confidence
- Next quarter pipeline and coverage
- Top opportunities and deal strategies
- At-risk deals and mitigation plans
- Resource needs and support requests

**4. Strategic Discussion** (20 min)
- Market trends and opportunities
- Competitive landscape and positioning
- Product roadmap and upcoming releases
- Partner program updates and changes
- Joint marketing and demand generation plans

**5. Action Planning** (10 min)
- Key takeaways and insights
- Action items with owners and due dates
- Support commitments from vendor
- Goals and priorities for next quarter
- Next QBR scheduling

**Post-QBR Follow-Up**

**Within 48 Hours**:
- Send meeting notes and action items
- Share presentation deck
- Begin executing on commitments

**Within 1 Week**:
- Complete any immediate action items
- Provide requested resources or support
- Update CRM with QBR notes and outcomes

**Ongoing**:
- Track action item completion
- Monitor progress on goals
- Provide regular updates and check-ins

### Monthly Check-In Structure

**Format**: 30-minute call or video conference

**Agenda**:
1. **Quick Wins and Updates** (5 min)
2. **Pipeline Review** (10 min)
   - New opportunities and deal registrations
   - Deal progression and stage changes
   - Stalled or at-risk deals
3. **Support and Enablement** (10 min)
   - Challenges and blockers
   - Resource requests
   - Training or enablement needs
4. **Action Items and Next Steps** (5 min)

**Frequency**: Monthly for Silver+ partners, quarterly for Registered partners

### Annual Business Planning

**Timing**: 6-8 weeks before fiscal year start

**Process**:

**Step 1: Performance Retrospective**
- Review full year performance
- Analyze what worked and what didn't
- Identify lessons learned
- Celebrate successes and wins

**Step 2: Market and Opportunity Assessment**
- Analyze market trends and dynamics
- Identify whitespace and opportunities
- Assess competitive landscape
- Evaluate partner's strengths and capabilities

**Step 3: Goal Setting**
- Set revenue targets for next year
- Define market expansion goals
- Establish capability development objectives
- Align on strategic priorities

**Step 4: Business Plan Development**
- Target account identification
- Go-to-market strategy and tactics
- Marketing and demand generation plan
- Enablement and certification roadmap
- Resource allocation and investment

**Step 5: Quarterly Milestones**
- Break annual goals into quarterly targets
- Define key milestones and checkpoints
- Establish success metrics and KPIs
- Create accountability and tracking mechanisms

**Step 6: Approval and Commitment**
- Present plan to partner and vendor leadership
- Secure executive sponsorship and support
- Finalize commitments and agreements
- Kick off execution

## Performance Improvement

### Partner Segmentation by Performance

**Top Performers (20%)**
- **Characteristics**: Consistently exceed quota, high engagement, strong customer success
- **Strategy**: Invest heavily, expand relationship, leverage as references
- **Actions**:
  - Increase quota and territory
  - Provide exclusive opportunities and benefits
  - Involve in advisory councils and product feedback
  - Showcase as success stories
  - Explore co-innovation and strategic initiatives

**Core Performers (30%)**
- **Characteristics**: Meet quota consistently, good engagement, solid execution
- **Strategy**: Maintain and optimize, help reach top tier
- **Actions**:
  - Provide standard support and resources
  - Identify growth opportunities and expansion
  - Offer advanced training and specialization
  - Encourage best practice sharing
  - Support tier advancement

**Developing Partners (30%)**
- **Characteristics**: Inconsistent performance, moderate engagement, potential
- **Strategy**: Invest in enablement and coaching, drive improvement
- **Actions**:
  - Diagnose root causes of underperformance
  - Provide targeted coaching and support
  - Increase enablement and training
  - Set clear improvement goals and timelines
  - Monitor progress closely

**At-Risk/Inactive Partners (20%)**
- **Characteristics**: Low or no performance, minimal engagement, lack of commitment
- **Strategy**: Re-engage or exit, reallocate resources
- **Actions**:
  - Assess viability and commitment
  - Provide ultimatum with clear expectations
  - Offer re-onboarding or intensive support (if committed)
  - Exit partnership if no improvement (if not committed)
  - Reallocate resources to higher-performing partners

### Performance Coaching Framework

**Diagnostic Phase**

**Identify Performance Gaps**:
- Compare actual vs. target performance
- Analyze trends and patterns
- Segment by metric category (pipeline, sales, customer success)

**Root Cause Analysis**:
- **Capability**: Lack of knowledge, skills, or resources?
- **Capacity**: Insufficient time, people, or bandwidth?
- **Commitment**: Lack of motivation, prioritization, or alignment?
- **Market**: External factors (competition, economy, market dynamics)?

**Coaching Plan Development**

**Set Clear Goals**:
- Specific, measurable improvement targets
- Realistic timeline for achievement
- Consequences for success and failure

**Identify Interventions**:
- **Training**: Additional product, sales, or technical training
- **Resources**: Marketing support, MDF, lead generation
- **Process**: Deal coaching, pipeline reviews, account planning
- **Support**: Dedicated resources, executive engagement, technical specialists

**Execution and Monitoring**

**Regular Check-Ins**:
- Weekly or bi-weekly coaching calls
- Review progress on goals and action items
- Provide feedback and guidance
- Adjust plan as needed

**Track Leading Indicators**:
- Activity metrics (calls, meetings, demos)
- Pipeline metrics (new opportunities, progression)
- Engagement metrics (training, portal usage)

**Celebrate Progress**:
- Recognize improvements and wins
- Reinforce positive behaviors
- Build confidence and momentum

**Evaluation and Decision**

**Success Criteria**:
- Achievement of improvement goals
- Sustained performance over time
- Increased engagement and commitment

**Outcomes**:
- **Success**: Graduate from coaching, return to standard support
- **Partial Success**: Continue coaching with adjusted goals
- **Failure**: Exit partnership or move to inactive status

### Partner Scorecards

**Scorecard Components**

**Revenue Performance (40%)**
- Quota attainment: 20%
- Revenue growth YoY: 10%
- Pipeline coverage: 10%

**Customer Success (25%)**
- Customer satisfaction (CSAT/NPS): 15%
- Customer retention: 10%

**Engagement and Enablement (20%)**
- Training and certification: 10%
- Portal and resource utilization: 5%
- MDF utilization: 5%

**Strategic Alignment (15%)**
- Product mix and strategic product adoption: 10%
- Market expansion and new customer acquisition: 5%

**Scoring Scale**:
- 90-100: Exceeds Expectations (Top Performer)
- 75-89: Meets Expectations (Core Performer)
- 60-74: Needs Improvement (Developing Partner)
- <60: Unsatisfactory (At-Risk Partner)

**Scorecard Uses**:
- Tier qualification and advancement
- Incentive and bonus calculation
- Resource allocation decisions
- Performance discussions and coaching
- Program optimization and benchmarking

## Partner Recognition and Rewards

### Recognition Programs

**Partner of the Year Awards**

**Categories**:
- Overall Partner of the Year
- Rookie Partner of the Year (new partners)
- Growth Partner of the Year (highest growth rate)
- Innovation Partner of the Year (creative solutions, co-innovation)
- Customer Success Partner of the Year (highest CSAT/NPS)
- Regional Partner of the Year (by geography)

**Selection Process**:
- Nominations from partner managers and customers
- Scorecard-based evaluation
- Executive review and selection
- Announcement at annual partner summit

**Awards**:
- Trophy and recognition
- Cash bonus or additional MDF
- Featured case study and PR
- Exclusive benefits for next year

**Quarterly Recognition**

**Top Performer Leaderboard**:
- Published monthly or quarterly
- Ranked by revenue, growth, or other metrics
- Shared in partner newsletter and portal
- Recognition in partner community

**Deal of the Quarter**:
- Largest deal, most strategic win, or best customer story
- Featured in internal and partner communications
- Recognition for partner team involved
- Bonus or SPIF for the win

### Incentive Programs

**Performance Bonuses**
- Quarterly bonuses for exceeding quota
- Annual rebates based on total revenue
- Tiered structure (higher % at higher attainment)

**SPIFs (Sales Performance Incentive Funds)**
- Short-term incentives for specific products or behaviors
- Time-limited (30-90 days)
- Clear rules and payout structure
- Promoted heavily to drive awareness

**Exclusive Benefits**
- Early access to new products and features
- Exclusive territories or named accounts
- Priority support and resources
- Executive access and influence
- Invitation to exclusive events

**Tier Advancement**
- Progression to higher partner tiers
- Enhanced benefits and support
- Increased commission rates
- Greater visibility and recognition
