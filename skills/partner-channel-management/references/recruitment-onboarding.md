# Partner Recruitment and Onboarding

Comprehensive frameworks for recruiting ideal partners, streamlining onboarding processes, and accelerating time-to-first-deal.

---

## Partner Recruitment

### Ideal Partner Profile (IPP)

**Firmographic Characteristics**

**Company Size and Scale**:
- Employee count: Appropriate for partner model (10-500 for most B2B)
- Annual revenue: $5M-$100M (adjust based on market)
- Years in business: 3+ years (established, stable)
- Growth trajectory: 10-30% annual growth
- Financial health: Profitable or well-funded

**Geographic Coverage**:
- Primary markets: Target geographies you want to expand into
- Office locations: Physical presence in target markets
- Remote capabilities: Ability to serve distributed customers
- Language capabilities: Native language support for markets

**Customer Base**:
- Customer count: 100-1,000 active customers
- Customer segments: Alignment with your ICP
- Customer industries: Vertical expertise matching your targets
- Customer satisfaction: High NPS and retention rates

**Capability Assessment**

**Sales Capabilities**:
- Sales team size: 5-50 sales professionals
- Sales methodology: Consultative, solution-oriented approach
- Average deal size: Alignment with your product pricing
- Sales cycle: Experience with similar complexity
- Win rate: 20-40% (demonstrates qualification discipline)

**Technical Capabilities**:
- Technical team size and expertise
- Relevant certifications and specializations
- Implementation methodology and track record
- Support infrastructure and SLAs
- Integration and customization capabilities

**Marketing Capabilities**:
- Marketing team and budget
- Demand generation programs and results
- Digital presence and thought leadership
- Event participation and sponsorship
- Content creation and distribution

**Strategic Fit**

**Product Portfolio Alignment**:
- Complementary products (not competitive)
- Solution gaps your product fills
- Cross-sell and upsell opportunities
- Technology stack compatibility

**Market Alignment**:
- Target customer segments match
- Industry vertical focus aligns
- Geographic markets overlap
- Go-to-market approach compatible

**Cultural Alignment**:
- Values and mission alignment
- Customer-centric philosophy
- Innovation and growth mindset
- Collaborative partnership approach
- Ethical business practices

**Partnership Readiness**:
- Executive commitment to partnership
- Willingness to invest in enablement
- Capacity to dedicate resources
- Track record with other partnerships
- Realistic expectations and goals

### Recruitment Process

**Step 1: Partner Identification**

**Research Methods**:
- Industry directories and databases (e.g., Crunchbase, LinkedIn)
- Competitor partner lists and ecosystems
- Industry association member directories
- Customer referrals and recommendations
- Analyst reports and market research
- Conference attendee lists and exhibitors

**Qualification Criteria**:
- Meets firmographic requirements
- Serves target customer segments
- Has complementary capabilities
- No conflicts of interest
- Positive reputation and references

**Prioritization**:
- Tier 1: Perfect fit, high strategic value, immediate outreach
- Tier 2: Good fit, moderate value, secondary outreach
- Tier 3: Potential fit, lower priority, nurture for future

**Step 2: Initial Outreach**

**Outreach Channels**:
- **Email**: Personalized message from partner team or executive
- **LinkedIn**: Connection request with personalized note
- **Phone**: Direct call to decision maker
- **Referral**: Introduction from mutual connection
- **Event**: In-person meeting at conference or event

**Outreach Message Template**:

```
Subject: Partnership Opportunity - [Your Company] + [Their Company]

Hi [Name],

I came across [Their Company] while researching [industry/market] leaders, 
and I'm impressed by [specific achievement or capability].

We're [Your Company], and we help [target customers] [achieve outcome] 
through [your solution]. We're building a partner program to expand our 
reach in [market/geography], and I believe there's a strong fit with 
[Their Company].

Our partners typically [quantified benefit] within [timeframe]. I'd love 
to explore how we might work together.

Are you open to a brief call next week to discuss?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Follow-Up Cadence**:
- Day 0: Initial outreach
- Day 3: Follow-up if no response
- Day 7: Second follow-up with additional value (case study, etc.)
- Day 14: Final follow-up, then move to nurture

**Step 3: Discovery and Qualification**

**Discovery Call Agenda** (30-45 minutes):

1. **Introductions** (5 min)
   - Brief company overviews
   - Roles and backgrounds

2. **Partner's Business** (10 min)
   - Target markets and customers
   - Product and service portfolio
   - Growth goals and challenges
   - Current partnerships and ecosystem

3. **Partnership Opportunity** (15 min)
   - Your partner program overview
   - Potential fit and value proposition
   - Success stories from similar partners
   - Economics and support model

4. **Mutual Fit Assessment** (10 min)
   - Alignment on markets and customers
   - Complementary capabilities
   - Partnership goals and expectations
   - Potential concerns or obstacles

5. **Next Steps** (5 min)
   - Mutual interest confirmation
   - Information exchange and due diligence
   - Timeline for decision
   - Follow-up actions and owners

**Qualification Questions**:

**Market and Customer Fit**:
- What customer segments do you serve?
- What industries or verticals do you focus on?
- What geographies do you cover?
- How many customers do you have?

**Capabilities and Capacity**:
- How large is your sales team?
- What's your average deal size and sales cycle?
- Do you have technical/implementation capabilities?
- What's your current revenue and growth rate?

**Partnership Experience**:
- What other technology partnerships do you have?
- How have those partnerships performed?
- What makes a partnership successful for you?
- What challenges have you faced with partners?

**Partnership Goals**:
- What are you hoping to achieve through partnership?
- What revenue goals do you have for this partnership?
- What resources can you dedicate to this partnership?
- What timeline are you thinking for getting started?

**Step 4: Mutual Evaluation**

**Information Exchange**:

**You Provide to Partner**:
- Detailed partner program overview and benefits
- Product information and positioning
- Customer case studies and references
- Competitive differentiation and market opportunity
- Partner success stories and testimonials
- Economics and commission structure

**Partner Provides to You**:
- Company overview and capabilities deck
- Customer references and case studies
- Financial information (if appropriate)
- Partnership proposal or business plan
- Team bios and certifications
- Current partner relationships

**Due Diligence**:

**Reference Checks**:
- Speak with 2-3 of their customers
- Speak with 1-2 of their current partners
- Check online reviews and reputation
- Verify certifications and claims

**Financial Review**:
- Review financial statements (if available)
- Check credit and financial stability
- Assess growth trajectory and health
- Understand funding and ownership

**Competitive Check**:
- Confirm no conflicts of interest
- Understand competitive product relationships
- Assess potential for channel conflict
- Verify exclusivity expectations

**Step 5: Partnership Proposal**

**Business Case Development**:

**Market Opportunity**:
- Total addressable market in partner's coverage area
- Partner's current market share and penetration
- Whitespace and growth opportunities
- Competitive landscape and positioning

**Revenue Projections**:
- Year 1: $X revenue (conservative estimate)
- Year 2: $Y revenue (based on ramp)
- Year 3: $Z revenue (at full productivity)
- Assumptions and key drivers

**Investment Required**:
- Partner resources (sales, technical, marketing)
- Training and certification time
- Marketing and demand generation budget
- Timeline to first deal and ROI

**Mutual Benefits**:
- For Partner: Revenue, market expansion, customer value
- For Vendor: Market coverage, revenue growth, customer success
- For Customers: Complete solutions, local support, expertise

**Proposal Presentation**:
- Executive-to-executive meeting
- Customized proposal deck
- Q&A and objection handling
- Negotiation of terms if needed
- Commitment and next steps

**Step 6: Agreement and Onboarding**

**Partner Agreement**:
- Review and negotiate terms
- Legal review and approval
- Signature and execution
- Kickoff planning

**Onboarding Transition**:
- Introduce partner manager
- Schedule onboarding kickoff
- Provide welcome package and portal access
- Begin onboarding process

## Partner Onboarding

### Onboarding Objectives

**Knowledge Goals**:
- Understand your products, value proposition, and differentiation
- Learn target customers, use cases, and buying process
- Master sales methodology and playbooks
- Know competitive landscape and positioning

**Skill Goals**:
- Conduct effective discovery and qualification
- Deliver compelling demos and presentations
- Handle objections and competitive situations
- Navigate your systems and processes

**Relationship Goals**:
- Build rapport with partner manager and team
- Understand escalation and support processes
- Connect with other partners (community)
- Establish communication cadence

**Business Goals**:
- Complete business plan for partnership
- Identify first target accounts and opportunities
- Set goals and milestones for first 90 days
- Close first deal within 90 days

### 90-Day Onboarding Plan

**Days 1-15: Foundation**

**Week 1: Welcome and Orientation**

**Day 1: Welcome Package**
- Welcome email from executive sponsor
- Partner portal login and access
- Onboarding checklist and timeline
- Partner manager introduction
- Quick start guide

**Day 2-3: Kickoff Call** (2 hours)
- Introductions and relationship building
- Partnership goals and expectations
- Onboarding plan review
- System access and setup
- Q&A and next steps

**Day 4-5: Product Overview Training** (4 hours)
- Product demonstration and walkthrough
- Key features and capabilities
- Use cases and customer stories
- Competitive differentiation
- Pricing and packaging

**Week 2: Sales Fundamentals**

**Sales Training** (8 hours over week):
- Target customer profiles and personas
- Value proposition and messaging
- Sales methodology and process
- Discovery questions and qualification
- Demo best practices
- Objection handling
- Competitive positioning

**Deliverables**:
- Complete product training quiz (80%+ to pass)
- Review sales playbook and materials
- Practice demo with partner manager
- Identify 5 target accounts

**Week 3: Business Planning**

**Business Plan Development**:
- Market opportunity assessment
- Target account identification
- Revenue goals and milestones
- Resource allocation and roles
- Marketing and demand generation plan
- 90-day action plan

**Business Plan Review**:
- Present plan to partner manager
- Refine and finalize
- Get approval and commitment
- Establish success metrics

**Days 16-30: Activation**

**Week 4: Marketing Enablement**

**Marketing Assets Review**:
- Co-branded marketing materials
- Email templates and sequences
- Social media content and guidelines
- Case studies and customer stories
- Competitive battlecards
- ROI calculators and tools

**Marketing Plan**:
- Demand generation tactics
- Content and campaign calendar
- Event participation plan
- MDF request (if applicable)

**Week 5: Technical Enablement** (if applicable)

**Technical Training**:
- Architecture and integration overview
- Implementation methodology
- Common configurations and customizations
- Troubleshooting and support
- Sandbox environment access

**Certification** (if required):
- Technical certification exam
- Hands-on lab exercises
- Implementation project review

**Week 6: First Opportunity**

**Joint Sales Activity**:
- Identify first target opportunity
- Conduct joint discovery call
- Develop proposal and demo
- Partner manager shadows and coaches
- Debrief and feedback

**Days 31-60: Acceleration**

**Week 7-8: Pipeline Building**

**Activities**:
- Outreach to target account list
- Demand generation campaign launch
- Networking and referral generation
- Deal registration for active opportunities

**Support**:
- Weekly pipeline review with partner manager
- Deal coaching and strategy sessions
- Resource requests and escalations

**Week 9-10: Deal Progression**

**Activities**:
- Advance opportunities through pipeline
- Conduct demos and presentations
- Develop proposals and quotes
- Negotiate and close deals

**Support**:
- Partner manager joins key calls/meetings
- Executive engagement for strategic deals
- Deal desk support for quotes and contracts

**Days 61-90: Optimization**

**Week 11-12: First Win**

**Activities**:
- Close first deal (goal)
- Celebrate and recognize success
- Customer onboarding and handoff
- Case study development

**Week 13: 90-Day Review**

**Review Meeting** (1-2 hours):
- Assess progress against goals
- Review pipeline and forecast
- Identify successes and challenges
- Gather feedback on onboarding
- Refine business plan for next 90 days
- Set goals for months 4-6

**Deliverables**:
- 90-day performance report
- Updated business plan
- Feedback survey completion
- Commitment to next phase

### Onboarding Best Practices

**Structured and Repeatable**

**Onboarding Checklist**:
- Create detailed checklist for each phase
- Track completion and progress
- Automate reminders and notifications
- Measure time-to-completion metrics

**Standardized Content**:
- Develop core training modules
- Create reusable templates and tools
- Build library of recorded sessions
- Maintain up-to-date materials

**Personalized and Flexible**

**Adapt to Partner Needs**:
- Assess prior knowledge and experience
- Customize pace and depth of training
- Focus on partner's specific market/vertical
- Accommodate partner's schedule and availability

**Multiple Learning Modalities**:
- Live instructor-led training
- Self-paced e-learning modules
- Video tutorials and demos
- Written guides and playbooks
- Hands-on exercises and practice

**Engaging and Interactive**

**Active Learning**:
- Role-play and practice scenarios
- Hands-on product exploration
- Real-world case studies
- Peer learning and collaboration

**Gamification**:
- Badges and certifications
- Leaderboards for onboarding progress
- Rewards for milestones achieved
- Friendly competition among cohorts

**Supported and Accountable**

**Dedicated Partner Manager**:
- Single point of contact
- Regular check-ins and coaching
- Proactive support and guidance
- Escalation and problem-solving

**Clear Expectations**:
- Defined milestones and deadlines
- Success criteria and metrics
- Consequences for non-completion
- Rewards for achievement

**Measured and Optimized**

**Onboarding Metrics**:
- Time to complete onboarding
- Training completion rates
- Certification pass rates
- Time to first deal
- First-year revenue per partner
- Partner satisfaction with onboarding

**Continuous Improvement**:
- Gather partner feedback
- Analyze completion and performance data
- Identify bottlenecks and drop-off points
- Test and iterate on content and process
- Share best practices across team

### Onboarding Technology

**Partner Portal**
- Centralized hub for all onboarding content
- Progress tracking and checklist
- Resource library and downloads
- Communication and collaboration tools

**Learning Management System (LMS)**
- Structured training courses and paths
- Video hosting and delivery
- Quizzes and assessments
- Certification management
- Progress tracking and reporting

**Partner Relationship Management (PRM)**
- Onboarding workflow automation
- Task assignment and tracking
- Document management and e-signature

**Communication Tools**
- Email automation for onboarding sequences
- Slack or Teams channels for real-time support
- Video conferencing for live training
- Webinar platforms for group sessions

### Common Onboarding Challenges

**Challenge: Partner Lacks Time or Commitment**

**Symptoms**:
- Slow progress through onboarding
- Missed training sessions
- Incomplete assignments
- Lack of engagement

**Solutions**:
- Assess commitment during recruitment
- Set clear expectations upfront
- Require executive sponsorship
- Offer flexible, self-paced options
- Escalate to partner leadership if needed

**Challenge: Information Overload**

**Symptoms**:
- Partner overwhelmed by content volume
- Low retention of training material
- Confusion about priorities
- Requests to slow down or repeat

**Solutions**:
- Focus on essential information first
- Break content into smaller modules
- Provide job aids and quick references
- Reinforce through repetition and practice
- Offer just-in-time learning resources

**Challenge: Lack of Relevant Opportunities**

**Symptoms**:
- Partner can't find target customers
- No active pipeline after onboarding
- Difficulty applying training to real situations
- Frustration with lack of traction

**Solutions**:
- Provide lead generation support
- Share target account lists
- Offer MDF for demand generation
- Connect partner with existing prospects
- Help identify whitespace in their customer base

**Challenge: Technical Complexity**

**Symptoms**:
- Struggle with product demos
- Inability to answer technical questions
- Lack of confidence in technical discussions
- Reliance on vendor for all technical support

**Solutions**:
- Provide more hands-on technical training
- Offer sandbox environments for practice
- Create technical FAQ and troubleshooting guides
- Assign technical specialist for support
- Consider requiring technical certification
