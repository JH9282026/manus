# Podcast Production

Produce professional-quality podcasts from initial concept through publication and promotion.

## Overview

Podcast production encompasses the complete workflow of creating audio content for distribution, including pre-production planning, recording techniques, post-production editing, publishing, and audience growth. This skill covers equipment selection, recording best practices, editing workflows, and distribution strategies for various podcast formats.

## Podcast Formats

| Format | Description | Best For | Considerations |
|--------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| Solo Commentary | Single host sharing insights | Expertise sharing, personal brand | Requires strong presence, preparation |
| Interview | Host interviewing guests | Expert insights, diverse perspectives | Guest scheduling, preparation |
| Co-hosted | Multiple regular hosts | Dynamic conversation, varied viewpoints | Chemistry, balanced participation |
| Panel Discussion | Multiple guests discussing topic | Diverse opinions, debate | Moderation, audio management |
| Narrative/Storytelling | Scripted, produced stories | Investigative, documentary | High production time, scripting |
| Hybrid | Mix of formats | Variety, flexibility | Consistent structure needed |

## Pre-Production

### Concept and Strategy

**Define Core Elements:**
- Niche and target audience (specific > broad)
- Unique value proposition
- Episode format and length
- Release schedule (weekly, biweekly, monthly)
- Long-term goals (audience size, monetization, brand building)

**Content Planning:**
- Episode topics and themes
- Guest identification and outreach
- Content calendar (3-6 episodes ahead)
- Season structure (if applicable)

### Guest Management

**Outreach:**
- Research potential guests thoroughly
- Personalized invitation highlighting their work
- Clear value proposition (audience reach, topic relevance)
- Flexible scheduling options

**Preparation:**
- Send prep kit 1 week before recording:
  - Recording date/time and duration
  - Technical requirements (microphone, headphones, quiet space)
  - Topic overview and potential questions
  - Any materials to review
- Confirm 24-48 hours before recording

## Recording Equipment

### Essential Setup

**Microphone:**
- USB: Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB, Audio-Technica ATR2100x, Samson Q2U
- XLR (requires interface): Shure SM7B, Electro-Voice RE20, Rode PodMic
- Dynamic mics reduce room noise, condensers capture detail

**Audio Interface (for XLR mics):**
- Focusrite Scarlett 2i2/4i4
- PreSonus AudioBox
- Zoom PodTrak P4 (podcast-specific)

**Headphones:**
- Closed-back to prevent bleed
- Audio-Technica M50x, Beyerdynamic DT 770 Pro, Sony MDR-7506
- Essential for monitoring and catching issues

**Recording Software:**
- Free: Audacity, GarageBand (Mac)
- Paid: Adobe Audition, Hindenburg Journalist, Reaper
- Remote: Riverside.fm, SquadCast, Zencastr

### Room Treatment

**Minimize Reflections:**
- Soft surfaces (rugs, curtains, furniture)
- Acoustic panels or foam (DIY or purchased)
- Closet with clothes (makeshift booth)
- Avoid hard, parallel walls

**Reduce Noise:**
- Turn off HVAC, appliances, fans
- Close windows (traffic noise)
- Silence phones and notifications
- Record during quiet times

## Recording Techniques

### Microphone Technique

**Positioning:**
- 4-8 inches from mouth (dynamic mics)
- Slightly off-axis to reduce plosives
- Consistent distance throughout recording
- Pop filter 2-4 inches from mic

**Levels:**
- Peak at -12dB to -6dB (yellow on meters)
- Avoid clipping (red/0dB)
- Consistent levels across speakers
- Test recording before full session

### Recording Best Practices

**Pre-Flight Check:**
- Test all equipment and connections
- Record 30-60 seconds, playback to verify
- Check levels for all participants
- Ensure headphones working

**During Recording:**
- Monitor audio continuously
- Note timestamps for issues or highlights
- Maintain energy and engagement
- Allow pauses for editing (don't rush)

**Remote Recording:**
- Use platforms with local recording (Riverside, SquadCast)
- Guests record locally, upload high-quality files
- Backup recording (Zoom, local DAW recording)
- Test connection and audio before starting

### Batch Recording

**Efficiency Strategy:**
- Record multiple episodes in one session
- Maintain consistent energy across episodes
- Reduces setup/teardown time
- Creates content buffer

## Post-Production

### Editing Workflow

**Three-Pass Framework:**

**1. Content Edit:**
- Remove false starts, long pauses, repetitions
- Reorder segments for better flow
- Cut tangents or off-topic sections
- Tighten pacing

**2. Technical Edit:**
- Remove mouth clicks, plosives, breaths (excessive only)
- Reduce background noise
- Level volume inconsistencies
- De-ess harsh sibilance

**3. Final Polish:**
- Add intro/outro music
- Insert sponsor messages or ads
- Add transitions between segments
- Final quality check on headphones

### Audio Processing

**Noise Reduction:**
- Capture room tone (30 seconds silence)
- Apply noise reduction plugin (iZotope RX, Audacity)
- Subtle application (avoid artifacts)

**EQ:**
- High-pass filter at 80-100 Hz (remove rumble)
- Boost 2-5 kHz for presence and clarity
- Cut problem frequencies (boxiness, harshness)

**Compression:**
- Even out dynamic range
- Gentle settings (2:1-4:1 ratio)
- Makes quiet parts audible, controls peaks

**De-essing:**
- Reduce harsh "s" sounds
- Target 4-9 kHz range
- Subtle application

**Loudness Normalization:**
- -16 LUFS for stereo podcasts
- -19 LUFS for mono podcasts
- True peak below -1 dBTP
- Ensures consistent loudness across platforms

### AI-Powered Tools

**Descript:**
- Transcription-based editing
- Edit audio by editing text
- Automatic filler word removal
- Overdub for corrections

**Adobe Podcast Enhance:**
- AI-powered audio cleanup
- One-click enhancement
- Noise reduction and EQ

## Publishing and Distribution

### Podcast Hosting

**Hosting Platforms:**
- Buzzsprout: Beginner-friendly, analytics
- Libsyn: Established, reliable
- Transistor: Modern, unlimited shows
- Anchor (Spotify): Free, Spotify integration

**Features to Consider:**
- Storage and bandwidth limits
- Analytics and insights
- Distribution to directories
- Website and player embeds
- Monetization options

### Podcast Directories

**Essential Directories:**
- Apple Podcasts (largest audience)
- Spotify (growing rapidly)
- Google Podcasts
- Amazon Music/Audible
- YouTube (video or static image)

**Submission Requirements:**
- RSS feed from hosting platform
- Podcast artwork (3000x3000 px, JPG/PNG)
- Complete metadata (title, description, category)
- At least one published episode

### Show Notes and Metadata

**Episode Description:**
- Compelling summary (first 2-3 sentences crucial)
- Timestamps for key topics
- Guest bio and links
- Resources mentioned
- Call-to-action (subscribe, review, website)

**SEO Optimization:**
- Include relevant keywords naturally
- Descriptive episode titles
- Detailed show notes on website
- Transcripts for accessibility and SEO

## Promotion and Growth

### Launch Strategy

**Pre-Launch (2-4 weeks):**
- Create website and social media
- Build email list
- Tease content and guests
- Prepare 3-5 episodes for launch

**Launch Week:**
- Release 3 episodes simultaneously
- Ask friends/family for reviews
- Share across all channels
- Engage with early listeners

### Ongoing Promotion

**Social Media:**
- Audiograms (15-60 second clips with waveform)
- Quote graphics from episodes
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Engage with audience and guests

**Cross-Promotion:**
- Guest on other podcasts
- Invite podcasters as guests
- Collaborate on content
- Join podcast communities

**Email Marketing:**
- Build subscriber list
- Weekly or episode-based emails
- Exclusive content for subscribers
- Engage and build community

### Analytics and Optimization

**Key Metrics:**
- Downloads per episode
- Listener retention (drop-off points)
- Subscriber growth
- Geographic and demographic data
- Platform distribution

**Optimization:**
- Analyze successful episodes (topics, guests, format)
- Identify drop-off points, improve pacing
- Test episode lengths and formats
- Solicit listener feedback

## Workflow Optimization

### Project Management

**Tools:**
- ClickUp, Trello, Asana, Airtable
- Track episode status (planning, recorded, editing, published)
- Assign tasks and deadlines
- Centralize notes and resources

**Content Buffer:**
- Maintain 3-4 ready-to-publish episodes
- Handles unexpected delays
- Reduces stress and rushed production
- Allows for timely, topical episodes

### Templates and Automation

**Episode Templates:**
- Consistent intro/outro structure
- Standard processing chain
- Show notes template
- Social media post templates

**Automation:**
- Scheduled publishing
- Automatic distribution to directories
- Social media scheduling (Buffer, Hootsuite)
- Email automation

## Monetization

### Revenue Streams

**Sponsorships:**
- Direct outreach to relevant brands
- Podcast ad networks (Podcorn, AdvertiseCast)
- Typical rates: $18-25 CPM (cost per 1000 downloads)

**Listener Support:**
- Patreon: Exclusive content, early access
- Buy Me a Coffee: One-time donations
- Premium subscriptions

**Affiliate Marketing:**
- Promote products/services with affiliate links
- Earn commission on sales
- Disclose affiliate relationships

**Products and Services:**
- Courses, coaching, consulting
- Merchandise
- Live events or workshops
