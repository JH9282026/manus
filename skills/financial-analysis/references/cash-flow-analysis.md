# Cash Flow Analysis

Comprehensive guide to evaluating cash flow quality, analyzing operating/investing/financing activities, and assessing a company's ability to generate sustainable cash flows.

---

## Cash Flow Fundamentals

### Why Cash Flow Matters

**Cash is King**:
- Earnings can be manipulated; cash flow is harder to fake
- Cash pays bills, dividends, and debt
- Positive cash flow indicates financial health
- Free cash flow drives valuation

**Cash vs. Accrual Accounting**:

| Accrual Accounting | Cash Accounting |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Revenue when earned | Cash when received |
| Expenses when incurred | Cash when paid |
| Includes non-cash items | Only actual cash movements |
| Can diverge from cash reality | Reflects actual liquidity |

**Earnings vs. Cash Flow**:

**Profitable but Cash-Poor** (possible scenarios):
- Rapid growth (working capital investment)
- Long collection periods
- Heavy capital expenditures
- Aggressive revenue recognition

**Unprofitable but Cash-Rich** (possible scenarios):
- Large depreciation/amortization
- Deferred revenue model
- Asset sales
- One-time charges

## Operating Cash Flow Analysis

### Operating Cash Flow Components

**Indirect Method Reconciliation**:

```
Net Income

Adjustments for Non-Cash Items:
+ Depreciation and Amortization
+ Amortization of Intangibles
+ Stock-Based Compensation
+ Deferred Taxes
+ Loss on Sale of Assets (or - Gain)
+ Impairment Charges

Changes in Working Capital:
+ Decrease in Accounts Receivable (or - Increase)
+ Decrease in Inventory (or - Increase)
+ Decrease in Prepaid Expenses (or - Increase)
+ Increase in Accounts Payable (or - Decrease)
+ Increase in Accrued Expenses (or - Decrease)
+ Increase in Deferred Revenue (or - Decrease)

= Cash Flow from Operating Activities
```

### Non-Cash Adjustments

**Depreciation and Amortization**:
- Largest non-cash expense
- Added back because it doesn't require cash outflow
- Actual cash was spent when asset was purchased (CapEx)

**Stock-Based Compensation**:
- Non-cash expense (shares issued, not cash paid)
- Added back to operating cash flow
- Dilutes shareholders but doesn't use cash

**Deferred Taxes**:
- Difference between tax expense (income statement) and taxes paid (cash)
- Increase in deferred tax liability → added back
- Decrease in deferred tax liability → subtracted

**Gains/Losses on Asset Sales**:
- Non-operating items
- Gain subtracted (inflates net income, not operating cash)
- Loss added back (reduces net income, not operating cash)
- Actual cash proceeds appear in investing section

### Working Capital Analysis

**Working Capital Changes Impact on Cash**:

| Change | Impact on Cash | Explanation |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Accounts Receivable ↑ | Cash ↓ | Sales made but cash not collected |
| Accounts Receivable ↓ | Cash ↑ | Collecting past sales |
| Inventory ↑ | Cash ↓ | Cash tied up in inventory |
| Inventory ↓ | Cash ↑ | Selling inventory, converting to cash |
| Accounts Payable ↑ | Cash ↑ | Delaying payments, using supplier financing |
| Accounts Payable ↓ | Cash ↓ | Paying suppliers |
| Deferred Revenue ↑ | Cash ↑ | Collecting cash before earning revenue |
| Deferred Revenue ↓ | Cash ↓ | Earning previously collected cash |

**Working Capital Red Flags**:
- Accounts receivable growing faster than revenue
- Inventory building up without sales growth
- Accounts payable declining (liquidity pressure?)
- Deferred revenue declining (future revenue concerns?)

**Sustainable vs. Unsustainable OCF**:

**Sustainable**:
- Driven by profitability (net income)
- Minimal working capital swings
- Consistent over time

**Unsustainable**:
- Driven by working capital manipulation
- Stretching payables excessively
- Aggressive collection tactics
- One-time working capital releases

### Operating Cash Flow Quality

**High-Quality Operating Cash Flow**:
- OCF > Net Income (over time)
- Driven by core operations
- Minimal working capital volatility
- Consistent and predictable
- Growing with revenue

**Low-Quality Operating Cash Flow**:
- OCF < Net Income (persistently)
- Driven by working capital manipulation
- Volatile and unpredictable
- Declining despite revenue growth
- Negative despite profitability

**OCF to Net Income Ratio**:
```
OCF / Net Income Ratio

> 1.0: High quality (cash exceeds earnings)
≈ 1.0: Normal (cash matches earnings)
< 1.0: Potential concern (earnings not converting to cash)
```

**Example Analysis**:

| Year | Net Income | OCF | OCF/NI | Assessment |
|------|-----------|-----|--------|------------|
| 2021 | $100M | $120M | 1.20 | High quality |
| 2022 | $110M | $115M | 1.05 | Good |
| 2023 | $120M | $90M | 0.75 | Concern - investigate |

### Cash Flow Margin

```
Operating Cash Flow Margin = Operating Cash Flow / Revenue × 100%
```

**Interpretation**:
- **> 15%**: Excellent
- **10-15%**: Good
- **5-10%**: Acceptable
- **< 5%**: Weak

**Comparison**:
- Compare to net margin
- Compare to peers
- Track trend over time

**Example**:

| Company | Revenue | OCF | OCF Margin | Net Margin | Assessment |
|---------|---------|-----|-----------|-----------|------------|
| A | $1,000M | $150M | 15% | 10% | Strong cash generation |
| B | $1,000M | $80M | 8% | 12% | Earnings not converting to cash |

## Investing Cash Flow Analysis

### Capital Expenditures (CapEx)

**CapEx Analysis**:

**Maintenance CapEx**: Sustain current operations
**Growth CapEx**: Expand capacity

**Estimating Maintenance CapEx**:
```
Maintenance CapEx ≈ Depreciation Expense

Or:
Maintenance CapEx ≈ Industry Average CapEx as % of Revenue
```

**Growth CapEx**:
```
Growth CapEx = Total CapEx - Maintenance CapEx
```

**CapEx Intensity**:
```
CapEx Intensity = CapEx / Revenue × 100%
```

**Industry Benchmarks**:

| Industry | Typical CapEx Intensity | Rationale |
|----------|------------------------|----------|
| Software/SaaS | 2-5% | Low capital needs |
| Retail | 3-6% | Store buildouts, fixtures |
| Manufacturing | 5-10% | Machinery, equipment |
| Telecom | 15-20% | Network infrastructure |
| Utilities | 20-30% | Power plants, transmission |

**CapEx to Depreciation Ratio**:
```
CapEx / Depreciation

> 1.5: Heavy investment (growth or replacement)
1.0-1.5: Maintaining/growing assets
< 1.0: Underinvesting (assets aging)
```

**Red Flags**:
- CapEx consistently < Depreciation (underinvestment)
- CapEx spiking without clear growth strategy
- CapEx declining while competitors invest

### Acquisitions

**Acquisition Analysis**:

**Key Questions**:
- Strategic rationale?
- Price paid reasonable?
- Integration risks?
- Historical acquisition success?
- Financing method?

**Acquisition Red Flags**:
- Frequent acquisitions (serial acquirer)
- Large premiums paid
- Goodwill impairments on past deals
- Acquisitions to mask organic growth slowdown
- Poor integration track record

**Organic vs. Inorganic Growth**:
```
Organic Revenue Growth = (Current Revenue - Acquired Revenue) / Prior Revenue - 1
```

Separate organic growth from acquisition-driven growth.

### Asset Sales

**Positive Investing Cash Flow** (unusual):

Typically from:
- Asset sales
- Business divestitures
- Investment liquidations

**Analysis**:
- Strategic refocusing?
- Raising cash (liquidity concerns)?
- Selling underperforming assets?
- One-time or recurring?

**Red Flag**: Frequent asset sales to generate cash (may indicate operating problems).

## Financing Cash Flow Analysis

### Debt Analysis

**Debt Issuance**:

**Reasons for Issuing Debt**:
- Fund growth investments
- Refinance existing debt
- Optimize capital structure
- Fund acquisitions
- Return cash to shareholders

**Analysis**:
- Purpose of debt?
- Terms (rate, maturity, covenants)?
- Impact on leverage ratios?
- Ability to service debt?

**Debt Repayment**:

**Positive Signal**: Deleveraging, improving financial flexibility

**Analysis**:
- Scheduled repayment or early payoff?
- Impact on interest expense?
- Opportunity cost (could cash be better used)?

### Equity Analysis

**Equity Issuance**:

**Reasons for Issuing Equity**:
- Fund growth (especially for unprofitable companies)
- Reduce leverage
- Acquisition currency
- Employee stock compensation

**Analysis**:
- Dilution impact?
- Use of proceeds?
- Market timing (issuing when stock price high)?
- Frequency (repeated issuance = red flag)?

**Red Flag**: Frequent equity issuance (burning cash, can't self-fund).

**Share Repurchases**:

**Reasons for Buybacks**:
- Return cash to shareholders
- Offset dilution from stock compensation
- Signal undervaluation
- Improve per-share metrics (EPS)
- Optimize capital structure

**Analysis**:
- Price paid reasonable (compare to intrinsic value)?
- Consistent program or opportunistic?
- Funded by cash flow or debt?
- Impact on leverage?

**Buyback Quality Assessment**:

**Good Buybacks**:
- Funded by free cash flow
- Purchased at reasonable prices
- Consistent with capital allocation strategy
- Doesn't compromise financial flexibility

**Bad Buybacks**:
- Funded by debt (increasing leverage)
- Purchased at inflated prices
- To manipulate EPS
- At expense of necessary investments

### Dividend Analysis

**Dividend Metrics**:

**Dividend Payout Ratio**:
```
Payout Ratio = Dividends / Net Income × 100%
```

**Dividend Coverage Ratio**:
```
Coverage Ratio = Free Cash Flow / Dividends
```

**Interpretation**:
- Coverage > 2.0x: Safe
- Coverage 1.5-2.0x: Adequate
- Coverage < 1.5x: At risk

**Dividend Sustainability**:

**Sustainable**:
- Payout ratio < 60%
- Covered by free cash flow
- History of stable/growing dividends
- Strong balance sheet

**At Risk**:
- Payout ratio > 80%
- Dividends > Free cash flow
- Declining earnings/cash flow
- High leverage

**Dividend Policy Analysis**:

| Policy | Characteristics | Best For |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| Stable Dollar | Fixed amount per share | Mature, stable companies |
| Constant Payout | Fixed % of earnings | Companies with volatile earnings |
| Residual | Pay after funding investments | Growth companies |
| Low Regular + Extra | Base + special dividends | Cyclical companies |

## Free Cash Flow Analysis

### Free Cash Flow Definitions

**Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)**:
```
FCFF = Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures

Or:
FCFF = EBIT × (1 - Tax Rate) + Depreciation - CapEx - Change in NWC

Or:
FCFF = NOPAT + Depreciation - CapEx - Change in NWC
```

**Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)**:
```
FCFE = FCFF - Interest × (1 - Tax Rate) + Net Borrowing

Or:
FCFE = Net Income + Depreciation - CapEx - Change in NWC + Net Borrowing

Or:
FCFE = Operating Cash Flow - CapEx + Net Borrowing
```

**Levered vs. Unlevered FCF**:
- **FCFF**: Unlevered (before debt payments), available to all investors
- **FCFE**: Levered (after debt payments), available to equity holders

### Free Cash Flow Analysis

**FCF Margin**:
```
FCF Margin = Free Cash Flow / Revenue × 100%
```

**Interpretation**:
- **> 10%**: Excellent
- **5-10%**: Good
- **0-5%**: Weak
- **< 0%**: Burning cash

**FCF Yield**:
```
FCF Yield = Free Cash Flow / Market Capitalization × 100%
```

**Interpretation**:
- **> 8%**: Potentially undervalued
- **4-8%**: Fair value
- **< 4%**: Potentially overvalued

**FCF Growth**:
```
FCF Growth = (Current FCF - Prior FCF) / Prior FCF × 100%
```

**Healthy Pattern**: FCF growing at or above revenue growth rate.

### FCF Quality Assessment

**High-Quality FCF**:
- Positive and growing
- Driven by operating cash flow (not working capital manipulation)
- Sustainable CapEx levels
- Consistent over time
- Supports dividends and buybacks

**Low-Quality FCF**:
- Negative or declining
- Driven by unsustainable working capital changes
- Underinvesting in CapEx
- Volatile
- Insufficient to cover dividends

**FCF Conversion**:
```
FCF Conversion = Free Cash Flow / Net Income
```

**Interpretation**:
- **> 1.0**: Excellent (generating more cash than earnings)
- **0.8-1.0**: Good
- **< 0.8**: Concern (earnings not converting to cash)

## Cash Flow Patterns by Company Stage

### Startup/Early Growth

**Typical Pattern**:
- **Operating**: Negative (burning cash)
- **Investing**: Negative (building infrastructure)
- **Financing**: Positive (raising capital)

**Analysis Focus**:
- Burn rate (monthly cash consumption)
- Runway (months of cash remaining)
- Path to profitability
- Capital efficiency

**Burn Rate**:
```
Monthly Burn Rate = (Beginning Cash - Ending Cash) / Months

Runway = Current Cash / Monthly Burn Rate
```

### Growth Stage

**Typical Pattern**:
- **Operating**: Positive but modest (reinvesting in growth)
- **Investing**: Negative (growth CapEx, acquisitions)
- **Financing**: Mixed (may raise capital or start returning cash)

**Analysis Focus**:
- Operating leverage (OCF growing faster than revenue)
- Capital efficiency (revenue per dollar invested)
- Path to free cash flow positive

### Mature/Stable

**Typical Pattern**:
- **Operating**: Positive and strong
- **Investing**: Negative but moderate (maintenance CapEx)
- **Financing**: Negative (dividends, buybacks, debt repayment)

**Analysis Focus**:
- Free cash flow generation
- Capital allocation (dividends, buybacks, M&A)
- Maintaining competitive position

### Declining/Distressed

**Typical Pattern**:
- **Operating**: Declining or negative
- **Investing**: Positive (asset sales)
- **Financing**: Positive (raising capital to survive)

**Analysis Focus**:
- Liquidity (can company survive?)
- Restructuring potential
- Asset value vs. liabilities

## Cash Flow Red Flags

### Operating Cash Flow Red Flags

- [ ] OCF < Net Income (persistently)
- [ ] Negative OCF despite profitability
- [ ] OCF declining while revenue grows
- [ ] Large working capital swings
- [ ] Accounts receivable growing faster than revenue
- [ ] Inventory building without sales growth
- [ ] Deferred revenue declining
- [ ] Stretching payables excessively

### Investing Cash Flow Red Flags

- [ ] CapEx < Depreciation (underinvesting)
- [ ] Frequent asset sales (liquidity concerns?)
- [ ] Serial acquisitions with poor integration
- [ ] Goodwill impairments on past acquisitions
- [ ] CapEx spiking without clear strategy

### Financing Cash Flow Red Flags

- [ ] Frequent equity issuance (burning cash)
- [ ] Increasing leverage without clear purpose
- [ ] Dividends > Free cash flow
- [ ] Buybacks funded by debt
- [ ] Debt refinancing at higher rates

### Overall Cash Flow Red Flags

- [ ] Declining cash balance
- [ ] Negative free cash flow (for mature company)
- [ ] Cash flow volatility
- [ ] Divergence between earnings and cash flow
- [ ] Reliance on financing to fund operations

## Cash Flow Forecasting

### Operating Cash Flow Forecast

**Step 1: Forecast Net Income**
- Project revenue
- Estimate margins
- Calculate net income

**Step 2: Add Back Non-Cash Items**
- Depreciation (% of PP&E or % of revenue)
- Amortization
- Stock-based compensation

**Step 3: Forecast Working Capital Changes**
- Accounts receivable (based on DSO)
- Inventory (based on DIO)
- Accounts payable (based on DPO)

**Example**:
```
Year 1 Forecast:
Net Income: $100M
+ Depreciation: $30M
- Increase in A/R: -$10M (revenue growth)
- Increase in Inventory: -$5M (revenue growth)
+ Increase in A/P: $8M (COGS growth)
= Operating Cash Flow: $123M
```

### Free Cash Flow Forecast

```
Free Cash Flow = Operating Cash Flow - CapEx

Where:
CapEx = Maintenance CapEx + Growth CapEx

Maintenance CapEx ≈ Depreciation
Growth CapEx = (Revenue Growth × CapEx Intensity)
```

**Example**:
```
Operating Cash Flow: $123M
- Maintenance CapEx: $30M
- Growth CapEx: $20M
= Free Cash Flow: $73M
```

### Scenario Analysis

**Base Case**: Most likely scenario
**Bull Case**: Optimistic assumptions
**Bear Case**: Pessimistic assumptions

**Example**:

| Scenario | Revenue Growth | OCF Margin | CapEx | FCF |
|----------|---------------|-----------|-------|-----|
| Bull | 15% | 18% | $40M | $90M |
| Base | 10% | 15% | $50M | $73M |
| Bear | 5% | 12% | $60M | $50M |

## Cash Flow Valuation

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Model

**Enterprise Value (using FCFF)**:
```
Enterprise Value = Σ [FCFF_t / (1 + WACC)^t] + Terminal Value / (1 + WACC)^n

Where:
Terminal Value = FCFF_n+1 / (WACC - g)
              = FCFF_n × (1 + g) / (WACC - g)

g = perpetual growth rate
```

**Equity Value (using FCFE)**:
```
Equity Value = Σ [FCFE_t / (1 + r_e)^t] + Terminal Value / (1 + r_e)^n

Where:
Terminal Value = FCFE_n+1 / (r_e - g)
r_e = cost of equity
```

**Relationship**:
```
Equity Value = Enterprise Value - Net Debt

Where:
Net Debt = Total Debt - Cash
```

### FCF Multiples

**Price-to-FCF**:
```
P/FCF = Market Cap / Free Cash Flow to Equity
```

**EV/FCF**:
```
EV/FCF = Enterprise Value / Free Cash Flow to Firm
```

**Interpretation**: Similar to P/E, but based on cash flow instead of earnings.

**Typical Ranges**:
- **< 15x**: Potentially undervalued
- **15-25x**: Fair value
- **> 25x**: Potentially overvalued (or high growth)

## Cash Flow Analysis Checklist

### Operating Cash Flow
- [ ] OCF positive and growing?
- [ ] OCF > Net Income?
- [ ] OCF margin healthy (>10%)?
- [ ] Working capital changes sustainable?
- [ ] Accounts receivable growth reasonable?
- [ ] Inventory levels appropriate?
- [ ] Deferred revenue growing (if applicable)?

### Investing Cash Flow
- [ ] CapEx appropriate for business?
- [ ] CapEx > Depreciation (investing for growth)?
- [ ] Acquisitions strategic and value-creating?
- [ ] Asset sales justified?

### Financing Cash Flow
- [ ] Leverage ratios healthy?
- [ ] Dividends covered by FCF?
- [ ] Buybacks at reasonable prices?
- [ ] Equity issuance justified?

### Free Cash Flow
- [ ] FCF positive?
- [ ] FCF growing?
- [ ] FCF margin healthy (>5%)?
- [ ] FCF supports dividends and buybacks?
- [ ] FCF yield attractive?

### Overall Assessment
- [ ] Cash balance stable or growing?
- [ ] Cash flow pattern appropriate for company stage?
- [ ] No major red flags?
- [ ] Cash flow supports valuation?
