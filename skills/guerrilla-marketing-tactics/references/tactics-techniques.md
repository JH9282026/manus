# Guerrilla Marketing Tactics & Techniques

## Comprehensive Tactics Catalog

This reference provides detailed breakdowns of specific guerrilla marketing tactics, including implementation strategies, cost considerations, best practices, and real-world applications.

---

## Street Marketing Tactics

### 1. Sidewalk Chalk Art

**Description**: Creating temporary art or messages on sidewalks using washable chalk to capture pedestrian attention.

**Implementation**:
- Scout high-traffic pedestrian areas
- Create weather-resistant designs
- Use stencils for consistent branding
- Time creation for peak foot traffic
- Photograph for social media sharing

**Cost**: £100-£500
- Chalk and materials: £50-£100
- Artist fees: £50-£400
- Permits (if required): £0-£100

**Best For**:
- Local businesses
- Event promotions
- Product launches
- Community engagement

**Advantages**:
- Very low cost
- Environmentally friendly (washes away)
- Eye-catching and artistic
- No permanent installation

**Disadvantages**:
- Weather-dependent
- Temporary (can wash away quickly)
- May require permits in some areas
- Limited to pedestrian zones

**Example**: A coffee shop creates elaborate latte art designs on sidewalks leading to their location, with arrows and "Follow the coffee" messaging.

### 2. Flash Mobs

**Description**: Organized groups of people who assemble suddenly in public spaces to perform a coordinated action, then disperse.

**Implementation**:
- Recruit and rehearse participants
- Choose high-traffic, public locations
- Coordinate timing for maximum audience
- Film from multiple angles
- Include clear brand messaging
- Have participants share on social media

**Cost**: £500-£5,000
- Choreographer/organizer: £200-£1,000
- Participant coordination: £100-£500
- Costumes/props: £100-£1,000
- Video production: £100-£2,500
- Permits: £0-£500

**Best For**:
- Product launches
- Brand awareness campaigns
- Entertainment industry
- Experiential marketing

**Advantages**:
- Highly shareable and viral potential
- Creates memorable experiences
- Generates media coverage
- Engages bystanders emotionally

**Disadvantages**:
- Requires significant coordination
- Can be disruptive
- May face legal/permit challenges
- Difficult to control outcomes

**Example**: T-Mobile's "Life's for Sharing" flash mob at Liverpool Street Station featured hundreds of dancers surprising commuters, generating millions of YouTube views.

### 3. Street Teams

**Description**: Groups of brand representatives who engage directly with consumers in public spaces, distributing samples, flyers, or creating interactions.

**Implementation**:
- Recruit enthusiastic, personable team members
- Provide clear brand training
- Equip with samples, materials, or props
- Target specific locations and times
- Track engagement and conversions

**Cost**: £1,000-£5,000
- Team member compensation: £500-£3,000
- Materials and samples: £200-£1,000
- Uniforms/branded clothing: £100-£500
- Transportation: £100-£500
- Permits: £0-£500

**Best For**:
- Product sampling
- Event promotion
- Local market penetration
- Direct consumer feedback

**Advantages**:
- Direct consumer interaction
- Immediate feedback
- Flexible and adaptable
- Builds personal connections

**Disadvantages**:
- Labor-intensive
- Can be perceived as intrusive
- Weather-dependent
- Requires ongoing management

**Example**: Red Bull's street teams distribute free samples at sporting events, college campuses, and high-energy locations, reinforcing brand positioning.

### 4. Reverse Graffiti

**Description**: Creating images or messages by selectively cleaning dirty surfaces (walls, sidewalks) rather than adding paint or materials.

**Implementation**:
- Identify dirty surfaces in high-traffic areas
- Create stencils for consistent messaging
- Use pressure washers or cleaning tools
- Document before/after for social media
- Ensure legal compliance

**Cost**: £200-£1,500
- Pressure washer rental: £50-£200
- Stencil creation: £50-£300
- Labor: £100-£800
- Permits: £0-£200

**Best For**:
- Eco-friendly brands
- Urban environments
- Cleaning products
- Social causes

**Advantages**:
- Environmentally friendly
- Legally defensible (cleaning, not vandalizing)
- Eye-catching contrast
- Aligns with sustainability values

**Disadvantages**:
- Requires dirty surfaces
- Temporary (surfaces get dirty again)
- Weather can affect visibility
- Limited to certain environments

**Example**: Domino's "Paving for Pizza" campaign used reverse graffiti to highlight their pothole repair initiative, creating clean brand impressions on dirty sidewalks.

### 5. Human Billboards

**Description**: People wearing or carrying branded messages, costumes, or props in public spaces.

**Implementation**:
- Design eye-catching costumes or signage
- Position in high-traffic areas
- Train participants on engagement
- Rotate locations for maximum exposure
- Encourage photo opportunities

**Cost**: £500-£3,000
- Costume/prop creation: £200-£1,500
- Participant compensation: £200-£1,000
- Permits: £0-£500

**Best For**:
- Local businesses
- Event promotion
- Grand openings
- Seasonal campaigns

**Advantages**:
- Mobile and flexible
- Creates photo opportunities
- Direct engagement possible
- Relatively low cost

**Disadvantages**:
- Weather-dependent
- Can be physically demanding
- May face permit requirements
- Limited reach per person

**Example**: Statue of Liberty costume performers outside tax preparation offices during tax season, creating memorable brand associations.

## Ambient Marketing Tactics

### 6. Transformed Public Objects

**Description**: Temporarily modifying existing public infrastructure (benches, poles, statues) to convey brand messages.

**Implementation**:
- Identify suitable public objects
- Design non-damaging modifications
- Obtain necessary permissions
- Install during off-peak hours
- Document for social sharing
- Remove cleanly afterward

**Cost**: £500-£5,000
- Design and fabrication: £200-£2,000
- Installation: £100-£1,000
- Permits and permissions: £100-£1,000
- Removal and cleanup: £100-£1,000

**Best For**:
- Brand awareness
- Product demonstrations
- Creative positioning
- Urban campaigns

**Advantages**:
- Highly visible and photographable
- Integrates with environment
- Creates "double-take" moments
- Generates social media content

**Disadvantages**:
- Requires permissions
- Installation complexity
- Potential for damage liability
- May face public/authority objections

**Examples**:
- Kit Kat transforming park benches into giant chocolate bars with "Have a Break" messaging
- Gold Toe dressing the Wall Street Bull in underwear during New York Fashion Week
- IKEA placing branded sofas at bus stops in Australia

### 7. Floor Graphics

**Description**: Large-scale graphics applied to floors in high-traffic areas to create visual impact from above.

**Implementation**:
- Design for aerial perspective
- Choose high-traffic indoor/outdoor locations
- Use durable, slip-resistant materials
- Obtain venue permissions
- Photograph from multiple angles

**Cost**: £1,000-£10,000
- Design and printing: £500-£3,000
- Installation: £300-£3,000
- Permits and permissions: £100-£2,000
- Removal: £100-£2,000

**Best For**:
- Shopping malls
- Transportation hubs
- Convention centers
- Product demonstrations

**Advantages**:
- Impossible to ignore
- Creates unique photo opportunities
- Can demonstrate product benefits
- High visual impact

**Disadvantages**:
- Expensive installation
- Requires venue cooperation
- Can be damaged by traffic
- Safety considerations

**Example**: Frontline's giant dog image on a mall floor, where people walking above appeared as fleas, garnered significant media attention and perfectly demonstrated the product benefit.

### 8. Elevator/Escalator Wraps

**Description**: Transforming elevators or escalators into branded experiences through creative wrapping or modification.

**Implementation**:
- Partner with building management
- Design immersive experiences
- Use high-quality, removable materials
- Consider user experience and safety
- Document reactions

**Cost**: £2,000-£15,000
- Design and printing: £1,000-£5,000
- Installation: £500-£5,000
- Permits and permissions: £500-£3,000
- Removal: £200-£2,000

**Best For**:
- Shopping centers
- Office buildings
- Hotels
- Transportation hubs

**Advantages**:
- Captive audience
- Immersive experience
- High dwell time
- Memorable impact

**Disadvantages**:
- High cost
- Requires venue partnership
- Installation complexity
- Limited locations

**Example**: A fitness brand wrapping an escalator to look like a treadmill, encouraging people to take the stairs instead.

## Experiential Marketing Tactics

**Description**: Temporary retail spaces or brand experiences that appear for limited times in unexpected locations.

**Implementation**:
- Secure temporary venue
- Design immersive brand environment
- Create shareable moments
- Staff with brand ambassadors
- Promote through social media
- Capture content for amplification

**Cost**: £5,000-£50,000+
- Venue rental: £1,000-£20,000
- Build-out and design: £2,000-£15,000
- Staffing: £500-£5,000
- Inventory/samples: £500-£5,000
- Marketing and promotion: £1,000-£5,000

**Best For**:
- Product launches
- Brand experiences
- Testing new markets
- Seasonal campaigns

**Advantages**:
- Creates buzz and exclusivity
- Allows product trial
- Generates content and media coverage
- Tests market response

**Disadvantages**:
- Significant investment required
- Logistical complexity
- Limited duration

## Event Ambush Marketing Tactics

### 12. Proximity Marketing

**Description**: Positioning brand messages or activations near major events without official sponsorship.

**Implementation**:
- Identify high-traffic routes to events
- Secure nearby locations
- Create relevant messaging
- Avoid trademark infringement
- Engage attendees before/after event

**Cost**: £2,000-£20,000
- Location rental: £500-£10,000
- Materials and signage: £500-£5,000
- Staffing: £500-£3,000
- Legal review: £500-£2,000

**Best For**:
- Sporting events
- Concerts and festivals
- Conventions
- Major cultural events

**Advantages**:
- Leverages event awareness
- Cost-effective vs. sponsorship
- Reaches target audience
- Creates association with event

**Disadvantages**:
- Legal risks
- Potential sponsor backlash
- May face restrictions
- Ethical considerations

**Example**: Stella Artois placing ads around the 2011 US Open without being an official sponsor, capturing tennis fan attention.

### 13. Social Media Hijacking

**Description**: Creating content that capitalizes on event hashtags and conversations without official sponsorship.

**Implementation**:
- Monitor event hashtags and trends
- Create relevant, timely content
- Engage authentically with conversations
- Avoid trademark infringement
- Add value to discussions

**Cost**: £500-£5,000
- Content creation: £300-£3,000
- Social media management: £200-£2,000

**Best For**:
- Real-time marketing
- Brand awareness
- Demonstrating relevance
- Engaging younger audiences

**Advantages**:
- Low cost
- Immediate execution
- Reaches engaged audience
- Demonstrates agility

**Disadvantages**:
- Can appear opportunistic
- Risk of backlash
- Requires constant monitoring
- Easy to get wrong

**Example**: Oreo's "You can still dunk in the dark" tweet during the 2013 Super Bowl blackout became a legendary example of real-time marketing.

- Concept development: £200-£5,000

**Implementation**:
- Identify relevant placement opportunities
- Negotiate partnerships
- Ensure natural integration
- Track exposure and impact

- Placement fees: £500-£500,000+

- Negotiation and management: £400-£50,000

**Best For**:
- Consumer products

**Advantages**:

- Reaches engaged audiences
- Creates aspirational associations
- Long-term exposure

**Disadvantages**:
- Can be expensive
- Difficult to measure impact

- Engage with content created

**Cost**: £500-£10,000
- Product costs: £200-£3,000
- Influencer research: £100-£2,000
- Packaging and shipping: £100-£1,000
- Relationship management: £100-£4,000

**Best For**:
- Consumer products
- Building authentic advocacy
- Reaching niche audiences
- Generating user-generated content

**Advantages**:
- Authentic endorsements
- Cost-effective
- Builds relationships
- Generates organic content

**Disadvantages**:
- No guaranteed coverage
- Difficult to control messaging

**Example**: Beauty brands sending products to micro-influencers who organically share reviews and tutorials, generating authentic buzz.

## Tactical Selection Framework

### Choosing the Right Tactic

Consider these factors when selecting guerrilla tactics:

**Budget**:
- Under £1,000: Chalk art, stickers, social media stunts
- £1,000-£5,000: Street teams, flash mobs, sampling
- £5,000-£25,000: Pop-ups, installations, video campaigns
- £25,000+: Large-scale installations, AR experiences, major activations

**Target Audience**:
- Young/Digital: Social media stunts, AR, viral videos
- Urban Professionals: Ambient marketing, commuter activations
- Local Community: Street teams, pop-ups, grassroots
- Event Attendees: Ambush marketing, proximity tactics

**Objectives**:
- Awareness: Street marketing, ambient, viral videos
- Engagement: Experiential, interactive installations
- Trial: Sampling, pop-ups
- Buzz: Flash mobs, stunts, viral campaigns

**Brand Personality**:
- Playful: Flash mobs, humor-based stunts
- Innovative: AR, interactive installations
- Authentic: Grassroots, influencer seeding
- Bold: Ambush marketing, controversial stunts

**Timeline**:
- Immediate (days): Social media stunts, chalk art
- Short (weeks): Street teams, sampling
- Medium (months): Pop-ups, installations
- Long (months+): AR development, major activations

## Combination Strategies

The most effective guerrilla campaigns often combine multiple tactics:

**Physical + Digital**:
- Street installation + social media hashtag
- Pop-up experience + live streaming
- Ambient marketing + QR codes

**Experiential + Content**:
- Interactive installation + video documentation
- Flash mob + viral video
- Sampling + user-generated content campaign

**Stealth + Amplification**:
- Influencer seeding + PR outreach
- Product placement + social media discussion
- Grassroots + media coverage

**Example**: A campaign might include a street installation (physical), QR codes linking to AR experience (digital), influencer documentation (amplification), and media outreach (PR), creating multiple touchpoints and maximizing impact.
