# Music Theory Troubleshooting

Common problems and solutions in composition and analysis.

## Voice Leading Issues

### Problem: Parallel Fifths and Octaves

**Symptoms:**
- Two voices move in parallel perfect fifths or octaves
- Sounds hollow or medieval
- Reduces independence of voices

**Diagnosis:**
- Check outer voices (soprano and bass) especially
- Look for similar motion to perfect intervals
- Common in inexperienced four-part writing

**Solutions:**
1. Use contrary motion between voices
2. Keep one voice stationary (oblique motion)
3. Move to different chord inversion
4. Revoice chord to avoid parallel motion
5. Use imperfect intervals (thirds, sixths) instead

### Problem: Unresolved Leading Tone

**Symptoms:**
- Leading tone doesn't resolve up to tonic
- Sounds incomplete or unsatisfying
- Weakens cadential effect

**Diagnosis:**
- Leading tone (7th scale degree) moves down or stays same
- Common in V-I or vii°-I progressions
- Often occurs in inner voices

**Solutions:**
1. Resolve leading tone up by half step to tonic
2. If doubling tonic in I chord, leading tone must resolve
3. Exception: leading tone may move down if in inner voice and not creating parallel fifths
4. In V7-I, resolve both leading tone (up) and seventh (down)

### Problem: Awkward Voice Leaps

**Symptoms:**
- Voices jump by large intervals unnecessarily
- Difficult to sing or play
- Sounds disjunct and unnatural

**Diagnosis:**
- Leaps larger than perfect fifth without preparation
- Multiple leaps in same direction
- Leaps not resolved by stepwise motion in opposite direction

**Solutions:**
1. Move voices by smallest possible interval
2. Resolve leaps larger than fourth by step in opposite direction
3. Use common tones between chords
4. Employ contrary motion between voices
5. Consider chord inversions for smoother voice leading

## Harmonic Progression Issues

### Problem: Weak or Illogical Progressions

**Symptoms:**
- Chord progression sounds aimless
- Lacks sense of direction or resolution
- Doesn't support melody effectively

**Diagnosis:**
- Progressions move against functional harmony
- Too many weak progressions (I-iii, vi-iii)
- Lack of dominant-tonic relationships

**Solutions:**
1. Follow functional harmony: Tonic → Predominant → Dominant → Tonic
2. Use strong progressions (V-I, IV-I, ii-V-I)
3. Employ circle of fifths progressions (vi-ii-V-I)
4. Avoid retrogression (moving backward in function)
5. Use secondary dominants to strengthen progressions

### Problem: Excessive Chromaticism

**Symptoms:**
- Too many accidentals obscure tonality
- Sounds disorienting or atonal unintentionally
- Difficult to analyze or understand

**Diagnosis:**
- Overuse of borrowed chords
- Too many secondary dominants
- Lack of diatonic foundation

**Solutions:**
1. Establish clear tonic before introducing chromaticism
2. Use chromatic chords purposefully for specific effects
3. Return to diatonic harmony regularly
4. Ensure chromatic chords resolve logically
5. Balance chromatic and diatonic elements

### Problem: Monotonous Harmonic Rhythm

**Symptoms:**
- Chords change too predictably
- Lacks rhythmic interest
- Sounds mechanical or boring

**Diagnosis:**
- Chords change at same rate throughout
- No variation in harmonic rhythm
- Doesn't support melodic rhythm

**Solutions:**
1. Vary rate of chord changes
2. Use faster harmonic rhythm for tension
3. Slow harmonic rhythm for stability
4. Align important chords with strong beats
5. Create harmonic rhythm supporting melody

## Melodic Writing Issues

### Problem: Unsingable or Awkward Melody

**Symptoms:**
- Melody difficult to remember or perform
- Too many large leaps
- Lacks coherent shape or direction

**Diagnosis:**
- Excessive leaps without stepwise resolution
- No clear melodic contour
- Insufficient repetition or sequence

**Solutions:**
1. Use primarily stepwise motion (60-70%)
2. Resolve large leaps by step in opposite direction
3. Create clear melodic contour with climax point
4. Use repetition and sequence for coherence
5. Test melody by singing it

### Problem: Lack of Melodic Interest

**Symptoms:**
- Melody sounds boring or predictable
- Too much repetition
- Lacks rhythmic variety

**Diagnosis:**
- Excessive use of same pitches or patterns
- Monotonous rhythm
- No variation or development

**Solutions:**
1. Vary rhythmic patterns
2. Use sequence at different pitch levels
3. Employ melodic embellishment (passing tones, neighbor tones)
4. Create rhythmic syncopation
5. Develop motifs through variation

### Problem: Melody Doesn't Fit Harmony

**Symptoms:**
- Melody clashes with chords
- Too many non-chord tones
- Sounds dissonant unintentionally

**Diagnosis:**
- Melody emphasizes non-chord tones on strong beats
- Dissonances not resolved properly
- Harmony doesn't support melodic direction

**Solutions:**
1. Place chord tones on strong beats
2. Use non-chord tones as passing or neighbor tones
3. Resolve dissonances by step
4. Adjust harmony to support melody
5. Ensure melody outlines chord progression

## Modulation Issues

### Problem: Abrupt or Awkward Key Changes

**Symptoms:**
- Modulation sounds jarring or unexpected
- Lacks smooth transition
- Confuses listener

**Diagnosis:**
- No preparation for new key
- Distant key relationship without connection
- Missing pivot chord or common tone

**Solutions:**
1. Use pivot chord common to both keys
2. Prepare new key with its dominant
3. Use common-tone modulation for distant keys
4. Employ chromatic voice leading
5. Consider if direct modulation is intentional effect

### Problem: Inability to Return to Original Key

**Symptoms:**
- Modulation goes too far from tonic
- Difficult to return smoothly
- Piece feels unresolved

**Diagnosis:**
- Modulated to distantly related key
- Stayed in new key too long
- No clear path back to tonic

**Solutions:**
1. Modulate to closely related keys (dominant, subdominant, relative minor/major)
2. Plan return modulation before leaving tonic
3. Use sequence to move through keys back to tonic
4. Employ dominant preparation of original key
5. Consider if new key should be final tonic

## Formal Structure Issues

### Problem: Unbalanced or Unclear Form

**Symptoms:**
- Sections are disproportionate
- Form is difficult to perceive
- Lacks clear structure

**Diagnosis:**
- Irregular phrase lengths without purpose
- Sections too long or too short
- No clear demarcation between sections

**Solutions:**
1. Use regular phrase lengths (4, 8, 16 measures)
2. Create clear cadences at section endings
3. Balance section lengths proportionally
4. Use contrasting material for different sections
5. Employ transitions between sections

### Problem: Lack of Contrast

**Symptoms:**
- All sections sound similar
- Monotonous throughout
- No sense of development or variety

**Diagnosis:**
- Same melodic material in all sections
- No harmonic or rhythmic contrast
- Consistent texture throughout

**Solutions:**
1. Create contrasting B section in ABA form
2. Modulate to different key for contrast
3. Vary texture (homophonic vs. polyphonic)
4. Change rhythmic character
5. Use different instrumentation or register

## Counterpoint Issues

### Problem: Lack of Independence Between Voices

**Symptoms:**
- Voices move in parallel motion
- No rhythmic independence
- Sounds like harmonized melody rather than counterpoint

**Diagnosis:**
- Voices move in same rhythm
- Excessive parallel motion
- No contrary or oblique motion

**Solutions:**
1. Use contrary motion between voices
2. Create rhythmic independence (different note values)
3. Give each voice melodic interest
4. Avoid parallel perfect intervals
5. Use imitation and sequence

### Problem: Dissonance Treatment Errors

**Symptoms:**
- Dissonances sound harsh or unresolved
- Unprepared dissonances on strong beats
- Dissonances don't resolve properly

**Diagnosis:**
- Dissonances on strong beats without preparation
- Dissonances resolve by leap instead of step
- Excessive dissonance without resolution

**Solutions:**
1. Place dissonances on weak beats
2. Resolve dissonances by step
3. Prepare suspensions on previous beat
4. Use passing tones between chord tones
5. Employ neighbor tones returning to same pitch

## Orchestration Issues

### Problem: Muddy or Unclear Texture

**Symptoms:**
- Instruments blend together indistinctly
- Melody not clearly audible
- Bass line unclear

**Diagnosis:**
- Too many instruments in same register
- Excessive doubling
- Poor spacing between voices

**Solutions:**
1. Spread voices across different registers
2. Avoid clustering instruments in middle register
3. Keep bass line clear and distinct
4. Use selective doubling for emphasis
5. Create space between melody and accompaniment

### Problem: Unbalanced Orchestration

**Symptoms:**
- Some instruments overpower others
- Melody buried in texture
- Inconsistent dynamic levels

**Diagnosis:**
- Too many loud instruments
- Melody in weak register or instrument
- Poor understanding of instrument strengths

**Solutions:**
1. Place melody in prominent instrument or register
2. Balance number of instruments per section
3. Use dynamics to control balance
4. Understand relative strengths of instruments
5. Double melody for prominence if needed
