# Event Logistics and Execution

## Venue Selection Criteria

| Factor | Considerations |
|---|---|
| Capacity | Seated, standing, breakout rooms, networking space |
| Location | Accessibility, proximity to airport/hotels, neighborhood |
| Technology | Built-in A/V, Wi-Fi capacity, power access |
| Catering | In-house vs outside, dietary accommodation |
| Accessibility | ADA compliance, elevator access, parking |
| Ambiance | Aligns with event theme and brand |
| Cost | Room rental, F&B minimums, service charges |
| Flexibility | Setup options, load-in/load-out timing |

## Vendor Management

### Key Vendor Categories
- **A/V production**: Sound, lighting, video, staging
- **Catering**: Food, beverage, service staff
- **Decor/design**: Floral, signage, props, branding
- **Photography/video**: Event documentation
- **Entertainment**: Speakers, performers, DJs
- **Transportation**: Shuttles, parking, logistics
- **Security**: Crowd management, access control
- **Staffing**: Registration, ushers, brand ambassadors

### Contract Essentials
- Detailed scope of work with specifications
- Payment terms and cancellation policy
- Insurance requirements and liability
- Setup/teardown timeline
- Contact persons and escalation process
- Force majeure clause

## Run of Show (ROS)

### ROS Template Structure

| Time | Activity | Location | Lead | A/V | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6:00 AM | Vendor load-in | Loading dock | Production mgr | — | Security escort |
| 7:00 AM | Registration opens | Lobby | Reg team | Music playlist | Signage in place |
| 8:00 AM | Doors open | Main hall | Usher team | House lights | Welcome slide |
| 8:30 AM | Opening keynote | Main stage | MC | Presentation + lav mic | Prompter ready |

### ROS Best Practices
- Build in buffer time between sessions (minimum 15 minutes)
- Include technical rehearsal for all speakers
- Assign a stage manager for real-time ROS management
- Create backup plans for each critical element
- Distribute ROS to all vendors and team members

## Attendee Experience Design

### Experience Touchpoints
1. **Pre-event**: Communications, app download, networking matching
2. **Arrival**: Check-in, welcome, wayfinding, swag
3. **Content**: Sessions, keynotes, workshops, panels
4. **Networking**: Structured activities, lounges, receptions
5. **F&B**: Meal quality, dietary options, networking over meals
6. **Departure**: Closing, transportation, follow-up preview
7. **Post-event**: Thank you, recordings, community access

### Engagement Techniques
- Live polling and Q&A during sessions
- Networking app with interest-based matching
- Gamification (session attendance, booth visits)
- Photo opportunities and social media walls
- Surprise and delight moments throughout
