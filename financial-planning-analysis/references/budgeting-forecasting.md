# Budgeting and Forecasting Methods

## Annual Budget Process

### Budget Process Steps

1. **Planning Assumptions**
   - Macroeconomic assumptions (GDP, inflation, FX rates)
   - Company-specific assumptions (pricing, headcount, CapEx)
   - Market assumptions (growth rates, competitive dynamics)
   - Communicate assumptions package to all departments

2. **Revenue Budget**
   - Build by business unit, product, geography, customer segment
   - Use driver-based approach (volume × price × mix)
   - Include pipeline conversion assumptions for new business
   - Reconcile top-down targets with bottom-up builds

3. **Expense Budget**
   - Personnel: Headcount plan × compensation × benefits
   - Non-personnel: By category with driver linkage
   - Discretionary vs committed spend classification
   - Investment spend (growth initiatives) flagged separately

4. **Capital Budget**
   - Maintenance CapEx (sustain current operations)
   - Growth CapEx (expand capacity, new capabilities)
   - ROI analysis for each major project (>threshold)
   - Prioritization framework for capital allocation

5. **Consolidation and Review**
   - Roll up department budgets to company level
   - Challenge sessions with executive team
   - Iterate until aligned with strategic targets
   - Board presentation and approval

## Rolling Forecast Implementation

### Why Rolling Forecasts
- Traditional annual budgets are stale by Q2
- Rolling forecasts maintain constant forward visibility
- Enable faster response to market changes
- Reduce annual budget cycle effort over time

### Rolling Forecast Design

| Element | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Horizon | 12-18 months rolling |
| Frequency | Monthly (at minimum quarterly) |
| Granularity | Less than budget (focus on key drivers) |
| Detail | P&L + key balance sheet + cash flow |
| Process | 5-7 business day cycle |

### Forecast vs Budget Distinction
- **Budget**: Approved financial plan, basis for performance evaluation
- **Forecast**: Current best estimate of expected results
- Track both: Budget variance (accountability) and Forecast variance (accuracy)

## Driver-Based Planning

### Identifying Key Drivers

| Business Type | Revenue Drivers | Cost Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| SaaS | MRR, churn, expansion, new logos | Headcount, CAC, hosting costs |
| Retail | Store count, traffic, conversion, AOV | Labor hours, occupancy, COGS |
| Manufacturing | Units, price, capacity utilization | Materials, labor, overhead |
| Services | Headcount, utilization, bill rate | Compensation, travel, tools |
| Media | Subscribers, ARPU, ad revenue | Content cost, distribution |

### Driver Model Benefits
- Changes in assumptions automatically cascade through the model
- Scenarios can be built by adjusting a few key drivers
- Connects financial outcomes to operational decisions
- Enables non-finance managers to understand budget drivers

## Scenario Planning for FP&A

### Standard Scenarios

| Scenario | Revenue | Expenses | Cash | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Upside | Beat plan | Investment mode | Building reserves | Opportunity planning |
| Base | On plan | On plan | Stable | Performance target |
| Downside | Miss plan by 10-15% | Cost containment | Preserve runway | Risk preparation |
| Severe downside | Miss by 25%+ | Emergency cuts | Cash crisis mgmt | Stress testing |

### Trigger-Based Actions
Define specific actions tied to scenario triggers:
- If revenue trails plan by >10% for 2 consecutive months → Freeze discretionary hiring
- If cash drops below X months runway → Implement cost reduction plan
- If key customer churns → Activate revenue recovery plan
