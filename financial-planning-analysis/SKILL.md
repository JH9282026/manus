# Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A)

Execute budgeting, forecasting, variance analysis, management reporting, and business partnering to drive data-informed financial decision-making across the organization.

## Overview

Provide frameworks for modern FP&A functions covering zero-based and driver-based budgeting, rolling forecasts, variance analysis, management reporting, and strategic business partnering. Bridge the gap between finance and operations with actionable insights.

## FP&A Annual Calendar

| Month | Activity | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|

## Budgeting Approaches

| Approach | Description | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| Incremental | Prior year ± % adjustment | Simple, fast | Perpetuates inefficiency |
| Zero-based (ZBB) | Justify every dollar from zero | Eliminates waste | Time-intensive |
| Driver-based | Linked to operational KPIs | Accurate, dynamic | Requires driver identification |
| Activity-based | Cost per activity/output | Precise cost allocation | Complex data requirements |
| Rolling forecast | Continuously extending horizon | Always current | Requires process discipline |

## Variance Analysis Framework

### Variance Types

| Variance | Formula | Investigates |
|---|---|---|
| Volume | (Actual volume - Budget volume) × Budget price | Demand changes |
| Price/Rate | (Actual price - Budget price) × Actual volume | Pricing changes |
| Mix | Impact of product/customer mix shift | Portfolio changes |
| Timing | Actual vs planned timing of recognition | Phasing issues |
| FX | Impact of exchange rate changes | Currency exposure |

### Materiality Thresholds
- Investigate variances >5% or >$50K (adjust for company size)
- Prioritize by P&L impact
- Distinguish controllable vs uncontrollable variances
- Track recurring variances for forecasting model improvement
