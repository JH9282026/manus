# Brand Positioning & Messaging

Develop strategic positioning and compelling messaging that differentiates and resonates.

## Overview

This skill defines how brands occupy distinctive positions in customer minds relative to competitors. It combines analytical rigor with creative strategy to develop positioning platforms and messaging frameworks that communicate value compellingly across all touchpoints.

## Positioning Statement Template

**For** [target audience]
**Who** [need or opportunity]
**[Brand]** is a [category]
**That** [key benefit/reason to believe]
**Unlike** [competitive alternative]
**[Brand]** [primary differentiation]

### Example

"For upscale American families who want the safest vehicles, Volvo is the family automobile that offers maximum safety. Unlike competitors focused on performance or luxury, Volvo makes safety the primary design consideration."

## Positioning Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Price | Premium, value, or economy | Rolex (premium), Walmart (economy) |
| Quality | Superior craftsmanship | Mercedes-Benz, Patagonia |
| Benefit | Functional or emotional advantage | FedEx (reliability), Disney (magic) |
| Use/Application | Specific occasions | Gatorade (athletics), NyQuil (nighttime) |
| User | Target identity | GoPro (adventurers), Slack (teams) |
| Competitor | Against established player | Avis "We try harder" |
| Category | New category creation | Red Bull (energy drinks) |

## Messaging Architecture

### Hierarchy

1. **Master Narrative**: Overarching brand story
2. **Positioning Statement**: Strategic position (internal)
3. **Tagline**: Public expression of essence
4. **Value Proposition**: Customer benefit statement
5. **Key Messages**: 3-5 supporting pillars
6. **Proof Points**: Evidence for each message

### Audience Adaptation

| Audience | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| Prospects | Problem/solution, benefits |
| Customers | Value reinforcement, expansion |
| Employees | Purpose, culture, pride |
| Investors | Growth, opportunity, leadership |
| Partners | Mutual value, alignment |

## Perceptual Mapping

Plot competitors on two meaningful axes to identify:
- Current market positioning
- White space opportunities
- Repositioning trajectories

**Common Axes:**
- Price vs Quality
- Traditional vs Innovative
- Serious vs Playful
- Mass vs Premium
