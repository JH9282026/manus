# Positioning Frameworks

Strategic frameworks for developing brand positioning.

---

## Positioning Development Process

### Phase 1: Research

1. **Market Analysis**: Trends, size, dynamics
2. **Competitive Audit**: Positioning of key competitors
3. **Customer Research**: Needs, perceptions, language
4. **Internal Assessment**: Capabilities, differentiators

### Phase 2: Strategy

1. **Synthesize Insights**: Identify opportunities
2. **Define Target**: Priority audiences
3. **Craft Position**: Differentiated, relevant claim
4. **Validate**: Test with customers and stakeholders

### Phase 3: Activation

1. **Messaging Architecture**: Build from position
2. **Creative Brief**: Guide expression
3. **Launch**: Internal then external
4. **Monitor**: Track effectiveness

## Points of Difference (PODs)

### Types

| POD Type | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Product | Unique features/performance | Tesla (EV tech) |
| Service | Superior support | Nordstrom |
| People | Exceptional talent | McKinsey |
| Channel | Unique distribution | Warby Parker |
| Image | Brand associations | Apple |

### POD Criteria

- **Desirable**: Customers want it
- **Deliverable**: You can actually do it
- **Differentiating**: Competitors can't match it

---

## Points of Parity (POPs)

### Category POPs

Minimum requirements to compete:
- A smartphone must have apps, calling, internet
- A bank must be secure, accessible

### Competitive POPs

Neutralizing competitor advantages:
- If competitor claims speed, you need adequate speed
- Table stakes that don't differentiate but are required

### White Space Analysis

1. Map current competitors
2. Identify empty quadrants/areas
3. Assess viability of unoccupied positions
4. Evaluate fit with brand capabilities

## Repositioning

### When to Reposition

- Market fundamentally changed
- Competitive pressure requires response
- Brand evolved beyond original position
- Current position limits growth

### Repositioning Risks

- Alienating existing customers
- Confusion during transition
- Loss of accumulated equity
- Competitor response
