# Creative Writing & Storytelling

Create compelling, emotionally resonant content across diverse formats—from fiction and memoir to brand storytelling and marketing narratives.

## Overview

Combine structural story frameworks with artful prose techniques to produce writing that captivates audiences, communicates messages memorably, and achieves creative or commercial objectives. Stories are 22x more memorable than facts alone.

## Story Elements

### Character Development
- **Want**: External goal (what they pursue)
- **Need**: Internal need (what they truly require)
- **Flaw**: Weakness creating conflict
- **Ghost**: Past wound driving behavior

### Conflict Types
- Person vs. Person
- Person vs. Self
- Person vs. Nature
- Person vs. Society
- Person vs. Technology

### Narrative Voice Options
- First person (intimate, limited)
- Third limited (balanced)
- Third omniscient (godlike)
- Second person (immersive, unusual)

## Prose Craft Principles

### Show vs. Tell
Demonstrate through concrete sensory details, actions, and dialogue.
- Tell: "She was nervous."
- Show: "Her hands trembled as she reached for the envelope."

### Dialogue Craft
- Reveal character through speech patterns
- Advance plot through conversation
- Use subtext (what's unsaid)
- Vary dialogue tags minimally
