# Character Development

Techniques for creating dimensional, believable characters.

---

## Character Architecture

### Core Elements
| Element | Question | Purpose |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Want | What do they pursue? | External goal |
| Need | What do they truly require? | Internal growth |
| Flaw | What holds them back? | Source of conflict |
| Ghost | What past wound drives them? | Motivation depth |
| Lie | What false belief do they hold? | Arc foundation |
| Truth | What must they learn? | Arc resolution |

## Character Arcs

### Positive Arc
Character overcomes flaw, learns truth, achieves growth.
- Most common in commercial fiction
- Satisfying emotional journey

### Negative Arc
Character succumbs to flaw, rejects truth, degrades.
- Tragedy, cautionary tale
- Breaking Bad, Macbeth

### Flat Arc
Character already knows truth, changes world around them.
- Action heroes, moral compass characters
- Wonder Woman, Atticus Finch

## Voice Development

### Distinctive Dialogue
- Vocabulary reflecting education/background
- Speech patterns and rhythms
- Favorite phrases or verbal tics
- What they avoid saying

### Internal Monologue
- Thought patterns
- Priorities in observation
- Emotional processing style
- Memory triggers

---

## Characterization Methods

### Direct
Explicit description of character traits.

### Indirect (more powerful)
- Actions under pressure
- Dialogue and subtext
- Reactions of others
- Choices made
- Physical mannerisms
