# Prose Techniques

Writing craft, dialogue, and stylistic techniques.

---

## Show vs. Tell

### Principle
Demonstrate emotions and situations through concrete details.

### Examples
| Tell | Show |
|------|------|
| She was angry | Her jaw tightened as she slammed the folder shut |
| He was nervous | He wiped his palms on his pants for the third time |
| The room was messy | Takeout containers covered every surface; yesterday's clothes formed a path to the bed |

### When to Tell
- Transitions between scenes
- Summarizing time passage
- Backstory that doesn't deserve scenes

## Dialogue Craft

### Subtext
What characters mean vs. what they say:
- "I'm fine." (meaning: I'm not fine)
- "If you want to." (meaning: I want you to)

### Dialogue Tags
- "said" is invisible—use it most
- Action beats instead of tags
- Avoid creative tags: "exclaimed," "expostulated"

### Pacing with Beats
```
"I don't think we should." She turned away, crossing
her arms. "Not after everything."
```

---

## Sentence Craft

### Vary Length
- Short sentences for impact and action
- Long sentences for flow and description
- Mix creates rhythm

### Active vs. Passive
- Active: Subject does action
- Passive: Subject receives action
- Prefer active; use passive strategically

### Sensory Details
Engage multiple senses:
- Sight (visual)
- Sound (auditory)
- Touch (tactile)
- Smell (olfactory)
- Taste (gustatory)

---

## Pacing Techniques

### Speed Up
- Short sentences
- Quick dialogue
- Action verbs
- Skip transitions

### Slow Down
- Longer sentences
- Description and reflection
- Sensory details
- Internal monologue
