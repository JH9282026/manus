# Story Structures

Narrative frameworks for organizing compelling stories.

---

## Hero's Journey (Joseph Campbell)

### Three Acts
**Departure**
1. Ordinary World
2. Call to Adventure
3. Refusal of the Call
4. Meeting the Mentor
5. Crossing the Threshold

**Initiation**
6. Tests, Allies, Enemies
7. Approach to the Inmost Cave
8. Ordeal (death and rebirth)
9. Reward (seizing the sword)

**Return**
10. The Road Back
11. Resurrection
12. Return with the Elixir

### Best For
- Epic narratives
- Brand origin stories
- Transformational journeys

---

## Three-Act Structure

### Act I: Setup (25%)
- Introduce protagonist and world
- Establish status quo
- Inciting incident
- First act turn (point of no return)

### Act II: Confrontation (50%)
- Rising action and complications
- Midpoint reversal
- Escalating stakes
- All is lost moment

### Act III: Resolution (25%)
- Climax
- Falling action
- Resolution and new equilibrium

## Story Circle (Dan Harmon)

### Eight Beats
1. **You**: Character in comfort zone
2. **Need**: They want something
3. **Go**: Enter unfamiliar situation
4. **Search**: Adapt to new reality
5. **Find**: Get what they wanted
6. **Take**: Pay heavy price
7. **Return**: Back to familiar
8. **Change**: Having transformed

### Best For
- Episodic content
- Short-form narratives
- Marketing stories
