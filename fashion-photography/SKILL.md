# Fashion Photography

Create compelling fashion imagery that showcases clothing, accessories, and style for editorial and commercial applications.

## Overview

Fashion photography combines technical lighting expertise with creative direction to produce images that sell products and tell visual stories. Success requires mastering studio and location lighting, directing models, understanding fashion industry standards, and post-processing for polished results. This skill covers equipment selection, lighting techniques, posing strategies, and workflows for editorial, commercial, and e-commerce fashion photography.

## Essential Equipment

### Camera Bodies
- **High Resolution**: 24MP minimum, 45MP+ ideal for detail and cropping flexibility
- **Excellent Color Accuracy**: Critical for representing fabrics and colors truthfully
- **Fast Autofocus**: Face and eye detection for sharp focus on models
- **Recommended**: Canon EOS R5 (45MP), Sony A7R V (61MP), Nikon Z8

### Lenses
**Versatile Zoom**:
- **24-70mm f/2.8**: Standard workhorse, covers most scenarios
- **70-200mm f/2.8**: Compression, flattering perspective, detail shots

**Prime Lenses**:
- **50mm f/1.2-f/1.4**: Sharp, beautiful bokeh, low-light capability
- **85mm f/1.4**: Classic portrait length, flattering compression
- **35mm f/1.4**: Environmental fashion, wider perspective

### Lighting Equipment
**Studio Strobes**:
- **Monolights**: Self-contained flash units (300-500Ws typical)
- **Pack and Head Systems**: More powerful, professional standard

**Light Modifiers**:
- **Octabox** (3-5 feet): Soft, controllable light with round catchlights
- **Stripbox** (1x4 feet): Full-length shots, rim lighting
- **Beauty Dish** (22-28 inches): Directed light for beauty and fashion
- **Umbrellas**: Soft, wrap-around light, portable
- **Grids**: Focus light, create dramatic spots

**Continuous Lights**:
- **LED Panels**: Real-time visualization, video capability
- **Color Temperature Control**: Match ambient or creative effects

### Accessories
- **Light Stands**: Sturdy, adjustable height
- **Reflectors**: White, silver, gold for fill light
- **Flags/Gobos**: Control light spill, create shadows
- **Backdrop System**: Seamless paper or fabric backgrounds
- **Tethering Cable**: Live view on computer for client approval

## Studio Lighting Setups

### Single Light Setup
**Configuration**:
- One large octabox (4-5 feet) as key light
- Positioned 45° to side, slightly above model eye level
- Reflector opposite for fill light

**Settings**:
- Key light: 100% power
- Reflector: Bounces light to fill shadows
- Creates soft, natural-looking light

**Best For**: Beauty shots, simple portraits, e-commerce

### Two-Light Setup
**Key + Fill**:
- **Key Light**: Octabox at 45°, main illumination
- **Fill Light**: Softbox or umbrella near camera, 50% power
- Reduces shadows, even illumination

**Key + Rim**:
- **Key Light**: Main illumination from front/side
- **Rim/Hair Light**: Behind model, aimed at hair/shoulders
- Creates separation from background, adds dimension

### Three-Point Lighting
**Classic Setup**:
- **Key Light**: Primary illumination (octabox, 45°, 80-100% power)
- **Fill Light**: Opposite key, reduces shadows (softbox, 50% power)
- **Back/Rim Light**: Behind subject, separation and highlights

**Settings**:
- Key: f/8, ISO 100, 1/200s (sync speed)
- Adjust strobe power for proper exposure
- Meter each light independently

### Clamshell Lighting
**Configuration**:
- Two lights (or light + reflector) above and below model's face
- Creates soft, glamorous, even illumination
- Minimizes shadows under eyes and chin

**Best For**: Beauty, headshots, flattering portraits

### Dramatic/Low-Key Lighting
**Setup**:
- Hard light source (beauty dish with grid or bare bulb)
- Positioned for spotlight effect
- Minimal fill light for high contrast

**Best For**: Edgy fashion, emphasizing makeup, dramatic mood

## Natural Light and Location Shooting

### Window Light
**Setup**:
- Position model near large window
- Diffuse with sheer curtain if harsh
- Reflector opposite window for fill

**Angles**:
- **Side Light**: 90° to model, dimensional
- **Front Light**: Model facing window, soft and even
- **Backlight**: Window behind model, rim light effect

### Outdoor Fashion
**Golden Hour**:
- First/last hour of sunlight
- Soft, warm, flattering light
- Long shadows add drama

**Overcast Days**:
- Soft, even light (natural softbox)
- No harsh shadows
- Consistent results

**Direct Sun**:
- Use as backlight or rim light
- Reflector or fill flash for front
- Avoid midday overhead sun (unflattering)

### On-Location Lighting
**Portable Strobes**:
- Battery-powered monolights or speedlights
- Overpower ambient for dramatic effect
- Balance with ambient for natural look

**Reflectors**:
- Bounce sunlight for fill
- Gold for warm glow, silver for bright fill

## Posing and Direction

### Model Communication
**Establish Rapport**:
- Introduce yourself, explain vision
- Make model comfortable and confident
- Provide clear, specific direction
- Give positive feedback

**Effective Direction**:
- Demonstrate poses yourself
- Use reference images
- Give actionable cues ("Chin down slightly," "Shift weight to back foot")
- Encourage movement for natural expressions

### Posing Fundamentals

**Body Positioning**:
- **Weight Shift**: Weight on back foot, front leg relaxed (creates curves)
- **Angles**: Body at 45° to camera (more flattering than straight-on)
- **Hands**: Relaxed, purposeful (avoid awkward hanging)
- **Posture**: Elongated spine, shoulders back

**Facial Expressions**:
- **Jawline**: Slightly forward and down (defines jaw, reduces double chin)
- **Eyes**: Engage with camera or look away (avoid dead stare)
- **Mouth**: Relaxed, slight smile, or editorial pout
- **Catchlights**: Ensure eyes have light reflection (adds life)

**Movement**:
- Walking, turning, hair flips
- Captures natural, dynamic moments
- Creates flow and energy in images

### Fashion-Specific Poses

**Editorial**:
- Dramatic, artistic, unconventional
- Emphasizes mood and story over product
- Creative angles and expressions

**Commercial/E-commerce**:
- Clean, clear view of clothing
- Neutral expressions
- Consistent poses across products
- Focus on garment fit and details

**Lookbook**:
- Lifestyle-oriented
- Shows clothing in context
- Natural, relatable poses

## Camera Settings

**Aperture**:
- **f/2.8-f/5.6**: Shallow depth of field, subject isolation, beautiful bokeh
- **f/8-f/11**: More depth, ensure full outfit in focus
- **f/2 or wider**: Extreme bokeh, artistic portraits

**Shutter Speed**:
- **1/200s**: Flash sync speed (studio strobes)
- **1/125-1/250s**: General fashion photography
- **1/500s+**: Outdoor, bright light, or freezing movement

**ISO**:
- **100-400**: Studio with strobes (ample light)
- **800-1600**: Natural light or low-light scenarios
- Keep as low as possible for clean images

**White Balance**:
- **Custom**: Use gray card for accurate colors
- **Flash/Daylight**: 5500K for strobes
- **Shoot RAW**: Allows adjustment in post

**Focus**:
- **Single Point AF**: Precise control, focus on eyes
- **Face/Eye Detection**: Modern cameras excel at tracking
- **Continuous AF**: For moving models

## Post-Processing

### Color Grading
**Fashion Aesthetic**:
- Clean, polished, high-end look
- Accurate color representation (critical for e-commerce)
- Consistent style across series

**Skin Retouching**:
- **Frequency Separation**: Separate texture from color/tone
- **Blemish Removal**: Heal tool for temporary imperfections
- **Avoid Over-Smoothing**: Maintain skin texture and pores
- **Even Skin Tone**: Correct color casts and blotchiness

**Eyes and Teeth**:
- **Brighten Eyes**: Dodge tool, subtle
- **Enhance Iris**: Clarity and saturation
- **Whiten Teeth**: Desaturate yellows, lighten slightly

### Advanced Techniques
**Dodging and Burning**:
- Lighten (dodge) and darken (burn) specific areas
- Sculpt face and body
- Add dimension and depth

**Liquify Tool**:
- Subtle adjustments to body shape
- Smooth fabric wrinkles
- Refine silhouette
- Use sparingly and ethically

**Color Correction**:
- Match colors across images in series
- Ensure accurate fabric colors
- Consistent white balance

**Sharpening**:
- Moderate sharpening for crisp details
- Focus on eyes, hair, fabric textures
- Avoid over-sharpening (creates halos)
