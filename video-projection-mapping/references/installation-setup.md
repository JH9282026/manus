# Installation Setup

Comprehensive guide to planning, installing, and managing projection mapping installations from small-scale to large productions.

## Equipment Selection

### Projector Selection Criteria

#### Brightness (Lumens)

**Calculation**: Surface area × ambient light factor = required lumens

**Ambient Light Factors**:
- **Pitch Black**: 10-15 lumens/sq ft
- **Dark Room**: 15-25 lumens/sq ft
- **Dim Room**: 25-40 lumens/sq ft
- **Moderate Light**: 40-80 lumens/sq ft
- **Bright Room**: 80-150 lumens/sq ft
- **Outdoor Dusk**: 100-200 lumens/sq ft
- **Outdoor Night (street lights)**: 150-300 lumens/sq ft
- **Daylight**: 300+ lumens/sq ft (often impractical)

**Example**:
- 30 ft wide × 20 ft tall = 600 sq ft
- Outdoor night (200 lumens/sq ft)
- Required: 600 × 200 = 120,000 lumens
- Solution: 4 × 30,000-lumen projectors

**Projector Brightness Categories**:
- **Portable** (2,000-4,000 lumens): Small indoor spaces, dark rooms
- **Installation** (4,000-7,000 lumens): Medium indoor spaces, controlled lighting
- **Large Venue** (7,000-15,000 lumens): Large indoor spaces, auditoriums
- **High-Brightness** (15,000-30,000 lumens): Outdoor, large buildings, bright environments
- **Ultra-High** (30,000+ lumens): Mega-events, massive buildings, extreme conditions

---

#### Resolution

**Common Resolutions**:
- **WXGA** (1280x800): Budget, lower detail
- **HD** (1920x1080): Standard, good detail, most common
- **WUXGA** (1920x1200): Slightly taller than HD, more vertical space
- **4K UHD** (3840x2160): High detail, large surfaces, close viewing
- **4K DCI** (4096x2160): Cinema standard, slightly wider than UHD

**Selection**:
- **Small surfaces, far viewing**: HD sufficient
- **Large surfaces, close viewing**: 4K for detail
- **Text or fine details**: Higher resolution preferred
- **Abstract content**: Lower resolution acceptable

---

#### Throw Ratio and Lens Options

**Throw Ratio**: Distance / Width

**Categories**:
- **Ultra-Short Throw** (0.4:1 or less): Very close to surface, limited distortion
- **Short Throw** (0.5-1.0:1): Close placement, good for tight spaces
- **Standard Throw** (1.2-2.0:1): Moderate distance, versatile
- **Long Throw** (2.0-4.0:1): Far placement, large venues
- **Ultra-Long Throw** (4.0:1+): Very far placement, architectural mapping

**Interchangeable Lenses**:
- Professional projectors offer multiple lens options
- Allows flexibility in placement
- More expensive but versatile

**Lens Shift**:
- Vertical and horizontal shift without moving projector
- Reduces keystone distortion
- Essential for professional installations

---

#### Projector Technology

**LCD** (Liquid Crystal Display):
- **Pros**: Good color accuracy, sharp images, affordable
- **Cons**: Lower contrast than DLP, visible pixel grid (screen door effect)
- **Use**: Budget to mid-range installations

**DLP** (Digital Light Processing):
- **Pros**: High contrast, smooth images, no screen door effect
- **Cons**: Rainbow effect (color separation for some viewers), single-chip models less bright
- **Use**: Professional installations, high-contrast content

**3LCD**:
- **Pros**: Excellent color, high brightness, no rainbow effect
- **Cons**: Larger, heavier, more expensive
- **Use**: Large venues, color-critical applications

**Laser** (Laser Light Source):
- **Pros**: Long lifespan (20,000+ hours), instant on/off, consistent brightness, no lamp replacement
- **Cons**: Higher initial cost
- **Use**: Permanent installations, 24/7 operation, low maintenance

**Lamp-Based**:
- **Pros**: Lower initial cost
- **Cons**: Lamp replacement needed (2,000-5,000 hours), brightness degrades over time, warm-up time
- **Use**: Temporary installations, budget-conscious projects

### Computer Specifications

**Minimum** (Small installations, simple content):
- **CPU**: Intel i5 or AMD Ryzen 5
- **RAM**: 16GB
- **GPU**: NVIDIA GTX 1660 or AMD RX 580 (4GB VRAM)
- **Storage**: 256GB SSD
- **Outputs**: 1-2 HDMI/DisplayPort

**Recommended** (Medium installations, real-time effects):
- **CPU**: Intel i7/i9 or AMD Ryzen 7/9
- **RAM**: 32GB
- **GPU**: NVIDIA RTX 3060/3070 or AMD RX 6700 XT (8GB+ VRAM)
- **Storage**: 512GB NVMe SSD
- **Outputs**: 2-4 HDMI/DisplayPort

**Professional** (Large installations, multi-projector, generative):
- **CPU**: Intel i9 or AMD Ryzen 9 (high core count)
- **RAM**: 64GB+
- **GPU**: NVIDIA RTX 3080/3090/4090 or dual GPUs (12GB+ VRAM each)
- **Storage**: 1TB+ NVMe SSD
- **Outputs**: 4+ outputs (multiple GPUs or professional video cards)

**Operating System**:
- **Windows**: Widest software compatibility
- **macOS**: Preferred for some software (MadMapper, Resolume)
- **Linux**: Limited software support, MapMap compatible

---

### Cables and Connectivity

**HDMI**:
- **Standard**: Up to 50 feet (15m) without signal loss
- **Active**: Up to 100 feet (30m) with built-in signal booster
- **Fiber Optic**: 300+ feet (100m+) for long runs
- **Version**: HDMI 2.0 for 4K@60Hz, HDMI 2.1 for higher resolutions/frame rates

**DisplayPort**:
- **Standard**: Up to 50 feet (15m)
- **Active**: Up to 100 feet (30m)
- **Fiber Optic**: 300+ feet (100m+)
- **Version**: DisplayPort 1.4 for 4K@120Hz or 8K@60Hz

**SDI** (Serial Digital Interface):
- **Professional**: Broadcast standard, very long runs (300+ feet)
- **Versions**: HD-SDI (1080p), 3G-SDI (1080p@60Hz), 6G-SDI (4K@30Hz), 12G-SDI (4K@60Hz)
- **Requires**: SDI output card in computer, SDI input on projector (or converter)

**Wireless**:
- **HDMI Wireless**: Up to 150 feet, potential latency and compression
- **Use**: When cables impractical, accept potential quality loss
- **Not Recommended**: Professional installations (reliability concerns)

**Recommendations**:
- Use shortest cable possible (reduces signal loss)
- Use certified cables (cheap cables cause issues)
- Test cables before installation
- Have backup cables on-site

---

### Mounting and Rigging

**Ceiling Mounts**:
- **Fixed**: Projector in fixed position, adjust manually
- **Adjustable**: Tilt, swivel, extend for positioning
- **Weight Capacity**: Ensure mount supports projector weight + safety margin

**Tripods and Stands**:
- **Portable**: Quick setup, adjustable height
- **Stability**: Use sandbags or weights for stability
- **Height**: Ensure sufficient height for desired throw

**Truss and Rigging**:
- **Professional**: Aluminum truss, rigging hardware
- **Safety**: Use safety cables, follow load ratings
- **Hire Professional**: For overhead rigging, hire certified rigger

**Projector Cages**:
- **Security**: Protect projector from theft or tampering
- **Ventilation**: Ensure adequate airflow (projectors generate heat)

## Installation Planning

### Site Survey

**Measurements**:
- Surface dimensions (width, height, depth if 3D)
- Projector placement options (distance, height, angle)
- Power outlet locations
- Cable run distances

**Photography**:
- Multiple angles of surface
- Daytime and nighttime (if outdoor)
- Surrounding environment
- Potential obstructions

**Ambient Light Assessment**:
- Measure light levels (lux meter or smartphone app)
- Identify light sources (street lights, windows, other fixtures)
- Determine if light can be controlled (turn off, block)

**Access and Logistics**:
- Vehicle access (loading/unloading equipment)
- Stairs, elevators, narrow passages
- Rigging points (ceiling, walls, truss)
- Security (equipment storage, overnight)

**Permits and Permissions**:
- Building owner permission
- City permits (public projections)
- Noise ordinances (if using audio)
- Coordination with authorities (police, fire department)

---

### Throw Distance Calculation

**Formula**: Distance = Throw Ratio × Width

**Example**:
- Surface: 40 feet wide
- Projector: 1.5-2.0:1 throw ratio (zoom lens)
- Min Distance: 1.5 × 40 = 60 feet
- Max Distance: 2.0 × 40 = 80 feet
- Placement: Anywhere between 60-80 feet

**Adjustments**:
- **Lens Shift**: Allows off-center placement without keystone
- **Keystone**: Software correction for angled placement (minimize use)
- **Zoom**: Adjust within throw ratio range

**Tools**:
- Projector manufacturer's throw calculator (online tools)
- Measure tape or laser distance measurer

---

### Power Requirements

**Calculate Total Power**:
- Projector wattage × number of projectors
- Computer wattage
- Additional equipment (audio, lighting, sensors)
- Add 20% safety margin

**Example**:
- 2 projectors × 500W = 1,000W
- Computer: 300W
- Audio: 200W
- Total: 1,500W
- With margin: 1,500 × 1.2 = 1,800W
- Circuit: 1,800W / 120V = 15A (use 20A circuit for safety)

**Power Distribution**:
- Use power strips with surge protection
- Distribute load across multiple circuits if available
- Label all power cables
- Have backup power plan (generator for outdoor events)

---

### Cable Management

**Planning**:
- Measure cable runs (add extra length for routing)
- Identify cable paths (overhead, floor, walls)
- Avoid high-traffic areas (tripping hazard)

**Protection**:
- **Cable Covers**: Rubber or plastic covers for floor cables
- **Tape**: Gaffer tape (not duct tape) for temporary securing
- **Conduit**: PVC or metal conduit for permanent installations
- **Overhead**: Suspend cables overhead when possible

**Organization**:
- Label all cables (source, destination)
- Use cable ties or velcro straps (not zip ties for reusable setups)
- Color-code cables (power = orange, video = blue, etc.)
- Create cable diagram for reference

## Installation Process

### Step 1: Physical Setup

1. **Position Projector(s)**:
   - Mount at calculated throw distance
   - Ensure stable, level mounting
   - Point at center of surface
   - Secure all mounting hardware

2. **Run Cables**:
   - Power cables to projectors and computer
   - Video cables from computer to projectors
   - Secure and protect cables
   - Test all connections

3. **Set Up Computer**:
   - Position in accessible location (for control)
   - Connect to power and projectors
   - Ensure adequate ventilation (computers generate heat)

4. **Safety Check**:
   - Verify all mounts are secure
   - Test safety cables (if overhead rigging)
   - Check for tripping hazards
   - Ensure fire exits not blocked

---

### Step 2: Computer Configuration

**Display Settings** (Windows):
1. Right-click desktop > Display Settings
2. Detect projectors (click "Detect")
3. Set projectors as "Extend" (not "Duplicate")
4. Arrange displays (drag icons to match physical layout)
5. Set resolution for each projector (match native resolution)
6. Apply settings

**Display Settings** (macOS):
1. System Preferences > Displays
2. Arrangement tab
3. Uncheck "Mirror Displays"
4. Arrange displays (drag icons to match physical layout)
5. Set resolution for each display
6. Enable "Displays have separate Spaces" in Mission Control (for full-screen apps)

**Projector Settings**:
- Set aspect ratio (16:9, 16:10, etc.)
- Disable keystone correction (use software instead)
- Set to "Presentation" or "Bright" mode (maximum brightness)
- Disable eco mode (reduces brightness)
- Set color temperature (match across all projectors)

---

### Step 3: Software Setup

1. **Launch Mapping Software**:
   - Open MadMapper, Resolume, HeavyM, etc.
   - Configure output settings (select projector displays)

2. **Import Content**:
   - Load video files, images, or generative patches
   - Organize in library or timeline

3. **Create Mapping Surfaces**:
   - Add surfaces (quads, meshes, Bezier shapes)
   - Roughly position to match projection surface

4. **Assign Content to Surfaces**:
   - Drag content onto surfaces
   - Adjust content properties (scale, position, rotation)

---

### Step 4: Calibration

1. **Project Test Pattern**:
   - Use grid, checkerboard, or high-contrast image
   - Ensure pattern visible on entire surface

2. **Adjust Projector Focus and Zoom**:
   - Focus for sharp image
   - Zoom to cover desired area
   - Lock focus and zoom rings (prevent drift)

3. **Warp Mapping Surfaces**:
   - Adjust corner points to match physical surface edges
   - Add control points for curved edges
   - Fine-tune alignment (zoom in on software for precision)

4. **Multi-Projector Blending** (if applicable):
   - Define overlap zones
   - Apply edge blending
   - Adjust blend width and gamma
   - Color match projectors

5. **Verify Alignment**:
   - Test with actual content (not just test pattern)
   - View from multiple angles
   - Make final adjustments

6. **Save Calibration**:
   - Save project file
   - Export calibration data (if software supports)
   - Document settings (photos, notes)

---

### Step 5: Content Playback and Testing

1. **Load Final Content**:
   - Import all content files
   - Organize in timeline or cue list

2. **Program Playback**:
   - Set up timeline, cues, or loops
   - Configure control (keyboard, MIDI, OSC, timecode)

3. **Test Playback**:
   - Play through entire show/installation
   - Check for frame rate issues, stuttering
   - Verify audio sync (if applicable)
   - Test all control inputs

4. **Adjust Brightness and Color**:
   - Fine-tune brightness, contrast, saturation
   - Ensure visibility in actual ambient light conditions
   - Make content adjustments if needed

5. **Rehearse** (for live events):
   - Run through show with performers/presenters
   - Practice cue timing
   - Identify and fix issues

---

## Interactive System Setup

### Motion Tracking (Kinect, RealSense)

**Hardware**:
- Microsoft Kinect (v1, v2, or Azure)
- Intel RealSense (D435, D455, L515)
- Mount camera with view of interaction area

**Software**:
- TouchDesigner (Kinect CHOP, RealSense TOP)
- Isadora (Kinect actor)
- Processing (libraries for Kinect, RealSense)

**Setup**:
1. Connect sensor to computer (USB)
2. Install drivers and SDKs
3. In software, add sensor input
4. Calibrate sensor (depth range, tracking area)
5. Map sensor data to visual parameters
6. Test interaction (adjust sensitivity, response)

**Applications**:
- Particles follow or avoid people
- Trigger animations when someone approaches
- Create virtual shadows or reflections
- Interactive games or experiences

---

### Touch Detection

**Methods**:

**1. Infrared Touch Frame**:
- Frame around surface with IR emitters and receivers
- Detects touch by interruption of IR beams
- Accurate, multi-touch capable
- Expensive, requires custom fabrication

**2. Computer Vision**:
- Camera detects touch via image analysis
- Requires good lighting and contrast
- Software: TouchDesigner, Processing, OpenCV
- Affordable, flexible, but less accurate

**3. Capacitive Surface**:
- Conductive material detects touch
- Accurate, responsive
- Requires custom surface fabrication
- Limited to conductive materials

**Setup** (Computer Vision):
1. Mount camera with view of surface
2. Calibrate camera (background subtraction, threshold)
3. Detect touch points (blob detection)
4. Map touch coordinates to projection coordinates
5. Trigger content based on touch location

---

### Audio Reactivity

**Input**:
- Microphone (ambient sound)
- Audio interface (music playback)
- Line input (DJ mixer, audio system)

**Software**:
- Resolume Arena (audio analysis)
- HeavyM (music sync)
- TouchDesigner (Audio Analysis CHOP)
- MadMapper (audio input)

**Setup**:
1. Connect audio input to computer
2. In software, enable audio analysis
3. Map audio features to visual parameters:
   - **Volume**: Overall loudness controls brightness, scale
   - **Bass**: Low frequencies control color, movement
   - **Mids**: Mid frequencies control effects, patterns
   - **Highs**: High frequencies control particles, details
   - **Beats**: Beat detection triggers animations, flashes
4. Adjust sensitivity and response
5. Test with actual audio (music, ambient sound)

## Large-Scale Production Management

### Team Roles

**Creative Director**: Overall vision, concept, artistic direction

**Technical Director**: Technical planning, equipment selection, troubleshooting

**Content Creator**: Design and produce video content

**Projection Mapper**: Calibration, alignment, software operation

**Rigger**: Install and secure projectors, truss, mounting (certified)

**Electrician**: Power distribution, electrical safety (licensed)

**Audio Engineer**: Sound design, audio system setup (if applicable)

**Production Manager**: Logistics, scheduling, coordination

---

### Timeline and Scheduling

**Pre-Production** (weeks to months before):
- Concept development
- Site survey
- Equipment selection and rental/purchase
- Content creation
- Permits and permissions

**Load-In** (day before or day of):
- Transport equipment to site
- Install projectors and rigging
- Run cables and power
- Set up computer and software

**Technical Rehearsal**:
- Calibration and alignment
- Content loading and testing
- Troubleshooting
- Rehearse with performers (if applicable)

**Show/Installation**:
- Final checks
- Run show or activate installation
- Monitor for issues
- Make adjustments as needed

**Load-Out** (after event):
- Power down equipment
- Dismantle and pack
- Transport back to storage
- Return rentals

---

### Redundancy and Backup

**Critical Systems**:
- **Projectors**: Have backup projector on-site (or spare lamp)
- **Computer**: Backup computer with identical setup
- **Content**: Multiple copies on different drives
- **Cables**: Spare cables for all connections
- **Power**: Backup power source (generator, UPS)

**Failover Plan**:
1. Identify critical failure points
2. Prepare backup for each
3. Test failover procedure
4. Document steps for quick recovery
5. Assign team member to manage backups

---

### Documentation

**Technical Documentation**:
- Equipment list (make, model, serial numbers)
- Cable diagram (connections, lengths)
- Power distribution plan
- Projector settings (brightness, color, position)
- Software settings (calibration, content, cues)

**Visual Documentation**:
- Photos of setup (equipment, cables, rigging)
- Photos of projection (multiple angles, day/night)
- Video recording of show/installation
- Screenshots of software (calibration, timeline)

**Post-Event Report**:
- What worked well
- Issues encountered and solutions
- Lessons learned
- Recommendations for future projects

## Troubleshooting On-Site

### No Image from Projector

**Check**:
- [ ] Projector powered on
- [ ] Lens cap removed
- [ ] Input source selected (HDMI 1, HDMI 2, etc.)
- [ ] Cable connected securely (both ends)
- [ ] Computer display settings (projector enabled, not disabled)
- [ ] Try different cable or port

---

### Image Misaligned

**Check**:
- [ ] Projector moved or vibrated
- [ ] Zoom or focus ring shifted
- [ ] Software calibration changed
- [ ] Reload saved calibration
- [ ] Recalibrate if necessary

---

### Low Frame Rate / Stuttering

**Check**:
- [ ] Close unnecessary applications
- [ ] Reduce content resolution or effects
- [ ] Check CPU/GPU usage (Task Manager / Activity Monitor)
- [ ] Ensure computer not overheating (check fans, ventilation)
- [ ] Use HAP codec instead of H.264
- [ ] Reduce number of simultaneous layers

---

### Projector Overheating

**Check**:
- [ ] Ensure adequate ventilation (not blocked)
- [ ] Clean air filters (if accessible)
- [ ] Reduce ambient temperature (fans, AC)
- [ ] Reduce brightness (eco mode) if possible
- [ ] Allow projector to cool down periodically

---

### Audio Not Syncing

**Check**:
- [ ] Audio input connected and enabled
- [ ] Audio buffer settings (reduce for lower latency)
- [ ] Frame rate match (audio and video)
- [ ] Software audio analysis settings
- [ ] Test with different audio source

---
