# 360° Video & VR Video Production

Create immersive 360° and VR video experiences from capture through distribution.

## Overview

This skill covers the complete 360°/VR video production pipeline: equipment selection, capture techniques, stitching workflows, spatial audio, VR storytelling principles, and platform distribution. Use it for virtual tours, immersive documentaries, VR training, marketing experiences, and any spherical video project.

## Quick Start: Format Selection

| Project Type | Format | Capture Method | Distribution |
|-------------|--------|----------------|--------------|
| Virtual tours | Mono 360° | Consumer/prosumer camera | Web, mobile |
| Immersive documentary | Mono/stereo 360° | Professional camera | YouTube VR, platforms |
| VR training | Stereo 360° + interactive | Multi-camera rig + hotspots | Enterprise VR |
| Premium brand experience | Stereo 360° | Professional setup | Headset apps |
| Social media | Mono 360° | Consumer camera | Facebook, YouTube |

## The Immersive Spectrum

| Content Type | Viewer Freedom | Interactivity | Hardware |
|-------------|----------------|---------------|----------|
| Traditional Video | None | None | Any screen |
| 360° Monoscopic | Rotational | View direction | Phone, headset |
| 360° Stereoscopic | Rotational + depth | View direction | VR headset |
| Room-Scale VR | Full movement | High | VR headset + tracking |
| Volumetric Video | Movement within scene | Medium-High | VR headset |

## Monoscopic vs. Stereoscopic Decision

| Factor | Monoscopic | Stereoscopic |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Budget | Lower | Higher |
| Workflow | Simpler | Complex |
| Distribution | Universal (web, mobile, headset) | Headset-focused |
| Immersion | Good | Excellent |
| File Size | Smaller | Larger |
| Production Speed | Faster | Slower |
| Best For | Web, social, mass distribution | Premium VR, headset experiences |

**Choose Monoscopic when**: Budget is limited, need web/mobile distribution, simpler workflow preferred.

**Choose Stereoscopic when**: Depth perception critical, headset-only distribution acceptable, premium experience required.

## Core Production Workflow

### 1. Pre-Production

- Scout locations with 360° camera to test
- Storyboard all 360° of each scene
- Map viewer attention flow
- Plan crew hiding positions
- Identify natural lighting conditions

### 2. Capture Settings

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Resolution | 5.7K minimum, 8K+ preferred |
| Frame rate | 30fps standard, 60fps for smooth motion |
| Exposure | Manual, balanced across sphere |
| White balance | Manual, consistent throughout |

### 3. On-Set Essentials

- **Camera position** = viewer position (eye level default: 5-6 feet)
- **Minimum subject distance**: 4-6 feet (avoid fisheye distortion)
- **Hide crew/equipment**: Everything in sphere is visible
- **Lighting**: Use practical lights in scene, no off-camera stands
- **Audio**: Spatial mic at camera position

### 4. Post-Production Pipeline

1. Ingest and organize footage
2. Stitch (in-camera or desktop software)
3. Edit in 360°-capable NLE (Premiere Pro, Final Cut, DaVinci)
4. Color grade
5. Spatial audio sync and mix
6. Stabilize if needed
7. Export with correct metadata
8. Test on target devices

## Directing Attention Without Framing

In 360° video, viewers control where they look. Guide attention using:

| Technique | How It Works |
|-----------|--------------|
| Light/contrast | Brightest area attracts attention |
| Movement | Motion captures peripheral vision |
| Color saturation | Saturated colors stand out |
| Spatial audio | Sound from a direction triggers head turn |
| Direct address | Characters speaking to/looking at viewer |
| Blocking | Stage action in predictable viewing cone |

**Default view**: Assume viewers start facing forward. Place important initial content there.

**Avoid FOMO**: Don't put critical simultaneous action in opposite directions.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Camera too close to subjects | Distortion, discomfort | Maintain 4-6 ft minimum |
| Excessive camera movement | Motion sickness | Static or slow, smooth motion |
| Visible equipment/crew | Breaks immersion | Remote control, plan hiding |
| Poor stitch quality | Visible seams, artifacts | Check all angles, proper software |
| Wrong/missing metadata | Won't play as 360° | Use proper export/injection tools |
| Mono audio only | Breaks spatial reality | Capture and mix spatial audio |
| Too many fast cuts | Disorientation | Longer takes, smooth transitions |
