## Essential Accessories

### Mounting Equipment

**Monopods (Primary)**
- Keeps tripod out of nadir (bottom of sphere)
- Invisible selfie stick option
- Extension poles for height
- Light stand adapters

**Tripods (Secondary)**
- Specialized low-profile designs
- Accept nadir patching in post
- Use when stability critical
- Hide with logo/graphic overlay

**Specialty Mounts**
- Suction mounts (vehicles, windows)
- Helmet mounts (POV)
- Clamps (rails, pipes)
- Drone mounts (regulated)

### Audio Equipment

**Spatial Audio Microphones**
- Zoom H3-VR (ambisonic, affordable)
- Sennheiser AMBEO VR Mic (professional)
- Rode NT-SF1 (high quality)
- Core Sound TetraMic (compact)

**Supplementary Audio**
- Lavalier microphones (hidden on talent)
- Boom microphones (for specific sources)
- External recorders (backup, quality)

### Stabilization

**In-Camera**
- Most modern 360° cameras include stabilization
- FlowState (Insta360)
- HyperSmooth (GoPro)

**Post-Production**
- Software stabilization available
- Horizon leveling
- Smooth camera movement correction

**Physical**
- Invisible selfie sticks (monopod that disappears)
- Limited gimbal options (360° gimbals rare)

### Other Essential Items

- Lens protectors (360° cameras are exposed)
- Microfiber cleaning cloths
- Lens cleaning solution
- Remote triggers/smartphone app
- Extra batteries (high power consumption)
- High-speed memory cards (V60/V90 class)
- Card reader for fast offload
- Hard cases for transport

## Technical Specifications to Consider

### Resolution

- **5.7K**: Minimum for quality results
- **8K**: Preferred for professional work
- **Higher**: Future-proofing, cropping flexibility

Note: 360° distributes pixels across entire sphere, so effective resolution when viewing any direction is much lower than stated resolution.

### Sensor Size

- Larger sensors = better low-light performance
- 1-inch sensors (prosumer) significantly outperform smaller sensors
- Consider shooting environment lighting conditions

### Bitrate

- Higher bitrate = better quality, larger files
- 100+ Mbps for professional work
- Consider storage and transfer implications

### Dynamic Range

- Important for mixed lighting (bright sky, dark interior)
- HDR modes help but may introduce artifacts
- Consider lighting control vs. dynamic range needs

### Audio

- Built-in mics often inadequate for professional work
- External spatial audio highly recommended
- Check input options (3.5mm, USB, XLR)
