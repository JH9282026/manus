# 360° Camera Systems

Comprehensive guide to 360° camera hardware for different production needs.

---

## Professional Multi-Camera Rigs

### Google Jump / GoPro Odyssey
- **Configuration**: 16 GoPro cameras in circular array
- **Resolution**: 8K stereoscopic
- **Pros**: High quality, proven workflow
- **Cons**: Discontinued, complex setup

### Insta360 Titan
- **Sensors**: 8 × Micro Four Thirds sensors
- **Resolution**: 11K monoscopic, 10K 3D
- **Frame Rate**: Up to 30fps at max resolution
- **Dynamic Range**: 10 stops
- **Best For**: Cinematic VR, high-end productions

### Z CAM V1 Pro
- **Sensors**: 9 × synchronized 4K sensors  
- **Resolution**: 8K+ after stitching
- **Workflow**: Professional cinema pipeline
- **Price Range**: $15,000+

---

## Prosumer Cameras

### Insta360 Pro 2
- **Resolution**: 8K 3D at 30fps
- **Features**: FlowState stabilization, live monitoring
- **Stitching**: Built-in or Insta360 Stitcher
- **Price**: ~$5,000
- **Best For**: Professional 360 video production

### Kandao Obsidian R/S
- **Resolution**: 8K (R model), 6K 3D (S model)
- **Sensors**: 6 cameras with overlap
- **Features**: Real-time preview, RAW capture
- **Price**: $3,500-$7,000

### QooCam 8K Enterprise
- **Resolution**: 8K at 30fps
- **Sensors**: Dual 20MP sensors
- **Features**: 10-bit color, internal stabilization
- **Price**: ~$1,500

## Consumer 360 Cameras

### Insta360 One X3
- **Resolution**: 5.7K at 30fps, 4K at 60fps
- **Sensors**: Dual 1/2" sensors
- **Features**: Invisible selfie stick, FlowState
- **Price**: ~$450
- **Best For**: Action, social content

### GoPro MAX
- **Resolution**: 5.6K at 30fps
- **Features**: MAX HyperSmooth, PowerPano
- **Waterproof**: 5 meters
- **Price**: ~$500
- **Best For**: Action sports, travel

### Ricoh Theta Z1
- **Resolution**: 7K photo, 4K video
- **Sensors**: Dual 1" sensors
- **Features**: RAW capture, manual controls
- **Price**: ~$1,000
- **Best For**: Real estate, architecture

### Ricoh Theta X
- **Resolution**: 5.7K video, 11K photo
- **Features**: Touchscreen, modular battery
- **Price**: ~$800
- **Best For**: Virtual tours, events

## Sensor and Lens Considerations

### Sensor Size Impact
| Sensor Size | Low Light | Dynamic Range | Depth of Field |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| 1/2.3" | Poor | 8 stops | Deep |
| 1" | Moderate | 10 stops | Moderate |
| Micro 4/3 | Good | 12 stops | Shallow option |
| APS-C/FF | Excellent | 14+ stops | Creative control |

### Lens Configurations
- **Dual fisheye**: Most common, simple stitching
- **4+ camera array**: Better parallax handling
- **Catadioptric**: Single-shot, limited resolution

## Accessories and Support

### Essential Accessories
1. **Monopod**: Invisible stick for dynamic shots
2. **Tripod with small footprint**: Minimize nadir patch
3. **Extension pole**: Overhead/low angles
4. **ND filters**: Exposure control outdoors
5. **Protective case**: Lens protection critical

### Monitoring Solutions
- **Insta360**: Mobile app live preview
- **Pro cameras**: Dedicated monitors, HDMI out
- **VR headset preview**: Real-time VR monitoring

## Camera Selection Decision Tree

1. **Budget under $500?** → Insta360 One X3 or GoPro MAX
2. **Need 8K resolution?** → Insta360 Pro 2 or QooCam 8K
3. **Stereoscopic 3D required?** → Insta360 Titan or Kandao QooCam 3
4. **Real estate/tours?** → Ricoh Theta Z1/X or Matterport
5. **Cinema production?** → Multi-camera rig or Insta360 Titan
6. **Action/adventure?** → GoPro MAX (waterproof) or Insta360 One X3
