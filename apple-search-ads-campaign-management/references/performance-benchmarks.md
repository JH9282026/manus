# Apple Search Ads Performance Benchmarks

Industry benchmarks, performance targets, and KPI guidelines for evaluating Apple Search Ads campaign performance across different app categories and campaign types.

---

## Important Disclaimer

Performance benchmarks vary significantly based on:
- **App category** (games vs. utilities vs. productivity, etc.)
- **App quality** (ratings, reviews, ASO strength)
- **Competition level** (saturated vs. emerging categories)
- **Geographic market** (US vs. international)
- **Seasonality** (holidays, back-to-school, etc.)
- **App monetization** (free, freemium, paid, subscription)
- **User lifetime value** (LTV)

Use these benchmarks as **directional guidance**, not absolute targets. Your specific performance will vary.

## ROI and LTV Benchmarks

### Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) Targets

| App Monetization Model | Minimum ROAS | Good ROAS | Excellent ROAS |
|------------------------|--------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Free (Ad-Supported)** | 1.5x | 3x | 5x+ |
| **Freemium (IAP)** | 2x | 4x | 6x+ |
| **Paid App** | 3x | 5x | 8x+ |
| **Subscription** | 2x (30-day) | 4x (30-day) | 6x+ (30-day) |

**Calculation:**
- ROAS = Revenue from Acquired Users ÷ Ad Spend
- For subscriptions, use 30-day, 90-day, or LTV depending on business model

### Acceptable CPA Based on LTV

**Rule of Thumb:**
- **CPA should be ≤ 30% of LTV** for sustainable growth
- **CPA should be ≤ 50% of LTV** for aggressive growth
- **CPA > 50% of LTV** is typically unprofitable long-term

**Example:**
- LTV = $20
- Target CPA = $6 (30% of LTV)
- Maximum CPA = $10 (50% of LTV)

### Payback Period Targets

| Business Model | Target Payback Period |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **Free (Ad-Supported)** | 30-60 days |
| **Freemium (IAP)** | 60-90 days |
| **Subscription** | 90-180 days |
| **Paid App** | Immediate (on install) |

**Payback Period** = Time until revenue from user equals acquisition cost

## Performance Improvement Timeline

### What to Expect Over Time

**Week 1:**
- Initial data collection
- High variability
- Don't make major changes yet

**Week 2-4:**
- Performance stabilizes
- Begin optimization (add negatives, adjust bids)
- Identify initial winners and losers

**Month 2:**
- Clearer performance trends
- Scale winning keywords/campaigns
- Pause or optimize losing elements

**Month 3+:**
- Mature campaigns
- Continuous optimization
- Expect 20-40% improvement in CPA from Month 1 to Month 3 with active optimization

## Red Flags: When Performance Needs Attention

### Low Impressions
- **Benchmark:** <100 impressions/day for brand campaign
- **Likely Issues:** Bids too low, keywords not relevant, ASO weak, budget too low

### Low Tap-Through Rate
- **Benchmark:** <1% for any campaign
- **Likely Issues:** Ad not compelling, keywords not relevant, poor ASO (icon, screenshots)

### Low Conversion Rate
- **Benchmark:** <5% for any campaign
- **Likely Issues:** Poor product page, app not relevant to keywords, bad ratings/reviews, pricing issues

### High Cost Per Tap
- **Benchmark:** >$3 for most categories (except finance, games)
- **Likely Issues:** Overbidding, high competition, keywords too broad

### High Cost Per Acquisition
- **Benchmark:** >50% of LTV
- **Likely Issues:** Poor conversion rate, high CPT, keywords not relevant, need optimization

## Using Benchmarks Effectively

### Do:
- Use as directional guidance
- Compare your performance to relevant category benchmarks
- Track trends over time (am I improving?)
- Set realistic goals based on benchmarks
- Adjust expectations based on your specific situation

### Don't:
- Treat as absolute targets
- Compare across very different app categories
- Panic if you're not hitting "excellent" benchmarks immediately
- Ignore your specific business model and LTV
- Make major changes based on short-term data

### Key Principle:
**The only benchmark that truly matters is profitability.**

If your CPA is higher than industry average but you're still profitable (CPA < LTV with acceptable payback period), you're succeeding. Conversely, if your CPA is below average but still unprofitable for your business model, you need to improve.

Focus on:
1. **Profitability:** CPA ≤ 30-50% of LTV
2. **Improvement:** Are metrics improving over time?
3. **Relative Performance:** How do different campaigns/keywords compare to each other?
4. **Business Goals:** Are you hitting your growth and revenue targets?
