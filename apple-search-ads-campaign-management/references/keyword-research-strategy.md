# Apple Search Ads Keyword Research Strategy

Comprehensive guide to conducting keyword research, organizing keywords, mining search terms, and building effective keyword strategies for Apple Search Ads campaigns.

---

## Keyword Research Fundamentals

### Why Keyword Research Matters

Keyword research is the foundation of successful Apple Search Ads campaigns. Unlike traditional search advertising, App Store search behavior is unique:

- **Short queries:** Users typically search with 1-3 words
- **High intent:** App Store searches indicate strong download intent
- **Limited inventory:** Fewer ad positions than web search
- **Relevance-gated:** Ads won't show if app isn't relevant, regardless of bid
- **ASO synergy:** Keyword research informs both paid and organic strategies

### Keyword Categories

**1. Brand Keywords**
- App name and variations
- Publisher/company name
- Branded features or characters
- Misspellings of brand terms
- **Priority:** Highest (protect brand searches)
- **Volume:** Lower but highest conversion rates
- **Competition:** Low (unless competitors bid on your brand)

**2. Category Keywords (Generic)**
- App category terms (e.g., "photo editor", "fitness tracker")
- Feature-based terms (e.g., "calorie counter", "video filters")
- Use case terms (e.g., "workout at home", "edit selfies")
- Benefit-based terms (e.g., "lose weight", "improve photos")
- **Priority:** High (primary growth driver)
- **Volume:** High
- **Competition:** High

**3. Competitor Keywords**
- Direct competitor app names
- Competitor brand names
- Competitor feature names
- **Priority:** Medium (conquest strategy)
- **Volume:** Medium
- **Competition:** Very high
- **Note:** Requires strong relevance to show ads

**4. Discovery Keywords**
- Broad category terms
- Related interest areas
- Adjacent use cases
- **Priority:** Medium (research and expansion)
- **Volume:** Variable
- **Competition:** Variable

## Keyword Research Sources

### 1. App Store Search Suggestions

**Method:**
- Open App Store on iOS device
- Type seed keywords in search bar
- Note autocomplete suggestions
- Tap search and review "Related Searches" at bottom
- Explore suggested apps and their metadata

**Best Practices:**
- Use incognito/new device to avoid personalized results
- Test variations and synonyms
- Note search volume indicators (suggestions appear for popular terms)
- Document exact phrasing of suggestions

### 2. App Store Connect Search Ads Suggestions

**Method:**
- Log into App Store Connect
- Navigate to Search Ads section
- Review Apple's keyword recommendations for your app
- Export suggested keywords list

**Benefits:**
- Apple's algorithm suggests relevant keywords
- Based on app metadata and category
- Includes relevance scoring
- Good starting point for new campaigns

### 3. Competitor Analysis

**Method:**
- Identify top 10-20 competitors in your category
- Review their app titles (most important for keywords)
- Analyze their subtitles (30 characters, high weight)
- Study their screenshots (text in screenshots can indicate key features)
- Read their descriptions (lower weight but shows positioning)
- Note their category selections

**Tools:**
- Manual review in App Store
- Third-party ASO tools (App Annie, Sensor Tower, AppTweak, Mobile Action)
- Competitor keyword tracking tools

**Extract:**
- Keywords in competitor titles
- Keywords in competitor subtitles
- Feature terms competitors emphasize
- Use cases competitors target
- Competitor brand names (for conquest campaigns)

### 4. App Store Category Exploration

**Method:**
- Browse your app's category in App Store
- Review top charts (free, paid, grossing)
- Note common themes in top app titles
- Identify category-defining keywords
- Explore subcategories

**Insights:**
- Category-standard terminology
- Emerging trends in category
- Seasonal keyword opportunities
- Niche subcategory keywords

### 5. Search Terms Report Mining

**Method (After Campaign Launch):**
- Run campaigns with Search Match and Broad Match
- Wait 1-2 weeks for data accumulation
- Export Search Terms Report from Apple Ads dashboard
- Analyze actual search queries triggering ads
- Identify high-performing terms to add as exact match keywords
- Identify irrelevant terms to add as negative keywords

**Metrics to Analyze:**
- Impressions (search volume indicator)
- Taps (interest indicator)
- Tap-Through Rate (relevance indicator)
- Installs (conversion indicator)
- Conversion Rate (quality indicator)
- Cost Per Acquisition (efficiency indicator)

**Actions:**
- Add high-volume, high-conversion terms as exact match keywords
- Add medium-performing terms as broad match keywords
- Add irrelevant terms as negative keywords
- Pause or exclude low-performing terms

### 6. Customer Research

**Method:**
- Survey existing users: "How would you search for an app like this?"
- Review app store reviews for language users use
- Analyze customer support tickets for common questions/needs
- Monitor social media mentions for terminology
- Conduct user interviews about app discovery

**Insights:**
- Real user language (vs. industry jargon)
- Pain points users are trying to solve
- Alternative use cases you hadn't considered
- Regional/demographic language variations

### 7. Keyword Research Tools

**Third-Party ASO Tools:**
- **Sensor Tower:** Keyword research, competitor tracking, search volume estimates
- **App Annie (data.ai):** Market intelligence, keyword suggestions
- **AppTweak:** Keyword optimization, ASO insights
- **Mobile Action:** Keyword tracking, competitor analysis
- **SearchMan:** Keyword research and tracking

**Features:**
- Search volume estimates (directional, not exact)
- Keyword difficulty scores
- Competitor keyword tracking
- Keyword suggestions based on app
- Trending keywords in category

**Limitations:**
- Apple doesn't provide exact search volume data
- Estimates are directional, not precise
- Tools use proprietary algorithms (vary by tool)
- Paid subscriptions required for full features

### 8. Google Trends and Web Search Data

**Method:**
- Use Google Trends to identify trending topics
- Research web search keywords related to app category
- Adapt web keywords to mobile/app context

**Considerations:**
- Web search behavior differs from App Store search
- Shorter queries in App Store
- More action-oriented in App Store
- Use as inspiration, not direct translation

## Keyword Organization Framework

### Campaign-Level Organization

**Brand Campaign**
- All brand-related keywords
- Protects brand searches from competitors
- Highest priority, aggressive bidding

**Category Campaign**
- Generic, non-branded keywords
- Organized into themed ad groups
- Primary growth driver

**Competitor Campaign**
- Competitor brand names and app names
- Conquest strategy
- Monitor relevance and ROI closely

**Discovery Campaign**
- Search Match (no keywords, auto-matching)
- Broad match keywords for exploration
- Keyword research and expansion

### Ad Group-Level Organization (Within Category Campaign)

**Theme-Based Ad Groups:**

Organize keywords into tightly themed ad groups for better performance tracking and optimization:

**Example: Fitness App**

*Ad Group: Workout Tracking*
- workout tracker
- fitness tracker
- exercise log
- workout log
- training tracker

*Ad Group: Calorie Counting*
- calorie counter
- calorie tracker
- food diary
- nutrition tracker
- diet tracker

*Ad Group: Weight Loss*
- lose weight
- weight loss app
- diet app
- weight tracker
- weight loss tracker

*Ad Group: Home Workouts*
- home workout
- workout at home
- home fitness
- bodyweight workout
- no equipment workout

**Benefits of Theme-Based Organization:**
- Easier performance analysis by theme
- More relevant ad creative per theme (if using custom product pages)
- Better bid management (adjust bids by theme performance)
- Clearer insights into which features/use cases drive installs
- Easier to scale winning themes

### Match Type Organization

**Separate Ad Groups by Match Type:**

**Exact Match Ad Group**
- Proven high-performers
- Brand keywords
- Precise control
- Aggressive bidding

**Broad Match Ad Group**
- Discovery and reach
- Category keywords
- Moderate bidding
- Monitor for irrelevant traffic

**Search Match Ad Group**
- Automatic matching
- No keywords needed
- Moderate bidding
- Mine for new keyword ideas

**Why Separate?**
- Different bidding strategies per match type
- Easier performance analysis
- Prevents broad match from cannibalizing exact match budget
- Clearer attribution of performance

## Keyword Selection Criteria

### Relevance (Most Important)

**Questions to Ask:**
- Does this keyword accurately describe my app?
- Would a user searching this term find my app useful?
- Is my app competitive for this search query?
- Does my app metadata support this keyword?

**Relevance Check:**
- Search the keyword in App Store
- Review top 10 results
- Assess if your app fits among these results
- If your app seems out of place, keyword may not be relevant enough

**Apple's Relevance Gate:**
- Ads won't show if app isn't relevant to search query
- High bid doesn't override low relevance
- Strong ASO improves relevance scoring
- Keyword must appear in app metadata (title, subtitle, keyword field) or be semantically related

### Search Volume

**Indicators:**
- Appears in App Store autocomplete suggestions (high volume)
- Appears in "Related Searches" (medium-high volume)
- Estimated volume from third-party tools (directional)
- Impressions in Search Terms Report (actual data)

**Strategy:**
- **High volume keywords:** Competitive, expensive, but scalable
- **Medium volume keywords:** Sweet spot for many apps (less competition, still meaningful volume)
- **Low volume keywords:** Long-tail, cheap, highly targeted (good for niche apps)

**Balance:**
- Include mix of high, medium, and low volume keywords
- High volume for scale
- Medium volume for efficiency
- Low volume for precision and cost control

### Competition Level

**Assessment:**
- Search keyword in App Store
- Count number of ads shown (high competition = multiple ads)
- Review quality of competing apps (strong competitors = harder to win)
- Check suggested bid in Apple Ads (high suggested bid = high competition)

**Strategy:**
- **High competition:** Requires higher bids, strong ASO, compelling creative
- **Medium competition:** Opportunity for efficient growth
- **Low competition:** Easier to win, but verify sufficient search volume

### Conversion Potential

**High Conversion Indicators:**
- Specific, action-oriented keywords (e.g., "calorie counter" vs "health")
- Keywords indicating clear intent (e.g., "photo editor" vs "photos")
- Keywords matching core app functionality
- Keywords used by converting users (from Search Terms Report)

**Low Conversion Indicators:**
- Very broad, generic keywords (e.g., "app", "free", "games")
- Informational keywords (e.g., "what is", "how to")
- Keywords tangentially related to app

## Keyword Research Process

### Step 1: Brainstorm Seed Keywords (30 minutes)

1. List your app's core features (5-10)
2. List use cases/problems your app solves (5-10)
3. List your app category and subcategories
4. List your main competitors (5-10)
5. List your brand terms (app name, company name, variations)

**Output:** 30-50 seed keywords

### Step 2: Expand with Research Sources (2-3 hours)

1. **App Store Search Suggestions:**
   - Enter each seed keyword in App Store search
   - Document autocomplete suggestions
   - Note "Related Searches"
   - Compile list (aim for 100-200 keywords)

2. **Competitor Analysis:**
   - Review top 10 competitor app titles and subtitles
   - Extract keywords (aim for 50-100 keywords)

3. **App Store Connect Suggestions:**
   - Export Apple's keyword recommendations
   - Add to master list (aim for 50-100 keywords)

4. **Third-Party Tools (if available):**
   - Run keyword research in ASO tool
   - Export suggestions (aim for 100-200 keywords)

**Output:** 300-600 total keywords

### Step 3: Categorize and Organize (1-2 hours)

1. **Remove Duplicates:**
   - Consolidate identical keywords
   - Keep variations (singular/plural, different word order)

2. **Categorize by Type:**
   - Brand keywords (separate list)
   - Category keywords (separate list)
   - Competitor keywords (separate list)
   - Discovery keywords (separate list)

3. **Organize Category Keywords by Theme:**
   - Group related keywords together
   - Create 5-15 themed groups
   - Each group should have 5-20 keywords

**Output:** Organized keyword lists by campaign and theme

### Step 4: Prioritize and Filter (1 hour)

1. **Assess Relevance:**
   - Remove keywords not relevant to app
   - Flag borderline keywords for testing

2. **Assess Search Volume:**
   - Prioritize keywords appearing in autocomplete
   - Flag low-volume keywords (may still be valuable for long-tail)

3. **Assess Competition:**
   - Note highly competitive keywords (require higher bids)
   - Identify opportunity keywords (medium competition, good relevance)

4. **Create Priority Tiers:**
   - **Tier 1 (High Priority):** High relevance, medium-high volume, manageable competition
   - **Tier 2 (Medium Priority):** Good relevance, variable volume, test and optimize
   - **Tier 3 (Low Priority/Test):** Borderline relevance, low volume, or very high competition

**Output:** Prioritized keyword lists ready for campaign setup

### Step 5: Compile Negative Keywords (30 minutes)

1. **Brainstorm Irrelevant Terms:**
   - Keywords that might trigger ads but aren't relevant
   - Competitor names (if not running competitor campaigns)
   - Free/cheap/discount (if app is paid and you don't want price-sensitive users)
   - Platform terms ("Android", "PC", "web" if iOS-only)

2. **Review Search Terms Report (After Launch):**
   - Identify irrelevant search terms
   - Add as negative keywords

**Output:** Negative keyword list (start with 20-50, expand over time)

## Search Terms Report Mining

### Accessing Search Terms Report

1. Log into Apple Ads dashboard
2. Navigate to campaign or ad group
3. Click "Search Terms" tab
4. Set date range (minimum 7 days for meaningful data)
5. Export to CSV for analysis

### Key Metrics in Search Terms Report

| Metric | What It Tells You | Action |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| **Impressions** | Search volume for this term | High impressions = popular search |
| **Taps** | User interest in your ad | High taps = compelling ad |
| **Tap-Through Rate (TTR)** | Ad relevance and appeal | Low TTR = improve ad or add negative |
| **Installs** | Conversion success | High installs = add as exact match keyword |
| **Conversion Rate** | Post-tap quality | Low CR = improve product page or add negative |
| **Cost Per Tap (CPT)** | Efficiency of tap acquisition | High CPT = lower bid or add negative |
| **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)** | Efficiency of install acquisition | High CPA = optimize or pause |

### Mining Workflow

**Weekly Search Terms Review (30-60 minutes):**

1. **Export Search Terms Report** for all campaigns (last 7 days)

2. **Identify Winners (Add as Exact Match Keywords):**
   - Filter for: Installs ≥ 5, CPA ≤ Target CPA
   - Sort by: Installs (descending)
   - Action: Add top 10-20 terms as exact match keywords in relevant campaign
   - Benefit: Capture high-performing traffic with precise control

3. **Identify Potential Winners (Add as Broad Match Keywords):**
   - Filter for: Installs ≥ 2, CPA ≤ 1.5x Target CPA
   - Sort by: Conversion Rate (descending)
   - Action: Add top 20-30 terms as broad match keywords
   - Benefit: Test expansion while maintaining some control

4. **Identify Losers (Add as Negative Keywords):**
   - Filter for: Spend ≥ $50, Installs = 0
   - OR: CPA ≥ 3x Target CPA
   - Sort by: Spend (descending)
   - Action: Add top 20-30 terms as negative keywords
   - Benefit: Stop wasting budget on non-converting traffic

5. **Identify Irrelevant Terms (Add as Negative Keywords):**
   - Manually review search terms
   - Flag terms clearly not relevant to app
   - Action: Add as negative keywords (use broad match negative for broad exclusions)
   - Benefit: Improve traffic quality, reduce wasted spend

6. **Identify New Themes:**
   - Look for patterns in search terms
   - Identify new keyword themes not in current campaigns
   - Action: Create new ad groups for new themes
   - Benefit: Expand reach into new relevant areas

### Advanced Search Terms Analysis

**Segment by Match Type:**
- Analyze search terms from Exact Match ad groups separately from Broad Match and Search Match
- Exact Match: Should only show exact matches or close variants (if not, investigate)
- Broad Match: Review for relevance, add negatives liberally
- Search Match: Richest source of new keyword ideas, but also most irrelevant traffic

**Segment by Campaign Type:**
- Brand campaign search terms: Should be brand-related (if not, add negatives)
- Category campaign search terms: Should be category-relevant (mine for winners)
- Competitor campaign search terms: Should be competitor-related (verify relevance)
- Discovery campaign search terms: Most diverse, requires most filtering

**Segment by Performance:**
- High performers: Scale by adding as exact match, increasing bids
- Medium performers: Monitor and optimize
- Low performers: Add negatives or lower bids
- Zero converters: Add as negatives if spend is significant

## Keyword Expansion Strategies

### 1. Synonym Expansion

For each core keyword, identify synonyms and related terms:

**Example: Photo Editor App**
- Core: "photo editor"
- Synonyms: "image editor", "picture editor", "photo editing app", "pic editor"
- Related: "photo enhancer", "photo retoucher", "photo filter", "photo effects"

### 2. Feature Expansion

List all app features, create keywords for each:

**Example: Fitness App**
- Feature: Calorie tracking → Keywords: "calorie counter", "calorie tracker", "food diary"
- Feature: Workout plans → Keywords: "workout plans", "training programs", "exercise routines"
- Feature: Progress tracking → Keywords: "fitness tracker", "progress tracker", "workout log"

### 3. Use Case Expansion

Identify different use cases, create keywords for each:

**Example: Note-Taking App**
- Use case: Student notes → Keywords: "student notes", "class notes", "study notes"
- Use case: Meeting notes → Keywords: "meeting notes", "work notes", "business notes"
- Use case: To-do lists → Keywords: "to do list", "task list", "checklist"

### 4. Audience Expansion

Identify different audience segments, create keywords for each:

**Example: Language Learning App**
- Audience: Beginners → Keywords: "learn [language] beginner", "[language] for beginners"
- Audience: Travelers → Keywords: "travel [language]", "[language] for travel"
- Audience: Business → Keywords: "business [language]", "[language] for work"

### 5. Modifier Expansion

Add modifiers to core keywords:

**Common Modifiers:**
- **Quality:** best, top, good, professional, premium
- **Price:** free, cheap, affordable (use cautiously)
- **Platform:** iOS, iPhone, iPad, Apple Watch
- **Action:** learn, create, make, edit, track, manage
- **Time:** daily, weekly, quick, fast
- **Difficulty:** easy, simple, beginner, advanced

**Example:**
- Core: "photo editor"
- With modifiers: "best photo editor", "free photo editor", "professional photo editor", "easy photo editor"

### 6. Long-Tail Expansion

Create longer, more specific keyword phrases:

**Benefits:**
- Lower competition
- Higher intent
- Lower cost
- More qualified traffic

**Example: Meditation App**
- Short-tail: "meditation" (high competition, broad)
- Long-tail: "guided meditation for sleep" (lower competition, specific intent)
- Long-tail: "meditation for anxiety" (lower competition, specific use case)

## Negative Keyword Strategy

### Why Negative Keywords Matter

- **Cost Control:** Prevent wasted spend on irrelevant clicks
- **Quality Improvement:** Improve overall campaign quality and conversion rates
- **Relevance:** Ensure ads only show for relevant searches
- **Efficiency:** Improve CPA by filtering out low-quality traffic

### Types of Negative Keywords

**1. Irrelevant Terms**
- Keywords completely unrelated to your app
- Example for fitness app: "recipes", "restaurants", "clothing"

**2. Competitor Names (If Not Targeting)**
- Competitor app names and brands
- Only if you're NOT running competitor campaigns

**3. Wrong Platform**
- "Android", "Google Play", "PC", "Mac", "web" (if iOS-only app)

**4. Wrong Intent**
- "Free" (if app is paid and you don't want price-sensitive users)
- "Cheap", "discount" (if premium positioning)
- "Tutorial", "how to", "guide" (if users are looking for information, not an app)

**5. Wrong Category**
- Keywords from adjacent but different categories
- Example for photo editor: "video editor" (different category)

**6. Brand Protection**
- Your own brand name (in competitor campaigns, to prevent self-competition)

### Negative Keyword Match Types

**Exact Match Negative:**
- Excludes only exact match of keyword
- Use for: Specific terms you want to exclude precisely
- Example: Negative exact "free" excludes "free" but not "free trial" or "best free"

**Broad Match Negative:**
- Excludes any search containing the keyword
- Use for: Broad exclusions
- Example: Negative broad "Android" excludes "Android app", "best Android", "Android vs iOS", etc.

### Building Your Negative Keyword List

**Initial Negative Keywords (Before Launch):**

Brainstorm 20-50 negative keywords based on:
1. Irrelevant categories
2. Wrong platforms
3. Competitor names (if not targeting)
4. Known irrelevant terms from your industry

**Ongoing Negative Keywords (After Launch):**

Weekly review of Search Terms Report:
1. Export search terms
2. Filter for: Spend > $20, Installs = 0
3. Review for irrelevant terms
4. Add as negative keywords
5. Also manually scan for obviously irrelevant terms (even with low spend)

**Negative Keyword Organization:**

- **Account-level negatives:** Apply to all campaigns (e.g., "Android", "PC")
- **Campaign-level negatives:** Apply to specific campaign (e.g., your brand name in competitor campaign)
- **Ad group-level negatives:** Apply to specific ad group (rare, use for very specific exclusions)

## Keyword Performance Benchmarks

### What to Expect (Directional Benchmarks)

**Brand Keywords:**
- Tap-Through Rate: 5-15%
- Conversion Rate: 30-60%
- CPA: Lowest of all keyword types
- Impression Share: Aim for 80%+

**Category Keywords:**
- Tap-Through Rate: 2-8%
- Conversion Rate: 10-30%
- CPA: Medium (2-3x brand CPA)
- Impression Share: Variable (depends on competition and budget)

**Competitor Keywords:**
- Tap-Through Rate: 1-5%
- Conversion Rate: 5-20%
- CPA: Highest (3-5x brand CPA)
- Impression Share: Low-medium (high competition)

**Discovery Keywords (Search Match, Broad Match):**
- Tap-Through Rate: 1-5%
- Conversion Rate: 5-25%
- CPA: Variable (wide range)
- Impression Share: Variable

**Note:** Benchmarks vary significantly by app category, competition level, and app quality. Use as directional guidance, not absolute targets.
