## Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them


### Mistake 1: Too Generic

**Problem**: Taglines that could apply to any company in the category.

**Examples of Generic Taglines**:
- "Quality You Can Trust"
- "Your Partner in Success"
- "Excellence in Everything We Do"

**Solution**: Focus on specific differentiation. Ask: "Could a competitor use this truthfully?"


### Mistake 2: Too Long

**Problem**: Taglines that are difficult to remember and use.

**Guideline**: Aim for 3-7 words. Longer requires exceptional craft.

**Solution**: Edit ruthlessly. Remove every unnecessary word.


### Mistake 3: Too Clever

**Problem**: Wordplay that obscures meaning or alienates audiences.

**Solution**: Ensure the message is clear even if the wordplay is missed. Test comprehension.


### Mistake 4: Making Undeliverable Promises

**Problem**: Taglines that promise what the brand cannot consistently deliver.

**Example**: "The Best Service Every Time" when service is inconsistent.

**Solution**: Be aspirational but achievable. Ensure operations can support the promise.


### Mistake 5: Ignoring Global Considerations

**Problem**: Taglines that translate poorly or have negative meanings in other languages.

**Famous Example**: "Come Alive with Pepsi" reportedly translated to "Pepsi brings your ancestors back from the grave" in Chinese (possibly apocryphal but illustrative).

**Solution**: Test in all key markets before finalizing.


### Mistake 6: Following Trends

**Problem**: Taglines using trendy language that dates quickly.

**Solution**: Opt for timeless language that will remain relevant for years.


### Mistake 7: No Clear Benefit

**Problem**: Taglines focused on features rather than consumer benefits.

**Solution**: Always answer "What's in it for the customer?"


### Mistake 8: Copycat Approaches

**Problem**: Taglines that too closely resemble competitor phrases.

**Solution**: Research competitive taglines thoroughly. Create distinctive alternatives.


### Mistake 9: Insufficient Testing

**Problem**: Launching taglines based on internal preference rather than consumer response.

**Solution**: Always test with target audience before finalizing.


### Mistake 10: Lack of Strategic Foundation

**Problem**: Creating taglines without clear positioning strategy.

**Solution**: Always complete positioning work before tagline development.
