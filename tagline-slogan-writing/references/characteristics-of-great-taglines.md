## Characteristics of Great Taglines



### The MUSIC Framework for Effective Taglines

Great taglines share common characteristics that can be evaluated using the MUSIC framework:

**M - Memorable**
The tagline should be easy to remember after a single exposure. Memory is enhanced through:
- Brevity (fewer words are easier to recall)
- Rhythm and rhyme
- Alliteration and wordplay
- Emotional resonance
- Unusual phrasing

**U - Unique**
The tagline should be distinctive to your brand. It should not be applicable to competitors. Test uniqueness by asking: "Could a competitor use this line authentically?"

**S - Simple**
The tagline should be easily understood by the target audience. Avoid:
- Jargon and technical terms
- Complex sentence structures
- Abstract concepts
- Multiple messages

**I - Impactful**
The tagline should create an emotional or intellectual response. Impact comes from:
- Relevance to consumer needs
- Compelling benefit promises
- Provocative statements
- Aspirational messaging

**C - Connected**
The tagline should clearly connect to the brand and its positioning. It should reinforce rather than contradict other brand elements.



### Additional Characteristics

**Timeless Quality**
Great taglines remain relevant across years or decades. Avoid:
- Trendy language that dates quickly
- References to specific events
- Technology-dependent phrasing

**Versatility**
Effective taglines work across media and contexts:
- Print advertisements
- Television commercials
- Digital marketing
- Product packaging
- Social media

**Authentic Voice**
The tagline should sound like something the brand would naturally say, reflecting its personality and values.

**Actionable (When Appropriate)**
Many effective taglines suggest action:
- "Just Do It" (Nike)
- "Think Different" (Apple)
- "Eat Fresh" (Subway)
