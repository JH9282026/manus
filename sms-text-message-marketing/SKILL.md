# SMS/Text Message Marketing

## Introduction to SMS Marketing

SMS marketing is the practice of sending promotional, transactional, or informational messages to customers via text message. With open rates exceeding 98% and response rates far surpassing email, SMS has become one of the most effective direct marketing channels available to businesses.

Unlike other marketing channels that compete for attention in crowded inboxes or social feeds, SMS messages arrive directly on users' phones—devices that most people keep within arm's reach at all times. This immediacy and intimacy make SMS uniquely powerful for time-sensitive communications, personalized offers, and building customer relationships.

### Why SMS Marketing Works

**Exceptional Open Rates:**
While email open rates hover around 20-25%, SMS messages achieve open rates of 98% or higher. Most text messages are read within three minutes of receipt, making SMS the fastest way to reach your audience.

**Widespread Accessibility:**
Unlike apps or social media platforms, SMS works on every mobile phone—smartphone or feature phone. No app download is required, no account creation, no learning curve. This universal accessibility makes SMS effective across demographics.

**Personal and Direct:**
Text messages feel personal. They arrive in the same place as messages from friends and family, creating an intimate connection between brand and customer. This personal context means users pay attention to SMS in ways they don't with other marketing channels.

**High Engagement:**
SMS messages generate response rates of 45% compared to just 6% for email. When you need customers to take action—whether redeeming a coupon, confirming an appointment, or clicking a link—SMS delivers.

**Immediate Impact:**
90% of SMS messages are read within three minutes. For flash sales, limited-time offers, or urgent notifications, no other channel matches SMS for speed.

## Types of SMS Campaigns

### Promotional SMS

Promotional messages market products, services, or offers. These include sales announcements, discount codes, new product launches, and seasonal campaigns.

**Examples:**

**Flash Sale:**
> "FLASH SALE ⚡ 30% off everything for the next 4 hours only! Use code FLASH30 at checkout. Shop now: [link] Reply STOP to opt out"

**New Product:**
> "NEW ARRIVAL! Our bestselling jacket is back in stock—now in 5 new colors. Be first to shop: [link] Text STOP to unsubscribe"

**Seasonal:**
> "Happy holidays from [Brand]! 🎁 Here's a gift for you: $15 off your next order. Use code HOLIDAY15 by Dec 31. Shop: [link]"

### Transactional SMS

Transactional messages provide information about purchases, accounts, or services. These are typically triggered by user actions and don't require explicit marketing opt-in (though best practices suggest clear communication about all message types).

**Examples:**

**Order Confirmation:**
> "[Brand]: Thanks for your order #12345! We'll send tracking info once it ships. View order: [link] Questions? Reply to this message."

**Shipping Update:**
> "Your [Brand] order is on its way! 📦 Expected delivery: Friday, Oct 15. Track package: [link]"

**Appointment Confirmation:**
> "Appointment confirmed: Dr. Smith, Mon Oct 18 at 2:30 PM. Reply Y to confirm, N to reschedule. Questions? Call 555-1234"

### Reminder SMS

Reminder messages help customers remember appointments, renewals, or time-sensitive opportunities.

**Examples:**

**Appointment Reminder:**
> "Reminder: Your appointment at [Business] is tomorrow at 10 AM. Reply C to confirm or R to reschedule."

**Cart Abandonment:**
> "Hi [Name], you left something in your cart! Complete your order and get free shipping: [link] Your cart expires in 24 hours."

**Renewal Reminder:**
> "Your [Product] subscription renews in 3 days. Update payment info or manage subscription: [link] Questions? Reply here."

### Alert and Notification SMS

Alert messages communicate urgent or important information, such as security alerts, service notifications, or emergency updates.

**Examples:**

**Security Alert:**
> "[Bank] Alert: Unusual activity detected on your account. If this was you, reply YES. If not, call 1-800-555-1234 immediately."

**Service Notification:**
> "Scheduled maintenance: [Service] will be unavailable Sat 2-4 AM EST. We apologize for any inconvenience."

**Weather/Emergency:**
> "[Store Location] is closing early today due to weather. We'll reopen tomorrow at regular hours. Stay safe!"

### Conversational SMS

Conversational SMS involves two-way communication, allowing customers to ask questions, get support, or complete transactions via text.

**Examples:**

**Customer Support:**
> "Hi! Thanks for texting [Brand]. How can I help you today? Reply with your question and a team member will respond shortly."

**Concierge:**
> "Hi [Name], I'm [Agent] from [Brand]. I see you were browsing fall jackets. Need help finding the perfect one? I'm here to help!"

**Feedback Request:**
> "How was your recent visit to [Store]? Reply 1-5 (5 being best). We read every response!"

## SMS Marketing Fundamentals

### Understanding SMS Constraints

**Character Limits:**
- Standard SMS: 160 characters
- With special characters (emoji, Unicode): may reduce to 70 characters
- Messages longer than 160 characters are split and may arrive out of order
- Most platforms support concatenated messages up to 1,600 characters

**Best Practice:** Keep messages under 160 characters when possible. If longer messages are necessary, ensure they make sense even if segments arrive out of order.

### Message Structure

A complete SMS marketing message typically includes:

1. **Brand Identifier** - Who is this from?
2. **Value/Offer** - What's in it for the recipient?
3. **Call to Action** - What should they do?
4. **Link** (optional) - Where to take action
5. **Opt-out Instructions** - How to unsubscribe

**Example Structure:**
> "[Brand]: [Offer/Value]. [CTA]: [Link]. Reply STOP to opt out"

**Applied Example:**
> "ACME Co: 25% off all shoes this weekend only! Shop now: acme.co/shoes Reply STOP to opt out"

### Writing Concise SMS Copy

Every character counts in SMS. Here's how to write effectively within constraints:

**Techniques for Concision:**

**Remove Articles:**
- Before: "Get a free sample of the new product"
- After: "Get free sample of new product"

**Use Abbreviations (carefully):**
- "through" → "thru"
- "your" → "yr" (use sparingly)
- "today" → "today" (some abbreviations confuse readers)

**Use Numbers:**
- "twenty percent" → "20%"
- "fifteen dollars" → "$15"

**Cut Filler Words:**
- "We are excited to announce" → "Announcing:"
- "Don't miss out on" → omit entirely

**Use Symbols:**
- "and" → "&"
- "at" → "@" (where clear)
- "plus" → "+"

**Before and After:**

**Before (178 characters):**
> "Hello! We are excited to announce that we are having a big sale this weekend. You can save up to 40% off on all of our products. Shop now at our website!"

**After (142 characters):**
> "[Brand]: BIG SALE! Up to 40% off everything this weekend. Shop now: [link] Reply STOP to unsubscribe"

## Timing and Frequency

When and how often you send SMS matters as much as what you say.

### Optimal Timing

**General Guidelines:**
- Send during business hours (9 AM - 9 PM local time)
- Avoid early mornings and late nights
- Consider time zones for national campaigns
- Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays typically perform best
- Weekends can work for B2C, less so for B2B

**Context-Specific Timing:**

| Message Type | Optimal Timing |
|--------------|----------------|
| Flash sales | Midday (11 AM - 2 PM) |
| Restaurant promos | 10-11 AM (before lunch decision) |
| Appointment reminders | 24-48 hours before |
| Order shipping | Immediately when shipped |
| Abandoned cart | 1-3 hours after abandonment |

### Frequency Guidelines

**Too Few Messages:**
- Subscribers forget about you
- Miss opportunities for engagement
- Lower lifetime value

**Too Many Messages:**
- Opt-outs increase dramatically
- Brand perception suffers
- Complaints and carrier issues

**Recommended Frequency:**
- Promotional: 4-6 messages per month maximum
- Transactional: As needed (triggered by actions)
- Combination programs: Stay under 10 total per month

**Best Practice:** Let subscribers choose their frequency. Offer options like "Weekly deals" vs. "Flash sale alerts only."

## Call-to-Action in SMS

Every SMS should have a clear call-to-action telling recipients exactly what to do next.

### Effective SMS CTAs

**Link-Based CTAs:**
> "Shop now: [link]"

> "Complete your order: [link]"

**Reply-Based CTAs:**
> "Reply YES to confirm"

**Action-Based CTAs:**

- Create urgency when authentic ("Today only," "Ends tonight")
- Make the next step absolutely clear
- Use shortened URLs to save characters

## SMS Compliance

SMS marketing is heavily regulated. Non-compliance can result in significant fines and legal action.

### Key Regulations

**TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) - United States:**
- Requires prior express written consent for marketing messages
- Must provide clear opt-out mechanism
- Autodialers and prerecorded messages have additional restrictions
- Fines: $500-$1,500 per unsolicited message

**CAN-SPAM Act:**
- While primarily for email, some provisions apply to SMS
- Requires accurate sender identification
- Must honor opt-out requests promptly

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) - European Union:**
- Requires explicit consent for marketing
- Must explain how data will be used
- Right to be forgotten applies
- Significant penalties for violations

**CASL (Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation):**
- Requires express or implied consent
- Must include sender identification
- Must include unsubscribe mechanism

### Compliance Requirements

**Obtaining Consent:**
- Use clear, conspicuous language
- Explain what types of messages subscribers will receive
- Document consent (timestamp, IP address, form used)
- Consent must be freely given, not required for purchase

**Example Opt-In Language:**
> "By texting JOIN to 12345, you agree to receive marketing messages from [Brand] at this number. Message frequency varies. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to unsubscribe, HELP for help."

**Required Message Elements:**
- Clear brand identification
- Opt-out instructions (typically "Reply STOP to unsubscribe")
- Message frequency disclosure (in initial opt-in)

**Honoring Opt-Outs:**
- Process STOP requests immediately
- Send confirmation of opt-out
- Remove number from all marketing lists
- Keep records of opt-outs

**Opt-Out Confirmation:**
> "You've been unsubscribed from [Brand] texts. You'll no longer receive marketing messages. Reply START to re-subscribe."

## Shortcodes and Keywords

### Shortcodes

Shortcodes are 5-6 digit numbers used for high-volume SMS programs.

**Types:**
- **Dedicated Shortcode:** Your brand only (e.g., 12345)
- **Shared Shortcode:** Multiple brands use same number with different keywords

**Benefits of Dedicated Shortcodes:**
- Full brand ownership
- Custom keywords
- Higher deliverability
- Better for high-volume programs

### Keywords

Keywords are words customers text to opt in or trigger responses.

**Keyword Best Practices:**
- Keep keywords short and memorable
- Use brand-relevant terms
- Avoid easily misspelled words
- Consider multiple keywords for different campaigns

**Examples:**
- "Text JOIN to 12345" (general opt-in)
- "Text DEALS to 12345" (promotional list)
- "Text ORDER to 12345" (check order status)
- "Text HELP to 12345" (customer support)

## SMS Landing Pages

When SMS links lead to web pages, those pages need special optimization.

### Mobile Landing Page Requirements

- **Fast loading** (under 3 seconds)
- **Mobile-responsive** design
- **Clear value proposition** above fold
- **Prominent CTA** (button, not text link)
- **Minimal form fields** if collecting data
- **Auto-apply** promo codes when possible

### Landing Page Best Practices

**Match Message to Page:**
If SMS mentions "25% off shoes," landing page should show shoes with discount applied.

**Reduce Friction:**
Pre-fill known information, minimize steps, offer guest checkout.

**Clear Next Steps:**
Make the primary action obvious and easy.

## Integration with Other Channels

SMS works best as part of an integrated marketing strategy.

### SMS + Email

**Complementary Approach:**
- Email for detailed content, stories, longer offers
- SMS for urgency, reminders, time-sensitive updates

**Cross-Promotion:**
- Include SMS opt-in in emails
- Send SMS reminder for important emails

**Example Workflow:**
1. Email announces sale (Day 1)
2. SMS reminder (Day 2): "Don't forget! Sale ends tomorrow: [link]"
3. Email with last-chance message (Day 3 AM)

### SMS + Social Media

- Cross-promote SMS list on social
- Use SMS for exclusive offers not on social
- Coordinate campaign timing

### SMS + App Push

- SMS reaches users without your app
- Push for app-installed users (free)
- SMS for critical messages (higher open rate)

## Measuring SMS Success

### Key Metrics

**Delivery Rate:**
Percentage of messages successfully delivered
> Target: 95%+ delivery rate

**Open Rate:**
Percentage of delivered messages opened (hard to track in SMS)
> Assumption: 98%+ for opt-in lists

**Click-Through Rate (CTR):**
Percentage of recipients who clicked a link
> Average: 10-20% for promotional SMS

**Conversion Rate:**
Percentage who completed desired action
> Varies by offer and audience

**Opt-Out Rate:**
Percentage who unsubscribed after a message
> Target: Under 2% per message

**Response Rate:**
For two-way SMS, percentage who replied
> Average: 45% for conversational SMS

### Attribution

**Tracking Methods:**
- Unique discount codes per campaign
- UTM parameters on links
- Dedicated landing pages
- Unique phone numbers

**Attribution Challenges:**
- Cross-device behavior
- Offline redemptions
- Assisted conversions

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Sending Without Consent
**Problem:** Texting numbers without proper opt-in
**Solution:** Always obtain documented consent before sending

### Mistake 2: Over-Messaging
**Problem:** Sending too frequently, causing opt-outs
**Solution:** Limit to 4-6 promotional messages per month

### Mistake 3: Poor Timing
**Problem:** Sending at 6 AM or 11 PM
**Solution:** Send during business hours in recipient's time zone

### Mistake 4: Generic Messages
**Problem:** "Check out our sale!" with no personalization
**Solution:** Use names, past behavior, and relevant offers

### Mistake 5: Missing Opt-Out
**Problem:** Not including unsubscribe instructions
**Solution:** Always include "Reply STOP to opt out"

### Mistake 6: Broken Links
**Problem:** Links that don't work on mobile
**Solution:** Test all links on multiple devices before sending

### Mistake 7: Too Long
**Problem:** Messages exceeding 160 characters unnecessarily
**Solution:** Edit ruthlessly; get to the point

### Mistake 8: No Clear CTA
**Problem:** Vague messages without direction
**Solution:** Always tell recipients exactly what to do

### A/B Testing for SMS

Optimize performance through systematic testing.

**What to Test:**
- Message copy and tone
- Offer type and amount
- CTA wording
- Send time
- Personalization elements
- Emoji usage

**Testing Process:**
1. Form hypothesis (e.g., "Urgency will increase CTR")
2. Create variants (A: standard, B: with urgency)
3. Split audience randomly (at least 1,000 per variant)
4. Send simultaneously
5. Measure results (CTR, conversion, opt-out)
6. Apply learnings to future campaigns

**Test Example:**

**Variant A:**
> "New arrivals just dropped. Shop now: [link]"

**Variant B:**
> "🚨 New arrivals selling fast! Shop now before they're gone: [link]"

**Results:**
- A: 8% CTR, 2.1% conversion

## SMS Marketing ROI

### Calculating SMS ROI

Measure return on investment for SMS campaigns.

**ROI Formula:**
```
ROI = (Revenue from SMS - Cost of SMS) / Cost of SMS × 100
```

**Cost Components:**
- Platform fees (monthly subscription or per message)
- Message costs (usually $0.01-0.05 per message)
- Content creation time
- List building costs
- Compliance costs

**Revenue Attribution:**
- Unique discount codes
- UTM parameters on links
- Dedicated landing pages
- Post-purchase surveys ("How did you hear about us?")

**Example Calculation:**
```
Campaign costs: $500
Messages sent: 10,000
Unique clicks: 1,200 (12% CTR)
Conversions: 180 (15% conversion rate)
Average order value: $75
Revenue: $13,500

ROI = ($13,500 - $500) / $500 × 100 = 2,600%
```

### Case Study 2: Appointment Reminders

**Challenge:** Reduce no-shows for medical practice

**Strategy:**
- Send reminders at 48 hours and 2 hours before
- Include confirmation/reschedule options
- Personalize with doctor name and appointment type

**Messages:**

**48-Hour Reminder:**
> "Reminder: Your appointment with Dr. Smith is Thursday at 2 PM. Reply C to confirm or R to reschedule."

**2-Hour Reminder:**

**Results:**
- No-show rate decreased from 18% to 4%

- Patient satisfaction increased
- Staff time on reminder calls reduced by 80%

## Final Thoughts

SMS marketing's effectiveness comes from its unique position: direct access to customers' most personal devices. This privilege must be respected through relevance, value, and restraint.

The most successful SMS programs share common traits: clear consent processes, valuable offers, appropriate frequency, strong personalization, and rigorous compliance. They treat SMS not as a cheap broadcast channel but as a direct line to customers that must be earned and maintained.

As technology evolves—with RCS, AI-driven personalization, and conversational commerce—SMS marketing will become even more powerful. But the fundamentals remain: send messages your customers want to receive, when they want to receive them, with clear value and easy opt-out.

Start small, test consistently, measure rigorously, and always put the customer experience first. Follow these principles, and SMS will become one of your most valuable marketing channels.
