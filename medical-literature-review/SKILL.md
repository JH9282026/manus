# Medical Literature Review

Systematically review and synthesize medical research literature to support evidence-based decisions.

## Overview

Conduct structured literature reviews following established methodologies (PRISMA, Cochrane). Identify, evaluate, and synthesize published medical research for clinical practice, research planning, and healthcare policy.

|------------|-------|----------|-----------|

## PRISMA-Guided Process

1. **Define question** — Use PICO format (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome)
2. **Search strategy** — Define databases, keywords, inclusion/exclusion criteria
3. **Screen** — Title/abstract screening → Full-text review
4. **Extract data** — Standardized extraction forms
5. **Assess quality** — Risk of bias, evidence grading
6. **Synthesize** — Narrative or statistical synthesis
7. **Report** — PRISMA-compliant reporting

## Evidence Hierarchy

| Level | Study Type | Strength |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| I | Systematic review of RCTs | Highest |
| II | Randomized controlled trial (RCT) | High |
| III | Controlled trial without randomization | Moderate |
| IV | Cohort or case-control study | Moderate |
| V | Case series, case report | Low |
| VI | Expert opinion | Lowest |
