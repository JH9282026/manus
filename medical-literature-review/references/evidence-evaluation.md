# Evidence Evaluation & Synthesis

## Risk of Bias Assessment

### Cochrane Risk of Bias Tool (RoB 2)
| Domain | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Randomization process | Low/Some concerns/High |
| Deviations from interventions | Low/Some concerns/High |
| Missing outcome data | Low/Some concerns/High |
| Outcome measurement | Low/Some concerns/High |
| Selection of reported results | Low/Some concerns/High |
| Overall bias | Low/Some concerns/High |

### Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (Observational Studies)
- **Selection** (0-4 stars): Representativeness, selection of controls
- **Comparability** (0-2 stars): Control for confounders
- **Outcome** (0-3 stars): Assessment, follow-up adequacy

## GRADE Evidence Grading

| Quality Level | Definition |
|--------------|-----------|
| High | Very confident in effect estimate |
| Moderate | Moderately confident, true effect likely close |
| Low | Limited confidence, true effect may differ |
| Very Low | Very little confidence in effect estimate |

### Factors That Lower Quality
- Risk of bias
- Inconsistency (heterogeneity)
- Indirectness (applicability)
- Imprecision (wide confidence intervals)
- Publication bias

### Factors That Raise Quality
- Large effect size
- Dose-response gradient
- Plausible confounders would reduce effect

## Meta-Analysis Fundamentals

### When to Conduct Meta-Analysis
- Sufficient number of studies (≥3 with same outcome)
- Studies are clinically and methodologically similar
- Effect sizes can be extracted or calculated
- Heterogeneity is manageable (I² <75%)

### Key Metrics
| Metric | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Pooled effect size | Combined treatment effect (OR, RR, MD, SMD) |
| 95% CI | Confidence interval for pooled effect |
| I² | Heterogeneity index (0-100%) |
| p-value | Statistical significance |
| Funnel plot | Publication bias assessment |

### Heterogeneity Interpretation
| I² Value | Heterogeneity |
|----------|---------------|
| 0-25% | Low |
| 25-50% | Moderate |
| 50-75% | Substantial |
| 75-100% | Considerable |

### Sub-Group and Sensitivity Analyses
- Pre-specify subgroups in protocol
- Analyze by study quality, population, dose, duration
- Sensitivity analysis: remove one study at a time
- Assess robustness of findings

## Synthesis Reporting

### Narrative Synthesis Structure
1. Study characteristics summary table
2. Quality assessment results
3. Findings by outcome, organized by intervention
4. Consistency of findings across studies
5. Strength of evidence assessment
6. Clinical implications
7. Research gaps and future directions

### Forest Plot Elements
- Study name and year
- Effect size with 95% CI (horizontal line)
- Study weight (box size)
- Diamond for pooled estimate
- Line of no effect (vertical line at 1 or 0)
