## Pricing and ROI Presentation Strategies


### Pricing Presentation Approaches

**Value Before Price**
Always establish value before presenting price. The prospect should be thinking "this is worth a lot" before they see the number.

**Price Anchoring**
- Reference the cost of the problem first
- Mention what alternatives cost
- Show what other customers pay
- Present highest option first (if offering tiers)

**Investment Framing**
- Present as an investment, not a cost
- Focus on returns, not expenses
- Compare to what they're already spending
- Break down to per-unit or per-user costs


### ROI Presentation

**ROI Calculation Components**
- Investment: Total cost of ownership
- Returns: Quantified benefits
- Timeline: When benefits materialize
- Risk: Confidence factors

**ROI Presentation Formats**
- Simple ROI percentage
- Payback period
- Net present value
- Total value delivered

**Making ROI Credible**
- Use conservative estimates
- Show calculation methodology
- Reference similar customer results
- Allow for prospect verification


### Handling Price Objections

**Value Reinforcement**
- Restate the benefits and outcomes
- Compare price to cost of problem
- Highlight total value delivered
- Remind of unique differentiators

**Payment Options**
- Monthly vs. annual pricing
- Phased implementation
- Volume discounts
- Payment terms

### Legitimate Urgency Factors

**External Deadlines**
- Regulatory compliance dates
- Industry changes
- Competitive pressures
- Market windows

**Internal Drivers**
- Budget cycles
- Project timelines
- Executive mandates
- Growth plans

**Your Constraints**
- Limited capacity
- Price changes
- Implementation schedules
- Promotional periods


### Urgency Communication

**Cost of Delay**
- "Every month you wait costs you approximately..."
- "Your competitors are already implementing..."
- "The problem will only get worse over time..."

**Opportunity Cost**
- "The sooner you start, the sooner you'll see results..."
- "Early implementation means earlier ROI..."
- "This quarter's results could look very different..."

**Scarcity (When Genuine)**
- Limited availability
- Capacity constraints
- Exclusive terms
- Time-bound offers
