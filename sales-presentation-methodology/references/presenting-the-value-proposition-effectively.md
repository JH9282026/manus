## Presenting the Value Proposition Effectively



### Value Proposition Structure

A compelling value proposition answers: "Why should this prospect buy this solution from us right now?"

**Core Components**
- Target customer: Who is this for?
- Problem solved: What pain does it address?
- Unique solution: How do you solve it differently?
- Key benefits: What outcomes will they achieve?
- Proof: Why should they believe you?



### Differentiation in Value Presentation

**Competitive Positioning**
- Understand competitor offerings
- Identify unique strengths
- Focus on differences that matter to this prospect
- Avoid negative competitor attacks

**Category Differentiation**
- Position against alternatives (not just competitors)
- Compare to the status quo (doing nothing)
- Compare to internal solutions (build vs. buy)
- Compare to partial solutions

### Demo Preparation

**Environment Setup**
- Test all technology in advance
- Have backup plans for technical failures
- Prepare sample data relevant to the prospect
- Clean up test accounts and dummy data

**Demo Script Development**
- Plan the narrative arc
- Identify "wow" moments
- Prepare for common questions
- Time the demonstration



### Demo Execution

**Tell-Show-Tell Structure**
1. Tell them what you're about to show and why it matters
2. Show the feature or capability in action
3. Tell them what they just saw and reinforce the benefit

**Keep It Relevant**
- Show only features that address their needs
- Use their data or realistic scenarios
- Skip features that aren't relevant
- Customize the demo flow for this audience

**Interactive Demonstrations**
- Invite participation when appropriate
- Ask questions during the demo
- Let them drive if they want to
- Respond to real-time feedback



### Handling Demo Problems

**Technical Issues**
- Stay calm and professional
- Have screenshots or videos as backup
- Use problems to demonstrate support quality
- Never blame the technology
- Have a recovery plan rehearsed
- Turn issues into opportunities to demonstrate resilience

**Audience Disengagement**
- Watch for signs of confusion or boredom
- Adjust pace and content accordingly
- Ask checking questions
- Skip ahead if necessary
- Re-engage with questions or stories
- Offer to focus on areas of greatest interest
