## Closing Techniques Within Presentations


### Reading Buying Signals

**Verbal Signals**
- Questions about implementation
- Discussion of internal approval process
- Requests for references or contracts
- Forward-looking language ("When we implement...")

**Non-Verbal Signals**
- Nodding and agreement
- Note-taking
- Leaning forward
- Side conversations with colleagues


### Closing Approaches

**The Direct Close**
- "Based on what we've discussed, are you ready to move forward?"
- "I'd like to propose we start with [specific next step]. Does that work for you?"

**The Summary Close**
- Recap key points and agreements
- Confirm the value proposition
- Propose the logical next step

**The Alternative Close**
- "Would you prefer to start with the pilot program or the full implementation?"
- "Should we schedule implementation for this quarter or next?"

**The Assumptive Close**
- Proceed as if the decision is made
- Discuss implementation details
- Talk about next steps naturally

**The Trial Close**
- "If we can address your concern about [issue], would you be ready to proceed?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how close are you to moving forward?"


### Next Steps

Always end with clear next steps:
- Specific action items
- Responsible parties
- Deadlines and timelines
- Follow-up commitment
