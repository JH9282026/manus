## Industry-Specific Considerations


### B2B vs. B2C Sales Presentations

**B2B Presentations**
- Longer sales cycles with multiple touchpoints
- Multiple stakeholders with varying priorities
- Focus on ROI, efficiency, and business outcomes
- Emphasis on implementation and support
- Greater need for customization and personalization
- Often requires technical validation

**B2C Presentations**
- Shorter decision cycles
- Emotional drivers often predominant
- Focus on personal benefits and lifestyle improvement
- Emphasis on ease of use and immediate value
- Social proof and peer influence important
- Price sensitivity more prominent


### Enterprise Sales Presentations

Enterprise sales presentations have unique characteristics:
- Multiple stakeholders across departments
- Formal procurement processes
- Extended evaluation timelines
- Integration requirements with existing systems
- Security and compliance considerations
- Change management implications
- Contract negotiation complexity


### SaaS/Subscription Presentations

Subscription-based sales presentations focus on:
- Ongoing value rather than one-time benefit
- Total cost of ownership over time
- Customer success and support capabilities
- Product roadmap and continuous improvement
- Implementation and onboarding process
- Scalability and growth accommodation
