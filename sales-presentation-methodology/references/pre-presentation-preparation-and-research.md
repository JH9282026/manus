## Pre-Presentation Preparation and Research



### Prospect Research

Thorough preparation separates exceptional sales presentations from mediocre ones:

**Company Research**
- Industry position, market share, competitive landscape
- Recent news, press releases, earnings reports
- Strategic initiatives and announced priorities
- Company culture, values, and mission
- Organizational structure and key personnel

**Individual Research**
- LinkedIn profiles of attendees
- Professional background and career trajectory
- Published articles, interviews, or speaking engagements
- Mutual connections and possible introductions
- Communication style preferences

**Industry Research**
- Market trends and disruptions
- Regulatory environment and compliance requirements
- Common challenges facing similar companies
- Competitor activities and positioning



### Stakeholder Analysis

Understanding who will attend and their roles in the decision:

**Decision-Maker Mapping**
- Economic buyer (controls budget)
- Technical buyer (evaluates specifications)
- User buyer (will use the solution)
- Champion (internal advocate)
- Blocker (potential obstacle)

**Stakeholder Priorities**
- What does each stakeholder care about most?
- What are their individual success metrics?
- What objections might each raise?
- How does each prefer to receive information?



### Presentation Customization

Tailoring your presentation to the specific opportunity:

**Content Selection**
- Include only relevant features and benefits
- Choose case studies from similar industries
- Adjust technical depth for the audience
- Select appropriate proof points
- Remove anything that doesn't directly support your value proposition
- Prioritize content based on stated priorities

**Language Adaptation**
- Use their terminology and acronyms
- Reference their specific challenges
- Incorporate their stated goals
- Mirror their communication style
- Avoid jargon they may not understand
- Use industry-specific language when appropriate

**Personalization Elements**
- Reference previous conversations specifically
- Include their company name and context
- Use their branding colors in slides (subtly)
- Reference their industry trends and challenges
- Mention specific individuals by name when appropriate



### Rehearsal and Practice

Preparation extends beyond content to delivery:

**Individual Practice**
- Rehearse your presentation multiple times
- Time each section to ensure proper pacing
- Practice transitions between sections
- Anticipate difficult questions and prepare responses
- Record yourself and review for improvement opportunities

**Team Preparation**
- If presenting with colleagues, coordinate roles clearly
- Practice handoffs between presenters
- Ensure consistent messaging across team members
- Prepare for team Q&A dynamics
- Assign backup responsibilities for technical issues

**Technical Rehearsal**
- Test all technology in advance
- Practice screen sharing and demo navigation
- Verify audio and video quality
- Have backup plans for technical failures
- Test in conditions similar to the actual presentation
