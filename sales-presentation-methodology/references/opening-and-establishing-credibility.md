## Opening and Establishing Credibility



### The Critical First Minutes

The opening of your sales presentation sets the tone for everything that follows. You have roughly 30-60 seconds to capture attention and establish relevance.

**Attention-Grabbing Openers**
- Provocative question: "What if you could reduce customer churn by 40% in the next quarter?"
- Surprising statistic: "Companies in your industry lose an average of $2.5 million annually to this problem."
- Relevant story: "Last month, a company very similar to yours was facing exactly what you described..."
- Bold statement: "The approach you're currently using is costing you far more than you realize."



### Establishing Credibility

Building trust quickly is essential for sales presentations:

**Authority Establishment**
- Brief mention of relevant experience
- Notable clients or recognizable logos
- Industry awards or certifications
- Specific expertise relevant to their challenge

**Rapport Building**
- Reference previous conversations or research
- Find common ground or shared connections
- Show genuine interest in their business
- Demonstrate that you've done your homework

**Agenda Setting**
- Preview what you'll cover
- Confirm time expectations
- Invite interaction and questions
- Set collaborative tone



### The Power of "Why This Matters"

Connect immediately to the prospect's interests:

**Relevance Statement**
- "Based on our conversation last week, I know that [specific challenge] is a top priority for you this quarter..."
- "I've put together some insights specifically addressing the [specific issue] you mentioned..."
- "Given your goal to [specific objective], I want to show you how we've helped similar companies achieve that..."

**Executive Summary Opening**

For executive audiences, consider starting with an executive summary that previews key points:
- State the problem you're addressing
- Summarize your proposed solution
- Preview expected outcomes and ROI
- Outline what you'll cover in detail

This approach respects executives' time and allows them to engage more deeply on topics that interest them most.



### Building Psychological Safety

Create an environment where prospects feel comfortable engaging:

**Invitation to Participate**
- "Please feel free to interrupt with questions at any time"
- "I'd rather this be a conversation than a monologue"
- "Stop me if I'm covering ground you already know"

**Acknowledging Complexity**
- "I know you're evaluating multiple options"
- "These are complex decisions with many factors"
- "I want to make sure we address what matters most to you"

**Establishing Partnership Mindset**
- "My goal is to understand whether we're a good fit for each other"
- "I want to make sure our solution actually addresses your needs"
- "If it turns out we're not the right fit, I'll tell you that honestly"
