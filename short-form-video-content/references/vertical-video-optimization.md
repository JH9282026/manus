## Vertical Video Optimization



### Technical Requirements

**Aspect Ratio**: 9:16 (1080x1920 pixels) is the standard for all major short-form platforms

**Safe Zones**: Keep important content away from edges where platform UI overlays (username, captions, buttons) appear:
- Bottom 20% reserved for captions and UI
- Top 10% may have platform headers
- Sides need 5% buffer

**Resolution**: Shoot in 1080p minimum; 4K provides flexibility for cropping and zooming

**Frame Rate**: 30fps standard, 60fps for action/sports content

**File Format**: MP4 with H.264 codec is universally accepted



### Framing for Vertical

**Subject Placement**: Center frame or rule of thirds, positioned higher than lower

**Headroom**: Less headroom than horizontal video; subject can fill more frame

**Movement**: Pan and movement work differently in vertical; lateral movement more impactful than horizontal pans

**Text Space**: Plan for text overlays when framing; leave appropriate areas clear



### Converting Horizontal Content

When repurposing horizontal content for short-form:

**Cropping**: Identify the key action area and crop; may need to pan to follow action

**Reformatting**: Add borders, backgrounds, or context to fill vertical frame

**Split Screen**: Place horizontal video in center with context above/below

**Re-shooting**: When possible, capture vertical version during original production



### TikTok Strategy

**Posting Frequency**: 1-4 times per day for maximum growth
**Best Times**: Generally 7-9 AM, 12-3 PM, 7-11 PM (varies by audience)
**Sound Strategy**: Use trending sounds within first 48-72 hours of trend emergence
**Hashtag Usage**: 3-5 relevant hashtags, mix of broad and niche
**Native Features**: Utilize Duets, Stitches, and effects for algorithm preference
**Community Engagement**: Respond to comments quickly, especially in first hour

**TikTok-Specific Tactics**:
- Jump on trends early—being late to a trend provides minimal benefit
- Original sounds can go viral if highly shareable
- Series content (Part 1, Part 2) builds anticipation
- Reply to comments with video for double algorithm exposure
- Go Live regularly to deepen community connection



### Instagram Reels Strategy

**Posting Frequency**: 1-2 Reels per day, balanced with Stories and Feed posts
**Best Times**: 11 AM-1 PM, 7-9 PM (Instagram's audience tends slightly later)
**Sound Strategy**: Mix trending audio with original audio
**Hashtag Usage**: 3-8 hashtags, use Reel-specific hashtags
**Cross-Features**: Share Reels to Stories, coordinate with carousel posts
**Aesthetic Consistency**: Maintain visual brand consistency across all content

**Instagram-Specific Tactics**:
- Cover images matter—design thumbnail-worthy opening frames
- Carousels and Reels can complement each other
- Save trending audio even before using it
- Collaborate using Collab feature for shared distribution
- Use Instagram's text and sticker features natively



### YouTube Shorts Strategy

**Posting Frequency**: 1-2 Shorts per day, alongside long-form content strategy
**Best Times**: Similar to main YouTube content, varies by audience
**Sound Strategy**: YouTube's music library plus original audio
**Hashtag Usage**: #Shorts required, plus 2-3 relevant hashtags
**Integration**: Use Shorts to tease and drive traffic to long-form videos
**Subscriber Focus**: Strong CTAs to subscribe, as Shorts can drive channel growth

**YouTube-Specific Tactics**:
- Clip long-form content into multiple Shorts
- Shorts can rank in Google search results
- Use end of Short to tease longer content
- Vertical content from other platforms can work with adjustments
- YouTube's audience may prefer educational content



### Facebook Reels Strategy

**Posting Frequency**: 1-2 Reels per day
**Best Times**: Early morning (6-8 AM) and evening (7-9 PM)
**Sound Strategy**: Less trend-dependent than TikTok
**Hashtag Usage**: Minimal, Facebook hashtags less important
**Group Integration**: Share Reels in relevant Facebook Groups
**Business Focus**: Strong for local business and older demographic reach

**Facebook-Specific Tactics**:
- Cross-post from Instagram but customize captions
- Longer videos (60-90 seconds) can perform well
- Less need to follow trends, focus on value
- Facebook's audience engages differently—comments may be more substantive
- Professional/business content has strong audience
