## Video Script Briefs and Client Communication


### Essential Brief Components
Before writing any video script, gather comprehensive information:

**Project overview:**
- Video purpose and goals
- Target audience description
- Key messages (prioritized)
- Desired viewer action
- Tone and style guidelines

**Technical requirements:**
- Video length/duration
- Format and aspect ratio
- Platform(s) for distribution
- Brand guidelines
- Required elements (logos, taglines, legal disclaimers)

**Production context:**
- Budget constraints
- Timeline and deadlines
- Available assets (footage, graphics, music)
- Production style (live action, animation, mixed)
- Talent requirements

```
1. PROJECT BASICS

4. MESSAGING

   - Supporting points (max 3):
   - Proof points/evidence:
   - Must-include elements:

5. TONE & STYLE
   - Describe the desired tone in 3 words:
   - Reference videos you like:
   - What should this NOT feel like:

6. PRODUCTION
   - Production style preference:
   - Available assets:
   - Brand guidelines available:
   - Timeline and deadlines:
```


### Script Presentation Format
When presenting scripts to clients:

1. **Cover page:** Title, version, date, author
2. **Creative brief summary:** Confirm understanding of goals
3. **Script proper:** Formatted appropriately for video type
4. **Visual references:** Mood board, reference images, or storyboard
5. **Timing breakdown:** Section-by-section duration
6. **Production notes:** Talent needs, location suggestions, special requirements
7. **Revision process:** Next steps and feedback guidelines
