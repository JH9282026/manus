## VSL Structure and Framework



### The Classic VSL Framework

The most proven VSL structure follows this sequence:

1. **Hook/Pattern Interrupt** (0-30 seconds)
2. **Big Promise/Headline** (30-60 seconds)
3. **Credibility/Introduction** (1-3 minutes)
4. **Story/Backstory** (3-8 minutes)
5. **Problem Agitation** (8-15 minutes)
6. **Solution Introduction** (15-20 minutes)
7. **Product Presentation** (20-28 minutes)
8. **Proof and Testimonials** (28-33 minutes)
9. **Offer Stack** (33-38 minutes)
10. **Price Reveal and Justification** (38-42 minutes)
11. **Guarantee** (42-44 minutes)
12. **Urgency/Scarcity** (44-46 minutes)
13. **Final Call-to-Action** (46-48 minutes)



### The AIDA Framework for VSLs

**Attention**: Hook viewers immediately with a bold statement, question, or visual that stops them from clicking away.

**Interest**: Build curiosity by presenting a unique mechanism, untold story, or contrarian perspective that makes viewers want to learn more.

**Desire**: Create emotional connection to the outcome by painting vivid pictures of transformation and addressing both pain points and aspirations.

**Action**: Guide viewers to take the specific action you want, whether purchasing, scheduling a call, or opting in.



### The PAS Framework

**Problem**: Identify and articulate the viewer's problem better than they could express it themselves, demonstrating deep understanding.

**Agitation**: Amplify the emotional weight of the problem by exploring consequences, missed opportunities, and the cost of inaction.

**Solution**: Present your offer as the logical, perhaps only, solution to the problem you've established.



### The 4 P's Framework

**Promise**: What transformation or outcome will the viewer achieve?

**Picture**: Help viewers visualize life after the transformation.

**Proof**: Demonstrate that the promise is achievable and has been achieved by others.

**Push**: Create urgency and guide toward immediate action.
