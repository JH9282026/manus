## VSL Production Tips


### Equipment Recommendations

**Entry Level ($200-500)**
- Smartphone with good camera
- Ring light or natural lighting
- Lavalier microphone
- Simple tripod

**Intermediate ($500-2,000)**
- Mirrorless camera or DSLR
- 3-point lighting kit
- USB condenser microphone
- Quality tripod and backdrop

**Professional ($2,000+)**
- Cinema camera or high-end mirrorless
- Professional lighting setup
- Shotgun or studio microphone
- Teleprompter
- Dedicated shooting space


### Software Recommendations

**Video Editing**
- DaVinci Resolve (free/paid)
- Adobe Premiere Pro
- Final Cut Pro
- ScreenFlow (screen recording + editing)
- Camtasia

**Graphics and Animation**
- Canva
- Adobe After Effects
- Apple Motion
- Doodly (whiteboard videos)

**Script Writing**
- Google Docs
- Notion
- Scrivener
- Teleprompter apps


### DIY vs Professional Production

**DIY When:**
- Budget is limited
- Quick iteration is needed
- Authentic, unpolished style fits brand
- Testing concepts before major investment

**Hire Professional When:**
- High-ticket offer
- Brand image is important
- Technical requirements exceed your skills
- Time is more valuable than money


### Production Timeline

**Week 1**: Research, avatar development, competitive analysis
**Week 2**: Script writing and revision
**Week 3**: Pre-production and shoot preparation
**Week 4**: Production (filming/recording)
**Week 5**: Post-production and editing
**Week 6**: Testing, feedback, and refinements
**Week 7**: Launch preparation
**Week 8**: Launch and monitoring
