## Storytelling in VSLs



### The Hero's Journey in VSL Format

**The Ordinary World**: Establish the presenter's life before transformation—relatable struggles, common situation.

**The Call to Adventure**: A moment of realization or crisis that demanded change.

**Refusal of the Call**: Initial resistance, skepticism, or failed attempts.

**Meeting the Mentor**: Discovering the key insight, method, or information.

**Crossing the Threshold**: Committing to the new approach.

**Tests, Allies, Enemies**: Challenges faced during implementation.

**The Ordeal**: A major obstacle that tested commitment.

**The Reward**: Achieving the transformation.

**The Return**: Coming back to share the discovery with others.



### Story Elements That Sell

**Vulnerability**: Sharing genuine struggles builds trust and relatability.

**Specificity**: Concrete details make stories believable ("It was Tuesday, October 15th, when I...")

**Emotion**: Stories that evoke feeling are remembered and persuasive.

**Transformation**: Clear before/after contrast demonstrates what's possible.

**Universal Themes**: Connect personal stories to universal human experiences.



### Types of Stories to Include

1. **Origin Story**: How the presenter discovered the solution
2. **Customer Stories**: Testimonials as narrative
3. **Failure Stories**: Previous failed attempts that led to discovery
4. **Discovery Story**: The "aha moment" that changed everything
5. **Demonstration Stories**: Examples showing the solution in action
