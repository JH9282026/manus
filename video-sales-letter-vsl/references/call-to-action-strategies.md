# Call To Action Strategies

Detailed reference content for the Video Sales Letter Vsl skill.

---

## Call-to-Action Strategies


### CTA Best Practices

**Clarity**: Tell viewers exactly what to do
"Click the orange button below this video that says 'Get Instant Access'"

**Immediacy**: Use action-oriented language
"Get started now" rather than "Think about signing up"

**Simplicity**: Reduce the action to one step
"Just enter your email and you're in"

**Repetition**: State the CTA multiple times
Include CTAs throughout longer VSLs, not just at the end


### CTA Formulas

**The Direct CTA**
"Click the button below right now to get instant access to [product name]."

**The Choice CTA**
"You have two options. You can close this page and [continue struggling]. Or you can click the button below and [start transformation]."

**The Summary CTA**
"Remember, when you click that button, you're getting [list key items]. All for just [price]. Click now."

**The Risk-Reversal CTA**
"Click the button, try it for [guarantee period]. If it doesn't [deliver on promise], I'll refund every penny. You have nothing to lose."


### Multiple CTAs in Long VSLs

For VSLs over 20 minutes, include multiple CTA opportunities:

1. **Soft CTA** (around minute 15-20): "If you've seen enough and you're ready to get started, click the button below."
2. **Primary CTA** (after offer stack): Full CTA with all details
3. **Post-Guarantee CTA**: Reminder after removing risk
4. **Final CTA**: Last opportunity with urgency


### Factors Affecting Optimal Length

**Price Point**
- Under $50: 5-15 minutes
- $50-$500: 15-30 minutes
- $500-$2,000: 30-45 minutes
- Over $2,000: 45-90 minutes

**Audience Awareness**
- Problem-aware: Shorter (they already know they need help)
- Solution-aware: Medium (need differentiation)
- Unaware: Longer (need education)

**Product Complexity**
- Simple, clear products: Shorter
- Complex, multi-faceted offers: Longer
- Requires demonstration: Longer

**Traffic Temperature**
- Cold traffic: Longer (more trust-building needed)
- Warm traffic (email list): Medium
- Hot traffic (retargeting, referrals): Shorter


### Engagement and Retention

Average VSL viewing statistics:
- 50% of viewers watch at least 2 minutes
- 30% watch half the video
- 20% watch to the end
- 10% of viewers convert (highly dependent on other factors)

**Strategies to Improve Retention:**
- Open loops that promise payoff later
- Pattern interrupts every 3-5 minutes
- Emotional peaks throughout
- New information continuously
- Visible progress indicators
- Chapter markers for navigation
