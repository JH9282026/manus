## The Offer and Pricing Presentation



### Building the Offer Stack

**Core Product**: The main deliverable
"First, you're getting the complete [Product Name], including..."

**Bonuses**: Additional value items
"But that's not all. I'm also including [Bonus 1], which alone is worth..."

**Support Elements**: Access, community, coaching
"Plus, you'll get [support element], so you're never alone in this..."

**Fast-Action Bonuses**: Time-limited additions
"And if you act today, you'll also receive [time-sensitive bonus]..."



### Value Stacking

Create perceived value significantly higher than the price:

```
Core Program: $2,000 value
Bonus 1: $500 value
Bonus 2: $300 value
Bonus 3: $700 value
Total Value: $3,500

But you won't pay anywhere near that...
Not $3,500
Not $2,000
Not even $1,000

Today, you can get everything for just $497
```



### Price Anchoring

Establish reference points that make your price feel reasonable:

- Compare to cost of problem continuing
- Compare to cost of alternatives
- Compare to individual coaching at your rate
- Compare to what you've invested to develop the solution
- Compare to what others charge for similar solutions
