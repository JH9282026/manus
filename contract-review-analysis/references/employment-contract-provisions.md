# Employment Contract Key Provisions

Employment contracts establish the legal framework for the employer-employee relationship. Understanding and properly reviewing key provisions protects both parties' rights and prevents future disputes.

## Essential Employment Contract Elements

### 1. Job Description and Duties

Clearly defining the role prevents misunderstandings and sets performance expectations.

**Key Components**

**Job Title and Position**
- Official job title
- Department and reporting structure
- Level within organization
- Position in hierarchy

**Primary Responsibilities**
- Core duties and tasks
- Key deliverables and objectives
- Performance benchmarks
- Scope of authority and decision-making

**Reporting Relationships**
- Direct supervisor/manager
- Dotted-line reporting (if applicable)
- Direct reports (if managerial role)
- Cross-functional relationships

**Work Location and Schedule**
- Primary work location
- Remote/hybrid arrangements
- Standard work hours
- Flexibility and expectations
- Travel requirements

**Review Considerations**

**Clarity and Specificity**
- Are responsibilities clearly defined?
- Is scope of work reasonable and achievable?
- Are performance expectations measurable?
- Is reporting structure clear?

**Flexibility Clause**
- Does contract allow for reasonable duty modifications?
- Is language too broad ("other duties as assigned")?
- Are there limits on scope changes?
- How are significant changes handled?

**Red Flags**
- Vague or overly broad job descriptions
- Unrealistic expectations or workload
- Unclear reporting relationships
- Excessive "other duties" language

**Negotiation Points**
- Clarify ambiguous responsibilities
- Limit scope of "other duties"
- Define process for role changes
- Ensure alignment with discussed role

### 2. Compensation and Benefits

Compensation terms must be clear, compliant, and aligned with expectations.

**Base Compensation**

**Salary or Hourly Wage**
- Amount and currency
- Annual salary vs. hourly rate
- Pay frequency (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly)
- Payment method

**Overtime and Premium Pay**
- Overtime eligibility (exempt vs. non-exempt)
- Overtime rate calculation
- Weekend/holiday premium pay
- On-call or standby compensation

**Variable Compensation**

**Bonuses**
- Types (signing, annual, performance, retention)
- Calculation methodology
- Performance criteria and metrics
- Payment timing and conditions
- Clawback provisions

**Commissions**
- Commission structure and rates
- Calculation methodology
- Payment timing
- Treatment upon termination
- Chargebacks or adjustments

**Equity Compensation**
- Type (stock options, RSUs, phantom stock)
- Grant amount and strike price
- Vesting schedule and conditions
- Acceleration provisions
- Treatment upon termination or change of control

**Benefits**

**Health and Welfare**
- Health insurance (medical, dental, vision)
- Life and disability insurance
- Employee contribution amounts
- Eligibility and waiting periods
- Dependent coverage

**Retirement Benefits**
- 401(k) or pension plan
- Employer matching or contributions
- Vesting schedule
- Eligibility requirements

**Paid Time Off**
- Vacation days and accrual rate
- Sick leave
- Personal days
- Holidays
- Parental leave
- Sabbatical (if applicable)

**Other Benefits**
- Professional development and training
- Tuition reimbursement
- Relocation assistance
- Company car or car allowance
- Technology stipend
- Gym membership or wellness programs

**Review Considerations**

**Compliance**
- Meets minimum wage requirements
- Proper overtime classification (FLSA)
- Complies with equal pay laws
- Adheres to state/local requirements

**Clarity**
- All compensation elements clearly defined
- Calculation methods specified
- Payment timing established
- Conditions and contingencies stated

**Fairness**
- Competitive with market rates
- Aligned with role and experience
- Reasonable performance criteria
- Equitable compared to peers

**Red Flags**
- Below-market compensation
- Vague bonus or commission terms
- Unrealistic performance targets
- Excessive clawback provisions
- Benefits significantly below standard

**Negotiation Points**
- Base salary increase
- Signing or relocation bonus
- Enhanced equity grant
- Accelerated vesting
- Additional vacation time
- Professional development budget

### 3. Term and Termination

Termination provisions define how and when the employment relationship can end.

**Employment Term**

**At-Will Employment**
- Either party can terminate at any time
- With or without cause
- With or without notice
- Standard in most U.S. states

**Fixed-Term Employment**
- Specific start and end dates
- Automatic expiration unless renewed
- Early termination provisions
- Renewal or extension terms

**Termination Rights**

**Termination for Cause**
- Definition of "cause" (misconduct, poor performance, breach)
- Notice requirements
- Opportunity to cure
- Immediate vs. notice period

**Termination Without Cause**
- Employer's right to terminate
- Notice period required
- Severance eligibility
- Garden leave provisions

**Resignation**
- Employee notice requirements
- Transition obligations
- Return of property
- Final compensation

**Notice Periods**

**Standard Notice**
- Typical: 2 weeks to 3 months
- Varies by seniority and jurisdiction
- May be paid in lieu of notice
- Garden leave during notice period

**Severance and Separation Benefits**

**Severance Pay**
- Eligibility criteria
- Calculation method (weeks per year of service)
- Payment timing
- Conditions (release of claims)

**Continuation of Benefits**
- Health insurance continuation (COBRA)
- Duration of coverage
- Employee vs. employer payment

**Outplacement Services**
- Career counseling
- Job search assistance
- Resume and interview coaching

**Post-Termination Obligations**

**Return of Property**
- Company equipment and devices
- Documents and files
- Access credentials
- Company credit cards

**Final Compensation**
- Unpaid salary and wages
- Accrued but unused vacation
- Prorated bonus (if applicable)
- Expense reimbursement
- Equity treatment

**Continuing Obligations**
- Confidentiality (typically survives)
- Non-compete and non-solicitation
- IP assignment
- Cooperation with investigations

**Review Considerations**

**Clarity of Termination Rights**
- Clear definition of "cause"
- Reasonable grounds for termination
- Fair notice requirements
- Adequate severance provisions

**Compliance**
- Meets statutory notice requirements
- Complies with WARN Act (if applicable)
- Adheres to state/local laws
- Proper treatment of accrued benefits

**Red Flags**
- Overly broad "cause" definition
- No severance for without-cause termination
- Inadequate notice periods
- Forfeiture of earned compensation
- Unreasonable post-termination restrictions

**Negotiation Points**
- Narrow "cause" definition
- Longer notice period
- Enhanced severance package
- Accelerated equity vesting upon termination
- Outplacement services
- Non-disparagement provisions

### 4. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

Protecting sensitive information is critical for both parties.

**Scope of Confidential Information**

**Covered Information**
- Trade secrets and proprietary information
- Customer and client lists
- Financial information
- Business strategies and plans
- Technical data and specifications
- Employee information

**Exclusions**
- Information already public
- Information independently developed
- Information received from third parties
- Information required to be disclosed by law

**Obligations**

**During Employment**
- Maintain confidentiality
- Use only for business purposes
- Protect from unauthorized access
- Report breaches or suspected breaches

**After Employment**
- Continuing confidentiality obligations
- Return or destruction of information
- No use for personal benefit or competitors
- Duration of post-employment obligations (typically 2-5 years or indefinite for trade secrets)

**Permitted Disclosures**
- To authorized employees and contractors
- As required by law or court order
- To legal and financial advisors
- With prior written consent

**Review Considerations**

**Reasonableness**
- Definition not overly broad
- Exclusions are comprehensive
- Duration is reasonable
- Obligations are clear and achievable

**Compliance**
- Aligns with trade secret laws
- Complies with whistleblower protections
- Permits legally required disclosures

**Red Flags**
- Overly broad definition of confidential information
- Indefinite obligations for non-trade secret information
- No exceptions for legally required disclosures
- Restrictions on discussing employment terms

**Negotiation Points**
- Narrow definition of confidential information
- Clear exclusions and exceptions
- Reasonable duration (2-3 years for non-trade secrets)
- Explicit whistleblower protections
- Ability to discuss compensation with colleagues

### 5. Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation

Post-employment restrictions must be reasonable to be enforceable.

**Non-Compete Provisions**

**Scope**
- Prohibited activities (working for competitor, starting competing business)
- Geographic scope (city, state, country)
- Duration (typically 6 months to 2 years)
- Industry or market segment

**Reasonableness Factors**
- Necessary to protect legitimate business interests
- Not overly broad in scope, geography, or duration
- Does not impose undue hardship on employee
- Complies with state law (some states ban or limit non-competes)

**Non-Solicitation Provisions**

**Customer/Client Non-Solicitation**
- Prohibited from soliciting company customers
- Definition of "customer" (active, recent, prospective)
- Duration (typically 1-2 years)
- Geographic scope (if any)

**Employee Non-Solicitation**
- Prohibited from recruiting company employees
- Definition of "employee" (all, or specific roles/levels)
- Duration (typically 1-2 years)
- Exceptions (general job postings, unsolicited approaches)

**Non-Interference**
- Prohibited from interfering with business relationships
- Includes vendors, suppliers, partners
- Duration and scope

**Review Considerations**

**Enforceability**
- Complies with state law (some states ban non-competes)
- Reasonable in scope, geography, and duration
- Supported by consideration (employment, promotion, severance)
- Protects legitimate business interests

**Impact on Career**
- Does it prevent you from working in your field?
- Is geographic scope reasonable for your industry?
- Can you earn a living during restriction period?
- Are there carve-outs for certain roles or companies?

**Red Flags**
- Overly broad geographic scope (nationwide or global)
- Excessive duration (>2 years)
- Prevents all work in industry
- No consideration provided
- In state that bans or limits non-competes (CA, ND, OK)

**Negotiation Points**
- Eliminate or narrow non-compete
- Reduce geographic scope
- Shorten duration
- Carve out specific companies or roles
- Require severance payment during restriction period
- Focus on non-solicitation instead of non-compete

### 6. Intellectual Property Assignment

IP provisions determine ownership of work created during employment.

**Scope of Assignment**

**Work-for-Hire**
- All work created within scope of employment
- Automatically owned by employer
- Includes inventions, designs, writings, software

**Inventions and Discoveries**
- Inventions related to company business
- Inventions using company resources
- Inventions during work hours
- Assignment of all rights to employer

**Excluded Inventions**
- Inventions created entirely on own time
- Without company equipment or resources
- Not related to company business
- Not resulting from company work

**Prior Inventions**
- List of inventions created before employment
- Excluded from assignment
- Protects employee's pre-existing IP

**Obligations**

**Disclosure**
- Promptly disclose all inventions
- Provide sufficient detail
- Assist in determining ownership

**Assignment and Cooperation**
- Execute assignment documents
- Assist in obtaining patents/copyrights
- Provide testimony if needed
- Cooperation survives employment

**Moral Rights Waiver**
- Waive attribution and integrity rights
- Allow modifications and derivative works
- Permit use without credit

**Review Considerations**

**Reasonableness**
- Scope limited to work-related creations
- Adequate protection for personal projects
- Complies with state laws (e.g., California Labor Code § 2870)
- Clear definition of what is assigned

**Protection of Personal IP**
- Ability to list prior inventions
- Exclusion for personal projects
- Clear boundaries between work and personal

**Red Flags**
- Assignment of all inventions, regardless of relation to work
- No exclusion for personal projects
- Overly broad definition of company resources
- No ability to list prior inventions
- Violates state law protections

**Negotiation Points**
- Narrow scope to work-related inventions
- Explicit exclusion for personal projects
- List prior inventions
- Clarify what constitutes "company resources"
- Ensure compliance with state law (e.g., CA Labor Code § 2870)

### 7. Dispute Resolution and Governing Law

These provisions determine how conflicts are resolved.

**Governing Law**
- Which state's laws apply
- Important for employment law variations
- Typically employer's state

**Dispute Resolution Mechanism**

**Litigation**
- Traditional court process
- Public proceedings
- Right to jury trial
- Full discovery
- Appealable

**Arbitration**
- Private dispute resolution
- Binding decision by arbitrator
- Limited discovery
- Generally not appealable
- Potentially faster and cheaper

**Mediation**
- Facilitated negotiation
- Non-binding
- Preserves relationship
- Often required before arbitration/litigation

**Multi-Step Process**
- Negotiation → Mediation → Arbitration
- Escalating formality
- Encourages early resolution

**Arbitration Provisions**

**Scope**
- What disputes are covered
- Exclusions (injunctive relief for IP, confidentiality)

**Rules and Administrator**
- AAA, JAMS, or other arbitration organization
- Employment-specific rules

**Location and Costs**
- Venue for arbitration
- Fee allocation (employer typically pays arbitrator fees)

**Class Action Waiver**
- Prohibition on class or collective actions
- Individual arbitration only
- Enforceability varies by jurisdiction

**Review Considerations**

**Fairness**
- Mutual obligation to arbitrate
- Reasonable venue
- Fair cost allocation
- Preserves substantive rights

**Enforceability**
- Complies with FAA and state law
- Not unconscionable
- Adequate consideration

**Impact**
- Loss of jury trial right
- Limited discovery and appeal
- Confidentiality of proceedings
- Potential cost savings

**Red Flags**
- One-sided arbitration (only employee must arbitrate)
- Employer chooses arbitrator
- Employee pays arbitration costs
- Unreasonable venue (distant location)
- Shortened statute of limitations
- Waiver of substantive rights

**Negotiation Points**
- Mutual arbitration obligation
- Neutral arbitrator selection
- Employer pays arbitration fees
- Reasonable venue
- Preserve right to seek injunctive relief
- Opt-out right for arbitration
- Remove class action waiver

### 8. Additional Important Provisions

**Company Policies and Handbook**
- Incorporation by reference
- Requirement to comply
- Acknowledgment of receipt
- Understanding that policies can change

**Background Checks and Contingencies**
- Offer contingent on background check
- Drug testing requirements
- Reference checks
- Verification of credentials

**Immigration and Work Authorization**
- Requirement to provide work authorization
- I-9 compliance
- Visa sponsorship (if applicable)

**Representations and Warranties**
- Employee's authority to enter contract
- No conflicting obligations
- Accuracy of information provided

**Entire Agreement and Amendments**
- Contract supersedes prior agreements
- Modifications must be in writing
- Signed by authorized representative

**Severability**
- Invalid provisions severed
- Remainder remains enforceable
- Reformation to make enforceable if possible

**Indemnification**
- Employer indemnifies employee for authorized acts
- Employee indemnifies employer for misconduct
- Scope and limitations

## Employment Contract Review Checklist

### Before Signing
- [ ] Read entire contract carefully
- [ ] Understand all terms and obligations
- [ ] Verify compensation and benefits details
- [ ] Check termination and severance provisions
- [ ] Review restrictive covenants (non-compete, confidentiality)
- [ ] Assess IP assignment provisions
- [ ] Understand dispute resolution mechanism
- [ ] Identify any red flags or concerns
- [ ] Consider consulting employment attorney
- [ ] Negotiate unfavorable terms
- [ ] Obtain clarification on ambiguities
- [ ] Ensure all verbal promises are in writing
- [ ] Keep copy of signed contract

### Red Flags Requiring Legal Review
- Overly broad non-compete or confidentiality
- Below-market compensation
- Unfair termination provisions
- One-sided arbitration clause
- Excessive IP assignment
- Unclear or unreasonable job duties
- Significant discrepancies from offer letter
