# Brand Guidelines Management & Evolution

Create comprehensive brand guidelines and manage strategic brand evolution.

## Overview

This skill covers brand guidelines creation, governance frameworks, brand management processes, and strategic evolution planning. It ensures brands maintain consistency while adapting to changing markets, technologies, and organizational needs.

## Brand Guidelines Structure

### Core Sections

| Section | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| Brand Strategy | Purpose, vision, mission, values, positioning |
| Logo System | Primary/secondary logos, clear space, sizing |
| Color Palette | Primary, secondary, neutral with specifications |
| Typography | Typefaces, hierarchy, usage rules |
| Imagery | Photography, illustration style, icons |
| Voice & Tone | Writing guidelines, messaging |
| Applications | Templates, examples, specifications |
| Governance | Approval processes, contacts |

### Logo Specifications

**Document for each logo version:**
- Clear space requirements (measured in logo units)
- Minimum size (print and digital)
- Color variations (full color, mono, reversed)
- Incorrect usage examples (stretching, recoloring, etc.)
- File formats and naming conventions

### Color Specifications

**For each brand color, provide:**
- HEX code (web)
- RGB values (digital)
- CMYK values (print)
- Pantone/PMS (brand-critical print)
- Accessibility contrast ratios

## Brand Governance Framework

### Approval Tiers

| Material Type | Approver | Turnaround |
|--------------|----------|------------|
| Brand-critical (logo, campaigns) | Brand Director | 3-5 days |
| Marketing collateral | Marketing Manager | 2-3 days |
| Internal communications | Department Lead | 1 day |
| Templates/standard use | Self-service | Immediate |

### Compliance Monitoring

- Regular brand audits (quarterly/annual)
- Touchpoint consistency reviews
- Partner/vendor compliance checks
- Digital asset management systems

## Brand Evolution Planning

### When to Evolve

- Market positioning shift required
- Merger/acquisition integration
- Modernization needed
- Expansion to new markets
- Competitive repositioning

### Evolution vs Revolution

| Evolution | Revolution |
|-----------|------------|
| Incremental refinement | Complete transformation |
| Maintains recognition | Creates new identity |
| Lower risk | Higher risk, higher impact |
| Existing equity preserved | Requires new equity building |
