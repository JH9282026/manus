## Transition Strategies

### Direct Pivot

**When to Use**: High skill transferability, clear target role

**Approach**:
1. Map transferable skills to target role requirements
2. Reframe experience in resume and LinkedIn
3. Network into target industry/function
4. Apply strategically to receptive companies

**Timeline**: 3-6 months

### Bridge Role

**When to Use**: Significant skill gaps, need stepping stone

**Approach**:
1. Identify role that combines current and target skills
2. Use bridge role to build credibility in new area
3. Continue building target skills while employed
4. Transition to final target from bridge

**Timeline**: 12-24 months (including bridge role tenure)

### Lateral Transfer

**When to Use**: Same function, different industry

**Approach**:
1. Leverage functional expertise
2. Research target industry deeply
3. Network with industry insiders
4. Position as "fresh perspective" plus functional expertise

**Timeline**: 3-9 months

### Skills-First Transition

**When to Use**: Technical transitions requiring credentials

**Approach**:
1. Identify required skills/certifications
2. Complete training while employed
3. Build portfolio of projects
4. Apply once credentialed

**Timeline**: 6-18 months (depending on training required)

### Portfolio Career

**When to Use**: Seeking flexibility, multiple interests

**Approach**:
1. Start consulting/freelancing part-time
2. Build client base in target area
3. Transition to full-time independent work
4. Or use portfolio as bridge to full-time role

**Timeline**: 6-12 months to establish

## Skills Gap Analysis

### Gap Identification Process

1. **List target role requirements**
   - Review 10+ job descriptions
   - Note repeated skills and qualifications

2. **Assess current state**
   - Rate yourself on each requirement (1-5)
   - Gather external feedback if possible

3. **Prioritize gaps**
   - Must-have vs nice-to-have
   - Learnable vs difficult to develop

4. **Create development plan**
   - Resources for each gap
   - Timeline for development

### Gap Closing Strategies

| Gap Type | Strategy | Resources |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Technical Skills | Courses, certifications, projects | Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, bootcamps |
| Industry Knowledge | Reading, networking, informational interviews | Industry publications, conferences |
| Experience | Side projects, volunteering, consulting | Pro bono work, internal transfers |
| Credentials | Formal education, certifications | University programs, professional certs |
| Network | Networking, community involvement | Professional associations, LinkedIn |

## Narrative Development

### Transition Story Framework

**Structure**:
1. **Background**: Where you came from
2. **Trigger**: What prompted the change
3. **Connection**: How experiences connect to target
4. **Vision**: Where you're heading

**Example**:
"After 8 years in management consulting [background], I realized I wanted to have a deeper impact on the products people use daily [trigger]. Throughout my consulting career, I led product strategy engagements and became fascinated with how great products are built [connection]. Now I'm focused on transitioning to product management where I can combine my strategic thinking with hands-on product development [vision]."

### Addressing Concerns

| Concern | Reframe |
|---------|--------|
| "You don't have direct experience" | "I bring fresh perspective plus transferable skills" |
| "Why the change?" | Tell compelling, authentic story |
| "Will you stay?" | Show commitment through preparation |
| "You're overqualified" | Emphasize what you'll gain, not lose |

## Networking for Transitions

### Informational Interview Process

1. **Identify targets**
   - People in target role/industry
   - 2nd-degree connections preferred

2. **Request meeting**
   - Short, specific ask (20-30 minutes)
   - Clear purpose (learning, not job asking)

3. **Prepare questions**
   - Day-to-day role
   - Path to role
   - Skills needed
   - Advice for someone transitioning

4. **Follow up**
   - Thank you note
   - Stay in touch
   - Ask for additional connections

### Networking Script

"Hi [Name], I'm a [current role] exploring a transition into [target area]. I came across your profile and was impressed by your path from [background] to [current role]. I'd love to learn from your experience—would you be open to a brief 20-minute conversation? I'm particularly interested in [specific question]."

## Financial Planning for Transitions

### Runway Calculation

| Expense Category | Monthly Amount |
|------------------|---------------|
| Fixed Expenses | Rent, insurance, loan payments |
| Variable Expenses | Food, transportation, utilities |
| Transition Costs | Training, certifications, networking |
| Buffer | Emergency fund |

**Runway = Savings / (Monthly Expenses + Transition Costs)**

### Financial Strategies

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Transition while employed | Safest, but slower |
| Sabbatical | Focused time, requires savings |
| Part-time bridge | Income + time for transition |
| Consulting bridge | Income in related area + flexibility |

## Common Transition Paths

### Consulting → Industry

**Leverage**: Problem-solving, client management, strategic thinking
**Build**: Domain depth, operational experience, people management

### Military → Corporate

**Leverage**: Leadership, discipline, operations, security
**Build**: Corporate culture fluency, technical skills, industry knowledge

### Technical → Management

**Leverage**: Technical credibility, problem-solving
**Build**: People management, strategic thinking, business acumen

### Individual Contributor → Leadership

**Leverage**: Domain expertise, execution track record
**Build**: Delegation, coaching, strategic planning

### Industry Change

**Leverage**: Functional expertise, transferable skills
**Build**: Industry knowledge, network, vocabulary
