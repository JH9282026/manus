## Interview Preparation

### Company Research Checklist

| Area | Research Sources |
|------|------------------|
| Company Overview | Website, Wikipedia, press releases |
| Products/Services | Product pages, demos, reviews |
| Industry Position | Analyst reports, news articles |
| Culture | Glassdoor, LinkedIn, company blog |
| Leadership | LinkedIn, company bios, interviews |
| Recent News | Google News, press releases |
| Competitors | Industry analysis, comparison sites |
| Challenges | Industry reports, analyst commentary |

### Company Research Brief Template

```
## [Company Name] Research Brief

**Overview**: [1-2 sentence description]

**Products/Services**: [Key offerings]

**Industry Position**: [Market position, competitors]

**Recent News**: [Key developments]

**Culture & Values**: [From Glassdoor, employee reviews]

**Leadership**: [Key executives]

**Why I'm Interested**: [Genuine reasons]

**Questions to Ask**: [3-5 prepared questions]
```

## STAR Story Development

### Story Categories to Prepare

| Category | Stories Needed |
|----------|---------------|
| Leadership | 2-3 examples |
| Problem-Solving | 2-3 examples |
| Teamwork | 2-3 examples |
| Conflict Resolution | 1-2 examples |
| Failure/Learning | 1-2 examples |
| Initiative | 1-2 examples |
| Achievement | 2-3 examples |

### STAR Story Template

```
## Story Title: [Brief descriptor]

**Competency**: [What this demonstrates]

**Situation**: [Brief context - 2-3 sentences]
- When/where
- What was at stake

**Task**: [Your responsibility - 1-2 sentences]
- What you needed to accomplish

**Action**: [What YOU did - 3-5 sentences]
- Specific steps taken
- Focus on YOUR contributions

**Result**: [Outcome - 2-3 sentences]
- Quantified impact
- What you learned
```

### Story Example

**Story: Turning Around Underperforming Product**

**Competency**: Leadership, Problem-Solving

**Situation**: When I joined as Product Manager, our enterprise product was losing 15% of customers annually and had a negative NPS of -12. The previous PM had left, and the team was demoralized.

**Task**: I needed to stop the customer bleeding, improve satisfaction, and rebuild team morale within 6 months.

**Action**: 
- First, I conducted 30 customer interviews to understand root causes of dissatisfaction
- Identified that 80% of complaints centered on three features, so I ruthlessly prioritized those fixes
- Established weekly team standups and monthly retrospectives to rebuild team communication
- Created a customer advisory board to co-develop our roadmap

**Result**: Within 8 months, we reduced churn to 5%, improved NPS from -12 to +35, and shipped 12 customer-requested features. The team's engagement scores increased from 2.8 to 4.2 out of 5.

## Question Categories

### Behavioral Questions

| Question | STAR Story to Use |
|----------|-------------------|
| "Tell me about a time you led a difficult project" | Leadership story |
| "Describe a conflict with a colleague" | Conflict resolution |
| "When did you fail? What did you learn?" | Failure/learning |
| "Tell me about your biggest achievement" | Achievement story |
| "How do you handle tight deadlines?" | Problem-solving |

### Motivation Questions

| Question | Answer Framework |
|----------|------------------|
| "Why this company?" | Specific research + genuine connection |
| "Why this role?" | Skills match + career goals |
| "Why are you leaving?" | Positive framing + growth focus |
| "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" | Realistic growth + company connection |

### Weakness Questions

**Framework**: Real weakness + awareness + action taken

**Example**: "I used to struggle with delegation—I'd take on too much myself. I've worked on this by implementing a weekly task review where I consciously identify what can be delegated, and I've built a tracking system to follow up without micromanaging. It's still something I'm mindful of, but I've seen real improvement in my team's growth and my own capacity."

## Questions to Ask Interviewers

### Role Questions

- "What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?"
- "What are the biggest challenges the person in this role will face?"
- "How does this role contribute to the team/company goals?"

### Team/Culture Questions

- "How would you describe the team culture?"
- "What do you enjoy most about working here?"
- "How does the team collaborate across functions?"

### Growth Questions

- "What opportunities for growth and development exist?"
- "How do you support professional development?"
- "Where have successful people in this role gone next?"

### Closing Questions

- "Is there anything about my background that concerns you that I can address?"
- "What are the next steps in the process?"

## Interview Formats

### Phone Screen

| Duration | 20-30 minutes |
|----------|---------------|
| Focus | Fit check, basic qualifications |
| Preparation | Resume talking points, company basics |
| Tips | Stand up, smile, have notes ready |

### Video Interview

| Duration | 30-60 minutes |
|----------|---------------|
| Focus | Deeper exploration, behavioral |
| Preparation | Test technology, professional background |
| Tips | Camera at eye level, good lighting, minimize distractions |

### Panel Interview

| Duration | 45-60 minutes |
|----------|---------------|
| Focus | Multiple perspectives |
| Preparation | Research all panelists |
| Tips | Make eye contact with all, address questioner |

### Case Interview

| Duration | 30-45 minutes |
|----------|---------------|
| Focus | Problem-solving approach |
| Preparation | Practice frameworks |
| Tips | Think aloud, ask clarifying questions, structure approach |
