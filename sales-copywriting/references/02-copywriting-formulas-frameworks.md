# Copywriting Formulas and Frameworks

## AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

**When to Use**: Unaware or lightly aware audiences, homepage hero sections, social ads, email newsletters

**Structure**:
1. **Attention**: Catchy hook, bold statement, question, or statistic
2. **Interest**: Engaging information, captivating facts, stories, use cases
3. **Desire**: Demonstrate benefits, paint picture of improved life
4. **Action**: Clear, specific next step (sign up, download, purchase)

**Example**:
- Attention: "Tired of Wasting Hours on Social Media?"
- Interest: "Our app helps you track and limit your screen time automatically"
- Desire: "Imagine having 2 extra hours every day for things you love"
- Action: "Download Free Today"

## PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution)

**When to Use**: Problem-aware audiences, lead magnets, landing pages, email subject lines

**Structure**:
1. **Problem**: Identify specific problem reader experiences
2. **Agitation**: Intensify problem, detail negative consequences
3. **Solution**: Present product/service as definitive answer

**Example**:
- Problem: "Struggling to lose weight despite diet and exercise?"
- Agitation: "Every failed attempt chips away at your confidence and health"
- Solution: "Our personalized nutrition program has helped 10,000+ people lose weight and keep it off"

## Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

**When to Use**: Solution-aware audiences, sales page sections, testimonials, case studies

**Structure**:
1. **Before**: Current problematic situation
2. **After**: Vision of better future with problem solved
3. **Bridge**: How to get there (your solution)

## Promise-Picture-Proof-Push (PPPP)

**When to Use**: High-ticket items, sales pages, webinar registrations

**Structure**:
1. **Promise**: Declare how product will change their life
2. **Picture**: Paint specific, realistic image of transformation
3. **Proof**: Back up with facts, testimonials, research, case studies
4. **Push**: Prompt action with bonuses, discounts, guarantees

## FAB (Features, Advantages, Benefits)

**When to Use**: Product pages, price tables, feature lists

**Structure**:
1. **Feature**: What the product does
2. **Advantage**: Why that feature matters
3. **Benefit**: What result it gives the user

**Example**:
- Feature: "Cloud-based storage"
- Advantage: "Access files from any device"
- Benefit: "Work seamlessly whether you're at home, office, or traveling"

## The 4 P's Framework

**Picture**: Paint vivid scenario resonating with reader's problems/goals
**Promise**: Make specific promise based on product's best benefit
**Proof**: Provide credible evidence (facts, testimonials, comparisons)
**Push**: Guide to immediate action with urgency and ease

## Conclusion

Copywriting formulas provide proven structures for persuasive messaging. Choose based on audience awareness level, content type, and specific goals. Often, combining elements from multiple formulas creates the most effective copy.
