# Expansion and Monetization

Strategies for driving product-led expansion within accounts and converting free users to paying customers.

---

## Product-Led Expansion

### Understanding Expansion

**Definition:** Expansion is growth in revenue from existing customers through increased usage, additional users, feature upgrades, or cross-sells.

**Why Expansion Matters:**
- More cost-effective than new customer acquisition
- Indicates product value and satisfaction
- Drives Net Revenue Retention (NRR) above 100%
- Creates compounding growth
- Validates product-market fit

**Expansion in PLG:**
- Driven by product usage and value realization
- Self-service upgrade paths
- Viral growth within accounts
- Usage-based pricing naturally captures expansion

### Types of Product-Led Expansion

#### 1. User Expansion (Viral Growth)

**Definition:** Existing users invite or bring in additional users from their organization.

**Mechanisms:**

**Collaboration Features:**
- Product requires or benefits from multiple users
- Sharing, commenting, real-time collaboration
- Team workspaces and projects

**Invitation Mechanics:**
- Easy, in-product invitation flows
- Email invites with clear value proposition
- Incentives for inviting others (referral bonuses)

**Visibility and Sharing:**
- Shared links, documents, or outputs
- Recipients see product branding and value
- "Created with [Product]" attribution

**Network Effects:**
- Product becomes more valuable with more users
- Communication, collaboration, marketplace dynamics

**Examples:**
- **Slack:** Channels and mentions drive team-wide adoption
- **Figma:** Real-time collaboration requires inviting teammates
- **Notion:** Shared workspaces and pages
- **Loom:** Video recipients see Loom branding, driving awareness
- **Calendly:** Sharing scheduling links promotes product

**Tactics to Encourage User Expansion:**

**Make Inviting Easy:**
- Prominent "Invite Team" buttons
- Bulk invite options (paste email list)
- Integration with company directory (Active Directory, Google Workspace)

**Incentivize Invitations:**
- Referral bonuses (free storage, credits, features)
- Unlock features when team reaches size threshold
- Gamification ("Invite 5 teammates to unlock...")

**Highlight Collaboration Value:**
- Show what's possible with team (vs. solo)
- Case studies of team success
- In-app prompts: "This would be easier with your team"

**Reduce Friction for Invitees:**
- No account required to view shared content
- Easy signup process for invitees
- Onboarding tailored for invited users

#### 2. Usage Expansion

**Definition:** Existing users increase their usage frequency, depth, or breadth.

**Drivers:**

**Increased Frequency:**
- Product becomes part of daily workflow
- Habit formation and routine integration
- Notifications and reminders

**Deeper Usage:**
- Adoption of advanced features
- More complex workflows and use cases
- Power user behaviors

**Broader Usage:**
- Expansion to additional departments or projects
- New use cases discovered
- Cross-functional adoption

**Monetization:**
- Usage-based pricing captures expansion automatically
- Tiered pricing with usage limits
- Overage charges or automatic upgrades

**Examples:**
- **AWS:** Pay for compute, storage, bandwidth used (scales with usage)
- **Twilio:** Pay per API call or message sent
- **Snowflake:** Pay for data storage and compute time
- **Stripe:** Percentage of transaction volume

**Tactics to Encourage Usage Expansion:**

**Feature Discovery:**
- Highlight underutilized features
- Contextual prompts: "Did you know you can...?"
- Email campaigns showcasing advanced capabilities

**Use Case Education:**
- Show how product solves additional problems
- Case studies and customer stories
- Templates for different scenarios

**Proactive Insights:**
- Usage reports and analytics
- Recommendations based on behavior
- "Users like you also use..."

**Remove Barriers:**
- Simplify access to advanced features
- Improve performance and reliability
- Integrate with more tools

#### 3. Feature Expansion (Upsells)

**Definition:** Users upgrade to higher tiers or add premium features.

**Triggers:**

**Usage Limits:**
- User hits free tier limit (storage, users, projects)
- "You've reached your limit. Upgrade to continue."

**Feature Needs:**
- User tries to access premium feature
- "Unlock [feature] with Pro plan"

**Performance or Scale:**
- Need for higher performance, uptime, or capacity
- Enterprise security or compliance requirements

**Support and Services:**
- Need for priority support or dedicated account manager
- Professional services or training

**Examples:**
- **Slack:** Free tier limited to 10,000 messages and 10 integrations
- **Notion:** Free for personal use, paid for teams and advanced features
- **Zoom:** Free 40-minute meetings, paid for longer and more features
- **Canva:** Free tier with limited templates and features, Pro for full access

**Tactics to Encourage Feature Expansion:**

**In-Product Prompts:**
- Contextual upgrade offers when user encounters limit
- "Unlock this feature" buttons on premium features
- Comparison of current vs. upgraded capabilities

**Trial Premium Features:**
- Temporary access to premium features
- "Try Pro for 14 days free"
- Demonstrate value before asking for payment

**Value-Based Messaging:**
- Highlight benefits, not just features
- ROI calculators or value demonstrations
- Social proof ("Join 10,000+ Pro users")

**Seamless Upgrade Flow:**
- One-click upgrade from within product
- No sales call required
- Immediate access upon payment

#### 4. Cross-Sell

**Definition:** Selling complementary products or modules to existing customers.

**Approaches:**

**Product Suite:**
- Multiple products that work together
- Discounts for bundling
- Unified billing and administration

**Add-On Modules:**
- Core product with optional add-ons
- Pay for what you need
- Modular pricing

**Ecosystem Products:**
- Marketplace of third-party integrations
- Revenue share with partners
- Expand value without building everything

**Examples:**
- **HubSpot:** Marketing Hub, Sales Hub, Service Hub, CMS Hub
- **Atlassian:** Jira, Confluence, Trello, Bitbucket
- **Salesforce:** Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, etc.
- **Shopify:** Core e-commerce platform + apps and themes marketplace

**Tactics to Encourage Cross-Sell:**

**Integrated Experiences:**
- Seamless navigation between products
- Unified data and workflows
- Single sign-on and billing

**Contextual Recommendations:**
- Suggest complementary products based on usage
- "Users of [Product A] also use [Product B]"
- In-app prompts at relevant moments

**Bundling and Discounts:**
- Package deals for multiple products
- "Save 20% with the full suite"
- Upgrade paths from single to multi-product

### Expansion Metrics

**1. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**

**Definition:** Percentage of revenue retained from existing customers, including expansion and churn.

**Calculation:**
```
NRR = ((Starting MRR + Expansion MRR - Churned MRR - Contraction MRR) ÷ Starting MRR) × 100
```

**Interpretation:**
- NRR > 100%: Expansion exceeds churn (ideal)
- NRR = 100%: Expansion equals churn (neutral)
- NRR < 100%: Churn exceeds expansion (problem)

**Benchmarks:**
- Excellent: > 120%
- Good: 110-120%
- Acceptable: 100-110%
- Poor: < 100%

**Why It Matters:**
- Indicates product value and customer satisfaction
- Enables growth even without new customer acquisition
- Key metric for SaaS valuation

**2. Expansion Revenue**

**Definition:** Revenue generated from existing customers through upsells, cross-sells, or usage growth.

**Measurement:**
- Absolute dollars of expansion revenue
- Expansion revenue as % of total revenue
- Expansion revenue per account

**Target:** 20-40% of total revenue from expansion (varies by business model)

**3. Product-Qualified Accounts (PQA)**

**Definition:** Existing accounts showing signals of readiness for expansion.

**Signals:**
- High engagement or usage
- Approaching or hitting usage limits
- Multiple users or departments using product
- Exploring premium features
- Positive sentiment in support interactions

**Use:** Prioritize accounts for expansion outreach or automated campaigns.

**4. Seats per Account**

**Definition:** Average number of users per paying account.

**Calculation:** Total seats ÷ Number of accounts

**Trend:** Should grow over time as accounts expand.

**Use:** Track user expansion within accounts.

**5. Feature Adoption Rate**

**Definition:** Percentage of users adopting premium or advanced features.

**Calculation:** (Users using feature ÷ Total users) × 100

**Use:**
- Identify features driving upgrades
- Prioritize feature development
- Target users for upsell based on feature interest

## Monetization Models

### Freemium Model

**Definition:** Free tier with limited features or usage; paid tiers unlock more value.

**Structure:**

**Free Tier:**
- Genuinely useful, not just a trial
- Sufficient for individuals or small teams
- Limited by features, usage, or users
- Serves as top-of-funnel for paid tiers

**Paid Tiers:**
- Remove limitations of free tier
- Add premium features and capabilities
- Provide better support and SLAs
- Scale with customer size and needs

**When Freemium Works:**
- Low marginal cost per user
- Large addressable market (need volume)
- Product delivers value in free tier
- Clear differentiation between free and paid
- Viral or network effects

**Freemium Limitation Strategies:**

**1. Feature-Limited**

**Approach:** Free tier has subset of features; paid unlocks more.

**Examples:**
- **Slack:** Free tier limited to 10 integrations, 10,000 message history
- **Grammarly:** Free tier for basic grammar; paid for advanced suggestions
- **Canva:** Free tier with limited templates and features

**Pros:**
- Clear value differentiation
- Easy to understand

**Cons:**
- Must ensure free tier is still valuable
- Risk of free tier being "good enough"

**2. Usage-Limited**

**Approach:** Free tier has usage caps (storage, API calls, projects); paid increases limits.

**Examples:**
- **Mailchimp:** Free up to 500 contacts
- **Dropbox:** Free 2GB storage
- **Airtable:** Free tier limited to 1,200 records per base

**Pros:**
- Natural upgrade trigger (hit limit)
- Scales with customer value

**Cons:**
- Users may game limits or create multiple accounts
- Requires usage tracking and enforcement

**3. User-Limited**

**Approach:** Free for individuals; paid for teams or organizations.

**Examples:**
- **Notion:** Free for personal use, paid for teams
- **Figma:** Free for individuals, paid for teams
- **Todoist:** Free for individuals, paid for teams and collaboration

**Pros:**
- Encourages individual adoption, then team expansion
- Clear upgrade path as usage grows

**Cons:**
- Individuals may not convert if solo use sufficient
- Requires team features to drive upgrade

**4. Time-Limited (Reverse Trial)**

**Approach:** Free tier with time-based restrictions (e.g., limited monthly usage).

**Examples:**
- **Canva:** Limited number of designs per month on free tier
- **Loom:** Limited video length or number of videos on free tier

**Pros:**
- Encourages regular use and habit formation
- Natural upgrade trigger (run out of monthly quota)

**Cons:**
- Can frustrate users if limits too restrictive
- Requires careful balance

**5. Hybrid**

**Approach:** Combination of feature, usage, and user limits.

**Examples:**
- **Dropbox:** Storage limit + feature limits (e.g., no remote wipe)
- **Slack:** Message history limit + integration limit + feature limits

**Pros:**
- Multiple upgrade triggers
- Flexibility to optimize conversion

**Cons:**
- Can be complex to communicate
- Risk of confusing users

**Freemium Best Practices:**

**Make Free Tier Valuable:**
- Not just a crippled trial
- Genuinely useful for target segment (individuals, small teams)
- Builds trust and goodwill

**Clear Upgrade Path:**
- Obvious when and why to upgrade
- Transparent pricing and benefits
- Seamless upgrade flow

**Avoid Annoying Upsells:**
- Contextual, not constant
- Highlight value, not just features
- Respect user's choice to stay free

**Monitor Conversion Rate:**
- Target: 2-5% free-to-paid conversion
- Optimize through experimentation
- Balance free tier generosity with conversion

### Free Trial Model

**Definition:** Full product access for limited time (typically 7-30 days); requires payment after trial.

**Structure:**

**Trial Period:**
- Full access to all features
- Time-limited (7, 14, 30 days common)
- Goal: Experience full value before buying

**Post-Trial:**
- Payment required to continue
- Downgrade to limited free tier (if available)
- Or lose access entirely

**When Free Trial Works:**
- Product requires time to demonstrate full value
- Higher price point justifies trial period
- Complex features need exploration
- B2B or enterprise focus

**Trial Design Decisions:**

**1. Trial Length**

**7 Days:**
- Simple products with quick time-to-value
- Creates urgency
- Lower conversion but higher intent signups

**14 Days:**
- Most common, balanced approach
- Sufficient for most products
- Good conversion rates

**30 Days:**
- Complex products requiring longer evaluation
- Enterprise or high-touch sales
- Risk of users forgetting or losing momentum

**Unlimited (Until Usage Threshold):**
- Trial until user hits usage limit
- Aligns trial end with value realization
- Example: "Free until 100 contacts"

**2. Credit Card Requirement**

**Credit Card Required:**

**Pros:**
- Higher intent signups (serious buyers)
- Better trial-to-paid conversion (30-40%)
- Automatic conversion at trial end
- Reduces fraud and abuse

**Cons:**
- Lower signup volume (friction)
- User concerns about forgetting to cancel
- May exclude users without cards

**No Credit Card Required:**

**Pros:**
- Higher signup volume (lower friction)
- Broader reach
- Builds trust

**Cons:**
- Lower trial-to-paid conversion (15-25%)
- More tire-kickers and non-serious users
- Requires active decision to purchase

**Hybrid Approach:**
- No card required for basic trial
- Card required for extended trial or premium features
- Balances volume and conversion

**3. Trial Experience**

**Full Access:**
- All features available
- No artificial limitations
- True evaluation of product

**Onboarding and Support:**
- Proactive onboarding during trial
- Email drip campaign with tips and use cases
- Responsive support to answer questions
- Goal: Maximize activation during trial

**Trial Reminders:**
- Email reminders as trial end approaches
- "3 days left in your trial"
- Highlight value received and benefits of continuing
- Clear CTA to upgrade

**Trial-to-Paid Conversion Tactics:**

**Optimize Activation During Trial:**
- Guide users to aha moment quickly
- Ensure they experience core value
- Track activation rate during trial

**Proactive Engagement:**
- Email sequences with tips and best practices
- In-app messages highlighting features
- Personal outreach for high-value trials (enterprise)

**Reduce Friction in Payment:**
- One-click upgrade from within product
- Multiple payment options (card, invoice, PayPal)
- Clear pricing and no surprises

**Incentivize Early Conversion:**
- Discount for converting before trial ends
- "Upgrade now and save 20%"
- Bonus features or credits

**Address Objections:**
- Money-back guarantee
- Flexible cancellation policy
- Testimonials and social proof

**Trial-to-Paid Conversion Benchmarks:**
- With credit card: 30-40%
- Without credit card: 15-25%
- Varies widely by product, price, and market

### Usage-Based Pricing

**Definition:** Pricing scales with usage (API calls, storage, seats, transactions, compute time).

**Structure:**

**Metered Usage:**
- Pay for what you use
- Billed based on actual consumption
- Examples: API calls, storage GB, compute hours

**Tiered Usage:**
- Pricing tiers based on usage levels
- Volume discounts at higher tiers
- Examples: 0-1,000 calls ($0.01 each), 1,001-10,000 ($0.008 each)

**Hybrid:**
- Base subscription + usage overages
- Example: $50/month includes 10,000 API calls, $0.01 per additional call

**When Usage-Based Pricing Works:**
- Usage correlates with value received
- Customers have variable usage patterns
- Low barrier to entry (start small, scale up)
- Infrastructure or platform products

**Benefits:**

**For Customers:**
- Pay only for what they use (fair)
- Low initial cost (easy to start)
- Scales with their growth
- Predictable unit economics

**For Company:**
- Captures expansion automatically
- Aligns revenue with value delivered
- Encourages adoption (low entry barrier)
- Natural upsell as usage grows

**Challenges:**

**Predictability:**
- Customers may struggle to forecast costs
- Unexpected bills can cause dissatisfaction
- Solution: Usage alerts, spending caps, forecasting tools

**Transparency:**
- Must clearly communicate usage and costs
- Real-time usage dashboards
- Detailed billing breakdowns

**Complexity:**
- More complex to implement and explain
- Requires robust metering and billing infrastructure
- Solution: Clear documentation, examples, calculators

**Examples:**
- **AWS:** Pay for compute (EC2), storage (S3), bandwidth, and dozens of other services
- **Twilio:** Pay per API call, SMS sent, voice minute
- **Stripe:** 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction
- **Snowflake:** Pay for data storage (per TB) and compute time (per second)
- **Datadog:** Pay per host monitored

**Usage-Based Pricing Best Practices:**

**Clear Unit Economics:**
- Transparent pricing per unit
- Volume discounts clearly communicated
- No hidden fees

**Usage Visibility:**
- Real-time usage dashboards
- Historical usage reports
- Forecasting and budgeting tools

**Spending Controls:**
- Usage alerts ("You've used 80% of your budget")
- Spending caps or limits
- Approval workflows for overages

**Flexible Billing:**
- Monthly or annual billing options
- Prepaid credits or commitments (with discounts)
- Multiple payment methods

### Pricing Strategy

**Value-Based Pricing:**

**Principle:** Price based on value delivered to customer, not cost to provide.

**Process:**
1. Understand customer segments and their willingness to pay
2. Quantify value delivered (time saved, revenue generated, costs reduced)
3. Price as fraction of value (e.g., 10-20% of value)
4. Test and iterate based on conversion and feedback

**Example:**
- If product saves customer $10,000/year, price at $1,000-$2,000/year

**Pricing Tiers:**

**Typical Structure:**

| Tier | Target | Positioning | Features | Price |
|------|--------|-------------|----------|-------|
| **Free** | Individuals, trial users | Entry point, viral growth | Core features, limited usage | $0 |
| **Starter/Pro** | Small teams, power users | Self-serve, credit card | Enhanced features, higher limits | $10-50/user/month |
| **Business** | Growing companies | Self-serve or sales-assisted | Advanced features, integrations, support | $50-200/user/month |
| **Enterprise** | Large organizations | Sales-led, custom | Unlimited usage, security, SLAs, dedicated support | Custom pricing |

**Tier Differentiation:**

**Free Tier:**
- Core value proposition
- Limited by usage, features, or users
- Sufficient for individuals or small teams
- Drives awareness and adoption

**Mid Tier (Starter/Pro):**
- Removes free tier limitations
- Adds productivity and collaboration features
- Target: small teams and power users
- Self-serve purchase

**High Tier (Business):**
- Advanced features and integrations
- Better support and SLAs
- Admin and security controls
- Target: growing companies
- Self-serve or sales-assisted

**Enterprise Tier:**
- Unlimited usage and scale
- Enterprise security and compliance
- Dedicated support and services
- Custom contracts and pricing
- Target: large organizations
- Sales-led, custom deals

**Pricing Page Best Practices:**

**Clarity:**
- Clear differentiation between tiers
- Easy-to-scan feature comparison
- No jargon or confusing terms

**Transparency:**
- Pricing visible without "Contact us"
- No hidden fees or surprises
- Clear explanation of limits and overages

**Guidance:**
- Highlight most popular tier
- "Best for [use case]" labels
- Help users choose right tier

**Social Proof:**
- "Used by 10,000+ companies"
- Customer logos and testimonials
- Case studies and success stories

**Clear CTAs:**
- Prominent "Start Free Trial" or "Get Started" buttons
- Different CTAs for each tier
- Easy path to purchase

**Annual Discounts:**
- Offer discount for annual commitment (typically 15-20%)
- Improves cash flow and retention
- Clearly show savings

**FAQ:**
- Address common questions and objections
- "Can I change plans?" "What happens if I exceed limits?"
- Build confidence and reduce friction

## Product-Qualified Leads (PQLs)

### Understanding PQLs

**Definition:** Users who have experienced meaningful product value and show buying intent through their usage behavior.

**PQL vs. MQL vs. SQL:**

| Type | Definition | Qualification | Conversion Rate |
|------|------------|---------------|----------------|
| **MQL** | Marketing-Qualified Lead | Marketing engagement (email opens, content downloads) | 5-10% |
| **SQL** | Sales-Qualified Lead | Expressed interest in buying (demo request, contact sales) | 20-30% |
| **PQL** | Product-Qualified Lead | Product usage and value realization | 25-40% |

**Why PQLs Matter:**
- 5-8x higher conversion than MQLs
- Shorter sales cycles (already understand product)
- Lower CAC (product does qualification)
- Better retention (bought based on experience)
- Enables efficient, scalable sales motion

### Defining PQL Criteria

**Components of PQL Definition:**

**1. Usage-Based Signals**

**Frequency:**
- Number of logins (e.g., 5+ logins in 7 days)
- Session frequency (daily, weekly)
- Consistent engagement over time

**Depth:**
- Key feature usage (e.g., created 3+ projects)
- Advanced feature exploration
- Completion of important workflows

**Breadth:**
- Number of features used
- Diversity of use cases
- Cross-functional adoption

**Velocity:**
- Rapid increase in usage
- Accelerating engagement
- Approaching limits quickly

**Limits:**
- Approaching or hitting free tier limits
- Usage caps or restrictions
- Need for more capacity

**2. Behavioral Signals**

**Intent:**
- Viewed pricing page
- Explored premium features (hit paywall)
- Attempted to access restricted functionality
- Searched for upgrade or payment info

**Engagement:**
- Invited team members
- Integrated with other tools
- Customized settings or preferences
- Created significant content or data

**Support:**
- Contacted support about paid features
- Asked about enterprise capabilities
- Requested information on upgrades

**3. Firmographic Signals**

**Company Fit:**
- Company size matches ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- Industry or vertical fit
- Geographic location

**User Fit:**
- Job title indicates decision-making authority
- Department or role alignment
- Seniority level

**Account Fit:**
- Multiple users from same company
- Email domain indicates company size
- Existing customer or partner

### Example PQL Definitions

**Slack:**
- Account reaches 2,000 messages sent
- Indicates team communication value
- Strong correlation with conversion and retention

**Hightouch:**
- User sets up more than one integration
- Exceeds freemium product's basic offering
- Shows commitment and value realization

**Drift:**
- User has 100+ conversations on their website
- Demonstrates chatbot value
- Approaching usage limits

**Generic SaaS Product:**
- 5+ logins in first 14 days
- Created 3+ projects
- Invited at least 1 team member
- Viewed pricing page
- Company size > 50 employees

### PQL Scoring

**Purpose:** Prioritize PQLs based on likelihood to convert and account value.

**Scoring Model:**

Assign points to different actions and attributes:

**Usage Actions:**
- Daily login: +5 points
- Key feature used: +10 points
- Advanced feature explored: +15 points
- Invited team member: +20 points
- Hit usage limit: +25 points

**Behavioral Actions:**
- Viewed pricing page: +10 points
- Contacted support about paid features: +15 points
- Attempted premium feature: +20 points

**Firmographic Attributes:**
- Company size 50-200: +10 points
- Company size 200-1000: +20 points
- Company size 1000+: +30 points
- Target industry: +15 points
- Decision-maker title: +20 points

**Negative Signals:**
- No activity in 7 days: -10 points
- Support ticket with negative sentiment: -15 points

**Thresholds:**
- 0-50: Not yet qualified
- 51-100: Low-priority PQL
- 101-150: Medium-priority PQL
- 151+: High-priority PQL

**Refinement:**
- Analyze conversion rates by score range
- Adjust point values based on correlation with conversion
- Continuously iterate based on data

### PQL Metrics

**1. PQL Rate**

**Definition:** Percentage of signups that become PQLs.

**Calculation:** (Number of PQLs ÷ Total signups) × 100

**Target:** 10-30% (varies by product and PQL definition)

**Use:** Measure effectiveness of product in qualifying leads.

**2. Time to PQL**

**Definition:** Time from signup to reaching PQL status.

**Measurement:** Median or average time in days.

**Target:** Minimize (ideally within first 7-14 days).

**Use:** Optimize onboarding to accelerate PQL generation.

**3. PQL-to-Customer Conversion Rate**

**Definition:** Percentage of PQLs that convert to paying customers.

**Calculation:** (PQLs converted to paid ÷ Total PQLs) × 100

**Target:** 20-40% (significantly higher than MQL conversion).

**Use:** Validate PQL definition and prioritize sales efforts.

**4. PQL Volume**

**Definition:** Total number of PQLs generated in a period.

**Measurement:** Count of PQLs per week/month.

**Use:** Track growth and capacity planning for sales team.

### PQL-Driven Sales Motion

**Sales Engagement with PQLs:**

**Automated Outreach:**
- Triggered email when user becomes PQL
- Personalized based on usage and behavior
- Offer help, resources, or demo

**Sales-Assisted Conversion:**
- Sales rep reaches out to high-value PQLs
- Consultative approach (not cold pitch)
- Focus on helping user succeed, not just closing deal
- Offer guidance on best plan or features

**Self-Serve with Safety Net:**
- Encourage self-serve upgrade
- Sales available if user has questions
- Proactive outreach for enterprise-fit accounts

**Prioritization:**
- Focus on high-score PQLs first
- Segment by account value and fit
- Balance volume with quality

**Sales Playbook for PQLs:**

**Context:**
- Review user's product usage and behavior
- Understand their use case and goals
- Identify pain points or needs

**Outreach:**
- Personalized email or call
- Reference specific usage ("I noticed you've created 5 projects")
- Offer help, not sales pitch

**Conversation:**
- Ask about their experience and goals
- Understand blockers or questions
- Educate on features or plans that fit their needs
- Address objections or concerns

**Close:**
- Make it easy to upgrade (send link, walk through)
- Offer incentives if appropriate (discount, extended trial)
- Follow up if not ready yet

**Post-Conversion:**
- Ensure smooth onboarding to paid plan
- Check in after upgrade
- Identify expansion opportunities

### Implementing PQL Process

**Step 1: Define PQL Criteria**
- Analyze retained and converted users
- Identify actions correlated with conversion
- Set thresholds and scoring model
- Document PQL definition

**Step 2: Instrument Product**
- Track relevant events and behaviors
- Implement PQL scoring logic
- Integrate with CRM and sales tools

**Step 3: Automate PQL Identification**
- Real-time PQL scoring
- Automatic flagging in CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Alerts to sales team

**Step 4: Enable Sales Team**
- Train on PQL concept and process
- Provide context and usage data
- Develop PQL-specific playbooks
- Set expectations and quotas

**Step 5: Monitor and Optimize**
- Track PQL metrics (rate, conversion, time-to-PQL)
- Analyze which signals best predict conversion
- Refine PQL definition and scoring
- Iterate based on data and feedback

**Tools for PQL Management:**
- **Product Analytics:** Amplitude, Mixpanel, Heap (identify PQLs)
- **Customer Data Platform:** Segment, RudderStack (unify data)
- **CRM:** Salesforce, HubSpot (manage PQLs)
- **Sales Engagement:** Outreach, SalesLoft (automate outreach)
- **PQL-Specific:** Pocus, Correlated (purpose-built for PQL workflows)
