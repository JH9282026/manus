# Financial Statements

Comprehensive guide to analyzing income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements, including accounting policies, adjustments, and quality of earnings assessment.

---

## Income Statement Analysis

### Income Statement Structure

**Multi-Step Income Statement Format**:

```
Revenue (Sales)
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
= Gross Profit

- Operating Expenses
  - Selling, General & Administrative (SG&A)
  - Research & Development (R&D)
  - Depreciation & Amortization
= Operating Income (EBIT)

- Interest Expense
+ Interest Income
= Earnings Before Tax (EBT)

- Income Tax Expense
= Net Income

÷ Shares Outstanding
= Earnings Per Share (EPS)
```

### Revenue Recognition

**Revenue Recognition Principle** (ASC 606 / IFRS 15):

Recognize revenue when control of goods/services transfers to customer.

**Five-Step Model**:
1. Identify the contract
2. Identify performance obligations
3. Determine transaction price
4. Allocate price to performance obligations
5. Recognize revenue when obligations are satisfied

**Revenue Recognition Methods**:

| Method | When Used | Example |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Point in Time | Control transfers at specific moment | Retail sales |
| Over Time | Control transfers gradually | Construction contracts |
| Subscription | Ratably over subscription period | SaaS, magazines |
| Percentage of Completion | Long-term contracts | Large infrastructure projects |

**Red Flags in Revenue Recognition**:
- Revenue recognized before delivery
- Channel stuffing (pushing inventory to distributors)
- Bill-and-hold arrangements
- Side agreements that modify terms
- Significant unbilled receivables
- Revenue from related parties
- Frequent restatements

**Quality of Revenue Assessment**:

**High-Quality Revenue**:
- Cash-based (low receivables growth)
- Recurring and predictable
- Diversified customer base
- Consistent with industry practices
- Supported by strong fundamentals

**Low-Quality Revenue**:
- Aggressive recognition policies
- One-time or non-recurring
- Concentrated in few customers
- Growing faster than receivables can support
- Inconsistent with cash flow

### Expense Analysis

**Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**:

Direct costs of producing goods/services.

**Components**:
- Raw materials
- Direct labor
- Manufacturing overhead
- Freight-in

**Analysis**:
- Calculate gross margin trend
- Compare COGS as % of revenue to peers
- Identify cost drivers (materials, labor, overhead)

**Operating Expenses**:

**Selling, General & Administrative (SG&A)**:
- Sales commissions
- Marketing and advertising
- Executive compensation
- Office expenses
- Professional fees

**Research & Development (R&D)**:
- Product development
- Technology innovation
- Critical for tech, pharma, manufacturing

**Analysis**:
- Calculate operating leverage (% change in operating income / % change in revenue)
- Identify fixed vs. variable expenses
- Compare expense ratios to peers
- Assess scalability

**Depreciation & Amortization**:

**Depreciation**: Allocation of tangible asset cost over useful life
**Amortization**: Allocation of intangible asset cost over useful life

**Methods**:
- **Straight-line**: Equal expense each period (most common)
- **Accelerated**: Higher expense in early years (declining balance, sum-of-years-digits)
- **Units of production**: Based on usage

**Analysis**:
- Compare depreciation methods to peers
- Assess useful life assumptions (longer = lower annual expense)
- Calculate capital intensity (CapEx / Revenue)
- Compare depreciation to CapEx (if depreciation >> CapEx, assets aging)

### Non-Operating Items

**Interest Expense/Income**:
- Result of financing decisions
- Exclude when comparing operating performance

**Other Income/Expense**:
- Gains/losses on asset sales
- Foreign exchange gains/losses
- Investment income
- Impairment charges

**Analysis**:
- Separate operating from non-operating items
- Adjust for one-time items
- Calculate normalized earnings

### Earnings Quality Indicators

**High-Quality Earnings**:
- Sustainable and recurring
- Cash-based (not accrual-heavy)
- From core operations
- Conservative accounting
- Consistent with industry

**Low-Quality Earnings**:
- One-time gains
- Aggressive accounting
- Heavy reliance on estimates
- Frequent adjustments
- Divergence from cash flow

**Earnings Quality Checklist**:
- [ ] Operating income growing faster than revenue?
- [ ] Net income > Operating cash flow?
- [ ] Frequent "one-time" charges?
- [ ] Complex revenue recognition?
- [ ] Significant related-party transactions?
- [ ] Frequent accounting policy changes?
- [ ] Large deferred revenue changes?
- [ ] Goodwill impairments?

## Balance Sheet Analysis

### Balance Sheet Structure

**Balance Sheet Equation**:
```
Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity
```

**Classified Balance Sheet**:

```
ASSETS
Current Assets:
  Cash and Cash Equivalents
  Marketable Securities
  Accounts Receivable
  Inventory
  Prepaid Expenses
Total Current Assets

Non-Current Assets:
  Property, Plant & Equipment (net)
  Intangible Assets
  Goodwill
  Long-term Investments
  Other Assets
Total Non-Current Assets

TOTAL ASSETS

LIABILITIES
Current Liabilities:
  Accounts Payable
  Short-term Debt
  Accrued Expenses
  Current Portion of Long-term Debt
  Deferred Revenue
Total Current Liabilities

Non-Current Liabilities:
  Long-term Debt
  Deferred Tax Liabilities
  Pension Obligations
  Other Long-term Liabilities
Total Non-Current Liabilities

TOTAL LIABILITIES

SHAREHOLDERS' EQUITY
  Common Stock
  Additional Paid-in Capital
  Retained Earnings
  Treasury Stock
  Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income
TOTAL SHAREHOLDERS' EQUITY

TOTAL LIABILITIES & EQUITY
```

### Asset Analysis

**Cash and Cash Equivalents**:

**Components**:
- Cash on hand
- Demand deposits
- Short-term investments (maturity < 90 days)

**Analysis**:
- Adequate for operations?
- Excess cash (opportunity cost)?
- Geographic location (trapped overseas)?
- Restricted cash?

**Marketable Securities**:

Short-term investments (maturity > 90 days, < 1 year).

**Classification**:
- **Trading**: Held for short-term profit (mark-to-market through income)
- **Available-for-Sale**: May be sold (mark-to-market through OCI)
- **Held-to-Maturity**: Intend to hold (carried at amortized cost)

**Accounts Receivable**:

**Analysis**:
- Calculate DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)
- Review aging schedule
- Assess allowance for doubtful accounts
- Compare to credit terms

**Allowance for Doubtful Accounts**:
```
Net Receivables = Gross Receivables - Allowance for Doubtful Accounts

Allowance as % of Receivables = Allowance / Gross Receivables
```

**Red Flags**:
- Declining allowance as % of receivables (aggressive)
- DSO increasing faster than sales
- Large write-offs

**Inventory**:

**Valuation Methods**:
- **FIFO** (First-In, First-Out): Oldest costs to COGS, newest costs in inventory
- **LIFO** (Last-In, First-Out): Newest costs to COGS, oldest costs in inventory
- **Weighted Average**: Average cost

**FIFO vs. LIFO (in rising prices)**:

| Metric | FIFO | LIFO |
|--------|------|------|
| COGS | Lower | Higher |
| Gross Profit | Higher | Lower |
| Inventory | Higher (current costs) | Lower (old costs) |
| Taxes | Higher | Lower |

**Analysis**:
- Calculate inventory turnover
- Assess inventory composition (raw materials, WIP, finished goods)
- Look for obsolete inventory
- Compare valuation method to peers

**LIFO Reserve**:
```
LIFO Reserve = FIFO Inventory Value - LIFO Inventory Value
```

Use to convert LIFO to FIFO for comparability.

**Property, Plant & Equipment (PP&E)**:

**Components**:
- Land
- Buildings
- Machinery and equipment
- Furniture and fixtures
- Vehicles

**Presentation**:
```
PP&E (gross)
- Accumulated Depreciation
= PP&E (net)
```

**Analysis**:
- Calculate fixed asset turnover
- Assess age of assets: Accumulated Depreciation / Gross PP&E
- Compare CapEx to depreciation
- Evaluate useful life assumptions

**Intangible Assets**:

**Types**:
- **Definite Life**: Patents, copyrights, customer lists (amortized)
- **Indefinite Life**: Trademarks, brand names (not amortized, tested for impairment)

**Goodwill**:

Excess of purchase price over fair value of net assets acquired.

**Analysis**:
- Review impairment history
- Assess reasonableness of carrying value
- Compare to market cap (if goodwill > market cap, red flag)
- Understand acquisition strategy

**Goodwill Impairment Red Flags**:
- Frequent impairments
- Large impairments
- Goodwill from questionable acquisitions
- Goodwill as large % of assets

### Liability Analysis

**Accounts Payable**:

**Analysis**:
- Calculate DPO (Days Payable Outstanding)
- Compare to industry norms
- Assess supplier relationships

**Debt Analysis**:

**Types of Debt**:
- Short-term debt (< 1 year)
- Current portion of long-term debt
- Long-term debt (> 1 year)

**Debt Schedule Analysis**:
- Maturity profile
- Interest rates
- Covenants
- Secured vs. unsecured

**Key Metrics**:
- Debt-to-equity
- Debt-to-assets
- Interest coverage
- Debt maturity schedule

**Deferred Revenue (Unearned Revenue)**:

Cash received before revenue is earned.

**Analysis**:
- Common in subscription businesses
- Increasing deferred revenue = strong future revenue
- Decreasing deferred revenue = potential slowdown

**Pension Obligations**:

**Defined Benefit Plans**:

**Key Components**:
- **Projected Benefit Obligation (PBO)**: Present value of future pension payments
- **Plan Assets**: Investments set aside to fund pensions
- **Funded Status**: Plan Assets - PBO

**Analysis**:
- Underfunded plans (PBO > Plan Assets) = liability
- Review assumptions (discount rate, return on assets, salary growth)
- Assess cash funding requirements

### Equity Analysis

**Components of Shareholders' Equity**:

**Common Stock**: Par value × shares issued
**Additional Paid-in Capital (APIC)**: Amount received above par value
**Retained Earnings**: Cumulative net income - dividends
**Treasury Stock**: Repurchased shares (contra-equity)
**Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income (AOCI)**: Unrealized gains/losses

**Retained Earnings Reconciliation**:
```
Beginning Retained Earnings
+ Net Income
- Dividends
= Ending Retained Earnings
```

**Share Repurchases**:

**Analysis**:
- Reduces shares outstanding
- Increases EPS (fewer shares)
- Returns cash to shareholders
- Assess if shares repurchased at reasonable price

**Book Value Per Share**:
```
Book Value Per Share = Total Equity / Shares Outstanding
```

## Cash Flow Statement Analysis

### Cash Flow Statement Structure

**Three Sections**:
1. Operating Activities
2. Investing Activities
3. Financing Activities

**Cash Flow Equation**:
```
Cash Flow from Operations
+ Cash Flow from Investing
+ Cash Flow from Financing
= Net Change in Cash

+ Beginning Cash Balance
= Ending Cash Balance
```

### Operating Cash Flow

**Indirect Method** (most common):

```
Net Income
+ Depreciation & Amortization
+ Loss on Sale of Assets (or - Gain)
+ Increase in Accounts Payable
+ Decrease in Accounts Receivable
+ Decrease in Inventory
- Increase in Accounts Receivable
- Increase in Inventory
- Decrease in Accounts Payable
= Cash Flow from Operating Activities
```

**Direct Method** (less common):

```
Cash Received from Customers
- Cash Paid to Suppliers
- Cash Paid for Operating Expenses
- Cash Paid for Interest
- Cash Paid for Taxes
= Cash Flow from Operating Activities
```

**Analysis**:
- Compare to net income (should be similar over time)
- Assess quality (cash-based vs. accrual-based earnings)
- Evaluate working capital changes

**Operating Cash Flow Quality**:

**High Quality**:
- OCF > Net Income
- Positive and growing
- Driven by core operations
- Minimal working capital swings

**Low Quality**:
- OCF < Net Income (persistently)
- Negative despite profitability
- Driven by working capital manipulation
- Volatile and unpredictable

### Investing Cash Flow

**Typical Items**:
- Capital expenditures (CapEx)
- Acquisitions
- Purchases/sales of investments
- Purchases/sales of PP&E

**Analysis**:
- Assess capital intensity
- Evaluate acquisition strategy
- Compare CapEx to depreciation

**CapEx Analysis**:

**Maintenance CapEx**: Sustain current operations (≈ depreciation)
**Growth CapEx**: Expand capacity (> depreciation)

```
If CapEx >> Depreciation → Growth investments
If CapEx ≈ Depreciation → Maintaining assets
If CapEx < Depreciation → Underinvesting (assets aging)
```

### Financing Cash Flow

**Typical Items**:
- Debt issuance/repayment
- Equity issuance/repurchase
- Dividend payments

**Analysis**:
- Assess capital structure changes
- Evaluate shareholder returns (dividends, buybacks)
- Identify financing needs

**Financing Cash Flow Patterns**:

**Mature Company**:
- Positive operating cash flow
- Moderate investing (maintenance CapEx)
- Negative financing (dividends, buybacks, debt repayment)

**Growth Company**:
- Positive operating cash flow
- Large negative investing (growth CapEx, acquisitions)
- Positive financing (raising capital)

**Distressed Company**:
- Negative or weak operating cash flow
- Positive investing (asset sales)
- Positive financing (raising capital to survive)

### Free Cash Flow

**Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)**:
```
FCFF = Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures

Or:
FCFF = EBIT × (1 - Tax Rate) + Depreciation - CapEx - Change in NWC
```

**Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)**:
```
FCFE = FCFF - Interest × (1 - Tax Rate) + Net Borrowing

Or:
FCFE = Net Income + Depreciation - CapEx - Change in NWC + Net Borrowing
```

**Analysis**:
- FCFF: Cash available to all investors (debt + equity)
- FCFE: Cash available to equity holders
- Positive and growing FCF = healthy
- Negative FCF = burning cash (acceptable for growth companies)

## Statement Relationships

### How Statements Connect

**Net Income** (Income Statement) flows to:
- Retained Earnings (Balance Sheet)
- Starting point for Operating Cash Flow (Cash Flow Statement)

**Depreciation** (Income Statement) affects:
- Accumulated Depreciation (Balance Sheet)
- Added back in Operating Cash Flow (Cash Flow Statement)

**CapEx** (Cash Flow Statement) affects:
- PP&E (Balance Sheet)
- Depreciation (Income Statement)

**Debt Issuance/Repayment** (Cash Flow Statement) affects:
- Debt Balance (Balance Sheet)
- Interest Expense (Income Statement)

**Dividends** (Cash Flow Statement) affect:
- Retained Earnings (Balance Sheet)

### Reconciliation Example

**Income Statement**:
- Net Income: $100M

**Balance Sheet Changes**:
- Accounts Receivable increased $20M
- Inventory decreased $10M
- Accounts Payable increased $15M
- Depreciation: $30M
- CapEx: $50M

**Cash Flow Statement**:
```
Operating Activities:
  Net Income: $100M
  + Depreciation: $30M
  - Increase in A/R: -$20M
  + Decrease in Inventory: $10M
  + Increase in A/P: $15M
  = Operating Cash Flow: $135M

Investing Activities:
  - CapEx: -$50M
  = Investing Cash Flow: -$50M

Free Cash Flow = $135M - $50M = $85M
```

## Accounting Policy Analysis

### Key Accounting Policies

**Revenue Recognition**:
- When is revenue recognized?
- Any unusual terms or arrangements?
- Compare to industry standards

**Inventory Valuation**:
- FIFO, LIFO, or weighted average?
- Lower of cost or market?
- Obsolescence reserves adequate?

**Depreciation**:
- Straight-line or accelerated?
- Useful life assumptions reasonable?
- Salvage value assumptions?

**Goodwill and Intangibles**:
- Amortization periods reasonable?
- Impairment testing frequency and methodology?
- History of impairments?

**Pension Assumptions**:
- Discount rate reasonable?
- Expected return on assets realistic?
- Salary growth assumptions?

### Accounting Quality Assessment

**Conservative Accounting**:
- Recognize revenue later
- Recognize expenses earlier
- Higher reserves and allowances
- Shorter useful lives
- More frequent impairments

**Aggressive Accounting**:
- Recognize revenue earlier
- Capitalize expenses (vs. expensing)
- Lower reserves and allowances
- Longer useful lives
- Infrequent impairments

**Red Flags**:
- Frequent changes in accounting policies
- Policies differ significantly from peers
- Complex or unusual transactions
- Significant estimates and judgments
- History of restatements

## Adjustments for Comparability

### Common Adjustments

**One-Time Items**:
- Restructuring charges
- Asset impairments
- Gains/losses on asset sales
- Litigation settlements
- Acquisition-related costs

**Adjusted Earnings**:
```
Adjusted Net Income = Reported Net Income
                    + One-Time Charges (after-tax)
                    - One-Time Gains (after-tax)
```

**LIFO to FIFO Conversion**:
```
FIFO Inventory = LIFO Inventory + LIFO Reserve
FIFO COGS = LIFO COGS - Change in LIFO Reserve
FIFO Net Income = LIFO Net Income + (Change in LIFO Reserve × (1 - Tax Rate))
```

**Operating Lease Capitalization** (pre-ASC 842):

Convert operating leases to capital leases for comparability.

```
Capitalized Lease Asset = PV of Future Lease Payments
Lease Liability = PV of Future Lease Payments

Adjusted EBITDA = Reported EBITDA + Operating Lease Expense
Adjusted Interest Expense = Reported Interest + Imputed Interest on Leases
```

### Normalizing Earnings

**Normalized Earnings**: Sustainable earnings from core operations.

**Adjustments**:
1. Remove one-time items
2. Adjust for non-recurring events
3. Normalize cyclical effects
4. Remove non-operating items

**Example**:
```
Reported Net Income: $100M
+ Restructuring charge (after-tax): $10M
- Gain on asset sale (after-tax): $5M
+ Unusually high tax benefit: $3M
= Normalized Net Income: $108M
```

## Financial Statement Fraud Detection

### Common Fraud Schemes

**Revenue Fraud**:
- Fictitious revenue
- Premature revenue recognition
- Channel stuffing
- Round-tripping (selling and buying back)

**Expense Fraud**:
- Capitalizing expenses
- Understating liabilities
- Overstating assets
- Cookie jar reserves

**Disclosure Fraud**:
- Off-balance-sheet entities
- Related-party transactions
- Inadequate disclosures

### Fraud Red Flags

**Financial Statement Red Flags**:
- Revenue growing faster than peers (without explanation)
- Margins significantly higher than peers
- Receivables growing faster than revenue
- Frequent one-time charges
- Complex organizational structure
- Significant related-party transactions
- Frequent auditor changes
- Weak internal controls

**Management Red Flags**:
- Aggressive targets and incentives
- Dominance by single individual
- Weak board oversight
- History of regulatory issues
- Lavish lifestyle inconsistent with compensation

**Beneish M-Score** (fraud detection model):

Calculates probability of earnings manipulation using 8 variables:
1. Days Sales in Receivables Index
2. Gross Margin Index
3. Asset Quality Index
4. Sales Growth Index
5. Depreciation Index
6. SG&A Index
7. Leverage Index
8. Total Accruals to Total Assets

**Interpretation**:
- M-Score > -2.22: Likely manipulator
- M-Score < -2.22: Unlikely manipulator
