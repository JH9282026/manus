## Dialogue Editing and Cleanup

Dialogue is typically the most important audio element, as it carries the story and character information. Clean, intelligible dialogue is essential.



### Dialogue Editing Fundamentals

**Selecting Takes:**
- Choose takes with best performance AND best audio
- Consider room tone consistency
- Note technical issues that affect usability
- Document ADR needs for unusable takes

**Editing Techniques:**
- Use crossfades at edit points (typically 10-50ms)
- Match room tone across edits
- Remove or reduce unwanted sounds
- Preserve natural breathing patterns (unless distracting)
- Maintain sync with picture



### Common Dialogue Problems and Solutions

**Background Noise:**
- Use noise reduction tools
- Gate or expand to reduce noise between words
- Replace sections with room tone if severe
- Consider ADR for problematic sections

**Plosives (Ps and Bs):**
- Use high-pass filter to reduce low-frequency pops
- Apply spectral editing to remove energy bursts
- Use specialized de-plosive plugins

**Sibilance (Harsh S sounds):**
- Apply de-esser (frequency-specific compressor)
- Manual gain reduction on problem frequencies
- Use spectral editing for severe cases

**Mouth Clicks and Lip Smacks:**
- Manual removal with spectral editing
- Use specialized de-click plugins
- Edit out using surrounding audio

**Room Reverb/Echo:**
- Difficult to remove completely
- Use dereverb plugins (limited effectiveness)
- Prevent through proper recording technique
- ADR may be necessary for severe cases



### Room Tone Management

**What is Room Tone:**
- The "silence" of a specific location
- Contains ambient sounds unique to that space
- Essential for smooth dialogue editing

**Recording Room Tone:**
- Record 30-60 seconds at each location
- Capture with same microphone and settings as dialogue
- Include any consistent ambient sounds (HVAC, etc.)

**Using Room Tone:**
- Fill gaps between dialogue edits
- Cover unwanted sounds
- Smooth transitions between takes
- Create consistent audio baseline



### ADR (Automated Dialogue Replacement)

**When ADR is Needed:**
- Production audio is unusable (noise, distortion, etc.)
- Performance needs improvement
- Script changes after filming
- Dubbing for different languages

**ADR Process:**
1. Create ADR cue sheet with timecodes
2. Set up recording environment (quiet, treated room)
3. Play video for actor to match sync
4. Record multiple takes
5. Edit to match original timing and lip sync
6. Process to match production audio quality
