## Why Audio Is 50% of Video

The film industry adage that "audio is 50% of the video experience" is not hyperbole—it's a fundamental truth supported by psychology and viewer behavior research.



### Emotional Impact

Audio directly triggers emotional responses:

- **Music**: Sets mood, builds tension, creates emotional peaks
- **Sound effects**: Create impact, enhance action, establish reality
- **Ambient sound**: Establishes location, time, atmosphere
- **Silence**: Creates tension, emphasis, dramatic pauses
- **Voice tone**: Conveys emotion beyond words

Research shows that audiences process audio emotionally before cognitively, meaning sound shapes their feelings about content before they intellectually analyze it.



### Perception of Quality

Audio quality strongly influences perceived video quality:

- Studies show viewers rate video quality higher when audio is good, even with identical visuals
- Poor audio causes viewers to abandon content faster than poor video
- Professional audio creates trust and credibility
- Audio issues are immediately noticeable, while video issues may go unnoticed



### Narrative Function

Audio serves essential storytelling roles:

- **Dialogue**: Delivers plot, character, information
- **Sound effects**: Provide spatial awareness, physical reality
- **Music**: Guides emotional journey, signals genre
- **Ambient sound**: Establishes setting without exposition
- **Off-screen sound**: Expands the world beyond the frame



### Attention and Engagement

Audio captures and maintains attention:

- Sound draws attention to important visual elements
- Audio cues prepare viewers for upcoming events
- Rhythm of audio guides pacing perception
- Silence creates anticipation and emphasis

### Phase 1: Organization and Assessment

**Import and Organize:**
1. Import all audio files (production audio, music, sound effects)
2. Create organized folder structure
3. Label and color-code tracks by type
4. Sync audio with video if not embedded
5. Create backup of original files

**Assessment:**
1. Review all production audio for quality
2. Identify problems (noise, distortion, sync issues)
3. Note which takes are usable
4. Plan ADR (Automated Dialogue Replacement) if needed
5. Create spotting notes for sound effects and music



### Phase 2: Dialogue Editing

**Primary Dialogue Work:**
1. Select best takes for each line
2. Edit dialogue for timing and pacing
3. Remove unwanted sounds (breaths, clicks, pops)
4. Smooth transitions between takes
5. Ensure consistent room tone



### Phase 3: Sound Design

**Sound Effects and Foley:**
1. Spot (identify) all needed sound effects
2. Source or create sound effects
3. Record or select Foley sounds
4. Layer and design complex sounds
5. Sync effects to picture



### Phase 4: Music

**Music Integration:**
1. Select or commission music
2. Edit music to picture
3. Create music builds and transitions
4. Balance music with dialogue and effects
5. Ensure proper licensing



### Phase 5: Mixing

**Final Mix:**
1. Set proper levels for all elements
2. Apply EQ and dynamics processing
3. Create spatial positioning (panning, reverb)
4. Balance all elements together
5. Verify loudness standards



### Phase 6: Mastering and Delivery

**Final Delivery:**
1. Apply final mastering processing
2. Verify technical specifications
3. Export in required formats
4. Create deliverables (stems, M&E, full mix)
5. Archive project and assets
