# Feedback, Calibration, and Performance Improvement

## 360-Degree Feedback

### Rater Groups
- **Self**: Self-assessment for comparison
- **Manager**: Direct supervisor perspective
- **Peers**: Same-level colleagues (3-5 raters)
- **Direct reports**: Subordinate perspectives (for managers)
- **Cross-functional**: Stakeholders from other teams
- **External**: Clients or partners (optional)

### 360 Design Best Practices
- Use for development, not evaluation
- Ensure anonymity for all raters except manager
- Minimum 3 raters per group for anonymity
- Use validated competency models
- Combine quantitative ratings with open-ended comments
- Provide coaching support to interpret results
- Focus on patterns, not individual outlier ratings

### Competency Areas for 360

| Category | Example Competencies |
|---|---|
| Leadership | Vision, decision-making, delegation, coaching |
| Communication | Clarity, listening, influence, presentation |
| Collaboration | Teamwork, conflict resolution, inclusiveness |
| Execution | Planning, accountability, quality, adaptability |
| Innovation | Creativity, risk-taking, continuous improvement |
| Technical | Domain expertise, problem-solving, learning agility |

## Calibration Sessions

### Purpose
Ensure consistent, fair performance ratings across managers and teams by comparing assessments in a group setting.

### Calibration Process

1. **Pre-session**: Managers submit preliminary ratings with justification
2. **Presentation**: Each manager presents their team ratings (2-3 min per person)
3. **Discussion**: Group challenges ratings that seem too high or low
4. **Adjustment**: Move ratings based on evidence and cross-team comparison
5. **Finalization**: Lock ratings after consensus

### Calibration Ground Rules
- Focus on performance evidence, not personality
- Compare individuals at the same level/role
- Consider the full review period, not just recent events
- Avoid recency bias and halo/horn effects
- Document rationale for all rating changes
- Maintain confidentiality of discussion details

### Common Calibration Biases

| Bias | Description | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Central tendency | Rating everyone as "meets expectations" | Force distribution discussion |
| Recency | Overweighting recent events | Require examples from full period |
| Halo effect | One strength inflates overall rating | Rate each competency independently |
| Leniency | Rating everyone too high | Cross-team comparison |
| Strictness | Rating everyone too harshly | Benchmark against role expectations |

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

### When to Use a PIP
- Consistent underperformance despite feedback
- Specific, documented performance gaps
- After informal coaching has been attempted
- As a formal remediation tool (not punishment)

### PIP Components
1. **Performance gap description**: Specific, measurable gaps with examples
2. **Expected performance standard**: Clear target for success
3. **Action plan**: Specific steps the employee must take
4. **Support provided**: Training, coaching, resources from the company
5. **Timeline**: Typically 30, 60, or 90 days
6. **Check-in schedule**: Weekly meetings to review progress
7. **Consequences**: What happens if standards are/aren't met

### PIP Best Practices
- Involve HR from the start
- Focus on observable behaviors and measurable outcomes
- Provide genuine support and resources for improvement
- Document every check-in and progress discussion
- Set realistic but meaningful improvement targets
- Maintain confidentiality and dignity throughout
