# OKR and Goal-Setting Systems

## OKR Framework

### Anatomy of an OKR

**Objective**: Qualitative, inspirational goal statement
- "Become the market leader in customer satisfaction"
- "Build a world-class engineering culture"

**Key Results**: Quantitative measures of progress (3-5 per Objective)
- "Increase NPS from 42 to 65"
- "Reduce average response time from 4 hours to 30 minutes"
- "Achieve 95% customer retention rate"

### OKR Best Practices

| Principle | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Ambition | Set stretch goals (70% achievement = success) | Set sandbagged goals |
| Measurability | Use specific numbers and dates | Use vague language |
| Alignment | Connect to company/team OKRs | Create in isolation |
| Focus | 3-5 objectives per team per quarter | Overload with 10+ objectives |
| Transparency | Share OKRs publicly across org | Keep hidden in manager files |
| Decoupling | Separate from compensation decisions | Tie directly to bonuses |

### OKR Cadence

**Annual Planning**
1. Leadership sets 3-5 company-level OKRs
2. Communicate to all teams with context and rationale

**Quarterly Planning**
1. Teams draft OKRs aligned to company OKRs (bottom-up + top-down)
2. Cross-functional alignment sessions to resolve conflicts
3. Finalize and publish all OKRs

**Weekly Check-ins**
- Score Key Results on 0-1.0 scale
- Identify blockers and request support
- Adjust tactics (not OKRs) as needed

**Quarterly Review**
- Score all OKRs (0.0-1.0 scale)
- 0.7-1.0 = Green (achieved or exceeded)
- 0.4-0.6 = Yellow (partial progress)
- 0.0-0.3 = Red (missed, analyze why)
- Carry forward, modify, or retire for next quarter

## Goal Cascade Architecture

### Alignment Model

```
Company OKRs (3-5 objectives)
    ├── Department OKRs (aligned to company)
    │       ├── Team OKRs (aligned to department)
    │       │       └── Individual goals (aligned to team)
    │       └── Team OKRs
    └── Department OKRs
```

### Cascade Principles
- 60% of goals should cascade from above (alignment)
- 40% can be bottom-up (innovation, local needs)
- Every goal should connect to at least one higher-level objective
- Avoid creating goals for the sake of alignment — quality over quantity

## SMART Goal Writing

### Formula
"[Action verb] [specific outcome] by [date] as measured by [metric]"

### Examples
- "Increase quarterly revenue from $2M to $2.5M by Q3 2026"
- "Launch new onboarding program reducing time-to-productivity from 90 to 60 days by June 2026"
- "Reduce customer support ticket resolution time from 24 hours to 4 hours by end of Q2"

### Common SMART Mistakes
- **Not specific**: "Improve customer satisfaction" (by how much?)
- **Not measurable**: "Build better relationships" (how do you measure?)
- **Not achievable**: Setting 300% growth target without resources
- **Not relevant**: Goal disconnected from team/company priorities
- **Not time-bound**: "Someday we'll launch this feature"
