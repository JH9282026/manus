# Behavioral Economics in Conversions

Apply behavioral economics principles to influence economic decisions.

---

## Loss Aversion

**Research**: Losses are psychologically about twice as powerful as equivalent gains.

### Applications

**Framing as Loss Prevention**
- "Don't miss out" vs "Get access"
- "Stop losing leads" vs "Get more leads"
- "Protect your investment" vs "Grow your investment"

**Risk Reversal**
- Money-back guarantees remove fear of loss
- Free trials create ownership before payment
- Easy cancellation reduces commitment anxiety

**Downgrade Warnings**
- Highlight what users lose by downgrading
- Show features that will disappear
- Display data that will be deleted

### Examples

- "You're about to lose your premium features"
- "Don't let competitors get ahead"
- "100% money-back guarantee - zero risk"

## Endowment Effect

**Definition**: People value things more highly once they own them.

### Applications

**Free Trials**
- Create ownership feeling before payment
- Let users customize and invest time
- Build data and progress users don't want to lose

**Customization**
- Personalized products feel more "owned"
- Configuration creates psychological investment
- Saved preferences increase switching costs

**Progress Investment**
- Show users what they've built
- Display progress they've made
- Highlight data they've collected

### Examples

- "Your personalized dashboard"
- "Your 14-day trial workspace"
- Progress bars showing setup completion

---

## Sunk Cost Fallacy

**Definition**: People continue investing because of previously invested resources.

### Applications

**Progressive Forms**
- Investment in process increases completion
- Multi-step signups with progress indicators
- Save progress messaging

**Account Investment**
- Users less likely to abandon accounts with history
- Data accumulation increases stickiness
- Community engagement creates switching costs

### Examples

- "You're 80% done - just one more step!"
- "Don't lose your progress"
- "You've already connected 5 tools"

---

## Present Bias

**Definition**: People strongly prefer immediate rewards over future ones.

### Applications

**Immediate Gratification**
- Instant access, immediate downloads
- Quick wins that show value fast
- Visible progress indicators

**Quick Results**
- Emphasize near-term benefits
- "See results in 24 hours"
- "Instant setup, no waiting"

### Examples

- "Get immediate access"
- "See your first results today"
- "Instant download - start now"

---

## Hyperbolic Discounting

**Definition**: People prefer smaller, sooner rewards over larger, later ones.

### Applications

**Payment Framing**
- Monthly feels smaller than annual lump sum
- "Only $9.99/month" vs "$120/year"
- Payment plans for large purchases

**Immediate Bonuses**
- Extra value for acting now
- Instant rewards for signup
- Same-day benefits

### Examples

- "Get 3 months free when you start today"
- "Instant bonus: $50 credit"
- "Start now and save immediately"

## Price Perception

### Charm Pricing

Prices ending in 9 are perceived as significantly lower:
- $199 feels much cheaper than $200
- Works less well for luxury/premium positioning

### Anchoring

Higher original price makes sale price seem better:
- "Was $299, Now $149"
- "Save 50%"
- "Compare at $500"

### Decoy Pricing

Strategic third option makes target more attractive:
- Small: $10 (too limited)
- Medium: $25 (best value) - TARGET
- Large: $50 (marginal improvement)

### Payment Framing

Make costs feel smaller:
- "Only $1/day" vs "$365/year"
- "Less than your daily coffee"
- "For the price of a lunch"

## Mental Accounting

**Definition**: People categorize and treat money differently based on subjective criteria.

### Applications

**Investment Framing**
- "Investment in your career" vs "expense"
- "Pays for itself in 3 months"
- "Return on investment of 10x"

**Category Matching**
- Compare to spending in same mental category
- Entertainment spend vs education spend
- Personal vs business budget

### Examples

- "Less than you spend on coffee each month"
- "A fraction of what you'd pay a consultant"
- "Tax-deductible business expense"

---

## Default Effect

**Definition**: People tend to stick with the default or pre-selected option.

### Applications

**Strategic Defaults**
- Pre-select recommended plan (ethically)
- Opt-out vs opt-in framing
- Form pre-population with common responses

### Examples

- Recommended plan highlighted and pre-selected
- Newsletter signup checked by default (where legal)
- Smart defaults based on user type
