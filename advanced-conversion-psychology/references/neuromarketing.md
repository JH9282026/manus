# Neuromarketing Principles

Apply neuroscience insights to optimize visual and sensory elements.

---

## Eye Tracking Patterns

### F-Pattern

Users scan pages in an F-shape:
- Horizontal across the top
- Down the left side
- Shorter horizontal movements

**Best For**: Text-heavy pages

**Applications**:
- Place key information in top horizontal band
- Use left side for navigation and key points
- Break patterns strategically for attention

### Z-Pattern

Users scan in a Z-shape:
- Top left to top right
- Diagonally down
- Left to right again

**Best For**: Simpler, more visual pages

**Applications**:
- Logo top left
- CTA top right
- Key message bottom right

## Directing Attention

### Contrast

Difference draws the eye:
- Color contrast for CTAs
- Size contrast for headlines
- White space for isolation

### Movement

Animation captures attention (use sparingly):
- Subtle micro-animations
- Progress indicators
- Loading states

### Faces

Human faces attract attention:
- Eyes especially powerful
- Direct gaze creates connection
- Expression conveys emotion

### Directional Cues

Guide the eye:
- Arrows pointing to CTAs
- Eye gaze direction
- Body orientation
- Converging lines

### Whitespace

Isolation draws attention:
- Space around key elements
- Breathing room for CTAs
- Clean, uncluttered design

## Color Psychology

### Color Associations

| Color | Associations | Best For |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Red | Urgency, energy, passion | Clearance, food, urgency |
| Blue | Trust, calm, professionalism | Finance, healthcare, tech |
| Green | Nature, health, money | Environmental, health, finance |
| Yellow | Optimism, attention | Warning, children's products |
| Orange | Energy, enthusiasm | CTAs, affordable brands |
| Purple | Luxury, creativity | Premium brands, beauty |
| Black | Sophistication, luxury | High-end fashion, luxury |
| White | Purity, simplicity | Healthcare, tech, minimalist |

### Cultural Considerations

Color meanings vary by culture:
- White: Purity (West) vs Mourning (Asia)
- Red: Danger (West) vs Luck (China)
- Yellow: Caution (US) vs Sacred (India)

### CTA Button Colors

Effective CTA colors:
- Orange: Energetic, action-oriented
- Green: Positive, affirming
- Blue: Trustworthy, safe
- Red: Urgent, attention-grabbing

**Key Insight**: Contrast matters more than specific color.

## Memory and Recall

### Making Messages Memorable

**Repetition**
- Key messages repeated throughout
- Consistent positioning
- Multiple touchpoints

**Emotion**
- Emotional content better remembered
- Story-driven messaging
- Personal connections

**Story Structure**
- Narratives easier to recall
- Beginning-middle-end format
- Character-driven content

**Simplicity**
- Simple messages stick better
- One main idea per section
- Clear, concise language

**Novelty**
- Unexpected elements create stronger memories
- Pattern interrupts
- Surprising statistics

## Mirror Neurons

### Principle

Mirror neurons fire both when performing an action and when observing others perform it.

### Applications

- Show people using your product
- Demonstrate actions you want users to take
- Use video of satisfied customers
- Display the experience, not just the product

---

## Dopamine Triggers

### Principle

Dopamine is released in anticipation of rewards, not just when receiving them.

### Applications

**Variable Rewards**
- Unexpected bonuses and surprises
- Gamification elements
- Random acts of value

**Progress Indicators**
- Show advancement toward goals
- Celebrate milestones
- Visualize progress

**Gamification**
- Points and levels
- Achievements and badges
- Challenges and streaks

**Anticipation Building**
- Tease upcoming benefits
- Countdown to launch
- Preview exclusive content

---

## Cognitive Load

### Principle

Reducing mental effort increases conversion.

### Applications

**Simplify Choices**
- Fewer options
- Clear recommendations
- Default selections

**Visual Hierarchy**
- Clear importance ranking
- Consistent patterns
- Predictable layouts

**Chunking Information**
- Break into digestible pieces
- Use bullet points
- Progressive disclosure

**Familiar Patterns**
- Follow conventions
- Expected placements
- Consistent navigation
