# Scientific Research Methodology

## 1. Skill Description and Purpose

Scientific Research Methodology is a foundational capability for implementing rigorous scientific inquiry processes across all research domains. This skill provides systematic frameworks for formulating testable hypotheses, designing appropriate studies, collecting valid data, applying correct analytical methods, and interpreting results within proper scientific context. It serves as the methodological backbone ensuring research outputs meet the standards required for peer review, publication, and practical application.

The purpose of this skill encompasses the entire research lifecycle from conceptualization to dissemination. At the planning stage, it ensures research questions are well-formulated, feasible, and significant to the field. During design, it specifies appropriate methodologies matched to research objectives, whether quantitative, qualitative, or mixed methods. Throughout execution, it maintains quality controls and documentation standards. In analysis, it applies appropriate statistical or analytical techniques. Finally, in reporting, it ensures transparent, complete, and reproducible communication of findings.

This skill should be activated when initiating new research projects, evaluating proposed research designs, assessing the quality of existing research, developing grant proposals, creating research protocols, training research personnel, or establishing quality assurance frameworks for research programs. It applies equally to basic science, applied research, social sciences, engineering, and interdisciplinary investigations.

The methodological framework draws from philosophy of science principles, established disciplinary traditions, regulatory requirements for sponsored research, and emerging open science practices including preregistration, data sharing, and transparent reporting standards.

## 2. Required Inputs/Parameters

**Research Context:**
- Disciplinary domain and subdomain
- Research environment (academic, industrial, government)
- Funding source and sponsor requirements
- Regulatory framework (IRB, IACUC, biosafety)
- Timeline and resource constraints

**Research Question Specifications:**
- Primary research question in structured format
- Secondary and exploratory questions
- Theoretical framework or conceptual model
- Prior literature establishing knowledge gaps
- Hypotheses with directional predictions where appropriate

**Methodological Requirements:**
- Study type (observational, experimental, simulation, theoretical)
- Data collection methods and instruments
- Sampling strategy and sample size justification
- Variable definitions (independent, dependent, confounding, mediating)
- Analytical approach specifications

**Quality Parameters:**
- Validity requirements (internal, external, construct, statistical conclusion)
- Reliability standards for measurements
- Replication and reproducibility specifications
- Bias minimization strategies
- Ethical considerations and protections

## 3. Expected Outputs/Deliverables

**Research Design Documents:**
- Complete study protocol with rationale
- Sampling plan with power analysis
- Data collection instruments with validation evidence
- Standard operating procedures
- Data management plan

**Quality Assurance Materials:**
- Quality control checklists
- Training materials for research personnel
- Monitoring and auditing procedures
- Deviation reporting protocols
- Corrective action frameworks

**Analytical Plans:**
- Pre-specified statistical analysis plan
- Variable coding specifications
- Missing data handling procedures
- Sensitivity analysis specifications
- Decision rules for hypothesis testing

**Reporting Templates:**
- Structured reporting following discipline standards (CONSORT, STROBE, ARRIVE, PRISMA)
- Preregistration documentation
- Data availability statements
- Reproducibility documentation
- Limitations and future directions sections

**Ethical Documentation:**
- IRB/Ethics committee protocols
- Informed consent documents
- Data privacy and security plans
- Conflict of interest disclosures
- Authorship contribution frameworks

## 4. Example Use Cases

**Use Case 1: Interdisciplinary Grant Proposal**
A research team spanning biology, engineering, and data science develops a proposal for NIH funding on biosensor development. The skill ensures methodological coherence across disciplines, specifies appropriate experimental designs for device validation, develops integration points between computational and experimental workstreams, creates milestone-based evaluation frameworks, and ensures all human subjects considerations are addressed.

**Use Case 2: Laboratory Protocol Development**
A molecular biology laboratory establishes new protocols for CRISPR-based gene editing experiments. The skill develops standardized operating procedures, specifies positive and negative controls, creates quality metrics for editing efficiency, establishes documentation requirements for reproducibility, and designs experimental templates that minimize batch effects and technical variability.

**Use Case 3: Social Science Survey Research**
A political science department conducts nationwide opinion research on policy preferences. The skill develops sampling strategies for representativeness, validates survey instruments for construct validity, specifies administration protocols minimizing response bias, designs weighting procedures for non-response adjustment, and creates pre-analysis plans for transparent hypothesis testing.

**Use Case 4: Computational Research Reproducibility**
A computational chemistry group establishes standards for simulation-based research. The skill specifies hardware and software environment documentation requirements, creates version control protocols for code and data, develops validation procedures against known benchmarks, establishes uncertainty quantification methods, and creates templates for computational experiment notebooks.

**Use Case 5: Industry-Sponsored Research Design**
A pharmaceutical company sponsors academic research on drug mechanisms. The skill develops protocols ensuring scientific integrity under industry sponsorship, specifies data ownership and publication rights, creates analysis plans preventing selective reporting, establishes independent statistical analysis procedures, and ensures regulatory compliance for potential submission use.
