# Research Paper Analysis

## Overview

This skill provides comprehensive guidance and best practices for this domain.

## 1. Skill Description and Purpose

Research Paper Analysis is an advanced capability for systematically dissecting, evaluating, and synthesizing scientific publications across disciplines. This skill applies rigorous critical analysis frameworks to assess the validity, reliability, and significance of published research, enabling informed decisions about research credibility, reproducibility, and practical applicability. It transforms passive reading into active interrogation of scientific claims against methodological standards and evidence quality.

The core purpose of this skill serves multiple stakeholder needs. For researchers, it accelerates literature assimilation while ensuring critical engagement with sources. For practitioners, it evaluates whether findings justify implementation. For educators, it supports teaching of scientific reasoning and critical analysis. For decision-makers, it provides structured assessment of evidence quality to inform policy and investment. For peer reviewers and editors, it offers systematic evaluation frameworks.

This skill should be activated when evaluating papers for literature reviews, assessing evidence quality for systematic reviews, conducting peer review, preparing journal clubs or teaching discussions, evaluating research for investment or policy decisions, investigating reproducibility concerns, comparing competing findings across studies, or developing comprehensive understanding of a research area through detailed paper analysis.

The analytical framework incorporates discipline-specific methodological standards, statistical best practices, reporting guidelines (CONSORT, STROBE, PRISMA, ARRIVE), reproducibility criteria, and scientific reasoning principles. Analysis addresses internal validity, external validity, construct validity, statistical conclusion validity, and practical significance.

## 2. Required Inputs/Parameters

**Paper Specifications:**
- Full text of paper(s) to analyze (PDF, HTML, or accessible format)
- Supplementary materials and data availability
- Journal and publication context
- Citation information and bibliographic data
- Retraction or correction notices if any

**Analysis Scope:**
- Primary focus (methodology, statistics, claims, reproducibility)
- Depth of analysis required
- Comparison papers if conducting comparative analysis
- Specific concerns or questions to address
- Output format and audience

**Methodological Standards:**
- Discipline-specific evaluation criteria
- Applicable reporting guidelines
- Statistical standards for the field
- Reproducibility requirements
- Ethical standards relevant to the research

**Context Information:**
- Prior knowledge of the research area
- Related papers for context
- Known controversies or debates in the field
- Intended use of the analysis
- Expertise level of the audience

## 3. Expected Outputs/Deliverables

**Structured Paper Summary:**
- Research question and objectives
- Study design and methodology overview
- Key findings and claimed conclusions
- Novel contributions and significance claims
- Positioning within existing literature

**Methodological Evaluation:**
- Design appropriateness for research questions
- Control and comparison group adequacy
- Sampling strategy and sample size assessment
- Measurement validity and reliability
- Potential confounding and bias assessment

**Statistical Analysis Critique:**
- Statistical method appropriateness
- Assumption verification
- Effect size and confidence interval evaluation
- Multiple comparison handling
- Power and sample size adequacy
- p-hacking and selective reporting indicators

**Reproducibility Assessment:**
- Data availability and accessibility
- Code and analysis script availability
- Method description completeness
- Parameter and setting documentation
- Replication feasibility evaluation

**Synthesis and Recommendations:**
- Overall evidence quality rating
- Key strengths and limitations
- Recommendations for interpretation
- Suggestions for future research
- Practical implications assessment

## 4. Example Use Cases

**Use Case 1: Peer Review Support**
A journal editor receives a submitted manuscript in computational biology. The skill performs comprehensive methodological review, evaluates statistical analyses for appropriateness and correctness, checks code availability and reproducibility, assesses novelty claims against existing literature, and generates structured reviewer feedback with specific recommendations for revision.

**Use Case 2: Systematic Review Inclusion Assessment**
A research team conducts a systematic review on behavioral interventions. The skill applies Cochrane risk of bias tool to 47 included studies, extracts effect sizes and variance estimates, evaluates reporting completeness against CONSORT standards, identifies high-quality versus low-quality evidence, and creates summary tables for meta-analytic synthesis.

**Use Case 3: Investment Due Diligence**
A venture capital firm evaluates a biotech startup's foundational science. The skill analyzes the company's key publications, evaluates methodology and reproducibility of claimed discoveries, assesses statistical validity of efficacy claims, investigates citation patterns and field reception, and provides structured assessment of scientific credibility for investment decision.

**Use Case 4: Teaching and Journal Club**
A graduate seminar studies a landmark paper in machine learning. The skill develops comprehensive teaching notes including historical context, methodological innovations, statistical approaches, limitations and subsequent critiques, impact on the field, and discussion questions exploring the paper's strengths and weaknesses.

**Use Case 5: Replication Crisis Investigation**
A research integrity office investigates concerns about a highly-cited paper. The skill examines statistical patterns for GRIM/SPRITE inconsistencies, evaluates data distribution plausibility, checks image manipulation indicators, reviews supplementary materials for anomalies, and documents findings in structured report suitable for institutional review.

## 5. Prerequisites or Dependencies

**Access Requirements:**
- Full text access to papers (institutional or open access)
- Supplementary materials and datasets
- Access to cited literature for context
- Retraction databases (Retraction Watch)
- Preprint servers for recent submissions

**Analytical Tools:**
- Statistical verification tools (statcheck, GRIM test)
- Image analysis for figure integrity
- Reference management software
- Text analysis tools for plagiarism detection
- Data visualization for results examination

**Knowledge Base:**
- Discipline-specific methodological expertise
- Statistical methods familiarity
- Reporting guideline knowledge
- Field-specific conventions and standards
- Scientific integrity best practices

**Evaluation Frameworks:**
- Risk of bias assessment tools
- Reporting guideline checklists
- Statistical reporting standards
- Reproducibility checklists
- Evidence grading systems (GRADE)

**Documentation Standards:**
- Structured analysis templates
- Citation management
- Version tracking for ongoing analyses
- Collaborative review platforms
- Secure storage for confidential reviews
