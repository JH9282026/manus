# Leadership Development Planning

## New Leader Transition Plans

### First 30 Days: Listen and Learn
- Conduct listening tour with all stakeholders
- Meet each direct report individually (60 min each)
- Study team performance data and history
- Identify quick wins for early credibility
- Clarify expectations with your manager
- Document initial observations and hypotheses

### Days 31-60: Diagnose and Align
- Share preliminary observations with team
- Reset team goals and expectations
- Establish meeting cadence and norms
- Address one visible quick win
- Begin building key stakeholder relationships
- Identify team strengths and development needs

### Days 61-90: Act and Accelerate
- Implement priority changes
- Announce team vision and direction
- Launch development plans for key reports
- Establish performance tracking mechanisms
- Present 6-month roadmap to leadership
- Celebrate early wins with team

## Executive Presence Development

### Gravitas
- **Confidence**: Speak with conviction, own your expertise
- **Decisiveness**: Make clear decisions, avoid hedging language
- **Composure**: Stay calm under pressure, manage emotional reactions
- **Vision**: Articulate where you're heading and why

### Communication Excellence
- **Clarity**: Lead with the conclusion, then support
- **Concision**: Eliminate filler words, be precise
- **Conviction**: Use strong verbs, avoid qualifiers
- **Connection**: Read the room, adapt in real time

### Presence Enhancement Activities
1. Record and review your presentations monthly
2. Practice pause-pace-power technique
3. Seek speaking opportunities (all-hands, industry events)
4. Develop thought leadership content
5. Work with executive coach on blind spots

## Difficult Conversation Frameworks

### SBI Model (Situation-Behavior-Impact)
1. **Situation**: Describe the specific context
2. **Behavior**: State the observable behavior
3. **Impact**: Explain the effect on team, project, or results

### Performance Improvement Conversations
1. Express concern with specific examples
2. Ask for employee's perspective
3. Agree on performance gap
4. Co-create improvement plan with milestones
5. Set follow-up cadence
6. Document agreed actions

### Managing Up Conversations
1. Understand your manager's priorities and pressures
2. Come with solutions, not just problems
3. Use data to support requests
4. Align your asks with organizational goals
5. Follow up in writing

## Change Leadership Toolkit

### Kotter's 8-Step Process
1. Create urgency — Build case for change
2. Form coalition — Assemble guiding team
3. Create vision — Define clear direction
4. Communicate — Repeat vision 10x more than expected
5. Empower action — Remove barriers
6. Generate quick wins — Visible early successes
7. Build on change — Don't declare victory too early
8. Anchor in culture — Embed in systems and processes

### Resistance Management
| Resistance Type | Root Cause | Strategy |
|----------------|------------|----------|
| Rational | Lack of information | Data, evidence, Q&A |
| Emotional | Fear, loss of identity | Empathy, involvement, support |
| Political | Power shifts | Negotiation, coalition building |
| Habitual | Comfort with status quo | Training, gradual transition |

### Stakeholder Engagement Matrix
| Stakeholder Type | Engagement Level | Actions |
|-----------------|-----------------|---------|
| Champions | Active involvement | Empower, delegate leadership |
| Supporters | Regular updates | Maintain enthusiasm, address concerns |
| Fence-sitters | Targeted persuasion | Provide evidence, peer testimonials |
| Resistors | Direct engagement | Listen, address root concerns |
| Blockers | Executive escalation | Remove barriers, alternative paths |
