# Leadership Assessment Frameworks

## Leadership Style Assessment

### Transformational Leadership Indicators
- **Idealized Influence**: Serves as role model, earns trust and respect
- **Inspirational Motivation**: Articulates compelling vision, sets high expectations
- **Intellectual Stimulation**: Challenges assumptions, encourages innovation
- **Individualized Consideration**: Mentors, coaches, attends to individual needs

### Servant Leadership Characteristics
- **Listening**: Deeply understands others' perspectives
- **Empathy**: Accepts and recognizes individuals
- **Healing**: Helps others solve problems and conflicts
- **Awareness**: Self-aware and situationally aware
- **Persuasion**: Convinces rather than coerces
- **Stewardship**: Holds institution in trust for greater good

### Situational Leadership Matrix

| Employee Readiness | Leadership Style | Behavior |
|-------------------|-----------------|----------|
| Low competence, high commitment | Directing (S1) | High task, low relationship |
| Some competence, low commitment | Coaching (S2) | High task, high relationship |
| High competence, variable commitment | Supporting (S3) | Low task, high relationship |
| High competence, high commitment | Delegating (S4) | Low task, low relationship |

## Competency Assessment Dimensions

Rate each on a 1-5 scale with behavioral anchors:

### Strategic Thinking and Vision
- Sees big picture and long-term implications
- Connects daily work to organizational strategy
- Anticipates market and competitive shifts
- Makes decisions aligned with strategic priorities

### Communication and Influence
- Articulates ideas clearly and persuasively
- Adapts communication style to audience
- Builds consensus across diverse stakeholders
- Presents confidently to senior leadership

### Team Building and Development
- Selects and onboards talent effectively
- Creates psychologically safe environment
- Provides regular, constructive feedback
- Develops succession pipeline within team

### Decision-Making and Judgment
- Gathers appropriate data before deciding
- Balances speed with thoroughness
- Owns decisions and accepts accountability
- Adjusts course when evidence warrants

## Team Diagnostic Frameworks

### Lencioni's Five Dysfunctions Assessment

| Dysfunction | Symptom | Intervention |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Absence of trust | Invulnerability, hiding mistakes | Personal history exercises, vulnerability modeling |
| Fear of conflict | Artificial harmony | Permission to disagree, mining for conflict |
| Lack of commitment | Ambiguity, hedging | Cascading clarity, deadline setting |
| Avoidance of accountability | Low standards, resentment | Peer accountability, public goals |
| Inattention to results | Status-seeking, ego | Collective outcomes, visible scoreboard |

### Tuckman's Team Development Stages
- **Forming**: Orientation, dependence on leader
- **Storming**: Conflict, resistance to tasks and structure
- **Norming**: Cohesion, establishing processes
- **Performing**: Functional, autonomous, high output
- **Adjourning**: Dissolution, celebration, reflection

## 360-Degree Feedback Analysis

### Pattern Identification
1. Compare self-ratings against manager, peer, and report ratings
2. Identify blind spots (self-rating significantly higher than others)
3. Identify hidden strengths (self-rating lower than others)
4. Look for convergent themes across all rater groups
5. Flag specific behavioral examples in qualitative comments

### Action Planning from 360 Data
1. Select top 2-3 development priorities (not more)
2. Identify specific behavioral changes for each
3. Create accountability structure (coach, peer, manager)
4. Define observable success indicators
5. Schedule 90-day follow-up pulse check
