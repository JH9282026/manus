# Country Risk Frameworks

## Comprehensive Country Risk Profile Structure

### Executive Risk Summary
- Composite risk score (1-100 scale) with confidence interval
- Political stability assessment with regime continuity probability
- Economic vulnerability analysis with stress-test scenarios
- Social cohesion evaluation with unrest probability indicators
- Security threat landscape including terrorism, organized crime, cyber
- 12-month outlook with key monitoring indicators

### Political Risk Components
- Regime type and stability indicators
- Governance quality via corruption perception indices and institutional effectiveness
- Rule of law and judicial independence
- Political violence history and probability
- Government policy predictability and continuity

### Economic Risk Components
- GDP composition, growth trajectory, and diversification
- Debt-to-GDP ratio and fiscal sustainability
- Currency stability and foreign exchange reserves
- Trade dependencies and balance of payments
- Sovereign credit ratings (S&P/Moody's/Fitch scales from AAA to D)
- Inflation dynamics and monetary policy credibility

### Social Risk Components
- Demographic pressures: population growth, youth bulge, urbanization
- Income inequality (Gini coefficient) and poverty rates
- Ethnic/religious tensions and social fragmentation
- Education and human capital quality
- Migration patterns (brain drain, refugee flows)

## Key Data Sources

| Source Type | Examples | Purpose |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| Risk Indices | GPR, BGRI, ICRG, EIU | Quantitative benchmarking |
| Sovereign Ratings | S&P, Moody's, Fitch | Creditworthiness |
| Economic Data | IMF WEO, World Bank WDI | Macroeconomic fundamentals |
| Academic Sources | CFR, CSIS, Chatham House, RAND | Analytical frameworks |
| Government Sources | State Department, EU External Action | Official positions |

## Conditioning Factors
Systematically evaluate: geography (location, terrain, resources), economy (GDP, trade, innovation), demography (population pyramid, urbanization), military strength (conventional, nuclear, force projection), infrastructure (transport, energy, telecom), institutions (government stability, alliances, treaties), and historical heritage (colonial legacies, territorial grievances).

## Regional Geopolitical Assessment
- Power balance mapping with influence sphere visualization
- Conflict flashpoint identification with escalation probability
- Resource competition analysis with supply security implications
- Trade route vulnerability assessment including choke point risks
- Regional integration trajectory (fragmentation vs. cohesion)

## Bilateral Relations Analysis
- Relationship health scorecard with trajectory indicators
- Agreement/treaty inventory with compliance assessment
- Cooperation opportunity identification across domains
- Friction point analysis with escalation risk evaluation
