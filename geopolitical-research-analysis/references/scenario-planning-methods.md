# Scenario Planning & Stress Testing

## Scenario Planning Framework

### Building Geopolitical Scenarios
1. Identify 2-3 critical uncertainties (axes of uncertainty)
2. Develop 3-5 plausible scenarios spanning the outcome space
3. Assign probability weights based on current indicators
4. Map signposts and early warning indicators for each scenario
5. Assess impact across stakeholder-relevant dimensions
6. Develop strategic responses per scenario

### Scenario Report Structure
- Scenario narrative with key assumptions
- Probability weight and confidence level
- Key drivers and signposts to monitor
- Impact assessment (operational, financial, reputational)
- Strategic implications and recommended responses
- Early warning indicators for real-time tracking

## Supply Chain Geopolitical Stress Testing
1. Map critical dependencies by source country (minerals, components, energy)
2. Assess political stability and export policy trajectory for supplier countries
3. Analyze chokepoint risks for maritime shipping routes
4. Evaluate "friend-shoring" alternatives with political alignment scoring
5. Model scenario impacts: full decoupling, selective restrictions, status quo
6. Quantify supply disruption probability and cost impact per scenario

## Sanctions Regime Impact Analysis
1. Build comprehensive sanctions inventory (OFAC, EU, UK) with entity/sectoral scope
2. Map counterparty exposure across direct and indirect relationships
3. Assess secondary sanction risks for third-country operations
4. Evaluate enforcement trends and penalty precedents
5. Model variants: escalation, partial relaxation, status quo
6. Develop compliance enhancement roadmap

## Risk Assessment Matrix
- Define likelihood scale (1-5) and impact scale (1-5)
- Set risk tolerance thresholds by category
- Prioritize risks using heat map visualization
- Develop mitigation strategies: avoidance, transfer, reduction, acceptance
- Establish Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) for ongoing monitoring

## Methodological Approaches
- **PESTLE Analysis**: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental
- **Critical geographical analysis**: Power-space relationships
- **Historical inquiry**: Longue durée analysis of civilizational patterns
- **Political economy**: Link economic structures to political outcomes
- **Environmental geopolitics**: Climate-conflict nexuses

## Megatrend Analysis
Structural forces to track: US-China strategic competition, climate transition geopolitics, demographic shifts, technological sovereignty movements, deglobalization/regionalization, and energy transition dynamics.
