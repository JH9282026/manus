# LBO and Merger Models

## LBO Model Construction

### Purpose
Determine the maximum purchase price a financial sponsor (PE firm) can pay while achieving target returns (typically 20-25% IRR).

### LBO Model Structure

**Sources and Uses**
| Sources | Uses |
|---|---|
| Senior debt (bank) | Purchase equity |
| Subordinated debt (mezzanine) | Transaction fees |
| Equity contribution (sponsor) | Refinance existing debt |
| Rollover equity (management) | Cash to balance sheet |

### Step 1: Entry Assumptions
- Purchase price (EV/EBITDA multiple × EBITDA)
- Leverage (Total Debt / EBITDA, typically 4-6x)
- Sponsor equity check (purchase price - debt - rollover)
- Transaction fees (2-3% of EV)

### Step 2: Operating Model (5-7 year hold)
- Revenue growth assumptions
- Margin improvement (operational improvements)
- Working capital optimization
- CapEx requirements
- Management equity incentives

### Step 3: Debt Schedule
- Mandatory amortization by tranche
- Excess cash flow sweep (optional)
- Interest rates by tranche
- Debt paydown from operating cash flow

### Step 4: Exit Assumptions
- Exit year (typically Year 5)
- Exit multiple (equal to, below, or above entry)
- Calculate exit Enterprise Value
- Subtract remaining net debt = equity to sponsor

### Step 5: Returns Calculation
- **IRR**: Internal rate of return on sponsor equity
- **MOIC**: Multiple of invested capital (exit equity / entry equity)
- Target: 20-25% IRR, 2.0-3.0x MOIC

### Value Creation Bridge
Decompose returns into sources:
1. EBITDA growth (revenue growth + margin expansion)
2. Multiple expansion (exit multiple > entry multiple)
3. Debt paydown (leverage reduction from cash flow)

## Merger Model Construction

### Purpose
Assess whether an acquisition is accretive (increases EPS) or dilutive (decreases EPS) to the acquirer.

### Step 1: Standalone Models
- Build or obtain models for both acquirer and target
- Project earnings for 2-3 years forward

### Step 2: Transaction Assumptions
- Offer price and premium paid
- Consideration mix (cash / stock / combination)
- Transaction fees and financing costs
- Expected close date

### Step 3: Purchase Price Allocation
- Allocate excess purchase price over book value
- Create goodwill (residual after fair value allocation)
- Identify amortizable intangibles (customer relationships, technology)
- Determine incremental D&A from step-up

### Step 4: Synergy Modeling

| Synergy Type | Timeline | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue synergies | 2-3 years to realize | Cross-selling, new markets |
| Cost synergies | 1-2 years to realize | Headcount reduction, procurement |
| Financial synergies | Immediate | Lower cost of capital, tax benefits |

### Step 5: Pro Forma Combined EPS
1. Acquirer standalone EPS
2. + Target earnings contribution
3. + Synergies (after tax, phased in)
4. - Incremental D&A from purchase accounting
5. - Financing costs (interest on deal debt)
6. / New diluted share count (if stock consideration)
7. = Pro forma combined EPS

### Accretion/Dilution Test
- If pro forma EPS > acquirer standalone EPS → **Accretive** ✓
- If pro forma EPS < acquirer standalone EPS → **Dilutive** ✗
- Show breakeven analysis: What synergy level makes it accretive?
- Present sensitivity across offer prices and consideration mixes
