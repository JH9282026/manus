# Due Diligence Research

Conduct systematic investigation and analysis to verify claims, assess risks, and inform high-stakes decisions in M&A, investments, partnerships, and vendor selection.

## Overview

Provide structured frameworks for comprehensive due diligence across financial, operational, legal, commercial, and technology domains. Cover investigation methodologies, risk assessment, red flag identification, and deliverable creation for transaction support.

## Due Diligence Types

| Type | Focus | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Financial | Historical performance, projections | Are the numbers real? What are the trends? |
| Commercial | Market position, customers, competition | Is the business model sustainable? |
| Operational | Processes, systems, capabilities | Can the business execute? |
| Legal | Contracts, litigation, compliance | What are the legal risks? |
| Technology | Tech stack, IP, scalability | Is the technology sound? |
| HR/Management | Team, culture, key person risk | Who runs this and can they scale? |
| Environmental | Contamination, regulatory compliance | Are there environmental liabilities? |
| Tax | Structure, compliance, exposures | Are there hidden tax liabilities? |

## Due Diligence Process

### Phase 1: Planning (Week 1)
- Define scope and objectives based on deal type
- Assemble investigation team with domain expertise
- Create data request list organized by workstream
- Set up virtual data room access and protocols
- Establish timeline and milestone checkpoints

### Phase 2: Investigation (Weeks 2-4)
- Review documents in data room systematically
- Conduct management interviews and site visits
- Perform independent verification of key claims
- Analyze financial models and projections
- Assess market position through customer/competitor research

### Phase 3: Analysis (Week 4-5)
- Synthesize findings across workstreams
- Identify material risks and deal issues
- Quantify financial impact of findings
- Develop risk mitigation recommendations
- Prepare preliminary findings for deal team

### Phase 4: Reporting (Week 5-6)
- Write comprehensive due diligence report
- Present findings to decision-makers
- Provide input on deal structure and pricing
- Support negotiation with factual findings
- Document items for post-close integration

## Red Flag Indicators

| Category | Red Flags |
|---|---|
| Financial | Revenue concentration >30% in one customer, declining margins, unusual adjustments |
| Operational | Key person dependency, outdated systems, customer complaints trending up |
| Legal | Pending litigation, regulatory investigations, IP disputes |
| Commercial | Market share declining, customer churn increasing, pricing pressure |
| Technology | Technical debt, security vulnerabilities, no disaster recovery |
| HR | High turnover in leadership, no succession plan, cultural issues |
