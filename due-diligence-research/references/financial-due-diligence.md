# Financial Due Diligence

## Quality of Earnings Analysis

### Purpose
Determine sustainable, recurring earnings by adjusting reported financials for one-time items, accounting anomalies, and owner-specific expenses.

### Common Adjustments

| Category | Adjustment Type | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| One-time revenue | Remove non-recurring sales | Usually decrease |
| Owner compensation | Normalize to market rate | May increase or decrease |
| Related party transactions | Adjust to arm's length | Usually decrease |
| Deferred revenue recognition | Align timing | Varies |
| Inventory write-downs | Add back if non-recurring | Increase |
| Litigation settlements | Remove one-time costs | Increase |
| Restructuring costs | Remove if non-recurring | Increase |
| Pro forma adjustments | Add synergies, remove costs | Usually increase |

### Revenue Quality Assessment
- **Recurring vs one-time**: What percentage is subscription, contract, or repeat?
- **Customer concentration**: Revenue by top 5, 10, 20 customers
- **Growth drivers**: Organic vs acquisition, price vs volume
- **Backlog/pipeline**: Forward revenue visibility
- **Seasonality**: Quarterly patterns and year-end loading

## Working Capital Analysis

### Net Working Capital Components
- Accounts receivable (quality, aging, reserves)
- Inventory (turnover, obsolescence risk, valuation method)
- Prepaid expenses
- Accounts payable (terms, aging, key supplier relationships)
- Accrued liabilities (completeness, timing)
- Deferred revenue

### Working Capital Peg Calculation
1. Calculate monthly NWC for trailing 12 months
2. Exclude seasonal anomalies and one-time items
3. Determine average or median as the "peg"
4. Set adjustment mechanism in purchase agreement
5. Define true-up period and resolution process

## Financial Model Review

### Model Integrity Checks
- Verify formulas and calculations (trace precedents)
- Check assumptions against historical performance
- Test sensitivity of key assumptions
- Validate against industry benchmarks
- Review management case vs. base case vs. downside

### Projection Reasonableness
- Compare projected growth to historical CAGR
- Benchmark margins against industry peers
- Assess CapEx assumptions against maintenance needs
- Validate working capital assumptions against historical patterns
- Check for hockey-stick projections without supporting evidence

## Debt and Liability Review

- Review all debt instruments and covenants
- Identify change-of-control provisions
- Assess off-balance-sheet liabilities
- Review contingent liabilities and guarantees
- Evaluate pension/benefit obligations
- Check for tax liabilities or disputes
