# Technical Analysis Fundamentals for Stock Market Trading

## Overview
Technical analysis is the study of historical price and volume data to identify patterns, trends, and potential future price movements. It is based on the premise that market prices reflect all available information and that price movements follow identifiable patterns.

## Core Principles

### Market Action Discounts Everything
All known information is reflected in price.

**Implications:**
- Price movements incorporate all factors
- Fundamental analysis already priced in
- Focus on price action, not underlying causes
- Market is efficient in reflecting information

### Prices Move in Trends
Markets exhibit directional movement over time.

**Types of Trends:**
- **Uptrend:** Higher highs and higher lows
- **Downtrend:** Lower highs and lower lows
- **Sideways/Range:** Horizontal movement

**Trend Duration:**
- **Primary:** Long-term (months to years)
- **Secondary:** Medium-term (weeks to months)
- **Minor:** Short-term (days to weeks)

### History Tends to Repeat
Market psychology creates recurring patterns.

**Reasons:**
- Human behavior is consistent
- Fear and greed drive markets
- Patterns reflect collective psychology
- Historical patterns provide guidance

## Chart Types

### Line Charts
Connect closing prices with lines.

**Advantages:**
- Simple and clean
- Easy to identify trends
- Good for long-term view

**Disadvantages:**
- Limited information
- No intraday data
- Misses price extremes

### Bar Charts
Show open, high, low, close (OHLC).

**Components:**
- Vertical line: High to low range
- Left tick: Opening price
- Right tick: Closing price

**Advantages:**
- More information than line charts
- Shows price range
- Indicates volatility

### Candlestick Charts
Visual representation of OHLC data.

**Components:**
- Body: Open to close range
- Wicks/Shadows: High and low extremes
- Color: Green/white (up), red/black (down)

**Advantages:**
- Easy to read
- Visual pattern recognition
- Shows market sentiment
- Rich information density

**Common Patterns:**
- Doji: Indecision
- Hammer: Potential reversal
- Engulfing: Strong reversal signal
- Morning/Evening Star: Trend reversal

## Support and Resistance

### Support Levels
Price level where buying pressure prevents further decline.

**Characteristics:**
- Floor for price
- Buyers outnumber sellers
- Previous lows often become support
- Psychological round numbers

**Trading Implications:**
- Potential buying opportunity
- Place stop-loss below support
- Break below support is bearish signal

### Resistance Levels
Price level where selling pressure prevents further rise.

**Characteristics:**
- Ceiling for price
- Sellers outnumber buyers
- Previous highs often become resistance
- Psychological barriers

**Trading Implications:**
- Potential selling opportunity
- Place stop-loss above resistance
- Break above resistance is bullish signal

### Role Reversal
Support becomes resistance and vice versa.

**Mechanism:**
- Broken support becomes resistance
- Broken resistance becomes support
- Traders' psychology shifts
- Previous buyers become sellers (and vice versa)

## Trend Analysis

### Identifying Trends

**Uptrend Characteristics:**
- Series of higher highs
- Series of higher lows
- Price above moving averages
- Positive momentum

**Downtrend Characteristics:**
- Series of lower highs
- Series of lower lows
- Price below moving averages
- Negative momentum

**Sideways Trend:**
- Horizontal price movement
- No clear direction
- Range-bound trading
- Consolidation phase

### Trendlines
Lines connecting significant highs or lows.

**Uptrend Line:**
- Connect rising lows
- Support line
- Break below signals trend change

**Downtrend Line:**
- Connect declining highs
- Resistance line
- Break above signals trend change

**Validation:**
- Minimum two points to draw
- Three touches confirm validity
- Steeper lines less reliable
- Longer timeframe more significant

### Channels
Parallel lines containing price movement.

**Components:**
- Upper channel line (resistance)
- Lower channel line (support)
- Price oscillates between lines

**Trading Strategy:**
- Buy near lower channel
- Sell near upper channel
- Break signals trend change

## Volume Analysis

### Volume Significance
Number of shares traded confirms price movements.

**Principles:**
- Volume confirms trends
- High volume validates moves
- Low volume suggests weakness
- Volume precedes price

### Volume Patterns

**Increasing Volume:**
- Confirms trend strength
- Validates breakouts
- Indicates conviction
- Attracts more participants

**Decreasing Volume:**
- Suggests trend weakness
- Questions sustainability
- Indicates lack of interest
- Potential reversal warning

**Volume Spikes:**
- Climax buying/selling
- Potential exhaustion
- News-driven moves
- Reversal signals

### Volume Indicators

**On-Balance Volume (OBV):**
- Cumulative volume indicator
- Rising OBV confirms uptrend
- Falling OBV confirms downtrend
- Divergence signals reversal

**Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP):**
- Average price weighted by volume
- Intraday benchmark
- Institutional reference point
- Support/resistance level

## Timeframes

### Multiple Timeframe Analysis
Analyze different timeframes for complete picture.

**Common Timeframes:**
- **Long-term:** Monthly, weekly charts
- **Medium-term:** Daily charts
- **Short-term:** Hourly, 15-minute charts
- **Intraday:** 5-minute, 1-minute charts

**Strategy:**
- Identify trend on higher timeframe
- Find entry on lower timeframe
- Align trades with larger trend
- Avoid conflicting signals

### Timeframe Selection

**Day Trading:**
- 1-minute to 15-minute charts
- Intraday patterns
- Quick decisions
- High frequency

**Swing Trading:**
- Hourly to daily charts
- Multi-day holds
- Capture short-term trends
- Moderate frequency

**Position Trading:**
- Daily to weekly charts
- Weeks to months holds
- Follow major trends
- Low frequency

**Investing:**
- Weekly to monthly charts
- Long-term perspective
- Fundamental + technical
- Very low frequency

## Chart Patterns

### Continuation Patterns
Suggest trend will continue after consolidation.

**Flags and Pennants:**
- Brief consolidation
- Parallel lines (flag) or converging (pennant)
- Breakout in trend direction
- Measured move projection

**Triangles:**
- Symmetrical: Neutral, breakout either way
- Ascending: Bullish bias
- Descending: Bearish bias
- Converging trendlines

**Rectangles:**
- Horizontal consolidation
- Support and resistance levels
- Breakout direction indicates continuation
- Volume decreases during formation

### Reversal Patterns
Indicate potential trend change.

**Head and Shoulders:**
- Three peaks: left shoulder, head, right shoulder
- Neckline support
- Break below neckline bearish
- Inverse pattern bullish

**Double Top/Bottom:**
- Two peaks (top) or troughs (bottom)
- Failed attempt to break level
- Reversal confirmation on break
- Measured move target

**Rounding Top/Bottom:**
- Gradual curve formation
- Slow trend reversal
- Volume pattern important
- Long-term pattern

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing
Determine appropriate trade size.

**Factors:**
- Account size
- Risk tolerance
- Stop-loss distance
- Win rate and reward:risk ratio

**Formula:**
```
Position Size = (Account Risk) / (Entry - Stop Loss)
```

### Stop-Loss Orders
Predetermined exit point to limit losses.

**Types:**
- **Fixed:** Specific price level
- **Percentage:** % below entry
- **Technical:** Below support/above resistance
- **Trailing:** Moves with price

**Placement:**
- Below support (long positions)
- Above resistance (short positions)
- Beyond recent swing points
- Account for volatility

### Risk-Reward Ratio
Relationship between potential loss and gain.

**Calculation:**
```
Risk:Reward = (Entry - Stop Loss) / (Target - Entry)
```

**Guidelines:**
- Minimum 1:2 ratio recommended
- Higher ratio compensates for lower win rate
- Adjust based on strategy
- Consider probability of success

## Trading Psychology

### Emotional Discipline
Control emotions in trading decisions.

**Common Emotions:**
- **Fear:** Prevents entries, causes early exits
- **Greed:** Leads to overtrading, holding too long
- **Hope:** Keeps losing trades open
- **Regret:** Causes revenge trading

**Management:**
- Follow trading plan
- Use stop-losses
- Accept losses as part of process
- Take breaks after losses

### Cognitive Biases

**Confirmation Bias:**
- Seeking information that confirms beliefs
- Ignoring contradictory signals
- Solution: Actively seek opposing views

**Recency Bias:**
- Overweighting recent events
- Assuming recent trends continue
- Solution: Consider longer-term context

**Overconfidence:**
- Overestimating abilities
- Taking excessive risks
- Solution: Track performance objectively

## Best Practices

1. **Follow the Trend:** Trade with, not against, the trend
2. **Use Multiple Indicators:** Confirm signals with different tools
3. **Manage Risk:** Never risk more than 1-2% per trade
4. **Be Patient:** Wait for high-probability setups
5. **Keep Records:** Journal all trades for learning
6. **Stay Disciplined:** Follow your trading plan
7. **Continuous Learning:** Markets evolve, keep studying
8. **Control Emotions:** Don't let feelings drive decisions
9. **Start Small:** Practice with small positions
10. **Accept Losses:** They're part of trading

## Common Mistakes

1. **Trading Against Trend:** Fighting the market
2. **No Stop-Loss:** Unlimited risk exposure
3. **Overtrading:** Too many positions
4. **Revenge Trading:** Trying to recover losses quickly
5. **Ignoring Risk Management:** Risking too much per trade
6. **No Trading Plan:** Random, emotional decisions
7. **Overconfidence:** Thinking you can't lose
8. **Analysis Paralysis:** Too many indicators
9. **Chasing Prices:** FOMO-driven entries
10. **Not Adapting:** Using same strategy in all conditions
