# Trading Strategies and Risk Management

## Overview
Successful trading requires well-defined strategies and rigorous risk management. This guide covers common trading strategies, risk management principles, and best practices for consistent profitability.

## Trading Strategies

### Trend Following
Trade in direction of established trend.

**Principles:**
- "Trend is your friend"
- Enter on pullbacks
- Hold until trend reversal
- Use trailing stops

**Entry Signals:**
- Price above moving averages
- Higher highs and higher lows
- Breakout from consolidation
- Moving average crossovers

**Exit Signals:**
- Trend reversal
- Moving average crossover (opposite)
- Support/resistance break
- Trailing stop hit

**Advantages:**
- Clear direction
- Large profit potential
- Defined risk
- Works in all timeframes

**Challenges:**
- Whipsaws in ranging markets
- Late entries
- Giving back profits
- Requires patience

### Breakout Trading
Enter when price breaks key levels.

**Types:**
- **Range Breakout:** Break above resistance or below support
- **Pattern Breakout:** Triangle, flag, wedge completion
- **New High/Low:** Break to new price levels

**Confirmation:**
- Increased volume
- Strong momentum
- Follow-through
- Retest of breakout level

**False Breakouts:**
- Low volume
- Quick reversal
- Lack of follow-through
- Use stops to limit losses

**Strategy:**
- Identify key levels
- Wait for breakout
- Confirm with volume
- Enter on retest or continuation
- Set stop below/above breakout level

### Mean Reversion
Trade expectation that price returns to average.

**Premise:**
- Prices oscillate around mean
- Extreme moves tend to reverse
- Overbought/oversold conditions

**Indicators:**
- Bollinger Bands
- RSI
- Stochastic
- Standard deviation

**Entry Signals:**
- Price at extreme levels
- Overbought/oversold readings
- Divergence
- Reversal patterns

**Risk:**
- "Catching falling knife"
- Trend continuation
- Requires tight stops
- Best in ranging markets

### Swing Trading
Hold positions for days to weeks.

**Characteristics:**
- Capture short-term trends
- Technical analysis focus
- Multiple positions
- Active management

**Timeframes:**
- Daily charts for analysis
- Hourly for entry/exit
- Hold 2-10 days typically

**Strategy:**
- Identify swing points
- Enter on pullbacks
- Target next resistance/support
- Use technical stops

**Advantages:**
- Less time-intensive than day trading
- Capture larger moves
- Fewer transactions
- Lower stress

### Day Trading
Open and close positions within same day.

**Characteristics:**
- No overnight risk
- High frequency
- Small profits per trade
- Requires focus and discipline

**Common Strategies:**
- Scalping: Very short-term, small profits
- Momentum: Trade strong moves
- Range trading: Buy support, sell resistance
- News trading: Trade on announcements

**Requirements:**
- Fast execution
- Real-time data
- Adequate capital
- Time commitment
- Emotional control

### Position Trading
Long-term holds based on fundamentals and technicals.

**Characteristics:**
- Weeks to months holds
- Fundamental analysis important
- Fewer trades
- Larger position sizes

**Approach:**
- Identify long-term trends
- Use weekly/monthly charts
- Fundamental support
- Patient approach

**Advantages:**
- Less time-intensive
- Lower transaction costs
- Capture major trends
- Less stress

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing

**Fixed Dollar Risk**
Risk same dollar amount per trade.

**Formula:**
```
Position Size = Risk Amount / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)
```

**Example:**
- Account: $100,000
- Risk per trade: 1% = $1,000
- Entry: $50
- Stop: $48
- Position Size = $1,000 / ($50 - $48) = 500 shares

**Fixed Percentage Risk**
Risk percentage of account per trade.

**Guidelines:**
- Conservative: 0.5-1% per trade
- Moderate: 1-2% per trade
- Aggressive: 2-3% per trade
- Never > 5% per trade

**Kelly Criterion**
Optimal position size based on win rate and payoff ratio.

**Formula:**
```
Kelly % = W - [(1 - W) / R]
W = Win rate
R = Average win / Average loss
```

### Stop-Loss Strategies

**Types of Stops:**

**Fixed Stop**
- Specific price level
- Set at entry
- Doesn't move

**Percentage Stop**
- % below entry
- Simple to calculate
- Doesn't consider volatility

**Volatility Stop**
- Based on ATR
- Adapts to market conditions
- Example: 2× ATR from entry

**Technical Stop**
- Below support (long)
- Above resistance (short)
- Beyond swing points
- Pattern-based

**Trailing Stop**
- Moves with price
- Locks in profits
- Never moves against position
- Example: 10% trailing stop

**Time Stop**
- Exit after specific time
- If trade not working
- Frees capital
- Prevents hope trading

### Risk-Reward Ratio

**Calculation:**
```
Risk:Reward = (Entry - Stop) / (Target - Entry)
```

**Guidelines:**
- Minimum 1:2 ratio
- Higher ratio for lower win rate
- 1:3 or better ideal
- Consider probability

**Example:**
- Entry: $50
- Stop: $48 (risk $2)
- Target: $56 (reward $6)
- Risk:Reward = $2 / $6 = 1:3

**Win Rate vs. Risk:Reward:**
- 50% win rate needs 1:1 to break even
- 40% win rate needs 1:1.5 to break even
- 33% win rate needs 1:2 to break even

### Portfolio Risk Management

**Diversification:**
- Multiple positions
- Different sectors
- Various strategies
- Uncorrelated assets

**Correlation:**
- Avoid highly correlated positions
- True diversification reduces risk
- Consider market conditions

**Maximum Exposure:**
- Total risk across all positions
- Typically 5-10% of account
- Adjust for market conditions
- Consider correlation

**Drawdown Management:**
- Maximum acceptable drawdown
- Reduce size after losses
- Take break if needed
- Protect capital

## Trading Psychology

### Emotional Control

**Common Emotions:**
- **Fear:** Prevents action, early exits
- **Greed:** Overtrading, holding too long
- **Hope:** Keeping losers
- **Regret:** Revenge trading

**Management Techniques:**
- Follow trading plan
- Use mechanical stops
- Accept losses
- Take breaks
- Journal emotions

### Discipline

**Key Areas:**
- Following trading plan
- Taking all signals
- Cutting losses quickly
- Letting winners run
- Consistent position sizing

**Building Discipline:**
- Written trading plan
- Checklist for trades
- Review process
- Accountability
- Start small

### Cognitive Biases

**Confirmation Bias:**
- Seeking confirming information
- Ignoring contradictory signals
- Solution: Devil's advocate approach

**Recency Bias:**
- Overweighting recent events
- Assuming trends continue
- Solution: Longer-term perspective

**Overconfidence:**
- Overestimating abilities
- Taking excessive risks
- Solution: Track performance objectively

**Loss Aversion:**
- Holding losers too long
- Taking winners too early
- Solution: Mechanical rules

## Performance Tracking

### Key Metrics

**Win Rate:**
```
Win Rate = Winning Trades / Total Trades × 100%
```

**Average Win/Loss:**
```
Average Win = Total Profit / Number of Wins
Average Loss = Total Loss / Number of Losses
```

**Expectancy:**
```
Expectancy = (Win Rate × Average Win) - (Loss Rate × Average Loss)
```

**Profit Factor:**
```
Profit Factor = Gross Profit / Gross Loss
```

**Sharpe Ratio:**
```
Sharpe Ratio = (Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation
```

**Maximum Drawdown:**
Largest peak-to-trough decline.

**Benefits:**

- Improve strategy
- Track progress
- Accountability

## Best Practices

1. **Have a Plan:** Written trading plan
2. **Manage Risk:** Never risk more than you can afford
3. **Be Consistent:** Follow your rules
4. **Keep Records:** Journal all trades
5. **Control Emotions:** Don't let feelings drive decisions
6. **Start Small:** Build confidence gradually
7. **Continuous Learning:** Markets evolve
8. **Be Patient:** Wait for high-probability setups
9. **Accept Losses:** Part of the game
10. **Protect Capital:** Preservation first, profits second

## Common Mistakes

1. **No Trading Plan:** Random, emotional decisions
2. **Poor Risk Management:** Risking too much
3. **Overtrading:** Too many positions
4. **Revenge Trading:** Trying to recover losses
5. **Moving Stops:** Avoiding losses
6. **No Stop-Loss:** Unlimited risk
7. **Ignoring Signals:** Cherry-picking trades
8. **Overconfidence:** After winning streak
9. **Analysis Paralysis:** Too much information
10. **Not Adapting:** Same strategy in all conditions

## Conclusion

Successful trading combines well-defined strategies with rigorous risk management and emotional discipline. Focus on process over outcomes, protect your capital, and continuously learn and adapt. Remember: consistent profitability comes from following a proven plan, not from finding the "perfect" strategy.
