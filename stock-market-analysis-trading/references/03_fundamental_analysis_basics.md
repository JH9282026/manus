# Fundamental Analysis Basics for Stock Market Investing

## Overview
Fundamental analysis evaluates a company's intrinsic value by examining financial statements, management quality, competitive advantages, and economic factors. It helps investors identify undervalued or overvalued stocks for long-term investment decisions.

## Financial Statement Analysis

### Income Statement
Shows company's revenues, expenses, and profitability over period.

**Key Metrics:**
- **Revenue/Sales:** Total income from operations
- **Gross Profit:** Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold
- **Operating Income:** Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
- **Net Income:** Bottom line profit after all expenses and taxes
- **Earnings Per Share (EPS):** Net Income / Shares Outstanding

**Analysis:**
- Revenue growth trends
- Profit margin trends
- Expense management
- Year-over-year comparisons

### Balance Sheet
Snapshot of company's assets, liabilities, and equity at point in time.

**Components:**
- **Assets:** What company owns (cash, inventory, property)
- **Liabilities:** What company owes (debt, payables)
- **Shareholders' Equity:** Assets - Liabilities

**Key Metrics:**
- **Current Ratio:** Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- **Debt-to-Equity:** Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
- **Book Value Per Share:** Shareholders' Equity / Shares Outstanding

**Analysis:**
- Liquidity position
- Debt levels
- Asset quality
- Financial stability

### Cash Flow Statement
Tracks cash inflows and outflows.

**Categories:**
- **Operating Activities:** Cash from core business
- **Investing Activities:** Capital expenditures, acquisitions
- **Financing Activities:** Debt, equity, dividends

**Key Metrics:**
- **Free Cash Flow:** Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures
- **Cash Flow Per Share:** Operating Cash Flow / Shares Outstanding

**Importance:**
- Cash is king
- Profitability vs. cash generation
- Sustainability of operations
- Dividend payment ability

## Valuation Ratios

### Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
```
P/E = Stock Price / Earnings Per Share
```

**Interpretation:**
- High P/E: Growth expectations or overvalued
- Low P/E: Value opportunity or problems
- Compare to industry average
- Consider growth rate (PEG ratio)

### Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
```
P/B = Stock Price / Book Value Per Share
```

**Interpretation:**
- P/B < 1: Trading below book value
- P/B > 1: Premium to book value
- Useful for asset-heavy companies
- Less relevant for service/tech companies

### Price-to-Sales (P/S) Ratio
```
P/S = Market Cap / Total Revenue
```

**Uses:**
- Valuing unprofitable companies
- Comparing companies in same industry
- Less manipulable than earnings

### Enterprise Value to EBITDA (EV/EBITDA)
```
EV/EBITDA = Enterprise Value / EBITDA
Enterprise Value = Market Cap + Debt - Cash
EBITDA = Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization
```

**Advantages:**
- Accounts for debt
- Useful for comparing leveraged companies
- Industry-standard valuation metric

## Profitability Metrics

### Gross Margin
```
Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100%
```

**Indicates:**
- Pricing power
- Production efficiency
- Competitive position

### Operating Margin
```
Operating Margin = Operating Income / Revenue × 100%
```

**Shows:**
- Operational efficiency
- Cost management
- Core business profitability

### Net Profit Margin
```
Net Margin = Net Income / Revenue × 100%
```

**Reflects:**
- Overall profitability
- After all expenses
- Bottom-line efficiency

### Return on Equity (ROE)
```
ROE = Net Income / Shareholders' Equity × 100%
```

**Measures:**
- Profitability relative to equity
- Management effectiveness
- Shareholder value creation

**DuPont Analysis:**
```
ROE = Net Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier
```

### Return on Assets (ROA)
```
ROA = Net Income / Total Assets × 100%
```

**Indicates:**
- Asset utilization efficiency
- Profitability per dollar of assets

## Growth Metrics

### Revenue Growth
```
Revenue Growth = (Current Revenue - Previous Revenue) / Previous Revenue × 100%
```

**Analysis:**
- Consistent growth positive
- Compare to industry
- Organic vs. acquisition-driven
- Sustainable growth rate

### Earnings Growth
```
Earnings Growth = (Current EPS - Previous EPS) / Previous EPS × 100%
```

**Considerations:**
- Quality of earnings
- One-time items
- Sustainability
- Guidance and forecasts

### PEG Ratio
```
PEG = P/E Ratio / Annual EPS Growth Rate
```

**Interpretation:**
- PEG < 1: Potentially undervalued
- PEG = 1: Fairly valued
- PEG > 1: Potentially overvalued

## Dividend Analysis

### Dividend Yield
```
Dividend Yield = Annual Dividend Per Share / Stock Price × 100%
```

**Considerations:**
- Income generation
- Compare to alternatives
- Sustainability
- Growth potential

### Dividend Payout Ratio
```
Payout Ratio = Dividends / Net Income × 100%
```

**Interpretation:**
- < 50%: Room for growth
- 50-70%: Balanced
- > 70%: May be unsustainable
- > 100%: Paying more than earning

### Dividend Growth Rate
Historical rate of dividend increases.

**Importance:**
- Income growth potential
- Management confidence
- Financial health indicator

## Qualitative Factors

### Competitive Advantage (Moat)
Sustainable competitive advantages:
- **Brand Power:** Strong brand recognition
- **Network Effects:** Value increases with users
- **Cost Advantages:** Economies of scale
- **Switching Costs:** Difficult for customers to leave
- **Regulatory Barriers:** Licenses, patents

### Management Quality
- Track record
- Capital allocation decisions
- Transparency and communication
- Insider ownership
- Compensation structure

### Industry Analysis
- Growth prospects
- Competitive landscape
- Regulatory environment
- Technological disruption
- Cyclical vs. defensive

### Economic Factors
- Interest rates
- GDP growth
- Inflation
- Consumer confidence
- Employment trends

## Valuation Models

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
Present value of future cash flows.

**Steps:**
1. Project future cash flows
2. Determine discount rate (WACC)
3. Calculate terminal value
4. Discount to present value
5. Compare to current price

### Comparable Company Analysis
Compare valuation multiples to peers.

**Process:**
1. Identify comparable companies
2. Calculate valuation multiples
3. Apply median/average to target
4. Adjust for differences

### Precedent Transactions
Analyze similar M&A transactions.

**Uses:**
- Acquisition valuation
- Control premium assessment
- Market sentiment gauge

## Investment Strategies

### Value Investing
Buy undervalued stocks based on fundamentals.

**Characteristics:**
- Low P/E, P/B ratios
- High dividend yields
- Strong balance sheets
- Margin of safety

**Famous Practitioners:**
- Benjamin Graham
- Warren Buffett
- Charlie Munger

### Growth Investing
Invest in companies with high growth potential.

**Characteristics:**
- High revenue/earnings growth
- Expanding markets
- Innovative products
- Higher P/E ratios acceptable

**Focus:**
- Future potential over current value
- Disruptive companies
- Market leaders

### GARP (Growth at Reasonable Price)
Blend of value and growth.

**Approach:**
- Growth companies
- Reasonable valuations
- PEG ratio < 1
- Balance risk and reward

## Best Practices

1. **Read Financial Statements:** Understand the numbers
2. **Compare to Peers:** Industry context matters
3. **Look for Trends:** Multi-year analysis
4. **Consider Qualitative Factors:** Numbers aren't everything
5. **Margin of Safety:** Buy below intrinsic value
6. **Long-Term Perspective:** Fundamental analysis for long-term
7. **Diversify:** Don't put all eggs in one basket
8. **Stay Updated:** Monitor quarterly reports
9. **Be Patient:** Value takes time to realize
10. **Continuous Learning:** Markets and companies evolve

## Common Mistakes

1. **Ignoring Cash Flow:** Focusing only on earnings
2. **Not Reading Footnotes:** Missing important details
3. **Overpaying for Growth:** Excessive valuations
4. **Ignoring Debt:** Overlooking leverage risks
5. **Short-Term Focus:** Trading on quarterly results
6. **Confirmation Bias:** Seeking only supporting information
7. **Ignoring Industry Trends:** Company-specific focus only
8. **Not Adjusting for One-Time Items:** Distorted metrics
9. **Overcomplicating:** Analysis paralysis
10. **Emotional Decisions:** Fear and greed override analysis
