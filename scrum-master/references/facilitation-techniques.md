# Scrum Master Facilitation Techniques

Comprehensive guide to facilitating effective Scrum ceremonies and team meetings.

## Meeting Facilitation Fundamentals

### Preparation
- Clear agenda and objectives
- Time allocation for each topic
- Materials and tools ready
- Participants notified and prepared
- Room setup (physical or virtual)

### During Meeting
- Start on time
- Review agenda and objectives
- Timebox discussions
- Ensure all voices heard
- Capture decisions and actions
- Park off-topic items
- End on time with clear next steps

### Follow-Up
- Distribute notes and action items
- Track action completion
- Gather feedback on meeting effectiveness
- Continuously improve

## Facilitation Techniques

### Timeboxing
Set strict time limits for activities and enforce them consistently.

### Silent Brainstorming
Participants write ideas individually before discussing, ensuring all voices heard.

### Dot Voting
Each person gets limited votes to prioritize items democratically.

### Fist of Five
Quick consensus check: 0 fingers (block) to 5 fingers (strong support).

### Round Robin
Each person speaks in turn, ensuring equal participation.

### Parking Lot
Capture off-topic items for later discussion, keeping meeting focused.

## Handling Difficult Situations

### Dominant Participants
- Acknowledge their input
- Invite others to speak
- Use round robin technique
- Set speaking time limits

### Silent Participants
- Direct questions to them
- Use written techniques (sticky notes)
- Create safe environment
- Follow up one-on-one

### Off-Topic Discussions
- Acknowledge the topic
- Park for later
- Redirect to agenda
- Timebox if important

### Conflicts
- Acknowledge different perspectives
- Focus on interests, not positions
- Seek win-win solutions
- Take offline if needed

## Virtual Facilitation

### Tools
- Video conferencing (Zoom, Teams)
- Digital whiteboards (Miro, Mural)
- Polling and voting tools
- Breakout rooms

### Best Practices
- Camera on for engagement
- Mute when not speaking
- Use chat for questions
- Share screen for focus
- More breaks than in-person
- Over-communicate

## Retrospective Facilitation

### Formats
- Start-Stop-Continue
- 4 Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed for)
- Sailboat
- Mad-Sad-Glad
- Timeline
- Fishbone diagram

### Process
1. Set the stage (5 min)
2. Gather data (10 min)
3. Generate insights (15 min)
4. Decide what to do (15 min)
5. Close retrospective (5 min)

### Creating Safety
- Retrospective prime directive
- Confidentiality
- No blame
- Focus on improvement
- Celebrate successes
