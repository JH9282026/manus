# Culture Transformation

Assess, design, and implement organizational culture change programs.

---

## Culture Assessment

### Assessment Instruments

**Organizational Culture Assessment Instrument (OCAI)**
- Based on Competing Values Framework
- Measures current and preferred culture across 6 dimensions
- Quick to administer (15-minute survey)
- Produces culture profile mapped to 4 quadrants

**Denison Organizational Culture Survey**
- Measures 4 traits: Mission, Adaptability, Involvement, Consistency
- Benchmarked against global database
- Links culture to business performance outcomes
- Detailed diagnostic across 12 indices

**Barrett Values Assessment**
- Maps organizational values across 7 levels of consciousness
- Identifies cultural entropy (dysfunction) percentage
- Compares current vs. desired values
- Individual and organizational alignment analysis

### Culture Assessment Process

1. Select assessment instrument aligned to strategic questions
2. Administer to representative sample (aim for 70%+ response rate)
3. Analyze results identifying strengths, gaps, and tension points
4. Conduct focus groups to explore quantitative findings
5. Create culture profile report with current state and gap analysis
6. Present findings to leadership with strategic implications

## Culture Change Program Design

### Behavior-Based Approach

Culture changes when collective behaviors change. Define target culture through observable behaviors.

**Target Behavior Design:**
1. Identify 5-8 critical behaviors that define the desired culture
2. Make each behavior specific, observable, and measurable
3. Create behavioral anchors showing what "good" and "not good" look like
4. Design enablers (systems, processes, incentives) supporting target behaviors
5. Remove barriers making old behaviors easier than new ones

### Culture Levers

| Lever | Mechanism | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Leadership modeling | Leaders visibly demonstrate target behaviors | CEO holds open office hours (accessibility) |
| Storytelling | Share narratives reinforcing desired culture | Spotlight employees exemplifying values |
| Rituals and symbols | Create recurring practices embedding culture | Weekly innovation hour, failure celebration |
| Reward systems | Align recognition with target behaviors | Values-based recognition program |
| Hiring and promotion | Select and advance culture carriers | Include cultural fit in interview process |
| Space and environment | Physical/virtual design reflecting values | Open collaboration spaces, remote-first tools |

## Employee Engagement Strategy

### Engagement Model

Drive engagement through four pillars:

1. **Purpose** — Connect work to meaningful outcomes and organizational mission
2. **Growth** — Provide development opportunities, career paths, and stretch assignments
3. **Connection** — Build belonging through teams, communities, and relationships
4. **Autonomy** — Empower decision-making, flexible work, and ownership

### Engagement Measurement

| Method | Frequency | Depth | Action Speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual engagement survey | Yearly | Deep (40-60 questions) | Slow (quarterly action plans) |
| Pulse surveys | Monthly/quarterly | Focused (5-10 questions) | Medium (monthly actions) |
| Always-on feedback | Continuous | Targeted (1-3 questions) | Fast (real-time alerts) |
| Stay interviews | Semi-annual | Personal (1:1 conversation) | Fast (individual actions) |
| Exit interviews | At departure | Diagnostic (structured interview) | Slow (trend analysis) |
