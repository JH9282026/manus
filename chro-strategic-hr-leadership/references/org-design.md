# Organizational Design

Frameworks for designing effective organizational structures.

---

## Organization Design Process

### Phase 1: Strategic Alignment
- Clarify business strategy and operating model
- Identify organizational capabilities required
- Define design criteria (speed, efficiency, innovation, customer focus)
- Establish design principles and constraints

### Phase 2: Structure Design
- Define major organizational units and reporting relationships
- Determine grouping logic (function, product, geography, customer, matrix)
- Set span of control targets by level
- Design coordination mechanisms (committees, liaisons, shared services)
- Map decision rights using RACI or RAPID frameworks

### Phase 3: Role Design
- Define key roles and accountabilities
- Establish job architecture (families, levels, career tracks)
- Design role profiles with skills, competencies, and KPIs
- Identify critical roles requiring succession planning

### Phase 4: Implementation
- Develop transition plan with phased implementation
- Create communication plan for structural changes
- Manage talent placement and redeployment
- Implement new governance and decision-making processes
- Monitor and adjust based on performance data

## Structure Types

| Structure | Advantages | Disadvantages | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Functional | Expertise depth, efficiency | Siloed, slow cross-functional | Stable, single-product |
| Divisional | Market focus, accountability | Duplication, competition | Multi-product, diverse markets |
| Matrix | Flexibility, shared resources | Complexity, dual reporting | Complex, multi-dimensional |
| Network | Agility, innovation | Coordination cost, culture risk | Dynamic, project-based |
| Flat/agile | Speed, empowerment | Scaling challenges, career clarity | Startups, innovation units |

---

## Span of Control Guidelines

| Role Level | Recommended Span | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| C-suite | 5-8 | Strategic complexity, diverse functions |
| VP/Director | 5-10 | Balance oversight with empowerment |
| Manager | 8-12 | Operational efficiency, coaching capacity |
| Team lead | 10-15 | Standard work, experienced team |
| Supervisor (ops) | 15-25 | Routine work, clear processes |

**When to narrow spans:** New teams, complex work, development-heavy roles, high change
**When to widen spans:** Experienced teams, standardized processes, strong self-management
