# Customer Experience (CX) Management

Design and continuously improve customer experiences across all touchpoints to drive loyalty and business growth.

## Overview

Provide frameworks for understanding, designing, and measuring customer experiences. Integrate customer research, journey mapping, experience measurement, and improvement initiatives to create differentiated experiences that increase retention, lifetime value, and competitive advantage.

## Journey Mapping Process

1. **Define scope** — Select persona, journey name, start/end points
2. **Research** — Conduct customer interviews, analyze behavioral data
3. **Map current state** — Document stages, touchpoints, actions, thoughts, emotions
4. **Identify moments of truth** — Flag make-or-break interactions
5. **Analyze pain points** — Rate severity and frequency of friction
6. **Design future state** — Redesign experience to eliminate friction and create delight
7. **Prioritize improvements** — Use impact vs. effort matrix
8. **Implement and measure** — Deploy changes with clear success metrics

## CX Metrics Framework

| Metric | What It Measures | Collection Method | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| NPS | Customer loyalty/advocacy | Relationship survey | Industry-specific |
| CSAT | Satisfaction with interaction | Transactional survey | >80% |
| CES | Effort required | Post-interaction survey | <3 on 7-point scale |
| Churn Rate | Customer defection | Behavioral tracking | <5% annual (B2B) |
| CLV | Customer lifetime value | Financial analysis | Segment-specific |
| FCR | First contact resolution | Operational tracking | >70% |

## VoC Program Design

### Feedback Channel Integration

Combine structured feedback (surveys, NPS), unstructured feedback (reviews, social, support tickets), and behavioral signals (usage patterns, churn indicators) into a unified customer intelligence platform.

### Closed-Loop Action Process

1. **Capture** — Collect feedback across all channels
2. **Analyze** — Identify themes, root causes, and opportunities
3. **Act** — Route insights to accountable owners with SLAs
4. **Follow up** — Close the loop with customers who provided feedback
5. **Report** — Share insights and actions across the organization

## CX Improvement Prioritization

Use the CX Impact Matrix to prioritize improvements:

| Criteria | Weight | Measurement |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Customer impact | 30% | NPS/CSAT lift potential |
| Business impact | 25% | Revenue/retention effect |
| Volume affected | 20% | Customers experiencing the issue |
| Feasibility | 15% | Implementation complexity |
| Strategic alignment | 10% | Connection to company priorities |
