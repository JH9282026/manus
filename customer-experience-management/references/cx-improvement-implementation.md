# CX Improvement & Implementation

## Improvement Prioritization Framework

### Impact-Effort Matrix

Categorize all identified improvements into four quadrants:

| Quadrant | Impact | Effort | Action |
|----------|--------|--------|--------|
| Quick Wins | High | Low | Implement immediately |
| Strategic Projects | High | High | Plan and resource carefully |
| Fill-ins | Low | Low | Do when capacity allows |
| Time Sinks | Low | High | Deprioritize or eliminate |

### Scoring Methodology

Rate each improvement opportunity on these dimensions (1-10):

1. **Customer Impact Score** — How much will this reduce friction or increase satisfaction?
2. **Business Impact Score** — What is the revenue/cost/retention effect?
3. **Volume Score** — How many customers are affected?
4. **Feasibility Score** — How complex is implementation?
5. **Speed Score** — How quickly can we deliver results?

**Priority Score** = (Customer Impact × 0.3) + (Business Impact × 0.25) + (Volume × 0.2) + (Feasibility × 0.15) + (Speed × 0.1)

## Experience Design Principles

### Core CX Design Principles

1. **Effortless** — Minimize customer effort at every touchpoint
2. **Consistent** — Deliver uniform quality across all channels
3. **Personalized** — Tailor experiences to individual needs and context
4. **Proactive** — Anticipate needs before customers articulate them
5. **Emotional** — Create positive emotional connections
6. **Transparent** — Be open about processes, timelines, and limitations
7. **Recoverable** — Design excellent recovery for when things go wrong

### Service Recovery Framework (LEARN)

- **L**isten — Let the customer express their frustration fully
- **E**mpathize — Acknowledge their feelings genuinely
- **A**pologize — Take responsibility without excuses
- **R**esolve — Fix the problem quickly and completely
- **N**otify — Follow up to confirm satisfaction

### The Peak-End Rule

Customers judge experiences based on their most intense moment (peak) and the final moment (end), not the average. Design experiences with:
- A deliberate positive peak moment
- A strong positive ending
- Minimized negative peaks

## Building Organizational CX Capability

### CX Governance Structure

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| CX Executive Sponsor | Strategic direction, budget, cross-functional authority |
| CX Leader | Program management, measurement, improvement coordination |
| CX Champions (per department) | Department-level CX initiatives and accountability |
| Frontline Teams | Daily CX delivery and feedback collection |
| Analytics Team | Data analysis, insight generation, reporting |

### CX Culture Development

1. **Leadership modeling** — Executives regularly review customer feedback
2. **Customer immersion** — All employees interact with customers periodically
3. **Recognition programs** — Celebrate CX wins and customer-centric behaviors
4. **CX metrics in performance reviews** — Tie compensation to CX outcomes
5. **Customer stories** — Share real customer stories in all-hands meetings
6. **Empowerment** — Give frontline employees authority to resolve issues

### Change Management for CX

Apply Kotter's 8-step model:
1. Create urgency with customer impact data
2. Build a guiding coalition of CX champions
3. Develop CX vision and strategy
4. Communicate the vision repeatedly
5. Empower broad-based action
6. Generate short-term wins (quick wins from impact matrix)
7. Consolidate gains and produce more change
8. Anchor CX thinking in organizational culture
