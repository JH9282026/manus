# Voice of Customer & CX Measurement

## VoC Program Architecture

### Feedback Collection Framework

| Method | Type | Timing | Best For |
|--------|------|--------|----------|
| Relationship NPS | Survey | Quarterly/Semi-annual | Overall loyalty tracking |
| Transactional CSAT | Survey | Post-interaction | Specific touchpoint quality |
| CES Survey | Survey | Post-support/purchase | Effort measurement |
| In-app feedback | Digital | Real-time | Product experience |
| Social listening | Passive | Continuous | Sentiment and trends |
| Online reviews | Passive | Continuous | Public perception |
| Support ticket analysis | Passive | Continuous | Issue identification |
| Customer interviews | Qualitative | Periodic | Deep understanding |
| Advisory boards | Qualitative | Quarterly | Strategic input |

### Survey Design Best Practices

- Keep surveys under 5 minutes (3-5 questions for transactional)
- Ask the metric question first (NPS/CSAT/CES)
- Include one open-ended "why" question
- Use consistent scales across all surveys
- Randomize response options to avoid bias
- Test on mobile devices (60%+ response on mobile)
- Send within 24 hours of the interaction
- Target 15-25% response rate for transactional surveys

## CX Metrics Deep Dive

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Question**: "How likely are you to recommend [company] to a friend or colleague?" (0-10 scale)

**Calculation**: NPS = % Promoters (9-10) - % Detractors (0-6)

**Score Range**: -100 to +100

**Interpretation**:
- Above 0: More promoters than detractors
- Above 20: Favorable
- Above 50: Excellent
- Above 80: World-class

**Driver Analysis**: Correlate open-text responses with scores to identify what drives promotion and detraction.

### Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

**Question**: "How satisfied were you with [interaction]?" (1-5 or 1-7 scale)

**Calculation**: CSAT = (Satisfied responses / Total responses) × 100

**Best Practice**: Define "satisfied" as top-2 box on a 5-point scale

### Customer Effort Score (CES)

**Question**: "To what extent do you agree: [Company] made it easy for me to handle my issue" (1-7 scale)

**Calculation**: CES = Average score (lower is better if using effort framing)

**Why Effort Matters**: CES is the strongest predictor of future purchase behavior — 96% of high-effort customers become disloyal vs. 9% of low-effort customers.

## CX ROI Calculation

### The CX-Revenue Connection

**Revenue Impact Framework**:
1. Calculate revenue per customer segment
2. Measure NPS/CSAT by segment
3. Compare retention rates by satisfaction level
4. Calculate incremental revenue from improving satisfaction
5. Factor in word-of-mouth impact (promoters refer 2-3x more)

### ROI Formula

```
CX ROI = (Revenue gain from CX improvements - Cost of CX program) / Cost of CX program × 100
```

**Revenue Gain Components**:
- Reduced churn (retained revenue)
- Increased share of wallet (expansion revenue)
- Referral revenue (new customer acquisition from promoters)
- Reduced service costs (fewer complaints, lower handle time)

### Benchmarking CX Investment

- Leading companies invest 5-10% of revenue in CX
- Average CX program ROI: 200-500% over 3 years
- NPS leaders grow revenue 2.5x faster than laggards
