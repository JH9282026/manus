# Leasing Strategy

Optimize lease negotiations, tenant mix, and retention programs.

---

## Tenant Mix Strategy

### Optimal Tenant Composition

Design tenant mix to maximize:
- **Rent stability** — Balance credit tenants (anchor) with higher-rent growth tenants
- **Synergy** — Tenants that drive traffic and business for each other
- **Diversification** — No single tenant exceeds 15-20% of revenue
- **Lease expiration distribution** — Spread expirations to avoid clustering

### Tenant Mix by Property Type

**Office:**
- Anchor tenant (30-50% of building) — credit, long-term stability
- Mid-size tenants (20-30%) — growth companies, moderate credit
- Small/flex tenants (20-30%) — higher rent PSF, shorter terms

**Retail:**
- Anchor (department store, grocer) — drives traffic
- Mini-anchors (fitness, entertainment) — supplemental traffic
- Inline tenants — specialty retail, F&B, services
- Pad sites — outparcel drive-through, bank, fuel

## Lease Negotiation Strategy

### Landlord Negotiation Priorities

| Priority | Landlord Objective | Negotiation Tactic |
|---|---|---|
| Term length | Longer term = more predictable income | Offer concessions for 7-10 year terms |
| Rent escalations | Predictable growth above inflation | Fixed 3% annual increases |
| Expense recovery | Full pass-through of operating expenses | NNN structure, base year stops |
| Tenant credit | Minimize default risk | Personal guarantees, larger security deposits |
| Flexibility control | Limit tenant options | Narrow assignment/subletting rights |

### Tenant Retention Programs

Reduce turnover through proactive retention:
1. **Early renewal outreach** — Contact 18-24 months before expiration
2. **Tenant satisfaction surveys** — Annual feedback with action plans
3. **Property improvements** — Invest in common areas, amenities, technology
4. **Flexible solutions** — Right-sizing, expansion options, lease modifications
5. **Relationship management** — Regular property manager check-ins, tenant events

## Market Rent Analysis

### Comparable Rent Analysis

Gather comparable lease data:
- Recent deals in competitive set (same submarket, class, size range)
- Adjust for differences: floor level, views, TI, free rent, term
- Calculate effective rent after concession adjustment
- Identify rent trends (asking vs. effective, YoY change)

### Concession Benchmarking

| Market Condition | TI Allowance | Free Rent | Commission |
|---|---|---|---|
| Landlord's market (<5% vacancy) | Low ($20-40 PSF) | 0-1 month | 4-5% |
| Balanced market (5-10% vacancy) | Moderate ($40-60 PSF) | 1-3 months | 5-6% |
| Tenant's market (>10% vacancy) | High ($60-100+ PSF) | 3-6+ months | 6-7% |
