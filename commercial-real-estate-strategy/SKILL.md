## Portfolio Strategy Framework

### Portfolio Assessment Matrix

Evaluate each asset on two dimensions:
- **Market attractiveness** (high/low) — submarket growth, supply/demand dynamics, tenant demand
- **Asset competitiveness** (high/low) — physical quality, location, tenant roster, cash flow stability

| | High Market Attractiveness | Low Market Attractiveness |
|---|---|---|
| **High Asset Competitiveness** | Hold and grow — invest in value-add | Harvest — maximize cash flow, plan exit |
| **Low Asset Competitiveness** | Reposition or redevelop | Dispose — sell or redevelop |

## Lease Analysis Framework

### Key Lease Economics

| Metric | Definition | Benchmark Consideration |
|---|---|---|
| Effective rent | Net rent after concessions, TI, free rent | Compare to market effective rent |
| Tenant improvement (TI) | Landlord contribution to buildout | Varies by market and lease term |
| Free rent | Rent abatement period | Typically 1 month per year of term |
| Rent escalations | Annual rent increases | 2-3% fixed or CPI-based |
| Operating expense recovery | Tenant's share of building expenses | NNN, modified gross, full service |
| Renewal probability | Likelihood of tenant renewal | Industry avg 60-70% |

### Lease Comparison Analysis

For every lease decision, model:
1. Net present value (NPV) of lease cash flows over term
2. Effective rent per RSF after concessions
3. Tenant credit quality and financial strength
4. Lease flexibility (options, termination rights)
5. Space fungibility (ability to re-lease if vacated)
