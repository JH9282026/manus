## Typography and Readability



### Font Selection for Accessibility

**Recommended Font Families**:

*Sans-Serif Fonts* (generally preferred for presentations):
- Arial
- Calibri
- Verdana
- Tahoma
- Helvetica
- Open Sans
- Roboto

*Serif Fonts* (acceptable for headers or limited use):
- Georgia
- Times New Roman
- Palatino
- Cambria

**Fonts to Avoid**:
- Decorative or script fonts
- Ultra-thin or light weights
- Highly stylized fonts
- Fonts with unusual letterforms
- Condensed fonts for body text

**Font Characteristics for Accessibility**:
- Clear distinction between similar characters (I, l, 1; O, 0)
- Adequate letter spacing
- Consistent stroke width
- Open counters (interior spaces of letters)
- Distinct ascenders and descenders



### Line Spacing and Paragraph Spacing

**Line Height (Leading)**:
- Minimum 1.5 times the font size for body text
- Single spacing (1.0) only for titles and headers
- WCAG AAA recommends line spacing of at least 1.5

**Paragraph Spacing**:
- Space between paragraphs at least 1.5 times line spacing
- Use consistent spacing throughout the presentation
- Avoid cramped text blocks

**Letter Spacing (Tracking)**:
- Avoid condensed or compressed spacing
- Slightly increased letter spacing improves readability
- Maintain consistent spacing throughout



### Text Alignment

**Left Alignment** (Recommended):
- Creates consistent starting point for reading
- Easiest to read for most users
- Preferred for body text

**Center Alignment**:
- Acceptable for titles and short text
- Avoid for paragraphs or longer content
- Creates uneven edges that impede reading

**Right Alignment**:
- Use sparingly for specific design purposes
- Difficult for left-to-right readers
- Acceptable for small labels or captions

**Justified Alignment** (Avoid):
- Creates uneven spacing between words
- Difficult for users with dyslexia
- Can create "rivers" of white space
