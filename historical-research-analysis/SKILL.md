# Historical Research & Analysis

Systematically examine past events, trends, and patterns to extract insights that inform contemporary decisions.

## Overview

This skill applies historical methodology — archival research, primary source analysis, chronological reconstruction, and pattern identification — to business, policy, and strategic contexts.

| Regulatory decisions | Precedent Research | Decision patterns, prediction | `/references/historical-methods.md` |

## Process
1. Define scope: subject, temporal boundaries, geographic scope
2. Identify sources: primary (archives, records) and secondary (scholarship)
3. Collect and evaluate: provenance, reliability, corroboration
4. Analyze: chronological, thematic, comparative, or counterfactual
5. Synthesize: patterns, turning points, causal mechanisms
6. Connect to present: analogies with similarity/difference assessment

## Safeguards
- Verify through multiple source corroboration
- Avoid hindsight bias — analyze with information available at the time
- Acknowledge survivorship bias in available sources
- Distinguish findings from interpretations
- Note gaps and contested interpretations
