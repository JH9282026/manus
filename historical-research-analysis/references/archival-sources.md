# Archival Research & Sources

## Primary Source Types
- Corporate: board minutes, reports, memos, filings
- Government: legislation, regulatory decisions, court records
- Newspapers: contemporary accounts, editorials
- Correspondence: letters, emails documenting decisions
- Visual: photographs, maps, designs
- Oral histories: first-person participant accounts

## Source Evaluation
- Provenance: who, when, what context?
- Reliability: credible witness? Biases?
- Corroboration: confirmed by other sources?
- Representativeness: typical or outlier?
- Completeness: what might be missing?

## Oral History Collection
- Identify participants with firsthand knowledge
- Open-ended interview guides; record with permission
- Triangulate against documentary evidence
- Note memory limitations for distant events
