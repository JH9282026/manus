# Nonprofit Governance & Operations

## Board of Directors

### Board Composition
- 7-15 members (optimal range)
- Diverse expertise: finance, legal, marketing, sector expertise
- Term limits: 2-3 year terms, 2 consecutive terms max
- Annual self-assessment
- Conflict of interest policy required

### Board Roles
| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Chair | Lead board meetings, set agenda, partner with ED |
| Vice Chair | Support chair, lead in absence |
| Treasurer | Financial oversight, audit committee |
| Secretary | Meeting minutes, official records |
| Committee chairs | Lead standing committees |

### Board Committees
| Committee | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Executive | Urgent decisions between full board meetings |
| Finance | Budget review, audit oversight |
| Governance/Nominating | Board recruitment, bylaws |
| Fundraising/Development | Revenue strategy, donor cultivation |
| Programs | Program oversight, impact evaluation |

### Board Meeting Best Practices
- Meet 4-6 times per year minimum
- Distribute materials 7+ days in advance
- Consent agenda for routine items
- Focus on strategy, not operations
- Executive session (without staff) annually
- Detailed minutes with action items

## Volunteer Management

### Volunteer Lifecycle
1. **Recruit** — Clear role descriptions, multiple channels
2. **Screen** — Application, interview, background check
3. **Onboard** — Orientation, training, policies
4. **Manage** — Supervision, communication, support
5. **Recognize** — Appreciation, awards, references
6. **Retain** — Growth opportunities, community building

### Volunteer Role Design
| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Title | Specific, meaningful role name |
| Description | Clear tasks and expectations |
| Time commitment | Hours per week/month, duration |
| Skills needed | Required and preferred qualifications |
| Training | What training is provided |
| Supervision | Who they report to |
| Impact | How their work helps the mission |

## Regulatory Compliance

### Tax-Exempt Status Maintenance (501(c)(3))
- Annual Form 990 filing (public document)
- No private inurement or excessive compensation
- Limited lobbying (substantial part test or 501(h) election)
- No political campaign activity
- State registration for charitable solicitation
- Maintain corporate governance documents

### Financial Controls
- Separation of duties (authorize, record, custody)
- Annual independent audit (required above $750K federal funding)
- Board-approved budget
- Monthly financial statements to board
- Written financial policies
- Investment policy for reserves

### Employment Law
- Fair Labor Standards Act compliance
- Volunteer vs. employee distinction (DOL guidelines)
- Workers' compensation insurance
- Anti-discrimination policies
- Background checks for vulnerable population roles
