# Account Selection and Targeting

Master the process of identifying, scoring, and prioritizing high-value accounts for ABM programs using ideal customer profiles, intent data, and multi-dimensional account scoring.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Development

The ICP is the foundation of effective account selection, describing the characteristics of companies that derive maximum value from your solution and provide maximum value to your business.

### Building Your ICP Framework

**Step 1: Analyze Your Best Customers**

Identify your top 20-30 customers based on:
- **Revenue:** Highest contract values and total customer lifetime value
- **Profitability:** Lowest cost to acquire and serve
- **Growth:** Fastest expansion and upsell trajectory
- **Retention:** Longest tenure and lowest churn risk
- **Advocacy:** Most active references, case studies, and referrals
- **Strategic Value:** Market influence, competitive wins, innovation partnerships

**Step 2: Identify Common Attributes**

Analyze patterns across firmographic, technographic, and behavioral dimensions:

### Firmographic Attributes

Factual business characteristics that define account fit:

**Industry and Vertical**
- Primary industry classification (e.g., Financial Services, Healthcare, Manufacturing)
- Sub-vertical specialization (e.g., Community Banks, Medical Devices, Industrial Automation)
- Industry maturity and growth trajectory
- Regulatory environment and compliance requirements

**Company Size**
- **Employee Count:** Specific ranges (e.g., 500-2,000, 2,000-10,000, 10,000+)
- **Revenue Range:** Annual revenue bands (e.g., $50M-$250M, $250M-$1B, $1B+)
- **Growth Stage:** Startup, scale-up, mature, enterprise
- **Market Position:** Market leader, challenger, niche player

**Geographic Location**
- **Headquarters Location:** Country, region, city
- **Operating Regions:** Global, multi-regional, single-country
- **Market Presence:** Number of offices, geographic distribution
- **Regulatory Jurisdiction:** Relevant for compliance-sensitive solutions

**Business Model**
- **Revenue Model:** B2B, B2C, B2B2C, marketplace, platform
- **Sales Model:** Direct sales, channel/partner, e-commerce, hybrid
- **Customer Type:** Enterprise, mid-market, SMB, consumer
- **Pricing Model:** Subscription, transaction, license, usage-based

**Financial Health**
- **Funding Status:** Bootstrapped, venture-backed, private equity, public
- **Recent Funding:** Rounds raised, amounts, investors
- **Profitability:** Revenue growth, burn rate, path to profitability
- **Financial Stability:** Credit rating, financial disclosures

### Technographic Attributes

Technology infrastructure and digital maturity:

**Technology Stack**
- **Core Systems:** CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics)
- **Marketing Automation:** Marketo, Eloqua, Pardot, HubSpot
- **Data Infrastructure:** Cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP), data warehouses
- **Business Applications:** ERP, HRIS, finance, operations systems
- **Collaboration Tools:** Slack, Microsoft Teams, Google Workspace

**Technology Maturity**
- **Digital Transformation Stage:** Early, mid-journey, advanced
- **Cloud Adoption:** On-premise, hybrid, cloud-native
- **Data Sophistication:** Basic reporting, analytics, AI/ML capabilities
- **Integration Complexity:** Point solutions, integrated stack, enterprise architecture

**Technology Spend and Priorities**
- **IT Budget:** Technology investment levels
- **Strategic Initiatives:** Digital transformation, cloud migration, data modernization
- **Technology Partnerships:** Preferred vendors, strategic alliances
- **Innovation Appetite:** Early adopter, fast follower, conservative

**Compatibility and Integration**
- **Existing Tool Compatibility:** How well your solution integrates with their stack
- **API and Integration Requirements:** Technical integration complexity
- **Data Migration Needs:** Effort required to onboard
- **Security and Compliance:** Certifications and standards required

### Behavioral Attributes

How companies interact with your brand and market:

**Engagement History**
- **Website Activity:** Pages visited, time on site, return visits, content consumed
- **Content Consumption:** Whitepapers downloaded, webinars attended, videos watched
- **Event Participation:** Conference attendance, booth visits, session engagement
- **Social Media Activity:** Follows, shares, comments, engagement with thought leadership
- **Email Engagement:** Open rates, click-through rates, response patterns

**Buying Signals**
- **Intent Data:** Topic research, competitive analysis, solution exploration
- **Product Interest:** Demo requests, trial signups, pricing page visits
- **Sales Interactions:** Meetings held, proposals received, RFP participation
- **Buying Committee Activity:** Multiple stakeholders engaging simultaneously

**Organizational Dynamics**
- **Hiring Patterns:** Job postings for relevant roles (signals growth or new initiatives)
- **Leadership Changes:** New executives (often trigger technology evaluations)
- **Organizational Structure:** Centralized vs. decentralized decision-making
- **Change Readiness:** History of adopting new technologies and processes

**Market Activity**
- **Funding Events:** Recent capital raises (signals budget availability)
- **Mergers & Acquisitions:** M&A activity (triggers integration needs)
- **Expansion:** New markets, products, or offices (signals growth)
- **Regulatory Changes:** New compliance requirements (creates urgency)
- **Competitive Pressure:** Market disruption or competitive threats

### Needs and Pain Points

Business challenges your solution addresses:

**Primary Pain Points**
- Specific problems your best customers had before buying
- Urgency and severity of these problems
- Business impact of not solving (revenue loss, cost increase, risk exposure)
- Failed attempts to solve with other approaches

**Business Objectives**
- Strategic goals your solution helps achieve
- KPIs and metrics customers aim to improve
- Competitive advantages gained
- Transformation outcomes realized

**Use Cases and Applications**
- How different customer segments use your solution
- Primary vs. secondary use cases
- Feature adoption patterns
- Value realization timelines

### Buying Process Characteristics

**Decision-Making Structure**
- **Buying Committee Size:** Number of stakeholders involved
- **Decision-Maker Roles:** Titles and functions of key decision-makers
- **Approval Hierarchy:** Levels of approval required
- **Procurement Involvement:** Formal procurement process or direct purchase

**Evaluation Criteria**
- **Technical Requirements:** Must-have features and capabilities
- **Financial Criteria:** Budget ranges, ROI expectations, payback period
- **Vendor Requirements:** Company size, market presence, financial stability
- **Compliance Needs:** Security, privacy, regulatory certifications

**Sales Cycle Characteristics**
- **Typical Timeline:** Average time from first contact to close
- **Evaluation Process:** RFP, POC, pilot, or direct purchase
- **Budget Cycles:** Fiscal year timing, budget approval processes
- **Competitive Dynamics:** Typical competitors in deals, win/loss patterns

## Account Scoring Methodology

Develop a multi-dimensional scoring model to prioritize accounts:

### Three-Pillar Scoring Framework

**Pillar 1: Fit Score (40% weight)**
Measures how well an account matches your ICP:

**Firmographic Fit (20 points)**
- Industry match: 5 points
- Company size: 5 points
- Revenue range: 5 points
- Geographic location: 5 points

**Technographic Fit (10 points)**
- Technology stack compatibility: 5 points
- Digital maturity: 5 points

**Strategic Fit (10 points)**
- Market influence: 3 points
- Growth trajectory: 4 points
- Competitive significance: 3 points

**Pillar 2: Intent Score (35% weight)**
Measures buying signals and propensity to purchase:

**First-Party Intent (15 points)**
- Website visits: 5 points
- Content downloads: 5 points
- Product interest (demos, trials): 5 points

**Third-Party Intent (10 points)**
- Topic surge activity: 5 points
- Competitive research: 5 points

**Trigger Events (10 points)**
- Funding/M&A: 4 points
- Leadership changes: 3 points
- Hiring patterns: 3 points

**Pillar 3: Engagement Score (25% weight)**
Measures relationship strength and account activity:

**Marketing Engagement (10 points)**
- Email engagement: 3 points
- Event participation: 4 points
- Social media interaction: 3 points

**Sales Engagement (10 points)**
- Meetings held: 5 points
- Stakeholder relationships: 5 points

**Buying Committee Coverage (5 points)**
- Percentage of decision-makers engaged: 5 points

### Scoring Implementation

**Total Score Calculation:**
- **Fit Score (40%)** + **Intent Score (35%)** + **Engagement Score (25%)** = **Total Score (100 points)**

**Account Prioritization Tiers:**
- **80-100 points:** Tier 1 (Strategic ABM) — High fit, high intent, high engagement
- **60-79 points:** Tier 2 (ABM Lite) — High fit, moderate intent/engagement
- **40-59 points:** Tier 3 (Programmatic ABM) — High fit, low intent/engagement
- **Below 40 points:** Not ABM-worthy — Focus on demand generation or disqualify

**Dynamic Scoring:**
- Recalculate scores monthly or quarterly
- Accounts can move between tiers based on score changes
- Intent and engagement scores decay over time without activity
- Fit score remains relatively stable unless account characteristics change

## Data Sources for Account Selection

### First-Party Data

Information from your own systems:

**CRM Data**
- Existing customer accounts (for expansion ABM)
- Past opportunities and closed-lost deals
- Sales interactions and relationship history
- Account ownership and territory assignments

**Marketing Automation Data**
- Website visitor identification and activity
- Email engagement and campaign responses
- Form submissions and content downloads
- Webinar registrations and attendance

**Product Usage Data**
- Trial signups and product engagement
- Feature adoption and usage patterns
- User satisfaction and NPS scores
- Support interactions and ticket history

### Third-Party Data

External data sources to enrich account intelligence:

**Intent Data Providers**
- **Bombora:** Topic-based intent signals from content consumption
- **6sense:** Predictive analytics and buying stage identification
- **ZoomInfo:** Intent data combined with contact and company information
- **TechTarget:** Technology-specific intent from Priority Engine

**Company and Contact Data**
- **ZoomInfo:** Comprehensive B2B database with firmographics and contacts
- **Clearbit:** Real-time company enrichment and technographics
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Account insights and stakeholder identification
- **Crunchbase:** Funding data, company news, and growth signals

**Technographic Data**
- **BuiltWith:** Technology stack detection and tracking
- **Datanyze:** Technographic intelligence and install base data
- **HG Insights:** Technology usage and spend analytics
- **Clearbit:** Technology detection and digital maturity signals

**News and Trigger Events**
- **Owler:** Company news, competitive intelligence, and alerts
- **Crunchbase:** Funding announcements and M&A activity
- **Google Alerts:** Custom news monitoring for target accounts
- **LinkedIn:** Leadership changes, company updates, hiring activity

## Account List Building Process

### Step-by-Step Account Selection

**Step 1: Define ICP Criteria (Week 1)**
- Analyze best customers to identify common attributes
- Document firmographic, technographic, and behavioral criteria
- Establish minimum requirements and ideal characteristics
- Validate ICP with sales leadership and account executives

**Step 2: Generate Initial Account Universe (Week 2)**
- Use data providers to identify accounts matching ICP criteria
- Pull existing accounts from CRM that fit ICP
- Gather account suggestions from sales team
- Compile comprehensive list (typically 500-2,000 accounts)

**Step 3: Enrich and Score Accounts (Week 3)**
- Enrich accounts with firmographic, technographic, and intent data
- Calculate fit, intent, and engagement scores
- Rank accounts by total score
- Identify data gaps and conduct additional research

**Step 4: Sales Validation (Week 4)**
- Share top-scoring accounts with sales team
- Gather input on account accessibility and strategic value
- Validate buying committee contacts and relationships
- Adjust scores based on sales insights

**Step 5: Tier Assignment and Finalization (Week 5)**
- Assign accounts to tiers based on final scores and resource capacity
- Develop account plans for Tier 1 accounts
- Design campaign strategies for Tier 2/3 clusters
- Obtain final approval from sales and marketing leadership

**Step 6: Ongoing Monitoring and Refresh (Continuous)**
- Monitor account scores monthly
- Add new accounts as they meet ICP criteria
- Remove or downgrade underperforming accounts
- Quarterly comprehensive account list review

## Account Segmentation Strategies

### Segmentation Approaches

**Industry/Vertical Segmentation**
- Group accounts by industry for tailored messaging
- Develop vertical-specific content and campaigns
- Assign industry specialists or subject matter experts
- Best for: Companies with clear vertical focus or industry-specific solutions

**Use Case Segmentation**
- Cluster accounts by primary use case or business challenge
- Create use case-specific value propositions and content
- Tailor product demonstrations and proof points
- Best for: Solutions with multiple distinct use cases

**Buying Stage Segmentation**
- Segment by position in buying journey (awareness, consideration, decision)
- Deliver stage-appropriate content and engagement
- Adjust messaging intensity and sales involvement
- Best for: Accounts at different stages of evaluation

**Geographic Segmentation**
- Group accounts by region, country, or territory
- Align with sales territories and field marketing
- Localize content and messaging
- Best for: Global companies with regional sales teams

**Competitive Segmentation**
- Segment by current vendor or competitive situation
- Develop competitive displacement campaigns
- Tailor messaging to address competitor weaknesses
- Best for: Competitive markets with clear alternatives

**Account Relationship Segmentation**
- **Net-New:** No prior relationship, cold outreach required
- **Engaged:** Active engagement but no opportunity
- **Pipeline:** Active sales opportunity in progress
- **Customer:** Existing customer, focus on expansion
- **Churned:** Former customer, win-back campaign

## Common Account Selection Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Confusing ICP with Buyer Persona**
- **Problem:** ICP describes the company, persona describes the individual
- **Solution:** Develop both ICP (account-level) and buyer personas (stakeholder-level)

**Mistake 2: Static ICP That Never Evolves**
- **Problem:** Market conditions, product offerings, and customer needs change
- **Solution:** Review and update ICP quarterly based on win/loss analysis and market trends

**Mistake 3: Ignoring Sales Team Input**
- **Problem:** Marketing-only account selection leads to poor sales adoption
- **Solution:** Collaborative account selection with sales validation and commitment

**Mistake 4: Focusing Only on Fit, Ignoring Intent**
- **Problem:** Perfect-fit accounts with no buying signals waste resources
- **Solution:** Balance fit and intent in scoring model, prioritize accounts showing both

**Mistake 5: Selecting Too Many Accounts**
- **Problem:** Spreading resources too thin reduces personalization and impact
- **Solution:** Start small (10-25 Tier 1 accounts), scale based on results and capacity

**Mistake 6: Lack of Quantitative Validation**
- **Problem:** Relying on assumptions rather than data
- **Solution:** Analyze actual customer data (deal size, LTV, churn) to validate ICP

**Mistake 7: Copying Competitor ICPs**
- **Problem:** Your strengths and market position are unique
- **Solution:** Build ICP based on your actual best customers and differentiation

**Mistake 8: Thinking in Leads Instead of Accounts**
- **Problem:** Missing the full buying committee by focusing on individual contacts
- **Solution:** Account-based mindset, map all stakeholders within target accounts
