# ESG Frameworks and Sustainability Reporting

## Sustainability Report Structure (GRI Standards)

### Universal Standards (Required)
- GRI 1: Foundation (principles and requirements)
- GRI 2: General Disclosures (organization, governance, strategy)
- GRI 3: Material Topics (materiality assessment process)

### Topic Standards (Based on Material Topics)
- Environmental: Emissions (305), Energy (302), Water (303), Waste (306), Biodiversity (304)
- Social: Employment (401), Health & Safety (403), Training (404), Diversity (405), Human Rights (412)
- Governance: Anti-corruption (205), Tax (207), Public policy (415)

### Materiality Assessment Process
1. Identify potential ESG topics relevant to industry
2. Assess significance of impacts (environmental, social, economic)
3. Engage stakeholders (investors, employees, communities, customers)
4. Prioritize topics using materiality matrix
5. Validate with leadership and board
6. Disclose process and results in sustainability report

## TCFD Climate Risk Disclosure

### Four Pillars

| Pillar | Disclosure Requirements |
|---|---|
| Governance | Board oversight of climate risks, management role |
| Strategy | Climate risks/opportunities, scenario analysis results |
| Risk Management | Process for identifying, assessing, managing climate risks |
| Metrics & Targets | GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3), targets, progress |

### Climate Scenario Analysis
- Use at least two scenarios (e.g., 1.5°C and 3°C warming)
- Assess financial impact on revenues, costs, assets, capital
- Consider both transition risks and physical risks
- Model over short (1-5yr), medium (5-15yr), and long (15-30yr) terms
- Reference IEA, NGFS, or IPCC scenarios

## ESG Rating Improvement

### Major ESG Rating Agencies

| Agency | Focus | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| MSCI | Risk-based | AAA to CCC |
| Sustainalytics | Risk exposure | 0-100 (lower = better) |
| CDP | Climate disclosure | A to D- |
| ISS ESG | Performance | A+ to D- |
| S&P Global CSA | Comprehensive | 0-100 |

### Improvement Strategy
1. Understand methodology of target rating agencies
2. Conduct gap analysis against peer leaders
3. Improve disclosure completeness (often biggest quick win)
4. Implement policies for identified gaps
5. Engage proactively with rating agencies
6. Track and report progress on key metrics

## Science-Based Targets Initiative (SBTi)

### Target Setting Process
1. Commit publicly to set SBT (letter of commitment)
2. Develop target aligned with SBTi criteria
3. Submit target for validation (6-month review)
4. Communicate and track progress annually

### Target Types
- **Absolute target**: Reduce total emissions by X% by year Y
- **Intensity target**: Reduce emissions per unit of output
- **1.5°C aligned**: ~4.2% absolute reduction per year
- **Well-below 2°C**: ~2.5% absolute reduction per year
- **Net-zero**: Long-term target for value chain neutrality
