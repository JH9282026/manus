# Delivery Techniques

Vocal techniques, body language, stage presence, and anxiety management for confident, engaging presentation delivery.

---

## Vocal Techniques

### Pace and Rhythm

**Optimal speaking pace:**
- 140-160 words per minute for clarity
- Slower (120-130 wpm) for complex or emotional content
- Faster (160-180 wpm) for energy and excitement
- Vary pace to maintain interest

**Strategic pausing:**
- Pause after key points (3-5 seconds)
- Pause before important statements for anticipation
- Pause to allow audience processing time
- Replace filler words with pauses

**Rhythm and cadence:**
- Vary sentence length (short for impact, longer for explanation)
- Use repetition for emphasis
- Create musicality through variation
- Avoid monotonous patterns

### Volume and Projection

**Volume modulation:**
- Speak louder for emphasis and energy
- Speak softer for intimacy or dramatic effect
- Project to back of room without shouting
- Test acoustics before presentation

**Projection techniques:**
- Breathe from diaphragm, not chest
- Open throat and mouth for resonance
- Articulate consonants clearly
- Imagine speaking to person in back row

### Pitch and Tone

**Pitch variation:**
- Avoid monotone delivery
- Rise in pitch for questions or excitement
- Lower pitch for authority and seriousness
- Natural variation shows enthusiasm

**Tone alignment:**
- Match tone to content (serious, playful, urgent)
- Authentic emotion resonates with audience
- Avoid sarcasm unless intentional and clear
- Smile when appropriate (audience can hear it)

### Articulation and Pronunciation

**Clear articulation:**
- Pronounce all syllables
- Don't drop word endings
- Slow down for technical terms
- Practice difficult words in advance

**Common pitfalls:**
- Mumbling or trailing off at sentence end
- Running words together
- Mispronouncing names or terms
- Regional accent considerations (clarity over neutrality)

## Eliminating Filler Words

### Common Fillers

- "Um," "uh," "er"
- "Like," "you know," "so"
- "Actually," "basically," "literally"
- "I mean," "kind of," "sort of"

### Elimination Strategies

**Awareness:**
- Record yourself to identify patterns
- Ask colleague to count fillers during practice
- Note specific triggers (transitions, thinking time)

**Replacement:**
- Replace fillers with silence (pause)
- Breathe instead of filling
- Embrace brief silence as confidence
- Plan transitions in advance

**Practice techniques:**
- Slow down overall pace
- Prepare thoroughly to reduce uncertainty
- Practice with "filler jar" (penalty for each use)
- Focus on one filler at a time for elimination

## Body Language and Stage Presence

### Posture and Stance

**Power posture:**
- Stand tall with shoulders back
- Weight evenly distributed on both feet
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Avoid swaying or shifting weight

**Open vs. closed body language:**
- **Open:** arms uncrossed, palms visible, facing audience
- **Closed:** crossed arms, hands in pockets, turned away
- Open posture conveys confidence and approachability
- Avoid defensive or protective positions

### Gestures

**Purposeful gestures:**
- Use gestures to emphasize points
- Keep gestures above waist, below shoulders
- Larger gestures for larger audiences
- Match gesture size to content importance

**Gesture vocabulary:**
- **Counting:** fingers for numbered points
- **Size/scale:** hands apart to show magnitude
- **Direction:** pointing to indicate movement or change
- **Emphasis:** hand chop or fist for strong points

**Avoid:**
- Repetitive gestures (becomes distracting)
- Hands in pockets or behind back
- Fidgeting with objects (pen, notes, clicker)
- Crossing arms or defensive positions

### Eye Contact

**Eye contact patterns:**
- **Small groups (< 20):** make eye contact with each person
- **Medium groups (20-100):** scan sections, hold gaze 3-5 seconds
- **Large audiences (100+):** focus on individuals in different sections
- Avoid looking at slides, notes, or ceiling

**Building connection:**
- Start with friendly faces for confidence
- Return to engaged listeners for energy
- Include all sections of room
- Hold eye contact through complete thought

### Facial Expressions

**Authentic expression:**
- Smile when appropriate (genuine, not forced)
- Show emotion aligned with content
- Avoid blank or stern expression
- Let face reflect enthusiasm for topic

**Awareness:**
- Record yourself to see default expression
- Practice in mirror
- Ask for feedback on approachability
- Relax facial muscles to avoid tension

### Movement and Staging

**Strategic movement:**
- Move with purpose, not pacing
- Step forward for emphasis or intimacy
- Step back for audience reflection time
- Use stage geography (different areas for different topics)

**Avoid:**
- Pacing back and forth
- Swaying or rocking
- Hiding behind podium
- Turning back to audience to read slides

## Managing Presentation Anxiety

### Understanding Anxiety

**Physical symptoms:**
- Increased heart rate
- Sweating
- Shaking hands or voice
- Dry mouth
- Nausea or butterflies

**Cognitive symptoms:**
- Racing thoughts
- Fear of forgetting
- Catastrophic thinking
- Self-doubt

**Reframing anxiety:**
- Anxiety and excitement are physiologically identical
- Reframe as "I'm excited" instead of "I'm nervous"
- Adrenaline enhances performance when channeled
- Even experienced speakers feel nervous

### Pre-Presentation Preparation

**Thorough preparation:**
- Practice multiple times (minimum 3 full run-throughs)
- Rehearse in similar environment when possible
- Anticipate questions and prepare responses
- Know material deeply (reduces uncertainty)

**Pre-presentation routine:**
- Arrive early to familiarize with space
- Test technology and equipment
- Do physical warm-up (stretching, movement)
- Vocal warm-up (humming, tongue twisters)
- Review opening and closing (most important parts)

**Power posing:**
- 2 minutes in expansive posture before presenting
- Stand tall, arms raised or hands on hips
- Increases testosterone, decreases cortisol
- Boosts confidence and reduces stress

### Breathing Techniques

**4-7-8 breathing:**
1. Inhale through nose for 4 counts
2. Hold breath for 7 counts
3. Exhale through mouth for 8 counts
4. Repeat 3-4 times

**Box breathing:**
1. Inhale for 4 counts
2. Hold for 4 counts
3. Exhale for 4 counts
4. Hold for 4 counts
5. Repeat as needed

**Diaphragmatic breathing:**
- Breathe deeply into belly, not chest
- Slows heart rate and calms nervous system
- Practice regularly, not just before presentations

### Visualization and Mental Rehearsal

**Positive visualization:**
- Imagine successful presentation in detail
- See audience engaged and responsive
- Feel confidence and enjoyment
- Visualize handling challenges smoothly

**Mental rehearsal:**
- Walk through presentation mentally
- Visualize transitions and key moments
- Practice recovery from potential mistakes
- Build neural pathways for success

### In-the-Moment Techniques

**Grounding:**
- Feel feet on floor
- Notice physical sensations
- Focus on present moment
- Avoid future catastrophizing

**Audience focus:**
- Shift attention from self to audience
- Focus on serving and helping them
- Remember: they want you to succeed
- See friendly faces for reassurance

**Emergency recovery:**
- Pause and breathe if you lose place
- "Let me return to that point..."
- Have water available for dry mouth
- Acknowledge mistake and move on (audience is forgiving)

### Progressive Exposure

**Build confidence gradually:**
1. Start with small, low-stakes presentations
2. Gradually increase audience size
3. Increase presentation length
4. Increase stakes and importance
5. Celebrate each success

**Seek opportunities:**
- Volunteer for team updates
- Join Toastmasters or speaking groups
- Present at local meetups
- Build experience systematically

## Confidence-Building Strategies

### Preparation as Confidence

**Know your material:**
- Deep expertise reduces anxiety
- Prepare more than you'll present
- Anticipate questions
- Have backup content ready

**Practice deliberately:**
- Full run-throughs with timing
- Practice with feedback
- Record and review yourself
- Simulate presentation conditions

### Positive Self-Talk

**Affirmations:**
- "I am prepared and knowledgeable"
- "I have valuable insights to share"
- "The audience wants me to succeed"
- "I am confident and capable"

**Reframe negative thoughts:**
- "I'm going to fail" → "I'm prepared and ready"
- "Everyone will judge me" → "The audience is supportive"
- "I'll forget everything" → "I know this material well"

### Post-Presentation Reflection

**Constructive review:**
- Note what went well (celebrate successes)
- Identify specific areas for improvement
- Avoid harsh self-criticism
- Seek constructive feedback from trusted sources

**Build on success:**
- Keep record of positive feedback
- Review before next presentation
- Track improvement over time
- Recognize growth and progress
