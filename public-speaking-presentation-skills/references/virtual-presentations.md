# Virtual Presentations

Technical setup, engagement strategies, and best practices for effective virtual presentations, webinars, and online meetings.

---

## Technical Setup

### Camera Setup

**Camera positioning:**
- Position camera at eye level (use laptop stand or stack books)
- Center yourself in frame with slight headroom
- Maintain 12-18 inches from camera
- Ensure camera is stable (no wobbling)

**Framing:**
- Head and shoulders visible (not too close or far)
- Look directly at camera for "eye contact"
- Avoid cutting off top of head
- Leave slight space above head

**Camera quality:**
- Use external webcam for better quality (Logitech C920 or better)
- Clean camera lens before presentation
- Test camera in advance
- Have backup device ready

### Lighting

**Three-point lighting (ideal):**
- **Key light:** main light source in front, slightly to side
- **Fill light:** softer light on opposite side to reduce shadows
- **Back light:** light from behind to separate from background

**Practical lighting:**
- Face window or light source (don't backlight)
- Use desk lamp or ring light for even illumination
- Avoid overhead lighting (creates shadows)
- Test lighting at presentation time of day

**Lighting quality:**
- Soft, diffused light (not harsh direct light)
- Avoid color casts (warm or cool tones)
- Ensure face is evenly lit
- No shadows or dark spots

### Audio Quality

**Microphone options:**
- **Best:** external USB microphone (Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica)
- **Good:** headset with boom microphone
- **Acceptable:** laptop built-in (if in quiet space)
- **Avoid:** earbuds or poor-quality mics

**Audio environment:**
- Quiet space without echo
- Close doors and windows
- Silence phone and notifications
- Use "Do Not Disturb" mode
- Inform household members

**Audio settings:**
- Test audio levels in advance
- Use platform's audio test feature
- Mute when not speaking (if appropriate)
- Have backup audio device ready

### Background

**Background options:**
- **Professional:** clean, uncluttered physical background
- **Branded:** company logo or branded backdrop
- **Virtual:** platform virtual background (test in advance)
- **Blurred:** blur background to minimize distractions

**Background considerations:**
- Avoid busy or distracting backgrounds
- Ensure good lighting on background
- Test virtual backgrounds (some create artifacts)
- Consider what's visible in frame

### Internet Connection

**Connection requirements:**
- Minimum 3 Mbps upload speed for video
- Wired ethernet connection preferred over WiFi
- Close unnecessary applications and browser tabs
- Disable automatic updates during presentation
- Have mobile hotspot as backup

**Bandwidth optimization:**
- Turn off video if connection is unstable
- Reduce video quality if needed
- Ask others on network to pause streaming
- Test connection before presentation

## Platform-Specific Features

### Zoom

**Key features:**
- Screen sharing with annotation
- Breakout rooms for small group discussions
- Polling for audience engagement
- Chat for questions and interaction
- Recording for later viewing

**Best practices:**
- Enable "Gallery View" to see participants
- Use "Spotlight Video" for co-presenters
- Mute participants on entry (large groups)
- Enable waiting room for security
- Share specific window, not entire screen

### Microsoft Teams

**Key features:**
- Integration with Microsoft 365
- Live captions and transcription
- Together Mode for engagement
- Whiteboard for collaboration
- Meeting recordings

**Best practices:**
- Use "Presenter" mode for polished delivery
- Enable live captions for accessibility
- Use "Raise Hand" feature for Q&A
- Share PowerPoint directly for better control

### Google Meet

**Key features:**
- Simple, browser-based access
- Live captions
- Screen sharing
- Breakout rooms (Google Workspace)
- Recording (Google Workspace)

**Best practices:**
- Use "Present" mode for full-screen sharing
- Enable captions for accessibility
- Use Q&A feature for large meetings
- Share specific tab, not entire screen

### Webinar Platforms (Webex, GoToWebinar)

**Key features:**
- Large audience capacity
- Registration and attendee management
- Polls and surveys
- Q&A moderation
- Analytics and reporting

**Best practices:**
- Assign moderator for Q&A
- Use polls every 10-15 minutes
- Enable practice session for rehearsal
- Provide handouts via platform

## Virtual Engagement Strategies

### Energy and Presence

**Increased energy:**
- 20-30% more energy than in-person
- Exaggerate facial expressions slightly
- Use more vocal variety
- Maintain enthusiasm throughout

**Camera presence:**
- Look at camera, not screen (for eye contact)
- Smile and show warmth
- Use gestures visible in frame
- Avoid reading from notes

### Attention Maintenance

**Engagement every 3-4 minutes:**
- Ask questions (rhetorical or direct)
- Use polls or surveys
- Share relevant chat messages
- Change visual (slide, screen share, video)
- Tell brief story or example

**Combat "Zoom fatigue":**
- Keep presentations concise (max 45 min without break)
- Build in breaks for longer sessions
- Vary format (presentation, discussion, breakout)
- Encourage camera-off breaks if needed

### Interactive Elements

**Polls and surveys:**
- Use for audience opinions or knowledge checks
- Share results immediately
- Discuss surprising or interesting results
- Prepare polls in advance

**Chat interaction:**
- Encourage questions and comments in chat
- Acknowledge and respond to chat messages
- Use chat for resource sharing
- Assign moderator to monitor chat

**Q&A management:**
- Dedicate specific time for Q&A
- Use platform Q&A feature for organization
- Address questions by name
- Moderate or filter questions if needed

**Breakout rooms:**
- Small group discussions (3-5 people)
- Provide clear instructions and timing
- Visit rooms to check progress
- Debrief key insights when returning

## Screen Sharing Best Practices

### Preparation

**Before sharing:**
- Close unnecessary applications and tabs
- Disable notifications (Do Not Disturb mode)
- Clear desktop of sensitive information
- Prepare all materials in advance
- Test screen sharing before presentation

**What to share:**
- Specific application window (not entire screen)
- PowerPoint in presentation mode
- Browser tab (not entire browser)
- Whiteboard or collaboration tool

### During Screen Sharing

**Navigation:**
- Minimize switching between applications
- Use keyboard shortcuts for efficiency
- Avoid scrolling too quickly
- Pause to allow audience processing time

**Annotation:**
- Use platform annotation tools to highlight
- Draw attention to specific elements
- Use pointer or spotlight feature
- Clear annotations when moving to next topic

**Accessibility:**
- Zoom in on small text or details
- Use high-contrast colors
- Describe visual elements verbally
- Provide alternative text descriptions

## Virtual Presentation Delivery

### Opening Strong

**First 30 seconds:**
- Greet audience warmly
- Confirm audio and video working
- Set expectations (duration, interaction, Q&A)
- Start with engaging hook

**Technical check:**
- "Can everyone see my screen?"
- "Can you hear me clearly?"
- "Please use chat for questions"
- Address technical issues immediately

### Maintaining Connection

**Verbal engagement:**
- Use participants' names when possible
- Acknowledge chat comments
- Ask for reactions (thumbs up, emoji)
- Invite questions throughout

**Visual engagement:**
- Vary between camera and screen sharing
- Use video clips for variety
- Show your face periodically
- Use visually engaging slides

### Handling Technical Issues

**Common problems:**
- Audio cutting out
- Screen sharing not working
- Participants can't see or hear
- Internet connection drops

**Recovery strategies:**
- Stay calm and professional
- Have backup plan (phone dial-in, alternative platform)
- Communicate clearly about issue
- Use chat if audio fails
- Reschedule if necessary

**Prevention:**
- Test everything in advance
- Have backup device ready
- Know platform support resources
- Arrive early to troubleshoot

## Virtual-Specific Presentation Design

### Slide Design for Virtual

**Larger text:**
- Minimum 28-32 pt for body text
- Assume smaller screens
- Test on laptop screen, not large monitor
- Increase contrast for clarity

**Simplified visuals:**
- Less content per slide
- Larger images and graphics
- Avoid small details
- Use high-contrast colors

**Frequent slide changes:**
- Change slides every 30-60 seconds
- Maintain visual interest
- Use builds and animations
- Avoid static slides for long periods

### Video Integration

**Pre-recorded video:**
- Keep videos short (30-90 seconds)
- Test video playback in advance
- Share computer audio when playing
- Have backup if video fails

**Live demonstration:**
- Practice in advance
- Ensure clear visibility
- Narrate actions clearly
- Have screenshots as backup
