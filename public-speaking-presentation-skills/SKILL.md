## Presentation Development Process

### 1. Audience Analysis and Objectives

Start by defining:
- **Audience profile** — size, composition, knowledge level, expectations, decision-making authority
- **Presentation goal** — inform, persuade, inspire, entertain, or call to action
- **Key messages** — maximum 3 main points for retention
- **Success metrics** — decisions made, actions taken, feedback received

### 2. Content Structure

Use proven frameworks:
- **Three-act structure** — Setup, Confrontation, Resolution
- **Monroe's Motivated Sequence** — Attention, Need, Satisfaction, Visualization, Action
- **Rule of Three** — organize content in groups of three for memorability
- **Primacy and recency effects** — strongest content at beginning and end

### 3. Opening Hooks

Choose from:
- Surprising statistic or data point
- Personal story establishing credibility
- Provocative question
- Bold statement or contrarian view
- Relevant quote from authority

### 4. Visual Design Principles

Follow these guidelines:
- **6x6 rule** — maximum 6 bullets, 6 words each per slide
- **One idea per slide** — avoid cognitive overload
- **High-quality visuals** — images over text when possible
- **Consistent hierarchy** — strategic use of white space, color, and typography
- **Minimal animation** — only when it enhances understanding

### 5. Delivery Optimization

**Vocal techniques:**
- Pace variation (slow for emphasis, faster for energy)
- Strategic pauses for dramatic effect
- Volume modulation for impact
- Eliminate filler words ("um," "uh," "like")

**Body language:**
- Open stance and confident posture
- Purposeful gestures aligned with content
- Eye contact patterns for audience size
- Facial expressions matching message
- Strategic stage movement

### 6. Anxiety Management

Techniques for confidence:
- **Pre-presentation routine** — consistent ritual to enter performance state
- **Breathing exercises** — 4-7-8 technique, box breathing
- **Reframe anxiety as excitement** — physiological responses are identical
- **Power posing** — 2 minutes in expansive posture before presenting
- **Visualization** — mental rehearsal of successful delivery
- **Emergency recovery** — prepared phrases for mistakes or technical issues

## Context-Specific Optimization

### TED-Style Talks (15-18 minutes)

- Single powerful idea with clear framework
- Personal story establishing emotional connection
- Minimal slides (10-15 maximum) with high visual impact
- Memorized delivery with natural conversational tone
- Strategic use of silence and movement
- Powerful closing with clear call to action

### Board and Executive Presentations

- Executive summary slide with key metrics upfront
- Data visualization best practices for financial information
- Anticipate questions based on results and recommendations
- Confident language avoiding hedging ("I think," "maybe")
- Leave-behind document with detailed appendix
- Time management (typically 30 min presentation + 15 min Q&A)

### Virtual Presentations

- Enhanced energy and expressiveness for camera
- Camera positioning at eye level, proper lighting (three-point lighting)
- Engagement techniques every 3-4 minutes (polls, chat, questions)
- Screen sharing choreography
- Virtual eye contact (look at camera, not screen)
- Backup plans for technical failures

### Sales Pitches

- Problem-solution-benefit structure
- Customer success stories and social proof
- Objection handling preparation
- Clear next steps and closing techniques
- Tailored to decision-maker priorities
- Confidence without arrogance
