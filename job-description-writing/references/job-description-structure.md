# Job Description Structure

## Title Best Practices
- Industry-standard, searchable terms
- Include seniority: Junior, Senior, Lead, Director, VP
- Avoid: "Rockstar," "Ninja," "Guru"
- 2-5 words max

## Summary
- Role purpose: why this job exists
- Reporting relationship and team
- 2-3 exciting aspects
- 3-5 sentences max

Example: "As Senior Product Manager, you will lead strategy for our analytics platform serving 2M+ users. Reporting to VP Product, you own the roadmap and drive cross-functional delivery."

## Responsibilities
- Start with strong action verbs (Lead, Develop, Analyze)
- Order by importance and time allocation
- 6-10 bullets; mix strategic and tactical
- Specify scope: team size, budget, geography
- Present tense, imperative mood

## Requirements
- Separate Required from Preferred/Nice-to-Have
- Required: absolute minimum to succeed
- Preferred: enhance candidacy but not mandatory
- Focus on skills and outcomes, not credentials alone
- Specify years of experience as ranges, not minimums

## What to Avoid
- "Must have 10+ years" (screens out qualified diverse candidates)
- Excessive requirements (women apply at 100% match; men at 60%)
- Gendered language ("aggressive," "nurturing")
- Vague descriptions ("other duties as assigned" as primary content)
