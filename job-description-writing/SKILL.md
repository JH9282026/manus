# Job Description Writing

Write clear, inclusive, and high-converting job descriptions that attract top talent while ensuring legal compliance.

## Overview

Job descriptions are a company's most visible recruiting asset. A well-written JD attracts qualified candidates, sets clear expectations, reduces mis-hires, and protects the company legally. Poorly written JDs — with vague requirements, exclusionary language, or missing information — actively repel strong candidates. This skill covers structure, inclusive language, legal compliance, SEO optimization, and conversion best practices.

## Job Description Structure Template

| Section | Purpose | Length | Priority |
|---------|---------|--------|----------|
| Job title | Searchable, clear role name | 3–6 words | Critical |
| Hook/intro | Sell the opportunity and company | 2–3 sentences | High |
| About the company | Culture and mission snapshot | 3–4 sentences | Medium |
| Role summary | What the person will do day-to-day | 3–5 sentences | Critical |
| Responsibilities | Specific duties and expectations | 6–10 bullet points | Critical |
| Requirements (must-have) | Non-negotiable qualifications | 4–6 bullet points | Critical |
| Nice-to-have | Preferred but not required skills | 3–5 bullet points | Medium |
| Compensation & benefits | Salary range, perks, equity | Detailed list | High |
| Application process | How to apply, timeline, next steps | 2–3 sentences | Medium |
| EEO statement | Legal compliance statement | Standard paragraph | Required |

## Job Title Best Practices

| Do | Don't | Why |
|----|-------|-----|
| Senior Software Engineer | Code Ninja / Rockstar | Candidates search standard titles |
| Marketing Manager | Marketing Guru | Non-standard titles reduce search visibility |
| Customer Support Specialist | Customer Happiness Hero | Unclear seniority and function |
| Data Analyst II | Junior Data Person | Include level for clarity |
| Account Executive, Enterprise | Sales Rep | Specify segment or focus area |

### SEO Title Optimization
- Use the title candidates actually search (check job board autocomplete)
- Include seniority level (Junior, Mid, Senior, Lead, Staff, Principal)
- Add specialty if relevant (Frontend, Backend, Full Stack, iOS)
- Keep under 60 characters for full display in search results
- Avoid internal jargon or abbreviations unknown outside your company

## Requirements Writing Framework

### Must-Have vs. Nice-to-Have Decision Guide

| Criterion | Must-Have | Nice-to-Have |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| Can they learn it in 90 days? | No → Must-have | Yes → Nice-to-have |
| Does the job literally require it on day 1? | Yes → Must-have | No → Nice-to-have |
| Is it a legal/certification requirement? | Yes → Must-have | N/A |
| Does research show diverse candidates self-select out? | Move to Nice-to-have | Keep or remove |
| Years of experience | Avoid exact numbers | Use "experience with X" instead |

### Inclusive Requirements Language

| Exclusionary | Inclusive Alternative | Impact |
|-------------|----------------------|--------|
| "10+ years of experience required" | "Extensive experience with enterprise systems" | Experience ≠ years; removes age bias |
| "Must be a native English speaker" | "Strong written and verbal communication skills" | Removes national origin bias |
| "Culture fit" | "Values alignment with [specific values]" | "Culture fit" enables homogeneity bias |
| "Young, dynamic team" | "Collaborative, fast-paced team" | Removes age discrimination signal |
| "He/she will..." | "You will..." or "The candidate will..." | Gender-neutral language |
| "Digital native" | "Proficient with modern digital tools" | Removes generational bias |

## Compensation & Benefits Section

### Pay Transparency Requirements (US 2026)

| Jurisdiction | Requirement | Penalty |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Colorado | Salary range required in all postings | $500–$10K per violation |
| California | Salary range required, pay data reporting | $100–$10K per violation |
| New York State | Salary range required | $1K–$3K per violation |
| Washington | Salary range + benefits description required | $500+ per violation |
| Connecticut | Salary range on request or in posting | Varies |
| Federal (proposed) | Monitoring — check current status | — |

### Benefits Listing Priority
1. **Compensation:** Salary range, bonus structure, equity/stock options
2. **Health:** Medical, dental, vision, mental health support
3. **Time off:** PTO days, holidays, parental leave, sabbatical
4. **Flexibility:** Remote/hybrid policy, flexible hours, compressed workweek
5. **Growth:** Learning budget, conference attendance, tuition reimbursement
6. **Financial:** 401(k) match, ESPP, financial wellness programs
7. **Unique perks:** Relevant differentiators (not generic)

## Legal Compliance Checklist

| Requirement | Regulation | Action |
|------------|------------|--------|
| No discriminatory language | Title VII, ADA, ADEA | Review for protected class references |
| Essential functions identified | ADA | Distinguish essential vs. marginal functions |
| Physical requirements stated | ADA | Only if genuinely required; state with/without accommodation |
| Pay transparency | State laws (CA, CO, NY, WA) | Include salary range |
| EEO statement | Federal contractors (OFCCP) | Include standard EEO language |
| FLSA classification | Fair Labor Standards Act | Clearly state exempt vs. non-exempt |
| Background check notice | FCRA, state laws | Disclose if background check is required |
| Work authorization | Immigration law | State sponsorship availability |

## Conversion Optimization

### JD Performance Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark | Excellent | Action If Below |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| View-to-apply rate | 8–12% | >15% | Simplify requirements, improve hook |
| Application completion rate | 40–60% | >70% | Shorten application form |
| Qualified applicant rate | 30–50% | >60% | Clarify requirements, add screening Qs |
| Time to fill | 30–45 days | <25 days | Improve title SEO, expand distribution |
| Diversity of applicant pool | Track demographics | Improving trend | Audit language for bias |

### High-Impact Writing Techniques
- **Lead with impact, not tasks** — "You'll build the recommendation engine powering 10M daily searches" > "Maintain codebase"
- **Use "you" language** — second person is 25% more engaging than third person
- **Show growth path** — "In this role, you'll grow toward..." increases senior applicant interest
- **Be specific about tools and tech** — "Python, PostgreSQL, AWS" > "various technologies"
- **State the team** — "Join a team of 8 engineers" gives tangible context

## Best Practices

- **Keep it under 700 words** — applications drop 30%+ for JDs over 1,000 words
- **Limit must-have requirements to 5–6** — every additional requirement reduces female applicants disproportionately
- **Always include salary range** — JDs with pay ranges get 30% more applicants
- **Write in active voice** — "You will lead..." not "The candidate is expected to..."
- **Test your JD** — run through gender decoder tools and readability checkers
- **Update quarterly** — stale JDs with outdated tech stacks waste everyone's time
