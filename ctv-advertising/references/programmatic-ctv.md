# Programmatic CTV Buying Guide

Comprehensive guide to buying Connected TV inventory programmatically through demand-side platforms (DSPs).

---

## Programmatic CTV Overview

### What is Programmatic CTV?

Programmatic CTV is the automated buying and selling of Connected TV advertising inventory through demand-side platforms (DSPs) and supply-side platforms (SSPs). Advertisers bid on CTV impressions in real-time, targeting specific households based on data.

### Benefits of Programmatic CTV

**Scale**
- Access inventory across multiple CTV platforms simultaneously
- Reach 100+ million US households through single interface
- Aggregate long-tail inventory (smaller apps and services)

**Efficiency**
- Automated buying reduces manual insertion orders
- Real-time bidding optimizes CPMs
- Centralized campaign management

**Targeting**
- Advanced audience targeting using first and third-party data
- Household-level targeting
- Cross-device targeting and retargeting

**Flexibility**
- Adjust campaigns in real-time
- Test and optimize quickly
- Control frequency across platforms

**Transparency**
- See exactly where ads run
- Access to detailed performance data
- Control over inventory quality

### Programmatic vs. Direct Buying

| Aspect | Programmatic | Direct (Platform Buys) |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Inventory Access** | Multi-platform aggregated | Single platform |
| **Minimum Spend** | $10,000-$25,000 | $5,000-$50,000 |
| **Targeting** | Advanced data-driven | Platform-specific data |
| **CPMs** | $15-$45 (typically lower) | $25-$65 (typically higher) |
| **Control** | High (real-time adjustments) | Medium (managed service) |
| **Transparency** | High (see all placements) | Medium (platform reporting) |
| **Premium Inventory** | Limited (mostly available direct) | High (platform exclusives) |
| **Setup Complexity** | High (requires DSP expertise) | Low (managed by platform) |

## Programmatic CTV Campaign Setup

### Step 1: Define Campaign Objectives

**Awareness Campaigns**
- Objective: Maximize reach and frequency
- KPIs: Reach, frequency, completion rate, brand lift
- Bidding: CPM (cost per thousand impressions)

**Consideration Campaigns**
- Objective: Drive engagement and website visits
- KPIs: Website visits, engagement rate, time on site
- Bidding: CPM or vCPM (viewable CPM)

**Conversion Campaigns**
- Objective: Drive conversions and sales
- KPIs: Conversions, CPA, ROAS
- Bidding: CPA (cost per acquisition) or ROAS-based

### Step 2: Audience Strategy

**First-Party Data**
- Upload customer lists (emails, addresses, phone numbers)
- Match to household IDs
- Create lookalike audiences
- Retarget website visitors

**Third-Party Data**
- Purchase data (credit card transactions)
- Demographic data (age, income, household composition)
- Behavioral data (auto intenders, home shoppers, etc.)
- Interest-based data (sports fans, travelers, etc.)

**Contextual Targeting**
- Content genre (sports, news, entertainment, etc.)
- Specific shows or networks
- Daypart (time of day)
- Device type (Roku, Fire TV, Samsung, etc.)

**Geographic Targeting**
- DMA (designated market area)
- State, city, zip code
- Custom geofences

### Step 3: Inventory Selection

**Inventory Types**

**Open Exchange**
- Publicly available inventory
- Real-time bidding
- Lowest CPMs ($15-$30)
- Variable quality

**Private Marketplaces (PMPs)**
- Invitation-only inventory
- Pre-negotiated pricing
- Higher CPMs ($25-$45)
- Better quality and transparency

**Programmatic Guaranteed (PG)**
- Reserved inventory at fixed price
- Guaranteed impressions
- Highest CPMs ($35-$60)
- Premium quality

**Inventory Quality Controls**
- Whitelist: Only buy from approved apps/publishers
- Blacklist: Exclude specific apps/publishers
- Brand safety filters: Exclude inappropriate content
- Viewability thresholds: Only buy viewable impressions
- Completion rate thresholds: Only buy high-completion placements

### Step 4: Creative Setup

**Creative Specifications**
- Follow universal CTV specs (1080p, 16:9, H.264, AAC audio, -24 LUFS)
- Upload multiple durations (15s, 30s)
- Upload multiple creative versions for A/B testing
- Include companion banners (if supported)

**Creative Trafficking**
- Upload to DSP creative library
- Add tracking pixels (impression, quartile, completion)
- Set up click-through URLs (if interactive)
- Assign creatives to line items

### Step 5: Bidding & Budget

**Bidding Strategies**

**Fixed CPM**
- Set maximum CPM bid
- Simple and predictable
- Good for awareness campaigns

**Dynamic CPM**
- DSP adjusts bids based on performance
- Optimizes for completion rate or engagement
- Good for consideration campaigns

**CPA Bidding**
- DSP optimizes bids to hit target CPA
- Requires conversion tracking
- Good for conversion campaigns
- Needs learning period (2-4 weeks)

**ROAS Bidding**
- DSP optimizes bids to hit target ROAS
- Requires revenue tracking
- Good for e-commerce campaigns
- Needs significant conversion volume

**Budget Allocation**
- Daily budget: Spend evenly throughout campaign
- Lifetime budget: Spend over campaign duration (DSP paces)
- Daypart budgets: Allocate more to high-performing times
- Platform budgets: Allocate across CTV platforms

### Step 6: Frequency Management

**Frequency Caps**
- Set maximum impressions per household per day/week/month
- Recommended: 1 per day, 3-5 per week, 10-15 per month
- Apply at campaign level (across all line items)
- Use household-level frequency (not device-level)

**Frequency Optimization**
- Monitor frequency distribution reports
- Adjust caps if >20% of households see 10+ impressions
- Coordinate with direct platform buys (if running simultaneously)

### Step 7: Measurement Setup

**Conversion Tracking**
- Install DSP conversion pixel on website
- Define conversion events (purchases, sign-ups, etc.)
- Set attribution window (1-30 days)
- Enable cross-device tracking

**Brand Lift Measurement**
- Set up brand lift study (if available)
- Define metrics (awareness, consideration, intent)
- DSP serves surveys to exposed and control groups

**Third-Party Measurement**
- Integrate with attribution platform (iSpot, TVSquared, etc.)
- Set up foot traffic measurement (Placed, Foursquare)
- Enable viewability measurement (IAS, DoubleVerify)

## Campaign Optimization

### Week 1-2: Learning Phase

**Monitor Daily**
- Impressions pacing (on track to hit goals?)
- CPMs (within expected range?)
- Completion rates (85%+ for quality placements?)
- Frequency distribution (any households over-exposed?)

**Initial Optimizations**
- Pause low-completion placements (<70%)
- Increase bids on high-performing placements
- Adjust daypart targeting based on performance
- Refine audience segments

### Week 3-4: Optimization Phase

**Performance Analysis**
- Which platforms drive best completion rates?
- Which audiences drive best engagement/conversions?
- Which creatives perform best?
- Which dayparts perform best?
- What's the optimal frequency?

**Optimization Actions**
- Shift budget to top-performing platforms
- Expand top-performing audiences
- Pause underperforming creatives
- Adjust daypart targeting
- Refine frequency caps
- Test new inventory sources (PMPs)

### Ongoing Optimization

**Weekly Actions**
- Review performance dashboards
- Adjust bids and budgets
- Refresh creative (every 4-6 weeks)
- Test new audiences
- Expand to new platforms

**Monthly Actions**
- Comprehensive performance review
- Audience analysis and refinement
- Creative refresh planning
- Budget reallocation
- Measurement and attribution analysis

## Programmatic CTV Best Practices

### 1. Start with Private Marketplaces (PMPs)

**Why**
- Better inventory quality than open exchange
- More transparency (know exactly where ads run)
- Higher completion rates
- Lower fraud risk

**How**
- Work with DSP rep to access PMPs
- Negotiate pricing and volume commitments
- Test multiple PMPs to find best performers

### 2. Implement Strict Quality Controls

**Inventory Quality**
- Whitelist known high-quality apps and publishers
- Blacklist low-quality or fraudulent inventory
- Set minimum completion rate threshold (80%+)
- Enable brand safety filters

**Viewability**
- Only buy viewable impressions (100% viewable for CTV)
- Monitor viewability rates (should be 95%+ for CTV)
- Pause placements with low viewability

**Fraud Prevention**
- Enable fraud detection (IAS, DoubleVerify, etc.)
- Monitor for suspicious patterns (abnormal completion rates, etc.)
- Work with DSP to investigate and block fraudulent inventory

### 3. Leverage First-Party Data

**Customer Matching**
- Upload customer lists (emails, addresses, phone numbers)
- Match to household IDs (typically 40-60% match rate)
- Target existing customers for retention/upsell
- Exclude existing customers for acquisition campaigns

**Lookalike Audiences**
- Create lookalike audiences from best customers
- Test multiple lookalike percentages (1%, 5%, 10%)
- Expand reach while maintaining relevance

**Website Retargeting**
- Retarget website visitors on CTV
- Create segments by page visited or action taken
- Use for abandoned cart recovery, product launches, etc.

### 4. Optimize Frequency

**Set Household-Level Caps**
- 1 impression per household per day
- 3-5 impressions per household per week
- 10-15 impressions per household per month

**Monitor Distribution**
- Review frequency distribution reports weekly
- Ensure <20% of households see 10+ impressions
- Adjust caps if over-delivery occurs

**Coordinate Across Channels**
- If running direct platform buys, coordinate frequency
- Use household graphs to manage cross-platform frequency
- Avoid oversaturation

### 5. Test and Learn

**A/B Testing Framework**
- Test one variable at a time
- Run tests for minimum 2 weeks
- Ensure statistical significance (minimum 1,000 impressions per variant)
- Implement winning variant, test next variable

**Variables to Test**
- Creative versions (messaging, visuals, CTA)
- Ad duration (15s vs. 30s)
- Audience segments
- Dayparts
- Platforms/inventory sources
- Bidding strategies
- Frequency caps

### 6. Use Dynamic Creative Optimization (DCO)

**What is DCO?**
- Automatically serve different creative versions to different audiences
- Personalize messaging based on audience data
- Optimize creative in real-time based on performance

**Use Cases**
- Serve different product ads based on browsing history
- Customize messaging by demographic (age, income, etc.)
- Adjust CTA based on funnel stage (awareness vs. conversion)
- Localize creative by geography

**Implementation**
- Work with DSP to enable DCO
- Create modular creative assets (backgrounds, products, CTAs, etc.)
- Define rules for creative assembly
- Monitor performance by creative variant

### 7. Implement Cross-Device Strategies

**CTV to Mobile/Desktop**
- Serve CTV ads for awareness
- Retarget on mobile/desktop for conversion
- Use household graphs to connect devices
- Measure cross-device conversions

**Sequential Messaging**
- Serve awareness message on CTV
- Serve consideration message on mobile
- Serve conversion message on desktop
- Guide users through funnel across devices

### 8. Monitor and Optimize by Daypart

**Daypart Analysis**
- Morning (6am-12pm): Lower inventory, lower CPMs
- Afternoon (12pm-6pm): Moderate inventory, moderate CPMs
- Evening (6pm-11pm): Highest inventory, highest CPMs, best engagement
- Late Night (11pm-6am): Lower inventory, lower CPMs, niche audiences

**Optimization**
- Allocate more budget to high-performing dayparts
- Test different creatives by daypart
- Adjust bids by daypart (higher for evening, lower for morning)
- Consider audience behavior (B2B vs. B2C)

## Common Programmatic CTV Challenges

### Challenge: High CPMs
**Problem**: Programmatic CTV CPMs higher than expected
**Solutions**:
- Focus on open exchange vs. PMPs (lower CPMs)
- Expand geographic targeting (avoid high-cost DMAs)
- Test less competitive dayparts (morning, afternoon)
- Negotiate PMP pricing with publishers
- Use dynamic bidding to optimize CPMs

### Challenge: Low Match Rates
**Problem**: First-party data match rates below 40%
**Solutions**:
- Use multiple data onboarding partners
- Ensure data quality (clean, standardized addresses/emails)
- Expand to lookalike audiences
- Use third-party data to supplement

### Challenge: Inventory Quality Issues
**Problem**: Ads running on low-quality apps or fraudulent inventory
**Solutions**:
- Implement strict whitelists
- Use PMPs instead of open exchange
- Enable fraud detection tools
- Monitor completion rates (should be 85%+)
- Work with DSP to investigate and block bad inventory

### Challenge: Attribution Difficulty
**Problem**: Hard to measure CTV impact on conversions
**Solutions**:
- Implement conversion pixels and cross-device tracking
- Use brand lift studies for upper-funnel campaigns
- Add QR codes or promo codes for direct attribution
- Work with third-party attribution platforms
- Use control groups to measure incrementality

### Challenge: Frequency Management
**Problem**: Some households seeing ads too frequently
**Solutions**:
- Implement household-level frequency caps
- Monitor frequency distribution reports weekly
- Coordinate with direct platform buys
- Use household graphs for cross-platform frequency management

### Challenge: Limited Premium Inventory
**Problem**: Can't access premium CTV inventory programmatically
**Solutions**:
- Negotiate PMPs with premium publishers
- Use programmatic guaranteed (PG) deals
- Supplement with direct platform buys (Hulu, Roku, etc.)
- Focus on quality over quantity

**Programmatic Dominance**
- 80%+ of CTV ads now bought programmatically
- Shift from direct to programmatic continues
- More premium inventory becoming available programmatically

**FAST Channel Growth**
- Free Ad-Supported Streaming TV (FAST) growing rapidly
- Pluto TV, Tubi, Xumo, Samsung TV Plus, etc.
- Lower CPMs, high engagement
- Accessible via programmatic

**Advanced Targeting**
- Increased use of first-party data
- ACR data becoming more available
- Household-level targeting standard
- Cross-device targeting improving

**Measurement Standardization**
- Industry moving toward unified metrics
- Cross-platform measurement improving
- Attribution getting more sophisticated
- Privacy-compliant solutions emerging

**Programmatic Guaranteed Growth**
- More publishers offering PG deals
- Combines automation with guaranteed delivery
- Premium inventory at fixed pricing
- Reduces manual IO process
