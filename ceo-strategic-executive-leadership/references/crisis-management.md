# Crisis Management

Frameworks and playbooks for crisis response.

---

## Crisis Preparedness

### Crisis Categories

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Operational | Product failure, service outage, supply chain |
| Financial | Liquidity, fraud, accounting issues |
| Reputational | PR crisis, social media, executive misconduct |
| Legal/Regulatory | Lawsuit, regulatory action, compliance |
| Cyber | Data breach, ransomware, system compromise |
| Natural | Disaster, pandemic, facility damage |
| Personnel | Key executive departure, safety incident |

### Crisis Team Structure

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| CEO | Overall leadership, key decisions, external face |
| Crisis Lead | Day-to-day crisis management |
| Communications | Media, stakeholder communication |
| Legal | Legal exposure, regulatory coordination |
| Operations | Business continuity, restoration |
| HR | Employee matters, safety |
| Finance | Financial impact, resources |

## Crisis Response Framework

### Response Phases

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| **Detect** | Identify crisis, assess severity |
| **Activate** | Assemble team, establish command |
| **Assess** | Gather facts, evaluate impact |
| **Plan** | Develop response strategy |
| **Execute** | Implement response actions |
| **Communicate** | Stakeholder communication |
| **Monitor** | Track situation evolution |
| **Recover** | Restore operations, reputation |
| **Learn** | Post-crisis analysis, improvements |

### Initial Response (First 1-4 Hours)

1. Assess situation severity and scope
2. Activate crisis team
3. Establish facts (known and unknown)
4. Secure safety of people and assets
5. Draft initial holding statement
6. Notify board chair (if significant)
7. Preserve evidence and documentation

### Severity Assessment

| Level | Definition | CEO Involvement |
|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Level 1 | Minor, operational impact | Informed |
| Level 2 | Moderate, business impact | Actively involved |
| Level 3 | Major, company-wide | Leading response |
| Level 4 | Existential threat | Full dedication |

## Crisis Communication

### Communication Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Speed | Communicate quickly, even with incomplete info |
| Accuracy | Only state what you know |
| Transparency | Be honest about situation |
| Empathy | Acknowledge impact on stakeholders |
| Accountability | Take appropriate responsibility |
| Action | Focus on what you're doing |

### Stakeholder Communication Priority

| Priority | Stakeholder | Method |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Safety-affected | Direct, immediate |
| 2 | Employees | Internal channels |
| 3 | Board | Direct from CEO |
| 4 | Key customers | Personal outreach |
| 5 | Media | Press statement/conference |
| 6 | Regulators | Formal notification |
| 7 | General public | Public statement |

### Communication Templates

**Initial Holding Statement**:
> "We are aware of [situation]. We are actively investigating and will provide updates as we learn more. Our priority is [safety/customers/resolution]. We take this matter seriously and are committed to addressing it fully."

**CEO Statement Template**:
> "I want to address [situation] directly. [What happened]. [What we're doing]. [What this means for stakeholders]. We are committed to [resolution/improvement]. I will provide updates as we have more information."

## Post-Crisis Activities

### After-Action Review

| Element | Assessment |
|---------|------------|
| Timeline | What happened and when |
| Response | What we did well |
| Gaps | What could be improved |
| Root Cause | Why this happened |
| Recommendations | Changes to prevent recurrence |

### Reputation Recovery

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| Stabilize | Address immediate reputation damage |
| Remediate | Fix underlying issues |
| Communicate | Share actions taken |
| Rebuild | Positive actions and messaging |
| Monitor | Track reputation recovery |
