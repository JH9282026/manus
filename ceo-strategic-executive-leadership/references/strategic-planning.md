# Strategic Planning

Frameworks and processes for corporate strategy development.

---

## Strategic Planning Process

### Phase 1: Strategic Assessment

**External Analysis**:
| Framework | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| PESTEL | Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal |
| Porter's Five Forces | Industry competitive dynamics |
| Market Analysis | Size, growth, segmentation |
| Competitive Analysis | Competitor positioning, capabilities |

**Internal Analysis**:
| Framework | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| SWOT | Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats |
| Resource Audit | Capabilities, assets, gaps |
| Value Chain | Activities creating competitive advantage |
| Financial Analysis | Performance, trends, benchmarks |

### Phase 2: Strategy Formulation

**Vision Development**:
- Aspirational future state (10-20 years)
- Inspiring and memorable
- Guides strategic choices

**Mission Articulation**:
- What we do
- For whom
- How we do it
- Why it matters

**Strategic Choice Framework**:

| Choice | Key Questions |
|--------|---------------|
| Where to Play | Markets, segments, geographies, channels |
| How to Win | Differentiation, competitive advantage |
| Capabilities | What must we excel at? |
| Management Systems | How will we measure and govern? |

### Phase 3: Strategy Operationalization

**Strategic Pillars**: 3-5 major strategic priorities

**Strategic Initiatives**: Programs and investments supporting each pillar

**Resource Allocation**: Budget and capital aligned to strategy

**Metrics and Targets**: KPIs for each pillar and initiative

## Strategic Planning Frameworks

### Balanced Scorecard

| Perspective | Focus | Example Metrics |
|-------------|-------|----------------|
| Financial | Shareholder value | Revenue, profit, ROIC |
| Customer | Customer success | NPS, retention, share |
| Internal Process | Operational excellence | Quality, efficiency, cycle time |
| Learning & Growth | Capabilities | Employee engagement, innovation |

### OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)

**Structure**:
- Objective: Qualitative, inspirational goal
- Key Results: 3-5 measurable outcomes

**Cascade**:
- Company OKRs → Department OKRs → Team OKRs

### Strategy Map

Visual representation showing:
- Strategic objectives by perspective
- Cause-and-effect linkages
- How activities drive outcomes
