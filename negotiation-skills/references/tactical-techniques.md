# Negotiation Tactics & Techniques

## Opening Techniques

### Setting the Tone
- Start with rapport building (2-5 minutes)
- Establish collaborative framing: "Let's find something that works for both of us"
- Set agenda and process expectations
- Confirm authority levels on both sides

### Opening Moves
| Technique | Description | When to Use |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Bold anchor | Start with ambitious first offer | Strong BATNA, good data |
| Bracket | Set range around your target | Uncertain about counterpart's range |
| Nibble | Raise small additional requests | After main terms agreed |
| Flinch | Visible reaction to their offer | Counter unreasonable opening |
| Silence | Pause after their offer | Force them to improve or justify |

## Handling Difficult Tactics

### Common Counter-Tactics
| Their Tactic | Your Response |
|-------------|-------------|
| Good cop/bad cop | Identify the tactic, negotiate with decision maker |
| Take it or leave it | Test the ultimatum: "What if we adjusted X?" |
| Deadline pressure | Verify deadline is real, don't rush |
| Emotional outburst | Stay calm, take a break if needed |
| Lowball/highball | Don't counter-anchor; re-anchor with data |
| Nibbling | "I'd need to revisit the entire package" |
| Authority escalation | Establish authority upfront |

## Closing Techniques

### Reaching Agreement
| Technique | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| Summary close | Recap all agreed points, confirm |
| Split the difference | Meet in the middle on final gap |
| If-then | "If you can do X, I'll agree to Y" |
| Trial close | "It sounds like we're close. Are you comfortable with...?" |
| Deadline | "I can hold this offer until Friday" |

### Post-Agreement
1. Document all terms immediately
2. Confirm understanding in writing
3. Identify next steps and timelines
4. Build relationship for future negotiations
5. Conduct post-mortem: what worked, what didn't

## Vendor Negotiation Specifics

### Leverage Points
- Volume commitments in exchange for discounts
- Multi-year contracts for better rates
- Early payment terms for price reduction
- Reference/case study participation
- Bundle multiple products/services
- Competitive alternatives (demonstrate options)

### Contract Terms to Negotiate
| Term | Leverage |
|------|---------|
| Price | Volume, competition, multi-year |
| Payment terms | Net 30/60/90, early pay discount |
| SLA/guarantees | Credits for underperformance |
| Termination | Notice period, early exit clauses |
| Renewal | Cap on annual increases |
| Liability | Indemnification, insurance requirements |

## Salary Negotiation Specifics

### Total Compensation Components
- Base salary
- Annual bonus (target %)
- Equity/stock (RSUs, options, vesting)
- Sign-on bonus
- Relocation package
- Benefits (health, 401k match, PTO)
- Professional development budget
- Remote work flexibility

### Negotiation Script Framework
1. Express enthusiasm for the role
2. Present market data supporting your ask
3. Reference specific value you'll bring
4. State your target range (anchor high)
5. Listen and ask clarifying questions
6. Negotiate package holistically
7. Get final offer in writing
