# Negotiation Frameworks

## Principled Negotiation (Harvard Method)

### Four Principles
1. **Separate people from the problem** — Address emotions and relationships independently from substantive issues
2. **Focus on interests, not positions** — Understand underlying needs behind stated demands
3. **Generate options for mutual gain** — Brainstorm creative solutions before deciding
4. **Use objective criteria** — Base agreements on fair standards, benchmarks, precedent

### Interest Discovery Questions
- "Why is that important to you?"
- "What would that enable you to do?"
- "What concerns would that address?"
- "What would happen if we couldn't agree on this point?"
- "Help me understand what's behind that request."

## BATNA Analysis

### Developing Your BATNA
1. List all alternatives if negotiation fails
2. Evaluate each alternative realistically
3. Select the best alternative
4. Improve your BATNA before negotiating (strengthens position)

### Using BATNA
- Never accept an offer worse than your BATNA
- Don't reveal your BATNA unless it's strong
- Assess the other party's BATNA (weaker = more leverage for you)
- Having multiple alternatives increases confidence

## Anchoring Strategy

### First Offer Guidelines
- Make the first offer when you have good information
- Anchor aggressively but credibly
- Support anchor with objective justification
- Expect counter-anchoring

### Salary Negotiation Anchoring
1. Research market rate (Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, Payscale)
2. Identify your target (75th-90th percentile for strong candidates)
3. Anchor 10-20% above your target
4. Support with specific accomplishments and market data
5. Negotiate total compensation, not just base salary

## Concession Strategy

### Concession Rules
- Never make a concession without getting something in return
- Start with less important concessions
- Make concessions progressively smaller (signals approaching limit)
- Always express reluctance when conceding
- Track all concessions on both sides

## Multi-Issue Negotiation

### Package Deals
- Negotiate all issues simultaneously (not sequentially)
- Trade low-priority concessions for high-priority gains
- Create value through differences in priorities
- Present offers as complete packages

### Value Creation Opportunities
| Difference | Opportunity |
|-----------|-------------|
| Different priorities | Trade high-for-low priority items |
| Different risk tolerance | Shift risk to less risk-averse party |
| Different time preferences | Structure payments/timelines accordingly |
| Different capabilities | Assign tasks to lower-cost party |
