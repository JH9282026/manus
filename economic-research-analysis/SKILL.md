# Economic Research & Analysis

Perform rigorous economic analysis covering macroeconomic trends, sectoral dynamics, policy impacts, and economic forecasting to support strategic decision-making.

## Overview

Provide frameworks for systematic economic research using established economic indicators, analytical models, and forecasting techniques. Cover macro analysis, sectoral economics, policy assessment, and economic scenario planning.

## Key Economic Indicators

| Indicator | Frequency | Source | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| GDP Growth | Quarterly | BEA, IMF | Overall economic health |
| CPI / PCE | Monthly | BLS, BEA | Inflation measurement |
| Unemployment Rate | Monthly | BLS | Labor market health |
| Non-Farm Payrolls | Monthly | BLS | Job creation strength |
| Fed Funds Rate | 8x/year | Federal Reserve | Monetary policy stance |
| PMI (Manufacturing/Services) | Monthly | ISM | Business activity leading indicator |
| Consumer Confidence | Monthly | Conference Board | Spending outlook |
| Yield Curve | Continuous | Treasury | Recession predictor |
| Housing Starts | Monthly | Census Bureau | Construction activity |
| Trade Balance | Monthly | Census/BEA | International trade health |

## Economic Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Define Research Scope
Specify geography, time period, sectors, and key questions.

### Step 2: Gather Data
Pull from official statistical agencies, central banks, and international organizations.

### Step 3: Analyze Current Conditions
Assess where the economy is in the business cycle using leading, coincident, and lagging indicators.

### Step 4: Model Scenarios
Build base, upside, and downside scenarios with explicit assumptions.

### Step 5: Assess Policy Implications
Evaluate impact of fiscal, monetary, and regulatory policies on outcomes.

### Step 6: Synthesize and Recommend
Translate economic findings into actionable strategic implications.

## Business Cycle Phases

| Phase | GDP | Employment | Inflation | Monetary Policy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Expansion | Rising | Increasing | Rising | Tightening |
| Peak | Slowing | Full employment | High | Tight |
| Contraction | Falling | Declining | Falling | Easing |
| Trough | Bottoming | Low | Low | Easy |
