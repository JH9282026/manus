# Macroeconomic Analysis Methods

## GDP Analysis

### GDP Components (Expenditure Approach)
- **C (Consumption)**: ~68% of US GDP — household spending
- **I (Investment)**: ~18% — business fixed investment, residential, inventories
- **G (Government)**: ~17% — federal, state, local spending
- **NX (Net Exports)**: ~-3% — exports minus imports

### GDP Growth Decomposition
Break GDP growth into contributions from each component:
- Which components are driving growth?
- Is growth broad-based or concentrated?
- How does composition compare to historical patterns?

### Real vs Nominal GDP
- Always use real (inflation-adjusted) GDP for growth analysis
- GDP deflator measures price changes in all domestically produced goods
- CPI measures price changes in consumer basket only

## Inflation Analysis

### Inflation Measures

| Measure | Coverage | Excludes | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPI-U | Urban consumers | Nothing | Consumer inflation |
| Core CPI | Urban consumers | Food, energy | Trend inflation |
| PCE | All consumers | Nothing | Fed preferred measure |
| Core PCE | All consumers | Food, energy | Monetary policy targeting |
| PPI | Producers | Consumer prices | Input cost pressures |
| GDP Deflator | All domestic production | Imports | Broad economy |

### Inflation Decomposition
- Break into goods vs services inflation
- Identify supply-side vs demand-side drivers
- Track shelter/housing component (large CPI weight)
- Monitor wage growth relative to productivity

## Interest Rate Analysis

### Yield Curve Interpretation

| Shape | Signal | Historical Accuracy |
|---|---|---|
| Normal (upward) | Economic expansion expected | Standard baseline |
| Flat | Uncertainty, transition | Precedes changes |
| Inverted | Recession signal (12-18 months) | Predicted last 7 recessions |
| Steep | Recovery, policy easing | Early cycle indicator |

### Transmission Mechanisms
- Short-term rates → money market, savings, variable-rate loans
- Long-term rates → mortgages, corporate bonds, capital investment
- Real rates (nominal minus inflation expectations) → investment decisions
- Term premium → risk appetite and policy expectations

## Recession Analysis

### Recession Probability Models
- **Yield curve spread**: 10Y-2Y or 10Y-3M Treasury spread
- **Leading Economic Index (LEI)**: Conference Board composite of 10 leading indicators
- **Sahm Rule**: Recession triggered when 3-month moving average of unemployment rises 0.5pp+ from prior 12-month low
- **Purchasing Managers Index**: PMI below 50 for sustained period
- **Credit conditions**: Bank lending standards tightening

### Historical Recession Characteristics
- Average post-WWII recession: 10 months duration
- Average GDP decline: -2.5% peak to trough
- Average unemployment increase: 3-4 percentage points
- Recovery typically takes 2-4 years to return to pre-recession levels
