# Sectoral and Policy Analysis

## Sectoral Analysis Framework

### Industry Analysis Steps
1. Define industry boundaries and classification (NAICS/SIC)
2. Assess industry size, growth rate, and lifecycle stage
3. Analyze competitive dynamics (Porter's Five Forces)
4. Identify key value chain participants and power dynamics
5. Evaluate regulatory environment and compliance requirements
6. Assess technology disruption risks and opportunities
7. Model industry financial performance and benchmarks

### Key Sector Metrics

| Sector | Key Metrics | Data Sources |
|---|---|---|
| Manufacturing | Industrial production, capacity utilization, PMI | Federal Reserve, ISM |
| Housing | Starts, permits, prices, inventory, mortgage rates | Census, FHFA, Freddie Mac |
| Retail | Retail sales, consumer confidence, credit card data | Census, Conference Board |
| Technology | R&D spending, patent filings, venture funding | NSF, USPTO, PitchBook |
| Healthcare | Spending as % GDP, utilization rates, drug approvals | CMS, FDA |
| Energy | Production, consumption, prices, rig count | EIA, Baker Hughes |
| Financial | Bank lending, credit spreads, delinquency rates | Fed, FDIC |

## Trade Analysis

### Trade Balance Components
- Goods trade balance (merchandise)
- Services trade balance
- Current account balance (goods + services + income + transfers)
- Capital account flows

### Trade Impact Assessment
- Tariff impact modeling on prices and volumes
- Currency effect analysis (appreciation vs depreciation)
- Supply chain dependency mapping
- Comparative advantage analysis
- Trade agreement impact evaluation

---

## Policy Impact Analysis

### Fiscal Policy Assessment
- Government spending multiplier effects
- Tax policy impact on investment and consumption
- Deficit/debt sustainability analysis
- Infrastructure spending economic impact
- Transfer payment effects on consumption

### Monetary Policy Assessment
- Rate change transmission to economy (6-18 month lag)
- Quantitative easing/tightening effects on financial conditions
- Forward guidance impact on expectations
- Financial stability implications

### Regulatory Impact
- Compliance cost estimation
- Market structure effects
- Innovation impact assessment
- Employment effects
- Consumer welfare analysis

## Economic Forecasting Methods

| Method | Horizon | Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leading indicators | 3-12 months | Timely, proven track record | Limited precision |
| Econometric models | 1-5 years | Quantitative, scenario-capable | Model risk, assumption-dependent |
| DSGE models | 1-10 years | Theoretically grounded | Complex, stylized |
| Survey-based | 1-2 years | Market consensus | Herd mentality |
| Nowcasting | Current quarter | Real-time data | Short horizon |
| Scenario analysis | Variable | Captures uncertainty | Subjective probabilities |
