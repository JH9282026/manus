# Equipment Setup

Detailed specifications, purchasing recommendations, and configuration guides for stop-motion animation equipment at all budget levels.

## Camera Selection and Setup

### Smartphone Cameras (Beginner: $0-$200)

**Recommended Devices**:
- iPhone 11 or newer (excellent image quality, stable apps)
- Samsung Galaxy S20 or newer (high resolution, manual controls)
- Google Pixel 5 or newer (superior computational photography)

**Apps**:
- **Stop Motion Studio** (iOS/Android): $5-$10
  - Onion skinning
  - Frame-by-frame capture
  - Audio recording and sync
  - Export to various formats
  - Grid overlay for alignment
- **iMotion** (iOS): Free with in-app purchases
  - Time-lapse and stop-motion modes
  - Manual camera controls
  - Remote capture via Apple Watch

**Setup**:
1. Lock exposure and focus (tap and hold on screen)
2. Disable auto-brightness and auto-lock
3. Use highest resolution available
4. Enable grid for composition
5. Connect to power source for long shoots
6. Use remote shutter or timer to avoid shake

**Advantages**:
- Low cost, accessible
- Portable, easy to set up
- Intuitive apps with built-in features
- Sufficient quality for web and social media

**Limitations**:
- Limited manual control
- Smaller sensor (more noise in low light)
- Difficult to achieve shallow depth of field
- Battery life concerns

### Entry-Level DSLR/Mirrorless (Intermediate: $400-$1,200)

**Recommended Models**:
- **Canon EOS Rebel T7i / T8i**: $600-$800
  - 24MP sensor, articulating screen, Wi-Fi
  - Compatible with Canon EOS Utility for tethering
- **Sony Alpha a6400**: $900-$1,000
  - 24MP APS-C sensor, fast autofocus (set to manual for stop-motion)
  - Excellent video features, compact size
- **Nikon D5600**: $600-$700
  - 24MP sensor, articulating screen, Bluetooth
  - Compatible with Nikon Camera Control Pro
- **Fujifilm X-T30**: $800-$900
  - 26MP sensor, film simulations, compact
  - Excellent color science

**Essential Settings**:
- **Mode**: Manual (M)
- **Format**: RAW for maximum editing flexibility, JPEG for faster workflow
- **ISO**: Lowest possible (100-400) for minimal noise
- **Aperture**: f/8-f/16 for deep depth of field, f/2.8-f/5.6 for shallow
- **Shutter Speed**: 1/60 or faster to prevent motion blur if subject moves
- **White Balance**: Manual (set to match lighting, typically 5000-5500K)
- **Focus**: Manual (autofocus disabled)
- **Image Stabilization**: Off (camera is on tripod)

**Tethering Setup**:
1. Connect camera to computer via USB cable (TetherTools recommended)
2. Install manufacturer software (Canon EOS Utility, Nikon Camera Control Pro)
3. Or use Dragonframe, Stop Motion Studio, or other third-party software
4. Configure software to save images to designated folder
5. Enable live view on computer screen
6. Use onion skinning to compare previous frame

**Advantages**:
- Full manual control over exposure, focus, depth of field
- Larger sensor for better image quality
- Interchangeable lenses for creative options
- Tethering capability for precise control

**Limitations**:
- Steeper learning curve
- Requires additional equipment (lenses, tripod, lighting)
- More expensive than smartphone

### Professional DSLR/Mirrorless (Advanced: $1,500-$4,000+)

**Recommended Models**:
- **Canon EOS R5**: $3,900
  - 45MP full-frame sensor, 8K video, in-body stabilization (turn off for stop-motion)
  - Exceptional image quality, professional build
- **Canon EOS R6**: $2,500
  - 20MP full-frame sensor, excellent low-light performance
  - More affordable than R5, still professional quality
- **Sony A7 IV**: $2,500
  - 33MP full-frame sensor, advanced autofocus (use manual for stop-motion)
  - Excellent dynamic range, compact size
- **Nikon Z6 II**: $2,000
  - 24MP full-frame sensor, dual card slots
  - Robust build, excellent ergonomics

**Professional Accessories**:
- **AC Adapter**: Essential for long shoots (prevents battery drain)
  - Canon ACK-E6 for EOS R5/R6
  - Sony AC-PW20 for Alpha series
- **Tethering Cable**: TetherTools TetherPro USB-C or USB 3.0 (15-30 ft)
- **Lens Selection**:
  - **50mm f/1.8**: Affordable, versatile, good for medium shots
  - **35mm f/2**: Wider angle, good for environmental shots
  - **85mm f/1.8**: Portrait lens, beautiful shallow depth of field
  - **Macro 100mm f/2.8**: Extreme close-ups, tiny subjects

**Advantages**:
- Maximum image quality and resolution
- Superior low-light performance
- Professional features and build quality
- Extensive lens ecosystem

**Limitations**:
- High cost
- Overkill for beginner projects
- Requires expertise to maximize potential

## Stabilization and Support

### Tripods

**Beginner Tripods** ($20-$100):
- **AmazonBasics Lightweight Tripod**: $25
  - Adequate for smartphones and light cameras
  - Compact, portable
  - Limited stability for precise work
- **Joby GorillaPod 3K**: $50
  - Flexible legs for creative positioning
  - Grips irregular surfaces
  - Good for small setups

**Intermediate Tripods** ($100-$300):
- **Manfrotto Compact Action Tripod**: $100
  - Sturdy, reliable
  - Fluid head for smooth adjustments
  - Supports up to 11 lbs
- **Vanguard Alta Pro 263AB**: $150
  - Multi-angle center column for overhead shots
  - Sturdy, versatile
  - Supports up to 15 lbs

**Professional Tripods** ($300-$1,000+):
- **Manfrotto MT055XPRO3**: $250 (legs only)
  - Extremely stable, professional build
  - 90° center column for overhead shots
  - Supports up to 19 lbs
- **Manfrotto 502AH Fluid Head**: $200
  - Smooth pan and tilt for camera movements
  - Quick-release plate
  - Combine with MT055XPRO3 legs
- **Gitzo GT2542 Mountaineer**: $700+
  - Carbon fiber, lightweight yet stable
  - Professional-grade stability
  - Supports heavy camera setups

**C-Stands for Overhead Shots**:
- **Impact C-Stand with Grip Head**: $150-$200
  - Essential for flat-lay and cutout animation
  - Holds camera directly overhead
  - Requires camera mount adapter (Magic Arm or similar)

### Stabilization Tips
- **Hard Floors**: Prefer hard floors over carpet (less vibration)
- **Weight**: Add weight to tripod for extra stability
- **Avoid Touching**: Never touch camera or tripod during shoot
- **Isolate**: Work in area free from foot traffic and vibrations

## Lighting Equipment

### Beginner Lighting ($20-$100)

**Desk Lamps**:
- Use 2-3 adjustable desk lamps
- Daylight-balanced LED bulbs (5000-5500K)
- Diffuse with white paper, tracing paper, or thin white fabric
- Clamp to tables or stands for positioning

**DIY Diffusion**:
- White printer paper taped over lamp
- Tracing paper or parchment paper
- White bedsheet or pillowcase
- Wax paper (keep away from hot bulbs)

**Setup**:
1. Position key light 45° to side of subject
2. Add fill light opposite side (lower intensity)
3. Optional back light behind subject
4. Diffuse all lights to soften shadows
5. Avoid natural light (use blackout curtains)

### Intermediate Lighting ($100-$500)

**Softbox Kits**:
- **Neewer 700W Softbox Kit**: $100-$150
  - Two softboxes with stands
  - Daylight-balanced bulbs included
  - Soft, even light
- **LimoStudio 700W Softbox**: $80
  - Affordable, effective
  - Good for small to medium setups

**LED Panel Lights**:
- **Neewer 660 LED Panel**: $70 each
  - Dimmable, adjustable color temperature
  - Lightweight, portable
  - Can be battery-powered or AC
- **GVM 800D-RGB LED Panel**: $150
  - RGB color control for creative effects
  - High CRI (color accuracy)
  - App control via Bluetooth

**Setup**:
1. Use softboxes for main lighting (key and fill)
2. LED panels for accent, back, or hair lights
3. Adjust color temperature to match (all lights same Kelvin)
4. Use dimmers to control intensity ratios
5. Test with camera, adjust until consistent

### Professional Lighting ($500-$2,000+)

**Flat Panel LED Lights**:
- **Aputure Amaran 200d**: $300
  - Powerful, daylight-balanced
  - Bowens mount for modifiers
  - Quiet, flicker-free
- **Godox SL-60W**: $120
  - Budget-friendly professional option
  - Bowens mount, dimmable
  - Good color accuracy
- **Flashpoint FV150**: $200
  - Bi-color (adjust color temperature)
  - High CRI, professional quality
  - Compact, versatile

**Light Modifiers**:
- **Softboxes**: Soften light, reduce harsh shadows
- **Umbrellas**: Bounce or shoot-through for soft light
- **Grids**: Focus light, prevent spill
- **Barn Doors**: Shape and direct light
- **Gels**: Color correction or creative color effects

**Advanced Setup**:
1. Bounce lights off white ceiling for soft, natural overhead light
2. Use grids and barn doors to control spill
3. Add colored gels for mood or time-of-day effects
4. Use multiple lights for complex scenes (3-5 lights)
5. Light background separately from subject

### Lighting Consistency Tips
- **Avoid Natural Light**: Close curtains, shoot at night, or use blackout
- **Lock Settings**: Manual camera settings prevent auto-adjustments
- **Secure Lights**: Tape or clamp lights so they don't shift
- **Mark Positions**: Tape floor to mark light positions if moved
- **Test Shots**: Capture test frames, check for flickering or color shifts
- **Continuous Lights Only**: Avoid flash or strobe (inconsistent)

## Software

### Beginner Software (Free - $50)

**Stop Motion Studio** (Mobile & Desktop):
- **Platform**: iOS, Android, macOS, Windows
- **Price**: Free (basic), $10 (Pro mobile), $50 (Pro desktop)
- **Features**:
  - Onion skinning (see previous frames)
  - Frame-by-frame capture
  - Audio recording and import
  - Chroma key (green screen)
  - Time-lapse mode
  - Export to video formats
- **Best For**: Beginners, quick projects, mobile shooting

**OpenShot** (Desktop):
- **Platform**: Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Price**: Free (open-source)
- **Features**:
  - Video editing (not capture)
  - Import image sequences
  - Transitions, effects, titles
  - Audio editing
  - Export to various formats
- **Best For**: Editing captured frames, post-production

**DaVinci Resolve** (Desktop):
- **Platform**: Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Price**: Free (Studio version $295)
- **Features**:
  - Professional video editing
  - Advanced color correction
  - Audio post-production (Fairlight)
  - Visual effects (Fusion)
  - Import image sequences
- **Best For**: Advanced editing, color grading, professional finishing

### Professional Software ($300-$500)

**Dragonframe** (Industry Standard):
- **Platform**: Windows, macOS
- **Price**: $395 (full version)
- **Features**:
  - Direct camera control (Canon, Nikon, Sony, etc.)
  - Onion skinning with adjustable opacity
  - Real-time playback
  - Frame-by-frame control
  - DMX lighting control
  - Audio import and scrubbing
  - X-sheet (exposure sheet) for planning
  - Step-by-step capture workflow
  - Export to video or image sequence
- **Hardware**: Optional Dragonframe Bluetooth Controller ($150)
- **Best For**: Professional productions, precise control, industry workflows

**Setup with Dragonframe**:
1. Install Dragonframe on computer
2. Connect camera via USB (tethering)
3. Configure camera settings in Dragonframe
4. Set up scene, frame composition
5. Enable onion skinning to see previous frame
6. Capture frame using keyboard, controller, or on-screen button
7. Make adjustment to subject
8. Repeat capture process
9. Use playback to review animation
10. Export final sequence

**Adobe After Effects** (Post-Production):
- **Platform**: Windows, macOS
- **Price**: $20.99/month (subscription)
- **Features**:
  - Import image sequences
  - Advanced compositing
  - Visual effects, motion graphics
  - Remove rigs and supports
  - Add digital effects
- **Best For**: Post-production effects, compositing, advanced finishing

## Posing Tools and Stabilizers

### Adhesives and Stabilizers

**Blu-Tack / Sticky Tack** ($5):
- Reusable adhesive putty
- Holds lightweight objects in place
- Doesn't leave residue
- Easy to remove and reposition
- Best for: Small objects, lightweight characters

**Magnets** ($10-$20):
- Small neodymium magnets (strong, tiny)
- Place under set surface, attach to metal objects
- Or attach to character feet, use metal set floor
- Best for: Metal objects, creative rigging solutions

**Hot Glue** ($10 for gun + sticks):
- Temporary adhesion for props and set pieces
- Remove with rubbing alcohol
- Can damage delicate surfaces
- Best for: Set construction, securing props

**Tape** ($5-$10):
- Masking tape, painter's tape (low-tack, removable)
- Paper tape for delicate surfaces
- Double-sided tape for flat objects
- Best for: Marking positions, securing lightweight items

### Armatures

**Wire Armatures** (Beginner-Intermediate: $10-$50):
- **Aluminum Wire** (12-16 gauge): $10-$20
  - Bendable, holds shape
  - Wrap in masking tape for clay grip
  - Create skeleton matching character proportions
- **Armature Wire Kits**: $30-$50
  - Pre-cut lengths, various gauges
  - Includes tools (pliers, cutters)

**Building Wire Armature**:
1. Sketch character proportions
2. Cut wire to lengths (legs, arms, spine, neck)
3. Twist wires together at joints
4. Create loops for hands and feet (attachment points)
5. Wrap in masking tape or floral tape for clay grip
6. Test flexibility and strength
7. Apply clay or other materials over armature

**Ball-and-Socket Armatures** (Professional: $100-$500+):
- **Pre-Made Armatures**: $150-$300
  - Companies: Animation Supplies, Armaverse, Stop Motion Pro
  - Precision-machined joints
  - Hold poses firmly
  - Durable for long productions
- **Custom Armatures**: $300-$1,000+
  - Built to exact character specifications
  - Professional fabrication
  - Used in feature films

**Tie-Down Systems**:
- Drill holes in character feet
- Tap holes in set floor (threaded inserts)
- Screw character feet to floor between frames
- Provides maximum stability
- Used in professional productions

### Rigging and Supports

**Temporary Rigging**:
- Wire or rod supports attached to character
- Hold character in mid-air or difficult poses
- Remove in post-production (paint out or mask)
- Attach to hidden points (back, under clothing)

**Rigging Removal in Post**:
1. Shoot clean plate (frame without character)
2. In After Effects or Photoshop, layer clean plate over rigged frame
3. Mask out rig, reveal clean background
4. Repeat for all frames with visible rigs

## Stages and Backdrops

### Beginner Stages ($10-$50)

**Foam Board**:
- Large sheets from craft stores ($5-$10 each)
- Sturdy, flat surface
- Cut to size, paint or cover with paper
- Lightweight, easy to store

**Cardboard**:
- Free or very cheap
- Less sturdy than foam board
- Good for quick tests or disposable sets

**Setup**:
1. Create base (floor) from foam board
2. Add backdrop (vertical board or curved seamless)
3. Paint or cover with printed backgrounds
4. Secure with tape or glue

### Intermediate Stages ($50-$200)

**Portable Folding Tables**:
- Stable work surface
- Adjustable height
- Easy to move and store
- $50-$100

**Custom-Built Stages**:
- Plywood or MDF base
- Build walls, floors, modular pieces
- Paint, texture, detail
- $100-$200 in materials

**Green Screens**:
- Fabric or painted backdrop
- Chroma key in post-production
- Replace with digital backgrounds
- $30-$100 for fabric or paint

### Professional Stages ($200-$2,000+)

**Modular Set Systems**:
- Interchangeable walls, floors, pieces
- Professional construction (wood, plaster, paint)
- Detailed textures and props
- $500-$2,000+ depending on complexity

**Miniature Set Building**:
- Scale models of environments
- Forced perspective for depth
- Detailed painting and weathering
- Requires model-making skills

**Lighting Integration**:
- Practical lights in set (lamps, windows)
- Wire LED lights into set pieces
- Adds realism and atmosphere

## Maintenance and Care

### Camera Care
- Keep lens clean (microfiber cloth, lens cleaner)
- Store in dry environment (silica gel packets)
- Avoid extreme temperatures
- Use lens cap when not shooting
- Clean sensor periodically (professional service or DIY kit)

### Lighting Care
- Allow bulbs to cool before moving
- Store in protective cases
- Check for flickering (replace bulbs if needed)
- Keep diffusion materials clean

### Armature Care
- Avoid over-bending (metal fatigue)
- Store flat or hanging to prevent warping
- Clean clay residue with solvent
- Tighten ball-and-socket joints if loosening

### Set Care
- Store in dry location
- Protect from dust (cover with cloth)
- Repair damage promptly
- Document set layout (photos) for reassembly
