# Coalition Building & Grassroots Advocacy

## Coalition Strategy
1. Map potential partners across sectors (business, nonprofit, community, labor)
2. Assess alignment of policy positions and willingness to collaborate
3. Develop governance structure: lead organization, decision process, resource sharing
4. Create unified messaging platform accommodating member priorities
5. Coordinate joint advocacy activities (hill days, sign-on letters, media)
6. Manage ongoing coalition communications and conflict resolution

## Grassroots Mobilization
- Develop action alerts with legislator contact info, talking points, and message templates
- Segment audiences for customized messaging (employees, customers, community)
- Coordinate social media amplification campaigns
- Organize in-district constituent meetings with legislators
- Track response rates and legislative impact in real time
- Escalate engagement tactics as deadlines approach

## Grasstops Engagement
- Identify high-influence individuals: executives, community leaders, major donors
- Develop personalized engagement plans for each grasstops advocate
- Facilitate direct conversations with policymakers
- Leverage personal relationships and community standing

## Grassroots Toolkit Components
- Talking points tailored to audience and issue
- Legislator contact information (office, phone, email)
- Sample messages for calls, emails, and social media
- FAQ addressing common questions and objections
- Tracking form to report constituent contacts

## Coalition Governance Best Practices
- Establish clear roles, decision-making processes, and communication protocols
- Hold regular coordination calls (weekly during active campaigns)
- Share intelligence and coordinate messaging to avoid contradictions
- Maintain member engagement through recognition and progress updates
