# Legislative Engagement

## Position Papers and Policy Briefs
- Concise (2-4 pages) documents articulating organizational positions
- Include supporting evidence, economic data, and stakeholder impact analysis
- Customize for each legislator with district-specific data

## Legislative Testimony
- Prepare oral remarks (typically 5 minutes) plus written statement
- Include supporting exhibits, data tables, and impact stories
- Structure: introduce organization → state position → present evidence → make specific ask

## Legislator Meeting Preparation
- Research official's positions, committee assignments, and recent votes
- Prepare briefing package with district-specific impact data
- Identify constituent stories that illustrate policy impact
- Define clear "ask" — specific action requested
- Follow up within 48 hours with thank-you and supporting materials

## Bill Analysis Framework
1. Identify relevant provisions affecting organizational operations
2. Quantify financial and operational impact
3. Assess likelihood of passage (committee status, co-sponsors, political dynamics)
4. Determine priority level and resource allocation
5. Develop amendment strategy if full opposition is unlikely

## Letters of Support/Opposition
- Address to relevant committee chair or floor leader
- State position clearly in first paragraph
- Provide 2-3 supporting arguments with evidence
- Include specific impact data
- Close with explicit request for action
