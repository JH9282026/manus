# Government Relations & Public Affairs

Strategically engage government stakeholders, advocate for policy positions, and navigate regulatory environments to advance organizational interests.

## Overview

This skill covers the full spectrum of government affairs: lobbying strategy, legislative engagement, regulatory rulemaking responses, coalition building, grassroots mobilization, PAC management, and compliance with lobbying disclosure requirements. It ensures all advocacy maintains legal compliance while maximizing policy influence.

## Government Relations Strategy Framework

### Annual Planning
1. Define policy priorities ranked by organizational impact
2. Map legislative and regulatory calendars
3. Identify key decision-makers and their positions
4. Develop stakeholder engagement plans per official
5. Set budget, metrics, and success criteria
6. Coordinate with communications for consistent messaging

### Stakeholder Mapping
- Categorize officials by: supportive, persuadable, neutral, opposed
- Track committee assignments, voting records, and staff contacts
- Assess influence relationships between officials
- Identify champion candidates for priority issues

## Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Law/Rule | Key Obligation |
|-------------|----------|---------------|
| Federal lobbying registration | Lobbying Disclosure Act | Register, report quarterly |
| State lobbying | State-specific laws | Register per jurisdiction |
| PAC compliance | FECA | Contribution limits, FEC reporting |
| Gift and ethics rules | Congressional/agency rules | Gift limits, cooling-off periods |
| Foreign agent registration | FARA | If representing foreign interests |
