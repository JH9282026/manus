# Validation Methods

Systematic approaches to testing and validating business model hypotheses.

---

## Validation Hierarchy

### Three Types of Risk
1. **Desirability Risk**: Do customers want this?
2. **Feasibility Risk**: Can we build/deliver this?
3. **Viability Risk**: Can we make money?

### Testing Priority
Test in order of risk and cost:
1. Desirability (low cost to test)
2. Feasibility (medium cost)
3. Viability (requires actual operations)

---

## Validation Methods

### Customer Discovery Interviews
**Purpose:** Understand customer problems and needs

**Best Practices:**
- Focus on past behavior, not future intentions
- Ask about specific situations
- Avoid leading questions
- Listen more than talk

**Sample Questions:**
- Tell me about the last time you [experienced problem]
- What have you tried to solve this?
- What's most frustrating about current solutions?
- How much time/money does this cost you?

**Sample Size:** 10-30 interviews per segment

### Landing Page Tests
**Purpose:** Test value proposition resonance

**Elements to Test:**
- Headlines and messaging
- Value propositions
- Pricing displays
- Call-to-action effectiveness

**Metrics:**
- Conversion rate (email signup)
- Time on page
- Scroll depth
- Click-through on CTAs

**Validation Threshold:** 5-10% email conversion suggests interest

### Concierge MVP
**Purpose:** Manually deliver value to validate solution

**Approach:**
- Deliver service manually, no automation
- Learn exactly what customers need
- Understand operational requirements
- Gather direct feedback

**Best For:** Service businesses, complex solutions

**Transition:** Automate as you learn what works

### Wizard of Oz MVP
**Purpose:** Appear automated, operate manually

**Approach:**
- Build front-end experience
- Handle back-end manually
- Test user experience and demand
- Validate before building technology

**Best For:** Technology-heavy products, AI/ML solutions

### Prototype Testing
**Purpose:** Validate product concepts

**Fidelity Levels:**
- Paper prototypes: Rough concepts
- Wireframes: Structure and flow
- Clickable prototypes: Interactive experience
- Functional prototypes: Core functionality

**What to Test:**
- Usability and navigation
- Feature understanding
- Value perception
- Purchase intent

### Pilot Programs
**Purpose:** Full model validation at limited scale

**Structure:**
- Limited customer set (10-50)
- Full product/service delivery
- Complete pricing and operations
- Intensive feedback collection

**Success Criteria:**
- Customer satisfaction (NPS >50)
- Willingness to pay
- Retention and referrals
- Operational metrics

## Hypothesis Framework

### Hypothesis Structure
```
We believe [customer segment]
Has [problem/need]
We can help by [solution]
We'll know we're right when [measurable outcome]
```

### Prioritization Matrix

| Hypothesis | Risk Level | Test Difficulty | Priority |
|------------|------------|-----------------|----------|
| Customer has problem | High | Low | 1 |
| Customer will pay | High | Medium | 2 |
| We can deliver solution | Medium | Medium | 3 |
| Unit economics work | High | High | 4 |

### Experiment Design

**For Each Hypothesis:**
- Specific hypothesis statement
- Test method selected
- Success/failure criteria defined
- Sample size determined
- Timeline established
- Resources allocated

### Pivot Triggers

Consider pivoting when:
- Customer interviews reveal different problem
- Landing page tests show low interest
- Pilot customers won't pay target price
- Unit economics don't work at scale
- Competition makes differentiation impossible

**Pivot Types:**
- Customer segment pivot
- Problem pivot
- Solution pivot
- Channel pivot
- Revenue model pivot
- Technology pivot
