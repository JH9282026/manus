# Personal Finance & Wealth Management

## Overview

This skill provides comprehensive guidance and best practices for this domain.

## Skill Description and Purpose

The Personal Finance & Wealth Management skill empowers AI agents to deliver comprehensive financial guidance, analysis, and strategic planning across all dimensions of personal financial health. This skill encompasses the full spectrum of money management—from daily budgeting and expense tracking to sophisticated investment portfolio construction, retirement optimization, and multi-generational wealth transfer strategies.

This skill serves as a financial advisor capability, enabling agents to analyze complex financial situations, identify optimization opportunities, create actionable financial plans, and provide ongoing monitoring and adjustment recommendations. The skill integrates quantitative financial modeling with behavioral finance principles to deliver personalized, actionable guidance.

**Core Value Propositions:**
- Transform raw financial data into actionable insights and strategic recommendations
- Optimize across multiple financial dimensions simultaneously (taxes, investments, debt, insurance)
- Provide scenario modeling for major financial decisions (home purchase, career change, early retirement)
- Enable progress tracking toward financial independence and specific monetary goals
- Identify inefficiencies, risks, and opportunities within existing financial structures

**When to Deploy This Skill:**
- Users seeking comprehensive financial health assessments
- Investment portfolio analysis and rebalancing recommendations
- Retirement readiness evaluation and optimization strategies
- Tax planning and tax-loss harvesting opportunities
- Debt payoff strategy optimization (avalanche vs. snowball methods)
- Major purchase or life event financial planning
- FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) pathway analysis
- Estate planning and wealth transfer structuring

## Required Inputs/Parameters

### Primary Financial Data Inputs

**Income Information:**
- Gross annual income (salary, wages, self-employment)
- Additional income streams (rental income, dividends, interest, side businesses)
- Income frequency and payment schedule
- Expected income growth rate or trajectory
- Bonus and variable compensation structures

**Expense Data:**
- Monthly fixed expenses (housing, utilities, insurance premiums, subscriptions)
- Variable expenses (groceries, transportation, entertainment, dining)
- Discretionary spending categories and amounts
- Debt payment obligations (minimum payments and current payment amounts)
- Historical spending patterns (3-12 months preferred)

**Asset Information:**
- Cash and liquid savings (checking, savings, money market accounts)
- Investment accounts with holdings detail:
  * Taxable brokerage accounts (individual stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, bonds)
  * Retirement accounts (401k, 403b, IRA, Roth IRA, SEP IRA, Solo 401k)
  * Health Savings Accounts (HSA)
- Real estate holdings (primary residence, rental properties, REITs)
- Alternative investments (cryptocurrency, commodities, private equity)
- Business ownership interests and valuations
- Personal property of significant value

**Liability Information:**
- Mortgage details (balance, interest rate, term, payment amount)
- Student loans (balance, rate, repayment plan type)
- Auto loans and leases
- Credit card balances and interest rates
- Personal loans and lines of credit
- Other obligations (alimony, child support, tax liens)

**Insurance Coverage:**
- Life insurance (term, whole, universal) with coverage amounts and premiums
- Disability insurance (short-term, long-term) coverage details
- Health insurance (plan type, deductibles, out-of-pocket maximums)
- Property and casualty insurance
- Umbrella liability coverage

**Goals and Preferences:**
- Target retirement age and desired retirement lifestyle
- Specific financial goals with timelines (home purchase, education funding, travel)
- Risk tolerance assessment (conservative, moderate, aggressive)
- Investment preferences or restrictions (ESG, sector exclusions)
- Legacy and estate planning objectives

## Expected Outputs/Deliverables

### Comprehensive Financial Analysis Report

**Net Worth Statement:**
- Detailed asset inventory with current valuations
- Complete liability schedule with payoff projections
- Net worth calculation with historical trend analysis
- Asset allocation breakdown by category and tax treatment

**Cash Flow Analysis:**
- Monthly income vs. expense breakdown
- Savings rate calculation (target: 20%+ of gross income)
- Discretionary spending identification
- Cash flow optimization recommendations

**Budget Framework:**
- Zero-based budget template aligned with goals
- Category-specific spending targets
- Emergency fund adequacy assessment (3-6 months expenses minimum)
- Automated savings and investment allocation plan

### Investment Strategy Deliverables

**Portfolio Analysis:**
- Current asset allocation vs. target allocation
- Expense ratio analysis across all holdings
- Tax efficiency assessment (asset location optimization)
- Diversification score and concentration risk identification
- Performance attribution and benchmark comparison

**Investment Recommendations:**
- Specific fund or ETF recommendations with ticker symbols
- Rebalancing action items with share quantities
- Tax-loss harvesting opportunities with wash sale considerations
- Dollar-cost averaging schedule for new contributions

**Retirement Projections:**
- Monte Carlo simulation results (success probability)
- Safe withdrawal rate analysis (4% rule and dynamic strategies)
- Social Security optimization strategies (claiming age analysis)
- Required savings rate to achieve retirement goals
- Retirement income sources breakdown

### Tax Optimization Deliverables

**Tax Planning Strategies:**
- Marginal and effective tax rate calculations
- Tax-advantaged account contribution optimization
- Roth conversion ladder analysis
- Capital gains harvesting opportunities
- Charitable giving strategies (donor-advised funds, QCDs)

**Debt Management Plan:**
- Debt payoff priority ranking (avalanche method for interest savings)
- Refinancing opportunity analysis
- Debt consolidation evaluation
- Payoff timeline projections with accelerated payment scenarios

### Goal Tracking Dashboard

**Progress Metrics:**
- Financial independence number and progress percentage
- Goal-specific funding status
- Net worth growth trajectory
- Savings rate trends
- Investment return performance

## Example Use Cases

### Use Case 1: FIRE Pathway Analysis

A 35-year-old software engineer earning $180,000 annually seeks to achieve financial independence by age 50. The agent analyzes current savings of $450,000 across 401k, Roth IRA, and taxable accounts. Output includes: required savings rate of 45% ($81,000/year), optimal asset allocation (80% equities/20% bonds), Roth conversion ladder strategy for early access to retirement funds, projected FI number of $2.1M based on $84,000 annual expenses, and Monte Carlo simulation showing 92% success probability with current trajectory.

### Use Case 2: Debt Payoff Optimization

A household with $45,000 in student loans (5.5% rate), $12,000 credit card debt (19.99% APR), and $8,000 auto loan (4.5% rate) seeks optimal payoff strategy. The agent recommends avalanche method prioritizing credit card debt, calculates $3,847 interest savings vs. snowball method, provides month-by-month payoff schedule, identifies $400/month additional payment capacity from expense optimization, and projects debt-free date of 28 months with accelerated payments.

### Use Case 3: Retirement Readiness Assessment

A 58-year-old couple with combined retirement savings of $1.2M seeks to retire at 62. The agent analyzes Social Security benefits (recommending delayed claiming to age 67 for 24% increase), evaluates pension options, models healthcare costs until Medicare eligibility, stress-tests portfolio against sequence-of-returns risk, and recommends shifting to 60/40 allocation with 2-year cash buffer strategy.

### Use Case 4: Investment Portfolio Rebalancing

An investor's portfolio has drifted from 70/30 to 82/18 stocks/bonds allocation after market gains. The agent identifies specific rebalancing trades, prioritizes selling in taxable accounts for tax-loss harvesting, recommends directing new 401k contributions to bonds, calculates tax implications of each trade, and provides specific share quantities to sell/buy across 12 holdings.

### Use Case 5: Tax-Efficient Withdrawal Strategy

A retiree with $800,000 in traditional IRA, $200,000 in Roth IRA, and $150,000 in taxable accounts needs $60,000 annual income. The agent designs withdrawal sequence to minimize lifetime taxes, recommends Roth conversions up to 22% bracket ceiling, identifies qualified dividend and long-term capital gains harvesting at 0% rate, and projects $127,000 tax savings over 25-year retirement horizon.

## Prerequisites or Dependencies

### Required Tools and Access

**Financial Calculation Capabilities:**
- Compound interest and time-value-of-money calculations
- Monte Carlo simulation engine for retirement projections
- Tax bracket and marginal rate calculations (federal and state)
- Amortization schedule generation
- Internal rate of return (IRR) and net present value (NPV) calculations

**Data Sources:**
- Current tax brackets and contribution limits (updated annually)
- Historical market return data for projection modeling
- Social Security benefit calculation formulas
- State-specific tax rules and regulations
- Current interest rates (federal funds rate, mortgage rates, savings rates)

**Reference Frameworks:**
- Modern Portfolio Theory for asset allocation
- Trinity Study and safe withdrawal rate research
- Tax-loss harvesting rules including wash sale regulations
- Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) tables
- Estate tax exemption limits and rules

### Knowledge Prerequisites

**Financial Concepts:**
- Understanding of tax-advantaged account types and rules
- Investment vehicle characteristics (stocks, bonds, ETFs, mutual funds, REITs)
- Insurance product structures and coverage analysis
- Debt instruments and interest calculations
- Estate planning vehicles (trusts, beneficiary designations)

**Regulatory Knowledge:**
- IRS contribution limits and income phase-outs
- Early withdrawal penalties and exceptions
- Roth conversion rules and pro-rata calculations
- Social Security benefit calculations and claiming strategies
- Medicare enrollment rules and IRMAA surcharges

### User Prerequisites

- Willingness to provide comprehensive financial data
- Access to account statements and financial documents
- Clear articulation of financial goals and risk tolerance
- Understanding that recommendations are educational, not licensed financial advice
- Commitment to implementing and maintaining recommended strategies
