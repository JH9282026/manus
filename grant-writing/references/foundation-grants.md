# Foundation & Corporate Grants

## Letter of Inquiry (LOI)
- 1-3 pages summarizing organization, need, proposed project, and funding request
- Match tone to funder (formal for large foundations, warmer for community)
- Include: organization intro, problem statement, solution summary, request amount, alignment

## Foundation Application Process
1. LOI submitted → Foundation reviews → Invitation to full proposal (if successful)
2. Full proposal with extended narrative, budget, supporting documents
3. Site visit by foundation staff (tour programs, meet leadership, review financials)
4. Funding decision communicated
5. Grant agreement signed, funds disbursed per schedule

## Relationship Building with Funders

### Before Applying
- Research thoroughly; review 990s, annual reports, past grantees
- Attend funder information sessions
- Request pre-submission meeting if offered

### During Application
- Submit polished, complete applications on time
- Respond promptly to information requests
- Be transparent about challenges

### After Decision
- Thank funders regardless of outcome
- Ask for feedback on declined proposals
- Submit reports on time with compelling impact data
- Share success stories and invite to events

## Corporate Grant Writing
- Highlight business alignment with corporate priorities
- Describe employee involvement opportunities
- Outline brand visibility and recognition possibilities
- Quantify measurable community impact
- Address CSR/ESG themes relevant to company
