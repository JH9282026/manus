# Government & Research Grants

## Federal Grant Application Requirements

### Standard Forms
- SF-424 (Application for Federal Assistance)
- SF-424A (Budget Information)
- SF-424B (Assurances)

### Common Attachments
- Project narrative addressing each review criterion
- Budget and budget justification
- Organizational chart and key staff resumes
- Letters of support from partners
- Logic model and work plan
- Evaluation plan with data collection methods

## NOFO/RFA Analysis
1. Read full announcement multiple times
2. Create compliance checklist of every requirement
3. Map review criteria to proposal sections
4. Note page limits, formatting rules, and submission method
5. Identify eligibility requirements and match requirements
6. Note deadlines — submit 48+ hours early

## Peer Review Process
- Reviewers score against published criteria (typically significance, approach, innovation, capacity)
- Numerical scoring (1-5 or 1-9) with criteria weighting
- Make proposal reviewer-friendly: use headers matching criteria, bold key points

## Research Grant Components

### Literature Review
- Position research within existing knowledge; identify gaps

### Research Questions / Hypotheses
- "This study examines whether [intervention] improves [outcome] among [population]"

### Methodology
- Study design, sampling, data collection instruments, analysis methods
- Address validity, reliability, and generalizability

### Human Subjects Protection
- IRB approval process, informed consent, privacy protections, risk mitigation

## Federal Budget Development
- **Personnel**: Salary + fringe benefits, % time on project
- **Consultants**: Fees, expertise justification
- **Travel**: Per diem rates, purpose justification
- **Equipment**: Items over threshold (often $5,000), useful life
- **Supplies**: Consumable materials
- **Other**: Subcontracts, participant costs, printing
- **Indirect costs**: Negotiated rate or de minimis (10%)

## Grant Reporting Best Practices
- Submit on time or early
- Be honest about challenges and adaptations
- Quantify achievements with outcome data
- Include compelling beneficiary stories
- Connect activities to stated objectives
- Request budget modifications before spending changes
