# Guerrilla Marketing Campaign Execution

## Complete Execution Framework

This reference provides a comprehensive, step-by-step guide to planning, executing, and measuring guerrilla marketing campaigns from concept to completion.

---

## Phase 1: Strategic Planning

### Step 1: Define Campaign Objectives

**SMART Goal Framework**:

Every guerrilla campaign should have Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound objectives.

**Example Objectives**:
- Increase brand awareness by 40% among 18-34 year-olds in London within 3 months
- Generate 10,000 social media mentions using campaign hashtag within 2 weeks
- Acquire 5,000 email subscribers through experiential activation within 1 month
- Drive 25% increase in foot traffic to retail location during campaign period
- Generate £50,000 in earned media value within campaign window

**Objective Categories**:

1. **Awareness Objectives**:
   - Brand recognition
   - Reach and impressions
   - Media coverage
   - Social media mentions

2. **Engagement Objectives**:
   - Participation rates
   - Time spent with brand
   - User-generated content
   - Social media engagement

3. **Conversion Objectives**:
   - Lead generation
   - Sales increases
   - Trial/sampling
   - App downloads

4. **Relationship Objectives**:
   - Community building
   - Brand sentiment
   - Customer loyalty
   - Advocacy development

### Step 3: Concept Development

**Ideation Process**:

1. **Brainstorming Session**:
   - Gather diverse team members
   - Set "no bad ideas" ground rules
   - Generate 50+ initial concepts
   - Build on others' ideas
   - Document everything

2. **Concept Filtering**:
   - Alignment with brand values
   - Relevance to objectives
   - Feasibility assessment
   - Budget compatibility
   - Legal/ethical screening
   - Narrow to top 5-10 concepts

3. **Concept Development**:
   - Flesh out execution details
   - Identify required resources
   - Map customer journey
   - Plan documentation strategy
   - Consider amplification opportunities

4. **Concept Testing**:
   - Present to focus groups
   - Gather feedback from stakeholders
   - Test with small pilot if possible
   - Refine based on input
   - Select final concept

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Brand Alignment | 20% | 1-10 |
| Surprise Factor | 15% | 1-10 |
| Shareability | 15% | 1-10 |
| Feasibility | 15% | 1-10 |
| Budget Fit | 15% | 1-10 |
| Legal/Ethical | 10% | Pass/Fail |
| Emotional Impact | 10% | 1-10 |

**ROI Calculation**:

**Traditional ROI**:
```
ROI = [(Revenue - Investment) / Investment] × 100

Example:
Revenue: £25,000
Investment: £5,000
ROI = [(£25,000 - £5,000) / £5,000] × 100 = 400%
```

**CLTV-Based ROI**:
```
ROI = [(Total CLTV - Investment) / Investment] × 100

Example:
New Customers: 200
Average CLTV: £150
Total CLTV: £30,000
Investment: £5,000
ROI = [(£30,000 - £5,000) / £5,000] × 100 = 500%
```

**Earned Media Value**:
```
EMV = (Impressions × CPM) / 1,000

Example:
Impressions: 5,000,000
CPM: £10
EMV = (5,000,000 × £10) / 1,000 = £50,000
```

## Execution Best Practices

### Do's

✓ **Over-communicate**: Keep all stakeholders informed throughout
✓ **Document everything**: Capture more content than you think you need
✓ **Stay flexible**: Be ready to adapt to unexpected situations
✓ **Prioritize safety**: Never compromise public or team safety
✓ **Engage authentically**: Create genuine connections with participants
✓ **Monitor continuously**: Track metrics and sentiment in real-time
✓ **Thank everyone**: Show appreciation to team, partners, and participants
✓ **Learn constantly**: Treat every campaign as a learning opportunity
✓ **Plan contingencies**: Have backup plans for critical elements
✓ **Celebrate success**: Recognize team effort and achievements

### Don'ts

✗ **Don't wing it**: Thorough planning is essential for success
✗ **Don't ignore permits**: Legal compliance protects your brand
✗ **Don't skimp on insurance**: Protect against liability
✗ **Don't forget cleanup**: Leave locations better than you found them
✗ **Don't ignore feedback**: Listen to criticism and learn from it
✗ **Don't overpromise**: Set realistic expectations with stakeholders
✗ **Don't neglect follow-up**: Capitalize on momentum after campaign
✗ **Don't repeat mistakes**: Learn from failures and iterate
✗ **Don't sacrifice quality**: Maintain professional standards throughout
✗ **Don't forget the objective**: Keep campaign goals front and center

## Conclusion

Successful guerrilla marketing campaign execution requires meticulous planning, creative problem-solving, and flawless execution. By following this comprehensive framework—from strategic planning through post-campaign optimization—you can maximize the impact of your guerrilla marketing efforts while minimizing risks and challenges.

Remember that every campaign is a learning opportunity. Document your processes, analyze your results, and continuously refine your approach. With each campaign, you'll build expertise, develop best practices, and create increasingly effective guerrilla marketing activations.

The key to execution excellence is balancing careful planning with the flexibility to adapt in real-time. Stay true to your brand, prioritize authentic engagement, and always keep your audience at the center of your strategy.
