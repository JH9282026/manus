# Macroeconomic Indicators

Traditional economic factors affecting cryptocurrency markets.

---

## Inflation Indicators

### Consumer Price Index (CPI)
- Monthly release, high market impact
- Above expectations → risk-off (crypto down)
- Below expectations → risk-on (crypto up)

### Crypto Correlation
- Bitcoin narrative as "inflation hedge"
- Historical performance mixed
- Correlation increases during macro uncertainty

---

## Interest Rates

### Federal Reserve Impact
- Rate hikes → liquidity reduction → risk assets down
- Rate cuts → liquidity increase → risk assets up
- Forward guidance matters as much as action

### Correlation Patterns
| Environment | Crypto Impact |
|-------------|---------------|
| Rising rates | Negative pressure |
| Peak rates | Neutral to positive |
| Falling rates | Positive |
| QE (money printing) | Strongly positive |
| QT (balance sheet reduction) | Negative |

---

## Liquidity Metrics

### M2 Money Supply
- Expansion correlates with crypto rallies
- Contraction correlates with drawdowns
- Global M2 more relevant than US alone

### Dollar Index (DXY)
- Strong dollar → crypto pressure
- Weak dollar → crypto tailwind
- Inverse correlation typical

---

## Traditional Market Correlation

### Stock Market
| Index | BTC Correlation |
|-------|----------------|
| Nasdaq | 0.5-0.8 (high) |
| S&P 500 | 0.4-0.7 |
| Gold | Variable |

### Correlation Regimes
- Risk-on: Crypto follows tech stocks
- Risk-off: Correlation increases
- Crypto-specific: Lower correlation

---

## Key Data Releases

### High Impact
- FOMC decisions (8x/year)
- CPI release (monthly)
- Jobs report (monthly)
- GDP (quarterly)

### Trading Implications
- Reduce position before major releases
- Wider stops during announcements
- Wait for settlement before new positions
