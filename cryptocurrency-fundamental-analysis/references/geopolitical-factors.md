# Geopolitical Factors

Regulatory developments and geopolitical impacts on cryptocurrency.

---

## Regulatory Landscape

### Major Jurisdictions
| Region | Approach | Key Issues |
|--------|----------|------------|
| US | Enforcement-led | Securities classification, stablecoin regulation |
| EU | MiCA framework | Comprehensive regulation |
| China | Banned | Mining ban, trading prohibition |
| Japan | Licensed | Exchange regulation |
| Singapore | Permissive | Clear licensing |

### Regulatory Risk Assessment
- Security vs. commodity classification
- Exchange licensing requirements
- Stablecoin reserve requirements
- DeFi regulatory approach
- Tax reporting obligations

---

## Sanctions and Capital Controls

### Crypto as Escape Valve
- Capital flight during crises
- Sanctions evasion concerns
- Remittance corridors
- Currency devaluation hedge

### Historical Examples
- Russia-Ukraine: USDT premium in affected regions
- Iran: Mining for sanction circumvention
- Venezuela: Bitcoin adoption during hyperinflation
- Lebanon: Stablecoin adoption during banking crisis

## Crisis Response Patterns

### Initial Reaction
- Risk-off: Crypto sells with risk assets
- Flight to stables: USDT/USDC demand
- Exchange outflows

### Secondary Reaction (weeks)
- Alternative asset narrative
- Recovery often stronger than traditional
- Regional premiums emerge

### Investment Implications
- Scale in during crisis drawdowns
- Monitor exchange flows
- Watch stablecoin premiums
- Extended time horizons
