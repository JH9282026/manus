# Tokenomics Analysis

Framework for evaluating cryptocurrency economic design.

---

## Supply Analysis

### Supply Types
- **Fixed Supply**: Bitcoin (21M cap)
- **Inflationary**: Ethereum (pre-merge)
- **Deflationary**: Ethereum (post-merge with burns)
- **Elastic**: Algorithmic supply adjustment

### Emission Models
| Model | Example | Characteristic |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| Halving | Bitcoin | Supply shock every 4 years |
| Linear | Early tokens | Predictable inflation |
| Burn Mechanism | BNB, ETH | Demand-driven deflation |
| Mint/Burn | Stablecoins | Peg maintenance |

## Distribution Assessment

### Allocation Categories
- Public sale (ICO, IEO, IDO)
- Private sale/investors
- Team and founders
- Advisors
- Ecosystem/treasury
- Mining/staking rewards

### Healthy Benchmarks
- Team < 20% with 4+ year vesting
- 1-year cliff common
- Linear vesting post-cliff
- No large single unlock events

### Red Flags
- >30% insider allocation
- Short vesting (<2 years)
- Large cliff unlocks
- Concentrated whale wallets

| Governance | Voting rights | UNI, AAVE |

- Required for platform use?
- Staking yield attractiveness?

## Valuation Framework

### Market Cap Analysis
```
Market Cap = Price × Circulating Supply
FDV = Price × Max Supply
MC/FDV Ratio = Dilution indicator
```

### NVT Ratio
```
NVT = Network Value / Daily Transaction Volume
```
- High NVT: Overvalued or store of value
- Low NVT: Undervalued or high utility
