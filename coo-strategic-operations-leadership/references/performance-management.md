# Performance Management

Frameworks for measuring and improving organizational performance.

---

## Balanced Scorecard

### Four Perspectives
1. **Financial**: Revenue, profitability, cost efficiency
2. **Customer**: Satisfaction, retention, acquisition
3. **Internal Process**: Quality, efficiency, innovation
4. **Learning & Growth**: Skills, culture, systems

### Strategy Map
- Cause-and-effect relationships
- Leading and lagging indicators
- Strategic objectives by perspective
- Initiative alignment

---

## OKR Framework

### Structure
- **Objective**: Qualitative, inspirational goal
- **Key Results**: 3-5 measurable outcomes

### Example
```
Objective: Achieve operational excellence in manufacturing

KR1: Reduce defect rate from 2% to 0.5%
KR2: Improve on-time delivery from 85% to 98%
KR3: Decrease cycle time by 20%
KR4: Achieve 90% employee engagement score
```

### Cadence
- Annual: Company OKRs
- Quarterly: Team OKRs
- Weekly: Check-ins and scoring

---

## KPI Dashboard Design

### KPI Selection Criteria
- Aligned with strategy
- Measurable and timely
- Actionable
- Owned by someone
- Balanced (leading/lagging)

### Dashboard Principles
- Visual clarity
- Real-time or near real-time
- Drill-down capability
- Exception highlighting
- Trend visibility

### Tiered Structure
- Tier 1: Executive metrics (5-7)
- Tier 2: Department metrics (15-20)
- Tier 3: Operational metrics (30-50)
