# Industry-Specific Presentation Formats

Create presentations tailored to the standards, expectations, and audience norms of specific industries and professional contexts.

## Overview

A pitch deck for VCs looks nothing like a medical conference poster, which looks nothing like a government regulatory briefing. Each industry has established conventions for slide count, data presentation, visual style, and narrative structure. Violating these conventions signals inexperience and reduces credibility. This skill provides industry-specific frameworks for the most common high-stakes presentation formats.

## VC Pitch Deck Framework

### Standard Slide Sequence

| Slide # | Title | Content | Time |
|---------|-------|---------|------|
| 1 | Title/Hook | Company name, one-line description, logo | 30s |
| 2 | Problem | Pain point with quantified impact | 1–2 min |
| 3 | Solution | Your product/approach (show, don't tell) | 2 min |
| 4 | Demo/Product | Screenshots, video, or live demo | 2–3 min |
| 5 | Market Size | TAM → SAM → SOM with sources | 1 min |
| 6 | Business Model | How you make money, unit economics | 1–2 min |
| 7 | Traction | Key metrics, growth chart, logos | 2 min |
| 8 | Go-to-Market | Customer acquisition strategy | 1 min |
| 9 | Competition | Differentiation matrix (not just 2×2) | 1 min |
| 10 | Team | Founders with relevant experience | 1 min |
| 11 | Financials | Revenue projection, key assumptions | 1 min |
| 12 | The Ask | Funding amount, use of funds, timeline | 1 min |

### Pitch Deck Design Rules
- Maximum 30 words per slide (investors read the deck, not the slides)
- One idea per slide — if you need two ideas, make two slides
- Use charts over tables — visual data is processed 60,000× faster
- Include a "traction" slide even if early — show any signal of product-market fit
- Avoid clip art, gradients, and 3D charts — clean and modern design only

## Sales Deck Framework

### Consultative Sales Deck Structure

| Section | Slides | Purpose | Key Element |
|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Hook | 1–2 | Capture attention with buyer's problem | Industry-specific pain point data |
| Context | 2–3 | Show understanding of their world | Market trends, competitive landscape |
| Solution | 3–5 | How your product solves their problem | Demo, screenshots, architecture diagram |
| Proof | 2–3 | Evidence it works for similar companies | Case studies, ROI data, testimonials |
| Proposal | 1–2 | Pricing, implementation, next steps | Clear CTA and timeline |

### Sales Deck by Deal Size

| Deal Size | Deck Style | Decision Maker | Emphasis |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| <$10K | Self-serve / short deck (5–8 slides) | Individual buyer | Speed, ease, ROI |
| $10K–$100K | Standard consultative (12–15 slides) | Director/VP | ROI, integration, support |
| $100K–$1M | Executive narrative (15–20 slides) | C-suite | Strategic value, risk mitigation |
| >$1M | Custom proposal document (20+ slides) | Board/committee | Business case, change management |

## Medical/Scientific Presentation Framework

### Standard Conference Presentation Structure

| Section | Slides | Content | Design Note |
|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Title | 1 | Study title, authors, affiliations, disclosures | Required format per conference |
| Background | 2–3 | Context, existing literature, knowledge gap | Include key citations |
| Objective | 1 | Clear research question or hypothesis | One slide, one question |
| Methods | 3–5 | Study design, population, procedures, analysis | Flowcharts, CONSORT diagrams |
| Results | 5–10 | Data with tables, figures, p-values, CI | Let data speak — minimal interpretation |
| Discussion | 2–3 | Interpretation, limitations, comparison | Balanced and transparent |
| Conclusions | 1 | Key takeaways (3–5 bullet points max) | Directly answer the objective |
| References | 1 | Key citations (not exhaustive) | Available upon request for full list |

### Scientific Presentation Rules
- All claims must be supported by cited evidence
- Include conflict of interest disclosure on title slide
- Use CONSORT/STROBE/PRISMA diagrams as appropriate
- P-values with confidence intervals, not just significance stars
- Avoid conclusions not supported by the data
- Use accessible color palettes for colorblind audience members

## Board Meeting Presentation

| Section | Slides | Content | Data Density |
|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Executive summary | 1–2 | Key decisions needed, top-line metrics | High — KPI dashboard |
| Financial performance | 3–5 | P&L, cash flow, variance analysis | Very high — tables and charts |
| Strategic updates | 3–5 | Progress on key initiatives | Medium — milestones and status |
| Risk assessment | 2–3 | Top risks, mitigation actions | Medium — risk matrix |
| Market/competitive | 2–3 | Market trends, competitive moves | Medium — selective data |
| Recommendations | 1–2 | Proposed decisions with supporting rationale | Low — clear asks |
| Appendix | Variable | Detailed backup data | Very high — reference only |

## Best Practices

- **Know the norms before you design** — research what's standard in the industry and venue
- **Respect time limits strictly** — overrunning is the fastest way to lose an audience
- **Rehearse with someone from the industry** — insider feedback catches norm violations
- **Provide handout/leave-behind versions** — more detailed than presentation slides, with speaker notes
- **Adapt visual density to audience** — scientists expect data density; investors expect clarity and simplicity
- **Always include a clear ask or next step** — end with what you want the audience to do
