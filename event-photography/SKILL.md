# Event Photography

Capture compelling images at live events with professional techniques for planning, shooting, and rapid delivery.

## Overview

Event photography documents live gatherings—weddings, corporate functions, conferences, concerts, and social celebrations. Success requires anticipating moments, mastering challenging lighting conditions, working efficiently under pressure, and delivering polished images quickly. This skill covers pre-event planning, on-site execution strategies, equipment selection, and streamlined post-processing workflows.

## Pre-Event Planning

### Client Consultation Checklist

- **Event Purpose & Type**: Wedding, corporate, conference, concert, private party
- **Must-Have Shots**: Key moments, VIPs, specific groups, sponsor visibility
- **Usage Rights**: Social media, press releases, commercial use, print materials
- **Delivery Timeline**: Same-day teasers, 24-hour turnaround, 72-hour full gallery
- **Special Requirements**: Branding guidelines, restricted areas, coordination with videographers

### Shot List Development

| Event Type | Essential Shots |
|------------|----------------|
| Corporate | Keynote speakers, networking moments, branding/signage, group photos, candid interactions |
| Wedding | Ceremony key moments, family portraits, reception details, first dance, candid emotions |
| Conference | Registration area, attendees networking, presentations, exhibition booths, panel discussions |
| Concert/Performance | Stage action, crowd energy, lighting effects, artist close-ups, venue atmosphere |

### Venue Scouting

Arrive 30-60 minutes early to:

- Identify optimal shooting positions for key moments
- Test lighting conditions in different areas
- Locate power outlets for charging station
- Plan movement paths to avoid disrupting event flow
- Capture empty venue shots for context
- Coordinate with event staff and other photographers

## Equipment Selection

### Camera Bodies

**Essential Features**:
- Excellent low-light autofocus (Sony A7 IV, Canon R6 III, Nikon Z6 III)
- Dual card slots for redundancy (simultaneous backup recording)
- High ISO performance (clean images at ISO 3200-6400)
- Fast burst mode (20+ fps for fleeting moments)
- Long battery life or quick-swap batteries
- Weather sealing for outdoor events

**Recommended Setup**: Two camera bodies to avoid lens changes during critical moments.

### Lens Strategy

| Focal Length | Purpose | Recommended Aperture |
|--------------|---------|---------------------|
| 16-35mm | Venue establishing shots, large groups, environmental context | f/2.8 |
| 24-70mm | General coverage, candid moments, medium groups | f/2.8 |
| 70-200mm | Stage reach, distant details, intimate candids without intrusion | f/2.8 |
| 35mm or 50mm | Low-light primes for reception/dim venues, beautiful bokeh | f/1.4-f/1.8 |

**Optimal Two-Body Setup**: 24-70mm f/2.8 on primary body, 70-200mm f/2.8 on secondary.

### Lighting Equipment

- **On-Camera Flash**: With bounce card or small diffuser for fill light
- **Off-Camera Speedlights**: For formal portraits and step-and-repeat setups
- **Compact LED Panels**: Continuous light for video-friendly illumination
- **Light Modifiers**: Small softbox or umbrella for softer flash output

### Essential Accessories

- **Memory Cards**: Fast CFexpress or UHS-II cards, 128GB+ capacity, bring 3-4 cards
- **Batteries**: 4-6 charged batteries minimum
- **Sling Strap**: Quick access to dual cameras
- **Portable Charger**: For staging area
- **Microfiber Cloth**: Lens cleaning
- **Gaffer Tape**: Emergency repairs
- **Business Cards**: Networking opportunities

## Camera Settings for Events

### Exposure Triangle Balance

**Shutter Speed Priority**:
- **Minimum**: 1/125s for stationary subjects
- **Recommended**: 1/250s for general event coverage
- **Fast Action**: 1/500s+ for dancing, sports, performances

**Aperture Strategy**:
- **Isolation**: f/1.4-f/2.8 for subject separation and low-light performance
- **Group Shots**: f/4-f/5.6 to ensure everyone is in focus
- **Large Groups**: f/8 for maximum depth of field

**ISO Management**:
- Start at ISO 100-400 in well-lit areas
- Push to ISO 1600-3200 in dim venues
- Modern cameras handle ISO 6400+ acceptably
- Use Auto ISO with minimum shutter speed set to 1/250s

### Shooting Mode Recommendations

**Manual Mode + Auto ISO**: Full control over aperture and shutter speed while camera adjusts ISO for changing light.

**Aperture Priority**: Set wide aperture (f/2.8), monitor shutter speed, increase ISO if it drops below 1/250s.

### Focus Settings

- **Continuous AF (AI Servo/AF-C)**: For moving subjects
- **Face/Eye Detection**: Modern mirrorless cameras excel at tracking
- **Single Point AF**: For precise control in crowded scenes
- **Back Button Focus**: Separate focus from shutter release for better control

### File Format

**RAW + JPEG**: RAW for editing flexibility, JPEG for quick client previews or social media.

## Low-Light Mastery

### Flash Techniques

**Bounced Flash**:
- Aim flash at white/neutral ceiling or wall
- Creates large, soft light source
- Reduces harsh shadows and red-eye
- Adjust flash exposure compensation (-1 to +1 EV)

**Direct Flash with Diffuser**:
- Use when no bounce surface available
- Attach small softbox or diffusion dome
- Reduces harshness but less effective than bounce

**Off-Camera Flash**:
- Position flash at 45° angle for dimensional lighting
- Use wireless triggers for flexibility
- Ideal for formal portraits and step-and-repeat

### Available Light Techniques

- **Embrace High ISO**: Modern sensors handle noise well
- **Stabilization**: Use IBIS and lens stabilization for slower shutter speeds
- **Find Light Sources**: Position subjects near windows, lamps, or stage lights
- **Expose for Highlights**: Protect bright areas, lift shadows in post

## Shooting Strategies

### Anticipation and Observation

- **Read Body Language**: Predict hugs, laughter, emotional moments
- **Position Strategically**: Be where action will happen before it happens
- **Watch for Patterns**: Speeches end with applause, toasts precede clinking glasses
- **Continuous Awareness**: Scan entire room, not just immediate area

### Composition Techniques

**Rule of Thirds**: Position subjects at intersection points for dynamic compositions.

**Layering**: Include foreground, subject, and background elements for depth.

**Framing**: Use doorways, windows, or people to frame main subject.

**Candid vs. Posed Balance**:
- **Candid**: 70-80% of shots—authentic emotions and interactions
- **Posed**: 20-30%—formal groups, VIP portraits, client-requested shots

### Movement and Angles

- **Change Elevation**: Shoot from high positions for crowd scenes, low for dynamic perspectives
- **Move Constantly**: Different angles reveal different stories
- **Get Close**: Capture intimate details and emotions
- **Environmental Context**: Wide shots establish venue and atmosphere

### Burst Mode Strategy

- **Use Selectively**: Critical moments like cake cutting, first kiss, award presentations
- **Avoid Overuse**: Creates excessive culling workload
- **3-5 Frame Bursts**: Usually sufficient for most moments

## On-Site Workflow

### Staging Area Setup

- **Location**: Near restrooms and dining, away from main action
- **Power Access**: For charging batteries and devices
- **Secure Storage**: Backup gear, extra cards, personal items
- **Quick Access**: Batteries, memory cards, cleaning supplies

### In-Camera Organization

- **Rate/Flag Images**: Mark keepers during downtime (speeches, dinner)
- **Protect Critical Shots**: Lock important images to prevent accidental deletion
- **Monitor Card Space**: Swap cards before they fill completely
- **Check Exposure**: Review histogram regularly, especially when lighting changes

### Professional Conduct

- **Dress Appropriately**: Match or slightly exceed event dress code
- **Move Discreetly**: Avoid blocking views or creating distractions
- **Respect Boundaries**: Don't interrupt moments for photos
- **Coordinate with Others**: Communicate with videographers and other photographers
- **Stay Alert**: Avoid eating, drinking, or phone use during key moments

## Post-Processing Workflow

### Immediate Culling (Within 2 Hours)

1. **Import to Fast Software**: Photo Mechanic or Lightroom
2. **First Pass**: Delete obvious rejects (closed eyes, severe blur, duplicates)
3. **Second Pass**: Rate keepers (1-star system or color labels)
4. **Select Best**: Flag hero images for priority editing

### Editing Strategy

**Batch Processing**:
- Create 2-3 presets for different lighting conditions
- Apply to similar images for consistency
- Adjust individual images as needed

**Key Adjustments**:
- **Exposure**: Consistent brightness across similar scenes
- **White Balance**: Correct color casts from mixed lighting
- **Skin Tones**: Natural, flattering rendering
- **Contrast**: Subtle enhancement without crushing blacks
- **Sharpening**: Moderate, avoid over-sharpening
- **Noise Reduction**: AI-powered tools (Topaz, DxO) for high-ISO images

**Avoid Over-Editing**:
- Maintain natural look
- Subtle adjustments over dramatic filters
- Consistent style throughout gallery

### Delivery Timeline

| Deliverable | Timeline | Purpose |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| Teaser (5-10 images) | Same day or next morning | Social media, immediate gratification |
| Full gallery (edited) | 48-72 hours | Client review and selection |
| Final high-res files | 1 week | Print-ready, archival quality |

### Export Settings

**Web Gallery**: JPEG, sRGB color space, 2000px long edge, quality 85-90

**High-Resolution**: JPEG or TIFF, Adobe RGB, full resolution, quality 95-100

**Social Media**: JPEG, sRGB, 1080-2048px, optimized for platform

## Common Challenges and Solutions

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Mixed lighting (tungsten + LED + daylight) | Shoot RAW, correct white balance in post, or use custom white balance |
| Crowded venue, limited movement | Use longer lens (70-200mm) to shoot over heads |
| Dark reception hall | Bounce flash, increase ISO to 3200-6400, use fast primes |
| Fast-moving subjects (dancing) | Shutter speed 1/500s+, continuous AF, burst mode |
| Harsh overhead lighting | Use flash to fill shadows, position subjects near better light |
| Client wants "everything" | Set expectations early, deliver 300-500 edited images for 4-hour event |
| Equipment failure | Always bring backup camera body and extra batteries |
