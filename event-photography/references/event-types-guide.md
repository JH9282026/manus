# Event Types Guide

Comprehensive coverage requirements and strategies for different event categories.

## Wedding Photography

### Timeline and Key Moments

**Pre-Ceremony (1-2 hours)**:
- Bride and bridesmaids getting ready
- Dress details (hanging, close-ups of lace/beading)
- Accessories (shoes, jewelry, bouquet, invitations)
- Groom and groomsmen preparation
- First look (if applicable)
- Family portraits before ceremony

**Ceremony (30-60 minutes)**:
- Venue details and guest arrival
- Processional (each person walking down aisle)
- Bride's entrance (multiple angles if possible)
- Exchange of vows and rings
- First kiss
- Recessional
- Confetti/petal toss or exit

**Post-Ceremony (30-45 minutes)**:
- Couple portraits in various locations
- Wedding party group shots
- Extended family combinations
- Creative couple shots

**Reception (3-4 hours)**:
- Venue and table details
- Grand entrance
- First dance
- Parent dances
- Toasts and speeches
- Cake cutting
- Bouquet and garter toss
- Dancing and candid moments
- Sparkler or grand exit

### Wedding-Specific Challenges

**Ceremony Restrictions**:
- Some venues prohibit flash during ceremony
- Limited photographer movement (stay in designated areas)
- Solution: Scout ceremony space early, use high ISO and fast lenses, position strategically

**Family Portrait Chaos**:
- Large families, unclear groupings, people disappearing
- Solution: Create detailed family portrait list with names, designate family "wrangler" to gather people

**Timeline Delays**:
- Weddings rarely run on schedule
- Solution: Build 15-30 minute buffer into timeline, prioritize must-have shots, communicate with coordinator

### Equipment Recommendations

- **Primary Body**: Full-frame with excellent low-light performance
- **Secondary Body**: Backup with different lens
- **Lenses**: 24-70mm f/2.8, 70-200mm f/2.8, 35mm or 50mm f/1.4
- **Flash**: On-camera speedlight with bounce capability
- **Backup Everything**: Second body, extra batteries, multiple memory cards

## Corporate Event Photography

### Event Types

**Conferences and Conventions**:
- Registration and check-in
- Keynote presentations
- Breakout sessions
- Exhibition hall and vendor booths
- Networking sessions
- Evening receptions

**Corporate Parties and Celebrations**:
- Company milestones, holiday parties, team building
- Branding and decor
- Executives and VIPs
- Employee interactions
- Entertainment or activities
- Awards presentations

**Product Launches and Press Events**:
- Product displays and demonstrations
- Media coverage and interviews
- Executive presentations
- Attendee reactions
- Step-and-repeat photography

### Corporate Photography Priorities

**Branding Visibility**:
- Capture logos, signage, branded materials prominently
- Include sponsor recognition
- Show company colors and visual identity

**Stakeholder Coverage**:
- Executives and board members
- Key clients and partners
- Award recipients
- Speakers and panelists

**Diversity and Inclusion**:
- Represent diverse attendees
- Show inclusive environment
- Avoid tokenism—capture authentic interactions

**Professional Presentation**:
- Clean, polished editing style
- Flattering but natural portraits
- Avoid unflattering candids (eating, awkward expressions)
- Suitable for corporate communications and press releases

### Shot List Template

1. **Venue and Setup** (before attendees arrive)
2. **Registration and Arrival** (people checking in, networking)
3. **Keynote/Main Presentation** (speaker at podium, audience reactions, slides if visible)
4. **Breakout Sessions** (multiple rooms, variety of speakers)
5. **Exhibition/Vendor Area** (booths, demonstrations, interactions)
6. **Networking** (small groups conversing, business card exchanges)
7. **Meals and Breaks** (catering setup, people dining and socializing)
8. **Awards or Recognition** (presentations, recipients, applause)
9. **Executive Portraits** (formal shots of leadership)
10. **Closing and Departure** (final remarks, attendees leaving)

### Delivery Considerations

**Fast Turnaround**:
- Corporate clients often need images within 24 hours for press releases
- Deliver teaser gallery same day for social media
- Provide high-res finals within 48 hours

**Usage Rights**:
- Corporate clients typically need broad usage rights
- Clarify licensing in contract (internal use, advertising, press, social media)
- Price accordingly for commercial usage

**File Organization**:
- Separate folders: Keynote, Breakout Sessions, Networking, Executives, Branding
- Clear file naming for easy client navigation
- Provide both web-optimized and print-ready versions

## Concert and Performance Photography

### Access and Credentials

**Photo Pit Access**:
- Typically first three songs, no flash rule
- Position yourself strategically for best angles
- Be aware of other photographers and security

**From Audience**:
- Longer lenses required (70-200mm, 100-400mm)
- Respect venue rules (some prohibit professional cameras)
- Avoid blocking other attendees' views

### Lighting Challenges

**Stage Lighting Characteristics**:
- Rapidly changing colors and intensity
- High contrast (bright spots, deep shadows)
- Spotlights create harsh lighting
- Backlighting and silhouettes common

**Camera Settings for Stage Lighting**:
- **Shutter Speed**: 1/250s minimum, 1/500s+ for energetic performers
- **Aperture**: f/2.8 or wider for light gathering
- **ISO**: 1600-6400 typical, up to 12,800 for dark venues
- **White Balance**: Auto or custom (stage lights vary wildly)
- **Metering**: Spot or center-weighted to avoid underexposure from dark backgrounds

### Composition for Performances

**Capture Energy and Emotion**:
- Peak action moments (jumps, guitar solos, dramatic gestures)
- Facial expressions and emotion
- Interaction between performers
- Crowd reactions and energy

**Variety of Shots**:
- **Wide**: Full stage, lighting rig, crowd context
- **Medium**: Performer with instrument, small groups
- **Tight**: Face, hands on instrument, emotional close-ups
- **Detail**: Instruments, equipment, stage details

**Timing and Anticipation**:
- Learn song structure to anticipate climactic moments
- Watch for cues (deep breath before high note, wind-up before jump)
- Shoot in bursts during peak action

### Post-Processing for Concerts

**Color Management**:
- Stage lighting creates extreme color casts
- Decide: keep dramatic colors or normalize white balance
- Consistency across similar lighting scenes

**Noise Reduction**:
- High ISO creates significant noise
- Use AI-powered noise reduction (Topaz DeNoise, DxO PureRAW)
- Balance noise reduction with detail preservation

**Contrast and Exposure**:
- Lift shadows to reveal detail without losing mood
- Protect highlights (spotlights easily blow out)
- Increase contrast for punch and drama

## Conference and Trade Show Photography

### Coverage Strategy

**Establishing Shots**:
- Venue exterior and signage
- Registration area
- Main hall or exhibition floor (wide angles showing scale)
- Branding and sponsor visibility

**Session Coverage**:
- Speakers presenting (multiple angles)
- Audience engagement (note-taking, listening, reactions)
- Slides or presentation content (if requested)
- Q&A interactions

**Exhibition Floor**:
- Booth setups and displays
- Product demonstrations
- Attendee-exhibitor interactions
- Busy floor showing activity
- Individual booth detail shots

**Networking and Social**:
- Coffee breaks and meal times
- Small group conversations
- Business card exchanges
- Casual interactions

### Multi-Day Event Management

**Consistency Across Days**:
- Maintain similar shooting style and settings
- Cover similar moments each day for variety
- Avoid repetitive compositions

**Daily Deliverables**:
- Provide same-day highlights for social media
- Client can promote ongoing event
- Builds excitement for remaining days

**Stamina and Preparation**:
- Conferences can be 8-12 hour days
- Comfortable shoes essential
- Stay hydrated and take brief breaks
- Charge batteries during lunch sessions

### Attendee Privacy and Consent

**Public Event Photography**:
- Generally, attendees at public events have reduced expectation of privacy
- Event registration often includes photo consent clause
- Still, be respectful and avoid intrusive shooting

**Sensitive Situations**:
- Avoid photographing people eating (unflattering)
- Be cautious with phones/screens (may show confidential info)
- Don't photograph attendees in distress or private conversations

**Model Releases**:
- Not typically required for editorial event coverage
- Required if images will be used in advertising
- Clarify usage with client and obtain releases if needed

## Private Parties and Social Events

### Event Types

- Birthday parties (milestone birthdays, children's parties)
- Anniversary celebrations
- Graduation parties
- Holiday gatherings
- Retirement parties
- Bar/Bat Mitzvahs, Quinceañeras, Sweet 16s

### Client Expectations

**Casual vs. Formal**:
- Understand tone of event (backyard BBQ vs. formal gala)
- Adjust shooting style accordingly
- Casual: more candids, relaxed posing
- Formal: structured portraits, elegant compositions

**Guest Comfort**:
- Private parties have more intimate atmosphere
- Guests may be camera-shy or uncomfortable
- Be friendly and approachable
- Explain what you're doing if asked

**Must-Have Moments**:
- Guest of honor arrival or entrance
- Cake cutting or candle blowing
- Toasts and speeches
- Gift opening (if applicable)
- Group photos (family, friends)
- Candid interactions and laughter

### Children's Party Photography

**Unique Challenges**:
- Fast-moving, unpredictable subjects
- Low angles required (get down to child's eye level)
- Short attention spans (can't pose for long)
- Parental expectations (every child photographed)

**Technical Approach**:
- **Shutter Speed**: 1/500s minimum for active children
- **Continuous AF**: Track moving subjects
- **Burst Mode**: Capture fleeting expressions
- **Wide Aperture**: Isolate subject from chaotic backgrounds

**Safety and Conduct**:
- Never photograph children alone or in compromising situations
- Be aware of parental concerns about privacy
- Some parents don't want their children photographed—respect this
- Obtain clear consent from event host regarding child photography

### Milestone Celebrations

**Bar/Bat Mitzvah, Quinceañera, Sweet 16**:
- Blend of formal ceremony and party reception
- Cultural and religious traditions (research beforehand)
- Formal portraits of honoree and family
- Ceremonial moments (candle lighting, first dance, court presentation)
- Party atmosphere (dancing, games, entertainment)

**Anniversary and Retirement Parties**:
- Focus on guest of honor and their relationships
- Capture speeches and toasts (emotional moments)
- Group photos with family, friends, colleagues
- Memorabilia and displays (photo collages, awards, gifts)
- Candid interactions showing celebration and appreciation
