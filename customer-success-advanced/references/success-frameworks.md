# Customer Success Frameworks

Comprehensive frameworks and methodologies for building and scaling customer success programs across the customer lifecycle.

## Core Customer Success Frameworks

### LAER Framework (Land, Adopt, Expand, Renew)

A lifecycle model that guides customer engagement from acquisition through growth:

**Land: Acquire the Customer**
- **Objective:** Convert prospect to customer with smooth sales-to-CS handoff
- **Key Activities:**
  - Align on success criteria and expectations during sales process
  - Set realistic timelines and deliverables
  - Introduce CS team before contract signature
  - Conduct pre-kickoff discovery and planning
- **Success Metrics:** Clean handoff, accurate customer data, aligned expectations
- **Handoff Checklist:**
  - Customer goals and success criteria documented
  - Technical requirements and integration needs identified
  - Key stakeholders and buying committee mapped
  - Contract terms and SLAs understood
  - Kickoff call scheduled

**Adopt: Drive Product Usage and Value Realization**
- **Objective:** Ensure customers actively use product and achieve initial value
- **Key Activities:**
  - Execute structured onboarding program
  - Provide training and enablement resources
  - Monitor usage and adoption metrics
  - Identify and address adoption barriers
  - Celebrate early wins and milestones
- **Success Metrics:** Time-to-value, feature adoption rate, active user percentage, first value milestone achieved
- **Adoption Stages:**
  - **Initial Setup (Week 1-2):** Technical configuration, integration, data migration
  - **First Use (Week 2-4):** Core feature training, initial user activation
  - **Habit Formation (Week 4-8):** Regular usage patterns, workflow integration
  - **Proficiency (Week 8-12):** Advanced features, power user development

**Expand: Grow Revenue Within Account**
- **Objective:** Identify and execute upsell, cross-sell, and expansion opportunities
- **Key Activities:**
  - Monitor usage patterns for expansion signals
  - Conduct business reviews highlighting ROI and value
  - Introduce additional products and features
  - Facilitate internal advocacy and departmental expansion
  - Develop expansion proposals and business cases
- **Success Metrics:** Expansion revenue, upsell/cross-sell rate, average revenue per account (ARPA)
- **Expansion Triggers:**
  - Approaching plan limits (users, volume, features)
  - High engagement and satisfaction scores
  - Expressed interest in additional capabilities
  - Organizational growth or new initiatives
  - Competitive displacement opportunities

**Renew: Secure Contract Renewal**
- **Objective:** Ensure timely renewal with minimal friction and churn risk
- **Key Activities:**
  - Monitor renewal pipeline and forecast
  - Conduct renewal readiness assessments
  - Address concerns and objections proactively
  - Demonstrate ongoing value and ROI
  - Negotiate renewal terms and pricing
- **Success Metrics:** Renewal rate, on-time renewal percentage, renewal expansion rate
- **Renewal Timeline:**
  - **180 days out:** Initial renewal planning, health check
  - **120 days out:** Business review, value demonstration, expansion discussion
  - **90 days out:** Renewal proposal, pricing discussion
  - **60 days out:** Contract negotiation, objection handling
  - **30 days out:** Final approval, contract execution
  - **0 days:** Renewal celebration, next-year planning

### V2MOM Framework (Vision, Values, Methods, Obstacles, Measures)

A strategic planning framework for defining CS program direction:

**Vision: Define the Ultimate Goal**
- **Question:** What do we want to achieve for our customers?
- **Example:** "Every customer achieves measurable ROI within 90 days and becomes a lifelong advocate"
- **Components:**
  - Customer outcomes and success definition
  - Business impact and revenue goals
  - Team aspirations and culture
  - Market position and competitive differentiation

**Values: Establish Guiding Principles**
- **Question:** What principles guide our decisions and actions?
- **Examples:**
  - Customer outcomes over product features
  - Proactive engagement over reactive support
  - Data-driven decisions over intuition
  - Collaboration over silos
  - Continuous improvement over status quo

**Methods: Define Action Steps**
- **Question:** How will we achieve our vision?
- **Examples:**
  - Implement health scoring and risk management
  - Build segmented engagement models
  - Develop expansion playbooks
  - Invest in CS technology and automation
  - Create customer advocacy program

**Obstacles: Identify Challenges**
- **Question:** What barriers might prevent success?
- **Examples:**
  - Limited CS team capacity and resources
  - Lack of product usage data and insights
  - Misalignment between sales and CS
  - Insufficient customer engagement
  - Competitive pressure and market changes

**Measures: Track Success**
- **Question:** How will we know we're succeeding?
- **Examples:**
  - Net Revenue Retention (NRR) >120%
  - Gross Revenue Retention (GRR) >95%
  - NPS >50
  - Expansion revenue 25% of total revenue
  - Time-to-value <60 days

### People-Process-Technology (P-P-T) Framework

A holistic approach to building scalable CS programs:

**People: Build the Right Team**

**Team Structure:**
- **Customer Success Managers (CSMs):** Relationship owners, strategic advisors
- **Onboarding Specialists:** New customer implementation experts
- **Renewal Managers:** Contract renewal and expansion focus
- **CS Operations:** Data, analytics, process, technology
- **Customer Success Engineers:** Technical implementation and support
- **Executive Sponsors:** C-level engagement for strategic accounts

**Skills and Competencies:**
- **Relationship Building:** Empathy, communication, trust development
- **Business Acumen:** Understanding customer business, industry knowledge, strategic thinking
- **Product Expertise:** Deep product knowledge, use case mastery
- **Data Analysis:** Metrics interpretation, trend identification, insights generation
- **Project Management:** Organization, prioritization, execution
- **Sales Skills:** Expansion selling, negotiation, objection handling

**Hiring and Development:**
- Define clear role descriptions and success criteria
- Hire for customer empathy and business orientation
- Provide comprehensive onboarding and training
- Create career paths and growth opportunities
- Foster continuous learning and skill development

**Process: Standardize and Scale**

**Core CS Processes:**

1. **Sales-to-CS Handoff**
   - Standardized handoff template and checklist
   - Transition meeting with sales, CS, and customer
   - Documentation of goals, expectations, and commitments
   - Clear ownership and accountability

2. **Onboarding Process**
   - Segmented onboarding tracks by customer type
   - Defined milestones and success criteria
   - Multi-modal training delivery (live, recorded, documentation)
   - Progress tracking and intervention triggers

3. **Ongoing Engagement**
   - Cadence and touchpoint schedules by segment
   - Business review templates and agendas
   - Communication playbooks and messaging
   - Escalation procedures and governance

4. **Health Monitoring and Risk Management**
   - Health score calculation and tracking
   - At-risk account identification and alerts
   - Intervention playbooks and recovery strategies
   - Executive escalation processes

5. **Expansion and Growth**
   - Expansion opportunity identification criteria
   - Upsell/cross-sell playbooks and messaging
   - Proposal templates and business case frameworks
   - Collaboration with sales on expansion deals

6. **Renewal Management**
   - Renewal pipeline tracking and forecasting
   - Renewal readiness assessments
   - Renewal conversation guides and objection handling
   - Contract negotiation and approval workflows

**Process Documentation:**
- Create detailed playbooks for each process
- Document best practices and lessons learned
- Maintain centralized knowledge repository
- Regularly review and update processes
- Train team on standard operating procedures

**Technology: Enable Scale and Efficiency**

**CS Technology Stack:**

1. **Customer Success Platform (CSP)**
   - **Purpose:** Centralized CS management, health scoring, automation
   - **Examples:** Gainsight, ChurnZero, Totango, Catalyst
   - **Capabilities:**
     - Customer health scoring and risk alerts
     - Playbook automation and workflow management
     - Success planning and goal tracking
     - Reporting and analytics dashboards
     - Email and communication tracking

2. **Customer Relationship Management (CRM)**
   - **Purpose:** Account and contact management, opportunity tracking
   - **Examples:** Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics
   - **Integration:** Bidirectional sync with CS platform

3. **Product Analytics**
   - **Purpose:** Usage tracking, feature adoption, user behavior analysis
   - **Examples:** Pendo, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Heap
   - **Capabilities:**
     - User activity tracking and segmentation
     - Feature adoption and usage trends
     - In-app messaging and guidance
     - Product analytics and insights

4. **Communication and Support**
   - **Purpose:** Customer communication, support ticketing, knowledge management
   - **Examples:** Intercom, Zendesk, Front, Freshdesk
   - **Capabilities:**
     - Multi-channel communication (email, chat, phone)
     - Support ticket management and tracking
     - Knowledge base and self-service resources
     - Customer feedback and surveys

5. **Survey and Feedback**
   - **Purpose:** Customer satisfaction measurement, feedback collection
   - **Examples:** Delighted, SurveyMonkey, Typeform, Qualtrics
   - **Capabilities:**
     - NPS, CSAT, CES surveys
     - Automated survey triggers
     - Sentiment analysis
     - Feedback aggregation and reporting

6. **Business Intelligence and Reporting**
   - **Purpose:** Data visualization, executive reporting, trend analysis
   - **Examples:** Tableau, Looker, Power BI, Domo
   - **Capabilities:**
     - Custom dashboards and visualizations
     - Cross-platform data integration
     - Predictive analytics and forecasting
     - Executive summaries and KPI tracking

**Technology Integration:**
- Ensure seamless data flow between systems
- Eliminate manual data entry and duplication
- Create unified customer view across platforms
- Automate workflows and processes
- Maintain data quality and governance

## Customer Journey Mapping

Visualize and optimize the customer experience across lifecycle stages:

### Journey Mapping Process

**Step 1: Define Customer Segments**
- Identify distinct customer types (enterprise, mid-market, SMB)
- Consider product tiers and use cases
- Account for industry and vertical differences
- Recognize maturity and sophistication levels

**Step 2: Map Lifecycle Stages**

Define key stages in customer journey:

1. **Pre-Sale:** Evaluation, decision, contracting
2. **Onboarding:** Implementation, training, initial adoption
3. **Adoption:** Regular usage, value realization, optimization
4. **Growth:** Expansion, upsell, cross-sell
5. **Renewal:** Contract renewal, negotiation
6. **Advocacy:** References, referrals, community participation
7. **Churn:** At-risk, recovery attempts, exit

**Step 3: Identify Touchpoints**

For each stage, document:
- **Customer Actions:** What customers do at this stage
- **Customer Goals:** What customers want to achieve
- **Customer Emotions:** How customers feel (excited, confused, frustrated, satisfied)
- **Company Touchpoints:** All interactions (emails, calls, meetings, in-app messages)
- **Responsible Teams:** Who owns each touchpoint (sales, CS, support, product)

**Step 4: Analyze Pain Points and Opportunities**
- **Pain Points:** Where customers struggle, get stuck, or become frustrated
- **Friction:** Unnecessary complexity, delays, or barriers
- **Gaps:** Missing touchpoints or information
- **Opportunities:** Moments to deliver exceptional value or delight

**Step 5: Design Ideal Experience**
- Define desired customer experience at each stage
- Eliminate or reduce pain points and friction
- Add touchpoints to fill gaps
- Enhance moments of delight and value delivery
- Ensure consistency across channels and teams

**Step 6: Implement and Measure**
- Build processes and playbooks for ideal experience
- Train teams on new touchpoints and interactions
- Implement technology to enable and automate
- Measure customer satisfaction and outcomes at each stage
- Continuously iterate and improve

### Journey Mapping Example: SaaS Onboarding

**Stage: Days 1-7 (Initial Setup)**

**Customer Goals:**
- Understand product capabilities
- Complete technical setup
- Get first users active

**Customer Emotions:**
- Excited about new solution
- Anxious about complexity
- Eager to see value

**Touchpoints:**
- Day 0: Welcome email with getting started guide
- Day 1: Kickoff call with CSM and implementation team
- Day 2: Technical setup session with CS Engineer
- Day 3: In-app tutorial and product tour
- Day 5: Check-in email from CSM
- Day 7: First milestone celebration (first users active)

**Pain Points:**
- Technical setup more complex than expected
- Unclear next steps after kickoff
- Difficulty finding resources and documentation

**Improvements:**
- Pre-kickoff technical assessment to identify complexity
- Detailed implementation plan with clear milestones
- Centralized onboarding portal with all resources
- Proactive check-ins at days 2, 4, and 6
- In-app guidance and contextual help

## Engagement Models by Segment

### High-Touch Model (Enterprise)

**Customer Profile:**
- Annual Contract Value (ACV): $100K+
- Complex product with multiple use cases
- Large buying committee and user base
- Strategic importance to business

**CSM:Customer Ratio:** 1:10-30 accounts

**Engagement Cadence:**
- **Weekly:** Usage monitoring, proactive outreach for issues
- **Bi-Weekly:** Check-in calls, progress reviews
- **Monthly:** Strategic planning, optimization recommendations
- **Quarterly:** Executive Business Reviews (QBRs)
- **Annually:** Strategic account planning, renewal discussions

**Key Activities:**
- Dedicated CSM as single point of contact
- Customized success plans and goal tracking
- Executive-to-executive relationship building
- On-site visits and workshops (as appropriate)
- Proactive risk management and intervention
- White-glove support and prioritization
- Strategic expansion planning and execution

**Technology Support:**
- Manual tracking and personalized outreach
- Custom dashboards and reporting
- Collaborative success planning tools
- Executive presentation materials

### Medium-Touch Model (Mid-Market)

**Customer Profile:**
- Annual Contract Value (ACV): $25K-$100K
- Moderate product complexity
- Smaller buying committee and user base
- Important but not strategic accounts

**CSM:Customer Ratio:** 1:30-100 accounts

**Engagement Cadence:**
- **Monthly:** Check-in calls or emails
- **Quarterly:** Business reviews (virtual)
- **Bi-Annually:** Strategic planning and renewal prep
- **As-Needed:** Issue resolution, expansion discussions

**Key Activities:**
- Assigned CSM with shared book of business
- Standardized success plans with customization
- Group webinars and training sessions
- Automated email nurture with personal touches
- Health score monitoring with intervention triggers
- Expansion opportunity identification and pursuit
- Renewal management and negotiation

**Technology Support:**
- CS platform for automation and tracking
- Templated email campaigns with personalization
- Automated health scoring and alerts
- Self-service resources and knowledge base

### Low-Touch/Tech-Touch Model (SMB)

**Customer Profile:**
- Annual Contract Value (ACV): <$25K
- Simple product with clear use cases
- Small user base, often single decision-maker
- High volume, transactional relationship

**CSM:Customer Ratio:** 1:100-1,000+ accounts (or no dedicated CSM)

**Engagement Cadence:**
- **Automated:** Triggered emails based on behavior and milestones
- **Quarterly:** Automated business review summaries
- **Annually:** Renewal reminders and offers
- **On-Demand:** Self-service support and resources

**Key Activities:**
- Automated onboarding sequences
- In-app messaging and product tours
- Email nurture campaigns (usage tips, best practices, feature announcements)
- Self-service knowledge base and video tutorials
- Community forums for peer support
- Chatbots and AI-powered assistance
- Automated expansion offers and upsell campaigns
- Renewal automation with minimal human touch

**Technology Support:**
- Fully automated workflows and campaigns
- In-app guidance and contextual help
- Self-service portals and resources
- AI chatbots and virtual assistants
- Automated health scoring and intervention

### Hybrid Model (Most Common)

**Approach:**
- Segment customers into tiers based on value and complexity
- Apply appropriate touch model to each tier
- Allow movement between tiers based on growth or risk
- Balance personalization with scalability

**Tiering Criteria:**
- **Tier 1 (High-Touch):** Top 10-20% by revenue, strategic accounts
- **Tier 2 (Medium-Touch):** Next 30-40% by revenue, growth potential
- **Tier 3 (Low-Touch):** Remaining 40-60%, transactional accounts

**Dynamic Tiering:**
- Promote accounts showing expansion potential
- Escalate at-risk accounts to higher touch temporarily
- Downgrade accounts with declining value or engagement
- Regularly review and adjust tier assignments
