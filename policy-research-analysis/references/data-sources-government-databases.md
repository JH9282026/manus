## Introduction

Policy research relies on diverse, high-quality data sources to inform evidence-based analysis and decision-making. Government databases provide extensive statistical data on demographics, economics, and social conditions, while academic institutions, think tanks, and non-governmental organizations contribute research, analysis, and specialized datasets. This comprehensive guide maps the landscape of policy research data sources, from federal statistical agencies to international organizations, enabling researchers to access the information needed for rigorous policy analysis.

## U.S. Government Data Sources

### U.S. Census Bureau

The Census Bureau is the premier source for demographic and economic data:

**Data.census.gov (formerly American FactFinder):**
- Primary access point for Census Bureau data
- Decennial Census data (every 10 years)
- American Community Survey (ACS) - annual detailed socioeconomic data
- Economic Census - comprehensive business and economic data
- Census of Governments - state and local government data
- Population estimates and projections

**Key Datasets:**

**Decennial Census:**
- Complete population count every 10 years
- Basic demographic information
- Geographic distribution
- Apportionment data for Congressional representation

**American Community Survey (ACS):**
- Annual survey of ~3.5 million households
- Detailed socioeconomic characteristics
- Income, education, employment, housing
- Available at multiple geographic levels
- 1-year and 5-year estimates

**Economic Census:**
- Every 5 years (years ending in 2 and 7)
- Comprehensive business statistics
- Industry-specific data
- Employment and payroll
- Sales and receipts

**Census of Governments:**
- Every 5 years (years ending in 2 and 7)
- State and local government organization
- Public employment and payroll
- Government finances

**Census Quick Facts:**
- Easy-to-access summary statistics
- State, county, and city data
- Key demographic and economic indicators
- Comparison tools

**Current Population Survey (CPS):**
- Monthly survey of ~60,000 households
- Labor force statistics
- Employment and unemployment
- Jointly conducted with Bureau of Labor Statistics
- Supplemental surveys on specific topics

**Applications:**
- Demographic analysis
- Economic research
- Social policy evaluation
- Resource allocation
- Program planning
- Grant applications

### Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)

Comprehensive labor market and economic data:

**Key Datasets:**

**Current Employment Statistics (CES):**
- Monthly survey of ~145,000 businesses
- Employment, hours, and earnings
- Industry-specific data
- National, state, and metro area levels

**Current Population Survey (CPS):**
- Labor force status
- Employment and unemployment rates
- Demographic characteristics of workers
- Job search activities

**American Time Use Survey (ATUS):**
- How Americans spend their time
- Work, childcare, leisure, volunteering
- Demographic breakdowns
- Annual data since 2003

**Consumer Expenditure Surveys:**
- Household spending patterns
- Product categories
- Demographic breakdowns (age, income, occupation)
- Quarterly and annual data

**Consumer Price Index (CPI):**
- Inflation measurement
- Price changes for goods and services
- Urban and rural areas
- Monthly data

**Producer Price Index (PPI):**
- Wholesale price changes
- Industry-specific indexes
- Commodity-based indexes

**Employment Cost Index (ECI):**
- Compensation trends
- Wages and benefits
- Industry and occupation data
- Quarterly data

**Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS):**
- Employment and wage estimates
- 800+ occupations
- National, state, and metro area data
- Annual data

**Applications:**
- Labor market analysis
- Wage and compensation studies
- Economic forecasting
- Policy impact assessment
- Cost-of-living adjustments
