# Snapchat Targeting and Audience Strategies

A comprehensive guide to Snapchat's targeting capabilities, audience segmentation, and strategies for reaching Gen Z and Millennial audiences effectively.

## Why Snapchat for Gen Z and Millennials

### Audience Reach and Demographics

Snapchat's user base makes it an essential platform for brands targeting younger consumers:

- **75% of 13-34 year olds** in over 25 countries use Snapchat
- **90% of 13-24 year olds** in 20+ countries are active on the platform
- **400+ million daily active users** globally
- **Average user opens the app 30+ times per day**
- **60% more likely to make impulse purchases** compared to other platforms

### Spending Power and Purchase Behavior

- **$4.4 trillion global spending power** (Gen Z + Millennials combined)
- **34% more likely to purchase products** discovered on Snapchat
- **60% more likely to make impulse purchases** on Snapchat vs. other platforms
- **Close friends have 4X more influence** on purchasing decisions than celebrities/influencers

### Platform Characteristics

**Authenticity-First Culture**:
- Users value genuine, unpolished content over highly produced ads
- Ephemeral nature encourages authentic sharing
- User-generated content (UGC) aesthetic performs better than studio-quality production

**High Engagement Environment**:
- Full-screen, immersive ad experiences
- Sound-on viewing (60% watch with audio)
- Vertical-first platform optimized for mobile

## Core Targeting Options

### 1. Demographic Targeting

Basic demographic filters form the foundation of audience targeting.

#### Age Targeting

Available age brackets:
- 13-17 (requires special compliance)
- 18-20
- 21-24
- 25-34
- 35-49
- 50+

**Best Practices**:
- Gen Z core: 18-24 age range
- Millennials: 25-34 age range
- Avoid targeting 13-17 unless specifically compliant with youth advertising policies
- Test adjacent age groups (e.g., 18-24 vs. 25-34) to identify optimal segment

#### Gender Targeting

Options:
- Male
- Female
- All genders

**Best Practices**:
- Start with "All" unless product is gender-specific
- Analyze performance by gender in reporting, then refine
- Beauty, fashion, and wellness often skew female but test assumptions

#### Language Targeting

- 15+ languages supported
- Targets users based on device language settings
- Essential for international campaigns

**Best Practices**:
- Match ad creative language to language targeting
- Consider regional dialects (e.g., Spanish in Spain vs. Mexico)

#### Device Targeting

Options:
- iOS
- Android
- Specific device models
- Mobile carrier

**Use Cases**:
- App install campaigns (target compatible devices)
- Premium product launches (target high-end devices)
- Carrier-specific promotions

### 2. Location Targeting

Snapchat offers granular geographic targeting from country-level to hyper-local.

#### Geographic Levels

| Level | Description | Use Case |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Country** | Target entire countries | International brand awareness |
| **Region/State** | Target states or provinces | Regional campaigns |
| **DMA** | Designated Market Areas (US) | TV market alignment |
| **City** | Target specific cities | Urban-focused campaigns |
| **Postal Code** | ZIP/postal code targeting | Hyper-local targeting |
| **Radius** | Custom radius around a point | Store location targeting |

#### Geo-Fencing

**Definition**: Target users within a specific geographic boundary, often around physical locations.

**Use Cases**:
- **Retail**: Target users near store locations
- **Events**: Target attendees at concerts, festivals, sports events
- **Competitive**: Target users near competitor locations
- **Tourism**: Target visitors in tourist areas

**Example Strategy**:
A ski resort targets users within a 5-mile radius of competitor resorts with ads promoting their superior amenities and a discount offer.

**Best Practices**:
- Minimum radius: 1 mile (smaller may have insufficient reach)
- Combine with demographic targeting for precision
- Use time-of-day targeting to reach users when they're most likely to visit

### 4. Behavioral Targeting

Target users based on their actions and patterns on Snapchat and beyond.

#### Purchase Behavior

- **High-value purchasers**: Users who make frequent or expensive purchases
- **Bargain hunters**: Users who respond to discounts and deals
- **Impulse buyers**: Users with history of spontaneous purchases
- **Category-specific buyers**: Users who purchase in specific product categories

**Use Cases**:
- E-commerce campaigns targeting high-value purchasers
- Flash sale promotions targeting bargain hunters

#### App Usage Patterns

- **Heavy app users**: Users who frequently download and engage with apps
- **Mobile gamers**: Users who play mobile games regularly
- **Shopping app users**: Users who frequently use e-commerce apps

**Use Cases**:
- App install campaigns targeting heavy app users
- Mobile game promotions targeting gamers

#### Content Engagement

- **Video viewers**: Users who watch video content frequently
- **AR Lens users**: Users who regularly engage with AR Lenses
- **Story viewers**: Users who consume Story content

**Use Cases**:
- Video ad campaigns targeting video viewers
- AR Lens campaigns targeting AR enthusiasts

#### Snapchat Activity Level

- **Power users**: Users who open Snapchat 30+ times per day
- **Regular users**: Users who open Snapchat 10-30 times per day
- **Light users**: Users who open Snapchat less frequently

**Best Practices**:
- Target power users for maximum engagement
- Exclude light users for conversion-focused campaigns

### 5. Custom Audiences

Leverage first-party data to create highly targeted audiences.

#### Snap Pixel Audiences

**Definition**: Audiences built from website visitors tracked by the Snap Pixel.

**Audience Types**:
- **All website visitors**: Anyone who visited your site
- **Page visitors**: Users who visited specific pages
- **Event-based**: Users who completed specific actions (Add to Cart, Purchase, Sign Up)
- **Time-based**: Visitors within specific timeframes (7 days, 30 days, 90 days, 180 days)

**Use Cases**:
- **Retargeting cart abandoners**: Target users who added to cart but didn't purchase
- **Upselling past purchasers**: Target buyers with complementary products
- **Re-engagement**: Target users who visited but didn't convert

**Best Practices**:
- Minimum audience size: 1,000 users
- Use shorter lookback windows (7-30 days) for time-sensitive offers
- Exclude recent purchasers from acquisition campaigns to avoid wasted spend

**Example Strategy**:
```
Audience 1: Visited product page in last 7 days, did NOT purchase
Audience 2: Added to cart in last 14 days, did NOT purchase
Audience 3: Purchased in last 30 days

Campaign 1: Target Audience 1 with product benefits
Campaign 2: Target Audience 2 with discount offer
Campaign 3: Target Audience 3 with complementary products
```

#### Customer Lists (Snap Audience Match)

**Definition**: Upload customer data (email, phone, Mobile Advertising ID) to target existing customers or prospects.

**Data Types**:
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers (with country code)
- Mobile Advertising IDs (MAID)

**Requirements**:
- Minimum 1,000 matched users
- Data must be hashed (SHA-256) before upload
- Users must have consented to marketing communications

**Use Cases**:
- **Customer retention**: Target existing customers with loyalty offers
- **Winback campaigns**: Re-engage lapsed customers
- **VIP targeting**: Target high-value customers with exclusive offers
- **Exclusion**: Exclude existing customers from acquisition campaigns

**Best Practices**:
- Clean data before upload (remove duplicates, invalid entries)
- Use multiple identifiers (email + phone) for higher match rates
- Refresh lists regularly (monthly) to maintain accuracy
- Segment lists by customer value, purchase recency, or product category

#### Lookalike Audiences

**Definition**: Snapchat identifies users similar to your existing customers or website visitors.

**Source Audiences**:
- Snap Pixel audiences (website visitors, converters)
- Customer lists (Snap Audience Match)
- Engagement audiences (ad interactors)

**Similarity Levels**:
- **1% (Narrow)**: Most similar to source audience, smaller reach
- **5% (Balanced)**: Balance of similarity and reach
- **10% (Broad)**: Less similar but larger reach

**Use Cases**:
- **Prospecting**: Find new customers similar to existing buyers
- **Scaling**: Expand reach beyond existing audiences
- **Cold acquisition**: Target users with no prior brand interaction

**Best Practices**:
- Source audience should be high-quality (purchasers, not just visitors)
- Minimum source audience size: 1,000 users (10,000+ recommended)
- Start with 1% lookalike, then expand to 5% and 10% as you scale
- Create multiple lookalikes from different source audiences (purchasers, high-value customers, recent buyers)

**Example Strategy**:
```
Source: Customers who purchased $100+ in last 90 days (5,000 users)
Lookalike 1%: 400,000 users (most similar)
Lookalike 5%: 2,000,000 users (balanced)
Lookalike 10%: 4,000,000 users (broadest)

Test all three with same creative and budget to identify optimal similarity level.
```

#### Mobile App Custom Audiences

**Definition**: Target users based on app interactions tracked by Mobile Measurement Partners (MMPs).

**Audience Types**:
- App installers
- In-app event completers (level completion, purchases, etc.)
- Active users (opened app in last X days)
- Lapsed users (haven't opened app in X days)

**Use Cases**:
- **Re-engagement**: Target lapsed users to return to app
- **Upsell**: Target active users with in-app purchase offers
- **Retention**: Target new installers with onboarding content

**Requirements**:
- MMP integration (Adjust, AppsFlyer, Kochava, etc.)
- App event tracking configured

#### Ad Engagement Audiences

**Definition**: Target users who previously interacted with your Snapchat ads.

**Engagement Types**:
- Viewed ad
- Swiped up on ad
- Completed video view
- Used AR Lens
- Engaged with Story Ad

**Use Cases**:
- **Sequential messaging**: Show different message to users who engaged with first ad
- **Conversion optimization**: Target engaged users with conversion-focused creative
- **Frequency capping**: Exclude users who already engaged to avoid fatigue

**Best Practices**:
- Minimum engagement threshold: 1,000 users
- Use 7-30 day lookback windows
- Combine with exclusions (e.g., target video viewers who didn't convert)

#### Profile Engagement Audiences

**Definition**: Target users who engaged with your Public Profile or organic content.

**Engagement Types**:
- Profile subscribers
- Story viewers
- Spotlight viewers
- Lens users (for branded Lenses)

**Use Cases**:
- **Subscriber activation**: Convert subscribers to customers
- **Organic amplification**: Boost organic content to engaged audience
- **Community building**: Nurture engaged community members

**Best Practices**:
- Prioritize subscribers (highest intent)
- Exclude subscribers from broad awareness campaigns (they already know you)
- Use for product launches and exclusive offers

## Advanced Targeting Strategies

### Smart Targeting (AI-Powered)

**Definition**: Snapchat's machine learning identifies high-value users beyond your specified targeting parameters.

**How It Works**:
- You set base targeting (demographics, interests)
- Snapchat's algorithm expands to similar users likely to convert
- Algorithm learns from conversion data to improve over time

**Performance Impact**:
- Average **8.8% increase in conversions** for adopters
- Particularly effective for Sales and App Promotion objectives

**Best Practices**:
- Requires sufficient conversion volume (15-20 conversions/week minimum)
- Start with broader base targeting (not hyper-narrow)
- Allow 7-14 days for algorithm learning
- Monitor performance closely in first 2 weeks

**When to Use**:
- Sales campaigns with proven conversion tracking
- App install campaigns with sufficient budget
- Scaling successful campaigns

**When to Avoid**:
- Brand new campaigns without performance data
- Highly niche products requiring precise targeting
- Low conversion volume campaigns

### Audience Layering and Exclusions

**Strategy**: Combine multiple targeting criteria and exclude specific audiences for precision.

**Layering Examples**:

**Example 1: Precision Targeting**
```
Include:
- Age: 18-24
- Gender: Female
- Location: Los Angeles, CA (25-mile radius)
- Interests: Beauty Enthusiasts, Fashion-Forward
- Behavior: High-value purchasers

Exclude:
- Purchased in last 30 days

Result: Young female beauty enthusiasts in LA who are high-value shoppers but haven't purchased recently.
```

**Example 2: Competitive Conquest**
```
Include:
- Age: 21-34
- Location: 5-mile radius around competitor stores
- Interests: Category-relevant interests

Exclude:
- Visited your website in last 90 days
- Existing customers

Result: Competitor customers who haven't engaged with your brand.
```

**Example 3: Funnel-Based Segmentation**
```
Campaign 1 (Awareness):
Include: Broad demographics + interests
Exclude: Website visitors (last 180 days)

Campaign 2 (Consideration):
Include: Website visitors (last 30 days)
Exclude: Add to cart (last 30 days)

Campaign 3 (Conversion):
Include: Add to cart (last 14 days)
Exclude: Purchasers (last 30 days)

Campaign 4 (Retention):
Include: Purchasers (last 90 days)
Exclude: Purchasers (last 14 days)
```

### Audience Testing Framework

**Systematic approach to identifying best-performing audiences:**

**Phase 1: Broad Testing (Week 1-2)**
- Test 3-5 broad audience segments
- Equal budget allocation
- Measure: CPA, ROAS, engagement rate

**Phase 2: Refinement (Week 3-4)**
- Double down on top 2 performers
- Test variations (age ranges, interest combinations)
- Measure: Incremental improvement

**Phase 3: Scaling (Week 5+)**
- Scale winning audiences with increased budget
- Introduce lookalikes of best performers
- Maintain testing budget (10-20%) for new audiences

**Testing Best Practices**:
- Minimum budget: $350 per audience per week
- Minimum duration: 7 days per test
- Test one variable at a time
- Use consistent creative across test groups
- Statistical significance: Wait for 100+ conversions before declaring winner

## Targeting Best Practices by Campaign Objective

### Awareness & Engagement Campaigns

**Recommended Targeting**:
- Broad demographics (age + location)
- 1-3 relevant interests (not 10+)
- Consider Smart Targeting for expansion

**Avoid**:
- Over-targeting (too many filters)
- Hyper-narrow audiences (limits reach)

### Traffic Campaigns

**Recommended Targeting**:
- Demographics + interests
- Lookalike audiences (5-10%)
- Retargeting engaged users from awareness campaigns

**Avoid**:
- Targeting existing customers (use exclusions)
- Too broad (wastes budget on uninterested users)

### Leads Campaigns

**Recommended Targeting**:
- Lookalike audiences (1-5%)
- Interest + behavioral targeting
- Retargeting website visitors

**Avoid**:
- Broad targeting (leads require higher intent)
- Targeting existing leads/customers

### App Promotion Campaigns

**Recommended Targeting**:
- Demographics + "Heavy app users" behavior
- Lookalike audiences of existing users
- Interest targeting relevant to app category

**Avoid**:
- Targeting existing app users (use exclusions)
- Incompatible device targeting

### Sales Campaigns

**Recommended Targeting**:
- Retargeting website visitors and cart abandoners
- Lookalike audiences (1-5%) of purchasers
- Smart Targeting enabled

**Avoid**:
- Broad cold audiences (higher CPA)
- Targeting recent purchasers (use 30-day exclusion)

## Audience Size and Budget Considerations

### Minimum Audience Sizes

| Objective | Minimum Audience Size | Recommended Size |
|-----------|----------------------|------------------|
| Awareness | 50,000 | 500,000+ |
| Traffic | 50,000 | 200,000+ |
| Leads | 50,000 | 100,000+ |
| App Promotion | 50,000 | 200,000+ |
| Sales | 50,000 | 100,000+ |

**Note**: Smaller audiences require higher bids and may have limited delivery.

### Budget Allocation by Audience Type

**Prospecting (Cold Audiences)**: 60-70% of budget
- Broad targeting
- Interest-based
- Lookalike audiences

**Retargeting (Warm Audiences)**: 30-40% of budget
- Website visitors
- Engagement audiences
- Customer lists

**Rationale**: Prospecting builds top-of-funnel, retargeting converts high-intent users.

## Conclusion

Effective Snapchat targeting requires:

1. **Understanding your audience**: Gen Z and Millennials value authenticity and respond to native content
2. **Strategic layering**: Combine demographics, interests, behaviors, and custom audiences
3. **Smart use of first-party data**: Leverage Pixel audiences, customer lists, and lookalikes
4. **Continuous testing**: Systematically test audiences to identify top performers
5. **Appropriate exclusions**: Avoid wasting budget on existing customers or recent converters
6. **Objective-specific strategies**: Match targeting approach to campaign objective

By leveraging Snapchat's robust targeting capabilities and following these best practices, advertisers can efficiently reach and convert their target audiences.
