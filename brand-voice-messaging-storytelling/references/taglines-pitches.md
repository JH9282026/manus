Frameworks for creating memorable brand statements.

## Tagline Development

### Tagline Types

| Type | Approach | Examples |
|------|----------|----------|
| Descriptive | What you do | "America runs on Dunkin'" |
| Imperative | Call to action | "Just Do It" (Nike), "Think Different" (Apple) |
| Superlative | Best, only, first | "The ultimate driving machine" (BMW) |
| Provocative | Question or challenge | "Got milk?", "Where's the beef?" |
| Specific | Unique benefit | "Melts in your mouth, not in your hands" |
| Emotive | Feeling or aspiration | "Open happiness" (Coca-Cola) |

### Tagline Development Process

1. **Start with brand essence** — What's the core truth?
2. **Brainstorm extensively** — Aim for 100+ options
3. **Test for criteria** — Memorability, meaning, uniqueness
4. **Check associations** — Unintended meanings?
5. **Verify availability** — Trademark search
6. **Refine and polish** — Perfect the language

### Tagline Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Question to Ask |
|-----------|----------------|
| Short | Is it 3-7 words? |
| Memorable | Does it stick? |
| Unique | Can anyone else say this? |
| Meaningful | Does it convey value? |
| Timeless | Will it last beyond trends? |
| Versatile | Does it work everywhere? |

### Famous Tagline Examples

| Brand | Tagline | Why It Works |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Nike | "Just Do It" | Universal motivation, action-oriented |
| Apple | "Think Different" | Values-based, differentiating |
| L'Oréal | "Because You're Worth It" | Emotional, self-affirming |
| MasterCard | "Priceless" | Emotional value beyond product |
| De Beers | "A Diamond is Forever" | Timeless, category-defining |
| FedEx | "When it absolutely..." | Specific benefit, memorable |

## Elevator Pitch

### Pitch Structure

| Element | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Hook | 5-10 sec | Attention-grabbing opening |
| Problem | 10-15 sec | What challenge you solve |
| Solution | 15-20 sec | How you solve it |
| Value | 10-15 sec | What makes you different |
| CTA | 5-10 sec | What you want next |

### Pitch Formulas

**Formula 1**: "You know how [problem]? Well, we [solution] so that [benefit]."

Example: "You know how most project management tools are so complicated that teams give up on them? We built one that's so simple, teams actually use it—and they finish projects 30% faster."

**Formula 2**: "We help [target] [achieve goal] by [unique approach]."

Example: "We help small business owners get more customers by making professional marketing easy and affordable."

### Pitch Variations

| Type | Length | Use Case |
|------|--------|----------|
| 30-Second | Brief | Networking, casual encounters |
| 60-Second | Standard | Meetings, introductions |
| 2-Minute | Extended | Formal presentations |
| Email | Written | Cold outreach, follow-ups |

### Pitch Tips

- Lead with the customer's problem, not your solution
- Use concrete examples and numbers
- Practice until it sounds natural, not memorized
- Adapt to your audience's interests
- End with a clear, easy next step

## Brand Manifesto

### What is a Brand Manifesto

A passionate declaration of your brand's beliefs, values, and purpose. A rallying cry that inspires both internal teams and external audiences.

### Manifesto Structure

| Section | Content | Tone |
|---------|---------|------|
| Opening | Bold statement or belief | Declarative, confident |
| Body | Elaboration with examples | Building, emotional |
| Closing | Call to action | Inspiring, forward-looking |

### Manifesto Examples

**Apple "Think Different"**:
> "Here's to the crazy ones. The misfits. The rebels. The troublemakers... Because the people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do."

**Nike**:
> "If you have a body, you are an athlete."

**Patagonia**:
> "We're in business to save our home planet."

### Writing Your Manifesto

1. **Start with core beliefs** — What do you stand for?
2. **Be bold** — Manifestos are not timid
3. **Use powerful language** — Emotive, active, present tense
4. **Include a call to action** — Invite participation
5. **Keep it focused** — 200-500 words
6. **Read it aloud** — It should sound inspiring

### Manifesto Checklist

- [ ] Clearly states brand beliefs
- [ ] Uses bold, confident language
- [ ] Emotionally resonant
- [ ] Present tense and active voice
- [ ] Includes call to action
- [ ] Appropriate length (200-500 words)
- [ ] Sounds inspiring when read aloud
- [ ] Authentic to brand values
- [ ] Differentiating from competitors

## Content Application

### Applying Taglines

| Placement | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| Logo Lockup | Paired with logo consistently |
| Website | Header, footer, key pages |
| Advertising | Consistent closer/sign-off |
| Social Media | Bio, profile, consistent use |
| Email | Signature, footer |
| Packaging | Product labels, materials |

### Applying Elevator Pitch

| Situation | Adaptation |
|-----------|------------|
| Networking | Conversational, question-inviting |
| Sales Call | Benefit-focused, solution-oriented |
| Investor Meeting | Growth and opportunity emphasis |
| Job Interview | Personal value contribution |
| Conference Talk | Audience problem-focused |

### Applying Manifesto

| Use Case | Format |
|----------|--------|
| Website | About page, dedicated page |
| Video | Brand film, anthem video |
| Print | Posters, brand book |
| Presentations | Opening or closing slide |
