# Voice Development

Comprehensive guide to developing and defining brand voice attributes.

---

## Voice Characteristics Spectrum

### Common Voice Attributes
- **Friendly**: Warm, approachable, welcoming
- **Professional**: Polished, credible, authoritative
- **Playful**: Fun, witty, lighthearted
- **Authoritative**: Expert, confident, commanding
- **Conversational**: Casual, relatable, natural
- **Inspirational**: Motivating, uplifting, empowering
- **Bold**: Daring, provocative, unconventional
- **Empathetic**: Understanding, caring, supportive

## Voice Attribute Definition Framework

For each of 3-5 selected attributes, document:

### Attribute Template
```
Attribute: [Name]
Means: [Clear explanation of characteristic]
Doesn't Mean: [Boundaries to prevent misinterpretation]
Do This: [Correct application examples]
Don't Do This: [Examples to avoid]
```

### Example: "Friendly"
- **Means**: Warm, approachable, welcoming, like talking to a helpful friend
- **Doesn't Mean**: Unprofessional, overly casual, using slang inappropriately
- **Do**: "Hey there! We're excited to help you get started."
- **Don't**: "Yo! What's up? Let's do this thing!"

## Voice Examples by Brand

### Mailchimp - Fun, Friendly, Helpful
- Uses humor without being silly
- Speaks directly to small business owners
- Makes complex topics approachable
- Example: "High fives! Your campaign is on its way."

### Nike - Inspirational, Motivational, Empowering
- Bold, action-oriented language
- Short, punchy sentences
- Speaks to the athlete in everyone
- Example: "You're faster than you think. Now prove it."

### Apple - Simple, Innovative, Human
- Clean, minimalist language
- Focuses on experience over specifications
- Makes technology feel personal
- Example: "The best iPhone we've ever made."

### Patagonia - Authentic, Activist, Adventurous
- Direct, honest communication
- Environmental advocacy throughout
- Speaks to outdoor enthusiasts who care
- Example: "Don't buy this jacket" (anti-consumerism campaign)

## Tone Adaptation Guidelines

### Tone Variables
- **Celebratory**: Excited, joyful, congratulatory
- **Empathetic**: Understanding, supportive, compassionate
- **Urgent**: Time-sensitive, important, action-required
- **Informative**: Educational, helpful, explanatory
- **Apologetic**: Sincere, solution-focused, accountable
- **Reassuring**: Calming, confident, supportive

### Context Factors
- **Audience**: New customers vs loyal advocates
- **Channel**: Social media vs formal email
- **Situation**: Celebration vs crisis
- **Message Type**: Marketing vs support vs legal

## Voice Guidelines Document Structure

### Section 1: Voice Overview
- Brand personality summary
- Core voice attributes (3-5)
- Voice positioning on spectrums
- Relationship to brand values

### Section 2: Voice in Detail
- Each attribute explained with examples
- "This, not that" comparisons
- Writing samples for each attribute

### Section 3: Tone Guidance
- Tone matrix for different contexts
- Channel-specific adaptations
- Scenario examples

### Section 4: Writing Mechanics
- Sentence length and structure
- Vocabulary preferences
- Grammar and punctuation style

### Section 5: Examples Library
- Headlines across channels
- Social media posts
- Email samples
- Customer service responses
- Error and success messages

### Section 6: Do's and Don'ts
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Language to use and avoid
- Boundary clarifications
