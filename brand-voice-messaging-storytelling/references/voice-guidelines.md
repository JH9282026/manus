# Voice Guidelines

Detailed frameworks for developing and documenting brand voice.

---

## Defining Brand Voice

### Voice Attribute Selection Process

1. Review brand personality and values
2. Analyze successful brand communications
3. Study competitor voices for differentiation
4. Research target audience preferences
5. Select 3-5 core voice attributes

### Voice Attribute Documentation Template

**Sample Copy**:
"[Example text demonstrating the attribute]"

## Voice Spectrum Mapping

### Dimension Scales

| Dimension | Left Extreme | Right Extreme | Your Brand Position |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|--------------------|
| Formality | Formal, corporate | Casual, conversational | [Mark position 1-10] |
| Mood | Serious, straightforward | Humorous, playful | [Mark position 1-10] |
| Attitude | Respectful, traditional | Irreverent, rule-breaking | [Mark position 1-10] |
| Energy | Enthusiastic, excited | Matter-of-fact, neutral | [Mark position 1-10] |
| Complexity | Technical, expert | Simple, accessible | [Mark position 1-10] |

### Creating Your Voice Spectrum

1. For each dimension, rate where your brand falls (1-10)
2. Document reasoning for each position
3. Identify which dimensions are most defining
4. Note flexibility ranges for different contexts

## Voice Guidelines Document Structure

### Section 1: Voice Overview
- Brand personality summary
- Core voice attributes (3-5)
- Voice positioning on spectrums
- Relationship to brand values

### Section 2: Voice in Detail
- Each attribute explained with examples
- "This, not that" comparisons
- Writing samples for each attribute
- Common misinterpretations addressed

### Section 3: Tone Guidance
- Tone matrix for different contexts
- Channel-specific adaptations
- Scenario examples
- Emotional range guidelines

### Section 4: Writing Mechanics
- Sentence length and structure
- Vocabulary preferences
- Grammar and punctuation style
- Formatting conventions

### Section 5: Examples Library
- Headlines across channels
- Social media posts
- Email samples
- Customer service responses
- Error and success messages

### Section 6: Do's and Don'ts
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Language to use and avoid
- Boundary clarifications
- Edge case guidance

## Voice Training Program

### Onboarding Process

1. Include voice guidelines in new hire materials
2. Create voice training modules
3. Assign voice mentors or champions
4. Provide practice exercises with feedback

### Ongoing Reinforcement

- Regular voice workshops
- Share exemplary content examples
- Voice audits with constructive feedback
- Update guidelines with new learnings

### Cross-Functional Alignment

- Marketing, sales, support, product all aligned
- Regular cross-team voice calibration sessions
- Shared terminology and message documents
- Feedback loops between teams

## Voice Effectiveness Measurement

### Quantitative Metrics

| Metric | Measurement Method |
|--------|-----------------|
| Brand Recall | Surveys asking about brand communication |
| Content Engagement | Rates across voice-consistent content |
| Message Comprehension | Testing understanding of key messages |
| Conversion by Approach | A/B testing different voice applications |

### Qualitative Indicators

- Customer feedback on communication style
- Brand perception studies
- Social listening for tone feedback
- Team confidence in voice application
