# DOOH Formats and Technology

## DOOH Format Overview

Digital Out-of-Home advertising encompasses a diverse range of formats, each designed to reach audiences in specific environments and contexts. Understanding these formats is essential for effective campaign planning and execution.

## Large Format Digital Billboards

### Description

Large format digital billboards are oversized digital displays located in high-traffic areas such as highways, major thoroughfares, airports, and city centers. These are the digital evolution of traditional roadside billboards.

### Characteristics

**Size**: Typically 14' x 48' (672 square feet) or larger, though sizes vary

**Technology**: LED displays capable of showing static images, animations, or video

**Viewing Distance**: Designed for visibility from 500+ feet away

**Viewing Time**: Brief exposure (typically 2-10 seconds as vehicles pass)

**Rotation**: Multiple advertisers share the screen, with ads rotating every 6-10 seconds

**Locations**: Highways, major roads, downtown areas, airports, stadiums

### Advantages

- **Massive Reach**: Exposure to thousands or millions of viewers in high-traffic locations
- **High Impact**: Large size creates memorable impressions
- **Brand Building**: Excellent for awareness and brand visibility
- **Geographic Targeting**: Select specific markets, neighborhoods, or corridors
- **Flexibility**: Change creative quickly for timely messaging

### Best Use Cases

- Broad brand awareness campaigns
- Product launches requiring mass reach
- Geographic market penetration
- Event promotion (concerts, sports, entertainment)
- Directional advertising ("Exit here for...")

### Pricing

**CPM Range**: $3-$15 depending on location, market size, and demand

**Factors Affecting Price**:
- Traffic volume and audience size
- Market size (major metro vs. smaller markets)
- Location quality (premium vs. standard)
- Time of year (Q4 typically more expensive)
- Length of campaign

## Transit Advertising

### Description

Digital screens located in transportation environments, including airports, subway stations, bus shelters, train platforms, taxis, and rideshare vehicles.

### Formats

**Airport Displays**: 
- Large screens in terminals, baggage claim, gates
- Captive audience with extended dwell time
- Affluent, business-oriented demographics
- High-quality production values expected

**Subway/Metro Screens**:
- Platform screens visible to waiting passengers
- In-car screens for riders
- High frequency, urban audiences
- Commuter-focused demographics

**Bus Shelters**:
- Street-level digital displays in bus waiting areas
- Pedestrian and vehicular visibility
- Urban and suburban locations
- Weather-protected viewing

**Taxi/Rideshare Screens**:
- In-vehicle screens for passenger viewing
- Captive audience during rides
- Urban, mobile audiences
- Proximity to destinations

### Advantages

- **Captive Audiences**: Waiting passengers have time to engage with messages
- **Commuter Reach**: Regular exposure to daily commuters
- **Urban Targeting**: Concentrated in cities and metro areas
- **Contextual Relevance**: Reach audiences during travel and commute
- **Extended Dwell Time**: Longer viewing opportunities than roadside billboards

### Best Use Cases

- Commuter-relevant products and services
- Urban market campaigns
- Mobile app promotion
- Entertainment and events
- Travel-related services

### Pricing

**CPM Range**: $5-$25 depending on format and location

**Premium Locations**: Major airports and subway systems command higher rates

## Place-Based Media

### Description

Digital screens strategically located within specific venues or environments relevant to target audiences, such as gyms, malls, gas stations, office buildings, bars, restaurants, medical offices, and colleges.

### Venue Types and Characteristics

**Fitness Centers/Gyms**:
- Health-conscious, active audiences
- Extended dwell time during workouts
- Receptive to health, wellness, nutrition products
- Younger demographics

**Shopping Malls**:
- Shoppers in purchase mindset
- Diverse demographics
- High foot traffic
- Proximity to retail locations

**Gas Stations/Convenience Stores**:
- Captive audience during fueling
- Impulse purchase opportunities
- Broad demographics
- Frequent visits

**Office Buildings**:
- Professional audiences
- Lobby and elevator screens
- B2B opportunities
- Higher income demographics

**Bars and Restaurants**:
- Social, leisure audiences
- Extended dwell time
- Entertainment and lifestyle focus
- Evening and weekend concentration

**Medical Offices**:
- Waiting room screens
- Health-focused audiences
- Extended, attentive dwell time
- Older demographics

**Colleges and Universities**:
- Young adult audiences
- Tech-savvy, mobile-first
- Student-relevant products and services
- High engagement potential

### Advantages

- **Contextual Targeting**: Reach audiences in relevant environments
- **Extended Engagement**: Longer dwell times enable more complex messaging
- **Audience Segmentation**: Venue selection enables precise targeting
- **Purchase Proximity**: Often near point of purchase
- **Lower Clutter**: Less competitive ad environment than some other formats

### Best Use Cases

- Products and services aligned with venue context
- Niche audience targeting
- Consideration-stage messaging (more time to engage)
- Local business promotion
- Sampling and experiential campaigns

### Pricing

**CPM Range**: $8-$30 depending on venue type and exclusivity

**Factors**: Venue quality, audience demographics, dwell time, exclusivity

## Point-of-Purchase (POP) Displays

### Description

Digital screens located in retail environments near store entrances, checkout areas, and product displays, designed to influence purchase decisions at the moment of shopping.

### Locations

- Grocery store checkout lanes
- Convenience store counters
- Pharmacy waiting areas
- Retail store entrances and aisles
- Quick-service restaurant menu boards

### Characteristics

**Proximity to Purchase**: Located where purchase decisions are made

**Short Dwell Time**: Brief exposure as shoppers move through store

**Purchase Mindset**: Audiences actively shopping

**Impulse Opportunity**: Can trigger immediate purchases

### Advantages

- **Purchase Influence**: Direct impact on buying decisions
- **Impulse Purchases**: Trigger unplanned purchases
- **Product Promotion**: Highlight specific products or offers
- **Retail Partnerships**: Coordinate with in-store promotions
- **Measurable Impact**: Can track sales lift directly

### Best Use Cases

- Consumer packaged goods (CPG) promotion
- Impulse purchase products
- In-store promotions and offers
- New product launches
- Seasonal campaigns

### Pricing

**CPM Range**: $10-$35 depending on retailer and location

**Premium Pricing**: Major retailers and high-traffic stores command higher rates

## Street Furniture and Urban Panels

### Description

Digital displays integrated into urban infrastructure, including bus shelters, kiosks, newsstands, and standalone urban panels in high-pedestrian areas.

### Characteristics

**Pedestrian Focus**: Designed for foot traffic visibility

**Urban Concentration**: Primarily in downtown and dense urban areas

**Eye-Level Viewing**: Positioned for close-range engagement

**Interactive Potential**: Some formats enable touch or mobile interaction

### Advantages

- **Pedestrian Reach**: Captures walking audiences
- **Urban Targeting**: Concentrated in city centers
- **Close Engagement**: Viewers can read detailed information
- **Interactive Opportunities**: Enable QR codes, NFC, or touch interaction
- **Neighborhood Targeting**: Precise geographic focus

### Best Use Cases

- Urban market campaigns
- Retail and restaurant promotion
- Entertainment and events
- Mobile app downloads
- Interactive experiences

### Pricing

**CPM Range**: $6-$20 depending on location and market

## High-Impact and Landmark Locations

### Description

Massive, iconic digital installations in high-profile locations like Times Square, Piccadilly Circus, Las Vegas Strip, or The Sphere in Las Vegas.

### Characteristics

**Massive Scale**: Enormous displays creating unforgettable impressions

**Iconic Locations**: World-famous landmarks and destinations

**Tourist and Local Reach**: Mix of residents and visitors

**PR Value**: Generate earned media and social sharing

**Premium Pricing**: Highest cost per impression in DOOH

### Advantages

- **Unmatched Impact**: Create memorable, attention-grabbing moments
- **Brand Prestige**: Association with iconic locations enhances brand perception
- **Earned Media**: Generate PR coverage and social media buzz
- **Global Visibility**: Reach international audiences
- **Creative Showcase**: Platform for innovative, artistic executions

### Best Use Cases

- Major brand campaigns and launches
- Luxury and premium brands
- Entertainment premieres and events
- Corporate announcements
- Brands seeking PR and social amplification

### Pricing

**Cost Range**: $50,000-$500,000+ per week depending on location and size

**Premium Locations**: Times Square, Piccadilly Circus, Las Vegas Strip command highest rates

## Display Technology

### LED Displays

**Description**: Light-emitting diode screens that create images through thousands of individual LED lights.

**Characteristics**:
- High brightness for outdoor visibility
- Wide viewing angles
- Weather-resistant
- Long lifespan (50,000-100,000 hours)
- Energy efficient
- Scalable to any size

**Applications**: Outdoor billboards, large format displays, stadium screens

**Advantages**: Excellent outdoor visibility, durability, flexibility

**Considerations**: Higher upfront cost, pixel pitch affects resolution

### LCD Displays

**Description**: Liquid crystal display screens, similar to televisions and computer monitors.

**Characteristics**:
- High resolution and image quality
- Lower cost than LED for smaller sizes
- Better for close viewing distances
- Requires protection for outdoor use
- Standard sizes and aspect ratios

**Applications**: Indoor place-based media, transit, point-of-purchase

**Advantages**: High image quality, cost-effective for smaller formats

**Considerations**: Limited outdoor use, less flexible sizing

### Projection Systems

**Description**: Digital projectors that display images on surfaces or screens.

**Characteristics**:
- Flexible placement and sizing
- Can project on buildings or structures
- Lower equipment cost
- Requires darkness or low light
- Temporary or event-based installations

**Applications**: Building projections, event activations, guerrilla marketing

**Advantages**: Dramatic impact, flexible, temporary installations

**Considerations**: Lighting dependent, weather sensitive, temporary

### Interactive Displays

**Description**: Touchscreen or sensor-enabled displays that respond to user interaction.

**Characteristics**:
- Touch, gesture, or proximity sensing
- Enable user engagement and interaction
- Collect user data and preferences
- Typically indoor or protected environments
- Higher engagement potential

**Applications**: Retail kiosks, mall directories, experiential activations

**Advantages**: High engagement, data collection, memorable experiences

**Considerations**: Higher cost, maintenance requirements, limited locations

## Content Management Systems (CMS)

### Description

Software platforms that control what content displays on DOOH screens, when, and for how long.

### Key Functions

**Content Scheduling**: Determine which ads play at what times

**Rotation Management**: Control how multiple advertisers share screen time

**Dynamic Rules**: Apply conditions (weather, time, location) to content delivery

**Remote Management**: Update content across networks from central location

**Reporting**: Track impressions, play times, and performance

**Quality Control**: Ensure content meets specifications and plays correctly

### Major CMS Platforms

- **Broadsign**: Leading DOOH CMS with programmatic integration
- **Vistar Media**: CMS with programmatic marketplace
- **Ayuda Media Systems**: Enterprise DOOH management
- **Scala**: Digital signage and DOOH content management

## Ad Serving Technology

### Description

Technology that determines which ad to display on a screen at any given moment, especially in programmatic environments.

### Components

**Ad Server**: Central system that stores creative assets and serves them to screens

**Decision Engine**: Determines which ad wins the opportunity to display based on targeting, bidding, and availability

**Delivery Tracking**: Records when and where ads are displayed for reporting and billing

**Creative Optimization**: Selects best-performing creative variants

### Programmatic Ad Serving

In programmatic DOOH, ad serving happens in real-time:

1. Screen becomes available
2. SSP sends bid request with screen data (location, audience, context)
3. DSPs evaluate and submit bids
4. Highest bid wins
5. Winning creative is served to screen
6. Impression is recorded and reported

This process happens in milliseconds, enabling dynamic, data-driven ad delivery.

## Measurement Technology

### In-Screen Sensors

**Cameras**: Computer vision technology detects viewers, counts impressions, and analyzes demographics (age, gender) and attention

**Beacons**: Bluetooth or Wi-Fi beacons detect nearby mobile devices to estimate audience size

**Proximity Sensors**: Detect presence of viewers near screens

**Privacy Considerations**: Sensors use anonymized, aggregated data; no individual identification or tracking

### Mobility Data

**Description**: Anonymized mobile device location data used to estimate DOOH audience size, demographics, and behavior.

**How It Works**: Data providers collect opt-in location data from mobile apps, aggregate and anonymize it, then match device locations with DOOH screen locations to estimate exposure.

**Applications**:
- Audience size estimation
- Demographic profiling
- Footfall attribution (tracking store visits after DOOH exposure)
- Reach and frequency measurement

**Providers**: PlaceIQ, Cuebiq, Foursquare, Near

### Third-Party Verification

**Description**: Independent services that verify ad delivery, viewability, and audience measurement.

**Functions**:
- Confirm ads played as scheduled
- Verify impression counts
- Validate audience estimates
- Detect fraud or discrepancies

**Providers**: Geopath, IAS (Integral Ad Science), DoubleVerify

## Emerging Technologies

### Artificial Intelligence

**Applications in DOOH**:
- **Audience Analysis**: AI analyzes camera data to understand viewer demographics and attention
- **Creative Optimization**: Machine learning identifies best-performing creative variants
- **Predictive Planning**: AI forecasts audience size and behavior for better campaign planning
- **Dynamic Content**: AI-driven content adaptation based on real-time conditions

### Augmented Reality

**Description**: AR-enabled DOOH experiences allow viewers to interact with screens using smartphones, overlaying digital content on the physical world.

**Applications**:
- Virtual product try-ons
- Interactive games and experiences
- 3D product visualizations
- Social media integration

**Benefits**: High engagement, memorable experiences, social sharing

### 5G Connectivity

**Impact on DOOH**:
- Faster content delivery and updates
- Richer, higher-resolution content
- Real-time interactivity
- Enhanced measurement and data collection
- Support for AR and VR experiences

### Blockchain

**Potential Applications**:
- Transparent, verifiable impression tracking
- Fraud prevention
- Smart contracts for automated buying and payment
- Decentralized ad exchanges

**Status**: Emerging technology with limited current adoption but growing interest

## Technical Specifications

### Creative Specifications

Common technical requirements for DOOH creative:

**File Formats**: MP4 (H.264 codec), JPG, PNG, animated GIF

**Resolution**: 1920x1080 (Full HD) minimum, 4K (3840x2160) for premium placements

**Aspect Ratios**: 16:9 (landscape), 9:16 (portrait), 1:1 (square), custom ratios for specific screens

**File Size**: Typically 50-200MB maximum, varies by platform

**Duration**: 10-15 seconds standard, up to 30 seconds for some formats

**Frame Rate**: 30 fps standard

**Color Space**: RGB

**Audio**: Generally not used in DOOH (silent environments)

**Safe Zones**: Account for screen bezels and viewing angles

### Platform Requirements

Different DOOH platforms and networks have specific technical requirements. Always consult platform specifications before creating creative.

**Best Practice**: Create flexible, modular creative systems that can be adapted to various specifications rather than creating unique assets for every placement.

## Conclusion

DOOH encompasses a diverse range of formats and technologies, from massive highway billboards to intimate place-based screens, each serving different strategic purposes. Understanding these formats—their characteristics, advantages, and best use cases—enables effective campaign planning and execution. As technology continues to evolve with AI, AR, 5G, and advanced sensors, DOOH will become increasingly sophisticated, interactive, and measurable, offering advertisers ever more powerful ways to reach and engage audiences in the physical world.
