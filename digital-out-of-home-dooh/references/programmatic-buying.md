# Programmatic DOOH Buying

## Introduction to Programmatic DOOH

Programmatic Digital Out-of-Home (pDOOH) represents the automation of DOOH advertising buying, selling, and delivery through technology platforms. Instead of manual negotiations and insertion orders, pDOOH uses software-driven processes, real-time bidding, and data-driven targeting to make DOOH advertising more efficient, flexible, and effective.

Programmatic DOOH is one of the fastest-growing segments of digital advertising, projected to represent over 50% of DOOH spending by 2027. This growth is driven by advertiser demand for automation, better targeting, transparent measurement, and integration with other digital channels.

## How Programmatic DOOH Works

### The pDOOH Ecosystem

Programmatic DOOH operates through an interconnected ecosystem of technology platforms:

**Demand-Side Platforms (DSPs)**: Software that advertisers use to plan, buy, and manage pDOOH campaigns. DSPs connect to multiple supply sources, enabling advertisers to access diverse inventory through a single interface.

**Supply-Side Platforms (SSPs)**: Technology that media owners use to make their DOOH inventory available for programmatic buying. SSPs connect to multiple DSPs and ad exchanges, maximizing inventory monetization.

**Ad Exchanges**: Marketplaces where DOOH inventory is bought and sold through real-time bidding. Exchanges connect DSPs and SSPs, facilitating automated transactions.

**Data Management Platforms (DMPs)**: Systems that collect, organize, and activate audience data for targeting. DMPs provide the data signals that enable precise pDOOH targeting.

**Measurement Partners**: Third-party services that provide impression verification, attribution, and analytics.

### The Programmatic Transaction Process

1. **Inventory Availability**: A DOOH screen becomes available to display an ad (e.g., rotation slot opens)

2. **Bid Request**: The SSP sends a bid request to connected DSPs, including data about the screen (location, audience, time, context, floor price)

3. **Bid Evaluation**: DSPs evaluate the opportunity against active campaigns' targeting criteria, budgets, and bidding strategies

4. **Bid Submission**: DSPs that find the opportunity relevant submit bids indicating how much they're willing to pay

5. **Auction**: The ad exchange conducts an auction (typically second-price auction) to determine the winning bid

6. **Ad Serving**: The winning advertiser's creative is served to the screen and displayed

7. **Impression Recording**: The impression is recorded and reported to the advertiser

8. **Billing**: The advertiser is charged based on the winning bid price

This entire process happens in milliseconds, enabling real-time, data-driven ad delivery.

## Programmatic Buying Models

### Open Exchange (oRTB)

**Description**: Open marketplace where any advertiser can bid on available inventory in real-time through open real-time bidding (oRTB) protocols.

**How It Works**: 
- Inventory is made available to all DSPs connected to the exchange
- Advertisers compete in real-time auctions
- Highest bid wins the impression
- Pricing is dynamic based on demand

**Advantages**:
- Maximum inventory access and scale
- Competitive pricing through open auction
- Flexibility and efficiency
- Easy to start and scale

**Considerations**:
- Less control over specific placements
- Potential for lower-quality inventory
- Brand safety requires careful targeting
- Pricing can be volatile

**Best For**: Performance-focused campaigns requiring scale and efficiency

### Private Marketplace (PMP)

**Description**: Invitation-only auctions where selected advertisers bid on premium inventory with preferential terms.

**How It Works**:
- Media owners invite specific advertisers or agencies
- Participants bid in closed auction
- May include floor prices or preferred pricing
- Often includes premium or exclusive inventory

**Advantages**:
- Access to premium inventory
- Better pricing than open exchange
- More control and transparency
- Stronger media owner relationships
- Brand safety assurance

**Considerations**:
- Requires invitation or relationship
- May have minimum spend requirements
- Less inventory than open exchange

**Best For**: Brand campaigns requiring premium placements and quality assurance

### Programmatic Guaranteed (PG)

**Description**: Automated buying with fixed pricing and guaranteed impressions, combining programmatic efficiency with traditional deal certainty.

**How It Works**:
- Advertiser and media owner agree on fixed price and impression volume
- Transaction is executed programmatically through platforms
- Guaranteed delivery of agreed impressions
- No auction—fixed, negotiated pricing

**Advantages**:
- Guaranteed delivery and pricing
- Programmatic efficiency and reporting
- Premium inventory access
- Budget certainty
- Simplified planning

**Considerations**:
- Less flexibility than auction-based buying
- May require higher minimum spend
- Limited to specific media owner relationships

**Best For**: Major campaigns requiring guaranteed delivery and premium placements

### Preferred Deals

**Description**: Negotiated deals where advertisers get first look at inventory at fixed prices before it goes to open auction.

**How It Works**:
- Advertiser negotiates preferred access and pricing
- Gets first opportunity to buy inventory at agreed price
- If advertiser passes, inventory goes to open auction
- Combines negotiated terms with programmatic flexibility

**Advantages**:
- Preferred access to quality inventory
- Negotiated pricing
- Flexibility to accept or pass on each opportunity
- Programmatic efficiency

**Considerations**:
- Requires negotiation and relationships
- No delivery guarantee (can pass on inventory)
- May have minimum spend expectations

**Best For**: Advertisers wanting preferred access with flexibility

## Targeting Capabilities

Programmatic DOOH enables sophisticated targeting based on multiple data signals:

### Location-Based Targeting

**Geographic Targeting**: Target by country, region, state, city, zip code, or custom geographic boundaries

**Venue Type Targeting**: Select specific venue categories (airports, gyms, malls, transit, etc.)

**Proximity Targeting**: Target screens near specific locations (stores, competitors, events)

**Geo-Fencing**: Define custom geographic boundaries and target screens within them

**Radius Targeting**: Target screens within a specified radius of a point of interest

**Applications**:
- Local business promotion
- Store traffic driving
- Market-specific campaigns
- Competitive conquesting
- Event-based targeting

### Time-Based Targeting (Dayparting)

**Description**: Schedule ads for specific times of day, days of week, or date ranges when target audiences are most present or receptive.

**Options**:
- Time of day (morning, afternoon, evening, night)
- Day of week (weekdays, weekends, specific days)
- Date ranges (campaign flights, seasonal periods)
- Hourly precision

**Applications**:
- Breakfast ads in morning commute
- Lunch promotions midday
- Happy hour ads in evening
- Weekend entertainment promotion
- Seasonal campaigns

### Contextual Targeting

**Weather-Based**: Adapt messaging based on current or forecasted weather conditions
- Promote umbrellas when raining
- Advertise cold beverages on hot days
- Showcase winter products during cold snaps

**Event-Based**: Target around local events, sports games, concerts, or conferences
- Promote restaurants near event venues
- Advertise transportation during events
- Showcase relevant products or services

**Traffic-Based**: Adjust delivery based on traffic conditions
- Increase frequency during high-traffic periods
- Promote alternative routes or services

**Sports Scores**: Display messaging based on local team performance
- Celebrate wins with congratulatory messages
- Offer consolation promotions after losses

**News and Trends**: Respond to breaking news or trending topics (with appropriate brand safety)

### Audience-Based Targeting

**Demographic Targeting**: Target screens that over-index for specific age, gender, income, or education demographics based on mobility data and census information

**Behavioral Targeting**: Use mobility patterns and visit history to identify audiences with specific behaviors or interests
- Frequent gym-goers
- Regular restaurant diners
- Shoppers at specific retailers
- Commuters with specific routes

**Interest-Based Targeting**: Target audiences based on inferred interests from location visit patterns
- Sports enthusiasts (visit stadiums, sports bars)
- Fitness-focused (gyms, health food stores)
- Luxury shoppers (high-end retail visits)

**Lookalike Audiences**: Target screens that reach audiences similar to your existing customers based on mobility and behavioral patterns

**First-Party Data Integration**: Use your own customer data (hashed emails, loyalty IDs) to target or exclude specific audiences

### Retargeting

**Cross-Channel Retargeting**: Re-engage audiences exposed to DOOH ads on other digital channels
- Serve mobile ads to people who saw DOOH ads
- Retarget on social media, display, or CTV
- Sequential messaging across channels

**DOOH-to-DOOH Retargeting**: Reach audiences who were exposed to DOOH ads with follow-up DOOH messages in different locations

**Applications**:
- Reinforce messaging across touchpoints
- Move audiences through the funnel
- Increase frequency and recall
- Drive online or offline actions

## Campaign Setup and Management

### Campaign Planning

**Define Objectives**: Establish clear goals (awareness, traffic, conversions) and KPIs

**Identify Target Audience**: Define demographics, behaviors, locations, and contexts

**Set Budget**: Determine total budget and daily/weekly pacing

**Select Formats**: Choose DOOH formats aligned with objectives (large format, transit, place-based)

**Develop Creative**: Create DOOH-optimized creative assets in required specifications

**Establish Measurement Plan**: Define how success will be measured and tracked

### Campaign Setup in DSP

**Create Campaign**:
- Name and organize campaign
- Set campaign dates and budget
- Define pacing (even, ASAP, custom)

**Configure Targeting**:
- Select geographic targets
- Choose venue types or specific screens
- Set dayparting schedule
- Apply contextual triggers (weather, events)
- Add audience targeting
- Set frequency caps if desired

**Upload Creative**:
- Upload creative assets
- Assign creative to placements
- Set creative rotation (even, optimized)

**Set Bidding Strategy**:
- Choose bidding model (CPM, fixed price)
- Set maximum bid or target CPM
- Configure bid adjustments for priority targets

**Add Tracking**:
- Implement tracking pixels
- Set up conversion tracking
- Configure attribution settings

**Review and Launch**:
- Review all settings
- Submit for approval if required
- Launch campaign

### Campaign Monitoring

**Daily Monitoring**:
- Check delivery and pacing
- Review performance metrics
- Monitor budget spend
- Identify any issues or anomalies

**Performance Metrics to Track**:
- Impressions delivered
- Reach and frequency
- CPM and total spend
- Geographic and venue performance
- Time-of-day performance
- Creative performance
- Attribution metrics (traffic, conversions)

**Optimization Actions**:
- Adjust bids for better performance or efficiency
- Shift budget to top-performing locations or times
- Pause underperforming placements
- Test new targeting parameters
- Rotate or update creative
- Adjust frequency caps

### Campaign Optimization

**Performance Analysis**: Regularly analyze campaign data to identify optimization opportunities

**Bid Optimization**: 
- Increase bids for high-performing placements
- Decrease bids for less effective placements
- Test different bidding strategies

**Targeting Refinement**:
- Expand successful targeting parameters
- Narrow or exclude poor performers
- Test new audience segments or contexts

**Creative Optimization**:
- Identify top-performing creative variants
- Pause underperforming creative
- Test new creative approaches
- Implement dynamic creative optimization

**Budget Reallocation**:
- Shift budget to top-performing formats, locations, or times
- Reduce spend on underperformers
- Test new opportunities with small budget allocations

**Frequency Management**:
- Adjust frequency caps to balance reach and repetition
- Avoid ad fatigue with appropriate capping
- Increase frequency for key audiences or locations

## Data and Measurement

### Data Sources for pDOOH

**Mobility Data**: Anonymized location data from mobile devices used to estimate audience size, demographics, and behavior

**Census Data**: Demographic information about geographic areas

**Weather Data**: Real-time and forecasted weather conditions

**Traffic Data**: Vehicle and pedestrian traffic volumes

**Event Data**: Information about local events, sports games, concerts

**POI Data**: Points of interest (stores, restaurants, venues) for proximity targeting

**First-Party Data**: Advertiser's own customer data for targeting and measurement

### Measurement and Attribution

**Impression Tracking**: Record and report impressions delivered by screen, location, and time

**Reach and Frequency**: Estimate unique viewers and average exposure frequency

**Footfall Attribution**: Track store visits among audiences exposed to DOOH ads using mobility data

**Online Actions**: Measure website visits, app activity, or searches among exposed audiences

**Brand Lift**: Survey-based measurement of awareness, perception, or intent changes

**Sales Lift**: Connect DOOH exposure to actual purchases using loyalty data or device ID matching

**Multi-Touch Attribution**: Understand DOOH's role in customer journeys with multiple touchpoints

### Reporting

**Standard Reports**:
- Delivery summary (impressions, reach, frequency)
- Performance by dimension (location, time, creative)
- Budget and pacing
- Attribution metrics

**Custom Reports**: Tailored reports for specific KPIs or stakeholder needs

**Real-Time Dashboards**: Live performance monitoring during campaigns

**Post-Campaign Analysis**: Comprehensive campaign review with insights and recommendations

## Best Practices

### Strategic Planning

1. **Start with Clear Objectives**: Define specific, measurable goals before launching
2. **Integrate with Omnichannel Strategy**: Use pDOOH as part of coordinated multi-channel campaigns
3. **Leverage Data for Targeting**: Apply audience, location, and contextual data to improve relevance
4. **Test and Learn**: Start with tests across multiple approaches to identify what works
5. **Plan for Optimization**: Build in time and budget for ongoing optimization

### Buying and Activation

1. **Choose the Right Buying Model**: Match buying model to campaign objectives (open exchange for scale, PMP for quality)
2. **Set Appropriate Bids**: Bid competitively for priority inventory while maintaining efficiency
3. **Use Frequency Caps**: Prevent ad fatigue with appropriate frequency limits
4. **Implement Dayparting**: Schedule ads for times when target audiences are most present
5. **Monitor and Adjust**: Actively manage campaigns with regular optimization

### Creative and Messaging

1. **Design for DOOH**: Create DOOH-specific creative optimized for the medium
2. **Use Dynamic Creative**: Leverage DCO to adapt messaging based on context
3. **Test Creative Variants**: Run multiple versions to identify top performers
4. **Keep It Simple**: Use clear, concise messaging appropriate for viewing conditions
5. **Ensure Technical Compliance**: Follow platform specifications for creative assets

### Measurement and Optimization

1. **Implement Comprehensive Tracking**: Use multiple measurement methods for complete view
2. **Set Realistic KPIs**: Establish appropriate benchmarks based on objectives and channel characteristics
3. **Analyze Regularly**: Review performance data weekly to identify trends and opportunities
4. **Optimize Continuously**: Make data-driven adjustments throughout campaign
5. **Document Learnings**: Capture insights to inform future campaigns

## Challenges and Solutions

### Challenge: Inventory Fragmentation

**Problem**: DOOH inventory is distributed across many media owners and platforms, making it difficult to access comprehensively.

**Solutions**:
- Use DSPs that aggregate inventory from multiple sources
- Establish relationships with major SSPs and media owners
- Leverage private marketplaces for preferred access
- Work with agencies that have broad inventory relationships

### Challenge: Data Quality and Availability

**Problem**: Audience data for DOOH targeting may be less precise or comprehensive than other digital channels.

**Solutions**:
- Use multiple data sources to triangulate insights
- Focus on contextual and location-based targeting where data is strongest
- Validate data quality with measurement partners
- Combine DOOH data with first-party data for better targeting

### Challenge: Attribution Complexity

**Problem**: Connecting DOOH exposure to business outcomes remains more challenging than purely digital channels.

**Solutions**:
- Implement multiple attribution methods (footfall, online actions, brand lift)
- Use device ID passback for cross-channel attribution
- Conduct incrementality tests to measure true impact
- Set appropriate attribution windows (14-30 days)
- Focus on full-funnel metrics, not just direct response

### Challenge: Creative Production

**Problem**: Creating effective DOOH creative requires different approaches and multiple format variations.

**Solutions**:
- Develop DOOH-specific creative guidelines
- Use flexible, modular creative systems
- Leverage dynamic creative platforms
- Partner with DOOH-experienced creative teams
- Test and iterate based on performance data

Programmatic DOOH is expected to grow from approximately 30% of DOOH spending in 2024 to over 50% by 2027, driven by:
- Increasing advertiser adoption
- Expanding inventory availability
- Improving measurement and attribution
- Greater integration with other digital channels
- Enhanced targeting capabilities

**AI and Machine Learning**: Advanced algorithms for audience prediction, creative optimization, and campaign management

**Enhanced Measurement**: More sophisticated attribution models and real-time performance tracking

**Omnichannel Integration**: Deeper integration with mobile, CTV, social, and other digital channels

## Conclusion

Programmatic DOOH has transformed out-of-home advertising from a manual, static medium into a dynamic, data-driven channel that rivals other digital media in targeting precision, flexibility, and measurement. By automating buying processes, enabling sophisticated targeting, and providing transparent performance data, pDOOH makes DOOH advertising more accessible, efficient, and effective for advertisers of all sizes.

Success in programmatic DOOH requires understanding the ecosystem, selecting appropriate platforms and buying models, leveraging data for targeting, creating effective creative, and implementing comprehensive measurement. As the technology continues to evolve and adoption grows, programmatic DOOH will become an increasingly essential component of omnichannel marketing strategies, offering unique opportunities to reach and engage audiences in the physical world with the sophistication of digital advertising.
