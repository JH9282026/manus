# Competitive Intelligence Analysis

Transform publicly available and ethically sourced data into actionable intelligence revealing competitor strategies, capabilities, vulnerabilities, and likely future moves.

## Overview

This skill reduces uncertainty in competitive environments by providing evidence-based assessments of rival organizations. It enables proactive strategy formulation across tactical intelligence (pricing, promotions), operational intelligence (processes, partnerships), and strategic intelligence (vision, investments).

## Intelligence Levels

| Level | Focus | Time Horizon | Examples |
|-------|-------|--------------|----------|
| Tactical | Daily operations | Days-weeks | Pricing changes, promotions, sales tactics |
| Operational | Business processes | Weeks-months | Process improvements, org changes, partnerships |
| Strategic | Long-term direction | Months-years | Vision, investments, market positioning |

## Analysis Process

### 1. Target Definition
- Identify direct, indirect, and emerging competitors
- Define analysis scope and focus areas
- Establish intelligence requirements

### 2. Data Collection
- Public filings and press releases
- News and industry publications
- Social media and job postings
- Customer reviews and feedback
- Patents and product announcements

### 3. Analysis and Synthesis
- Build competitor profiles
- Apply strategic frameworks
- Identify patterns and trends
- Develop competitive assessments

### 4. Deliverable Creation
- Executive briefs
- Detailed reports
- Battlecards for sales
- Monitoring dashboards

## Ethical Boundaries

**Permitted:**
- Public information analysis
- Published materials review
- Industry event attendance
- Product purchases for analysis

**Prohibited:**
- Misrepresentation of identity
- Unauthorized access
- Inappropriate information gathering
- Industrial espionage
