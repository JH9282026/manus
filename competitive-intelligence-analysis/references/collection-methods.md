# Intelligence Collection Methods

Ethical approaches to gathering competitive intelligence.

---

## Primary Sources

### Company-Generated
- Official website content
- Press releases and news
- SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, S-1)
- Earnings calls and transcripts
- Investor presentations
- Patent filings

### Public Events
- Industry conferences
- Trade shows
- Webinars and podcasts
- Speaking engagements

---

## Secondary Sources

### Industry Analysis
- Analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- Industry publications
- Market research firms
- Academic research

### Digital Footprint
- Social media presence
- Job postings analysis
- Website traffic estimates (SimilarWeb)
- App store reviews
- SEO/SEM analysis

### Stakeholder Input
- Customer reviews (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius)
- Employee reviews (Glassdoor, Indeed)
- Supplier and partner relationships
- Expert network interviews

---

## Collection Best Practices

### Source Validation
1. Cross-reference multiple sources
2. Assess source credibility
3. Note publication dates
4. Document source URLs

### Data Currency
- Prioritize recent information
- Flag data older than 12 months
- Track information changes over time

### Organization
- Centralized intelligence repository
- Consistent tagging and categorization
- Version control for profiles
- Audit trail maintenance
