# Financial Analysis and Presentation

## DuPont Analysis
Decompose ROE into three drivers:
ROE = Net Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier

| Component | Measures | Improvement Levers |
|---|---|---|
| Net Margin | Profitability | Pricing, cost reduction |
| Asset Turnover | Efficiency | Revenue growth, asset optimization |
| Equity Multiplier | Leverage | Debt management, capital structure |

## Benchmarking Analysis

### Peer Selection Criteria
- Same industry/sub-industry (GICS classification)
- Similar revenue size (0.5x to 2.0x target)
- Similar business model and geographic mix
- Publicly traded (for data availability)
- 8-12 peers typically sufficient

### Benchmarking Metrics
- Revenue growth (1-year, 3-year CAGR)
- Gross margin, operating margin, net margin
- ROE, ROA, ROIC
- Revenue per employee
- EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E multiples
- Debt/equity, interest coverage

### Quartile Analysis
Rank target company against peers:
- Top quartile (>75th percentile): Strength
- Second quartile (50-75th): Above average
- Third quartile (25-50th): Below average
- Bottom quartile (<25th): Weakness / improvement area

## Financial Presentation Best Practices

### Executive Summary Slide
- 3-5 key takeaways in bullet form
- Revenue and profitability headline numbers
- Comparison to prior period and plan
- Outlook or forward guidance summary

### Financial Chart Guidelines
- Use waterfall charts for variance analysis
- Use line charts for trends over time
- Use bar charts for comparisons across categories
- Use stacked charts for composition analysis
- Label data points directly (avoid legend-only)
- Show absolute values and percentage changes

### Variance Analysis Format
Present actual vs budget/forecast with:
- $ variance (absolute difference)
- % variance (percentage difference)
- Favorable/unfavorable indication (color coding)
- Key driver explanation for material variances (>5%)

## Common Financial Analysis Pitfalls

1. **Ignoring quality of earnings**: Not all revenue is equal (recurring vs one-time)
2. **Looking at single ratios in isolation**: Always compare to peers and trends
3. **Confusing accounting profit with cash**: Cash flow analysis is essential
4. **Extrapolating unsustainable trends**: High growth rates mean-revert
5. **Ignoring off-balance-sheet items**: Leases, contingent liabilities, guarantees
6. **Not adjusting for one-time items**: Restructuring, litigation, asset sales
7. **Anchoring to management guidance**: Independent analysis is critical
