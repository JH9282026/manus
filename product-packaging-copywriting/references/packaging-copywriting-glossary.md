## Packaging Copywriting Glossary

**Bleed:** When design extends beyond the trim edge to prevent white borders

**Call-out:** A highlighted text element drawing attention to a specific feature

**Die line:** The cut line that defines the final package shape

**Facing:** The number of product units facing forward on a shelf

**Flash:** A burst or star shape containing promotional text

**INCI:** International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients, standardized ingredient naming

**Keyline:** The outline showing the package's die-cut shape

**PDQ:** Pretty Darn Quick display, a pre-packed retail display

**POP:** Point of Purchase, in-store displays and materials

**Primary Display Panel (PDP):** The main face of the package seen by consumers

**SKU:** Stock Keeping Unit, a unique product identifier

**Substrate:** The material on which packaging is printed

**UPC:** Universal Product Code, the barcode on packages

**Violator:** An attention-grabbing graphic element (like a "NEW!" burst)
