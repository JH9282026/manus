### Copywriting for Different Packaging Shapes

The physical form of packaging influences copy placement and strategy.

**Bottles and jars:**
- Limited front "billboard" space
- Label wrap-around opportunities
- Neck tags for promotions
- Cap/lid messaging possibilities

**Boxes and cartons:**
- Multiple panels to work with
- Clear front/back/side hierarchy
- Interior surfaces for extended content
- Flaps and closures for extra messaging

**Tubes:**
- Narrow, cylindrical surface
- Often displayed horizontally
- Consider "right-side-up" orientation
- Squeeze area may obscure text

**Pouches and bags:**
- Flat when empty, dimensional when filled
- Both sides potentially visible
- Resealable areas can carry messaging
- May hang or stand on shelf

**Clamshells and blister packs:**
- Visible product is key
- Limited copy real estate
- Security/tamper-evident features
- Hang tag or stand orientation
