# Persona Creation & Templates

## Persona Research Methods

### Quantitative Research

| Method | Purpose | Sample Size |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Customer surveys | Validate segment characteristics | 200+ per segment |
| Web analytics | Behavioral patterns | Full population |
| CRM data analysis | Purchase patterns, lifecycle | Full population |
| A/B test results | Preference validation | Statistical significance |

### Qualitative Research

| Method | Purpose | Sample Size |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Customer interviews | Deep motivation understanding | 8-12 per persona |
| Focus groups | Group dynamics, shared perspectives | 2-3 groups per persona |
| Ethnographic observation | In-context behavior | 5-8 per persona |
| Sales team interviews | Buying process insights | 5-10 reps |
| Support team interviews | Pain point identification | 5-10 agents |

## Detailed Persona Template

### Section 1: Identity

```
Name: [First Last — make it memorable]
Photo: [Stock photo representing the persona]
Title/Role: [Job title or life role]
Demographics: [Age, location, education, income, family status]
Quote: ["A sentence that captures their worldview"]
```

### Section 2: Background

```
Professional Background:
- Career path and current role
- Company type and size
- Key responsibilities
- Reporting structure
- Technology comfort level

Personal Context:
- Life stage and priorities
- Hobbies and interests
- Information sources
- Social media habits
```

### Section 3: Goals and Challenges

```
Primary Goals:
1. [Most important professional/personal goal]
2. [Second priority goal]
3. [Third priority goal]

Key Challenges:
1. [Biggest frustration or obstacle]
2. [Secondary challenge]
3. [Tertiary challenge]

What Success Looks Like:
- [Measurable outcome they desire]
```

### Section 4: Buying Behavior

```
Decision Process:
- Trigger: [What initiates their search]
- Research: [How they evaluate options]
- Influencers: [Who affects their decision]
- Criteria: [Top 3 decision factors]
- Timeline: [Typical purchase timeline]

Objections:
1. [Primary objection to your solution]
2. [Secondary objection]
3. [Tertiary objection]

Preferred Channels:
- Discovery: [Where they first learn about solutions]
- Research: [Where they evaluate deeply]
- Purchase: [How they prefer to buy]
- Support: [How they prefer to get help]
```

### Section 5: Messaging Guide

```
Value Proposition for This Persona:
[One sentence tailored to their specific needs]

Key Messages:
1. [Message addressing their primary goal]
2. [Message addressing their biggest challenge]
3. [Message overcoming their top objection]

Content Preferences:
- Format: [Blog, video, podcast, whitepaper, webinar]
- Length: [Short-form, long-form, quick tips]
- Tone: [Formal, casual, technical, inspirational]
```

## Persona Validation Checklist

- [ ] Based on actual customer data, not assumptions
- [ ] Validated through customer interviews
- [ ] Distinct from other personas (minimal overlap)
- [ ] Actionable for marketing, product, and sales
- [ ] Includes both rational and emotional dimensions
- [ ] Reviewed by customer-facing teams
- [ ] Updated within the last 12 months

## Common Persona Mistakes

1. **Too many personas** — Limit to 3-5 primary personas
2. **Based on demographics only** — Include motivations and behaviors
3. **Not data-backed** — Ground in real customer research
4. **Too generic** — Make specific enough to guide decisions
5. **Never updated** — Review quarterly, refresh annually
6. **Not shared** — Distribute widely and reference regularly
