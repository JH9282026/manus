# Real Estate Investment Due Diligence and Risk Assessment

## Due Diligence Process

### Property Inspection
**Structural:** Foundation, roof, walls, framing
**Systems:** HVAC, electrical, plumbing
**Environmental:** Mold, asbestos, lead paint, soil contamination
**Code Compliance:** Building codes, ADA requirements

### Financial Analysis
**Income Verification:** Rent rolls, lease agreements
**Expense Review:** Operating statements, tax returns
**Rent Comparables:** Market rate analysis
**Occupancy History:** Vacancy trends

### Legal Due Diligence
**Title Search:** Clear ownership, liens, easements
**Zoning:** Permitted uses, restrictions
**Permits:** Building permits, certificates of occupancy
**Litigation:** Pending lawsuits, disputes

### Market Analysis
**Comparable Sales:** Recent transactions
**Rental Market:** Supply, demand, trends
**Economic Factors:** Employment, population, development
**Competition:** Similar properties, new construction

## Risk Assessment

### Market Risk
**Economic Downturn:** Recession impact
**Oversupply:** New construction flooding market
**Demand Shifts:** Demographic or preference changes

### Property-Specific Risk
**Physical Condition:** Deferred maintenance, major repairs
**Tenant Risk:** Credit quality, lease terms, turnover
**Location:** Neighborhood decline, environmental issues

### Financial Risk
**Interest Rate Risk:** Variable rate loans
**Leverage Risk:** High debt-to-equity ratio
**Cash Flow Risk:** Insufficient reserves

### Mitigation Strategies
**Diversification:** Multiple properties, markets, types
**Insurance:** Property, liability, loss of income
**Reserves:** Emergency fund for repairs and vacancies
**Professional Team:** Property manager, attorney, accountant

## Conclusion
Thorough due diligence and risk assessment protect investors from costly mistakes and enable informed decision-making.
