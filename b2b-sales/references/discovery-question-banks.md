# Discovery Question Banks for B2B Sales

Comprehensive question libraries organized by methodology, stakeholder type, and sales stage.

---

## SPIN Selling Question Bank

### Situation Questions

**Company & Market Context:**
- "Tell me about your company's position in the market."
- "What's your current go-to-market strategy?"
- "How is your industry evolving right now?"
- "What are your company's top strategic priorities this year?"

**Team Structure:**
- "Walk me through your sales team structure."
- "How many reps do you have? What's the split between hunters and farmers?"
- "Who reports to you? What are their primary responsibilities?"
- "How is your team organized geographically or by segment?"

**Current Tools & Processes:**
- "What CRM platform are you using?"
- "What other sales tools are in your tech stack?"
- "Walk me through your current sales process from lead to close."
- "How do you currently handle [specific function]?"

**Performance Baseline:**
- "What's your team's current quota attainment rate?"
- "What's your average deal size and sales cycle length?"
- "How accurate are your forecasts typically?"
- "What's your win rate on qualified opportunities?"

### Problem Questions

**Process Challenges:**
- "What's the biggest challenge your team faces in hitting quota?"
- "Where do deals typically get stuck in your pipeline?"
- "What's preventing your reps from being more productive?"
- "What aspects of your current process are most frustrating for your team?"

**Visibility & Forecasting:**
- "How confident are you in your pipeline visibility?"
- "What makes forecasting difficult for you?"
- "How often are you surprised by deals slipping or falling through?"
- "What information do you wish you had about your deals that you don't have today?"

**Efficiency & Productivity:**
- "How much time do your reps spend on non-selling activities?"
- "What manual tasks are eating up your team's time?"
- "Where are you seeing inefficiencies in your sales process?"
- "What's preventing your team from focusing on high-value activities?"

**Quality & Consistency:**
- "How consistent is performance across your team?"
- "What's the gap between your top and bottom performers?"
- "How do you ensure reps are following your sales methodology?"
- "What makes it hard to replicate your top performers' success?"

**Customer Experience:**
- "What feedback do you get from prospects about your sales process?"
- "Where do prospects express frustration in the buying journey?"
- "How personalized are your reps' interactions with prospects?"
- "What's your no-decision rate, and why do deals stall?"

### Implication Questions

**Revenue Impact:**
- "If your forecast accuracy doesn't improve, how does that affect your ability to plan?"
- "What's the cost of missing quota by 15% this quarter?"
- "How does pipeline unpredictability impact your hiring decisions?"
- "What revenue are you leaving on the table with your current win rate?"

**Team Impact:**
- "How does this inconsistency affect team morale?"
- "What happens when your top performers leave and take their knowledge with them?"
- "How much time do managers spend firefighting instead of coaching?"
- "What's the impact on ramp time for new hires?"

**Competitive Impact:**
- "How are competitors taking advantage of your longer sales cycles?"
- "What market share are you losing while deals sit in your pipeline?"
- "How does this affect your ability to compete for top sales talent?"
- "What happens if your competitors solve this problem before you do?"

**Executive & Board Impact:**
- "How does forecast inaccuracy affect your relationship with the board?"
- "What pressure are you under from leadership to improve these metrics?"
- "How does this impact your ability to secure budget for growth initiatives?"
- "What's at stake for you personally if these issues persist?"

**Customer Impact:**
- "How does this inconsistency affect customer experience?"
- "What's the long-term impact on customer lifetime value?"
- "How does a poor buying experience affect referrals and word-of-mouth?"
- "What's the cost of losing deals to 'no decision' instead of competitors?"

**Scaling Impact:**
- "How will these challenges compound as you try to scale?"
- "What happens when you double your team size with these inefficiencies?"
- "How does this limit your ability to enter new markets?"
- "What's preventing you from achieving your growth targets?"

### Need-Payoff Questions

**Value Realization:**
- "If you could improve forecast accuracy by 30%, what would that enable you to do?"
- "How valuable would it be to reduce your sales cycle by 20%?"
- "What would it mean if your reps could focus 40% more time on selling?"
- "How would increasing win rates by 15% impact your annual revenue?"

**Team Performance:**
- "What would it be worth to bring your bottom performers up to average?"
- "How would it help if new reps ramped to productivity 30% faster?"
- "What could you achieve with consistent execution across your entire team?"
- "How valuable is real-time visibility into deal health?"

**Strategic Outcomes:**
- "If you could predict pipeline gaps 60 days in advance, how would that change your planning?"
- "What would it mean to have confidence in your forecast every week?"
- "How would this impact your ability to make strategic investments?"
- "What opportunities would open up with predictable revenue growth?"

**Personal Impact:**
- "How would solving this affect your day-to-day stress level?"
- "What would it mean for your career to consistently hit your number?"
- "How would this change your relationship with the executive team?"
- "What could you focus on if you weren't constantly firefighting?"

## MEDDIC Question Bank

### Metrics (M)

**Quantifying Current State:**
- "What metrics are you trying to improve?"
- "How are you currently measuring success in this area?"
- "What's your baseline performance on [relevant metric]?"
- "What's the financial impact of the current state?"

**Defining Success:**
- "What would success look like in 6 months? 12 months?"
- "What improvement percentage would make this a home run?"
- "How do you calculate ROI for investments like this?"
- "What payback period do you need to justify this investment?"

**Quantifying Value:**
- "If we improve [metric] by X%, what's that worth to you?"
- "What's the cost of not solving this problem?"
- "How much revenue/cost/time is at stake?"
- "What's the opportunity cost of maintaining the status quo?"

### Economic Buyer (E)

**Identifying:**
- "Who has final approval authority for this investment?"
- "Whose budget will this come from?"
- "Who signed the contract for your current solution?"
- "Who can say 'yes' when everyone else says 'no'?"

**Accessing:**
- "What's the best way to engage [Economic Buyer]?"
- "Can you introduce me to [Economic Buyer]?"
- "When should we involve [Economic Buyer] in the process?"
- "What does [Economic Buyer] care most about?"

**Understanding:**
- "What are [Economic Buyer]'s top priorities this year?"
- "What metrics is [Economic Buyer] measured on?"
- "What keeps [Economic Buyer] up at night?"
- "How does [Economic Buyer] typically make decisions like this?"

### Decision Criteria (D)

**Uncovering:**
- "What criteria will you use to evaluate solutions?"
- "What are your must-have requirements vs. nice-to-haves?"
- "How will you compare different vendors?"
- "What would disqualify a vendor from consideration?"

**Prioritizing:**
- "Which criteria are most important to you?"
- "How are these criteria weighted in your evaluation?"
- "What trade-offs are you willing to make?"
- "If you had to choose between X and Y, which matters more?"

**Influencing:**
- "Have you considered [unique capability] as a criterion?"
- "Based on your goals, shouldn't [differentiator] be a must-have?"
- "How important is [your strength] to achieving your objectives?"
- "What's the risk of not having [your unique value]?"

### Decision Process (D)

**Mapping:**
- "Walk me through your typical buying process for a solution like this."
- "What are the formal approval stages?"
- "Who needs to sign off at each stage?"
- "What triggers progression from one stage to the next?"

**Timeline:**
- "What's your timeline for making a decision?"
- "What's driving that timeline?"
- "What could slow down or accelerate the process?"
- "When do you need the solution implemented and delivering value?"

**Risks:**
- "What could derail this process?"
- "What's happened in past purchases that caused delays?"
- "Who might raise objections, and what would they be?"
- "What approvals have surprised you in the past?"

**Validation:**
- "Have you purchased similar solutions before? How did that go?"
- "What did you learn from previous buying processes?"
- "What would you do differently this time?"
- "What's different about this purchase compared to past ones?"

### Identify Pain (I)

**Uncovering:**
- "What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"
- "What problem is driving this evaluation?"
- "What's not working with your current approach?"
- "What triggered you to start looking for a solution?"

**Quantifying:**
- "How long has this been a problem?"
- "What's this costing you in time, money, or resources?"
- "How many people/deals/customers are affected?"
- "What's the total annual impact?"

**Urgency:**
- "What happens if you don't solve this?"
- "Why is solving this a priority now vs. 6 months ago?"
- "What's the cost of delaying a decision?"
- "What's at risk if this continues?"

**Personal Pain:**
- "How does this affect you personally?"
- "What's the impact on your team's morale?"
- "How does this problem make you feel?"
- "What would solving this mean for your career?"

### Champion (C)

**Identifying:**
- "Who internally would benefit most from this solution?"
- "Who's been advocating for change?"
- "Who has the ear of the Economic Buyer?"
- "Who's most frustrated with the current state?"

**Qualifying:**
- "How much influence do you have in this decision?"
- "Can you introduce me to [Economic Buyer/key stakeholder]?"
- "What's your relationship with [decision-maker]?"
- "Have you successfully championed initiatives like this before?"

**Developing:**
- "What information would help you build the internal business case?"
- "What objections do you anticipate from others?"
- "How can I help you sell this internally?"
- "What do you need from me to be successful?"

**Testing:**
- "What's happening behind the scenes that I should know about?"
- "Who's likely to oppose this, and why?"
- "What's the political landscape I should understand?"
- "What advice do you have for navigating this process?"

## Challenger Sale Question Bank

### Teaching Questions

**Reframing Thinking:**
- "Have you considered that [contrarian insight]?"
- "What if I told you that [surprising fact]?"
- "Most companies in your industry believe X, but our research shows Y. What's your take?"
- "You mentioned [assumption]. Let me challenge that thinking..."

**Uncovering Blind Spots:**
- "What trends in your industry are you most concerned about?"
- "What are your competitors doing that you're not?"
- "What opportunities might you be missing with your current approach?"
- "What don't you know about your customers that you wish you did?"

**Creating Urgency:**
- "Did you know that [industry trend] is accelerating faster than most realize?"
- "What's your plan for [emerging threat/opportunity]?"
- "How are you preparing for [market shift]?"
- "What's the cost of being late to [trend]?"

### Tailoring Questions

**Understanding Priorities:**
- "What are your top 3 priorities this quarter?"
- "What does success look like for you personally?"
- "What metrics are you measured on?"
- "What's most important to you: [option A] or [option B]?"

**Adapting to Role:**
- "As a [role], what concerns you most about [topic]?"
- "How does this impact your [specific responsibility]?"
- "What would make this a win for your team?"
- "What's the biggest risk from your perspective?"

### Taking Control Questions

**Qualifying Hard:**
- "Based on what you've shared, I'm not sure we're the right fit. Here's why..."
- "This solution requires [commitment]. Are you prepared for that?"
- "Most companies that succeed with us have [characteristic]. Do you?"
- "If we can't deliver [outcome], should we even continue this conversation?"

**Pushing Back:**
- "I hear you want [unrealistic request]. Let me explain why that's not in your best interest..."
- "That timeline is aggressive. Here's what's realistic and why..."
- "You mentioned [approach]. I'd recommend against that because..."
- "Before we go further, let's discuss investment range. This typically costs [range]. Is that in the ballpark?"

**Setting Expectations:**
- "Here's what I need from you to make this successful..."
- "If we do X, I need you to commit to Y. Fair?"
- "Let's be clear about next steps. I propose [plan]. Does that work?"
- "What would prevent you from moving forward if we address your concerns?"

## Gap Selling Question Bank

### Current State Questions

**Understanding Today:**
- "Walk me through your current process for [X]."
- "What does a typical [workflow/day/deal] look like today?"
- "How are you currently handling [challenge]?"
- "What tools and resources are you using?"

**Quantifying Current State:**
- "What's this costing you in time/money/resources?"
- "How many [deals/customers/hours] are affected?"
- "What's your current performance on [metric]?"
- "How long have you been operating this way?"

**Identifying Problems:**
- "What's not working about your current approach?"
- "Where are the bottlenecks?"
- "What's most frustrating about the current state?"
- "What's preventing you from achieving better results?"

### Future State Questions

**Defining Desired Outcomes:**
- "Where do you want to be in 12 months?"
- "What would success look like?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?"
- "What's your ideal state?"

**Quantifying Future State:**
- "What would it be worth to achieve that?"
- "How would that impact your key metrics?"
- "What would that enable you to do?"
- "What's the value of reaching that goal?"

**Understanding Drivers:**
- "Why is this goal important to you?"
- "What's driving this desired change?"
- "Who else cares about achieving this?"
- "What happens when you reach this state?"

### Gap Questions

**Calculating the Gap:**
- "What's the difference between where you are and where you want to be?"
- "How big is the gap in [specific metric]?"
- "What's it costing you to stay in the current state?"
- "What's the opportunity cost of not reaching your goal?"

**Creating Urgency:**
- "What happens if you don't close this gap?"
- "How much longer can you afford to operate this way?"
- "What's the cost of delaying a decision by 3 months? 6 months?"
- "What are you losing every day/week/month?"

**Quantifying Total Impact:**
- "If we add up all the costs and missed opportunities, what's the total annual impact?"
- "What's the 3-year cost of not solving this?"
- "How does this gap affect other areas of your business?"
- "What's the ripple effect of this problem?"

## Advanced Discovery Techniques

### The 5 Whys

Start with a problem and ask "why" five times to get to the root cause:

1. "Why is that a problem?"
2. "Why does that happen?"
3. "Why is that the case?"
4. "Why hasn't this been addressed?"
5. "Why is it important to solve now?"

### The Columbo Technique

Act slightly confused to get prospects to explain more:
- "I'm not sure I understand. Can you explain that again?"
- "Help me understand why that's the case."
- "That's interesting. Tell me more about that."
- "I'm curious about [X]. Can you elaborate?"

### The Negative Reverse

Take away the solution to increase desire:
- "Based on what you've shared, I'm not sure we're the right fit."
- "This might be more than you need."
- "I'm concerned this won't work for you because..."
- "Maybe we should wait until [condition is met]."

### The Hypothetical

Use hypothetical scenarios to uncover true priorities:
- "If budget weren't a concern, what would you do?"
- "If you could solve only one problem, which would it be?"
- "Imagine it's a year from now and this has been a huge success. What happened?"
- "What if I told you [scenario]? How would that change things?"

### The Comparison

Use comparisons to understand priorities:
- "How does this compare to [other initiative]?"
- "If you had to choose between [A] and [B], which matters more?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how important is this vs. [other priority]?"
- "What would you sacrifice to make this happen?"

## Question Frameworks

### SPIN Sequence Template

1. **Situation:** "Tell me about [current state]."
2. **Problem:** "What challenges are you experiencing with [situation]?"
3. **Implication:** "How does [problem] affect [broader impact]?"
4. **Need-Payoff:** "What would it mean if you could [solve problem]?"

### Pain-Impact-Vision Framework

1. **Pain:** "What's the problem?"
2. **Impact:** "What's the cost/consequence?"
3. **Vision:** "What would success look like?"

### Current-Future-Gap Framework

1. **Current:** "Where are you today?"
2. **Future:** "Where do you want to be?"
3. **Gap:** "What's preventing you from getting there?"

### Problem-Cause-Effect Framework

1. **Problem:** "What's not working?"
2. **Cause:** "Why is that happening?"
3. **Effect:** "What's the impact?"

## Best Practices for Discovery Questions

### Do's

- **Ask open-ended questions** that require more than yes/no answers
- **Listen more than you talk** (aim for 70% buyer talk time)
- **Ask follow-up questions** to go deeper
- **Pause after asking** to give them time to think
- **Take notes** to show you're listening and capture details
- **Confirm understanding** by summarizing what you heard
- **Ask permission** before diving into sensitive topics
- **Build on their answers** rather than jumping to your next question

### Don'ts

- **Don't interrogate** — make it a conversation, not an interview
- **Don't ask questions you should already know** the answer to
- **Don't ask leading questions** that telegraph the answer you want
- **Don't interrupt** when they're answering
- **Don't jump to solutions** before fully understanding the problem
- **Don't ask multiple questions at once** — one at a time
- **Don't make it about you** — focus on them
- **Don't skip the hard questions** about budget, authority, and timeline

---

## Conclusion

Effective discovery is the foundation of successful B2B sales. Use these question banks as a starting point, but always:

1. **Customize questions** to your specific industry, product, and buyer
2. **Sequence questions logically** to build understanding progressively
3. **Listen actively** and ask follow-up questions based on their answers
4. **Adapt in real-time** based on what you're learning
5. **Focus on understanding** before prescribing solutions
6. **Document insights** to inform your approach and share with your team

The best salespeople don't just ask questions — they have genuine curiosity about their prospects' businesses and use questions to uncover insights that lead to mutual value creation.
