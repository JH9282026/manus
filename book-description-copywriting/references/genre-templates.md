# Genre-Specific Templates

Proven description structures for each major genre.

---

## Fiction Templates

### Romance

```
[HOOK about meeting/connection]
[CHARACTER 1 intro with desire/flaw]
[CHARACTER 2 intro with desire/flaw]
[CONFLICT keeping them apart]
[STAKES - what they'll lose]
[INTRIGUE - will they choose love?]
[COMPARISON TITLES]
```

**Example:**
> She swore she'd never fall for another cowboy. Then she met Jake.
>
> Sophie Martinez has exactly four days until her family's annual Christmas gathering—and zero plans to explain another year of being single. Enter Jake: stranded traveler, unlikely savior, and hands-down the most infuriating man she's ever met.
>
> Their arrangement is simple: he plays devoted boyfriend, she provides room and board. No feelings, no complications, no falling for each other.
>
> *For fans of fake dating and small-town romance.*

---

### Thriller/Suspense

```
[HOOK with immediate danger]
[PROTAGONIST in crisis]
[INCITING INCIDENT]
[ESCALATING THREAT]
[STAKES - life/death/devastating loss]
[CLIFFHANGER question]
[COMPARISON TITLES]
```

**Example:**
> She thought she knew her neighbors. She was dead wrong.
>
> Emma Chen has finally found her dream home—a quiet street in a picture-perfect suburb where everyone waves hello. But when her neighbor vanishes without a trace, Emma starts noticing things that don't add up.
>
> The couple who never leaves the house. The package deliveries at 3 AM. The screams she's almost certain she heard.
>
> *For fans of Ruth Ware and Riley Sager.*

---

### Mystery

```
[HOOK with crime/puzzle]
[DETECTIVE/SLEUTH intro]
[SETTING atmosphere]
[SUSPECTS/complications]
[PERSONAL STAKES]
[PROMISE of solution]
```

---

### Fantasy

```
[HOOK with world/magic element]
[PROTAGONIST with power/position]
[WORLD establishment (brief)]
[QUEST or central conflict]
[STAKES - kingdom/realm/world]
[INTRIGUE about journey]
```

## Non-Fiction Templates

### Self-Help

```
[HOOK with reader's problem]
[PROBLEM amplification]
[SOLUTION introduction]
[KEY BENEFITS (bullets)]
[AUTHOR credibility]
[SOCIAL PROOF]
[CALL TO ACTION]
```

**Example:**
> You're not lazy. You're not broken. You're just using the wrong system.
>
> *The Productivity Paradox* reveals why traditional time management fails—and introduces the method that's helped over 100,000 professionals achieve more while working less.
>
> Inside, you'll discover:
> • The "Energy-First" framework that eliminates burnout
> • The 20-minute ritual that replaces 3 hours of scattered work
> • Why your to-do list is sabotaging your success

---

### Memoir

```
[HOOK with compelling story element]
[UNIQUE EXPERIENCE/PERSPECTIVE]
[EMOTIONAL JOURNEY promise]
[UNIVERSAL THEMES connection]
[WHY NOW - relevance]
```
