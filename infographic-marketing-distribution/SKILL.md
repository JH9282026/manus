# Infographic Marketing & Distribution

Maximize infographic reach, engagement, and ROI through strategic multi-channel distribution and promotion.

## Overview

Creating an infographic is only half the work — distribution determines its impact. This skill covers channel-specific distribution strategies, SEO optimization for visual content, social media formatting, outreach for earned media, repurposing workflows, and performance measurement. The goal is to turn every infographic into a multi-channel content asset that drives traffic, backlinks, and brand awareness.

## SEO Optimization for Infographics

### On-Page SEO Checklist
1. ☐ Descriptive file name: `industry-trend-infographic-2026.png` (not `infographic-final-v3.png`)
2. ☐ Alt text: Comprehensive description including key data points (150–300 characters)
3. ☐ Surrounding text: 300–500 words of context above/below the infographic
4. ☐ Schema markup: Use `ImageObject` schema with caption and description
5. ☐ Page title: Include "Infographic" + topic keyword
6. ☐ Meta description: Summarize key finding + mention it's visual content
7. ☐ Embed code: Provide copy-paste embed snippet for others to share (with backlink)
8. ☐ Transcription: Full text version of infographic data for accessibility and crawlability

### Link Building Tactics
- **Embed code with attribution link** — make sharing easy and backlink-included
- **Outreach to sources** — email everyone whose data you cited; they often share/link
- **Industry roundups** — pitch to "best infographics" or weekly roundup newsletters
- **Guest post with infographic** — write supporting article for industry blog, embed infographic
- **Press release** — for data-heavy infographics with newsworthy findings

## Outreach Email Framework

### For Industry Publications
- **Subject:** Data-driven infographic: [Key Finding] — exclusive for [Publication]
- **Body:** 2–3 sentence hook with most compelling stat, offer exclusive first-publish window, include preview image and full asset link
- **Follow-up:** 3–5 days later if no response, add additional context

### For Data Sources
- **Subject:** Featured your research in our latest infographic
- **Body:** Show how their data was cited, include finished infographic, suggest sharing with their audience

## Best Practices

- **Don't publish and pray** — every infographic needs a written distribution plan before production starts
- **Provide embed codes** — make it effortless for others to share with built-in attribution links
- **Repurpose aggressively** — one infographic should yield 8–12 content pieces across channels
- **Email your sources** — people love seeing their data visualized and will share it
- **Track backlinks** — infographics are one of the best link-building content types
- **Update annually** — refresh data and republish to maintain relevance and rankings
