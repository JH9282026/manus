# Content Strategy for Information Architecture

## Overview

Content strategy ensures content is useful, usable, and aligned with user needs and business goals. IA and content strategy are deeply intertwined—structure depends on content, content depends on structure.

---

## Content Inventory and Audit

**Content Inventory**: Complete list of all content. Spreadsheet with:
- URL/Location
- Title
- Content type (article, product, video, etc.)
- Owner/Author
- Last updated date
- Status (keep, revise, delete)

**Content Audit**: Qualitative assessment of content. Evaluate:
- Accuracy and currency
- Relevance to user needs
- Quality and tone
- SEO performance
- Redundancy and gaps

**Process**: 
1. Crawl site to generate inventory
2. Add metadata and assessment
3. Identify patterns (outdated content, gaps, redundancy)
4. Make recommendations (keep, update, consolidate, delete)

**Tools**: Screaming Frog, Content Analysis Tool, spreadsheets.

## Content Modeling

**Purpose**: Define content types and their attributes. Ensures consistency and enables reuse.

**Example - Blog Post Content Type**:
- Title (text, required)
- Author (reference to author object)
- Publish date (date, required)
- Body (rich text, required)
- Featured image (image, optional)
- Categories (multi-select, required)
- Tags (multi-select, optional)
- Related posts (reference to other posts)

**Benefits**: 
- Consistency across content
- Easier content creation (clear requirements)
- Enables structured content (can be displayed in multiple ways)
- Facilitates migration and integration

**Process**: Identify content types, define attributes for each, document relationships, create templates.

## Metadata Strategy

**Purpose**: Metadata describes content. Enables search, filtering, and organization.

**Types of Metadata**:
- Descriptive: Title, description, author, date
- Administrative: Status, version, owner, rights
- Structural: Format, relationships, hierarchy
- Technical: File type, size, resolution

**Controlled Vocabularies**: Standardized terms for tagging. Prevents 'blog' vs 'article' vs 'post' inconsistency.

**Taxonomy**: Hierarchical classification. Example: Electronics > Computers > Laptops > Gaming Laptops.

**Folksonomy**: User-generated tags. More flexible but less consistent.

**Best Practices**: Keep it simple, use consistent terms, make it easy to apply, review and refine regularly.

## Content Lifecycle

**Creation**: Who creates content? What's the process? What are the standards?

**Review and Approval**: Who reviews? What are approval criteria? How long does it take?

**Publication**: How is content published? Who has permissions? What's the schedule?

**Maintenance**: How often is content reviewed? Who's responsible for updates?

**Archival**: When is content archived? How is it marked? Is it still accessible?

**Deletion**: When is content deleted? Who approves? Is there a backup?

**Governance**: Document roles, responsibilities, and processes. Prevents content decay.

---

## Content Prioritization

**User Needs**: What content do users need most? Base on research, analytics, and support tickets.

**Business Goals**: What content drives business objectives? Conversions, engagement, education.

**Effort vs Impact**: Prioritize high-impact, low-effort content. Use 2×2 matrix.

**Content Gaps**: What's missing? Where are users searching but not finding?

**Content Redundancy**: What's duplicated? Consolidate or eliminate.

**Prioritization Framework**:
1. Must-have: Critical for core user tasks
2. Should-have: Important but not critical
3. Nice-to-have: Valuable but can wait
4. Won't-have: Out of scope or low value

## Writing for Findability

**Descriptive Headings**: Use clear, specific headings. 'How to Reset Your Password' not 'Password Help'.

**Front-Load Keywords**: Put important words first. Users scan, don't read every word.

**Use User Language**: Write in terms users search for, not internal jargon.

**Chunk Content**: Break long content into scannable sections with clear headings.

**Link Descriptively**: 'Download the 2026 annual report' not 'Click here'.

**Meta Descriptions**: Write compelling, accurate descriptions for search results.

**Alt Text**: Describe images for accessibility and SEO. Be specific and concise.
