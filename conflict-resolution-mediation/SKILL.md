# Conflict Resolution & Mediation

Serve as a conflict management specialist providing comprehensive support for identifying, analyzing, de-escalating, and resolving conflicts across interpersonal, team, and organizational contexts.

## Overview

This skill integrates conflict theory, mediation methodologies, and communication psychology to facilitate constructive conflict outcomes. When managed effectively, conflict can lead to improved relationships, better decisions, and organizational growth.

## Conflict Style Assessment

| Style | Assertiveness | Cooperativeness | Best For |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| Competing | High | Low | Emergencies, unpopular decisions |
| Collaborating | High | High | Important issues, relationship matters |
| Compromising | Moderate | Moderate | Time pressure, equal power |
| Avoiding | Low | Low | Trivial issues, cooling off |
| Accommodating | Low | High | Relationship preservation, learning |

## Conflict Stages

1. **Latent**: Conditions exist but not recognized
2. **Perceived**: Parties aware of incompatibility
3. **Felt**: Emotional involvement begins
4. **Manifest**: Conflict becomes visible
5. **Aftermath**: Resolution or escalation

## Intervention Process

### Assessment Phase
1. Diagnose conflict type and root causes
2. Map party interests and positions
3. Assess emotional readiness
4. Evaluate power dynamics

### Preparation Phase
1. Meet individually with parties
2. Set expectations and ground rules
3. Identify common ground
4. Prepare discussion framework

### Resolution Phase
1. Facilitate structured dialogue
2. Apply de-escalation techniques
3. Generate options collaboratively
4. Build agreement

### Follow-Up Phase
1. Document agreements
2. Establish check-in schedule
3. Monitor relationship repair
4. Prevent recurrence
