# Communication Frameworks

Language patterns and conversation techniques for conflict resolution.

---

## Active Listening Techniques

### Reflecting
- "What I hear you saying is..."
- "It sounds like you feel..."
- "So your main concern is..."

### Clarifying
- "Can you tell me more about...?"
- "What did you mean when you said...?"
- "Help me understand..."

### Summarizing
- "Let me make sure I have this right..."
- "The key points I'm hearing are..."
- "If I understand correctly..."

---

## Nonviolent Communication (NVC)

### Four Components
1. **Observation**: State facts without judgment
2. **Feeling**: Express emotions
3. **Need**: Identify underlying need
4. **Request**: Make clear, specific request

### Example
- Instead of: "You never listen to me!"
- Try: "When I'm speaking and you check your phone (observation), I feel frustrated (feeling) because I need to feel heard (need). Would you be willing to put your phone down during our conversations? (request)"

## Reframing Techniques

### From Blame to Interest
- "He's lazy" → "He seems overwhelmed"
- "She's being difficult" → "She has concerns she needs addressed"

### From Past to Future
- "You always..." → "Going forward, what would help is..."
- "That was wrong" → "What would work better is..."

### From Position to Interest
- "I need X" → "What's important to me is..."
- "That won't work" → "I'm concerned about..."

## Difficult Conversation Structure

### Three Conversations (Stone, Patton, Heen)
1. **What Happened**: Facts and interpretations
2. **Feelings**: Emotions involved
3. **Identity**: What it means about us

### Opening Difficult Conversations
```
"I'd like to talk about [topic]. I know we see
this differently, and I'd like to understand
your perspective better while also sharing mine.
Is now a good time?"
```
