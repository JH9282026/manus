# Direct Mail Copywriting

Write compelling direct mail pieces that drive measurable response through persuasive copy, strategic offers, and proven direct response techniques.

## Overview

Provide frameworks and techniques for every direct mail format — from sales letters and postcards to catalogs and dimensional mail. Cover envelope strategy, offer construction, response device design, and testing methodology using principles from legendary direct response copywriters.

## Direct Mail Format Selection

| Format | Cost | Response Rate | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sales letter (envelope) | $$$ | 1-5% | Complex offers, high-value products |
| Postcard | $ | 0.5-2% | Simple offers, awareness, traffic driving |
| Self-mailer | $$ | 0.5-3% | Mid-complexity offers, B2B |
| Catalog | $$$$ | 2-8% | Retail, multi-product, existing customers |
| Dimensional mail | $$$$$ | 5-15% | VIP prospects, B2B executive outreach |

## The Direct Mail Formula: AIDA

| Stage | Purpose | Techniques |
|---|---|---|
| Attention | Stop and open | Envelope teaser, bold headline |
| Interest | Read further | Story, problem agitation |
| Desire | Want the offer | Benefits, proof, testimonials |
| Action | Respond now | CTA, deadline, guarantee |

## Sales Letter Structure

1. **Headline/Johnson Box** — Biggest benefit or provocative question
2. **Opening** — Hook with story, question, or bold statement
3. **Problem agitation** — Amplify the pain point
4. **Solution introduction** — Present your product/service
5. **Benefits cascade** — List benefits with proof points
6. **Social proof** — Testimonials, case studies, credentials
7. **Offer presentation** — What they get, bonuses, pricing
8. **Risk reversal** — Money-back guarantee, free trial
9. **Call to action** — Specific next step with deadline
10. **P.S.** — Restate biggest benefit or deadline (most-read element after headline)

## Key Direct Mail Principles

- **The list is king** — 40% of success comes from the right audience
- **The offer matters more than the copy** — 40% from the offer
- **Copy is the remaining 20%** — But great copy multiplies everything
- **Long copy outsells short copy** for considered purchases
- **Always include a P.S.** — Second most-read element after headline
- **Test one variable at a time** — List, offer, format, headline
