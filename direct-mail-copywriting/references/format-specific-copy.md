# Format-Specific Direct Mail Copy

## Envelope Teaser Copy

### Teaser Strategies

| Strategy | Example | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Benefit promise | "Inside: How to cut your taxes by 30%" | Clear value proposition |
| Curiosity | "Don't open this unless you're serious about..." | Information products |
| Urgency | "Time-sensitive: Response required by March 15" | Deadline-driven offers |
| Official look | Plain envelope, no teaser | Financial, legal, medical |
| Dimensional hint | "Gift enclosed — open immediately" | Premium offers |
| Personalized | "[Name], this is only for residents of [City]" | Localized offers |

### When to Use No Teaser
- Billing-series look (plain #10 envelope, typed address)
- Works especially well for financial and B2B offers
- Relies on curiosity — recipient opens to find out who it's from

## Postcard Copywriting

### Front Side
- Single dominant image or bold headline
- Maximum 7 words for primary headline
- Include logo and brand recognition element
- Use bright, contrasting colors for visibility

### Back Side
- Headline restating core benefit
- 3-5 bullet points with key benefits
- Clear, prominent call to action
- Response mechanism (URL, QR code, phone number)
- Deadline or urgency element

### Postcard Best Practices
- Oversized postcards (6x9, 6x11) outperform standard size
- Glossy front, matte back for writability
- Include tracking URL or unique promo code for measurement
- Test different images — product shots vs lifestyle vs text-heavy

## Self-Mailer Copywriting

### Panel Layout (Tri-fold)
- **Panel 1 (Cover)**: Teaser headline + key visual
- **Panel 2 (Inside left)**: Problem + story
- **Panel 3 (Inside center)**: Solution + benefits
- **Panel 4 (Inside right)**: Offer + CTA
- **Panel 5 (Back)**: Testimonials + contact info
- **Panel 6 (Address)**: Mailing panel + secondary teaser

## Catalog Copywriting

### Product Description Formula
1. **Headline**: Benefit-focused product name
2. **Subhead**: Key differentiator or use case
3. **Body**: 2-3 sentences covering benefits, features, and usage
4. **Specs**: Size, material, color options, weight
5. **Price**: Clear pricing with any savings highlighted
6. **CTA**: "Order now" with item number

### Catalog Layout Principles
- Lead with best-selling or highest-margin products
- Place hero products on right-hand pages (higher attention)
- Use cross-selling callouts ("Pairs well with...")
- Include customer reviews alongside products
- Make ordering information accessible on every spread

## Dimensional Mail

### Types and Ideas
- Lumpy mail (object in envelope creates curiosity)
- Box mailers (premium unboxing experience)
- Tube mailers (stand out in mailbox)
- Pop-up mailers (interactive surprise)
- Gift-with-message (branded promotional items)

### Copy Approach
- Connect the dimensional element to your message metaphorically
- "We're handing you the keys to..." (include a key)
- "Don't let your profits go up in smoke" (include matches)
- Keep letter copy shorter — the dimensional element does heavy lifting
- Always include a strong CTA and response path
