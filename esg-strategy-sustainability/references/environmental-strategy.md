# Environmental Strategy

## Climate Strategy Development

### Net-Zero Roadmap Components
1. **Baseline assessment**: Complete Scope 1, 2, and 3 inventory
2. **Target setting**: Science-based targets (near-term and long-term net-zero)
3. **Reduction pathway**: Prioritized initiatives across operations and value chain
4. **Transition plan**: Capital allocation, technology adoption timeline
5. **Residual emissions**: High-quality carbon removal for hard-to-abate emissions
6. **Governance**: Board oversight, management accountability, incentive alignment

### Emission Reduction Priority Matrix

| Impact | Low Cost | High Cost |
|---|---|---|
| High reduction | Energy efficiency, renewable energy procurement | Electrification, process redesign |
| Medium reduction | Waste reduction, smart building controls | Fleet electrification, green chemistry |
| Low reduction | Behavioral programs, offsets | Carbon capture, green hydrogen |

## Energy Transition Strategy

### Renewable Energy Procurement Options

| Option | Commitment | Cost | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Utility green tariff | 1-3 years | Low premium | Scope 2 reduction |
| Virtual PPA | 10-20 years | Price risk/benefit | Additionality claims |
| On-site solar/wind | Asset life | Capital investment | Direct reduction |
| REC purchase | Annual | Market price | Scope 2 (market-based) |
| Community solar | 1-5 years | Moderate premium | Local impact |

## Circular Economy

### Circular Design Principles
- Design for durability and longevity
- Design for disassembly and repair
- Design for recycling and material recovery
- Eliminate toxic materials
- Use recycled and renewable materials
- Implement product-as-a-service models

### Waste Hierarchy
1. **Prevention**: Avoid waste generation entirely
2. **Reuse**: Use products/materials again without processing
3. **Recycling**: Process waste into new materials
4. **Recovery**: Extract energy from waste
5. **Disposal**: Landfill (last resort)

## Water Stewardship

### Water Risk Assessment
- Map water usage across operations and supply chain
- Identify facilities in water-stressed regions (WRI Aqueduct tool)
- Assess regulatory and reputational water risks
- Set context-based water targets aligned with local watershed needs

### Water Reduction Strategies
- Process water recycling and reuse
- Cooling system optimization
- Rainwater harvesting
- Smart irrigation and landscape management
- Supply chain water engagement programs

## Biodiversity and Nature

### TNFD Framework
The Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures mirrors TCFD structure:
- Governance of nature-related risks
- Strategy for managing dependencies and impacts
- Risk management processes
- Metrics and targets for nature-related issues

### Key Biodiversity Actions
- Conduct nature-related dependency and impact assessment
- Set no-deforestation commitments
- Implement biodiversity offsets for unavoidable impacts
- Engage supply chain on land use practices
- Report against TNFD recommendations
