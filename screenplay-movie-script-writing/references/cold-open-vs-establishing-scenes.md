### Cold Open vs. Establishing Scenes

**Cold Open:** Plunges audience into action immediately, often a sequence that hooks before the title card.

```
INT. BANK VAULT - NIGHT

DARKNESS. Then—a FLASHLIGHT beam cuts through.

Two figures in ski masks work silently. One drills into a 
safe deposit box. The other watches the door.

                         THIEF #1
                   (whispered)
          Thirty seconds.

SIRENS in the distance. Getting closer.
```

**Establishing Scene:** Introduces the world and protagonist before the conflict begins.

```
EXT. SUBURBAN NEIGHBORHOOD - DAWN

Sprinklers water perfect lawns. A JOGGER passes.

INT. WILLIAMS HOUSE - KITCHEN - CONTINUOUS

CAROL WILLIAMS (40s, polished, tense) prepares breakfast 
with military precision. Everything in its place.
```
