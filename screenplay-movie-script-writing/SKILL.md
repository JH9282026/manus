# Screenplay & Movie Script Writing

## Introduction to Screenplay Writing

Screenplay writing is the art and craft of writing scripts for film, television, and other visual media. Unlike novels or stage plays, screenplays are blueprints for visual storytelling—they describe what audiences will see and hear on screen while leaving room for directors, actors, and cinematographers to bring the story to life.

A screenplay is a highly structured document that follows strict formatting conventions established by the film industry over decades. These standards exist for practical reasons: they help producers estimate runtime, allow crew members to break down scenes for production, and enable actors to learn their dialogue efficiently.

Professional screenwriters understand that their work serves as the foundation for collaborative filmmaking. The screenplay must communicate story, character, and emotion clearly while remaining technically precise enough for production planning.
