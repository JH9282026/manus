# Powertrain Specifications

Detailed powertrain analysis for luxury and mass-market vehicles.

---

## Luxury Segment Powertrains

### BMW

**TwinPower Turbo Technology**
- B58 inline-6: 382 hp, used across 40i models
- S58 high-performance: 473-503 hp in M3/M4
- Electric: Dual motor 516 hp in iX xDrive50

**Key Features**:
- Twin-scroll turbochargers
- Valvetronic variable valve timing
- High Precision Injection

### Mercedes-Benz

**Engine Portfolio**
- M256 inline-6 with EQ Boost mild hybrid
- AMG handcrafted engines (One Man, One Engine)
- AMG C63 S E Performance: 671 hp hybrid system

**EQ Electric**:
- EQS 450+: 329 hp, 350-mile EPA range
- 108 kWh battery, 200 kW fast charging

### Porsche

**Motorsport-Derived**
- 911 GT3: 502 hp naturally aspirated flat-6
- 9,000 rpm redline
- 0-60 in 3.2 seconds

**Taycan Electric**:
- 800V architecture (industry-leading)
- Taycan Turbo S: 750 hp peak
- 0-60 in 2.6 seconds

## Mass-Market Segment Powertrains

### Ford

**EcoBoost Family**
- 2.3L turbo-4: 300-330 hp
- 2.7L twin-turbo V6: 325 hp
- 5.0L Coyote V8: 450 hp

**PowerBoost Hybrid**:
- F-150 PowerBoost: 430 hp combined
- 25 mpg combined
- Pro Power Onboard (up to 7.2 kW)

**Electric**:
- F-150 Lightning: 452-580 hp, 240-320 mile range
- Mustang Mach-E: 266-480 hp, 224-312 mile range

### Chevrolet

**Small Block V8 Heritage**
- LT2: 490 hp in Corvette Stingray
- LT6: 670 hp flat-plane crank in Z06
- 5.3L/6.2L V8 options in trucks

**Ultium Electric Platform**:
- Equinox EV: 210-290 hp, 300+ mile range target
- Starting under $30,000
- Silverado EV: 664 hp, 400+ mile range
