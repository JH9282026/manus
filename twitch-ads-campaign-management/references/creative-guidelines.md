# Twitch Ads Creative Guidelines

Best practices for creating engaging, culturally-appropriate ad creative for Twitch's gaming and streaming audience.

---

## Understanding Twitch Culture

### Platform DNA

**Core Values:**
- **Authenticity:** Community values genuine, unscripted content
- **Interaction:** Live chat, real-time engagement, two-way communication
- **Community:** Shared interests, inside jokes, collective experiences
- **Passion:** Deep enthusiasm for gaming, esports, creative content
- **Inclusivity:** Welcoming diverse creators and audiences

**What Works:**
- Self-aware humor ("We know you're here for the stream, but...")
- Gaming references and terminology
- Meme culture integration
- UGC-style production (less polished, more authentic)
- Interactive elements
- Creator partnerships
- Community-first messaging

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Overly corporate, stiff messaging
- Traditional TV commercial style
- Interrupting without acknowledgment
- Talking down to audience
- Ignoring gaming culture
- Generic, one-size-fits-all creative
- Fake authenticity

### Audience Mindset

**Viewer Characteristics:**
- **Savvy:** Ad-aware, skeptical of marketing
- **Engaged:** Actively watching, not passive consumption
- **Social:** Chat participation, community interaction
- **Loyal:** Strong creator and brand affinities
- **Vocal:** Will call out inauthentic or bad ads

**Viewing Context:**
- Often multi-screening (stream + chat + other tabs)
- Sound may be on or off initially
- Watching for extended periods (100+ minutes average)
- Emotionally invested in content
- Community experience, not solo viewing

## Video Creative Best Practices

### Front-Loading Strategy

**First 3 Seconds Critical:**
- Hook viewer immediately
- Show product/brand within first 2 seconds
- Use pattern interrupt (unexpected visual/sound)
- Ask engaging question
- Create curiosity gap

**Example Hooks:**
- "Gamers, we need to talk about your setup..."
- [Unexpected sound effect] + product reveal
- "What if I told you [surprising claim]?"
- Show relatable gaming frustration, then solution

**Why It Matters:**
- Viewers decide to engage in first 3 seconds
- Even unskippable ads can be mentally tuned out
- Strong start increases completion rate
- Sets tone for entire message

### Sound Design

**Design for Sound-Off First:**
- Many viewers initially muted (especially desktop Vertical Video)
- Use bold, readable text overlays
- Visual storytelling without audio dependency
- Subtitles for all spoken content

**Sound-On Enhancement:**
- Engaging music (not generic stock)
- Sound effects that match gaming culture
- Voice-over that sounds conversational, not announcer-style
- Audio branding (sonic logo)

**Audio Specifications:**
- Peak audio: -9dB (required)
- Avoid sudden loud sounds (jarring in stream context)
- Music should complement, not overpower
- Clear voice-over (if used)

### Pacing & Length

**15-Second Ads:**
- Single, focused message
- Quick product demo
- Strong CTA
- Best for: Simple products, awareness, retargeting

**30-Second Ads:**
- Most common and versatile
- Tell brief story
- Show problem + solution
- Multiple product benefits
- Best for: Most campaigns, balanced reach and message depth

**60-Second Ads:**
- Deeper storytelling
- Multiple scenes/scenarios
- Detailed product demonstration
- Higher production value expected
- Best for: Complex products, brand storytelling, premium campaigns
- Note: Extra fees apply, mid-roll only

**Recommendation:**
- Start with 30-second as baseline
- Test 15-second for efficiency
- Use 60-second only if message requires it
- Monitor Video Completion Rate (VCR) by length

### Visual Style Guidelines

**UGC-Style Creative:**
- Less polished, more authentic
- Feels like content, not ad
- Creator-style presentation
- Handheld camera, natural lighting
- Performs well with younger demographics

**Polished Professional:**
- High production value
- Cinematic quality
- Professional voice-over
- Branded graphics
- Performs well for premium products, older demographics

**Gaming-Integrated:**
- In-game footage
- Gaming UI elements
- Esports aesthetics
- RGB lighting, gaming setups
- Performs well for gaming-related products

**Hybrid Approach (Recommended):**
- Professional quality with authentic tone
- Polished but not sterile
- Gaming references with broad appeal
- High-quality UGC style

### Branding Integration

**Logo Placement:**
- Show within first 3 seconds
- Maintain presence throughout (corner bug or periodic appearance)
- End card with logo + CTA (minimum 3 seconds)
- Don't obstruct key visuals

**Brand Mentions:**
- Say brand name at least twice (beginning and end)
- Natural integration, not forced repetition
- Associate with key benefit

**Color Palette:**
- Use brand colors but consider Twitch's dark theme
- High contrast for visibility
- Avoid pure white backgrounds (jarring in dark interface)
- Test creative in context (beside stream)

## Vertical Video Creative (9:16)

### Mobile-First Design

**Framing:**
- Vertical composition (not cropped horizontal)
- Subjects centered, close-up
- Minimal negative space
- Bold, large elements

**Text Overlays:**
- Large, readable fonts (minimum 48pt)
- High contrast (white text on dark background or vice versa)
- Upper 2/3 of frame (avoid bottom where UI elements appear)
- Short phrases, not paragraphs

**Product Showcase:**
- Fill frame with product
- Vertical orientation for tall products
- Close-ups for details
- Demonstrate in vertical context (phone in hand, etc.)

### Desktop vs. Mobile Optimization

**Desktop Placement (Silent, Beside Stream):**
- Design for sound-off
- Text-heavy messaging
- Eye-catching visuals
- Subtle animation to draw attention
- Complement stream content, don't compete

**Mobile Placement (Full-Screen, Sound-On):**
- Leverage audio
- Immersive experience
- Strong opening (viewers can skip)
- Mobile-relevant CTAs ("Tap to learn more")

**Dual-Optimization Strategy:**
- Create for sound-off, enhance with sound-on
- Test both contexts
- Ensure message clear without audio
- Audio adds value but isn't required

## Interactive Ad Creative

### Why Interactive Works on Twitch

**Audience Expectations:**
- Gamers expect interaction
- Passive ads feel out of place
- Engagement is platform norm
- Rewards participation

**Performance Benefits:**
- 3-5x higher engagement rates
- Longer dwell time (more meaningful than impressions)
- Memorable brand experiences
- Viral potential (viewers share interactive experiences)

### Interactive Elements

**Mini-Games:**
- Simple, quick gameplay (15-30 seconds)
- Relevant to product/brand
- Reward completion (discount code, exclusive content)
- Leaderboards for competition

**Example:** Energy drink brand creates "reaction time test" mini-game

**Polls & Voting:**
- Ask audience preference
- Product feature voting
- "Which would you choose?" scenarios
- Show real-time results

**Example:** Gaming peripheral brand asks "RGB or minimalist design?"

**QR Codes:**
- Instant mobile engagement
- Link to exclusive offers
- App downloads
- Social media follows

**Placement:** Corner of video, visible for 10+ seconds

**Clickable Hotspots:**
- "Click to learn more" on specific product features
- Multiple CTAs within single ad
- Branching narratives
- Product configurators

**Gamified Experiences:**
- Collect items throughout ad
- Unlock rewards
- Achievement systems
- Progress bars

### Measurement for Interactive Ads

**Key Metrics:**
- **Interaction Rate:** % of viewers who engage
- **Dwell Time:** Average time spent interacting
- **Completion Rate:** % who finish interactive experience
- **Post-Interaction Conversion:** Actions taken after engaging

**Benchmarks:**
- Interaction Rate: 5-15% (vs. 0.3-0.8% CTR for standard ads)
- Dwell Time: 30-90 seconds (vs. 15-30 second ad view)
- Completion Rate: 60-80% of those who start

## Display Ad Creative

### Stream Display Ads

**Design Considerations:**
- Appears between stream and chat OR below stream
- Competes with live content for attention
- Must be eye-catching but not distracting
- Dark theme integration

**Visual Best Practices:**
- High contrast colors
- Bold, minimal text
- Clear product imagery
- Avoid white backgrounds
- Test beside actual stream content

**Messaging:**
- Single, clear message
- Strong CTA ("Shop Now", "Learn More")
- Benefit-focused headline
- Brand logo prominent

**Animation:**
- Subtle movement to draw eye
- Maximum 30-second loop
- Not distracting from stream
- Smooth, professional transitions

### Mid-Feed Ads (Mobile)

**Full-Screen Mobile Optimization:**
- Vertical or square format
- Thumb-friendly CTA placement
- Large, tappable elements
- Mobile-relevant offers (app installs, mobile shopping)

**Native Integration:**
- Looks like feed content
- "Sponsored" label clear but not obtrusive
- Matches Twitch mobile UI aesthetics
- Seamless scroll experience

### Super Leaderboard & Medium Rectangle

**IAB Standard Formats:**
- Super Leaderboard: 970x90
- Medium Rectangle: 300x250

**Design Approach:**
- Clean, uncluttered
- Readable from distance
- Clear brand and offer
- Professional quality

**Best Use Cases:**
- Retargeting (lower-cost inventory)
- Supplemental reach
- Always-on brand presence
- Direct response with clear CTA

## Creative Testing Framework

### A/B Testing Variables

**Test 1: Hook Variation**
- A: Question hook ("Tired of lag?")
- B: Statement hook ("Eliminate lag forever")
- C: Visual hook (product reveal with sound effect)
- Measure: VCR, CTR

**Test 2: Tone**
- A: Humorous, self-aware
- B: Serious, informative
- C: Inspirational, aspirational
- Measure: Engagement, brand lift

**Test 3: Length**
- A: 15 seconds
- B: 30 seconds
- Measure: VCR, conversion rate, cost per conversion

**Test 4: CTA Timing**
- A: CTA at beginning and end
- B: CTA at end only
- C: CTA throughout
- Measure: CTR, conversion rate

**Test 5: Visual Style**
- A: UGC-style
- B: Polished professional
- C: Gaming-integrated
- Measure: Engagement, brand perception

### Testing Methodology

**Setup:**
- Equal budget allocation to variations
- Same audience targeting
- Same placement
- Minimum 1,000 impressions per variation
- Run for minimum 1 week

**Analysis:**
- Statistical significance (95% confidence)
- Primary metric: Conversion rate or ROAS
- Secondary metrics: CTR, VCR, engagement
- Qualitative feedback (comments, sentiment)

**Implementation:**
- Declare winner based on primary metric
- Allocate 80% budget to winner
- Keep 20% for ongoing testing
- Iterate on winning elements

### Creative Refresh Cadence

**Monitoring Ad Fatigue:**
- Track CTR weekly
- Baseline CTR: First week performance
- Fatigue threshold: 20% decline from baseline

**Refresh Triggers:**
- CTR drops 20%+ from baseline
- VCR declines significantly
- Frequency reaches 5+ impressions per user
- Campaign runs 4+ weeks
- Seasonal relevance changes

**Refresh Strategy:**
- Maintain winning message/offer
- Change visuals, talent, scenarios
- Update music, voice-over
- Refresh graphics, text overlays
- Test new hooks while keeping core message

**Recommended Schedule:**
- Awareness campaigns: Refresh every 3-4 weeks
- Consideration campaigns: Refresh every 4-6 weeks
- Conversion campaigns: Refresh every 2-3 weeks (higher frequency)
- Retargeting: Refresh every 2 weeks (avoid annoyance)

## Creator Partnership Creative

### Sponsored Stream Content

**Integration Approaches:**

**Product Placement:**
- Creator uses product on stream
- Natural, organic integration
- Minimal scripting
- Authentic reactions

**Dedicated Segment:**
- 5-10 minute sponsored segment
- Creator discusses/demonstrates product
- Q&A with chat about product
- Clear disclosure

**Full Stream Sponsorship:**
- Brand presence throughout stream
- Custom overlays, alerts, graphics
- Periodic mentions
- Branded giveaways

**Challenge/Event:**
- Creator completes brand-related challenge
- Community participation
- Prizes and rewards
- Shareable moments

### Creative Guidelines for Creators

**Provide, Don't Prescribe:**
- Brand guidelines (colors, logo usage, key messages)
- Product information and benefits
- Suggested talking points (not scripts)
- Examples of good integrations
- Creative freedom within boundaries

**What to Provide:**
- High-res logos and brand assets
- Product samples (for authentic use)
- Key messages and claims (with substantiation)
- Hashtags and @mentions
- Disclosure language (FTC compliance)
- Do's and don'ts (brand safety)

**What NOT to Provide:**
- Word-for-word scripts (feels inauthentic)
- Overly restrictive guidelines
- Unrealistic expectations
- Excessive approval layers

### Disclosure Requirements

**FTC Compliance:**
- Clear and conspicuous disclosure
- "Sponsored by [Brand]" or "#ad" or "#sponsored"
- Disclosure at beginning of sponsored content
- Verbal disclosure in addition to text
- Can't be buried in description or hidden

**Twitch Requirements:**
- Use Twitch's built-in "Sponsored" label
- Verbal disclosure during stream
- Panel disclosure in channel description
- Ongoing disclosure (not just once)

**Best Practices:**
- Make disclosure natural, not grudging
- "This stream is sponsored by [Brand], who sent me this awesome [product]"
- Transparency builds trust
- Viewers appreciate honesty

## Compliance & Brand Safety

### Twitch Advertising Guidelines

**Required Elements:**
- Advertiser logo or brand name visible
- Accurate, verifiable claims
- Complies with Twitch Community Guidelines
- Age-appropriate (platform is 13+)
- Clear, not misleading

**Prohibited Content:**
- Illegal products or services
- Weapons, ammunition, explosives
- Tobacco, vaping products
- Recreational drugs, drug paraphernalia
- Adult content, sexual services
- Gambling (restrictions apply, varies by region)
- Hate speech, discriminatory content
- Shocking, disturbing, or graphic imagery
- Misleading or deceptive claims
- Malware, phishing, scams

**Restricted Content (Approval Required):**
- Alcohol (age-gated, regional restrictions)
- Political advertising (disclosure requirements)
- Financial services (regulatory compliance)
- Healthcare products (substantiation required)
- Contests and sweepstakes (official rules required)

### Creative Approval Process

**Timeline:**
- Submit creatives 5-7 business days before campaign start
- Review typically takes 2-3 business days
- Allow time for revisions if needed
- Rush approvals may not be available

**Submission Requirements:**
- Final video files (all lengths/formats)
- Display ad files (all sizes)
- Landing page URL
- Campaign details (dates, targeting, budget)
- Any third-party tags or tracking

**Review Criteria:**
- Policy compliance (prohibited/restricted content)
- Technical specifications (resolution, bitrate, etc.)
- Brand safety
- User experience (not deceptive, clear CTA)
- Quality standards

**Common Rejection Reasons:**
- Misleading claims without substantiation
- Poor video quality (low resolution, bad audio)
- Prohibited content
- Missing required elements (logo, disclosure)
- Technical spec violations
- Landing page issues (broken link, mismatch with ad)

**Revision Process:**
1. Receive feedback from Twitch team
2. Make required changes
3. Resubmit for approval
4. Additional 1-2 business days for re-review
5. Approval or further revision requests

## Creative Production Checklist

### Pre-Production

- [ ] Define campaign objective and KPIs
- [ ] Research Twitch audience and culture
- [ ] Review competitor ads on platform
- [ ] Develop creative brief
- [ ] Determine ad formats and lengths needed
- [ ] Plan for A/B testing variations
- [ ] Budget for production
- [ ] Assemble creative team

### Production

**Video Ads:**
- [ ] Script that respects Twitch culture
- [ ] Storyboard with front-loaded hook
- [ ] Shoot in correct aspect ratios (16:9 and 9:16)
- [ ] Capture high-quality footage (1920x1080 minimum)
- [ ] Record clean audio (-9dB peak)
- [ ] Include subtitles/text overlays
- [ ] Show logo within first 3 seconds
- [ ] Strong CTA at end (minimum 3 seconds)
- [ ] Export in H.264 MP4 format
- [ ] Test playback on multiple devices

**Display Ads:**
- [ ] Design in IAB standard sizes
- [ ] High contrast for dark theme visibility
- [ ] Bold, minimal text
- [ ] Clear product imagery
- [ ] Prominent logo
- [ ] Strong CTA
- [ ] File size under 150KB
- [ ] Animation under 30 seconds (if applicable)
- [ ] Export in JPEG, PNG, or GIF
- [ ] Test rendering on dark backgrounds

### Post-Production

- [ ] Color correction for visibility
- [ ] Audio mixing and mastering
- [ ] Add subtitles/captions
- [ ] Render in all required formats and lengths
- [ ] Quality assurance testing
- [ ] Verify technical specifications
- [ ] Test on multiple devices and placements
- [ ] Ensure SSL-compliant delivery

### Submission

- [ ] Prepare all creative assets
- [ ] Complete submission form
- [ ] Provide landing page URL
- [ ] Include campaign details
- [ ] Submit 5-7 business days before launch
- [ ] Monitor for approval or revision requests
- [ ] Make revisions promptly if needed
- [ ] Confirm final approval before campaign start

### Post-Launch

- [ ] Monitor creative performance (CTR, VCR)
- [ ] Track ad fatigue indicators
- [ ] Gather audience feedback
- [ ] Analyze A/B test results
- [ ] Plan creative refresh (3-4 weeks)
- [ ] Document learnings for future campaigns
- [ ] Iterate and improve
