### Cross-Platform Audience Layering

**Combine Twitch + Amazon Data:**

**Example 1: Gaming Peripheral Launch**
- Twitch: Watches FPS games (Valorant, CS:GO)
- Amazon: Purchased gaming mouse in last 90 days
- Result: High-intent audience for new gaming keyboard

**Example 2: Energy Drink Campaign**
- Twitch: Watches streams during late night hours (10pm-2am)
- Amazon: Purchased energy drinks or coffee
- Result: Targeted audience for energy drink brand

**Example 3: Streaming Equipment**
- Twitch: Watches "Just Chatting" or tech content
- Amazon: Searched for microphones, webcams, or lighting
- Result: Aspiring streamers, content creators

**Example 4: Apparel Brand**
- Twitch: Watches esports tournaments
- Amazon: Purchased gaming merchandise or apparel
- Result: Esports fans likely to buy team jerseys, branded apparel

## Behavioral Targeting Strategies

### Viewing Habit Targeting

**Session Length:**
- Short sessions (<30 min): Casual viewers, mobile users
- Medium sessions (30-120 min): Regular viewers, engaged audience
- Long sessions (2+ hours): Dedicated fans, binge watchers

**Frequency:**
- Daily viewers: Highly engaged, loyal audience
- Weekly viewers: Regular but less intense engagement
- Occasional viewers: Broader reach, lower engagement

**Time of Day:**
- Morning (6am-12pm): Casual viewing, international audiences
- Afternoon (12pm-6pm): Moderate traffic, varied content
- Evening (6pm-12am): Peak hours, highest engagement
- Late night (12am-6am): Dedicated viewers, specific content niches

**Day of Week:**
- Weekdays: Working professionals, after-work viewing
- Weekends: Longer sessions, tournament viewing, family content
- Friday/Saturday nights: Peak social viewing, party games

### Device Targeting

**Desktop/PC:**
- Longer viewing sessions
- Chat participation higher
- Multi-screen usage common
- Best for: Detailed product information, complex CTAs

**Mobile:**
- Shorter, frequent sessions
- On-the-go viewing
- Vertical video performs better
- Best for: Simple CTAs, app installs, quick conversions

**Tablet:**
- Medium session lengths
- Lean-back viewing experience
- Hybrid mobile/desktop behavior
- Best for: Visual storytelling, brand awareness

**Console:**
- Living room viewing
- Longer sessions
- Limited interactivity (no clicking)
- Best for: Brand awareness, video storytelling

**Streaming TV (Roku, Fire TV, etc.):**
- Big screen viewing
- Lean-back experience
- Family/group viewing potential
- Best for: Brand awareness, high-impact video

### Engagement Level Targeting

**Chat Participants:**
- Highly engaged community members
- Active, vocal audience
- Influenceable, participatory
- Best for: Community-building campaigns, interactive ads

**Subscribers:**
- Financially invested in content
- Loyal to specific creators
- Higher disposable income
- Best for: Premium products, subscription services

**Followers:**
- Interested but less committed
- Notification-driven viewing
- Growth potential
- Best for: Awareness campaigns, trial offers

**Viewers (non-followers):**
- Casual, browsing audience
- Discovery mode
- Broader reach
- Best for: Top-of-funnel awareness, broad campaigns

**Targeting Strategy:**

**Audience Characteristics:**

### Creator Partnership Targeting

**Micro-Influencers (1K-10K followers):**
- Niche audiences
- High engagement rates
- Authentic connections
- Cost-effective partnerships

**Mid-Tier Creators (10K-100K followers):**
- Established communities
- Consistent content
- Professional quality
- Balanced reach and engagement

**Top-Tier Streamers (100K+ followers):**
- Massive reach
- Celebrity status
- Premium pricing
- Brand awareness focus

**Selection Criteria:**
- Audience demographics alignment
- Engagement rate (not just follower count)
- Content quality and consistency
- Brand safety and reputation
- Previous partnership performance

### Lookalike Audience Strategies

**Seed Audiences:**
- Website converters
- Amazon purchasers of your products
- Email subscribers
- App users
- High-value customers

**Expansion Tactics:**
- 1% lookalike: Closest match, highest quality
- 5% lookalike: Balanced reach and similarity
- 10% lookalike: Broader reach, lower similarity

**Twitch-Specific Lookalikes:**
- Viewers of similar content categories
- Followers of similar creators
- Purchasers of similar products on Amazon
- Engagement behavior patterns

### Exclusion Targeting

**Audiences to Exclude:**

**Existing Customers:**
- Recent purchasers (for acquisition campaigns)
- Active subscribers (for trial offers)
- High-frequency buyers (for new customer promotions)

**Irrelevant Segments:**
- Wrong age demographics
- Incompatible geographic regions
- Unrelated content category viewers
- Low-value customer segments

**Brand Safety:**
- Mature content viewers (for family brands)
- Controversial content categories
- Specific games/creators misaligned with brand

**Performance-Based:**
- Low-converting audience segments
- High CPA segments
- Low engagement rate groups

## Targeting Optimization Framework

### Testing Methodology

**Phase 1: Broad Testing (Weeks 1-2)**
- Cast wide net across multiple audience segments
- Equal budget allocation to test groups
- Gather baseline performance data
- Identify top-performing segments

**Phase 2: Refinement (Weeks 3-4)**
- Increase budget to winning segments by 30-50%
- Reduce or pause underperforming segments
- Test variations within top performers
- Layer additional targeting criteria

**Phase 3: Scaling (Weeks 5+)**
- Allocate majority budget to proven winners
- Maintain 10-20% budget for ongoing testing
- Expand to lookalike audiences
- Optimize bid strategies per segment

### Performance Analysis

**Key Metrics by Segment:**
- Impressions and reach
- Click-through rate (CTR)
- Video completion rate (VCR)
- Cost per click (CPC)
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)
- Return on ad spend (ROAS)
- Engagement rate

**Segment Comparison:**
- Rank segments by ROAS
- Identify CPA outliers (high and low)
- Analyze CTR by demographic
- Compare VCR across content categories
- Evaluate engagement by device type

**Optimization Actions:**
- Reallocate budget from low ROAS to high ROAS segments
- Adjust bids based on segment performance
- Refine creative for underperforming demographics
- Expand successful segments with lookalikes
- Exclude consistently poor performers

### Seasonal & Event-Based Targeting

**Gaming Release Cycles:**
- New game launches (target fans of similar games)
- DLC/expansion releases (target existing players)
- Beta/early access periods (target early adopters)
- Free-to-play events (target casual gamers)

**Holiday Seasons:**
- Black Friday/Cyber Monday (deal seekers)
- Christmas (gift buyers, family gaming)
- Back to School (student gamers, dorm setups)
- Summer (increased gaming time, vacation)

**Esports Calendar:**
- Major tournament seasons
- Team roster changes (fan engagement spikes)
- Championship finals (peak viewership)
- Off-season (lower competition for ad space)

**Cultural Events:**
- Gaming conventions (E3, PAX, Gamescom)
- Streaming events (TwitchCon)
- Charity streams (high engagement, positive sentiment)
- Creator anniversaries (community celebrations)

## Targeting Best Practices Summary

### Do's

✓ **Layer multiple targeting criteria** for precision (e.g., age + interest + purchase behavior)

✓ **Test broad before narrowing** to discover unexpected high-performers

✓ **Use Amazon first-party data** to identify in-market shoppers

✓ **Align with content categories** relevant to your product/service

✓ **Consider device context** when designing creatives and CTAs

✓ **Leverage esports events** for peak engagement opportunities

✓ **Exclude existing customers** for acquisition campaigns

✓ **Create lookalike audiences** from your best converters

✓ **Monitor and optimize weekly** based on performance data

✓ **Respect Twitch culture** in messaging and creative approach

### Don'ts

✗ **Don't assume male-only audience** – 35% female and growing

✗ **Don't ignore non-gaming categories** – massive reach beyond gaming

✗ **Don't over-narrow initially** – need sufficient volume for optimization

✗ **Don't set and forget** – continuous optimization is critical

✗ **Don't use generic messaging** – Twitch culture values authenticity

✗ **Don't ignore mobile** – significant portion of viewership

✗ **Don't target all content** – brand safety and relevance matter

✗ **Don't overlook time-of-day** – performance varies significantly

✗ **Don't skip exclusion lists** – waste budget on irrelevant audiences

✗ **Don't ignore creator partnerships** – authentic influencer content performs
