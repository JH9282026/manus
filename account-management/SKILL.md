# Account Management

Execute strategic account management that drives predictable growth through systematic planning, relationship mapping, expansion execution, and renewal optimization.

## Overview

Strategic account management transforms reactive customer management into a proactive growth engine. This skill provides frameworks for account planning, stakeholder mapping, expansion identification, and renewal management — turning your existing customer base into your most efficient revenue source. In 2026, top-performing account managers use data-driven account intelligence alongside relationship skills.

## Relationship Mapping

### Stakeholder Classification

| Role | Definition | Engagement Strategy | Contact Frequency |
|------|-----------|--------------------|-----------------|
| Economic Buyer | Signs the check, budget authority | ROI-focused, executive alignment | Monthly |
| Technical Buyer | Evaluates technical fit | Product deep-dives, roadmap previews | Bi-weekly |
| Champion | Internal advocate, drives adoption | Enable with content, success stories | Weekly |
| Coach | Provides insider intelligence | Build trust, ask for guidance | As needed |
| User | Day-to-day product user | Training, best practices, quick wins | Ongoing |
| Blocker | Resistant to expansion/renewal | Understand concerns, address objections | Strategic |

### Multi-Threading Strategy

**Goal:** Minimum 3 active relationships per strategic account across different levels and functions.

| Thread Level | Target Persona | Engagement Method |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Executive (C-suite/VP) | Strategic alignment, vision | Executive sponsor program, dinners, advisory board |
| Management (Director/Mgr) | Operational success | QBRs, roadmap sessions, peer networking |
| Practitioner (Individual) | Daily value realization | Training, community, user groups, certifications |

**Risk indicator:** If all relationships are at one level (especially just the champion), the account is fragile.

## Expansion Playbook

### Upsell/Cross-Sell Signal Detection

| Signal | Type | Response | Timing |
|--------|------|----------|--------|
| Usage approaching plan limits | Upsell | Proactive tier upgrade conversation | When at 80% |
| New department requesting access | Cross-sell | Multi-team value workshop | Within 1 week |
| Customer mentions pain point your product solves | Cross-sell | Solution demo with use-case mapping | Within 3 days |
| Positive business outcomes documented | Upsell | ROI-based expansion proposal | At next QBR |
| Leadership change (new VP/CRO) | Either | Executive intro + vision alignment | Within 2 weeks |
| Budget cycle approaching | Either | Value summary + expansion proposal | 60–90 days before |
| Industry event or regulation change | Either | Relevance-based outreach | As it happens |

### Expansion Conversation Framework

1. **Anchor to outcomes** — "You've achieved [X result]. Based on that success..."
2. **Identify the gap** — "Other customers in [industry] are also using [feature] to solve [problem]..."
3. **Quantify the opportunity** — "Based on your data, we estimate this could deliver [Y value]..."
4. **Propose a pilot** — "Would a 30-day pilot with [team] make sense to validate?"
5. **Define success criteria** — "If we see [metrics], would that justify expanding?"

## Renewal Management

### Renewal Timeline

| Days Before Renewal | Action | Owner |
|---------------------|--------|-------|
| 180 | Health check — flag at-risk accounts | CS Ops |
| 120 | Account review — document value delivered | Account Manager |
| 90 | Renewal strategy meeting (internal) | AM + Manager |
| 60 | Renewal conversation with customer | Account Manager |
| 45 | Send renewal proposal/quote | AM + Finance |
| 30 | Follow up, negotiate if needed | Account Manager |
| 14 | Escalate unsigned renewals | AM + VP Sales |
| 7 | Final push — executive involvement if needed | VP/Exec Sponsor |
| 0 | Contract due — process renewal or manage churn | AM + Legal |

### Renewal Risk Factors & Mitigation

| Risk Factor | Probability Impact | Mitigation |
|------------|-------------------|------------|
| Champion left company | High | Multi-thread early; identify new champion within 30 days |
| Low product adoption | High | Emergency adoption plan with training and quick wins |
| Budget cuts announced | Medium | Demonstrate indispensability; offer flexible terms |
| Competitive evaluation | High | Executive engagement + value reinforcement + save offer |
| Merger/acquisition | Medium | Identify new decision makers early; demonstrate value to both entities |

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Net Revenue Retention | (Start ARR + Expansion − Contraction − Churn) / Start ARR | >110% |
| Gross Revenue Retention | (Start ARR − Contraction − Churn) / Start ARR | >90% |
| Expansion Revenue % | Expansion ARR / Total New ARR | >30% |
| On-time Renewal Rate | Renewals closed by due date / Total renewals | >85% |
| Multi-thread Score | Avg active contacts per account | >3 (Strategic) |
| Account Plan Completion | Accounts with current plans / Total managed accounts | >90% |
| Whitespace Penetration | Products adopted / Products available | >40% |

## Best Practices

1. **Plan every strategic account** — No account plan = no strategic account management; it's just reactive support
2. **Multi-thread relentlessly** — Single-threaded accounts are one departure away from churn
3. **Quantify value delivered** — If you can't show ROI, the customer will question the renewal
4. **Start renewal conversations early** — 90–120 days out, not 30 days before expiration
5. **Separate expansion from renewal** — Don't hold renewal hostage to upsell; close renewal first, then expand
6. **Use data, not gut feel** — Health scores, usage analytics, and sentiment data should drive account strategy
