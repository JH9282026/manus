# Equipment and Studio Setup

Comprehensive guide to cameras, lighting, audio equipment, and recording software for video podcast production.

---

## Studio Setup for Video Podcasts

### Space Considerations

#### Room Selection
- Minimum 10x10 feet for solo/interview setups
- Sound treatment capability
- Controllable lighting environment
- Electrical capacity for equipment

#### Acoustic Treatment
- Acoustic panels on walls (especially behind and beside)
- Bass traps in corners
- Carpet or rugs on hard floors
- Avoid parallel hard surfaces
- Consider ceiling treatment

#### Background Design
- Dedicated set or backdrop
- Bookshelf/plant setups are popular
- LED accent lighting
- Branded elements
- Depth and visual interest

### Equipment Checklist

#### Essential Equipment
- Camera(s)
- Tripod(s) or mounting system
- Key light, fill light, backlight
- Microphone(s)
- Audio interface
- Headphones
- Recording software/hardware
- Computer with sufficient processing power

#### Nice-to-Have Equipment
- Teleprompter
- Multiple camera angles
- Camera switcher
- Stream deck for controls
- Acoustic treatment
- Professional set design

## Camera Setup

### Single Camera Setup

The simplest approach with one camera:

**Positioning**:
- Eye-level or slightly above
- 3-6 feet from subject
- Frame for medium shot (waist up) or medium close-up (chest up)

**Movement Options**:
- Static setup (simplest)
- Motorized gimbal (adds subtle movement)
- PTZ camera (remote-controlled positioning)

**Limitations**:
- No cutting between angles
- Less visual variety
- Requires B-roll or graphics for visual interest

### Multi-Camera Setup

Two or more cameras for dynamic editing:

#### Two-Camera Interview Setup
- **Camera 1**: Wide shot capturing both host and guest
- **Camera 2**: Alternating close-ups (or two additional cameras for each person)

#### Three-Camera Setup
- **Camera 1**: Wide establishing shot
- **Camera 2**: Host close-up
- **Camera 3**: Guest close-up

### Camera Recommendations by Budget

| Budget | Camera Option | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| $300-500 | Webcam (Logitech C920/Brio) | Good starting point |
| $500-1,000 | Smartphone + mount | Excellent quality with good lighting |
| $1,000-2,000 | Sony ZV-E10, Canon M50 | Mirrorless entry-level |
| $2,000-4,000 | Sony A7C, Canon R6 | Full-frame mirrorless |
| $4,000+ | Sony FX3, Blackmagic | Professional cinema cameras |

### Camera Settings

**Recommended settings for video podcasts**:
- **Resolution**: 4K (future-proofing) or 1080p
- **Frame rate**: 24fps (cinematic) or 30fps (smooth)
- **Aperture**: f/2.8-f/4 for nice background blur
- **Shutter speed**: Double frame rate (1/50 for 24fps)
- **ISO**: As low as possible while maintaining exposure
- **White balance**: Match lighting (3200K tungsten, 5600K daylight)

## Lighting for Video Podcasts

### Three-Point Lighting System

The foundation of professional video lighting:

#### Key Light
- Main light source
- Position: 45° to side, slightly above eye level
- Creates primary illumination and modeling

#### Fill Light
- Softer, less intense than key
- Position: Opposite side of key light
- Reduces shadows created by key

#### Back Light (Rim/Hair Light)
- Separates subject from background
- Position: Behind and above subject
- Creates depth and dimension

### Lighting Equipment Options

| Equipment Type | Pros | Cons | Budget |
|---------------|------|------|--------|
| Ring Light | Even, flattering light | Can create unnatural catchlights | $50-200 |
| LED Panels | Adjustable, versatile | May need diffusion | $100-500 |
| Softboxes | Beautiful soft light | Takes up space | $100-300 |
| Key Lights (Elgato) | Designed for streaming | Limited power | $150-200 |
| Professional LEDs (Aputure) | Excellent quality | Higher cost | $300-1,500 |

### Lighting Tips

1. **Avoid overhead lighting only**: Creates unflattering shadows under eyes
2. **Diffuse harsh sources**: Use softboxes or diffusion material
3. **Match color temperatures**: Don't mix warm and cool lights
4. **Light the background**: Add depth and visual interest
5. **Use practical lights**: Lamps and LEDs in frame add atmosphere
6. **Control windows**: Block or use as primary light source (not both)

## Audio Equipment and Setup

### Microphone Types for Podcasting

#### Dynamic Microphones
- **Examples**: Shure SM7B, Rode PodMic, Audio-Technica ATR2100x
- **Characteristics**: Rugged, good noise rejection, forgiving
- **Best for**: Untreated rooms, multiple speakers

#### Condenser Microphones
- **Examples**: Rode NT1, Audio-Technica AT2020, Blue Yeti
- **Characteristics**: Sensitive, detailed, picks up room noise
- **Best for**: Treated studios, solo recording

#### Lavalier Microphones
- **Examples**: Rode Wireless Go, Sennheiser XS Lav
- **Characteristics**: Discreet, consistent position
- **Best for**: Video podcasts where boom/desk mics aren't aesthetic

### Audio Interface Requirements

For professional audio quality:
- XLR microphone input(s)
- Quality preamps
- Headphone output
- Low latency monitoring

**Recommendations**:
- **Solo**: Focusrite Scarlett Solo ($100)
- **Two hosts**: Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 ($170)
- **Multi-person**: Rode RODECaster Pro II ($600)

### Audio Setup Best Practices

1. **Microphone positioning**: 6-8 inches from mouth, off-axis to avoid plosives
2. **Pop filter**: Use foam or mesh filter for plosive protection
3. **Gain staging**: Set levels to peak around -12dB to -6dB
4. **Monitor with headphones**: Closed-back to prevent bleed
5. **Room treatment**: Address echo and reverb
6. **Consistent distance**: Maintain mic position throughout recording

## Recording Software and Platforms

### Local Recording Software

#### OBS Studio (Free)
- Multi-camera recording
- Scene switching
- Live streaming capable
- Learning curve for setup

#### Ecamm Live (Mac - $15-25/month)
- Intuitive interface
- Multi-camera support
- Live streaming
- Interview features

#### vMix (Windows - $60-1,200)
- Professional broadcasting
- Multi-camera switching
- Virtual sets
- Advanced features

#### Adobe Premiere Pro/DaVinci Resolve
- Record directly to NLE
- Professional editing workflow
- Multiple input support
- Advanced color/audio

### Cloud Recording Platforms

#### Riverside.fm ($15-24/month)
- Local recording quality
- Automatic sync
- Separate tracks
- Easy guest joining

#### SquadCast ($20+/month)
- High-quality remote recording
- Progressive upload
- Separate audio/video tracks
- Podcast-focused features

#### Zencastr ($20+/month)
- Simple interface
- Separate track recording
- Video capability
- Post-production features

#### Zoom (Free-$20/month)
- Widely accessible
- Local recording option
- Separate audio tracks possible
- Lower video quality than dedicated platforms
