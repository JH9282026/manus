# Production Techniques

Framing, composition, set design, visual elements, and remote guest setup for professional video podcasts.

---

## Framing and Composition

### Standard Shot Types

| Shot Type | Framing | Use |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Wide/Establishing | Full body + environment | Opening shots, showing space |
| Medium | Waist up | Main conversation shot |
| Medium Close-Up | Chest up | Primary video podcast framing |
| Close-Up | Face/shoulders | Emotional moments, emphasis |

### Composition Guidelines

#### Rule of Thirds
- Place eyes on upper third line
- Position face in left or right third
- Leave "look room" in direction of gaze

#### Headroom
- Small amount of space above head
- Not too much (feels empty)
- Not too little (feels cramped)

#### Eye Line
- Camera at eye level or slightly above
- Direct eye contact with lens creates connection
- Lower angle can feel powerful but unflattering

### Multi-Person Composition

#### Side-by-Side Setup
- Equal framing for both participants
- Slight angle toward each other
- Room between for visual breathing space

#### Face-to-Face Setup
- Cross-shooting with two cameras
- Each person framed looking toward other
- Creates natural conversational dynamic

## Background and Set Design

### Background Options

#### Bookshelf Setup
- Popular, professional look
- Books, plants, décor items
- LED accent lighting
- Avoid distracting items

#### Branded Background
- Logo placement (subtle)
- Brand colors incorporated
- Professional appearance
- Consistent episode look

#### Minimalist/Plain
- Solid color backdrop
- Clean, modern aesthetic
- Focus on speaker
- Easy to maintain

#### Studio Set
- Purpose-built environment
- Multiple shooting areas
- Dedicated equipment placement
- Professional production value

### Set Design Principles

1. **Depth**: Multiple planes create visual interest
2. **Color palette**: Cohesive colors, avoid clashing
3. **Lighting integration**: Practical lights add atmosphere
4. **Brand consistency**: Subtle branding elements
5. **Cleanliness**: No clutter or distracting elements
6. **Acoustic function**: Design can incorporate sound treatment

## Visual Elements and Graphics

### Lower Thirds and Name Tags

Standard information graphics displaying:
- Name of speaker
- Title/role
- Social handles
- Topic identifiers

**Design principles**:
- Readable at small sizes
- Consistent brand styling
- Not blocking face
- Appearing at appropriate moments

### On-Screen Graphics

#### Static Graphics
- Topic cards
- Quote displays
- Statistics and data
- Sponsor logos

#### Animated Graphics
- Intro/outro sequences
- Transition effects
- Logo animations
- Chapter markers

### Screen Recording Integration

For podcasts discussing:
- Websites or apps
- Tutorials and demonstrations
- News articles
- Social media content

**Best practices**:
- Clean desktop/browser
- Zoom to relevant areas
- Highlight cursor movement
- Picture-in-picture with speaker

## B-Roll and Supplemental Footage

### Types of B-Roll

#### Product/Subject B-Roll
- Close-ups of discussed items
- Demonstrations of products
- Visual examples

#### Lifestyle B-Roll
- Stock footage of relevant activities
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Environmental context

#### Graphic B-Roll
- Charts and data visualization
- Images and photographs
- Text animations

### B-Roll Sources

- **Self-shot**: Custom footage
- **Stock footage**: Storyblocks, Artgrid, Pexels
- **Screen recordings**: Software, websites
- **Photos and images**: Unsplash, stock libraries
- **AI-generated**: Runway, Pika, for specific needs

### Using B-Roll Effectively

1. **Cover jump cuts**: Smooth transitions between takes
2. **Illustrate concepts**: Visual representation of ideas
3. **Add variety**: Break up talking head monotony
4. **Show don't tell**: Visual examples over verbal descriptions
5. **Maintain relevance**: B-roll should support, not distract

### Guest Technical Requirements

Send guests pre-recording instructions:

#### Minimum Requirements
- Stable internet connection (10+ Mbps upload)
- Quiet recording environment
- Headphones or earbuds
- Webcam (built-in acceptable)
- Chrome or Firefox browser

#### Recommended Setup
- External microphone (USB or XLR)
- Proper lighting (ring light minimum)
- Professional background
- Hardwired ethernet connection

### Guest Instruction Template

**Pre-Recording Checklist for Guests**:
1. Test your internet speed (minimum 10 Mbps upload)
2. Find a quiet room with minimal echo
3. Position light in front of you (not behind)
4. Use headphones to prevent audio feedback
5. Close other applications and browser tabs
6. Join 5 minutes early for technical check
7. Have water nearby for long recordings
