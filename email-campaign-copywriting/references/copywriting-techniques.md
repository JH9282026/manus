# Email Copywriting Techniques

## Direct Response Formulas for Email

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
- **Attention**: Subject line + first sentence hook
- **Interest**: Present the relevant problem or opportunity
- **Desire**: Show benefits with proof and testimonials
- **Action**: Clear, specific CTA with urgency

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
- **Problem**: "Spending hours on reports no one reads?"
- **Agitate**: "Meanwhile, your competitors are making faster decisions with real-time data..."
- **Solve**: "Our dashboard builder creates executive-ready reports in minutes."

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge)
- **Before**: Describe current painful state
- **After**: Paint the ideal outcome
- **Bridge**: Show how your product/service bridges the gap

### 4 U's (Subject Lines)
- **Urgent**: Time-sensitive language
- **Unique**: Different from other emails in inbox
- **Ultra-specific**: Concrete numbers, details
- **Useful**: Clear benefit to reader

## CTA Best Practices

### Button Copy
- Use first person: "Start my free trial" > "Start your free trial"
- Be specific: "Get the guide" > "Click here"
- Reduce risk: "Try free for 14 days" > "Buy now"
- Create urgency: "Claim my spot (only 12 left)"

### CTA Placement
- Primary CTA should be visible without scrolling
- Repeat CTA at end of longer emails
- Use button format for primary action (not just text link)
- Contrasting color for CTA button

## Personalization Tactics

### Beyond First Name
- **Behavioral**: "Since you browsed [product category]..."
- **Purchase history**: "Based on your purchase of [product]..."
- **Location**: "Free next-day delivery to [city]"
- **Engagement**: "You've been a subscriber for [time]..."
- **Segment-specific**: Different versions for different personas
- **Dynamic content**: Swap images/offers based on segment

---

## Email Copy Mistakes to Avoid

1. Writing to everyone (lack of audience specificity)
2. Multiple competing CTAs in one email
3. Feature-heavy copy without benefits
4. Walls of text without visual breaks
5. No urgency or reason to act now
6. Generic subject lines that don't stand out
7. Ignoring mobile formatting (60%+ open on mobile)
8. Missing preview text optimization
9. Not matching subject line promise to email content
10. Sending without testing (rendering, links, personalization tokens)
