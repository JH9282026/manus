# Email Campaign Copywriting

Write strategic email sequences that nurture leads, drive conversions, and build lasting customer relationships across the entire customer lifecycle.

## Overview

Provide frameworks for every email campaign type — from welcome sequences and cart abandonment to product launches and win-back campaigns. Cover subject line optimization, personalization, sequence design, and copywriting formulas proven to drive open rates and conversions.

## Email Campaign Types

| Campaign Type | Trigger | Goal | Sequence Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| Welcome series | New subscriber/signup | Onboard, build trust | 3-7 emails over 1-2 weeks |
| Cart abandonment | Abandoned cart | Recover sale | 3 emails over 3 days |
| Product launch | Scheduled event | Generate sales | 5-10 emails over 2-3 weeks |
| Nurture/drip | Lead capture | Educate, qualify | 5-12 emails over 4-8 weeks |
| Re-engagement | Inactivity (60-90 days) | Win back or clean list | 3-5 emails over 2 weeks |
| Post-purchase | Order placed | Retain, cross-sell | 3-5 emails over 30 days |
| Cold outreach (B2B) | Prospecting | Book meeting | 4-6 emails over 3 weeks |

## Subject Line Formulas

| Formula | Example | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Curiosity gap | "The #1 mistake killing your conversions" | Content, education |
| Benefit + specificity | "Get 3x more leads in 30 days" | Product, offer |
| Question | "Are you making this pricing error?" | Engagement |
| Urgency/scarcity | "Last chance: 50% off ends tonight" | Promotions |
| Personalization | "[Name], your custom plan is ready" | Nurture, retention |
| Social proof | "Why 10,000+ marketers switched to..." | Trust building |
| How-to | "How to write emails that actually convert" | Education |

## Email Copywriting Framework: PAS

1. **Problem** — Open with the reader's pain point
2. **Agitation** — Amplify the consequences of not solving it
3. **Solution** — Present your product/offer as the answer

## Email Best Practices

- Write subject lines under 50 characters for mobile
- Use preview text strategically (extends the subject line)
- One primary CTA per email (supporting links are fine)
- Write at 7th-8th grade reading level
- Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Personalize beyond just the name (behavior, preferences)
- Test send times (Tuesday-Thursday, 10am tends to perform well)
- Always include an unsubscribe option (legally required)
