
### Post-Production Workflow

**Import and Organization**:
1. Import all footage to organized folder structure
2. Create proxy files for smooth editing if needed
3. Review and rate footage
4. Identify usable clips and best moments
5. Note any issues requiring correction

**Editing Aerial Sequences**:
- Begin with wide establishing shots
- Progress to tighter, more detailed shots
- Use movement to drive edits
- Match screen direction for continuity
- Vary shot lengths for rhythm

### Color Grading Aerial Footage

**Log Footage Workflow**:
1. Apply LUT or manual base conversion
2. Balance exposure across clips
3. Match color temperature
4. Adjust contrast and saturation
5. Apply creative grade
6. Add final polish

**Common Adjustments**:
- Lift shadows to reduce muddiness
- Control highlights in sky areas
- Enhance colors selectively
- Add graduated filters for sky
- Manage haze and atmospheric effects

**Matching Ground and Aerial Footage**:
- Match color temperature
- Align contrast levels
- Consistent saturation
- Grade as unified sequence

### Stabilization and Speed Ramping

**Stabilization Options**:
- Warp Stabilizer (Premiere Pro)
- Stabilizer effect (After Effects)
- ReelSteady GO
- Built-in stabilization in drone software

**Speed Ramping Techniques**:
- Smooth acceleration and deceleration
- Time remapping for impact moments
- Hyperlapse creation
- Slow motion for details

### Audio Considerations

**Ambient Audio**:
- Drone motor noise unusable for direct audio
- Record ambient audio separately
- Use microphone at subject location
- Add room tone in post

**Music and Sound Design**:
- Aerial footage often relies on music
- Match tempo to movement
- Use sound effects strategically
- Create emotional impact through audio
