
### Exposure Settings

**Shutter Speed**:
- Follow 180-degree rule: 1/(2 × frame rate)
- 24fps → 1/50 shutter
- 30fps → 1/60 shutter
- 60fps → 1/120 shutter
- Use ND filters to maintain proper exposure

**ND Filters**:
- Essential for daylight filming
- Maintains proper motion blur
- Common strengths: ND4, ND8, ND16, ND32, ND64
- Variable ND filters offer flexibility
- Polarizing ND filters reduce reflections

**ISO Settings**:
- Keep as low as possible
- Native ISO for best quality
- Higher ISO introduces noise
- Modern drones handle higher ISO better

**Aperture (If Available)**:
- Lower f-stop = shallower depth of field
- Higher f-stop = greater sharpness throughout
- Consider diffraction limits
- Balance with ISO needs

### Video Settings

**Resolution and Frame Rate Selection**:
- 4K/24fps: Cinematic, most common
- 4K/30fps: Standard video
- 4K/60fps: Slow motion, smooth footage
- 1080p: Smaller files, more frame rate options

**Color Profile Selection**:
- D-Log/D-Log M: Maximum dynamic range, requires grading
- HLG: HDR output, less grading needed
- Normal: Ready to use, limited flexibility

**Bitrate Considerations**:
- Higher bitrate = better quality
- Requires faster memory cards
- Larger file sizes
- Consider H.264 vs H.265 compression

### White Balance and Color

**White Balance Settings**:
- Auto: Convenient but can shift during flight
- Manual: Consistent color, recommended
- Match to lighting conditions
- Adjust in post if shooting Log

**Color Temperature Guidelines**:
- Daylight: 5500K-6500K
- Golden hour: 3200K-4500K
- Overcast: 6500K-7500K
- Shade: 7000K-8000K
