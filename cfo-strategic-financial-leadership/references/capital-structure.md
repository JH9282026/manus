# Capital Structure

Guidance on capital structure optimization and treasury management.

---

## Capital Structure Optimization

### Factors Affecting Optimal Structure

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Business Risk | Higher risk = lower leverage |
| Tax Position | More profitable = more debt benefit |
| Asset Type | Tangible assets support more debt |
| Cash Flow Stability | Stable = more debt capacity |
| Growth Stage | High growth often equity-funded |
| Market Conditions | Affects cost and availability |

### Capital Structure Analysis

| Metric | Calculation | Implications |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Debt/Equity | Total Debt / Total Equity | Financial risk |
| Debt/EBITDA | Net Debt / EBITDA | Leverage level |
| Interest Coverage | EBITDA / Interest | Debt service ability |
| Debt/Capital | Debt / (Debt + Equity) | Capital mix |

### Target Leverage

| Company Type | Typical Debt/EBITDA |
|--------------|--------------------|
| High Growth Tech | 0-1x |
| Mature Tech | 1-3x |
| Industrial | 2-3x |
| Utilities | 3-5x |
| PE-Backed | 4-6x |

## Financing Options

### Debt Instruments

| Type | Characteristics | Best For |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| Revolver | Flexible, short-term | Working capital |
| Term Loan | Amortizing, fixed schedule | General corporate |
| High Yield Bonds | Higher rate, flexible covenants | Growth financing |
| Investment Grade Bonds | Lower rate, longer maturity | Large corporates |
| Convertible | Debt convertible to equity | Growth with lower rate |

### Equity Instruments

| Type | Characteristics | Best For |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| Common Stock | Voting rights, residual claim | Growth funding |
| Preferred Stock | Priority, fixed dividend | Hybrid needs |
| PIPE | Private placement in public entity | Quick capital |
| Rights Offering | Pro-rata to existing holders | Existing shareholder focus |

## Treasury Management

### Cash Management

| Objective | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| Liquidity | Maintain operating cash buffer |
| Safety | Preserve principal |
| Yield | Optimize returns within policy |
| Accessibility | Match maturities to needs |

### Investment Policy

| Element | Policy Content |
|---------|----------------|
| Objectives | Safety, liquidity, yield priority |
| Permitted Instruments | Approved investment types |
| Credit Quality | Minimum ratings |
| Concentration | Limits per issuer |
| Maturity | Duration limits |
| Reporting | Performance reporting |

## Shareholder Returns

### Return Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Characteristics |
|-----------|----------------|
| Dividends | Regular cash return, commitment |
| Share Buybacks | Flexible, accretive when stock undervalued |
| Special Dividends | One-time return of excess cash |

### Capital Return Policy

| Consideration | Assessment |
|---------------|------------|
| Cash Generation | Sustainable free cash flow |
| Investment Needs | Growth CapEx and M&A pipeline |
| Balance Sheet | Target leverage and liquidity |
| Shareholder Preference | Investor base composition |
| Tax Efficiency | Dividend vs buyback tax treatment |

## Risk Management

### Financial Risks

| Risk | Hedging Approach |
|------|------------------|
| Currency | Forwards, options, natural hedge |
| Interest Rate | Swaps, caps, fixed/floating mix |
| Commodity | Futures, forwards, contracts |
| Credit | Insurance, diversification |

### Hedging Policy

| Element | Content |
|---------|--------|
| Objectives | Risk reduction vs speculation prohibition |
| Authorized Instruments | Permitted hedge types |
| Coverage Ratios | Percentage of exposure to hedge |
| Tenor | Maximum hedge duration |
| Counterparties | Approved counterparties |
| Reporting | Hedge effectiveness reporting |
