# Change Management Frameworks

## ADKAR Model (Prosci)

The ADKAR model focuses on individual change through five sequential stages:

### Awareness
- Communicate the need for change
- Explain business drivers and risks of not changing
- Connect change to organizational strategy

### Desire
- Build motivation to participate in change
- Address "What's in it for me?" questions
- Engage sponsors and influencers

### Knowledge
- Provide training on new processes, tools, behaviors
- Deliver information about how to change
- Build competency through practice

### Ability
- Enable hands-on application of new skills
- Provide coaching and support during transition
- Remove barriers to performance

### Reinforcement
- Sustain change through recognition and rewards
- Integrate into performance management
- Address regression and provide ongoing support

## Kotter's 8-Step Model

Kotter's framework addresses large-scale organizational transformation:

### Step 1: Create Urgency
- Identify potential threats and opportunities
- Develop scenarios showing what could happen
- Build a compelling case for change

### Step 2: Form a Powerful Coalition
- Assemble a group with enough power to lead change
- Encourage the group to work as a team
- Identify key stakeholders and influencers

### Step 3: Create a Vision for Change
- Develop a clear vision to direct the change effort
- Create strategies to achieve that vision
- Ensure the coalition can describe the vision in 5 minutes

### Step 4: Communicate the Vision
- Communicate the vision frequently and powerfully
- Embed it in everything you do
- Address concerns openly and honestly

### Step 5: Remove Obstacles
- Identify barriers to change
- Empower people to execute the vision
- Recognize and reward those making change happen

### Step 6: Create Short-Term Wins
- Plan for visible performance improvements
- Create those improvements
- Recognize and reward contributing employees

### Step 7: Build on the Change
- Analyze successes and identify improvements
- Set goals to continue building momentum
- Bring in additional change agents

### Step 8: Anchor the Changes in Culture
- Articulate connections between new behaviors and success
- Develop means to ensure leadership development
- Create succession plans to sustain the change

## Lewin's Change Model

Simple three-stage model for process and behavioral changes:

### Unfreeze
- Create awareness of need for change
- Reduce forces maintaining current behavior
- Build readiness and motivation

### Change
- Implement the new processes or behaviors
- Provide training and support
- Enable practice and learning

### Refreeze
- Stabilize the new state
- Reinforce new behaviors
- Integrate into organizational norms

## Framework Selection Guide

| Framework | Best For | Duration | Complexity |
|-----------|----------|----------|------------|
| ADKAR | Individual adoption focus | Months | Medium |
| Kotter | Major transformations | Years | High |
| Lewin | Simple process changes | Weeks-Months | Low |
| Hybrid | Technology + culture | Varies | High |
