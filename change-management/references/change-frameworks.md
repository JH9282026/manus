# Change Management Frameworks

Detailed implementation guidance for the three primary change management methodologies.

---

## Prosci ADKAR Model

ADKAR is a goal-oriented change management model focusing on individual transitions through five sequential building blocks.

### The Five Building Blocks

**1. Awareness** — Build understanding of why the change is needed
- Communicate business drivers and risks of not changing
- Share data supporting the change case
- Address "What's happening?" before "What do I need to do?"
- Use executive sponsors for credibility
- Deliver messages through preferred/trusted channels

**2. Desire** — Create motivation to participate and support the change
- Articulate personal benefits (WIIFM — What's In It For Me)
- Address personal fears and concerns directly
- Leverage peer influence and champion networks
- Connect change to individual career growth
- Provide choice and autonomy where possible

**3. Knowledge** — Equip people with information on how to change
- Deliver role-based training aligned to job impact
- Provide job aids, quick reference guides, and FAQs
- Offer multiple learning modalities (classroom, virtual, self-paced)
- Create practice environments and sandboxes
- Time knowledge delivery close to go-live

**4. Ability** — Develop capability to implement new skills and behaviors
- Provide hands-on practice opportunities
- Assign coaches and mentors for real-time support
- Allow time for proficiency development
- Remove barriers to performance
- Offer help desk and just-in-time support

**5. Reinforcement** — Sustain the change and prevent regression
- Celebrate successes publicly
- Align performance management with new behaviors
- Collect feedback and make visible adjustments
- Recognize and reward adoption
- Measure and communicate progress

### ADKAR Assessment

Conduct barrier point assessments to identify where individuals are stuck:
- Score each element 1-5 via survey
- The lowest-scoring element is the barrier point
- Target interventions at the barrier point, not subsequent elements

## Kotter's 8-Step Change Model

### Step-by-Step Implementation

**Step 1: Create Urgency**
- Present compelling data on market threats or opportunities
- Develop honest discussion about competitive realities
- Target 75% of leadership convinced before proceeding

**Step 2: Form a Guiding Coalition**
- Recruit influential leaders across functions
- Ensure coalition has position power, expertise, credibility, and leadership
- Build trust through off-site retreats and shared experiences

**Step 3: Create a Vision for Change**
- Develop a clear, compelling vision statement (1-2 sentences)
- Create supporting strategies for achieving the vision
- Test: Can you communicate it in 5 minutes and get understanding?

**Step 4: Communicate the Vision**
- Use every vehicle possible to communicate
- Lead by example — coalition models expected behavior
- Address concerns openly
- Repeat the vision 7+ times through multiple channels

**Step 5: Remove Obstacles**
- Identify and remove structural barriers
- Address managers undermining the change
- Align systems, structures, and processes with the vision
- Empower broad-based action

**Step 6: Create Short-Term Wins**
- Plan visible performance improvements within 6-12 months
- Create the wins — don't leave them to chance
- Recognize and reward people contributing to wins

**Step 7: Build on the Change**
- Use increased credibility to change more systems and structures
- Hire, promote, and develop employees who implement the vision
- Reinvigorate the process with new projects and change agents

**Step 8: Anchor Changes in Culture**
- Articulate connections between new behaviors and success
- Ensure leadership development and succession embody the new approaches
- Create explicit links between organizational success and new behaviors

## Lewin's Change Model

### Three-Phase Framework

**Phase 1: Unfreeze**
- Challenge existing assumptions and status quo
- Create psychological safety for change
- Present compelling evidence for why current state is unsustainable
- Reduce restraining forces holding current behavior in place
- Techniques: crisis communication, benchmarking data, customer feedback

**Phase 2: Change (Transition)**
- Implement the actual change through participation and involvement
- Provide training, coaching, and support
- Allow time for learning and adjustment
- Communicate frequently about progress
- Address rumors and misinformation immediately
- Techniques: pilot programs, phased rollouts, feedback loops

**Phase 3: Refreeze**
- Stabilize the new state as the norm
- Integrate changes into processes, policies, and culture
- Reinforce through recognition and performance management
- Document new standards and procedures
- Celebrate achievement and close the change initiative
- Techniques: policy updates, recognition programs, success documentation

### Force Field Analysis

Map driving forces (pushing toward change) against restraining forces (resisting change):
- List all forces on both sides
- Score strength of each force (1-5)
- Strategy: Strengthen driving forces AND reduce restraining forces
- Focus more on reducing restraining forces — less resistance yields more sustainable change
