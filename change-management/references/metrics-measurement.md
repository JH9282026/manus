# Change Metrics and Measurement

Define, track, and report change management effectiveness through quantitative and qualitative metrics.

---

## Change Metrics Framework

### Leading Indicators (Predictive)

Track these during implementation to predict adoption success:

| Metric | Measurement Method | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Communication reach | Email opens, town hall attendance, intranet views | 90%+ of affected population |
| Training completion | LMS completion rates by role | 95%+ before go-live |
| Training proficiency | Post-training assessment scores | 80%+ passing rate |
| Sponsor engagement | Sponsor activity log (events, communications) | Weekly visible actions |
| Champion activation | Champion activity reports | 80%+ active champions |
| Stakeholder sentiment | Pulse survey sentiment scores | Trending positive |
| Resistance level | Resistance log entries and escalations | Declining trend |

### Lagging Indicators (Outcome-Based)

Track these post-implementation to measure actual outcomes:

| Metric | Measurement Method | Target |
|---|---|---|
| System utilization | Login rates, feature usage, transaction volume | 85%+ within 90 days |
| Process compliance | Audit results, deviation rates | 90%+ within 6 months |
| Proficiency | Error rates, time-to-task, help desk tickets | Return to baseline within 60 days |
| Employee satisfaction | eNPS, engagement survey, exit interview themes | Within 5 points of pre-change |
| Business benefit realization | ROI tracking against business case | 80%+ of projected benefits within 18 months |
| Sustainability | Adoption rates 6-12 months post go-live | Sustained at 85%+ |

## ADKAR Assessment Tracking

### Individual ADKAR Scoring

Administer periodic surveys measuring each ADKAR element on 1-5 scale:

- **Awareness** — "I understand why this change is happening" 
- **Desire** — "I want to participate in and support this change"
- **Knowledge** — "I know how to change (skills and behaviors)"
- **Ability** — "I can implement the change on a day-to-day basis"
- **Reinforcement** — "The change is being supported and sustained"

### Barrier Point Analysis

- Calculate average score per element
- The element with the lowest average is the organizational barrier point
- Direct resources and interventions at the barrier point
- Reassess monthly to track barrier point movement

## Change Health Scorecard

### Dashboard Design

Create a single-page executive dashboard showing:

1. **Overall change health** — Green/Yellow/Red based on composite score
2. **ADKAR scores** — Bar chart showing scores per element with trend arrows
3. **Adoption curve** — Line chart of adoption percentage over time vs. target
4. **Resistance heat map** — By department/location showing resistance levels
5. **Milestone tracker** — Timeline showing completed and upcoming milestones
6. **Key risks** — Top 3 risks with mitigation status

### Reporting Cadence

| Audience | Report | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Executive sponsor | Health scorecard + escalations | Weekly |
| Steering committee | Comprehensive dashboard + decisions needed | Bi-weekly |
| Change team | Detailed metrics + action items | Weekly |
| Managers | Team-level adoption + support resources | Bi-weekly |
| All employees | Progress updates + success stories | Monthly |

## Benefit Realization Tracking

### Benefits Register

Maintain a register mapping each expected benefit to:
- Baseline measurement (pre-change)
- Target measurement (post-change)
- Measurement method and data source
- Measurement timeline
- Accountable owner
- Current status and variance

### ROI Calculation

Calculate change management ROI using:
- **Costs** — Change team, training, communication, tools, lost productivity
- **Benefits** — Faster adoption (reduced productivity dip), higher adoption rate, faster benefit realization
- **Formula** — (Value of benefits achieved - Cost of change management) / Cost of change management
- **Benchmark** — Prosci research indicates effective change management delivers 6x ROI
