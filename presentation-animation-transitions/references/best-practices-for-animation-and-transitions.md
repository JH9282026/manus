## Best Practices for Animation and Transitions


### Planning and Design

1. **Start with Content**: Design slides first, add animation last
2. **Storyboard Complex Sequences**: Plan elaborate animations on paper
3. **Maintain Consistency**: Use same animation styles throughout
4. **Follow Brand Guidelines**: Align motion design with visual identity
5. **Consider the Venue**: Large screens need simpler effects


### Timing and Pacing

6. **Keep It Quick**: Most animations should be 0.3-0.75 seconds
7. **Match Speech Rhythm**: Animations should complement narration
8. **Allow Reading Time**: Don't animate during reading
9. **Use Delays Purposefully**: Create anticipation and breathing room
10. **Vary Pace Intentionally**: Fast sections vs. slow emphasis


### Technical Execution

11. **Test Thoroughly**: Preview entire presentation multiple times
12. **Create Backup**: Have non-animated version available
13. **Know Your Triggers**: Understand what advances each animation
14. **Number Your Clicks**: Track how many clicks per slide
15. **Practice Transitions**: Know when slide will advance


### Professional Polish

16. **Less Is More**: When in doubt, use fewer animations
17. **Consistency Over Variety**: One well-executed style beats five different ones
18. **Purpose Over Flash**: Every animation should have a reason
19. **Accessibility Awareness**: Consider motion-sensitive viewers
20. **Continuous Improvement**: Review recordings to refine technique
