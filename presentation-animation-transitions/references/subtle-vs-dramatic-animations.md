## Subtle vs. Dramatic Animations



### When to Use Subtle Animations

**Characteristics**:
- Short duration (0.25-0.5 seconds)
- Simple effects (fade, slight slide)
- Smooth easing
- Minimal distance traveled
- Natural, almost unnoticeable

**Best For**:
- Professional contexts
- Frequent animations throughout presentation
- Supporting content rather than starring
- Maintaining focus on speaker/message

**Examples**:
- Soft fade in for bullet points
- Gentle slide for supporting images
- Brief pulse for emphasis
- Smooth morph between similar slides



### When to Use Dramatic Animations

**Characteristics**:
- Longer duration (0.75-2 seconds)
- Complex effects (zoom, rotate, custom paths)
- More pronounced easing
- Greater visual impact
- Intentionally noticeable

**Best For**:
- Key moments and reveals
- Section transitions
- Emotional peaks in narrative
- Creative presentations
- Limited, strategic use

**Examples**:
- Big zoom for headline reveal
- 3D flip for major transition
- Complex choreographed sequence
- Dramatic morph transformation
