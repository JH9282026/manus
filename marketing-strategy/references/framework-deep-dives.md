## SWOT Analysis Framework

### Conducting Effective SWOT

**Internal Analysis (Strengths & Weaknesses)**:

*Strengths Assessment*:
- What do we do better than competitors?
- What unique resources do we have?
- What do customers see as our strengths?
- What advantages do we have in the market?

*Weaknesses Assessment*:
- What could we improve?
- Where do we lack resources?
- What do competitors do better?
- What causes customer churn?

**External Analysis (Opportunities & Threats)**:

*Opportunities Assessment*:
- What market trends favor us?
- What gaps exist in the market?
- What new technologies can we leverage?
- What partnerships could we pursue?

*Threats Assessment*:
- What are competitors doing?
- What market trends work against us?
- What regulatory changes loom?
- What economic factors pose risks?

**SWOT Matrix Example**:

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----------|------------|
| • Strong brand recognition in enterprise segment<br>• Proprietary AI technology<br>• High customer retention (92%)<br>• Experienced leadership team | • Limited marketing budget vs. competitors<br>• Weak presence in SMB market<br>• Slow product development cycle<br>• Limited international presence |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|---------------|----------|
| • Growing demand for AI solutions<br>• Expansion into European markets<br>• Partnership with major platform<br>• New use cases emerging | • Well-funded competitor entering market<br>• Economic downturn reducing budgets<br>• Regulatory scrutiny of AI<br>• Talent shortage in key roles |

**SWOT to Strategy Conversion**:

*SO Strategies* (Strength-Opportunity):
- Use strengths to capitalize on opportunities
- Example: Leverage AI technology to capture growing market demand

*WO Strategies* (Weakness-Opportunity):
- Overcome weaknesses to pursue opportunities
- Example: Partner with local firms to enter international markets despite limited presence

*ST Strategies* (Strength-Threat):
- Use strengths to avoid threats
- Example: Emphasize high retention rate to defend against new competitors

*WT Strategies* (Weakness-Threat):
- Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats
- Example: Focus on enterprise segment where we're strong rather than competing in SMB

## Marketing Mix (4Ps/7Ps) Framework

### Product Strategy

**Product Lifecycle Management**:

| Stage | Characteristics | Marketing Focus |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **Introduction** | Low sales, high costs | Awareness, trial, education |
| **Growth** | Rapid sales increase | Market penetration, differentiation |
| **Maturity** | Peak sales, high competition | Retention, optimization, segmentation |
| **Decline** | Falling sales | Efficiency, harvesting, exit planning |

**Product Positioning Elements**:
- Core features and benefits
- Quality and performance levels
- Design and user experience
- Brand and packaging
- Support and services
- Warranties and guarantees

### Price Strategy

**Pricing Models**:

*Cost-Based Pricing*:
- Cost-plus: Cost + desired margin
- Break-even: Cover fixed and variable costs

*Value-Based Pricing*:
- Price based on perceived customer value
- Premium pricing for differentiated offerings

*Competition-Based Pricing*:
- Match competitors
- Price below competitors (penetration)
- Price above competitors (premium)

*Dynamic Pricing*:
- Adjust based on demand, time, customer segment
- Common in ecommerce, travel, SaaS

**Pricing Psychology**:
- Charm pricing ($99 vs. $100)
- Prestige pricing (round numbers for luxury)
- Bundle pricing (package deals)
- Anchor pricing (show higher price first)
- Decoy pricing (middle option most attractive)

### Place (Distribution) Strategy

**Distribution Channel Options**:

*Direct Channels*:
- Company website/ecommerce
- Company retail stores
- Direct sales team
- Mobile apps

*Indirect Channels*:
- Retailers and resellers
- Distributors and wholesalers
- Marketplaces (Amazon, App stores)
- Affiliate partners

**Channel Strategy Selection**:

| Factor | Direct | Indirect |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Control** | High | Low |
| **Margins** | High | Lower |
| **Reach** | Limited | Extensive |
| **Investment** | High | Lower |
| **Customer Data** | Full access | Limited |

## Marketing Funnel Framework

### RACE Planning Model

**Reach** (Awareness):
- Objective: Build awareness and attract visitors
- Channels: SEO, paid ads, social media, PR, content
- Metrics: Impressions, reach, traffic, brand awareness

**Act** (Engagement):
- Objective: Encourage interaction and engagement
- Channels: Landing pages, content, email, social
- Metrics: Bounce rate, time on site, pages per session, engagement rate

**Convert** (Conversion):
- Objective: Turn prospects into customers
- Channels: Landing pages, email, retargeting, sales
- Metrics: Conversion rate, cost per acquisition, sales

**Engage** (Retention):
- Objective: Build loyalty and encourage advocacy
- Channels: Email, loyalty programs, community, support
- Metrics: Retention rate, LTV, NPS, referral rate

### Funnel Optimization

**Top of Funnel (TOFU)**:
- Focus: Awareness and education
- Content: Blog posts, videos, infographics, social content
- Goal: Attract and educate broad audience

**Middle of Funnel (MOFU)**:
- Focus: Consideration and evaluation
- Content: Case studies, webinars, comparison guides, demos
- Goal: Nurture leads and build trust

**Bottom of Funnel (BOFU)**:
- Focus: Decision and purchase
- Content: Free trials, consultations, pricing, testimonials
- Goal: Convert to customers

**Post-Purchase**:
- Focus: Retention and advocacy
- Content: Onboarding, education, community, referral programs
- Goal: Maximize LTV and generate referrals
