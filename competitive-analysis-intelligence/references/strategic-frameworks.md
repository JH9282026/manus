# Strategic Analysis Frameworks

Comprehensive guide to applying strategic frameworks for competitive analysis.

---

## SWOT Analysis

### Structure
- **Strengths**: Internal positive attributes
- **Weaknesses**: Internal limitations
- **Opportunities**: External favorable factors
- **Threats**: External unfavorable factors

### Application
1. Conduct for target company and each competitor
2. Create comparative SWOT identifying relative advantages
3. Link each element to strategic implications
4. Prioritize by impact and likelihood

---

## Porter's Five Forces

### Forces Analysis
1. **Competitive Rivalry**: Number of competitors, industry growth, differentiation
2. **Supplier Power**: Supplier concentration, switching costs, input uniqueness
3. **Buyer Power**: Buyer concentration, price sensitivity, information availability
4. **Threat of Substitutes**: Substitute availability, switching costs, price-performance
5. **Threat of New Entrants**: Barriers to entry, capital requirements, regulations

### Scoring
Rate each force 1-5:
- 1-2: Weak force (favorable)
- 3: Moderate force
- 4-5: Strong force (unfavorable)

### Output
- Visual diagram with force ratings
- Narrative for each force
- Overall industry attractiveness score
- Strategic implications

## PESTEL Analysis

### Factors
- **Political**: Government policies, trade regulations, political stability
- **Economic**: GDP growth, inflation, interest rates, exchange rates
- **Social**: Demographics, cultural trends, consumer attitudes
- **Technological**: R&D activity, automation, innovation rate
- **Environmental**: Climate change, sustainability, regulations
- **Legal**: Employment law, consumer protection, antitrust

### Assessment
For each factor:
- Impact rating (High/Medium/Low)
- Trend direction (↑ ↓ →)
- Strategic implications

## Value Chain Analysis

### Primary Activities
1. Inbound Logistics
2. Operations
3. Outbound Logistics
4. Marketing & Sales
5. Service

### Support Activities
1. Firm Infrastructure
2. Human Resource Management
3. Technology Development
4. Procurement

### Application
- Map cost contributions by activity
- Identify value-adding activities
- Compare against competitors
- Find optimization opportunities

## BCG Matrix

### Quadrants
- **Stars**: High growth, high share (invest)
- **Cash Cows**: Low growth, high share (harvest)
- **Question Marks**: High growth, low share (decide)
- **Dogs**: Low growth, low share (divest/hold)

### Application
- Plot competitor product portfolios
- Identify strategic priorities
- Predict competitive moves
