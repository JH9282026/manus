# Commercial Photography

Create professional imagery for advertising, marketing, e-commerce, and business applications.

## Overview

Commercial photography produces images for business purposes, including advertising, marketing materials, e-commerce, corporate communications, and brand campaigns. This skill covers lighting techniques, equipment selection, business practices, client management, and delivering professional results that meet commercial standards.

## Lighting Fundamentals

### Three-Point Lighting

| Light | Purpose | Placement |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Key Light** | Main light source, defines form | 45\u00b0 from subject, slightly above eye level |
| **Fill Light** | Softens shadows from key light | Opposite key light, lower power |
| **Back/Rim Light** | Separates subject from background | Behind subject, creates edge highlight |

### Lighting Techniques

**High-Key Lighting:**
- Bright, even illumination
- Minimal shadows
- Clean, professional look
- Common for e-commerce, beauty

**Low-Key Lighting:**
- Dramatic shadows and contrast
- Moody, artistic feel
- Emphasizes texture and form
- Common for luxury products, portraits

**Side Lighting:**
- Emphasizes texture and dimension
- Creates depth and volume
- Effective for textured products (textiles, wood)

**Backlighting:**
- Illuminates from behind subject
- Creates rim light and separation
- Highlights transparency and edges

## Equipment

### Cameras and Lenses

**Cameras:**
- Full-frame DSLR or mirrorless (Canon R5, Sony A7 series, Nikon Z series)
- High resolution for detail and cropping flexibility
- Tethered shooting capability

**Lenses:**
- 24-70mm f/2.8: Versatile zoom
- 50mm f/1.8: Portrait and product
- 85mm f/1.4: Portrait, shallow depth of field
- 100mm macro: Product detail, jewelry

### Lighting Equipment

**Continuous Lights:**
- LED panels: Real-time preview, video capability
- High CRI (90+) for accurate colors
- Adjustable color temperature

**Strobe/Flash:**
- More powerful than continuous
- Freezes motion
- Shorter recycle time for faster shooting

**Modifiers:**
- Softboxes: Soft, even light
- Umbrellas: Broad, diffused light
- Beauty dishes: Focused, flattering light
- Grids/snoots: Controlled, directional light
- Reflectors: Bounce light, fill shadows

## Business Practices

### Client Management

**Pre-Shoot:**
- Detailed brief and shot list
- Mood boards and references
- Timeline and deliverables
- Usage rights and licensing
- Contract and deposit

**During Shoot:**
- Tethered shooting for client review
- Multiple angles and variations
- Backup equipment ready
- Professional demeanor

**Post-Shoot:**
- Timely delivery
- Organized file naming
- Usage rights documentation
- Follow-up and feedback

### Pricing

**Factors:**
- Usage rights (web, print, duration, exclusivity)
- Production complexity
- Post-production time
- Licensing and buyouts
- Experience and market rates

**Models:**
- Day rate + usage fees
- Per-image pricing
- Package pricing
- Retainer agreements

## Workflow

**1. Pre-Production:**
- Client consultation
- Concept development
- Location scouting or studio booking
- Equipment preparation
- Shot list creation

**2. Production:**
- Setup and lighting tests
- Tethered shooting
- Multiple angles and variations
- Client review and approval
- Backup files immediately

**3. Post-Production:**
- Culling and selection
- Color correction and grading
- Retouching and enhancement
- Resizing and formatting
- Delivery and archiving
