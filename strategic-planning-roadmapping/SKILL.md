# Strategic Planning & Roadmapping

## Overview

This skill provides comprehensive guidance and best practices for this domain.

## 1. Skill Description and Purpose

Strategic Planning & Roadmapping is a foundational business capability that transforms organizational vision into executable action plans. This skill enables the systematic analysis of an organization's current state, desired future state, and the strategic initiatives required to bridge that gap. It encompasses environmental scanning, competitive analysis, goal setting, initiative prioritization, and the creation of visual roadmaps that communicate strategy across all organizational levels.

The core purpose of this skill is threefold: first, to establish clarity around organizational direction by defining mission, vision, and strategic objectives; second, to create alignment between disparate teams and departments by providing a shared understanding of priorities and dependencies; and third, to enable informed decision-making by establishing frameworks for resource allocation and initiative selection.

This skill should be deployed when organizations face inflection points such as market entry, digital transformation, merger integration, competitive disruption, or periodic strategic refresh cycles (typically 3-5 year planning horizons). It is equally valuable for startups establishing their go-to-market strategy as it is for established enterprises recalibrating their competitive positioning.

The strategic planning process follows established methodologies including OGSM (Objectives, Goals, Strategies, Measures), Balanced Scorecard, OKR (Objectives and Key Results), and Hoshin Kanri. Roadmapping leverages visual frameworks such as Gantt-style timeline views, swim lane diagrams, Now-Next-Later formats, and technology radar visualizations depending on the strategic context and audience requirements.

## 2. Required Inputs/Parameters

### Organizational Context
- **Company Overview**: Industry sector, company size (revenue, employees), market position, organizational structure
- **Mission and Vision Statements**: Current articulation of organizational purpose and aspirational future state
- **Historical Performance Data**: 3-5 years of financial performance, market share trends, key operational metrics
- **Existing Strategic Documents**: Previous strategic plans, annual reports, board presentations

### Environmental Analysis Inputs
- **Market Data**: Total addressable market (TAM), serviceable addressable market (SAM), market growth rates, segmentation data
- **Competitive Intelligence**: Competitor profiles, market share distribution, competitive positioning maps, recent competitive moves
- **Industry Trends**: Technology trends, regulatory changes, macroeconomic factors, demographic shifts
- **Customer Insights**: Voice of customer data, satisfaction scores, churn analysis, segment profitability

### Stakeholder Inputs
- **Executive Priorities**: C-suite strategic themes, board directives, investor expectations
- **Departmental Objectives**: Functional goals from sales, marketing, operations, technology, HR, finance
- **Resource Constraints**: Budget parameters, headcount limitations, technology infrastructure constraints
- **Risk Tolerance**: Organizational appetite for strategic risk, innovation investment thresholds

### Planning Parameters
- **Planning Horizon**: Timeframe for strategic plan (typically 3, 5, or 10 years)
- **Review Cadence**: Frequency of strategic review cycles (quarterly, semi-annual, annual)
- **Governance Structure**: Decision-making authority, approval workflows, escalation paths

## 3. Expected Outputs/Deliverables

### Strategic Plan Document
A comprehensive document (typically 30-50 pages) containing:
- Executive summary with strategic narrative
- Environmental analysis synthesis (PESTLE, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT)
- Strategic pillars and objectives (3-5 major strategic themes)
- Initiative portfolio with prioritization rationale
- Resource allocation framework
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategies
- Success metrics and KPI definitions
- Governance and accountability structure

### Visual Roadmap Artifacts
- **Executive Roadmap**: High-level 1-page visualization showing strategic themes across time horizons (Now/Next/Later or quarterly/annual views)
- **Initiative Roadmap**: Detailed view of all strategic initiatives with dependencies, milestones, and resource requirements
- **Technology Roadmap**: Platform and capability evolution timeline (if applicable)
- **Product Roadmap**: Feature and offering development timeline (if applicable)

### Supporting Materials
- **Strategic Alignment Matrix**: Mapping of initiatives to strategic objectives with impact scoring
- **Investment Business Cases**: ROI analysis for major strategic investments
- **Communication Package**: Stakeholder-specific presentations for board, executives, managers, and employees
- **Implementation Playbook**: Detailed execution guidance for first 90-day priorities

### Quality Standards
- All deliverables must be internally consistent and logically coherent
- Financial projections must include sensitivity analysis and scenario modeling
- Roadmaps must be version-controlled with clear ownership and update protocols
- All strategic claims must be evidence-based with traceable data sources

## 4. Example Use Cases

### Use Case 1: SaaS Company Growth Strategy
A B2B SaaS company with $20M ARR seeks to develop a 3-year strategic plan to achieve $100M ARR. The strategic planning engagement would:
- Analyze current customer segments, unit economics, and competitive positioning
- Identify growth vectors: geographic expansion, product extension, market penetration, M&A
- Develop roadmap showing phased execution: Year 1 (product-market fit optimization), Year 2 (sales scaling), Year 3 (market expansion)
- Create investment thesis for Series B fundraising aligned with strategic roadmap

### Use Case 2: Manufacturing Digital Transformation
A traditional manufacturing company embarks on Industry 4.0 transformation. Strategic planning would:
- Assess current digital maturity across operations, supply chain, and customer experience
- Benchmark against industry leaders and identify capability gaps
- Develop 5-year technology roadmap with IoT, AI/ML, and automation initiatives
- Create change management roadmap addressing workforce upskilling and organizational design

### Use Case 3: Healthcare System Service Line Strategy
A regional health system develops strategy for its oncology service line. The engagement would:
- Analyze market demand, competitive landscape, and referral patterns
- Assess clinical capabilities, physician alignment, and facility capacity
- Develop roadmap for clinical program expansion, technology investment, and strategic partnerships
- Create implementation plan with regulatory compliance milestones

### Use Case 4: Private Equity Portfolio Company Transformation
A PE-backed company requires 100-day transformation plan. Strategic planning would:
- Conduct rapid diagnostic of value creation opportunities
- Prioritize initiatives by impact and implementation speed
- Develop detailed roadmap with weekly milestones for first 100 days
- Establish governance cadence for PE sponsor reporting

## 5. Prerequisites or Dependencies

### Required Tools and Platforms
- **Strategic Planning Software**: Cascade, Workboard, Gtmhub, or equivalent for OKR/strategy management
- **Roadmapping Tools**: ProductPlan, Aha!, Roadmunk, or Miro for visual roadmap creation
- **Data Analysis**: Excel/Google Sheets for financial modeling, Tableau/Power BI for data visualization
- **Collaboration**: Microsoft Teams, Slack, or equivalent for stakeholder coordination
- **Document Management**: Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 for document creation and version control

### Data Access Requirements
- Financial systems access for historical performance data
- CRM access for customer and sales data
- Market research databases (Gartner, Forrester, IBISWorld, Statista) for industry data
- Competitive intelligence sources (Crunchbase, PitchBook, SEC filings)

### Stakeholder Access
- Executive sponsor with authority to allocate resources and make strategic decisions
- Cross-functional working group representing all major business units
- Subject matter experts for technical or specialized domain input
- Board or investor access for alignment on strategic direction

### Prior Knowledge Requirements
- Understanding of strategic planning frameworks (OGSM, Balanced Scorecard, OKR)
- Financial literacy including P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow interpretation
- Industry-specific knowledge relevant to the organization's sector
- Change management principles for implementation planning

### Process Dependencies
- Completion of any pending organizational changes that may impact strategy
- Availability of accurate and current financial and operational data
- Stakeholder commitment to planning process timeline (typically 6-12 weeks for comprehensive strategic planning)
- Clear decision-making authority and governance structure
