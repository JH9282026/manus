# Photography for Design

## Overview

Photography in design serves both aesthetic and functional purposes. Selecting, editing, and integrating photos requires understanding composition, technical quality, and strategic use.

---

## Selecting Stock Photography

**Quality Criteria**:
- High resolution (minimum 2000px on longest side)
- Sharp focus on subject
- Good lighting and exposure
- Authentic, not overly staged
- Diverse and inclusive representation

**Composition**: Look for negative space where text can be placed. Avoid busy backgrounds that compete with content.

**Licensing**: Understand license terms. Royalty-free vs rights-managed. Commercial use allowed? Attribution required?

**Sources**: 
- Unsplash, Pexels: Free, high-quality
- Adobe Stock, Shutterstock: Paid, extensive libraries
- Specialized: Industry-specific stock sites

**Avoid Clichés**: Overused stock photos (handshake, diverse team high-fiving) feel inauthentic. Look for unique perspectives.

---

## Photo Editing for Design

**Cropping**: Remove distractions, improve composition, adjust aspect ratio. Follow rule of thirds.

**Exposure and Contrast**: Adjust brightness, shadows, highlights. Ensure good tonal range without clipping.

**Color Correction**: Fix white balance, adjust saturation. Match brand colors or create cohesive palette.

**Sharpening**: Add subtle sharpening for crisp details. Don't over-sharpen (creates halos).

**Retouching**: Remove distractions, blemishes, or unwanted elements. Keep it natural.

**Filters and Effects**: Use sparingly. Consistent treatment across all photos in project.

**Tools**: Photoshop (professional), Lightroom (batch processing), Figma (basic adjustments), online tools (Photopea, Pixlr).

---

## Integrating Photos with Design

**Text Overlay**: 
- Ensure sufficient contrast between text and photo
- Use overlays (dark/light gradient) to improve readability
- Place text in negative space
- Consider text shadows or outlines if necessary

**Color Harmony**: Extract colors from photo for design palette. Creates cohesive look.

**Masking and Clipping**: Remove backgrounds, create custom shapes, blend with design elements.

**Duotone and Color Treatments**: Apply color overlays for stylistic effect and brand consistency.

**Layering**: Combine multiple photos, blend with graphics, create depth through layering.

## Hero Images and Banners

**Purpose**: Capture attention, set tone, communicate brand personality.

**Composition**: 
- Strong focal point
- Negative space for text (usually left or right third)
- Horizontal orientation for banners
- Consider mobile crop (center-weighted)

**Resolution**: High resolution for large displays. Minimum 1920px wide for full-width banners.

**Optimization**: Compress for web without visible quality loss. Use WebP or modern formats.

**Responsive Considerations**: Provide multiple sizes or use art direction for different breakpoints.

---

## Product Photography

**Clean Backgrounds**: White or neutral backgrounds for e-commerce. Lifestyle shots for context.

**Consistent Lighting**: All products should have same lighting setup. Maintains professional look.

**Multiple Angles**: Front, side, back, detail shots. Help users understand product.

**Scale Reference**: Include size reference or dimensions. Helps set expectations.

**Lifestyle Context**: Show product in use. Helps users envision ownership.

**Technical Specs**: High resolution, accurate colors, sharp focus. Critical for e-commerce.

---

## Image Optimization

**File Formats**:
- JPEG: Photos, complex images. Good compression.
- PNG: Graphics with transparency. Larger file size.
- WebP: Modern format. Better compression than JPEG. Not universally supported.
- SVG: Vector graphics, logos, icons. Infinitely scalable.

**Compression**: Balance quality and file size. Tools: TinyPNG, ImageOptim, Squoosh.

**Responsive Images**: Use srcset and sizes attributes. Serve appropriate size for device.

**Lazy Loading**: Load images as user scrolls. Improves initial page load.

**CDN**: Serve images from CDN for faster delivery and automatic optimization.
