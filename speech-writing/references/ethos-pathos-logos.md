### Ethos, Pathos, Logos

Aristotle's three modes of persuasion:

**Ethos (Credibility)**:
- Speaker's character and expertise
- Established through experience and knowledge
- Built through honesty and goodwill
- Demonstrated, not claimed

**Pathos (Emotion)**:
- Appeal to audience feelings
- Created through stories and vivid language
- Connects message to values
- Motivates action beyond logic

**Logos (Logic)**:
- Appeal to reason and evidence
- Supported by facts, data, examples
- Follows clear argument structure
- Addresses counterarguments

**Balance**: Most speeches need all three; adjust proportions based on purpose and audience.
