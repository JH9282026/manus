# Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch planning, pricing strategy, and distribution channels.

---

## Launch Planning

### Launch Tiers
| Tier | Scope | Activities |
|------|-------|------------|
| Tier 1 | Major launch | Full campaign, PR, event |
| Tier 2 | Significant | Blog, email, social push |
| Tier 3 | Minor | Release notes, in-app |

### Launch Checklist
- [ ] Positioning and messaging finalized
- [ ] Sales enablement complete
- [ ] Marketing assets ready
- [ ] Support documentation updated
- [ ] Analytics tracking in place
- [ ] Success metrics defined

---

## Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Models
| Model | Best For |
|-------|----------|
| Per-user | Collaboration tools |
| Usage-based | Infrastructure, API |
| Flat rate | Simple products |
| Tiered | Feature differentiation |
| Freemium | Volume acquisition |

### Value-Based Pricing
1. Identify customer value drivers
2. Quantify economic benefit
3. Price as percentage of value
4. Test and optimize

## Distribution Channels

### Channel Options
- Direct sales
- Self-service/PLG
- Channel partners
- Marketplaces
- OEM/white-label

### Product-Led Growth
- Free trial or freemium
- Self-service onboarding
- In-product conversion
- Expansion triggers
- Viral loops

---

## Post-Launch

### Success Metrics
- Adoption rate
- Activation rate
- Conversion rate
- Revenue impact
- Customer feedback

### Iteration
- Collect feedback immediately
- Monitor metrics daily
- Rapid iteration on issues
- Plan follow-up releases
