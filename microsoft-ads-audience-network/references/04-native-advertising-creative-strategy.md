# Microsoft Audience Network Native Advertising and Creative Strategy

## Understanding Native Advertising

### What is Native Advertising?

Native advertising is a form of paid media where ads match the look, feel, and function of the platform on which they appear. Unlike traditional display ads that stand out as advertisements, native ads blend seamlessly with surrounding content, creating a more organic and less intrusive user experience.

### Native Advertising on Microsoft Audience Network

MSAN's native advertising solution delivers ads that:
- **Integrate visually** with the host platform's design
- **Match content context** of the surrounding page
- **Provide value** through relevant, engaging content
- **Respect user experience** by avoiding disruption
- **Adapt responsively** to different placements and devices

### Benefits of Native Format

**Enhanced User Engagement**:
- Higher click-through rates compared to banner ads
- Reduced ad blindness and banner fatigue
- Better user reception and brand perception
- Increased time spent with ad content
- Lower bounce rates from ad clicks

**Performance Advantages**:
- 3x more likely to generate brand searches
- 4x more likely to drive website visits
- 6x more likely to result in conversions
- Higher quality traffic and engagement
- Better alignment with user intent

**Brand Safety and Quality**:
- Premium placement environments
- Association with trusted Microsoft properties
- High-quality publisher partners
- Brand-safe content adjacency
- Professional, polished presentation

## Creative Asset Requirements

### Image Specifications

#### Required Aspect Ratios

For optimal performance and maximum placement coverage, provide images in all four aspect ratios:

**1. Landscape (1.91:1)**
- **Minimum Dimensions**: 703 x 368 pixels
- **Recommended**: 1200 x 628 pixels or higher
- **Use Cases**: Desktop feed placements, article recommendations
- **Best For**: Horizontal compositions, landscape photography, wide product shots

**2. Square (1:1)**
- **Minimum Dimensions**: 300 x 300 pixels
- **Recommended**: 1200 x 1200 pixels or higher
- **Use Cases**: Mobile feeds, social-style placements, grid layouts
- **Best For**: Product photography, centered compositions, portraits

**3. Vertical (1:2)**
- **Minimum Dimensions**: 300 x 600 pixels
- **Recommended**: 600 x 1200 pixels or higher
- **Use Cases**: Mobile-optimized placements, sidebar units
- **Best For**: Full-body shots, tall products, mobile-first creative

**4. Wide Banner (4:1)**
- **Minimum Dimensions**: 1200 x 300 pixels
- **Recommended**: 1600 x 400 pixels or higher
- **Use Cases**: Premium desktop placements, header units
- **Best For**: Panoramic scenes, multiple products, brand messaging

#### Technical Requirements

- **File Format**: JPG, PNG (JPG preferred for file size)
- **File Size**: Maximum 5MB per image
- **Color Space**: RGB
- **Resolution**: 72 DPI minimum (higher for quality)
- **Transparency**: Supported in PNG, but solid backgrounds recommended

#### Image Quality Standards

**Visual Quality**:
- High resolution and sharp focus
- Proper exposure and color balance
- Professional composition
- No pixelation or compression artifacts
- Consistent brand visual identity

**Content Guidelines**:
- Minimal or no embedded text (platform may reject)
- Clear subject matter
- Appropriate for all audiences (unless specifically targeted)
- Authentic, non-stock appearance when possible
- Relevant to ad message and offer

### Text Asset Specifications

#### Headlines

**Quantity and Variety**:
- Provide up to **15 headline variations**
- Mix of short headlines (25-30 characters) and long headlines (80-90 characters)
- Each headline should be distinct and unique
- Test different messaging approaches

**Content Guidelines**:
- Include brand name in at least one headline
- Relate to target keywords and audience interests
- Use clear, compelling language
- Avoid excessive punctuation or special characters
- Test different value propositions
- Include numbers or statistics when relevant
- Create urgency or curiosity when appropriate

**Headline Types to Test**:
1. **Benefit-Focused**: "Save 40% on Premium Software"
2. **Question-Based**: "Ready to Transform Your Marketing?"
3. **Statistic-Driven**: "Join 50,000+ Satisfied Customers"
4. **Urgency-Creating**: "Limited Time: Free Trial Ends Soon"
5. **Brand-Focused**: "Microsoft Certified Solutions for Business"
6. **Problem-Solving**: "Eliminate Manual Data Entry Forever"
7. **Social Proof**: "Rated #1 by Industry Experts"

#### Descriptions

**Quantity and Variety**:
- Provide up to **5 description variations**
- Descriptions can be 60-90 characters
- Each should highlight different benefits or features
- Vary messaging approach

**Content Guidelines**:
- Highlight key product benefits
- Address customer pain points
- Include social proof or credibility indicators
- Use clear, concise language
- Avoid jargon unless targeting professionals
- Ensure mobile readability
- Complement headlines rather than repeat them

**Description Types to Test**:
1. **Feature-Focused**: "Cloud-based platform with real-time analytics and automated reporting."
2. **Benefit-Focused**: "Increase productivity by 50% while reducing costs and errors."
3. **Social Proof**: "Trusted by Fortune 500 companies worldwide for mission-critical operations."
4. **Risk Reversal**: "30-day money-back guarantee. No credit card required for trial."
5. **Differentiation**: "The only solution with built-in AI and 24/7 expert support."

**CTA Selection Strategy**:

Align CTA with campaign objective and funnel stage:

**Awareness Stage**:

- "Watch Video"

**Consideration Stage**:
- "Get Details"

**Conversion Stage**:

### Video Assets

MSAN now supports video within native ad placements:

**Video Specifications**:
- **Format**: MP4, MOV
- **Length**: 6-30 seconds recommended (up to 2 minutes supported)
- **Aspect Ratios**: 16:9 (landscape), 1:1 (square), 9:16 (vertical)
- **Resolution**: 1080p minimum
- **File Size**: Maximum 500MB
- **Audio**: Optional but recommended

**Video Best Practices**:
- Capture attention in first 3 seconds
- Design for sound-off viewing (use captions)
- Include branding early (within first 5 seconds)
- Keep messaging clear and concise
- End with strong call-to-action
- Optimize for mobile viewing
- Test different video lengths

## Creative Strategy and Best Practices

### 1. Lifestyle Imagery Strategy

**Why Lifestyle Images Outperform**:
- Show products in real-world context
- Help users visualize themselves using product
- Create emotional connections
- Tell a story beyond product features
- Increase relatability and aspiration

**Lifestyle Image Guidelines**:

**Depict Real-Life Activities**:
- Show people using products naturally
- Capture authentic moments and expressions
- Demonstrate product benefits in action
- Create scenarios target audience relates to

**Evoke Appropriate Emotions**:
- Align emotional tone with brand values
- Consider desired customer feeling:
  - Confidence (business software)
  - Joy (consumer products)
  - Security (insurance, financial services)
  - Excitement (travel, entertainment)
  - Relief (problem-solving products)

**Color Psychology**:
- Use bright, inviting color palettes
- Ensure colors align with brand identity
- Consider cultural color associations
- Test warm vs. cool color schemes
- Maintain visual consistency across assets

**People in Images**:
- Feature diverse, representative individuals
- Show genuine expressions and interactions
- Ensure people match target demographic
- Avoid overly staged or stock-looking photos
- Consider eye contact and gaze direction

### 2. Product-Focused Imagery

When lifestyle images aren't appropriate:

**Product Photography Best Practices**:
- Clean, uncluttered backgrounds
- Professional lighting and composition
- Multiple angles or views
- Show scale and context
- Highlight key features visually
- Maintain consistent style across products

**When to Use Product-Focused Images**:
- E-commerce and shopping campaigns
- Technical or B2B products
- New product launches
- Feature-specific messaging
- Dynamic remarketing

### 3. Creative Variety and Testing

**Avoid Ad Fatigue**:
- Rotate images regularly (every 4-6 weeks)
- Test 5-10 image variations simultaneously
- Monitor frequency and performance metrics
- Refresh creative when performance declines
- Maintain creative pipeline for quick swaps

**Testing Framework**:

**Image Variables to Test**:
- Lifestyle vs. product-focused
- People vs. no people
- Close-up vs. wide shots
- Different color schemes
- Various emotional tones
- Seasonal vs. evergreen

**Copy Variables to Test**:
- Benefit-focused vs. feature-focused
- Question-based vs. statement-based
- Urgency vs. informational
- Different value propositions
- Various CTAs
- Short vs. long copy

**Combination Testing**:
- Let Microsoft's AI test asset combinations
- Monitor asset performance ratings
- Identify winning combinations
- Scale top performers
- Replace poor performers

### 4. Brand Consistency

**Visual Identity**:
- Use consistent color palette
- Maintain typography standards
- Include logo appropriately (not required in image)
- Follow brand photography guidelines
- Ensure cohesive look across campaigns

**Messaging Consistency**:
- Align with overall brand voice
- Use consistent terminology
- Maintain brand positioning
- Reflect brand values
- Ensure cross-channel consistency

### 5. Mobile-First Creative Approach

**Mobile Optimization**:
- Design for small screens first
- Ensure text is readable on mobile
- Use clear, simple compositions
- Avoid tiny details that won't show
- Test on actual mobile devices
- Consider thumb-friendly placements

**Vertical and Square Formats**:
- Prioritize 1:1 and 1:2 aspect ratios
- Design specifically for mobile feeds
- Optimize for portrait orientation
- Consider mobile user context

### 6. Contextual Relevance

**Audience Alignment**:
- Tailor creative to audience characteristics
- Match imagery to demographic preferences
- Use appropriate language and tone
- Reflect audience values and interests
- Consider cultural sensitivities

**Placement Consideration**:
- Design for premium editorial environments
- Ensure content adjacency appropriateness
- Match sophistication level of placement
- Consider user mindset on different platforms

## Creative Performance Monitoring

### Asset-Level Reporting

**Performance Ratings**:
- **Best**: Top-performing assets, scale these
- **Good**: Solid performers, keep active
- **Low**: Underperformers, consider replacing
- **Learning**: Insufficient data, allow more time

**Metrics to Monitor**:
- Click-through rate by asset
- Conversion rate by asset
- Engagement metrics
- Frequency and reach
- Asset combination performance

### Optimization Actions

**For Top Performers**:
- Increase exposure
- Create similar variations
- Apply learnings to other campaigns
- Protect from fatigue with rotation

**For Poor Performers**:
- Replace with new assets
- Analyze why they underperformed
- Test alternative approaches
- Remove to improve overall performance

**Continuous Improvement**:
- Refresh creative quarterly minimum
- Test new concepts monthly
- Build creative library
- Document performance insights
- Share learnings across teams
