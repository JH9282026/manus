# Content Strategy & Planning

Develop comprehensive content strategies that align business goals with audience needs through systematic planning, production, and measurement.

## Overview

Content strategy is the practice of planning, creating, distributing, and governing content to achieve business objectives. It's not "just blogging" — it encompasses every content touchpoint from website copy to social posts to sales enablement materials. This skill covers strategic frameworks for auditing existing content, defining content pillars, building editorial calendars, establishing governance, and measuring content performance against business goals.

## Content Audit Methodology

### Audit Inventory Template

| Field | Purpose | Source |
|-------|---------|--------|
| URL | Identify the page | CMS or crawl tool |
| Title | Content identification | Page metadata |
| Content type | Categorization | Manual classification |
| Word count | Content depth assessment | Crawl tool |
| Publish date | Freshness assessment | CMS metadata |
| Last updated | Maintenance tracking | CMS metadata |
| Organic traffic (last 90 days) | Performance measurement | Google Analytics |
| Organic keywords ranking | SEO assessment | Search Console, Ahrefs |
| Backlinks | Authority assessment | Ahrefs, Moz |
| Conversion actions | Business impact | Analytics events |
| Content quality score | Qualitative assessment | Manual review (1–5 scale) |

### Audit Action Decisions

| Score | Traffic | Action | Priority |
|-------|---------|--------|----------|
| High quality + high traffic | Growing | Keep as-is, optimize for conversion | Low |
| High quality + low traffic | Declining | Improve SEO, promote more | Medium |
| Low quality + high traffic | Stable | Rewrite to improve quality + retain traffic | High |
| Low quality + low traffic | Low/none | Consolidate, redirect, or remove | Medium |
| Outdated (>18 months, no updates) | Any | Update, merge, or archive | High |
| Duplicate/cannibalized | Competing | Merge into one authoritative piece | High |

## Content Pillars & Topic Clusters

### Pillar Strategy Framework

| Element | Description | Example (SaaS Company) |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|
| Pillar page | Comprehensive hub (3,000–5,000 words) | "Complete Guide to Customer Success" |
| Cluster content | Supporting articles (1,000–2,000 words) | "How to Build a Health Score Model" |
| Internal linking | Cluster → Pillar bidirectional links | Every cluster links to pillar and vice versa |
| Topic scope | Broad enough for 10+ clusters | Customer success = onboarding + churn + QBRs + health scoring |

### Pillar Development Process
1. **Identify 3–5 core topics** aligned with business products/services
2. **Keyword research** — map search volume and intent for each pillar
3. **Cluster mapping** — identify 10–20 subtopics per pillar using keyword research
4. **Content gap analysis** — assess existing content against cluster map
5. **Prioritize creation** — fill highest-value gaps first (search volume × business alignment)
6. **Build internal links** — connect all cluster content to pillar pages

| Stage | Owner | Deliverable | SLA |

### Brand Voice Guidelines

| Dimension | Define | Example Spectrum |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Tone | Formal ↔ Casual | "We recommend" vs. "Here's what we'd do" |
| Personality | Serious ↔ Playful | "Comprehensive analysis" vs. "Let's dig in!" |
| Expertise | Authoritative ↔ Conversational | "Research demonstrates" vs. "We've found that" |
| Audience | Technical ↔ General | Jargon-friendly vs. jargon-free |
| Perspective | First person ↔ Third person | "We believe" vs. "Companies should" |

## Best Practices

- **Start with the audience, not the product** — what does your audience need to learn, not what do you want to sell
- **Audit before creating** — most content libraries have 30–50% of content that should be updated or removed
- **Build pillar pages first** — they establish topical authority that lifts all cluster content
- **Measure what matters** — traffic without conversion is vanity; tie metrics to business outcomes
- **Establish governance early** — ad hoc content creation leads to inconsistency and waste
- **Repurpose aggressively** — one pillar article can become 10+ social posts, a webinar, an email series, and a video
