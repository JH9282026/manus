# Power and Natural Gas Markets

## Wholesale Electricity Markets

### ISO/RTO Structure

| ISO/RTO | Region | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| PJM | Mid-Atlantic, Midwest | Largest US market, capacity market |
| ERCOT | Texas | Energy-only market, isolated grid |
| CAISO | California | High renewables, duck curve |
| MISO | Central US | Large wind penetration |
| NYISO | New York | Congested, high prices |
| ISO-NE | New England | Gas-dependent, winter spikes |
| SPP | Central Plains | Wind-heavy, low prices |

### Electricity Pricing Components
- **Energy price (LMP)**: Locational marginal price at each node
- **Congestion**: Price difference between nodes due to transmission limits
- **Losses**: Energy lost in transmission
- **Capacity**: Payment for available generation capacity
- **Ancillary services**: Regulation, reserves, frequency response

### Key Power Market Dynamics
- Electricity cannot be economically stored at scale (evolving with batteries)
- Prices can spike to $1,000+/MWh during extreme events
- Negative prices occur during high renewable output, low demand
- Duck curve: Midday solar surplus creating steep evening ramp
- Capacity markets ensure sufficient generation is available

## Natural Gas Market Analysis

### Supply Fundamentals
- **Production**: Shale basins (Permian, Appalachia, Haynesville)
- **Associated gas**: Gas produced alongside oil (Permian link to oil prices)
- **LNG imports/exports**: Growing export capacity connecting US to global markets
- **Pipeline flows**: Interstate pipeline capacity and utilization

### Demand Fundamentals
- **Power generation**: ~40% of US gas demand, highly weather-sensitive
- **Industrial**: Petrochemicals, fertilizer, manufacturing
- **Residential/commercial**: Heating demand, highly seasonal
- **LNG exports**: Growing structural demand

### Storage Analysis
- Monitor weekly EIA storage reports (Thursday)
- Compare to 5-year average and range
- End-of-injection (November) and end-of-withdrawal (March) targets
- Storage as price ceiling/floor mechanism

### Natural Gas Hedging Strategies

| Strategy | Instrument | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Fixed-price forward | Swap/future | Lock in price for budget certainty |
| Collar | Call + put options | Limit upside exposure with floor |
| Basis swap | Location differential | Manage delivery point risk |
| Costless collar | Buy call, sell put | Zero premium hedging |
| Storage optimization | Physical + financial | Seasonal arbitrage |

## Weather Impact Analysis

### Degree Days
- **HDD (Heating Degree Days)**: 65°F - avg daily temp (winter gas demand)
- **CDD (Cooling Degree Days)**: Avg daily temp - 65°F (summer power demand)
- Compare to normal and prior year for demand forecasting
- Population-weighted degree days more accurate for demand estimation
