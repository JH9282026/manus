# Live Sound Engineering

Professional live sound engineering techniques.

## Overview

Live sound engineering encompasses two primary roles: Front of House (FOH) mixing and monitor mixing. The FOH engineer focuses on the sound the audience hears from a console positioned within the audience area, while the monitor engineer ensures performers on stage hear themselves and bandmates clearly through stage monitors or in-ear monitors (IEMs).

This skill covers sound reinforcement systems, mixing console operation, microphone techniques, gain structure, feedback prevention, and the unique challenges of live performance environments across venues from small clubs to large festivals.

## Core Concepts

### Front of House (FOH) Mixing

**Audience Focus** — Create well-balanced, clear mix for audience perception

**Equipment** — Mixing console centrally located in audience area, main PA speakers, effects processors

**Signal Chain** — Input transducers (mics, DIs) → mixing console → processing → main sound reinforcement system

**Challenges** — Variable venue acoustics, public exposure, environmental factors (wind, weather)

### Monitor Systems

**Performer Focus** — Provide clear, effective sound for performers on stage

**Monitor Types:**
- **Wedge Monitors** — Floor monitors angled upward; passive or active
- **Side-fills** — Larger monitors for broad stage coverage
- **In-Ear Monitors (IEMs)** — Personalized stereo mix, superior clarity and isolation

**Mixing Approaches:**
- **From FOH Console** — Use pre-fade auxiliary sends for smaller setups
- **Dedicated Monitor Console** — Separate console side-stage for larger productions

**Technical Requirements** — Clear sound, firm punchy bass, controlled high frequencies, minimal feedback risk

### Soundcheck Workflow

1. **Setup** — Position microphones and instruments
2. **Individual Checks** — Set levels and EQ for each input
3. **Band Playthrough** — Fine-tune overall balance
4. **FOH Adjustments** — Achieve desired audience sound
5. **Monitor Adjustments** — Constant communication with performers for their mixes
6. **Spill Management** — Account for difference between empty and full venue

## Professional Workflows

### Live Mixing Workflow

**Pre-Show:**
- Arrive early for setup and soundcheck
- Configure console and routing
- Set up microphones and DI boxes
- Establish gain structure
- Create monitor mixes

**During Show:**
- Continuously adjust levels and EQ
- Manage feedback quickly
- Respond to performer requests
- Maintain consistent sound quality
- Adapt to venue acoustics

**Post-Show:**
- Document settings for future shows
- Note any issues or improvements
- Pack down equipment carefully

### Skills Required

- Knowledge of acoustics and audio equipment
- Microphone placement expertise
- Gain structure and feedback prevention
- Compression and EQ techniques
- Quick troubleshooting abilities
- Understanding of musical styles
- Communication with performers and crew
