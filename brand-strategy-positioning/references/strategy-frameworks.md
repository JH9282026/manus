# Brand Strategy Frameworks

Detailed frameworks for developing brand strategy.

---

## Purpose Development

### Finding Your Why

1. Examine founder motivations and origin story
2. Identify change you want to create in the world
3. Explore what your organization would fight for
4. Consider what would be lost if brand disappeared
5. Find intersection of passion, capability, and need

### Purpose Statement Criteria

- Inspirational and aspirational
- Beyond profit and products
- Authentic to organizational DNA
- Actionable and measurable
- Enduring (not trend-dependent)

### Purpose Examples

| Brand | Purpose |
|-------|--------|
| Patagonia | "We're in business to save our home planet" |
| TOMS | "Using business to improve lives" |
| Dove | "Help women develop positive relationship with appearance" |
| Tesla | "Accelerate world's transition to sustainable energy" |

## Vision & Mission Development

### Vision Statement Characteristics

- **Inspiring**: Motivates stakeholders
- **Future-Focused**: Describes desired future state
- **Ambitious**: Stretches beyond current reality
- **Clear**: Easy to understand and remember
- **Timeless**: Relevant for 10-20+ years

### Mission Statement Template

"We [action] for [audience] by [method] to [outcome]."

### Examples

| Brand | Vision/Mission |
|-------|---------------|
| Microsoft | "A computer on every desk and in every home" |
| LinkedIn | "Create economic opportunity for every member of global workforce" |
| IKEA | "Create better everyday life for the many people" |
| TED | "Spread ideas" |

## Values Definition

### Value Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Core | Non-negotiable, authentic now |
| Aspirational | Working toward, not yet achieved |
| Permission-to-Play | Basic expectations (honesty) |

### Value Development Process

1. Gather leadership and stakeholders
2. Identify moments when organization was at its best
3. Explore celebrated vs not tolerated behaviors
4. Examine decisions that define character
5. Find patterns revealing underlying principles

### Value Articulation

- Limit to 3-5 for focus
- Use specific, actionable language
- Describe behaviors, not just words
- Provide examples in action

## Brand Pyramid Framework

```
                [Essence]
                   |
              [Personality]
                   |
          [Emotional Benefits]
                   |
         [Functional Benefits]
                   |
              [Attributes]
```

**From bottom to top:**
1. Attributes: What the brand has (features)
2. Functional Benefits: What you get (advantages)
3. Emotional Benefits: How it makes you feel
4. Personality: How it feels (character)
5. Essence: The core idea (fundamental truth)

## Golden Circle (Simon Sinek)

```
         [WHY]
       /       \
     [HOW]   [HOW]
       \       /
        [WHAT]
```

- **Why**: Purpose (why you exist)
- **How**: Process (how you do it)
- **What**: Product (what you deliver)

"People don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it."
