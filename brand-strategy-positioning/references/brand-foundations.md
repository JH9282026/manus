# Brand Foundations

Detailed frameworks for developing brand purpose, vision, mission, and values.

---

## Brand Purpose

### What is Brand Purpose

Brand purpose is the fundamental reason your brand exists beyond making money. It answers "Why?" at the deepest level—what positive impact does your brand seek to create in the world?

### Purpose-Driven Brand Examples

| Brand | Purpose Statement |
|-------|-------------------|
| Patagonia | "We're in business to save our home planet" |
| TOMS | "Using business to improve lives" |
| Dove | "Help women develop a positive relationship with the way they look" |
| Tesla | "To accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy" |

### Finding Your Purpose

1. Examine founder motivations and origin story
2. Identify the change you want to create in the world
3. Explore what your organization would fight for
4. Consider what would be lost if your brand disappeared
5. Find intersection of passion, capability, and market need

### Purpose Statement Characteristics

- Inspirational and aspirational
- Beyond profit and products
- Authentic to organizational DNA
- Actionable and measurable
- Enduring (not trend-dependent)

## Brand Vision

### What is Brand Vision

Brand vision describes the aspirational future state your brand is working toward. It paints a picture of what success looks like—not just for your organization, but for the world your brand helps create.

### Vision Statement Examples

| Brand | Vision Statement |
|-------|------------------|
| Microsoft | "A computer on every desk and in every home" |
| Amazon | "To be Earth's most customer-centric company" |
| LinkedIn | "Create economic opportunity for every member of the global workforce" |
| IKEA | "To create a better everyday life for the many people" |

### Vision Statement Characteristics

- **Inspiring**: Motivate stakeholders to work toward the goal
- **Future-Focused**: Describe a desired future state
- **Ambitious**: Stretch beyond current reality
- **Clear**: Easy to understand and remember
- **Timeless**: Remain relevant for 10-20+ years

## Brand Mission

### What is Brand Mission

Brand mission defines what your brand does, for whom, and how. It translates purpose into action, describing the practical work your organization performs.

### Mission Statement Examples

| Brand | Mission Statement |
|-------|-------------------|
| Google | "To organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful" |
| TED | "Spread ideas" |
| Spotify | "To unlock the potential of human creativity by giving a million creative artists the opportunity to live off their art" |
| Airbnb | "To help create a world where anyone can belong anywhere" |

### Mission Statement Formula

"We [action] for [audience] by [method] to [outcome]."

### Mission Statement Characteristics

- **Clear**: Unambiguous about what you do
- **Actionable**: Describe activities, not just aspirations
- **Specific**: Define target audience and approach
- **Differentiating**: Distinguish from competitors
- **Concise**: Brief enough to remember

## Brand Values

### Types of Values

| Type | Description | Role |
|------|-------------|------|
| Core Values | Non-negotiable principles that define identity | Foundation of culture |
| Aspirational Values | Principles you're working toward | Growth direction |
| Permission-to-Play | Basic standards expected of any organization | Table stakes |

### Defining Brand Values Process

1. Gather leadership and key stakeholders
2. Identify moments when the organization was at its best
3. Explore what behaviors are celebrated vs. not tolerated
4. Examine decisions that define organizational character
5. Look for patterns that reveal underlying principles

### Prioritizing Values

- Limit to 3-5 core values for focus and memorability
- Eliminate generic values that don't differentiate
- Test values against difficult decisions
- Ensure values are actionable, not abstract

### Articulating Values

- Use clear, specific language
- Describe behaviors, not just words
- Create memorable phrases
- Provide examples of values in action

### Living Brand Values

- **Internal Culture**: Embed in hiring, onboarding, performance management
- **External Communication**: Visible in marketing and customer interactions
- **Decision-Making Filter**: Use values as criteria for strategic decisions
