# Audience Research

Methodologies and frameworks for understanding target audiences.

---

## Audience Research Methods

### Primary Research

| Method | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Surveys | Quantitative data collection at scale | Preferences, behaviors, attitudes |
| Interviews | In-depth one-on-one conversations | Motivations, pain points, decision processes |
| Focus Groups | Moderated group discussions | Reactions to concepts, diverse perspectives |
| Observation | Watching customers in natural environments | Behaviors people may not self-report |

### Secondary Research

| Source | Description | Value |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Market Reports | Industry analyses from research firms | Market size, trends, competitive data |
| Competitor Analysis | Study how competitors define audiences | Market positioning opportunities |
| Industry Data | Trade associations, government statistics | Demographics and behavioral insights |

### Social Listening

Monitor social media conversations, online reviews, and forum discussions to understand:
- Customer language and terminology
- Common concerns and frustrations
- Sentiment toward your brand and competitors
- Emerging trends and needs

## Demographics

| Factor | Description | Impact on Strategy |
|--------|-------------|--------------------|
| Age | Generational cohorts | Values, communication preferences |
| Gender | Gender identity | Product preferences, messaging |
| Income | Purchasing power | Price sensitivity, premium positioning |
| Education | Knowledge level | Information preferences, complexity |
| Occupation | Professional context | Needs, decision authority |
| Location | Geographic/cultural factors | Distribution, localization |
| Family Status | Life stage, household | Product relevance, priorities |

## Psychographics

| Factor | Questions to Answer |
|--------|---------------------|
| Values & Beliefs | What matters most? What do they stand for? |
| Attitudes | How do they view your category and competitors? |
| Interests | What captures their attention? How do they spend leisure time? |
| Lifestyle | How do they live? What choices define their experience? |
| Personality | Risk-takers or conservative? Analytical or intuitive? |
| Motivations | What drives their decisions? What are they trying to achieve? |
| Pain Points | What frustrates them? What problems need solving? |
| Goals & Aspirations | What future do they want? What does success look like? |

## Behavioral Characteristics

| Factor | Analysis Focus |
|--------|----------------|

| Brand Loyalty | How attached are they to current solutions? |
| Usage Patterns | How frequently do they use products/services? |

## Customer Persona Development

### Persona Components

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Name and Photo | Humanizing elements that make personas memorable |
| Demographics | Age, occupation, location, family status |
| Psychographics | Values, attitudes, personality traits |
| Goals | What they're trying to achieve |
| Pain Points | Frustrations and challenges they face |
| Behaviors | How they research, decide, and purchase |
| Quotes | Actual language from research that captures their voice |
| Brands They Love | Examples of brands they admire (reveals expectations) |

### Persona Template

```
## [Persona Name]

**Demographics**: [Age, occupation, location, family status]

**Background**: [Brief context about their life and work]

**Goals**:
- [Primary goal]
- [Secondary goal]
- [Tertiary goal]

**Pain Points**:
- [Frustration 1]
- [Frustration 2]
- [Frustration 3]

**Quote**: "[Authentic statement that captures their perspective]"

**Decision Factors**: [What matters most when choosing solutions]

**Preferred Channels**: [Where they consume information]

**Brands They Love**: [Examples of brands they admire]
```

### Using Personas

- **Design Decisions**: Reference personas when making product and experience choices
- **Marketing**: Tailor messaging, channels, and creative to specific persona needs
- **Product Development**: Prioritize features based on persona goals and pain points

## Audience Segmentation

### Segmentation Criteria

| Approach | Basis | Best For |
|----------|-------|----------|
| Demographic | Age, income, location | Broad targeting |
| Psychographic | Values, lifestyle, personality | Emotional positioning |
| Behavioral | Purchase patterns, usage, loyalty | Product marketing |
| Geographic | Region, urban/rural, climate | Local marketing |

### Primary vs Secondary Audiences

| Audience | Definition | Strategy Focus |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| Primary | Core customer segment most likely to purchase and most valuable | Brand strategy should primarily address this group |
| Secondary | Important but not primary focus (influencers, future customers) | Secondary messaging and touchpoints |

### Niche vs Broad Targeting

| Approach | Benefits | Challenges |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Niche Targeting | Deep positioning, loyal customers | Limited market size |
| Broad Targeting | Larger market, scale potential | Increased competition, diluted positioning |
