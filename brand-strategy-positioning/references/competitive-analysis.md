# Competitive Analysis

Frameworks for understanding the competitive landscape.

---

## Competitor Identification

### Competitor Types

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Direct | Same product, same audience | Coke vs Pepsi |
| Indirect | Different product, same need | Netflix vs video games |
| Aspirational | Excellence to learn from | Any industry leader |
| Future | Emerging threats | Startups, disruptors |

### Competitor Set Selection

1. List all companies in your category
2. Identify who customers compare you to
3. Research who competes for same keywords
4. Include adjacent category players
5. Prioritize 5-10 key competitors

## Research Framework

### What to Analyze

| Area | Questions |
|------|----------|
| Positioning | How do they position? What space do they claim? |
| Messaging | What claims? What language? |
| Visual Identity | What does brand look like? |
| Products | What do they offer? How does it compare? |
| Pricing | How priced? What value claimed? |
| Marketing | Where/how do they reach customers? |
| Experience | What is end-to-end experience like? |

### Research Methods

- Website analysis (positioning, messaging)
- Social media monitoring (content, engagement)
- Customer reviews (strengths, weaknesses)
- Mystery shopping (experience firsthand)
- Industry reports (analyst evaluations)

## SWOT Analysis

### Framework

```
          Helpful               Harmful
        +---------------+---------------+
Internal|   STRENGTHS   |   WEAKNESSES  |
        +---------------+---------------+
External| OPPORTUNITIES |    THREATS    |
        +---------------+---------------+
```

### Strategic Implications

| Combination | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| S-O | Leverage strengths to capitalize on opportunities |
| W-O | Address weaknesses to access opportunities |
| S-T | Use strengths to mitigate threats |
| W-T | Address weaknesses exposed to threats |

## Competitive Positioning Map

### Creating the Map

1. Choose two meaningful differentiating dimensions
2. Plot each competitor on the grid
3. Identify clusters and gaps
4. Determine your current position
5. Evaluate potential repositioning

### Common Dimensions

| Axis 1 | Axis 2 |
|--------|--------|
| Price | Quality |
| Traditional | Innovative |
| Local | Global |
| Specialized | Broad |
| Premium | Value |
| Functional | Emotional |

## Competitive Intelligence Sources

### Public Sources

- Company websites and press releases
- SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q for public companies)
- LinkedIn (employees, growth, culture)
- Glassdoor (employee perspective)
- Social media (messaging, engagement)
- Review sites (customer feedback)
- Industry publications and reports

### Analysis Tools

- SimilarWeb (traffic, digital presence)
- SEMrush (search positioning)
- Brandwatch (social listening)
- Crayon (competitive intelligence)
