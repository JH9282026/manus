# Regulatory Frameworks Reference

## Data Privacy Regulations

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
**Scope**: EU/EEA data subjects
**Key Requirements**:
- Lawful basis for processing (consent, legitimate interest, contract, legal obligation)
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) for high-risk processing
- Data Protection Officer appointment (mandatory for certain organizations)
- 72-hour breach notification to supervisory authority
- Data subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection)
- Cross-border transfer mechanisms (SCCs, BCRs, adequacy decisions)
- Records of processing activities
- Privacy by design and by default

**Penalty Structure**: Up to €20M or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever higher)

### CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act)
**Scope**: California residents' personal information
**Key Requirements**:
- Right to know what data is collected
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt-out of data sale/sharing
- Non-discrimination for exercising rights
- Privacy policy updates within 12 months
- Service provider contractual requirements
- Sensitive personal information protections (CPRA)

**Penalty Structure**: $2,500 per unintentional violation, $7,500 per intentional violation

### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
**Scope**: Protected Health Information (PHI)
**Key Requirements**:
- Privacy Rule: Use and disclosure limitations
- Security Rule: Administrative, physical, technical safeguards
- Breach Notification Rule: Individual and HHS notification
- Business Associate Agreements
- Minimum necessary standard
- Patient rights (access, amendment, accounting of disclosures)

**Penalty Structure**: $100 to $50,000 per violation, up to $1.5M per year per category

## Financial Regulations

### SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)
**Scope**: Public companies and their auditors
**Key Requirements**:
- Section 302: CEO/CFO certification of financial reports
- Section 404: Internal control assessment and reporting
- Section 409: Real-time disclosure of material changes
- Audit committee independence
- Whistleblower protections
- Document retention requirements

### AML/KYC (Anti-Money Laundering / Know Your Customer)
**Key Requirements**:
- Customer Identification Program (CIP)
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for high-risk
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)
- Ongoing monitoring and screening

## Industry-Specific Standards

### PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry)
- Secure network and systems maintenance
- Cardholder data protection
- Vulnerability management
- Access control measures
- Network monitoring and testing
- Information security policy

### ISO 27001 (Information Security)
- Information security management system (ISMS)
- Risk assessment and treatment
- Statement of Applicability
- Continuous improvement (Plan-Do-Check-Act)
- Annual surveillance audits
