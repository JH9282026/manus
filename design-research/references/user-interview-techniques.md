# User Interview Techniques

## Overview

User interviews are a foundational research method for understanding user needs, behaviors, and motivations. Effective interviewing requires preparation, active listening, and skilled questioning.

---

## Interview Planning

**Define Research Questions**: What do you need to learn? Be specific. 'Understand user needs' is too broad. 'Understand how users currently manage their finances' is specific.

**Create Discussion Guide**: Outline topics and questions, but stay flexible. Guide should be a roadmap, not a script.

**Recruit Participants**: Define screening criteria based on target users. Recruit 5-8 participants per user segment. Offer appropriate incentives.

**Logistics**: Schedule 60-minute sessions (45 min interview, 15 min buffer). Choose quiet location or reliable video platform. Test equipment beforehand.

---

## Building Rapport

**Introduction**: Explain who you are, purpose of research, how data will be used. Emphasize there are no wrong answers.

**Informed Consent**: Get permission to record (if applicable). Explain confidentiality and right to stop at any time.

**Warm-up Questions**: Start with easy, non-threatening questions. 'Tell me about your role' or 'How long have you been doing X?'

**Set Expectations**: Explain you're interested in their honest experience, not validating your ideas. Encourage them to think aloud.

---

## Questioning Techniques

**Open-ended Questions**: 'Tell me about...', 'How do you...', 'What was that experience like?' Encourages detailed responses.

**Probing Questions**: 'Can you tell me more about that?', 'What do you mean by...?', 'Why is that important?' Digs deeper.

**Specific Questions**: 'What did you do next?', 'How often does that happen?', 'When was the last time?' Gets concrete details.

**Avoid Leading Questions**: Don't ask 'Don't you think X would be better?' Instead ask 'How do you feel about X?'

**Avoid Yes/No Questions**: 'Do you like X?' gets one-word answer. 'What do you think about X?' gets explanation.

---

## Active Listening

**Give Full Attention**: Put away distractions. Make eye contact (in person) or look at camera (video). Show you're engaged.

**Don't Interrupt**: Let participants finish their thoughts. Resist urge to jump in with next question.

**Embrace Silence**: Pause after participant finishes. They often add important details after a moment.

**Reflect and Clarify**: 'So what I'm hearing is...' or 'It sounds like...' Confirms understanding and shows you're listening.

**Take Notes**: Capture key quotes and observations. But don't let note-taking distract from listening.

---

## Handling Challenges

**Participant Goes Off-Topic**: Gently redirect: 'That's interesting. Going back to what you said about...'

**Short Answers**: Use probing questions. If still stuck, try 'Can you walk me through a specific example?'

**Participant Asks Your Opinion**: Deflect politely: 'I'm interested in your perspective. What do you think?'

**Participant Criticizes Your Design**: Don't defend. Say 'That's helpful feedback. Tell me more about what's not working for you.'

**Technical Issues**: Have backup plan (phone call if video fails). Don't waste time troubleshooting—move to backup quickly.

---

## Closing the Interview

**Wrap-up Questions**: 'Is there anything else you think I should know?' or 'What haven't I asked about that's important?'

**Thank Participant**: Express genuine appreciation for their time and insights. Explain next steps.

**Debrief**: Immediately after interview, review notes and capture key takeaways while fresh. Note any surprises or patterns.

---

## Analysis and Synthesis

**Transcribe**: If recorded, transcribe interviews. Makes analysis easier and captures exact quotes.

**Code Data**: Identify themes and tag relevant quotes. Use affinity mapping to group related insights.

**Look for Patterns**: What did multiple participants mention? What surprised you? What contradicts your assumptions?

**Create Artifacts**: Develop personas, journey maps, or insight reports to communicate findings to team.
