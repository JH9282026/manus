# Article and Content Summarization

Condense large bodies of text into structured key points, executive summaries, and actionable insights.

## Overview

This skill applies extractive, abstractive, and hybrid summarization techniques to transform complex documents into reader-appropriate condensations. Use it for articles, research papers, business reports, legal documents, books, transcripts, and any text requiring condensation.

## Summarization Techniques

### Extractive

Select and arrange most important sentences from original:
- Preserves exact wording
- Maintains factual precision
- Best for legal, technical, scientific content

### Abstractive

Generate novel text capturing document essence:
- Paraphrases and synthesizes
- Creates readable narratives
- Best for executive summaries

### Hybrid

Combine extraction for accuracy with abstraction for readability:
- Extract key points
- Rephrase for coherent flow
- Best general-purpose approach

| Bullet Points | Variable | Hierarchical list | Quick reference |

| Structured | Mirrors original | Section headings | Complex documents |
| Multi-Level | Short/Medium/Long | Progressive disclosure | Varied audiences |

## Summary Structure

### Executive Summary

1. **Key Finding**: Most important takeaway
2. **Context**: Brief background
3. **Main Points**: 3-5 critical points
4. **Conclusion**: Final recommendation or insight

### Bullet Point Summary

- **Primary Point**
  - Supporting detail
  - Evidence or data
- **Secondary Point**
  - Supporting detail
