# Academic and Research Summaries

Summarize research papers while preserving scholarly integrity.

---

## Structured Abstract Format

### IMRaD Structure

**Introduction/Background**
- Research question
- Gap in literature
- Study purpose

**Methods**
- Study design
- Sample/participants
- Key procedures
- Analysis approach

**Results**
- Main findings
- Key statistics
- Significant outcomes

**Discussion/Conclusions**
- Interpretation
- Implications
- Limitations
- Future research

---

## Preserving Methodology

### Essential Elements

- Study type (RCT, cohort, qualitative)
- Sample size
- Duration
- Key variables
- Statistical methods

### Example

"Randomized controlled trial (n=500) over 12 months comparing intervention vs. placebo, analyzed using intention-to-treat."

---

## Statistical Reporting

### Include

- Sample sizes
- Effect sizes
- P-values or confidence intervals
- Significance levels

### Format

"Results showed significant improvement (p < 0.01, 95% CI: 1.2-2.4)"

---

## Citation Handling

### When to Preserve

- Direct quotes
- Specific claims
- Methodology references
- Key prior findings

### Format

(Author, Year) or [#] depending on style

---

## Limitations Section

Always note:
- Study design limitations
- Generalizability concerns
- Potential biases
- Data limitations

---

## Length by Paper Type

| Paper Type | Summary Length |
|------------|----------------|
| Journal article | 200-300 words |
| Review paper | 300-500 words |
| Thesis/Dissertation | 500-800 words |
| Book chapter | 300-500 words |

---

## Literature Review Summaries

### Per-Paper Summary

- Citation
- Research question
- Methods (brief)
- Key findings
- Relevance to review

### Synthesis Format

Group by:
- Themes
- Chronology
- Methodology
- Findings
