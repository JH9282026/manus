# Multi-Source Synthesis

Combine and compare multiple documents into unified summaries.

---

## Synthesis vs Comparison

### Synthesis

Combine multiple sources into unified narrative:
- Integrate findings
- Identify patterns
- Create coherent whole

### Comparison

Highlight differences and similarities:
- Side-by-side analysis
- Agreements and disagreements
- Competing viewpoints

---

## Synthesis Process

### Step 1: Individual Analysis

Summarize each source separately:
- Key findings
- Methodology
- Conclusions

### Step 2: Theme Identification

Find common themes across sources:
- Recurring points
- Shared conclusions
- Common evidence

### Step 3: Integration

Combine into unified narrative:
- Organize by theme, not source
- Note where sources agree
- Acknowledge disagreements

---

## Comparison Structure

### Side-by-Side Format

| Aspect | Source A | Source B | Source C |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Finding 1 | X | Y | Z |
| Finding 2 | X | Y | Z |
| Conclusion | X | Y | Z |

### Narrative Format

**Area of Agreement**
- All sources concur that...

**Areas of Disagreement**
- Source A argues X, while Source B contends Y

**Unique Contributions**
- Only Source C addresses...

---

## Literature Review Format

### Thematic Organization

**Theme 1: [Topic]**
- Source A found...
- Source B concluded...
- However, Source C suggests...

**Theme 2: [Topic]**
- Multiple studies agree...
- Conflicting evidence exists...

### Chronological Organization

- Early research established...
- Subsequent studies refined...
- Recent work has shown...

---

## Competitive Analysis Format

### Per-Competitor Summary

- Company overview
- Key strategy
- Strengths
- Weaknesses

### Comparative Table

| Factor | Competitor A | Competitor B | Our Position |
|--------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| Factor 1 | Status | Status | Status |

## Citation Management

### Track Sources

- Number sources [1], [2], [3]
- Or use author-date (Smith, 2024)

### Attribution

- "According to [Source]..."
- "[Source] found that..."
- "Multiple sources agree..." [1,2,3]
