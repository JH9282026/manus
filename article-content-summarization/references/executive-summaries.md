# Executive Summary Writing

Create decision-focused summaries for business audiences.

---

## Purpose

Executive summaries enable busy decision-makers to:
- Quickly grasp key findings
- Understand implications
- Make informed decisions
- Prioritize further reading

---

## Structure

### Standard Format (200-300 words)

**Opening (1-2 sentences)**
- State the core finding or recommendation
- Lead with the most important information

**Context (2-3 sentences)**
- Brief background
- Why this matters
- Scope of analysis

**Key Findings (3-5 bullets)**
- Most critical points
- Data-backed where possible
- Prioritized by importance

**Implications**
- What this means for the organization
- Risks or opportunities identified

**Recommendations**
- Clear next steps
- Actionable items
- Decision points

---

## Tone Guidelines

### Do

- Be direct and confident
- Use active voice
- Quantify when possible
- Focus on business impact
- Prioritize ruthlessly

### Avoid

- Hedging language
- Technical jargon
- Lengthy background
- Buried conclusions
- Passive constructions

---

## Example Structure

```
KEY FINDING: [One-sentence bottom line]

CONTEXT: [Why this analysis was conducted]

FINDINGS:
• [Most important finding with data]
• [Second finding]
• [Third finding]

IMPLICATIONS: [Business impact]

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. [Priority action]
2. [Secondary action]
3. [Follow-up action]
```

---

## Length Guidelines

| Original Document | Executive Summary |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| 5-10 pages | 150-200 words |
| 10-25 pages | 200-300 words |
| 25-50 pages | 300-400 words |
| 50+ pages | 400-500 words |

---

## Data Presentation

### Include Key Metrics

- Revenue impact: $X
- Growth rate: X%
- Time savings: X hours
- Cost reduction: X%

### Format for Scanning

- Use bullets over paragraphs
- Bold key numbers
- Lead with conclusions
