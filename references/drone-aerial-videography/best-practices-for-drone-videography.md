
### Technical Best Practices

1. **Always Do Pre-Flight Checks**: Equipment failure in air is dangerous and expensive

2. **Monitor Battery Levels**: Land with 20-30% battery remaining

3. **Use Manual Camera Settings**: Prevents auto-adjustments during flight

4. **Shoot in Log/Flat Profile**: Maximizes post-production flexibility

5. **Always Use ND Filters**: Maintains proper motion blur in daylight

6. **Fly Lower Than You Think**: Best footage often at 50-200 feet

7. **Maintain Smooth Movements**: Slow, deliberate stick inputs

8. **Always Have Backup Batteries**: Plan for multiple flights

### Creative Best Practices

1. **Scout Locations Before Flying**: Know what shots you want

2. **Study Weather and Light**: Golden hour transforms footage

3. **Tell a Story**: Even short clips need narrative purpose

4. **Vary Your Shots**: Mix altitudes, movements, and compositions

5. **Include Context Elements**: Ground reference adds impact

6. **Less Is More**: Quality over quantity in final edit

7. **Match the Mood**: Movement speed affects emotion

8. **Know When Not to Fly**: Sometimes traditional shots work better

### Safety Best Practices

1. **Never Fly Over People**: Unless specifically certified and equipped

2. **Respect Privacy**: Don't film people without consent

3. **Maintain Visual Line of Sight**: Required and safer

4. **Check Airspace Every Flight**: Conditions can change

5. **Have an Emergency Plan**: Know what to do if things go wrong

6. **Fly Defensively**: Assume the unexpected

7. **Keep Learning**: Regulations and best practices evolve

8. **Join Community**: Learn from other pilots' experiences
