
### Music Format Considerations

Different radio formats attract different audiences and create different contexts for your advertising.

**Country:** Family-focused, often rural or suburban, value-conscious. Authentic, down-to-earth copy works well.

**Top 40/Contemporary Hits:** Young, trend-conscious, urban and suburban. Energetic, current-sounding copy.

**Classic Rock:** Adults 35-54, often male-skewing. Nostalgic references and rock-oriented production can resonate.

**Urban/Hip-Hop:** Young, diverse, culture-conscious. Contemporary language and production essential.

**Adult Contemporary:** Adults 25-54, slightly female-skewing. Warm, lifestyle-focused copy.

**News/Talk:** Educated, engaged, news-focused. More detail and information can work; listeners are patient and attentive.

**Sports Radio:** Male-skewing, competitive, engaged. Energy, statistics, and sports metaphors resonate.

**NPR/Public Radio:** Highly educated, affluent, values-driven. Thoughtful, substantive copy; avoid hard-sell approaches.

### Adapting Copy for Format

**Example—same product, different formats:**

*Country format (pickup truck):*
"There's nothing like hitting the back roads with a truck you can count on. The new Ford F-150—built for the long haul, just like you. Test drive at Valley Ford."

*Top 40 format (same truck):*
"Upgrade your ride. The all-new Ford F-150 with Apple CarPlay, built-in Wi-Fi, and power that doesn't quit. Valley Ford—where deals happen."

*Sports radio (same truck):*
"When you need to haul the whole tailgate party, you need the F-150. Best-in-class towing, best-in-class payload. Get yours at Valley Ford."
