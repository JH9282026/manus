# Theory of Constraints and Business Process Reengineering

Address bottlenecks and fundamentally redesign processes.

---

## Theory of Constraints (TOC)

### Five Focusing Steps

1. **IDENTIFY** the system's constraint (bottleneck)
2. **EXPLOIT** the constraint (maximize its capacity)
3. **SUBORDINATE** everything else to the constraint
4. **ELEVATE** the constraint (increase its capacity)
5. **REPEAT** if constraint has moved

### Identifying Bottlenecks

**Indicators**:
- Work-in-progress piling up before a resource
- Resources waiting for work after a process step
- Longest process time in value stream
- Resource with highest utilization

### Drum-Buffer-Rope Scheduling

- **Drum**: Constraint sets the pace for the system
- **Buffer**: Time buffer protects constraint from starvation
- **Rope**: Signals to release material based on constraint capacity

### Throughput Accounting

- **Throughput (T)**: Rate of generating money through sales
- **Investment (I)**: Money tied up in the system
- **Operating Expense (OE)**: Money spent to turn I into T

**Key Metrics**:
- Net Profit = T - OE
- ROI = (T - OE) / I
- Productivity = T / OE

## Business Process Reengineering (BPR)

### BPR vs Incremental Improvement

| Aspect | Incremental | BPR |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| Scope | Within existing processes | Fundamental redesign |
| Change | Continuous, gradual | Radical, dramatic |
| Risk | Lower | Higher |
| Investment | Moderate | Significant |
| Timeline | Ongoing | Project-based |
| Approach | Bottom-up | Top-down |

### When to Use BPR

- Current processes fundamentally broken
- Technology enables dramatically different approaches
- Competitive pressures demand breakthrough improvement
- Incremental improvement cannot achieve required results
- Organizational restructuring already planned

### BPR Methodology

1. **Envision**: Develop vision and objectives
2. **Initiate**: Build case for change, secure commitment
3. **Diagnose**: Document current processes, identify issues
4. **Redesign**: Create new process designs from scratch
5. **Reconstruct**: Implement new processes, technology, organization
6. **Evaluate**: Measure results, refine as needed

### BPR Success Factors

- Strong executive championship
- Full-time, cross-functional team
- Clean-slate thinking with practical constraints
- Robust change management
- Phased implementation with milestones
- Contingency plans for risk

### BPR Risks and Challenges

1. **Scope Creep**: Attempting too many processes
2. **Technology Focus**: Assuming technology alone solves problems
3. **Neglecting Culture**: Underestimating resistance
4. **Insufficient Resources**: Underinvesting
5. **Loss of Knowledge**: Discarding tacit knowledge
6. **Implementation Fatigue**: Organizations unable to sustain energy

## Choosing Between TOC and BPR

### Use TOC When

- Need to improve throughput quickly
- Constraint is identifiable
- Process structure is sound
- Incremental gains acceptable

### Use BPR When

- Process is fundamentally flawed
- Technology enables new approach
- Breakthrough improvement required
- Organization ready for radical change
