# Agile Process Improvement

Applying iterative improvement approaches for rapid results.

---

## Agile Principles for Process Improvement

- **Iterative Approach**: Short improvement sprints with rapid feedback
- **Customer Collaboration**: Continuous stakeholder involvement
- **Responding to Change**: Adapting based on learning
- **Working Solutions**: Delivering tangible improvements quickly
- **Self-Organizing Teams**: Empowered improvement teams

---

## PDCA Cycle (Deming Cycle)

### Plan-Do-Check-Act

1. **Plan**: Identify opportunity, analyze data, develop hypothesis
2. **Do**: Implement change on small scale (pilot)
3. **Check**: Evaluate results against hypothesis
4. **Act**: Standardize if successful, or return to Plan

### PDSA Variation

- **Study** (instead of Check): Deeper analysis of results
- Emphasizes learning and understanding
- Not just pass/fail evaluation

---

## Scrum for Process Improvement

### Scrum Elements Applied

- **Sprint**: 2-4 week improvement cycles
- **Backlog**: Prioritized list of improvement opportunities
- **Daily Standup**: Quick team coordination
- **Sprint Review**: Demonstrate improvements to stakeholders
- **Retrospective**: Reflect on improvement process itself

### Sprint Structure

**Sprint Planning**
- Select improvement items from backlog
- Define sprint goal
- Break into tasks

**Daily Standup**
- What did you accomplish?
- What will you do today?
- What obstacles exist?

**Sprint Review**
- Demonstrate improvements
- Gather stakeholder feedback
- Update backlog

**Retrospective**
- What went well?
- What could improve?
- What will we try next?

## Rapid Experimentation

### Experiment Design

1. Define clear hypothesis
2. Design minimum viable experiment
3. Establish success criteria before testing
4. Document all learning, including failures
5. Iterate quickly based on results
6. Scale successful experiments

### Hypothesis Format

"If we [change], then [outcome] because [rationale]."

Example: "If we add a checklist at step 3, then defects will decrease by 20% because operators will catch errors before they progress."

---

## Improvement Sprints

### 2-Week Sprint Example

**Week 1**:
- Day 1-2: Analyze current state, identify opportunity
- Day 3-4: Design improvement, prepare pilot
- Day 5: Begin pilot implementation

**Week 2**:
- Day 1-3: Run pilot, collect data
- Day 4: Analyze results
- Day 5: Review, decide to scale or iterate

## Kanban for Improvement

### Improvement Board

| Backlog | Analyzing | Implementing | Validating | Done |
|---------|-----------|--------------|------------|------|
| Ideas | In analysis | Being tested | Measuring | Complete |

### WIP Limits

Limit work-in-progress to ensure focus:
- Only 2-3 improvements in progress
- Complete before starting new
- Reduces context switching

---

## Continuous Improvement Culture

### Daily Kaizen

- Morning huddles to discuss issues
- Visual boards for metrics and ideas
- Quick problem-solving when issues arise
- End-of-day reflection

### Suggestion Systems

- Easy submission process
- Rapid evaluation (within days)
- Recognition for all suggestions
- Celebration of implemented improvements

### Gemba Walks

Leaders regularly visit workplace to:
- Observe actual work
- Ask questions
- Build relationships
- Identify opportunities
- Follow up on previous observations
