# Marathon Training Programs

## Beginner Program (First Marathon, 16-20 weeks)

### Principles
- Run/walk approach acceptable
- Never increase weekly mileage more than 10%
- Long run caps at 20-22 miles before taper
- 3-4 running days per week minimum
- Focus on time on feet, not pace

### Sample Peak Week (Week 14-15)
| Day | Workout |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | Rest |
| Tue | 5 mi easy |
| Wed | 7 mi with 3 mi at marathon pace |
| Thu | Rest or cross-train |
| Fri | 4 mi easy |
| Sat | 20 mi long run (easy pace) |
| Sun | 3 mi recovery or rest |
| **Total** | **39 miles** |

---

## Intermediate Program (Sub-4:00, 18 weeks)

### Key Workouts
- **Long runs**: Build to 22 miles, include marathon pace segments
- **Tempo runs**: 4-8 miles at half-marathon effort
- **Intervals**: 6-10 × 800m at 5K pace
- **Marathon pace runs**: 10-14 miles with 6-8 at goal pace

### Sample Peak Week
| Day | Workout |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | Rest |
| Tue | 8 mi with 6 × 800m at 5K pace |
| Wed | 7 mi easy |
| Thu | 10 mi with 6 mi at marathon pace |
| Fri | 5 mi easy |
| Sat | 20 mi long run (last 6 at marathon pace) |
| Sun | 4 mi recovery |
| **Total** | **54 miles** |

---

## Advanced Program (Sub-3:15, 18 weeks)

### Key Workouts
- **Long runs**: 20-22 miles with significant MP work
- **Tempo runs**: 8-10 miles at lactate threshold
- **Intervals**: 10-12 × 800m or 5-6 × mile
- **Marathon pace**: 14-16 miles with 10-12 at goal pace
- **Double days**: Easy AM + workout PM

## Tapering Protocol

### 3-Week Taper
| Week | Volume Reduction | Intensity |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| 3 weeks out | 75% of peak | Maintain key workouts |
| 2 weeks out | 60% of peak | Shorter versions of key workouts |
| Race week | 40% of peak | Light jogs + strides only |

### Taper Best Practices
- Maintain workout frequency (reduce duration, not frequency)
- Keep 1-2 short quality sessions (strides, short tempo)
- Sleep more (extra 30-60 minutes if possible)
- Hydrate consistently
- Practice race-day nutrition in final long runs
- Expect taper madness (feeling sluggish is normal)

## Injury Prevention

### Common Running Injuries
| Injury | Cause | Prevention |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Runner's knee (PFPS) | Overuse, weak quads/hips | Strength training, gradual mileage |
| IT band syndrome | Weak glutes, overpronation | Hip strengthening, foam rolling |
| Plantar fasciitis | Tight calves, unsupportive shoes | Calf stretches, proper footwear |
| Shin splints | Too much too soon | 10% mileage rule, soft surfaces |
| Achilles tendinitis | Tight calves, hill overload | Eccentric heel drops, gradual hills |

### Strength Training for Runners (2× per week)
- Squats: 3 × 12
- Single-leg deadlifts: 3 × 10 each
- Hip bridges: 3 × 15
- Calf raises: 3 × 15
- Planks: 3 × 45 seconds
- Side planks: 3 × 30 seconds each
